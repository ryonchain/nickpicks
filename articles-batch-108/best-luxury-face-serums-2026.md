## Vitamin C Serum: What to Look For

The form of vitamin C matters:

| Form | Potency | Stability | Irritation |
|---|---|---|---|
| L-ascorbic acid | Highest | Low (oxidizes fast) | Medium |
| Ascorbyl glucoside | Medium | High | Low |
| Sodium ascorbyl phosphate | Medium | High | Very low |
| Tetrahexyldecyl ascorbate | Medium-high | High | Very low |

**For most people:** L-ascorbic acid at 10–20% concentration gives the strongest results. Use in the morning, always follow with SPF.

## How to Build a Luxury Serum Routine

1. **AM:** Vitamin C serum (antioxidant protection) → moisturizer → SPF
2. **PM:** Retinol serum (cell renewal) → richer moisturizer
3. **2–3x/week (PM):** Exfoliating serum (AHA/BHA) in place of retinol — not both at once

Serums are layered thinnest to thickest. Wait 30–60 seconds between layers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**When will I see results?**
Vitamin C: brightening in 2–4 weeks. Retinol: texture improvement in 4–6 weeks, line reduction in 8–12 weeks.

**Can I use multiple serums?**
Yes. Layer vitamin C in the AM and retinol in the PM. Avoid mixing L-ascorbic acid with niacinamide (can cause flushing in some people).

**What's the best age to start a luxury serum?**
Mid-to-late 20s for prevention (vitamin C, antioxidants). Retinol in late 20s–30s. The earlier, the better for anti-aging.
