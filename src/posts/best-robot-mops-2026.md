## Robot Mop vs. Robot Vacuum Mop Combo: Which Do You Need?

**Dedicated robot mops** (Braava line): Designed specifically for mopping. Smaller footprint, more precise water control, no risk of wet dirt getting dragged around because there's no vacuum. Best for floors you mop frequently and vacuum separately.

**Combo units** (Roborock, Ecovacs, Narwal): Vacuum and mop in one pass. Convenient but historically worse at mopping than a dedicated mop, and many users worry about cross-contamination (sweeping and mopping with the same unit). Newer high-end combos have largely solved this with separate wet and dry paths.

**Which to choose:**
- You already have a robot vacuum → dedicated mop (Braava)
- You want one device for everything → combo unit (Roborock S8+, Ecovacs DEEBOT T30)
- Budget under $300 → combo unit, but expect compromise

## Floor Compatibility

| Floor Type | Robot Mop Safe? | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Hardwood (sealed) | Yes | Lower water volume, no soaking |
| LVP / Vinyl plank | Yes | Very water tolerant |
| Ceramic / Porcelain tile | Yes | More water is fine |
| Natural stone (unsealed) | No | Water can damage |
| Carpet | No | Robot mops should avoid or lift pad on carpet |

Most modern robot mops include carpet detection — they pause or lift the mopping pad when crossing carpet. Verify this feature before buying if you have mixed flooring.

## How to Get the Most From a Robot Mop

1. **Vacuum first.** If you're using a dedicated mop, always vacuum or sweep before mopping — mopping over large debris just pushes it around.
2. **Pre-treat dried spills.** Spray cleaner on stuck-on food before running the mop.
3. **Use the right pads.** Microfiber pads clean better than disposable dry pads for most surfaces.
4. **Rinse pads after each use.** Dirty pads spread dirty water.
5. **Set mop-only zones** if you have carpet adjacent to hard floors — most apps support this.
