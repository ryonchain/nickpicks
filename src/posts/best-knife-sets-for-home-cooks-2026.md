## Buying Guide: German vs. Japanese Knives

### German Knives
- **Profile:** Thicker spine, full bolster, heavier
- **Edge angle:** 17–22° per side — more robust
- **Best for:** Rocking cuts, heavy prep, chopping through bones
- **Maintenance:** Easy to sharpen, tolerant of casual use
- **Brands:** Wusthof, Henckels, Messermeister

### Japanese Knives
- **Profile:** Thinner spine, no bolster, lighter
- **Edge angle:** 10–15° per side — sharper but more delicate
- **Best for:** Precision slicing, fish, vegetables, quick cuts
- **Maintenance:** Sharper but more prone to chipping if misused
- **Brands:** Shun, Global, MAC, Miyabi

### Key Practical Points
- **Most home cooks:** Get a German chef's knife. Easier to maintain, harder to damage.
- **Technique-focused cooks:** Japanese knives reward skill with exceptional cutting feel.
- **Don't buy a big block set:** You'll use 2–3 knives. Buy those well.

### What You Actually Need
1. **8-inch chef's knife** — handles 80% of tasks
2. **3.5-inch paring knife** — small precision work
3. **Serrated bread knife** — bread, tomatoes, delicate pastry

Everything else in a block set collects dust.

### How to Sharpen at Home
- **Honing steel:** Quick edge realignment before each use — doesn't remove metal
- **Pull-through sharpener:** Fast but removes more steel, damages fine edges
- **Whetstone:** Best results, requires 15–20 minutes to learn

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are expensive knives worth it?**
Yes — up to a point. A $40 Victorinox genuinely outperforms many $100+ block sets. A $400 Wusthof set outperforms a $130 Henckels set, but the margin is smaller than the price suggests. The biggest gains come from proper sharpening, not price.

**Should I put knives in the dishwasher?**
No, even if the manufacturer says dishwasher-safe. The high-alkaline detergent and heat cycles dull edges and damage handles. Hand wash and towel dry immediately to preserve sharpness and finish.

**How often should I sharpen my knives?**
Home cooks: every 3–6 months for whetstoning, every 1–2 uses on a honing steel. Restaurants sharpen daily. The honing steel doesn't sharpen (doesn't remove metal) — it realigns the rolled edge between real sharpenings.

**Is a knife block safe?**
Yes, but countertop blocks collect moisture and bacteria between the slots. Magnetic wall strips are cleaner and easier to use. Both are safer than loose knives in a drawer.

**German or Japanese — which is right for me?**
If you use your knife heavily and prefer low maintenance: German. If you cook frequently, care about technique, and will maintain the edge properly: Japanese. For most beginners, start with German.
