## What to Look for in an Adult Puzzle

Not all 1000-piece puzzles are equal. The difference between a satisfying session and a frustrating one comes down to:

**Piece quality:**
- **Thickness** — thin cardboard pieces warp and tear; look for 1.8–2mm thickness
- **Cut precision** — poor cuts mean false fits, where pieces seem to snap in but don't belong
- **Surface finish** — matte/linen finish reduces glare; glossy finishes are harder to work with under most lighting

**Image quality:**
- **Color differentiation** — sky-heavy or water-heavy images are harder because sections look identical
- **Detail density** — busy illustrations give more visual anchors per square inch
- **Print sharpness** — blurry printing makes edge-finding harder

**Piece count and dimensions:**
- 1000 pieces: ~27" × 19" assembled, 2–6 hours for average puzzler
- 1500 pieces: ~32" × 22", adds 3–5 hours
- 2000+ pieces: committed weekend project

## Puzzle Tips for Adults

**Start with the border** — straighten edges make a framework for everything else. **Sort by color and pattern** — group pieces before you start building. **Work in sections** — identify distinct visual zones (sky, architecture, foreground) and build each separately. **Invest in a puzzle mat** — if you don't have a dedicated puzzle table, a roll-up mat lets you save and store an in-progress puzzle.
