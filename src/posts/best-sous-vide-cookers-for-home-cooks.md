## What Is Sous Vide and Why Should Home Cooks Care?

Sous vide (French for "under vacuum") is a cooking method where vacuum-sealed food is submerged in a precisely temperature-controlled water bath. You cook at the exact internal temperature you want the food to reach — there's no overcooking, no guessing, no cutting to check doneness.

**Practical examples:**
- Steak at 130°F (54.5°C) for medium-rare: cook for 1–4 hours, then sear 60 seconds per side. Perfect edge-to-edge medium-rare every time, not just in the center.
- Chicken breast at 140°F (60°C) for 1.5 hours: juicy, safe, never dry. Conventional baking at 165°F over-dries chicken; sous vide holds exactly 140°F throughout.
- Salmon at 122°F (50°C) for 30 minutes: silky, translucent, restaurant-grade texture impossible with pan cooking.

The device (an immersion circulator) clips to the side of any pot or container, heats water to a set temperature, and continuously circulates to maintain even heat. You bag the food in a zip-lock or vacuum bag, submerge it, and walk away.

## What Container to Use

Any pot or vessel works with a sous vide circulator. The most popular option is a clear polycarbonate food container (sold separately):

- **12-quart Cambro**: perfect for 1–4 steaks, fish fillets, chicken portions — [View on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00E2GJXQC?tag=nicklpicks-20) (~$15)
- **Large stockpot**: works fine, less visible
- **Ice chest/cooler**: retains heat longer, ideal for multi-hour cooks

A rack or weights keep bags fully submerged — if a bag floats, that part won't cook correctly.

## Temperature Quick Reference

| Food | Temperature | Time | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Steak (medium-rare) | 130°F (54.5°C) | 1–4 hrs | Edge-to-edge pink |
| Steak (medium) | 140°F (60°C) | 1–4 hrs | Slight pink center |
| Chicken breast | 140°F (60°C) | 1.5–4 hrs | Juicy, safe, not dry |
| Salmon | 122°F (50°C) | 30–45 min | Silky, translucent |
| Pork tenderloin | 140°F (60°C) | 1–3 hrs | Perfectly moist |
| Eggs (soft-poached) | 167°F (75°C) | 13 min | Runny yolk, set white |
| Carrots | 183°F (84°C) | 1 hr | Tender, concentrated flavor |

Always sear proteins after sous vide for Maillard reaction browning — a hot cast iron for 60 seconds per side.
