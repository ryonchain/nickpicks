## Newborn Stroller Requirements: The Non-Negotiables

**Full flat recline:** Newborns need to lie flat to protect their airway and support their developing spine. The standard is 170°+ recline (ideally fully flat). Strollers that only recline to 130–140° are not safe for newborns.

**Infant car seat compatibility:** Many parents use a snap-in infant car seat instead of the stroller seat for newborns. This works well but requires checking that the stroller is compatible with your specific car seat brand/model.

**5-point harness:** Required for safety as soon as your baby starts sitting up in the stroller seat.

**Weight and foldability:** Strollers you'll actually use are the ones you can fold with one hand, lift into a car trunk, and manage solo. For daily use, under 20 lbs is the practical threshold; under 15 lbs for travel.
