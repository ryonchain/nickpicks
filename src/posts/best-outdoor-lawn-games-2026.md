## Picking the Right Lawn Game

The best lawn game depends on your setup:

**Space constraints:**
- Small patio/deck: Bocce, Kan Jam, ring toss
- Medium lawn: Cornhole, ladder toss
- Large open space: Spikeball, Kubb, croquet

**Group size:**
- 2 people: Bocce, cornhole, ladder toss
- 4 people: Cornhole, Spikeball, Kan Jam
- 6–12 people: All of the above in rotation

**Activity level:**
- Relaxed: Bocce, horseshoes, ring toss
- Moderate: Cornhole, ladder toss, Kubb
- Active: Spikeball, badminton

Spikeball is played on a round trampoline-like net set at ankle height. Two teams of two hit a small ball onto the net — the opposing team has three touches to return it, bouncing it back off the net. If they can't, you score. Play to 21.

### 5. Kubb — Best Viking Strategy Lawn Game
**~$35** | [BUY ON AMAZON]

Kubb (pronounced "koob") is a Swedish lawn game with ancient roots. Players throw wooden batons to knock over opponents' wooden blocks (kubbs), then topple the King pin for the win. Downed kubbs can be relocated by the opponent, creating strategic positioning.

**Why We Recommend It:** Kubb rewards technique and strategy unlike most lawn games. It's the most social lawn game here — games take 20–40 minutes with casual conversation woven in. The wooden pieces feel substantial and look great. Equally popular at family gatherings and adult parties.

- Players: 2–12
- Space needed: 5 × 8 meters
- Setup time: 5 min
- Portability: Good (comes in a wooden box)
