## Buying Guide: Key Considerations

### Compatibility Check
Before buying, verify your system is compatible. Run the manufacturer's compatibility checker using your current thermostat's wiring:
- **Nest:** [nest.com/compatibility](https://nest.com/compatibility)
- **Ecobee:** [ecobee.com/compatibility-checker](https://www.ecobee.com/compatibility-checker)

### C-Wire Requirement
Most modern smart thermostats need a C-wire (common wire) for constant power. Older homes may not have one. Nest includes a power adapter; Ecobee includes a power extender kit. Check your current thermostat for a wire labeled "C" before buying.

### Single-Stage vs. Multi-Stage Systems
Most homes have single-stage HVAC (heat on/off). Larger homes may have multi-stage or variable-speed systems. Ecobee handles the widest range; Amazon and basic Nest models are limited to single-stage.

### Smart Home Ecosystems
- **Google Home users:** Nest is the natural fit
- **Amazon Echo/Alexa users:** Any option works; Amazon Smart Thermostat is deeply integrated
- **Apple HomeKit:** Ecobee has the strongest HomeKit + Siri support

### Energy Savings Estimates
EPA's ENERGY STAR program estimates 8% annual savings on heating and cooling with a smart thermostat — roughly $130/year for average households. Savings vary significantly by climate, home size, and how well you used your old thermostat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are smart thermostats hard to install?**
For most homes with standard wiring: no. Installation takes 20–45 minutes with basic tools (screwdriver). The thermostat apps guide you through wiring step-by-step. If your system has more than 6 wires or is a heat pump, read the compatibility guide carefully.

**Do smart thermostats work without Wi-Fi?**
Yes — smart thermostats function as regular programmable thermostats without Wi-Fi. Remote access, app control, and learning features require a Wi-Fi connection.

**How much can a smart thermostat save on my energy bill?**
Average savings: $100–180/year for households currently using manual or basic thermostats. Savings are highest if you previously left your HVAC at a constant temperature, rather than manually adjusting frequently.

**Can smart thermostats control multiple zones?**
Single thermostats control a single zone. For multi-zone homes, you need one thermostat per zone. Ecobee and Honeywell support systems where multiple thermostats coordinate; each still requires separate purchase and installation.

**Do smart thermostats work with heat pumps?**
Many do, but compatibility is more complex. Verify with the manufacturer's checker before purchasing. Ecobee has the broadest heat pump compatibility.
