## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### Suction Power (Watts/AW)
- **Under 60 AW:** Light duty, primarily hard floors
- **60–100 AW:** General use, low-to-medium pile carpet
- **100+ AW:** Heavy duty, thick carpet, embedded pet hair

### Run Time
- **Under 30 min:** Very small apartments only
- **30–45 min:** Adequate for most apartments and small homes
- **45–60 min:** Handles large homes (2000+ sq ft)
- **60+ min:** Large homes or cleaning sessions without interruption

### Battery Swappability
Some models (Dyson V15, Shark) support swappable batteries. If you have a large home, check if the model allows battery swapping mid-clean.

### Hard Floor vs. Carpet Mode
Models with automatic carpet detection (Dyson V12, newer Sharks) are more convenient. Others require manual mode switching. If your home is primarily hard floors, any model works. For mixed surfaces, look for seamless transitions.

### Self-Cleaning Brushroll
Pet owners: prioritize this feature above almost everything else. Without it, you'll spend 5–10 minutes per use removing tangled fur from the brushroll by hand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a cordless vacuum replace my corded vacuum?**
For most households: yes. Cordless vacuums have reached suction levels that match or exceed mid-range corded units. The trade-off is battery life — for cleaning sessions over 60 minutes, you'll need a corded unit or swappable battery.

**How long do cordless vacuum batteries last?**
The built-in battery typically lasts 3–5 years of regular use before capacity degrades noticeably. Replacement batteries cost $50–100 for most models. Dyson and Shark both sell replacement batteries.

**What suction level do I need for pet hair?**
At minimum 80+ AW for embedded pet hair in carpet. The Shark Stratos (100+ AW) and Dyson V12 (150 AW in max mode) are both strong enough for heavy shedders. Brushroll design matters equally — self-cleaning brushrolls are essential.

**Are cordless vacuums good for stairs?**
Yes — this is where they excel over full-size uprights. The handheld mode on most stick vacuums is ideal for stairs. The key is weight: under 6 lbs is preferable for stair work.

**How do I maintain a cordless vacuum?**
Empty the dustbin frequently (when 2/3 full to maintain suction). Wash filters monthly with cold water, allow 24 hours to dry before reinstalling. Clean the brushroll of hair after each use (or buy a self-cleaning model). Check for blockages in the suction tube periodically.
