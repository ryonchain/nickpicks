## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Smart Doorbell

**Wired vs. battery.** Battery doorbells (Ring 4, Arlo) install anywhere in 15 minutes — no electrician needed. Wired doorbells (Nest, Ring Wired) never need recharging and often unlock continuous recording. If you have existing doorbell wiring, wired is usually the better choice.

**Subscription plans.** Ring, Nest, and Arlo all require a monthly plan ($3–$10/month) to store video history in the cloud. Eufy is the exception — local storage, no subscription. If you want to avoid recurring fees, Eufy or Arlo with a SmartHub are your options.

**Smart home ecosystem.** Ring pairs best with Alexa and Amazon devices. Google Nest pairs best with Google Home. Eufy and Arlo support all three major platforms.

**Night vision.** Standard IR night vision produces black-and-white footage. Color night vision (Ring 4, Eufy E340, Google Nest) produces much clearer color footage in low light — worth it for identifying clothing color, vehicle color, or face detail at night.

## FAQ

**Do smart doorbells work without WiFi?**
Most smart doorbells require WiFi for live view, alerts, and cloud storage. Some (like Eufy with a HomeBase) can store locally without active internet, but remote access requires a connection.

**Can I install a smart doorbell without existing wiring?**
Yes — battery-powered models like the Ring Video Doorbell 4 install anywhere with two screws and no wiring. Average installation time is 15–20 minutes.

**What happens if my internet goes down?**
Cloud-dependent doorbells lose remote access and recording during an outage. Eufy with local storage continues recording. None of the standard models have LTE fallback.

**Are smart doorbells easy to steal?**
Most include anti-theft mounting hardware and tamper alerts. Ring also has a theft replacement policy for stolen devices purchased with a Ring Protect plan.
