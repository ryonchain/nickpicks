## What Changes at the Intermediate Level

**Head size:** Beginner racquets often have 110+ sq in heads. Intermediate players benefit from 98–105 sq in for better control without giving up too much sweet spot.

**Weight:** Intermediates can handle 10–11 oz strung. Heavier racquets (11+ oz) absorb shock better and provide more plow-through on heavy balls.

**Balance:** Head-light balance adds maneuverability. Head-heavy adds power but reduces feel and can contribute to arm fatigue.

**Stiffness:** Stiffer frames (RA 65+) transmit power efficiently but amplify vibration. Flexible frames (RA 55–64) are easier on the arm with better feel.

## Strings and Tension

Your racquet's performance is 50% strings. Factory strings (if the racquet comes pre-strung) are usually low-quality. Restringing immediately makes a significant difference.

**Best beginner-intermediate strings:**
- **Babolat RPM Blast (polyester):** Excellent topspin and durability. Rough on the arm for full swings. Tension: 48–52 lbs.
- **Wilson NXT 16 (multifilament):** Most comfortable string — similar feel to natural gut at a fraction of the cost. Tension: 52–57 lbs.
- **Hybrid (main poly + cross multi):** Best of both worlds — RPM Blast mains + NXT crosses.

**How often to restring:** Restring as many times per year as you play per week. Play twice a week = 2 times per year.

## Grip Size

Incorrect grip size leads to arm problems over time.

**Measure:** Middle knuckle to ring finger tip. Standard adult measurement falls 4 1/8" to 4 5/8".

**Tip:** If you're between sizes, go smaller and add an overgrip (adds ~1/16"). Never go larger than your measured size.
