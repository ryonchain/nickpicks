## What Makes a Good Cornhole Set

**Board dimensions:** Regulation ACA (American Cornhole Association) boards are 2' × 4' with the hole centered 9" from the top edge. For casual play, any size works. For competitive play, regulation dimensions matter.

**Board material:** Solid wood (plywood) is regulation standard. Hollow boards are cheaper and lighter but flex on uneven ground and feel "dead" for bag throws.

**Legs and height:** Regulation height is 12" at the front hole end. Quality sets have foldable legs that fold flat for storage.

**Bag fill:** Regulation bags use #2 corn kernels, but modern tournament bags use resin fill for consistent performance in all weather. For backyard use, corn-fill bags are fine. Serious players upgrade to fabric/resin bags.

**Board finish:** Painted wood is fine. Laminate-topped boards resist moisture and scuffing better than paint.

## Cornhole Rules Quick Reference

**Setup:** Boards 27 feet apart (front edge to front edge). Teams of 2 standing behind their board.

**Scoring:** Hole = 3 points. On-board bag = 1 point. Cancellation scoring (team A's points cancel team B's, net score counts).

**Win:** First team to reach or exceed 21 points wins.

**Cancellation scoring example:** Team A scores 7, Team B scores 5 → net result: Team A gets 2 points.
