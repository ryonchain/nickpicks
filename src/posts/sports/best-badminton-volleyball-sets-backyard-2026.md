## Combo Sets vs. Dedicated Sets

**Combo sets** include poles and a net that converts between badminton (5 feet high, shorter court) and volleyball (7'11-5/8" high per FIVB regulation, longer court). Great for families who want both sports without buying two separate setups. Price: $40–$120.

**Dedicated badminton sets** offer better net quality and a more stable badminton-specific setup. Better for players who primarily want to play badminton seriously. Price: $50–$150.

**Dedicated volleyball sets** use heavier poles, tighter nets, and regulation height adjustability. Better for players who want authentic volleyball play. Price: $80–$300+.

For most families, start with a combo set. If you develop a preference, upgrade to a dedicated setup later.

## Shuttlecocks: Nylon vs. Feather

**Nylon (plastic) shuttlecocks:** More durable, consistent flight, unaffected by humidity. Best for outdoor play. Yonex Mavis 300/350 are the standard.

**Feather shuttlecocks:** Used in professional badminton. Fly faster, feel more authentic, wear out in 1–3 games. Not practical for outdoor casual play. Best for gymnasium play.
