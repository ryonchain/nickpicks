## What Type of Fishing Are You Doing?

**Freshwater spinning (most beginners):** Bass, trout, walleye, catfish, panfish. Spinning combos are the easiest to use — open-face reel with a bail arm, simple cast-and-retrieve.

**Ultralight spinning:** Crappie, bluegill, small trout. Lighter rods and smaller reels for smaller lures and lines.

**Saltwater spinning:** Redfish, snook, flounder, striper. Requires corrosion-resistant materials and heavier setups.

**Baitcasting:** Advanced setup for bass fishing. Not recommended for beginners — prone to backlash until you develop feel.

For 90% of beginners, start with a medium-light or medium spinning combo.

## Fishing License

You need a fishing license in every US state. Buy yours online at your state's fish and wildlife agency website. Most states offer:
- Annual license: $20–$40
- 1-day license: $10–$15 (great for trips)
- Combo license (fishing + hunting): $40–$60

Never skip the license — fines range from $50 to $500+.
