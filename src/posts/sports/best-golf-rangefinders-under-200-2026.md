## Laser vs. GPS Rangefinders

**Laser rangefinders** measure the exact distance to whatever you point at — the flagstick, a hazard, a tree. Accuracy is ±1 yard with no subscription required. Best for shot-by-shot decision making.

**GPS rangefinders** (watches or handheld units) show distances to the front, center, and back of greens automatically without pointing at a target. Pre-loaded with 40,000+ courses. Best for pace of play and seeing full course layouts.

Most serious golfers prefer laser. GPS is great for casual rounds and walkers who don't want to stop and aim.

## Which One Should You Buy?

**Get a laser if:**
- You want ±1 yard accuracy on every shot
- You play varied courses and need flexible targeting
- You care about accurate flag distances (not just center of green)
- You're playing competitively or in handicap rounds

**Get a GPS if:**
- You want a device that works automatically without aiming
- You care about course management and hazard distances
- You prefer wearing a watch to carrying a device
- Pace of play is more important than pin-point accuracy

**Our pick under $200:** The **Bushnell Tour V5** for laser accuracy and JOLT feedback, or the **Blue Tees Series 3 Max** for the best budget laser value at $130.
