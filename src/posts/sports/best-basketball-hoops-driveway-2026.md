## Portable vs. In-Ground: Which Is Right for You?

### Portable Hoops
- Base fills with water or sand for stability
- Can be moved or stored in garage
- Pole flexibility in wind: slight wobble under hard dunks or winds
- Price range: $200–$600
- Best for: Renters, families who move, buyers on a budget

### In-Ground Hoops
- Pole cemented into the ground (typically 18"–36" deep)
- Zero wobble under hard play
- Professional, permanent appearance
- Price range: $400–$2,000+
- Best for: Homeowners who want permanent court-quality play

### Garage-Mounted Hoops
- Bolt to the garage wall or overhang structure
- Depends on roof pitch and garage height
- Very stable, no pole needed
- Price range: $200–$600
- Best for: Tight driveways without room for a pole

## Key Specs to Evaluate

**Backboard size and material:**
- 44–48" polycarbonate: Portable/budget hoops. Clear, durable, adequate for recreational play.
- 54" tempered glass: Better rebound response. Common on $400+ hoops.
- 60"+ tempered glass: Pro-grade backboard. Best rebound response, most durable.

**Height adjustment:** Look for an infinite height adjuster vs. "5-setting" adjusters. Infinite allows setting any height from 7.5'–10' — important for kids who need to adjust as they grow.

**Overhang:** The horizontal distance from the front of the pole to the back of the backboard. More overhang = backboard further from the pole = more authentic court play without running into the pole.

## Choosing the Right Rim

**Standard rims:** Fixed steel ring. Fine for recreational play, no dunking.

**Break-away rims:** Spring-loaded hinged ring that flexes on dunks and returns to position. Required for serious play. Prevents backboard damage from hard dunks. Look for "break-away rim" on any hoop you'll use for athletics.

**Double break-away rims:** Two-position hinge system, even more flex. Used on pro and serious recreational courts.
