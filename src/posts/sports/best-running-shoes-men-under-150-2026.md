## How to Choose Running Shoes

**Gait and pronation:** Normal (neutral) pronation, overpronation (foot rolls inward), or underpronation/supination (foot rolls outward). If you're unsure, get a free gait analysis at a running specialty store.

**Stack height:** Higher stack (30+ mm) = more cushioning = better for long distances and heavier runners. Lower stack (20–24 mm) = more ground feel = better for speed work and lighter runners.

**Drop:** The heel-to-toe drop (difference in stack height from heel to forefoot). Traditional running shoes: 8–12mm drop. Minimalist: 0–4mm. Most runners do well with 8–10mm.

**Purpose:** Daily trainer (high mileage, cushioned), tempo/race shoe (lighter, responsive), trail shoe (lugged outsole, protective), or all-around shoe (versatile use).
