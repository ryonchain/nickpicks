## Hard Cases vs. Soft Bags vs. Backpacks

**Hard plastic cases:** Classic tackle boxes with removable trays. Waterproof, stackable, durable. Best for boat fishing where the box stays onboard. Less convenient for hiking/walking to spots.

**Soft tackle bags:** Backpack-style bags with integrated tackle trays. Best for shore fishing, hiking to remote spots, and urban anglers who need to carry everything on their back.

**Vertical tackle organizers:** Compact, standalone cases that stand upright with lure-organization compartments. Best for single-species anglers with a focused lure selection.

**Tray-style tackle storage:** Flat, multiple-tray boxes that stack and interlock. Best for specific tackle categories (jigs, crankbaits, soft plastics).

## Tackle Organization Tips

1. **Organize by lure type, not by color:** Keep all crankbaits in one section, all soft plastics in another. Color can be secondary within each type.

2. **Separate sharps from softs:** Hooks and hard baits on one side, soft plastic worms and grubs on the other. Hooks damage soft plastics.

3. **Label each compartment:** Painters tape and a permanent marker. You'll thank yourself after a year.

4. **VCI rust prevention:** Add a VCI (vapor corrosion inhibitor) strip to boxes with hooks and metal lures. Prevents rust from humidity.
