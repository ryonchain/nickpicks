## Buying Guide: Converter vs. Full Standing Desk

### When a Converter Makes Sense
- You already have a desk you love (L-shape, solid wood, built-in storage)
- You want to test sit-stand ergonomics before a big investment
- Budget under $200
- Renting (can take converter with you, can't take a built-in desk)

### When a Full Standing Desk Makes More Sense
- Starting with a blank home office setup
- Converter's added height clears your monitor too high (common with low-sitting desk chairs)
- You want cable management integrated into the desk
- You want a cleaner look (converters are visually bulky)

### Key Measurements Before Buying
1. **Desk width:** Your converter must fit within your desk width
2. **Current desk height:** Add converter minimum height — this is your sitting height. Ensure keyboard is at elbow height when sitting.
3. **Maximum standing height needed:** Converter max height + desk height should equal your standing elbow height (~40–48 inches from floor for most adults)
4. **Weight of your setup:** Include monitors, laptop, accessories vs. converter weight capacity

### Ergonomic Heights
- **Monitor top:** At or slightly below eye level
- **Keyboard:** At sitting or standing elbow height (90° arm bend)
- **Common issue:** Converters raise monitors too high for standing use in low-desk + tall-chair setups

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are standing desk converters worth it?**
Research is mixed on sit-stand desks specifically reducing back pain, but users consistently report less fatigue during long work days. The key is alternating — 20–30 minutes standing per hour, not standing all day. If a converter removes friction from the sit-to-stand transition (especially electric models), you actually use it more.

**How heavy can a monitor be on a converter?**
Most converters handle 30–35 lbs. A 27-inch monitor weighs 7–14 lbs; dual monitors = 14–28 lbs. Plus keyboard and accessories. Check specifications before buying — exceeding weight capacity causes instability and wear.

**Can I use a standing desk converter with a laptop?**
Yes — most converters include laptop-compatible platforms. Pair with an external keyboard/mouse so your screen height and keyboard position can be optimized independently. Using a laptop screen only (bent neck looking down) eliminates the ergonomic benefit.

**How tall do I need to be for a standing desk converter?**
Most adults 5'2"–6'4" can find an ergonomic standing position with standard converters (4–20 inch height range). Taller users (6'3"+) may need to check maximum height specifications carefully. Very short users (under 5'2") may find minimum height still too high.

**Do I need a mat with a standing desk converter?**
Anti-fatigue mats significantly reduce fatigue during standing work. Studies show 30–45 minutes of standing causes measurable leg and lower back fatigue on hard floors. A $20–40 mat extends comfortable standing time significantly.
