## Buying Guide: How to Use Packing Cubes

### The Basic System
Pack one category per cube:
- **Large cube:** Tops / shirts
- **Medium cube:** Bottoms / pants / shorts
- **Small cube:** Underwear / socks
- **Separate cube or pouch:** Workout clothes
- **Bag:** Shoes

### Do I Need Compression Cubes?
Compression cubes are worth it if:
- You frequently overpay for checked bags
- You struggle to fit a week's clothes in a carry-on
- You're packing bulky items (fleeces, jeans, thick fabrics)

They're less valuable if you're a light packer or travel with mostly business/formal clothing (delicate fabrics don't compress as well).

### Size Guide
- **Small cubes (10"x7"):** Underwear, socks, accessories
- **Medium cubes (13"x10"):** 3–5 shirts or 2 pairs pants
- **Large cubes (17"x12"):** Full clothing category, heavy items
- **Carry-on trip:** 1 large + 1 medium + 1–2 small
- **Week-long checked bag:** 2 large + 2 medium + 2 small

### Weight Considerations
Frequent travelers count grams. Eagle Creek nylon is heavier but more durable. Ultralight alternatives (Sea to Summit Traveling Light) save 50–100g per cube if weight is critical.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do packing cubes actually help you fit more clothes?**
Standard cubes don't add space — they reorganize it, making every inch more accessible. Compression cubes do reduce volume. The real benefit is organization: you access clothes faster, unpack/repack in minutes, and avoid a chaotic exploded suitcase.

**How many packing cubes do I need?**
For a 3–5 day trip with a carry-on: 3–4 cubes. For a week+ with checked luggage: 6–8 cubes. The 6-piece BAGAIL set and 7-piece Gonex set handle most travelers' needs.

**Can I use packing cubes as organizers at home?**
Yes — many people use packing cubes to organize dresser drawers or closet shelves. The same category system works at home. Eagle Creek cubes are durable enough for daily use.

**Are packing cubes TSA-friendly?**
Yes. TSA can open cubes during screening if needed. They don't slow down security. Note: TSA can inspect compression cubes if the compressed shape looks unusual on the X-ray.

**What's the difference between packing cubes and compression bags?**
Packing cubes are structured cubes you zip; they organize, some compress. Compression bags (like Space Bags) remove air via a valve and compress dramatically more — but everything comes out wrinkled. Packing cubes keep clothes more organized and less wrinkled.
