Safe sleep is non-negotiable, and the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) is clear on the rules: no loose blankets in the crib until age 12+ months. Sleep sacks — wearable blankets — solve this problem while keeping babies warm and cozy. Swaddles serve a different function: they wrap newborns snugly to simulate the feeling of the womb, reducing the Moro (startle) reflex that wakes sleeping babies.

The distinction matters for shopping:
- **Newborn to ~4 months:** Swaddles (arms-in wrapping, either traditional blanket or Velcro/zip swaddle)
- **~4 months to 2+ years:** Sleep sacks (arms-free wearable blankets, some with weighted panels)

## Safe Sleep Standards: What You Need to Know

The AAP's safe sleep guidelines, updated in 2022, apply directly to sleep sacks and swaddles:

**For swaddles:**
- Always place swaddled babies on their back
- Stop swaddling as soon as baby shows signs of rolling (typically 3–4 months)
- Do not add padding or loose material on top of a swaddle
- Swaddles should be snug at the chest and torso but loose at the hips and legs

**For sleep sacks:**
- Choose the correct TOG (thermal overall grade) for your nursery temperature: 0.5 TOG for warm rooms (75°F+), 1.0 TOG for moderate (68–72°F), 2.5 TOG for cooler rooms (below 68°F)
- Sleep sacks should not have hoods or add-on accessories that could cover baby's face
- Use AAP-recommended sizes — a too-large sleep sack can shift over the face
