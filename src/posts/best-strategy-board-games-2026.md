## Beyond Catan: What Makes a Good Strategy Game?

Catan and Ticket to Ride are "gateway games" — they introduce mechanics that heavier strategy games build on. Once you're comfortable with resource collection and route-building, you're ready for games with:

- **More decision points per turn** — you'll have multiple paths, not just one obvious move
- **Longer planning horizons** — good moves now set up powerful plays three turns out
- **Asymmetric mechanics** — players may have different starting powers or strategies
- **Less randomness** — skilled play consistently outperforms lucky dice rolls

Wingspan is a competitive card game about bird enthusiasts building aviaries. Each bird card has a power that triggers when you play in its habitat — forest birds generate food, grassland birds lay eggs, wetland birds draw more cards. The engine builds on itself over four rounds.

### 2. Terraforming Mars — Best Long-Form Strategy
**~$60** | [BUY ON AMAZON]

Terraforming Mars puts players in the role of competing corporations working to raise Mars's temperature, oxygen level, and ocean coverage. Cards represent projects — cities, forests, animals, industry — and every card has prerequisites, costs, and both immediate and ongoing effects.

**Why We Recommend It:** The depth is extraordinary. With 200+ project cards, no two games follow the same arc, and card synergies reward careful study. Corporations have unique starting bonuses that push you toward different strategies. At 120–180 minutes it's a commitment, but a satisfying one.

- Play time: 120–180 min

- Best for: Players ready for long-haul strategy

### 3. Azul — Best Short Strategy Game
**~$35** | [BUY ON AMAZON]

Azul is a tile-drafting and pattern-building game. Players take turns drafting colored tiles from a shared pool to complete rows on their personal player board, then score points for the pattern they've built. Simple rules; deep tactics.

**Why We Recommend It:** Azul is the best proof that a strategy game doesn't need complexity to be compelling. Every tile you take denies your opponent something, and the penalty system for over-drafting creates real tension. Games run 30–45 minutes and work perfectly at two, three, or four players.

- Players: 2–4
- Play time: 30–45 min
- Complexity: Low-medium
- Best for: Short game nights, two-player sessions

Scythe is set in an alternate-history 1920s Europe where steam-powered mechs compete for territory and resources. Players control asymmetric factions with different mech abilities, starting positions, and player mats that unlock different action combinations.

### 5. Dominion — Best Deck-Building Game
**~$45** | [BUY ON AMAZON]

Dominion invented the deck-building genre. Players start with identical 10-card decks and use actions to buy kingdom cards that add new capabilities — draw more cards, gain more money, attack opponents, chain actions. The best deck wins.

**Why We Recommend It:** Dominion is the most replayable game on this list. Each game uses only 10 of 25+ available kingdom card types (hundreds more in expansions), meaning every game requires a fresh strategic read. A full game runs 30 minutes. The depth comes from recognizing which card combinations synergize, not from memorization.

- Players: 2–4
- Play time: 30 min
- Complexity: Medium
- Best for: Card game fans, short strategy sessions

### 6. 7 Wonders — Best for Larger Groups
**~$50** | [BUY ON AMAZON]

7 Wonders is a card-drafting civilization game where players develop ancient wonders across three ages. Each age, you draft a hand of cards, play one, and pass the rest. Build military strength, commerce, science, or construction to score points across multiple categories.

**Why We Recommend It:** 7 Wonders scales beautifully from 2–7 players without increasing play time — everyone drafts simultaneously, so there's no downtime regardless of group size. This makes it one of the best games for larger gatherings where turn-order wait time would otherwise drag.

- Players: 2–7
- Play time: 30–45 min
- Complexity: Medium
- Best for: Larger groups, fans of simultaneous play
