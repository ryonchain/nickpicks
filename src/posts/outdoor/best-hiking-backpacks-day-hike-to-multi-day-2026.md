## H2: How to Choose a Hiking Backpack by Trip Length

Pack capacity is measured in liters. The right size depends on how long you'll be out and how much you carry.

| Trip Type | Pack Size | What Fits |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Half-day hike | 10–20L | Water, snacks, first aid, layers |
| Full-day hike | 20–35L | Above + lunch, extra layers, rain gear |
| Overnight | 35–50L | Full sleeping kit, 1 night of food |
| 2–4 night trips | 50–60L | Full backpacking kit with food |
| 5+ night expeditions | 60–80L | Extended supplies, winter gear |

**Fit matters more than brand.** A pack that doesn't fit your torso length and hip width will cause shoulder and back pain no matter the quality. Measure your torso length (C7 vertebra to iliac crest) before buying.

## H2: Pack Features That Matter

**Hip belt** — should carry 80% of pack weight. A padded, fitted hip belt is essential for packs over 25L. Look for anatomically shaped belts that fit snugly on the iliac crest.

**Back panel ventilation** — mesh or suspended panel designs allow air circulation and prevent sweaty back. Critical for summer hiking.

**Hydration compatibility** — most packs 20L+ include a sleeve and exit port for a hydration reservoir (sold separately). Osprey HydraPouch and Platypus are compatible with most brands.

**Lid pocket or brain** — the top lid pocket provides quick access to sunscreen, trail snacks, and headlamp without digging into the main compartment.

**Trekking pole attachment** — horizontal and vertical attachment loops let you stow poles when scrambling hands-free terrain.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I fit a hiking backpack?**
A: Measure your torso from C7 vertebra (prominent bone at the base of your neck) to your iliac crest (top of hip bones). Most packs come in S/M/L sizes corresponding to torso length. Adjust the hip belt to sit on the iliac crest, then adjust shoulder straps and load lifter straps.

**Q: Should I buy a frameless or framed pack?**
A: Frameless packs under 20L are fine for day hikes. Once you carry sleeping gear, stove, and 3+ days of food (30+ lbs), you want a framed pack with a proper hip belt. Internal frame packs are the standard for backpacking.

**Q: What's the best backpack for thru-hiking?**
A: Thru-hikers (PCT, AT, CDT) tend to use ultralight options: Gossamer Gear Mariposa (26 oz, 60L), ULA Circuit (34 oz, 68L), or Hyperlite Mountain Gear 3400 Ice (26 oz, 55L). These aren't beginner packs but handle thousands of miles.
