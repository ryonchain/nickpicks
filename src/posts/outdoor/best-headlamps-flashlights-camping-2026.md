## H2: Headlamp vs. Flashlight — Which Should You Buy?

**Headlamps** are hands-free. You wear them on your head or helmet, and the light goes where you look. They're essential for cooking at camp after dark, setting up a tent at night, hiking at dawn or dusk, and any task requiring both hands.

**Flashlights** project a longer, more concentrated beam — better for scanning terrain, signaling, and reaching distances. They're better for camp perimeters and searching.

**Recommendation:** Every camper needs a headlamp. A flashlight is a nice-to-have second light for camp use. Buy the headlamp first.

## H2: Headlamp Features That Matter

**Lumens** — how bright the light is. 100–200 lumens is fine for camp tasks. 300–500 lumens handles trail hiking in complete darkness. 1,000+ lumens is for technical terrain or emergencies.

**Beam distance** — how far the light reaches in meters. Most headlamps list this on the packaging.

**Battery life** — always check battery life at the mode you'll actually use (medium or low, not maximum). Maximum mode often lasts only 1–2 hours.

**Water resistance** — IPX4 (splash-resistant) is minimum for camping. IPX7 or IPX8 (submersible) for wet conditions.

**Red light mode** — preserves night vision. Essential for astronomy and sleeping in a shared tent without waking others.

**Weight** — matters for backpacking. Under 3 oz for the headlamp plus batteries.

## FAQ

**Q: How many lumens do I need for camping?**
A: 150–300 lumens handles most camp tasks and hiking after dark. The high-lumen settings (400+) are for emergencies and technical terrain. Higher isn't always better — bright lights kill your dark adaptation quickly.

**Q: Rechargeable or battery-powered headlamp?**
A: Rechargeable is more convenient and economical for frequent campers. Battery-powered provides backup security — you can always buy batteries in a small town. Many premium headlamps (like Petzl Actik Core) accept both, giving you the best of both worlds.

**Q: How long should a headlamp last for camping?**
A: On medium mode, most quality headlamps last 10–50 hours per charge or set of batteries. A single 3-night camping trip uses maybe 10 hours of light. Bring a spare set of batteries regardless.
