## H2: Water Filtration vs. Purification — What's the Difference?

**Filtration** removes protozoa (Giardia, Cryptosporidium) and bacteria through a physical membrane. Most hollow fiber filters achieve this with 0.1–0.2 micron pores. Filtration does NOT remove viruses.

**Purification** eliminates protozoa, bacteria, AND viruses. Methods include UV light (SteriPen), chemical treatment (iodine, chlorine dioxide), and hollow fiber combined with activated carbon or additional chemical stages.

**North America and Europe:** Protozoa and bacteria are the main risks. A good hollow fiber filter is sufficient for most backcountry water in the US, Canada, and Western Europe.

**International travel:** Viruses are a significant risk in many regions. Use purification — not just filtration — when traveling internationally or in areas with heavy livestock grazing upstream.

## FAQ

**Q: Does boiling water purify it?**
A: Yes. Boiling for 1 minute (3 minutes above 6,500 feet elevation) kills all pathogens including viruses. The problem is fuel cost and cooling time. Filtration is faster and easier for day-to-day water collection.

**Q: How long do hollow fiber filters last?**
A: The Sawyer Squeeze is rated to 100,000 gallons. The Katadyn BeFree filter cartridge lasts 1,000 liters before replacement. In practice, most backpackers never replace their filter — just backflush it properly after each trip.

**Q: Is backcountry water in the US safe without filtering?**
A: No. Even remote, pristine-looking mountain streams can carry Giardia from wildlife. Always filter or treat water from natural sources. The symptoms of Giardia infection are severe and can last weeks.
