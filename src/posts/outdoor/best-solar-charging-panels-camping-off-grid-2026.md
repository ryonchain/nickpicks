## H2: Solar Charging Tips for Camping

**Orient panels perpendicular to the sun.** Solar output drops dramatically when panels aren't facing directly at the sun. Adjust angle throughout the day or use a panel with an integrated kickstand.

**Start charging early.** Solar output peaks around solar noon. Begin charging by 9 AM to make full use of peak hours.

**Keep panels clean.** Dirt, bird droppings, and dust reduce output. Wipe panels with a damp cloth before charging sessions.

**Charge a battery bank, not devices directly.** Direct solar charging is inconsistent — clouds cause voltage fluctuations that can slow or stop charging. Charging a battery bank first, then charging devices from the bank, is more reliable.

**Partial shade kills output.** Even a small shadow across one panel section can reduce total output by 50–80% due to how solar cells are wired. Find clear, unobstructed sun exposure.

## FAQ

**Q: Can solar panels charge a phone directly?**
A: Yes, but it works best in consistent direct sunlight. Clouds cause output fluctuations that can slow charging. For more reliable charging, use the solar panel to charge a power bank, then charge your phone from the power bank.

**Q: How many watts do I need to charge camping devices?**
A: A phone needs 10–20W. A tablet needs 15–25W. A laptop needs 45–65W. A CPAP machine needs 40–90W (varies by pressure setting). A 21–28W panel handles phone and small device charging. A 60–100W+ panel handles larger devices.

**Q: Do solar panels work on cloudy days?**
A: Yes, but output is reduced 50–90% compared to direct sunlight. You can still trickle-charge a power bank on overcast days, but don't plan on rapid charging. In the Pacific Northwest or on rainy trips, carry a pre-charged power bank as backup.
