## Body Wash vs. Bar Soap: Which Is Better?

Both work. Here's the practical difference:

**Bar soap:** Higher pH (9–10) than skin's natural pH (5.5). Cleansing agents (surfactants) are efficient but can be harsher. Better for heavy dirt or environmental exposure. More environmentally friendly packaging.

**Body wash:** Can be pH-balanced to be gentler. Allows for added moisturizing ingredients (glycerin, shea butter, hyaluronic acid). Easier to apply evenly. Most men's body washes are formulated to leave skin softer post-shower.

**For daily use:** Body wash with moisturizing ingredients is better for skin health, particularly if you shower frequently or have dry skin.

**For gym or work-heavy environments:** Either works — bar soap may be more practical for travel and locker rooms.

## Showering Tips for Better Skin

- **Water temperature:** Lukewarm, not hot. Hot water strips natural oils from skin more aggressively.
- **Pat dry, don't rub:** Rubbing removes moisture. Pat towel gently and apply moisturizer while skin is still slightly damp.
- **Frequency:** Daily showering is fine, but twice daily (if post-gym) should prompt using a gentler, more moisturizing formula.
- **Apply moisturizer post-shower:** Body wash deposits moisture during the shower; a body lotion or oil seals it in.
