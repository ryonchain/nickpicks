## Desk Size: How to Choose the Right Footprint

The biggest mistake when buying a gaming desk is choosing one that's too small. Here's the guide:

### Single Monitor Setup
- **Minimum:** 47" (120cm) wide × 24" (60cm) deep
- **Recommended:** 55" (140cm) × 28" (70cm) — room for keyboard, mouse, peripherals, and a drink without crowding

### Dual Monitor Setup
- **Minimum:** 60" (152cm) wide
- **Recommended:** 72"+ (183cm) or an L-shaped desk

### Triple Monitor / Ultrawide Setup
- **L-shaped desk** or 72"+ with center seating position

### Depth Matters Too
Standard desks are 24" deep. Gaming monitors work best at 24–30" viewing distance. A 24" deep desk with a 27" monitor means the monitor is at the very back edge. If you want your monitor further back (better for larger screens), prioritize desks that are 28–32" deep.

## Cable Management: What Good Looks Like

Cable chaos under a gaming desk is the #1 regret of gamers who buy cheap desks. Look for these features:

- **Grommets or cable pass-through holes:** Allow cables to route from the desktop surface down through the desk rather than hanging off the edge.
- **Cable management raceway or tray:** A mounted tray underneath the desk that holds a power strip and bundles cables together. Some desks include these; others require aftermarket additions (~$20–30).
- **Magnetic cable routing:** Premium feature on desks like the Secretlab Magnus. Cables snap into magnetic routing channels along the desk frame.
- **Wire clips or adhesive cable holders:** The budget alternative — velcro cable ties, adhesive cable clips, and a mounted power strip solve most cable problems for under $20.

## Stability: What Causes Wobble and How to Avoid It

Desk wobble during gaming is frustrating. The main factors:

- **Frame material:** Steel > aluminum > MDF or particleboard. Look for desks with steel frame legs.
- **Cross bracing:** An X-brace or H-brace under the desk significantly reduces side-to-side flex.
- **Grommet bolt tightness:** Most wobble in cheap desks comes from loosely assembled leg bolts. Tighten every bolt firmly and re-check after 2 weeks of use.
- **Standing desk stability:** Electric standing desks wobble more than fixed desks at height. Dual-motor columns and cross-bracing minimize this. Single-motor desks wobble more.
