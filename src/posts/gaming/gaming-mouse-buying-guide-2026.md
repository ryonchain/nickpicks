## DPI: What It Actually Means for Gaming

DPI (dots per inch) measures how far your cursor moves per inch of mouse movement. Despite marketing that emphasizes high DPI, most professional gamers use 400–1600 DPI. Here's why:

- **High DPI (3200+):** Cursor moves faster per inch. Useful for large screens at close range. Can feel floaty and imprecise for fine aim.
- **Low DPI (400–800):** More physical movement required per cursor movement. Offers more control for precision aiming in FPS titles.
- **The real metric is effective sensitivity:** DPI × in-game sensitivity multiplier. Two setups with the same effective sensitivity feel the same — DPI alone is misleading.
- **What to look for:** Any modern gaming mouse has more than enough DPI ceiling. The sensor quality matters more than the maximum DPI number.

## Wireless vs. Wired: The 2026 Reality

The wireless vs. wired debate has been largely settled. Top-tier wireless gaming mice now match wired in latency:

- **Wireless latency (top tier):** Logitech LIGHTSPEED and Razer HyperSpeed operate at 1ms or less — identical to wired in competitive conditions.
- **Wireless latency (budget tier):** Cheap wireless mice use Bluetooth or lower-quality 2.4GHz receivers with 8–16ms latency. This is the gap worth avoiding.
- **Battery life:** Most premium wireless gaming mice last 50–80 hours. Charging via USB-C while playing is possible on most models.
- **Cable drag (wired):** Heavy rubber cables create noticeable drag during play. Paracord cables (ultra-light braided) minimize this. Some players prefer the zero-drag experience of wireless.
- **Recommendation:** If budget allows ($100+), wireless is now the better choice. Under $100, wired offers better value per performance.

## Weight and Mouse Shape

**Weight:**
- Ultra-light (<70g): Favored by competitive players for arm-aim styles. Less fatigue over long sessions.
- Standard weight (80–100g): Balanced control. Fine for most gamers.
- Heavy (100g+): More stable for slow, deliberate wrist movements. Less popular in competitive contexts.

**Shape:**
- **Symmetrical:** Works for both right and left-handed users. Neutral grip support.
- **Ergonomic right-handed:** Sculpted for right-hand use with natural thumb rest. Better comfort for long sessions.
- **Claw grip:** Arched shape supports fingertip and claw grip styles.
- **Palm grip:** Longer body with more back surface area for the palm to rest.

## Polling Rate: 1000Hz vs. 8000Hz

**1000Hz (standard):** Reports mouse position 1000 times per second (every 1ms). This is enough for the vast majority of gamers.

**8000Hz:** Reports 8000 times per second (every 0.125ms). The difference is perceptible in controlled tests, especially at very high in-game sensitivities. Whether it's meaningfully impactful in real games is disputed.

**Verdict:** 1000Hz is fine for most gamers. 8000Hz is a marginal edge for highly sensitive players. The sensor and weight matter more.
