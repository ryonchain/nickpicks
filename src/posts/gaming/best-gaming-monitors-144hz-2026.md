## Refresh Rate vs. Resolution: The Competitive Gaming Trade-Off

This is the most important decision when buying a gaming monitor for competitive play:

- **1080p at 144Hz+:** Lower resolution means your GPU can push higher frame rates. If you play CS2, Valorant, or Apex Legends competitively, 1080p at 240Hz+ makes more sense than 1440p at 144Hz.
- **1440p at 144–165Hz:** The balanced choice for most gamers. Sharper image for single-player and esports titles. Requires a mid-tier GPU (RX 6700 XT or RTX 3060 Ti minimum).
- **4K at 60Hz:** Not recommended for competitive gaming. Frame rate matters more than resolution for fast-paced titles.
- **Response time (GtG):** Look for 1ms or less. IPS panels have improved dramatically — modern IPS monitors achieve 1ms GtG consistently.

---

## Panel Types Explained

- **IPS (In-Plane Switching):** Best color accuracy and viewing angles. Modern IPS panels match TN in response time. Best all-around choice for most gamers.
- **TN (Twisted Nematic):** Historically fastest response time, but washed out colors and poor viewing angles. Increasingly irrelevant — IPS has caught up on speed.
- **VA (Vertical Alignment):** Best contrast ratio and deep blacks. Higher response time than IPS/TN. Good for single-player games; suboptimal for fast-paced esports.
- **OLED:** Best image quality available. True blacks, instant response. Burn-in risk with static HUD elements. Expensive, but increasingly relevant at the enthusiast tier.

## Size and Distance: What Works Best for Gaming

- **24":** Ideal for competitive gaming at close range (50–65cm from screen). Entire screen is in peripheral view, reducing eye/head movement.
- **27":** Best all-rounder. Comfortable at 60–80cm for both gaming and content creation.
- **32"+:** Better for cinematic single-player. Requires more head movement to track across the screen — disadvantage in fast-paced competitive titles.
