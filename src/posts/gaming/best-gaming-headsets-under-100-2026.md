## What Separates Good Budget Headsets from Bad Ones

At the sub-$100 tier, the gap between the best and worst headsets is enormous. Here's what to filter on:

- **Driver size:** 40mm+ drivers deliver better bass and spatial separation. Avoid sub-40mm drivers in this price range.
- **Microphone type:** Cardioid mics (directional) reject background noise better than omnidirectional. Retractable or detachable mics indicate the brand invested in mic quality.
- **Headband padding:** This is the failure point of cheap headsets. Look for padded leather or memory foam headbands.
- **Cable quality:** Braided cables resist tangling and last longer. Rubber cables kink and eventually fray.
- **Platform compatibility:** Most headsets under $100 use 3.5mm analog or USB. Check if the headset supports your platform natively without an adapter.

## Wired vs. Wireless: Why Under $100 Means Wired

Wireless gaming headsets at the $100 and below price point make too many compromises — battery life is short, codec quality degrades audio, and connection reliability suffers compared to wired. Save wireless for the $130+ budget. At under $100, wired (3.5mm or USB) is the better choice every time.

---

## Platform Compatibility Quick Guide

- **3.5mm:** Universal. Works on PC, PS5, Xbox, Switch, and mobile out of the box.
- **USB-A:** PC and some PS5. May need adapter for Xbox or Switch.
- **USB-C:** PC and PS5. Limited Xbox/Switch support depending on console revision.
- **USB + 3.5mm:** Some headsets (like the HyperX Cloud III) offer both — USB for PC virtual surround, 3.5mm for console.
