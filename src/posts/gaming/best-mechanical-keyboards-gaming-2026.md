## Switch Types for Gaming: What Actually Matters

Switch type is the most important decision when buying a mechanical gaming keyboard. Here's what separates them:

- **Linear switches (Red, Speed Silver):** Smooth keystroke, no tactile bump, light actuation force. Preferred by most competitive FPS players for rapid key presses.
- **Tactile switches (Brown, Clear):** Slight bump at the actuation point. Good for players who want feedback without audible click. Popular for mixed gaming/typing use.
- **Clicky switches (Blue, Green):** Audible click and tactile bump. Great feel, terrible for team environments. Not recommended for streaming or shared spaces.
- **Hall Effect (magnetic) switches:** No mechanical contact means no wear, no debounce delay. Adjustable actuation point is the competitive edge — set it to 0.1mm for faster response or 2mm to avoid accidental presses.
- **Optical switches:** Light-based actuation eliminates spring contact. Faster debounce than traditional mechanical switches, though the advantage is smaller than marketing suggests.

## Form Factor Guide

**Full size (100%):** Includes numpad. Best for productivity, worst for competitive gaming (pushes mouse too far right).

**Tenkeyless / TKL (80%):** No numpad. Industry standard for competitive gamers — better mouse positioning.

**75%:** TKL with the layout compressed (function keys moved to a column). Compact without losing navigation keys.

**65%:** No function row. Very compact, requires FN combos for some functions. Popular in the enthusiast community.

**60%:** No function row, no navigation cluster. Maximum portability. Steeper learning curve for non-enthusiasts.
