## What City Parents Actually Need in a Stroller

**Compact fold:** The single most important spec for city life. You need to fold the stroller one-handed while holding your baby, fit it through a subway turnstile, and store it under a restaurant table. Measure your apartment elevator and the strollers you're considering before buying.

**Weight:** City strollers get carried up stairs constantly. Under 20 lbs is the target. Every pound matters when you're carrying a 15-lb baby on one arm.

**Maneuverability:** All-wheel suspension and swiveling front wheels are essential for navigating crowds and tight corners. Test the turning radius — some strollers require wide, slow turns that frustrate in busy spaces.

**Canopy coverage:** Sun coverage matters for long walks and outdoor café seating. Look for an extendable canopy with UPF 50+ rating.

**Storage basket:** Larger is much better. You're often using the stroller as your grocery cart. Deep, easy-access baskets save daily frustration.

**Travel system compatibility:** Can you attach an infant car seat directly? Travel system compatibility (particularly with popular brands like Chicco, Graco, and UPPAbaby) adds newborn-through-toddler versatility.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I buy an umbrella stroller or a compact stroller?**
A: Umbrella strollers are the most portable but typically lack suspension, recline, and storage. A lightweight compact stroller (13–16 lbs) gives you most of the portability of an umbrella stroller with significantly better functionality. Only use an umbrella stroller as a secondary backup.

**Q: What's the minimum age for a city stroller without an infant insert?**
A: Most city strollers can be used from birth with a separately purchased infant insert or bassinet attachment. Without one, wait until your baby has full head control (typically 3–6 months). Travel system-compatible strollers let you use the infant car seat in the stroller from birth.

**Q: What weight is considered lightweight for a city stroller?**
A: Under 15 lbs is the practical threshold for a city stroller you'll carry up stairs and fold on public transit. Under 13 lbs feels genuinely effortless. Heavier strollers (18–22 lbs) are viable if you have elevator access and rarely need to carry the stroller.
