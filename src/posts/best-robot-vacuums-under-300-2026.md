## Buying Guide: What Matters in a Robot Vacuum

### Navigation Type
- **Random/Sensor:** Bounces around until it covers the floor. Cheaper. Less efficient.
- **Camera/Grid mapping:** Navigates methodically but can struggle in low light.
- **LiDAR mapping:** Uses laser-based ranging to map rooms precisely. Best results, typically $250+.

### Suction Power (Pa)
- **1000–1500Pa:** Adequate for hard floors and light carpet
- **1500–2000Pa:** Good for medium-pile carpet and pet hair
- **2000Pa+:** Handles embedded debris and thick carpet

### Self-Empty Base
Worth it if you have pets or run the vacuum daily. The base holds 30–60 days of debris and eliminates constant manual emptying — the main pain point of robot vacuums.

### Battery Life
Most mid-range models get 60–100 minutes per charge. Larger homes (2000+ sq ft) may need models that auto-recharge and resume.

### Compatibility
Most models support Alexa and Google Assistant. Check for Wi-Fi band compatibility — many budget models require 2.4GHz networks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do robot vacuums actually clean as well as regular vacuums?**
For maintenance cleaning — keeping floors free of dust, crumbs, and daily debris — yes. For deep cleaning embedded dirt in carpet or whole-home spring cleaning, a corded vacuum is still better. Most users run a robot vacuum daily and use a traditional vacuum monthly for deep cleans.

**Can robot vacuums handle rugs and carpet transitions?**
Most can handle area rugs under 0.8 inches thick. Higher pile rugs (shag, thick area rugs) can cause navigation issues. Most robots have anti-drop sensors so they won't fall off rug edges.

**How do I prevent my robot vacuum from getting stuck?**
Tuck away loose cables, lift floor-length curtains, and use virtual no-go zones in the app. Keep the charging dock accessible. First-run setup takes some trial and error.

**Are robot vacuums safe around pets?**
Most pets learn to ignore them quickly. Some dogs or cats are startled at first. Run the vacuum when pets are out of the room initially. Pet fur performance varies by model — look for self-cleaning brushrolls if you have long-haired animals.

**How often should I run a robot vacuum?**
Daily or every other day for homes with pets, kids, or high traffic. Weekly is sufficient for clean, low-traffic homes. Regular runs prevent debris buildup that forces the robot to work harder.
