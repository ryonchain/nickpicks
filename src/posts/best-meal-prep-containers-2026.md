## Buying Guide: Glass vs. Plastic

### Glass Containers
- **Pros:** No chemical leaching, no odor/stain absorption, oven-safe, lasts years, cleaner look
- **Cons:** Heavier, breakable, more expensive, takes cabinet space
- **Best for:** Regular home meal preppers, oven reheating, long-term use

### Plastic Containers (BPA-free)
- **Pros:** Lightweight, cheap, less breakage risk, good for work/travel
- **Cons:** Can absorb odors, degrades over time, not oven-safe, some off-gassing concern even BPA-free
- **Best for:** Budget meal prep, office lunches, high-volume disposable-ish use

### Size Guide for Meal Prep
- **7–14 oz:** Snacks, sides, sauces, small portions
- **24–32 oz:** Standard entree portions
- **48–64 oz:** Large batch storage, family portions
- **Key sizes to own:** 1 medium (28–32 oz) + 1 large (48 oz) + 2 small (14 oz) covers most scenarios

### Compartment Designs
- **Single compartment:** Most versatile, best for single dishes
- **2-compartment:** Entree + side in one container — good for work lunches
- **3-compartment:** Full meal (protein + carb + veg) — commonly seen in bento-style

### Lid Design Matters
- **Snap lock:** Secure, basic, easy to lose pieces
- **Clip lock (4-latch):** Most secure, slight inconvenience to open/close
- **Screw top:** Most airtight, slowest to use
- **Press-vent:** Microwave-friendly, good for liquids

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does meal prepped food last in the fridge?**
Most cooked proteins and grains: 4–5 days. Cooked vegetables: 3–5 days. The containers above are airtight enough to reach the upper end of these ranges. Label containers with a prep date.

**Can I put glass containers directly from freezer to oven?**
Tempered glass (Glasslock) needs to defrost in the fridge first before oven use — rapid temperature change can crack it. Borosilicate glass (OXO) handles freezer-to-oven transitions better. Always read the manufacturer instructions.

**Are plastic meal prep containers safe in the microwave?**
BPA-free polypropylene (look for recycling code #5) is considered safe for microwave use. Remove lids before microwaving to allow steam to escape. Avoid microwaving plastic in damaged or cloudy containers.

**How do I remove food odors from plastic containers?**
Fill with a mixture of baking soda and warm water, let sit overnight, then wash. For stubborn odors (fish, garlic), sun exposure for several hours often neutralizes the smell. If the odor persists after 3–4 washes, it's time to replace.

**Should I buy meal prep containers in sets or individually?**
Sets provide better value and consistent sizing for stacking. Individual purchases are better if you know exactly what sizes you need. Most meal preppers start with a set and add individual sizes as gaps emerge.
