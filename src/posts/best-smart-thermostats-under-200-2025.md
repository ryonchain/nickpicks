## Buying Guide: Smart Thermostat Compatibility

**C-wire.** Most smart thermostats prefer or require a C-wire (common wire) for continuous power. Check your existing thermostat wiring. If no C-wire, most manufacturers include an adapter kit or have a battery-powered option (Sensi, Amazon Smart Thermostat).

**HVAC compatibility.** Before buying, use the compatibility checker on the manufacturer's website (nest.com/compatibility, ecobee.com, or resideo.com for Honeywell). Heat pumps, multi-stage systems, and dual-fuel systems need specific thermostat support.

**System type.** Most smart thermostats support standard 1-heat/1-cool systems. The Honeywell T9 covers the most complex systems. If you have radiant heat, electric baseboard, or a high-voltage system, check compatibility carefully — most smart thermostats are low-voltage only.

**Remote sensors.** If rooms in your home vary significantly in temperature (upper floors hotter in summer, bedrooms colder in winter), a thermostat with remote room sensor support (Ecobee, Honeywell T9) can meaningfully improve comfort.

## FAQ

**How much does a smart thermostat save?**
The EPA estimates 8% savings on heating/cooling costs. In the average US home spending $1,000/year on HVAC, that's $80/year. Most smart thermostats pay for themselves within 2–3 years.

**Do smart thermostats work with radiant heat?**
Most smart thermostats on this list do NOT support high-voltage radiant systems (electric baseboard, hydronic). Nest, Ecobee, and Honeywell T9 have specific compatibility lists — check before buying.

**Can I install a smart thermostat myself?**
Yes — most installations take 30–45 minutes and require only a screwdriver. The process is: turn off power at the breaker, photograph existing wiring, disconnect old wires, connect to new thermostat labels, restore power. Most brands have step-by-step video guides.

**Which thermostat works best with Alexa?**
The Amazon Smart Thermostat is the most native Alexa experience. Ecobee has Alexa built-in (acts as an Alexa device itself). Nest supports Alexa via skill but Google Home is the primary ecosystem.
