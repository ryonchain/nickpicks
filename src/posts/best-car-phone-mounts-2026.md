## Buying Guide: Mount Type Comparison

### Windshield Mounts
Mount directly on the windshield with suction cup. **Pros:** Best field of view, works in any vehicle. **Cons:** Legally restricted in some states (California and others prohibit objects on windshield — place in lower corner). Suction cup can fail in extreme heat.

### Dashboard Mounts
Same suction cup, mounts on the dashboard pad. **Pros:** Legal in all states, lower obstruction. **Cons:** Height may be too low in some vehicles; requires dashboard surface for suction.

### Vent Mounts
Clip onto air vents. **Pros:** No suction required, compact, eye-level placement. **Cons:** Vent airflow can heat phone; limited height adjustment; some car vents incompatible with clips.

### CD Slot Mounts
Insert into the CD slot. **Pros:** Extremely stable, no adhesives. **Cons:** Requires CD slot (not in many newer vehicles); less common application.

### Magnetic vs. Cradle
- **Magnetic mounts:** Fastest on/off, requires metal plate or MagSafe case. Better for frequent use.
- **Cradle mounts:** No modification to case, wider phone compatibility, more secure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are car phone mounts legal?**
In most US states, mounting on the windshield is either legal or regulated (limited to specific corners). Dashboard mounting is legal everywhere. Vent mounting is legal everywhere. Check your state's laws — California, Texas, and New York have specific windshield obstruction rules.

**Do magnetic mounts damage phone internals?**
Modern smartphone components (processors, battery, storage) are not affected by the small magnets in car mounts. The concern that magnets damage phones is outdated — applies to magnetic storage media like floppy disks, not modern flash storage. Magnetic mounts can temporarily affect compass apps while on the mount.

**Do magnetic mounts affect wireless charging?**
MagSafe mounts (using Apple's designed system): no effect. Standard magnetic mounts with aftermarket metal plates: may reduce wireless charging efficiency from outside the mount. Remove the phone from the magnetic mount before wireless charging via external pad if you notice slow charging.

**How do I keep the suction cup from falling in hot weather?**
Clean the dashboard surface with isopropyl alcohol before application. Apply the suction cup while the car is at temperature (not cold), then lock the lever. High-temperature suction adhesive discs are also available as aftermarket upgrades for persistent failures.

**What's the safest placement for a phone mount in a car?**
Studies suggest: as low as possible while maintaining full map visibility, positioned to minimize eye movement from the road. The center of the dashboard at the base of the windshield is typically best — closest to your natural road-watching focal point.
