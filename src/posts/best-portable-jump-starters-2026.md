## Buying Guide: What to Know Before You Buy

### Peak Amps vs. Cranking Amps
Marketing often leads with "peak amps" — the instantaneous maximum burst. More important is "cold cranking amps" (CCA), which reflects real-world starting power at 0°F. A unit claiming 2000 peak amps may only deliver 500–600 real CCA. Established brands like NOCO publish both metrics honestly.

### Engine Size Compatibility
- **Gas engines up to 4L:** 400A+
- **Gas engines 4–6L:** 800–1000A
- **Gas/diesel trucks 6–8L:** 1500–2000A
- **Diesel trucks 8L+:** 2000–3000A

### Safety Features
Non-negotiable: spark-proof clamps and reverse polarity protection. Connected backward, a jump starter without protection can damage your car's electronics. Premium brands protect against reverse polarity, short circuit, overcharge, and over-discharge.

### Battery Type
Lithium-ion units (all picks above) are lighter, more compact, and work better in cold weather than lead-acid units. Cold temperatures reduce capacity — keep jump starters in the car cabin rather than the trunk in winter.

### Additional Features
USB charging ports turn your jump starter into an emergency power bank. Built-in LED flashlights are genuinely useful for nighttime roadside situations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I use a portable jump starter?**
1. Ensure both vehicles are off. 2. Connect red (positive) clamp to dead battery's + terminal. 3. Connect black (negative) clamp to bare metal (ground) in your engine bay — not to the battery's negative terminal. 4. Turn on the jump starter. 5. Start your vehicle. 6. Remove clamps in reverse order.

**How long should I wait before starting the car?**
With most units, connect the clamps and start immediately. Some manufacturers recommend 30–60 seconds with the jump starter connected before cranking for deeply discharged batteries.

**Can a jump starter damage my car?**
Without protection, yes. With spark-proof and reverse polarity protection (all picks above), no. Modern vehicle electronics are sensitive — never connect a cheap unprotected unit to a newer car.

**How long do portable jump starters hold a charge?**
Most lithium-ion units hold a charge for 6–12 months in storage. Check and recharge every 3–6 months. A flat unit won't help you at the roadside.

**Can I use a jump starter to charge my phone?**
Yes — units with USB ports work as standard battery banks. Most have 10,000–20,000mAh capacity, which charges a phone 3–5 times. Check the USB output wattage to ensure it supports fast charging for your device.
