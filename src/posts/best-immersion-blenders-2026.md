## Why Choose an Immersion Blender Over a Regular Blender?

- Blend soups and sauces **directly in the pot** (no transferring hot liquids)
- Easier to clean — most blending heads are dishwasher safe
- Store in a drawer rather than taking up counter space
- Better for small batches — single smoothies, salad dressings, compound butters
- More affordable than a quality countertop blender

Trade-offs: Less power than a countertop blender for thick or frozen mixtures. Ice crushing and nut butters are usually out.

## Buying Guide

### Motor Power: What You Need
- **200–300W:** Light soups, soft fruits, sauces. Fine for occasional use.
- **300–400W:** Most home cooking. Handles butternut squash, leafy greens, smooth nut butters.
- **500–625W:** Thick frozen smoothies, fibrous vegetables, whole-food blending.

### Speed Control
**Variable triggers** (like Braun's 21-speed) give you more control than **preset speeds** for tasks like gently blending a delicate sauce vs aggressively pureeing soup. For most users, 5 preset speeds is plenty.

### Attachments
The most useful additions:
- **Whisk:** Whip cream, beat eggs without pulling out a hand mixer
- **Chopper/mini food processor:** Mince onions, herbs, garlic
- **Beaker/tall cup:** Blend directly for smoothies or sauces

### Cleaning Ease
The most practical immersion blenders have **dishwasher-safe blending shafts** that detach with a twist or single button. Cleaning a non-detachable blade head by hand is workable, just less convenient.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can an immersion blender crush ice?**
Generally, no. Even powerful models (Vitamix, Mueller 500W) risk motor strain on hard ice. For frozen drinks, use a countertop blender. Immersion blenders handle partially frozen fruit fine.

**Is it safe to blend hot soups with an immersion blender?**
Yes, and it's one of the biggest advantages — you blend directly in the pot, eliminating the risk of hot liquid splashing out of a countertop blender. Keep the blending head fully submerged while running to minimize spattering.

**How do I prevent splashing when blending hot soup?**
Tilt the pot slightly and start on low speed. Keep the blending head fully below the soup surface. If the soup is thin, use a deep, narrow container rather than a wide pot.

**What's the difference between an immersion blender and a stick blender?**
They're the same thing — "immersion blender," "hand blender," and "stick blender" all refer to the same tool. The Braun marketing uses "MQ" (MultiQuick); KitchenAid uses "hand blender." Same category.

**Can I use an immersion blender for making smoothies?**
Yes, using the included tall beaker or a large cup. Works best with fresh or partially thawed fruit and yogurt. Thick frozen smoothies require a powerful motor (400W+) and may need some liquid added.
