## Video vs Audio: Which Do You Need?

**Audio monitors** transmit sound only. They're simpler, cheaper, and often more reliable — fewer components means fewer failure points. The trade-off: you can't see if your baby kicked off their blanket or rolled over.

**Video monitors** add a camera and display (or phone app). You get visual confirmation that your baby is safe, which reduces anxiety for most parents. The trade-off: more setup, higher cost, and potential connectivity issues with WiFi models.

**Our recommendation:** Go video unless you're on a very tight budget. Seeing your baby sleep peacefully is worth the extra cost.

---

## WiFi vs Dedicated Frequency: The Key Trade-Off

**Dedicated frequency (DECT/FHSS)** monitors use their own radio signal between the camera and a dedicated receiver. They don't depend on your home internet, so they work during outages and don't compete with streaming or smart home devices.

**WiFi monitors** stream through your home network to a phone app. You can check on your baby from anywhere — the office, the grocery store, another room. But they go dark when your internet drops, and some require monthly subscriptions.

**Our recommendation:** Choose dedicated frequency for sleep reliability; choose WiFi if remote access when away from home is a priority.
