## Why Pour-Over?

Pour-over is manual drip coffee — you control water temperature, pour speed, and bloom time yourself instead of relying on a machine to do it for you. The result, when done right, is coffee with exceptional clarity, brightness, and complexity that most drip machines can't match.

The learning curve is shallow — 15 minutes of reading and one or two practice brews is enough to pull a genuinely excellent cup. The equipment cost is low. The daily ritual (grinding, blooming, slowly pouring) becomes something many people look forward to rather than rush through.

**What you actually need:**
1. A dripper (V60, Chemex, Kalita Wave, or similar)
2. Filters (specific to your dripper)
3. A server or mug
4. A kettle — ideally gooseneck for control (separate purchase, ~$25–$60)
5. A burr grinder (separate purchase, the most important thing you can buy)
6. Fresh whole bean coffee

Most kits cover items 1–3. Budget separately for a gooseneck kettle and grinder.

## The Three Main Dripper Styles Compared

**Hario V60:** Cone shape with a large single hole and spiral ridges. Produces a bright, clean, complex cup. The most technique-sensitive of the three — pour consistency matters. Beloved by specialty coffee enthusiasts worldwide.

**Chemex:** Hourglass-shaped glass carafe with proprietary thick paper filters. The thick filter removes more oils and fine particles than other filters, producing an exceptionally clean, light, delicate cup. Also the most beautiful brewer — it lives on a counter.

**Kalita Wave:** Flat-bottom dripper with three small holes. More forgiving than V60 because the flat bed extracts more evenly and the three holes drain consistently regardless of pour angle. Good for beginners who want excellent results with less precision.

## Brewing Quick Reference

| Dripper | Water Temp | Coffee:Water | Bloom Time | Total Brew |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| V60 | 200°F (93°C) | 1:15 (e.g. 30g:450ml) | 30–45 sec | 2.5–3.5 min |
| Chemex | 200°F (93°C) | 1:15–1:17 | 30–45 sec | 4–5 min |
| Kalita Wave | 200°F (93°C) | 1:15 | 30–45 sec | 3–4 min |

Start with these ratios and adjust to taste. If the coffee tastes bitter, grind coarser or reduce steep time. If it tastes sour or weak, grind finer or bloom longer.
