## Retinol Basics: What You Need to Know

**Retinol vs. Retinoids:** Retinol is an over-the-counter form of vitamin A. Prescription tretinoin (retinoic acid) is stronger but requires a doctor's visit. Over-the-counter retinol converts to retinoic acid in the skin — it works, just more gradually.

**Concentration guide:**
- **0.025%:** Ideal for beginners or very sensitive skin. Expect results in 6+ months.
- **0.05%:** Mid-strength, good for most first-timers. Results appear in 3–4 months.
- **0.1%:** Moderate strength, good for those who've used retinol before. Faster results, more dryness possible.
- **0.3–0.5%:** Strong OTC. Experienced retinol users only. Noticeable results in 8–12 weeks.
- **0.5–1%:** Near-prescription strength. High irritation risk; build up slowly.

**The purge is real, temporary, and worth it.** Most people experience 2–6 weeks of increased breakouts and dryness when starting retinol. This is cell turnover accelerating, not an allergic reaction. Push through with a gentle routine and lower frequency.

**Use at night only.** Retinol breaks down in UV light and increases photosensitivity. Always apply at night and wear SPF every morning.

## How to Build a Retinol Routine

**Start slow:** Apply twice per week for the first two weeks, then every other night for two weeks, then daily if your skin tolerates it. This significantly reduces the purge period.

**Sandwich method for sensitive skin:** Apply moisturizer first, then retinol, then moisturizer again. The moisturizer buffer reduces direct irritation without eliminating efficacy.

**Don't mix with AHAs/BHAs on the same night.** Using retinol and exfoliating acids simultaneously increases irritation dramatically. Use retinol one night and acids another.

**Morning sunscreen is mandatory.** Retinol thins the outermost skin layer temporarily, increasing sun sensitivity. An SPF 30+ broad-spectrum sunscreen applied every morning is non-negotiable while using retinol.

## FAQ

**When will I see results from retinol?**
Fine line reduction typically appears in 12 weeks. Skin texture improves in 4–8 weeks. Hyperpigmentation takes 3–6 months. Patience matters more than strength.

**Can I use retinol if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?**
No. Retinoids are contraindicated during pregnancy. Consult your OB for alternatives — azelaic acid and bakuchiol are commonly recommended as safer options during pregnancy.

**Is there a retinol alternative for very sensitive skin?**
Bakuchiol is a plant-derived ingredient with retinol-like effects at a gentler profile. It won't match prescription retinoid results, but it's a legitimate alternative for people who can't tolerate any retinol formulation.

**Can I use retinol in summer?**
Yes, with consistent daily SPF. Retinol doesn't make you more sensitive to the sun than usual — it's always been photosensitizing. The rule is the same in all seasons: SPF in the morning, retinol at night.
