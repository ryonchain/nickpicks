# Best Niacinamide Serums for Pores & Skin Tone 2026

Niacinamide (vitamin B3) might be the most versatile active ingredient in skincare. It minimizes the appearance of pores, reduces hyperpigmentation, regulates sebum production, strengthens the skin barrier, and reduces inflammation — often simultaneously. Unlike retinol or vitamin C, it causes almost no irritation and works on virtually every skin type. If you're going to add one serum to your routine this year, niacinamide is the easiest recommendation.

---

## What Niacinamide Does (and What It Doesn't)

**What it does:**
- Reduces the appearance of enlarged pores by regulating sebum and increasing skin firmness
- Fades dark spots, acne marks, and uneven skin tone by inhibiting melanosome transfer
- Controls excess oil without over-drying
- Strengthens the skin barrier by stimulating ceramide production
- Reduces redness and inflammation
- Works well alongside almost every other skincare active

**What it doesn't do:**
- It won't permanently close pores (nothing does — pore size is primarily genetic)
- It's not a replacement for retinol for deep wrinkle reduction
- It won't address severe hyperpigmentation as quickly as AHAs, kojic acid, or prescription compounds

**Optimal concentration:** 5–10% niacinamide is the therapeutic range supported by clinical research. Under 5% is less effective. Over 10% can cause temporary flushing in some users, especially early in use. The 10% sweet spot is standard in most well-formulated serums.

## Niacinamide Stacking Guide

Niacinamide plays well with virtually every skincare active:

- **+ Hyaluronic acid:** use together — HA hydrates, niacinamide improves barrier function
- **+ Vitamin C:** formerly thought to interact negatively (producing a yellow flush compound). Current research suggests this isn't a significant concern at room temperature with modern stable formulations.
- **+ Retinol:** excellent pairing — niacinamide counteracts the dryness and redness retinol can cause
- **+ AHAs/BHAs:** both can be used, though apply separately (HA + niacinamide morning, acid exfoliant evening)
- **+ SPF:** niacinamide does not increase UV sensitivity; safe for morning use

## FAQ

**How long before I see pore improvements from niacinamide?**
Pore appearance typically improves in 4–8 weeks of consistent daily use. Full dark spot fading takes 8–12 weeks. Oil control can be noticed within 1–2 weeks.

**Should I use niacinamide morning or night?**
Both. Unlike retinol and vitamin C which have specific timing requirements, niacinamide is appropriate for any time of day. Most people apply it twice daily.

**Can niacinamide cause purging?**
Niacinamide does not cause skin purging (the initial breakout period associated with cell-turnover accelerators like retinol). If you break out after starting a niacinamide product, it's more likely a reaction to another ingredient in the formula.

**Is there a maximum percentage of niacinamide I should use?**
Studies show benefits at 5–10%. Above 10%, some users experience a temporary niacin flush (redness). 12% products exist and are generally well tolerated, but if you experience flushing, switch to a 10% formula.
