## Facial Oils vs. Moisturizers: The Key Difference

**Oils are occlusives and emollients** — they fill the gaps in the skin barrier and prevent transepidermal water loss. They do not contain humectants (water-attracting agents), so they don't pull moisture from the environment or deeper skin layers.

**The correct use:** Apply facial oil after serums and before or after moisturizer as the final sealing step. The moisturizer provides humectants; the oil seals them in.

**Oils don't hydrate skin** in the true sense — they create conditions that let your skin stay hydrated. Understanding this prevents disappointment. Facial oils are most powerful in combination with a hydrating serum or moisturizer, not as a standalone product.

**Comedogenic ratings:** Oils are rated 0–5 for pore-clogging tendency. For dry skin that's also prone to congestion, avoid anything above a 2. Coconut oil (comedogenic rating: 4) is famously problematic. Jojoba oil (0–2), squalane (0), and rosehip oil (1) are reliably non-comedogenic.

## How to Incorporate Facial Oil Correctly

**Option A (oil after moisturizer):** Apply serum → moisturizer → facial oil. The oil seals everything in. Best for very dry or cold-climate skin.

**Option B (oil before moisturizer for dry skin):** Apply serum → facial oil → moisturizer. The moisturizer provides an additional barrier. Best when you want to ensure your humectant moisturizer gets full absorption before sealing.

**Option C (mixed into moisturizer):** Add 2–3 drops of facial oil to your moisturizer in your palm and apply together. Convenient and effective for dry skin patches.

**How much:** 3–5 drops for the whole face is typically sufficient. More doesn't equal better absorption — oil that doesn't penetrate sits on the surface.

## FAQ

**Will facial oil make me break out?**
Depends entirely on the oil. Squalane and jojoba oil (comedogenic rating 0–2) don't clog pores for most skin types. Coconut oil, wheat germ oil, and many "rich" plant oils have high comedogenic ratings and can cause breakouts in acne-prone skin. Stick to the low-rated oils listed above.

**Can I use facial oil in the morning?**
Yes, but use minimally under makeup — excess oil causes makeup to slip. One drop pressed into skin 5 minutes before foundation is the maximum. Richer oil applications are better suited to nighttime.

**Do I need facial oil if I already use a moisturizer?**
No — facial oils are supplementary, not mandatory. Dry skin in cold climates or during dry winter months often benefits from the added occlusion. For most women with dry skin using a good ceramide-based cream, facial oil is optional.

**Is expensive facial oil significantly better than drugstore options?**
For pure, single-ingredient oils like squalane or jojoba: no. For formulated multi-ingredient oils with proprietary blends or clinical testing (like Kiehl's, Tatcha): sometimes yes, but often the premium is brand and experience rather than efficacy.
