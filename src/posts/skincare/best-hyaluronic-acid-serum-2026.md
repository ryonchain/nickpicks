When it works, the results are visible: skin looks plumper, fine lines appear softened, and everything else in your routine absorbs better. When it doesn't work — usually because of poor formulation or incorrect application — it can actually dehydrate skin by drawing moisture from deeper layers rather than the environment.

## How Hyaluronic Acid Actually Works

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring polysaccharide that holds up to 1,000 times its weight in water. It's a humectant — it attracts and binds water molecules. The key insight most people miss: HA draws water *from wherever there's moisture*. In a humid environment, it pulls water from the air into your skin. In a dry environment with no moisture locked above it, it can pull from deeper skin layers and worsen dehydration.

**The rule:** always apply HA serum to damp skin (right after cleansing or toning), and always seal with a moisturizer immediately after. The moisturizer locks the water in that the HA attracted.

**Molecular weight matters.** High-molecular-weight HA sits on the surface of the skin and creates a film that prevents water loss. Low-molecular-weight HA penetrates deeper and hydrates from within. The best serums use a combination of both for surface and deep hydration.

## How to Layer HA Serum in Your Routine

**Correct order:** Cleanse → Tone (optional) → HA Serum (on damp skin) → Moisturizer → SPF (morning)

**The damp-skin rule is not optional.** Apply HA serum while your face is still slightly wet from toning or cleansing. The water on your skin's surface is what the HA grabs onto. Dry-skin application can backfire in low-humidity environments.

**You don't need to wait for HA to absorb.** Unlike vitamin C or retinol, which should be absorbed for a few minutes before layering, HA serums can be followed immediately with moisturizer. The moisturizer seals in the hydration you just applied.

## FAQ

**Can hyaluronic acid serum replace my moisturizer?**
No. HA is a humectant — it attracts water but doesn't seal it in. Without a moisturizer on top, the water HA drew in will evaporate, potentially leaving skin more dehydrated than before application. HA serum + moisturizer is the necessary combination.

**Why does my HA serum make my skin tight or drier?**
You're applying it to completely dry skin in a low-humidity environment. The HA pulls moisture from the deeper layers of your skin instead of the air. Apply to damp skin and follow immediately with moisturizer.

**Is more hyaluronic acid concentration always better?**
Not necessarily. Concentration matters less than formulation — specifically whether multiple molecular weights are present and whether the surrounding formula helps HA function correctly. A 0.1% multi-weight HA in a well-formulated base can outperform a 2% single-weight formula.

**Can men use hyaluronic acid serum?**
Absolutely. HA is appropriate for all genders and skin types. The hydration and plumping benefits apply equally regardless of skin type.
