## What Actually Works Around the Eyes

**Dark circles:** The most common causes are thin skin revealing underlying blood vessels (genetic, treated with caffeine or vitamin K), hyperpigmentation from sun exposure or rubbing (treated with niacinamide, vitamin C), and hollowing due to volume loss (not treatable with topicals — this is filler territory). Know which type you have; most eye cream marketing overpromises on dark circles.

**Puffiness:** Caused by fluid retention, allergies, salt intake, or poor sleep. Caffeine temporarily constricts blood vessels and reduces swelling. Cold temperatures help. No topical ingredient permanently eliminates puffiness — it's a lifestyle and circulatory issue.

**Fine lines and crow's feet:** Dehydration lines respond to humectants (hyaluronic acid, glycerin) quickly and dramatically. True wrinkles respond to retinol, peptides, and time. The two look similar but require different approaches.

**Key ingredients:**
- **Caffeine:** reduces puffiness and dark circle appearance via vasoconstriction
- **Retinol (low concentration):** stimulates collagen, reduces crow's feet over time
- **Peptides:** signal collagen production, improve firmness
- **Hyaluronic acid + glycerin:** immediate hydration of fine lines
- **Niacinamide:** brightens hyperpigmentation-based dark circles
- **Vitamin C:** brightens and provides antioxidant protection

## Application Technique Matters

**Use your ring finger.** The ring finger naturally applies less pressure than the index or middle finger — important for the thin skin around the eyes, which can break down collagen over time from aggressive rubbing.

**Apply around the orbital bone, not on the eyelid.** Eye cream migrates up with warmth and blinking. Apply a rice-grain amount tapped lightly along the orbital bone (the bony socket below the eye) and it will reach the necessary areas on its own.

**Morning vs. night:** Use caffeine-based formulas in the morning for immediate depuffing. Use retinol or peptide formulas at night for repair. Hydrating formulas work both times.

## FAQ

**Can I just use my face moisturizer around my eyes?**
Often yes — especially if it's fragrance-free and non-irritating. The main reason to use an eye-specific product is that many face actives (strong vitamin C, high-concentration retinol, AHAs) can be too potent for the thin orbital skin.

**Do I need both a morning and evening eye cream?**
Not necessarily. One good eye cream used consistently morning and night covers most needs. If you want to optimize, use a caffeine-based formula in the morning and a retinol or peptide formula at night.

**Will eye cream eliminate dark circles?**
Topicals can meaningfully reduce the *appearance* of dark circles for many people — especially those caused by vascular visibility (caffeine helps) or hyperpigmentation (vitamin C, niacinamide help). Structural hollowing (genetic deep-set eyes) is not addressable with topicals; dermal fillers are the only effective treatment for that.

**Is it safe to use retinol around the eyes?**
Yes, if it's a product specifically formulated for the eye area at appropriate concentrations. Don't apply your full-strength face retinol directly under the eyes — the skin is too thin and the concentration is too high. Use a dedicated retinol eye product.
