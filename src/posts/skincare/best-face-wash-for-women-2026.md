## What Makes a Good Face Wash

**pH matters.** Your skin's natural pH is 4.5–5.5 (mildly acidic). Most bar soaps and harsh cleansers have a pH of 9–10, which disrupts the acid mantle and triggers dryness and irritation. Look for cleansers with a pH of 5–6.

**Avoid sulfates for dry or sensitive skin.** Sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS) is a harsh surfactant that creates the foamy lather most of us grew up with. It's effective at removing oil but also strips moisture and can cause long-term barrier damage. Gentler alternatives: sodium laureth sulfate (SLES), cocamidopropyl betaine, decyl glucoside.

**Key ingredients by concern:**
- **Acne:** Salicylic acid (BHA), benzoyl peroxide, niacinamide
- **Dryness:** Ceramides, hyaluronic acid, glycerin, fatty acids
- **Oily skin:** Zinc PCA, niacinamide, salicylic acid
- **Hyperpigmentation:** Glycolic acid, lactic acid (AHA), vitamin C
- **Sensitivity:** Centella asiatica, allantoin, minimal fragrance, colloidal oatmeal

## How Often Should You Wash Your Face?

**Twice daily is the general recommendation** — morning and night. Morning cleansing removes sweat, dead skin cells, and nighttime skincare products. Evening cleansing removes sunscreen, makeup, and environmental pollutants (the most important wash of the day).

**Over-cleansing is a real problem.** Washing 3+ times per day or using harsh cleansers strips the skin barrier and stimulates oil overproduction as a compensatory response. If your skin feels tight immediately after washing, your cleanser is too harsh.

**Micellar water is not a full cleanse.** Micellar water (like Garnier Micellar) is a convenient makeup-removing option but doesn't fully clean pores. Use it as a first cleanse to remove heavy makeup or SPF, followed by a proper facial cleanser.

## FAQ

**Do I need a different cleanser for morning vs. night?**
Not necessarily, but it's worth considering. Many people use a gentle hydrating cleanser in the morning (since you're just removing overnight sweat/products) and a slightly more thorough formula at night for full makeup and SPF removal.

**Does my cleanser need to have active ingredients like salicylic acid or AHAs?**
Only if your skin needs targeted treatment and you're using a leave-on formula simultaneously. Wash-off actives are often less effective than leave-on counterparts, but they can still make a difference for oily and acne-prone skin types.

**Can I use the same cleanser on my face and body?**
Facial cleansers are formulated for the more delicate, thinner skin of the face. Body wash has different pH and ingredient considerations. While some people do use CeraVe or Cetaphil on both face and body successfully, the reverse (using body wash on the face) often causes irritation.

**My cleanser makes my skin feel tight. Is that normal?**
No — that "clean" feeling is barrier disruption, not cleanliness. Switch to a gentler formula immediately. Look for sulfate-free cleansers with ceramides or hyaluronic acid.
