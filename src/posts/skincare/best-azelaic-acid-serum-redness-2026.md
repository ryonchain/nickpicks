## What Azelaic Acid Does

Azelaic acid is a naturally occurring dicarboxylic acid found in grains like wheat, barley, and rye. At OTC concentrations of 10–20%, it delivers several distinct benefits:

**For rosacea and redness:**
Azelaic acid reduces the inflammatory mediators that cause rosacea flushing and persistent redness. Clinical studies show 15–20% azelaic acid is comparable to metronidazole gel (a commonly prescribed rosacea treatment) in reducing redness and papules.

**For acne:**
Azelaic acid has antibacterial activity against *P. acnes* and other acne-causing bacteria. It also reduces the keratin buildup inside pores that leads to comedone formation. Less irritating than benzoyl peroxide but comparably effective for mild-to-moderate acne.

**For hyperpigmentation:**
Azelaic acid inhibits tyrosinase, the enzyme that drives melanin production. This makes it effective for post-acne marks, melasma, and sun spots — without the irritation of hydroquinone and without vitamin C's stability issues.

**For sensitive skin:**
Unlike most actives at equivalent efficacy, azelaic acid is exceptionally well-tolerated. Minor tingling and temporary redness on first application are common and usually resolve within 1–2 weeks.

## How to Use Azelaic Acid

**Apply morning or evening.** Unlike retinol, azelaic acid doesn't increase UV sensitivity and can be used at any time. For rosacea reduction, twice-daily use produces faster results.

**Allow 10–15 minutes before layering.** Azelaic acid functions best at a specific pH. Applying moisturizer immediately can reduce efficacy. A short wait between azelaic application and the next skincare step allows it to fully absorb.

**Don't mix with vitamin C on the same application.** Both are brightening actives and can be used in the same routine — vitamin C in the morning, azelaic at night — but layering them simultaneously can cause pH interference.

**Expect mild tingling at first.** A temporary sensation of tingling or warmth on application is normal during the first 1–2 weeks as your skin adjusts. This typically resolves without discontinuing use.

## FAQ

**How long does azelaic acid take to work?**
Redness reduction: 4–6 weeks. Dark spot fading: 8–12 weeks. Acne improvement: 4–8 weeks. Results accumulate with consistent daily use.

**Is azelaic acid safe during pregnancy?**
Yes — it's one of the few evidence-based skincare actives that is pregnancy-safe. Confirm with your OB, but azelaic acid is widely used as a retinol and hydroquinone alternative during pregnancy for acne and hyperpigmentation.

**Can I use azelaic acid with retinol?**
Yes. Use one at a time — retinol at night, azelaic acid in the morning. Or alternate nights. Both target similar concerns (hyperpigmentation, texture) but via different mechanisms, so they're complementary over time.

**Is 10% OTC azelaic acid effective, or do I need prescription 15–20%?**
10% is clinically effective for mild rosacea and hyperpigmentation. For moderate-to-severe rosacea, prescription 15% (Finacea) is demonstrably more effective. If OTC results plateau after 12 weeks, a dermatology appointment is warranted.
