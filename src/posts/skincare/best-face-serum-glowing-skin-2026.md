## What Serum Does Your Skin Actually Need?

Before buying, identify your primary concern:

- **Dullness / uneven tone:** Vitamin C, niacinamide, glycolic acid, kojic acid
- **Dehydration / dry skin:** Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, panthenol, squalane
- **Fine lines / aging:** Retinol, peptides, vitamin C + ferulic acid
- **Acne / congested pores:** Niacinamide, salicylic acid, azelaic acid
- **Redness / rosacea:** Niacinamide, centella asiatica, green tea extract
- **Hyperpigmentation:** Vitamin C, tranexamic acid, azelaic acid, kojic acid

One serum targeting your primary concern is always more effective than stacking multiple serums targeting multiple concerns. Get your biggest problem solved first, then layer in additional serums if needed.

## Building the Right Serum Routine

**Morning routine:** Vitamin C serum is the ideal morning active — antioxidant protection against UV and environmental damage pairs perfectly with SPF. Apply after cleansing, before moisturizer and sunscreen.

**Evening routine:** Retinol, AHAs/BHAs, tranexamic acid, or other cell-turnover actives work best at night. These are often more photosensitizing and work in synergy with the skin's elevated nighttime repair activity.

**How many serums can you stack?** Two maximum in one routine — and only if they address different concerns without conflicting ingredients. Layering three or more serums is usually wasteful at best and irritating at worst. Get one serum working well before adding a second.

## FAQ

**How do I know which serum is right for my skin?**
Start with your primary concern: dullness, dark spots, fine lines, acne, redness, or dehydration. Choose one serum targeting that specific issue. Use it consistently for 8–12 weeks before evaluating. Only add a second serum if you have a clearly distinct second concern.

**When in the routine does serum go?**
After cleansing and toning, before moisturizer. The rule of thumb: thinnest to thickest consistency. Serums are thinner than moisturizers and should go first.

**Can I use multiple serums at once?**
You can layer two with different textures (e.g., a water-based hyaluronic acid serum first, then a vitamin C serum). But avoid layering multiple acid serums or ingredients with conflicting pH requirements. Simplicity is almost always better.

**How long until my serum shows results?**
Hydration: immediate to 1 week. Brightening (vitamin C): 4–8 weeks. Dark spot fading: 8–12 weeks. Wrinkle reduction (retinol): 12–24 weeks. Managing expectations is as important as the serum itself.
