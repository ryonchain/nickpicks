## Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Dash Cam

### Resolution
- **1080p:** Adequate for most situations; license plates readable at close range
- **2K (1440p):** Better plate capture at highway speeds; good night performance
- **4K:** Best image quality; larger file sizes; requires faster microSD card

### Single vs. Dual Channel
A front-only camera covers 80% of accidents. Add a rear camera if you're frequently rear-ended, drive in heavy traffic, or use rideshare. Dual-channel systems cost ~$50–100 more.

### Night Vision
Look for models with Sony STARVIS sensors or HDR processing. Night incidents are common and poor low-light footage is often useless for insurance claims.

### GPS
Records speed and coordinates on every clip — valuable for proving you were at the speed limit. Some insurers require GPS data for claims.

### Parking Mode
Records while your car is off, triggered by motion or impact. Requires either a hardwire kit or battery pack (sold separately). Valuable if you park on the street.

### Memory Card
Not included with most dash cams. Use Class 10 / U3 microSD cards rated for high-endurance recording. 64GB handles ~10 hours of 2K footage before overwriting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a dash cam legal in my state?**
Dash cams are legal in all 50 US states. Some states restrict placement on the windshield (California limits the top 5 inches or bottom 7 inches). Audio recording laws vary — check your state's two-party consent law if you record conversations.

**Will a dash cam drain my car battery?**
Continuous recording while parked can drain a battery. Parking mode dash cams have low-voltage cutoff to prevent this — they stop recording if battery drops below ~11.8V. A hardwire kit adds battery protection.

**How much storage do I need?**
64GB is sufficient for most drivers. At 2K resolution, 64GB stores about 8–10 hours of footage. Loop recording automatically overwrites oldest footage. If you drive 2+ hours daily, consider 128GB.

**Can dash cam footage be used as evidence in court?**
Yes. Clear footage is accepted as evidence in insurance claims and legal proceedings in most jurisdictions. Footage with GPS timestamps and audio is more compelling.

**What's the best microSD card for a dash cam?**
Samsung Pro Endurance, SanDisk High Endurance, and Kingston Canvas Go! are the most commonly recommended. Regular cards can fail after a few months of constant write-erase cycles.
