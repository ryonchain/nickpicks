## Gas vs. Battery vs. Corded: Which Type Is Right?

**Battery-powered (cordless):** The default recommendation for residential use. No gas to store, no emissions, starts instantly, quieter than gas. Runtime is the limitation — 20–45 minutes per charge depending on speed setting. For most driveways and yards, that's enough for one session.

**Corded electric:** Unlimited runtime, lightest weight, cheapest price. Limitation is the cord — you need an outdoor extension cord and you can't move far from an outlet. Good for decks, patios, and small yards.

**Gas:** More power, unlimited runtime, but: maintenance, emissions, noise, and ethanol fuel issues. Only worth it for large properties (1/2 acre+) where battery runtime is genuinely limiting. Outside the scope of this guide.

**CFM vs MPH:** Air volume (CFM — cubic feet per minute) moves more debris. Air speed (MPH) dislodges stuck debris and handles wet leaves. You want both. 300+ CFM is the minimum for a useful tool; 450+ CFM handles heavy wet leaves efficiently.

## When to Buy

Leaf blower prices peak in October when demand spikes. The best time to buy is **July through early September** — manufacturers clear inventory before the new model year and big-box stores run summer sales. Labor Day weekend typically sees 15–25% off outdoor power tools across major retailers.
