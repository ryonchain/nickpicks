## Why Throw Blankets Are Fall's Best Low-Effort Upgrade

A good throw blanket is one of those rare purchases that genuinely improves daily life: it makes the couch better for movie nights, the bed warmer during cold snaps, and the reading chair more comfortable on autumn evenings. The right throw sits within reach, doesn't pill or shed after 10 washes, and doesn't slide off when you shift position.

What to evaluate before buying:

- **Material:** Sherpa fleece is the warmest and softest; regular fleece is lighter and cheaper; chunky knit is decorative and warm but pills; weighted blankets use glass beads for pressure therapy.
- **Size:** Throw size (50"x60") covers one person on a couch. Oversized (60"x80") covers two people or wraps fully around one.
- **Weight:** Standard throws run 1–3 lbs. Weighted blankets come in 10, 15, and 20 lb versions (10% of body weight is the guideline).
- **Washability:** Machine-washable in a standard-size washer is the baseline. Heavy blankets may need a commercial machine.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Sherpa vs. fleece vs. weighted:** Sherpa is the warmest and softest single-layer option. Standard fleece is lighter and less warm but very washable. Weighted blankets serve a different purpose — pressure therapy — rather than pure warmth.

**Size up if you share:** A 50"x60" throw covers one person comfortably. If two people share it on a couch, step up to a 60"x80" or queen-size blanket.

**Check the weight rating on weighted blankets:** 10% of body weight is the common guideline. A 15 lb weighted blanket for a 120 lb person will feel oppressive; for a 150 lb person it's appropriate.

**Avoid "plush" blankets that don't specify machine washability:** Anything that requires dry cleaning or hand-washing is a practical problem for everyday use. Every pick on this list is machine washable.
