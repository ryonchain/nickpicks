## Indoor Plants for Fall: What to Know Before You Gift

Fall plant gifting has a built-in challenge: most plants dislike the cold, and shipping in fall means potential cold damage. The picks in this guide focus on plants sold through reputable Amazon sellers who ship in insulated packaging and plants that handle temperature variation better than tropical varieties.

For givers, the core question is whether to gift a plant (risk of shipping stress but complete gift) or a planter (safe to ship, requires recipient to choose their own plant). Both approaches are valid.

**What makes a good plant gift:**
- **Low watering requirements:** The most common plant killer is overwatering or neglect. Succulents, pothos, ZZ plants, and snake plants tolerate irregular watering — a beginner can't easily kill them.
- **Low light tolerance:** Homes and offices have variable lighting. Plants that need direct sunlight fail in most indoor settings.
- **Visual appeal:** The combination of the plant and its pot determines whether it earns a spot on the desk or windowsill.
- **Size for shipping:** Small to medium plants (4"–6" pot) ship successfully; large statement plants risk more damage and cost more in shipping.

The ZZ plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia) is the plant for offices, dark rooms, and people who genuinely forget to water. ## What to Look For When Buying

**Buy from reputable plant sellers on Amazon:** Plant quality varies significantly. Look for sellers with 500+ reviews and 4.5+ stars who specifically ship insulated in cold weather. Read recent reviews for shipping condition comments.

**Match the plant to the recipient's space:** Before gifting, consider the light levels in their home and whether they have pets (many common houseplants are toxic). When in doubt, choose succulents (non-toxic, low light, low water) or gift a planter only.

**Size matters for shipping:** Small plants (2"–4" pots) ship in better condition than large ones. A 4" succulent that arrives healthy is a better gift than a 10" monstera that arrives stressed.

**Include a care card:** Most Amazon plant sellers include basic care instructions. If the plant arrives without one, a brief note on watering schedule and light requirements makes the gift substantially more useful for beginner plant owners.
