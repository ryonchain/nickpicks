## Bedroom Space Heaters: What Actually Matters

A heater for the bedroom is different from a living room heater. The priorities shift:

- **Noise level:** A fan-forced ceramic heater that moves enough air to heat a bedroom quickly may be too loud for sleep. Oil-filled and infrared models run near-silently.
- **Safety:** Tip-over shutoff and overheating protection are non-negotiable for overnight use. OSHA requires these on any heater marketed for residential use, but verify they're actually present.
- **Room size:** Most personal heaters cover 150–200 sq ft. Space heaters marketed as "room heaters" cover 300–400 sq ft. Bedrooms under 150 sq ft can use a 750W personal heater; larger bedrooms need a 1500W model.
- **Thermostat and timer:** A programmable thermostat lets the heater maintain a target temperature rather than running at full blast continuously. A timer lets you pre-heat the room before bedtime.

**Heater types:**
- **Ceramic fan-forced:** Fast heat, moves air, louder — good for fast warmup
- **Infrared:** Heats objects and people directly, not air — efficient for spot heating, silent
- **Oil-filled radiator:** Silent, retains heat, slower to warm up — ideal for overnight
- **Micathermic:** Combines radiant and convection — fast and silent, higher cost

## What to Look For When Buying

**Safety certifications first:** Look for ETL or UL certification on the package, not just marketing language. Tip-over protection and overheat shutoff are required for overnight bedroom use.

**Match wattage to room size:** 750W heats rooms under 100 sq ft. 1500W handles 150–300 sq ft depending on insulation quality. If the bedroom has poor insulation or large windows, go to 1500W.

**Factor in electricity cost:** A 1500W heater running 8 hours per night costs approximately $0.12–$0.18 per hour depending on your electricity rate, or $1–$1.50/night. This adds up; a programmable thermostat pays for itself quickly by cycling the heater on and off rather than running continuously.

**Cord length matters for bedroom placement:** Most space heaters come with 6-foot cords. If your outlet is far from where you need the heater, measure before buying. Never use an extension cord with a space heater.
