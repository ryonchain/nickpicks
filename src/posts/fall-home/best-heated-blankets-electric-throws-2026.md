## What Makes a Heated Blanket Worth Buying

A bad heated blanket either doesn't heat evenly (cold patches), gets too hot and can't regulate itself, or lacks a proper auto-shutoff — all fire risks. The best options use carbon fiber or wire heating elements distributed evenly across the blanket, paired with digital thermostats and timers.

**Key features to look for:**
- **Auto-shutoff:** 1–10 hour shutoff is standard. Anything without auto-shutoff is a non-starter.
- **Machine washable:** The heating elements run through the blanket, so look for "machine washable" on the label — many are, but some require hand-washing the controller off first.
- **Heat levels:** 3 levels minimum. Premium blankets have 5–10 settings.
- **Dual zone:** For full-size blankets used by couples, dual zone lets each side set their own temperature.
- **Heat-up time:** Most heated blankets reach operating temp in 5–10 minutes. Faster is better for couch-side throws.

**Heated throw vs. heated blanket:**
- **Throws** (~50"×60"): Good for the couch or lap use. Lighter, lower wattage, usually single-zone.
- **Blankets** (twin through king): Designed for beds. Heavier, dual-zone options available, higher wattage.

## Energy Cost: How Much Do Heated Blankets Actually Use?

A heated throw typically runs at 50–100W. A full-size electric blanket runs at 100–200W at max settings. At average U.S. electricity rates (~$0.13/kWh):

- **Heated throw (75W) run for 4 hours:** ~$0.04/night
- **Full-size blanket (150W) run for 8 hours:** ~$0.16/night

Even running a heated blanket every night, the monthly cost is under $5 — less than turning up the thermostat by 2°F for a bedroom.

## Safety Notes

- **Never use a heated blanket with another electric blanket layered on top.** This creates a heat trap.
- **Don't fold a heated blanket while in use.** Folding concentrates heat and can damage the wiring.
- **Replace blankets older than 10 years.** Wiring degrades over time; older blankets without modern auto-shutoffs are a higher fire risk.
- **Don't use with infants or people who cannot sense temperature changes.** Stick to non-electric warmth for young children.
