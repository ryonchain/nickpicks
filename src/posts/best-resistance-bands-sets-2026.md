## Buying Guide: Which Band Type Do You Need?

### Loop Bands (Mini Bands)
Short circular bands, 12 inches in circumference. Best for lower body: glutes, hip abduction, clamshells, lateral walks. Also used for mobility and stretching. Entry point for resistance training.

### Tube Bands with Handles
Long bands with handles and attachments. Best for upper body: bicep curls, chest press, shoulder press, rows, tricep pushdowns. Most closely replicate cable machine exercises. Buy these for strength training.

### Pull-Up Assist / Full-Loop Bands
41-inch full-size loops. Used to assist pull-ups and dips, or add resistance to barbell work. Required for pull-up training progression.

### Flat Therapy Bands
Wide, flat strips. Clinical physical therapy standard. Ideal for gentle rehab, shoulder and hip strengthening, and low-impact flexibility work.

### Resistance Levels
- **Light (2–15 lbs):** Mobility, warmup, physical therapy, beginners
- **Medium (15–35 lbs):** General fitness, upper body, glute work
- **Heavy (35–80 lbs):** Strength training, athletic conditioning
- **Extra Heavy (80+ lbs):** Elite resistance training, pull-up assist for beginners

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can resistance bands replace free weights?**
For most home workouts, yes. For progressive overload strength programs (powerlifting, bodybuilding), bands supplement but don't fully replace weights. For general fitness, fat loss, mobility, and maintenance — bands are excellent and often preferred.

**How long do resistance bands last?**
Quality latex bands last 1–3 years with regular use. Signs of wear: small cracks, whitening in the latex, or visible stress marks near clip points (for tube bands). Inspect before each use.

**Can I build muscle with resistance bands?**
Yes, especially for beginners and intermediate lifters. Research shows muscle activation with bands is comparable to free weights for many exercises. The variable resistance (harder at full extension) can actually increase muscle engagement.

**Are resistance bands good for losing weight?**
Resistance training increases metabolic rate and builds muscle that burns more calories at rest. Bands are effective for resistance training. For weight loss, combine with cardio and caloric deficit.

**What resistance level should I start with?**
Beginners: start with light to medium (12–25 lbs range). You should be able to complete 12–15 reps with good form. If you can do 20+ reps easily, go heavier. If you can't do 8, go lighter.
