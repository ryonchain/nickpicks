## Why Switch to Traditional Wet Shaving?

**Cost:** Multi-blade cartridges cost $3–5 each. Double-edge safety razor blades cost $0.10–0.30 each. A $5 pack of DE blades lasts most men 2–3 months. Annual savings: $100–200+ for most men.

**Closeness:** A single sharp blade with the correct angle cuts closer than multiple blades stacked behind each other. Multiple blades pull hair before cutting it — a single blade doesn't.

**Skin quality:** Single-blade shaving causes less irritation, fewer ingrown hairs, and less razor burn for most men. The multi-blade "lift and cut" mechanism frequently cuts hairs below the follicle opening, causing ingrowns.

**Sustainability:** One DE handle for decades. Only blades replaced (stainless steel, widely recyclable).

---

## Safety Razor vs. Straight Razor

**Safety razor (DE):** Handle + double-edge blade. Beginner-friendly. The safety bar guards the blade angle — you can't cut yourself at the wrong angle. Learning curve: 2–4 weeks for confident technique.

**Straight razor (full hollow-ground):** Single folded blade, sharpened on a strop before each use, honed periodically on a whetstone. Zero safety guard. Full technique skill required. Learning curve: months of consistent practice. Reward: the closest shave possible with a blade.

**Shavette:** A straight razor with a replaceable blade cartridge instead of a permanent blade. Removes the stropping/honing requirement. Considered a middle ground — shavette technique is similar to a straight razor but without blade maintenance.

**Recommendation for beginners:** Start with a DE safety razor. Shave with it for 3–6 months to build lather, angle, and pressure technique before considering a straight razor.

## Essential Accessories for Wet Shaving

- **Shaving brush:** Badger or boar hair for working up lather. A decent brush makes mediocre soap perform better.
- **Shaving soap or cream:** Proraso, Taylor of Old Bond Street, or Cremo are excellent starting soaps.
- **Pre-shave oil:** Reduces friction and softens hair for a smoother first pass.
- **Alum block:** A post-shave mineral block that seals minor cuts and reduces irritation.
- **Aftershave balm (not splash):** Balm moisturizes; alcohol splash stings. Use a balm unless you enjoy the sting.
