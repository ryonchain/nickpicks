## Why Men Should Moisturize

Moisturizing isn't optional for healthy skin — it's maintenance. Here's what happens without it:

- **Dehydrated skin ages faster.** UV damage, wind, shaving, and daily environmental exposure break down collagen. Moisturizer + SPF dramatically slows this.
- **Shaving dries skin.** Every pass of a blade removes skin cells. Without replacement moisture, skin becomes chronically irritated.
- **Dry skin signals oil glands to overproduce.** Oily skin is often a symptom of under-moisturizing, not over-moisturizing.

**When to apply:** Morning (with SPF) and/or night (without SPF). Start with once-daily if you're new to it.

---

## Anti-Aging Ingredients That Actually Work

**Retinol (vitamin A derivative):** Clinically proven to increase cell turnover, stimulate collagen production, and reduce visible wrinkles. Requires consistent use (4–12 weeks) to see results. Can cause initial peeling — start with low concentration.

**Peptides:** Short amino acid chains that signal the skin to produce collagen. Less potent than retinol but more tolerable for sensitive skin. No sun sensitivity.

**Niacinamide (vitamin B3):** Reduces pore appearance, evens skin tone, anti-inflammatory. Pairs well with both retinol and SPF products.

**SPF:** The single most anti-aging ingredient available. Broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily use prevents 80%+ of visible aging caused by UV exposure. More impactful than any serum or treatment.

## AM vs PM Moisturizer Routines

**Morning:** Use an SPF-containing moisturizer (CeraVe AM, Cetaphil Men). SPF in the morning is non-negotiable if you go outside at all.

**Evening:** SPF is unnecessary at night. Use a richer hydrator or a retinol product (Neutrogena Rapid Wrinkle Repair). Night is when skin regenerates — active ingredients work best during this window.
