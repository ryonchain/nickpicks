# Best Twin XL Bedding Sets for College Dorms 2026: What to Buy and What to Skip

Dorm beds are Twin XL: 38 inches wide and 80 inches long. Standard twin sheets (75 inches long) won't fit. This is the one bedding fact that trips up most incoming freshmen, and it turns "I'll just bring sheets from home" into "I need to buy everything new in August."

This guide covers what to buy, what sizes to get, and which specific sets on Amazon are worth the money for a dorm room.

---

## The Quick Buying Guide

Before picking specific products, here's what every incoming college student needs:

1. **Fitted sheet (Twin XL) × 2** — you'll want a spare for laundry days
2. **Flat sheet (Twin XL) × 1** — optional if you sleep hot and use just a comforter
3. **Comforter or duvet (Twin, not Twin XL)** — comforters are labeled Twin but sized to hang over a Twin XL mattress
4. **Pillow × 1–2** — standard size works fine; your existing pillows work too
5. **Pillowcase(s)** — standard size

**Note on comforter sizing:** Most comforter manufacturers label "Twin" comforters at 66×86 or 68×90 inches. Those dimensions work for Twin XL mattresses because the comforter hangs over the sides. "Twin XL" comforters exist but are usually mattress-pad dimensions, not bedding. When in doubt, look for 68×90 or 90×90.
