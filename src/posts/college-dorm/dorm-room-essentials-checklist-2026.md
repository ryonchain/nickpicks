## Storage Solutions (The Real Challenge)

Dorm rooms average 150–200 square feet — often shared. You get half that. Smart storage is the difference between a livable room and a disaster.

## What to Skip

**Don't buy these before move-in:**

- A full-size coffee maker (check if your dorm allows it; most don't)
- Decorative throw pillows (they get in the way and end up on the floor)
- Printer (use the school library — you'll use it 3 times all year)
- Excessive kitchen supplies if your dorm has a communal kitchen
- Multiple sets of sheets (one good set is enough; do laundry weekly)

## Move-In Day Tips

1. **Measure your bed before ordering sheets.** Some schools have non-standard heights.
2. **Coordinate with your roommate** on shared items like a mini fridge, microwave, or rug.
3. **Pack a "first night" bag** — sheets, toiletries, chargers, phone — before the boxes.
4. **Check your school's prohibited items list** — candles, certain appliances, and specific mounting hardware are commonly banned.

Start with the bedding, desk setup, and bathroom basics. Everything else can wait until you know the room.
