Shared dorm bathrooms require a specific setup that doesn't translate directly from living at home. ## The Dorm Shower Reality Check

Most dorm showers are shared among 10–30 students per floor. This means:

- **Your stuff lives in your room**, not on a shelf in the shower
- **You walk to and from the shower** — which means carrying everything in a caddy
- **Floors and surfaces are always damp** — plastic and rust-proof materials only
- **Privacy is managed with a towel and flip flops**, not a private bathroom
- **Shower drainage is communal** — you'll want flip flops regardless of cleanliness

The caddy is the central organizing tool. Everything else hangs off the caddy choice.

## What to Leave at Home

- **Full-size bath towels:** Too slow to dry in the dorm environment. Use microfiber.
- **Candles and wax melts:** Fire code violation in most dorms.
- **Standard electric hair dryers over 1800 watts:** May trip breakers in older dorm buildings.
- **Excess beauty appliances:** Bring what you use daily. Excess just creates storage headaches.
