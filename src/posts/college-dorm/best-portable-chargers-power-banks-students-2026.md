## What to Look for in a Portable Charger for College

### Capacity (mAh)

Capacity is measured in milliamp hours (mAh). Higher mAh = more charges.

**Quick reference:**
- Typical smartphone battery: 3,500–5,000 mAh
- 10,000 mAh power bank = roughly 2–2.5 full phone charges
- 20,000 mAh = roughly 4–5 full phone charges
- 26,800 mAh+ = enough for laptops + phone

For phone-only use, **10,000 mAh** is the sweet spot: enough for 2+ charges, still pocket-size. For laptop charging, **20,000–27,000 mAh** is the target, but these units are larger.

### Charging Speed

Fast charging requires both the right power bank wattage and a USB-C PD (Power Delivery) cable. Standard 5W charging is slow; 20W+ is fast; 65W+ is required for meaningful laptop charging.

- **5W (standard USB-A):** Slow — adds about 1% battery per minute
- **18–20W (USB-C PD):** Fast phone charging — 0 to 50% in ~30 minutes
- **45W+ (USB-C PD):** Laptop-viable; charges most ultrabooks meaningfully
- **65W+ (USB-C PD):** Full laptop charging; on par with wall charger speed

### Number of Ports

Check how many devices you're charging simultaneously. If you carry a phone, earbuds, and tablet, a 2–3 port power bank lets you charge everything from one unit.

### Size and Weight

The best power bank is the one you actually carry. A 10,000 mAh power bank weighs 6–8 oz and fits in most pockets. A 26,800 mAh laptop power bank is 1.5–2 lbs and belongs in your backpack.

## Portable Charger Tips for Campus Life

**Charge your power bank overnight, every night.** A power bank you forgot to charge is useless. Make it part of your nightly routine: plug it in with your phone.

**Buy a second USB-C cable.** The power bank usually includes one cable, but having a dedicated cable in your bag means you're not searching for one in the morning.

**Know your laptop's charging wattage.** Most ultrabooks (MacBook Air, Dell XPS, ASUS VivoBook) charge at 45–65W. If your power bank puts out less than your laptop's charging wattage, it'll still charge — just more slowly. 20W is better than 0W during a 3-hour lecture.

**Don't fly with >100Wh.** Power banks above 100 Wh (approximately 27,000 mAh) require airline approval and may be prohibited. Check before packing for study abroad or breaks.
