## Do You Actually Need a Face Wash?

Yes — and bar soap doesn't count. Bar soaps are alkaline (pH 9–10) while your skin is naturally acidic (pH 4.5–5.5). Using bar soap on your face repeatedly disrupts the skin barrier, leading to dryness, oiliness as your skin overproduces sebum to compensate, and increased sensitivity. A proper face wash is formulated to cleanse at skin's natural pH.

**How often:** Once daily (morning or night) is adequate for most men. Twice daily (morning + night) for oily or acne-prone skin.

---

## Skin Type Quick Guide

**Oily skin:** Shiny within hours of washing, visible pores, prone to breakouts. Need: gel or foaming cleanser that removes excess sebum without triggering rebound oil production.

**Dry skin:** Feels tight or flaky after washing, rarely shiny, may feel rough. Need: cream or hydrating cleanser that cleans without removing natural moisture.

**Combination skin:** Oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin), normal to dry cheeks. Need: balanced cleanser — not so stripping it dries the cheeks, not so gentle it leaves the T-zone greasy.

**Sensitive skin:** Reacts to fragrances, alcohol, or harsh surfactants with redness or irritation. Need: fragrance-free, minimal-ingredient formula.

## Ingredients to Seek and Avoid

**Seek:**
- Ceramides (NP, AP, EOP): Repair and maintain skin barrier
- Hyaluronic acid: Hydration retention
- Glycerin: Humectant, draws moisture to skin
- Aloe vera: Soothing and anti-inflammatory
- Glycolic or lactic acid (low %, <2%): Gentle exfoliation

**Avoid for sensitive skin:**
- Sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS): Harsh surfactant, strips barrier
- Alcohol (denatured/SD): Drying and irritating
- Artificial fragrances: Common irritant and allergen
- Parabens: Potential sensitizers for reactive skin
