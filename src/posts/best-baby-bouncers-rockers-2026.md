## Automatic vs Manual: Which Is Right for You?

**Manual bouncers** move only when baby moves (via their own motion) or when you give them a gentle push. They're quieter, lighter, less expensive, and have no batteries to replace. Many pediatricians prefer them because the motion is gentler and more natural.

**Automatic bouncers** and **rockers** use motors to generate motion on a timer or in response to baby sounds/cries. They're more expensive and bulkier, but they keep baby soothed without you having to be next to them — a genuine lifesaver during long cooking sessions or work calls.

**Our recommendation:** A manual bouncer is the better default purchase. Add an automatic rocker only if you have a high-needs baby or need long stretches of hands-free time.
