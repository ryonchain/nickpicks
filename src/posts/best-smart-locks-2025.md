## Buying Guide: Smart Lock Essentials

**Installation compatibility.** Most smart locks replace a standard single-cylinder deadbolt and fit a standard door prep (2-1/8" backset). Measure your existing door prep before ordering. August is the exception — it mounts over your existing thumb turn.

**Hub vs. built-in WiFi.** Built-in WiFi locks (Schlage, Kwikset, August) connect directly to your router without additional hardware. Hub-based locks (Yale Z-Wave, Ultraloq without bridge) require a smart hub (SmartThings, Wink, Amazon Echo hub). Built-in WiFi is simpler; hub-based is more powerful for home automation.

**Battery life.** Smart locks run on AA batteries, typically lasting 6–12 months. All models include low-battery alerts. Keep a spare set of batteries on hand — most locks have a physical key slot or 9V emergency power option as backup.

**Auto-lock.** All five locks on this list support auto-lock — they lock automatically after a set delay. This is the single most valuable smart lock feature for security.

## FAQ

**Are smart locks safe?**
Quality smart locks (Grade 1 and 2 ANSI/BHMA certified) are as physically secure as traditional deadbolts. The digital side is protected by encrypted communication and two-factor authentication on the app. No known exploits exist for the major brands covered here.

**Can I still use a physical key with a smart lock?**
Most smart locks include a physical key cylinder (Schlage, Kwikset, Ultraloq). The August and Yale Assure Lock 2 are keyless by design, though the August attaches to your existing deadbolt (keeping your physical keys).

**What if my WiFi or battery dies?**
Most locks have a physical key override. Yale and Schlage have external 9V battery terminals for emergency power. Bluetooth-only access still works locally when WiFi is down.
