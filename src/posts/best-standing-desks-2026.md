## Why You Need a Standing Desk

Sitting for eight or more hours a day is hard on your body — full stop. Research consistently links prolonged sitting to increased risks of back pain, poor posture, metabolic issues, and reduced energy levels throughout the workday. An electric height-adjustable desk lets you shift between sitting and standing in seconds, which breaks up that sedentary time without disrupting your workflow.

The benefits compound quickly. Even alternating 30 minutes of standing per hour has been shown to reduce lower back discomfort and improve afternoon energy. Pairing a standing desk with a good anti-fatigue mat and monitor at eye level creates an ergonomic setup that genuinely changes how you feel after a long day of work.

## Key Specs to Know Before You Buy

**Height Range:** Look for a range of at least 22.8 inches (sitting) to 48.4 inches (standing) to accommodate most body types. Taller users (6'2"+) should target a maximum height of 50 inches or more.

**Weight Capacity:** Budget desks typically handle 110–150 lbs. Mid-range and premium models push 275–355 lbs, which matters if you're running multiple monitors, a heavy workstation, or arm mounts.

**Motor Type:** Dual-motor setups are quieter, faster, and significantly more stable than single-motor designs. Single-motor desks are fine for lighter loads and occasional adjustments.

**Memory Presets:** Most quality desks offer 3–4 programmable height presets. This is a must-have feature — manually dialing in your exact sitting and standing heights every time gets old fast.

**Stability:** Wobble at standing height is the most common complaint with budget desks. Look for a cross-brace or H-frame design and steel legs with a wall thickness of at least 2mm for a solid feel.

## FAQ

**Do I need a dual-motor standing desk?**
If you're placing more than 150 lbs on the desk or want maximum stability, yes. Dual-motor desks are quieter, more stable, and handle heavier loads without strain. For laptop-only setups, a single motor is sufficient.

**What height range do I need?**
For sitting, you generally need the desk at 22–30 inches depending on your height and chair. For standing, target 40–50 inches. If you're taller than 6'2", prioritize desks with a maximum height above 49 inches.

**Are standing desks worth it?**
Yes, with consistent use. The key is actually using the sit-stand functionality — set reminders or use an app to prompt position changes every 30–60 minutes. A standing desk you never raise is just an expensive desk.

**Can I use my existing desktop surface?**
Most electric desk frames (including the Flexispot E7 Pro and Uplift V2) are sold as frame-only options that accept standard tabletops. Measure your surface and check the frame's width range before purchasing.

**How long does assembly take?**
Budget 45–90 minutes for frame-only desks. The Vari Electric is the exception — it arrives mostly assembled and takes 10–15 minutes. Having a second person to help flip the frame and attach legs makes most assemblies significantly easier.
