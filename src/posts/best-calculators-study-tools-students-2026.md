## Best Flashcard and Study Apps

Physical flashcard apps have largely replaced index cards for most students — they're always with you, they adapt to what you don't know, and they use spaced repetition to maximize long-term retention.

### 6. Anki — Best Free Spaced Repetition App

Anki is the most powerful free flashcard app available. It uses a spaced repetition algorithm (SRS) that shows you cards right before you're about to forget them — dramatically more efficient than random review. Used by medical students, language learners, and law students who have enormous volumes of material to retain.

**The trade-off:** Anki has a steeper learning curve than Quizlet. The Android app is free; the iOS app costs a one-time $25 (the fee funds ongoing development).

**Best for:** Students who need to memorize large volumes of material — medical, dental, law, or any course with extensive vocabulary or definitions.

**Get started:** ankiweb.net (free desktop/Android) or search the App Store for "Anki flashcards."

### 7. Quizlet — Best All-Around Study App

Quizlet is the most popular student flashcard app for good reason. It's easy to create decks, there are millions of pre-made decks for common courses, and the study modes (Learn, Flashcards, Match, Test) cover different learning styles. The free tier is sufficient for most students; Quizlet Plus ($36/year) adds advanced features.

**Best for:** Students who want a quick start with pre-made decks, group studying, or multiple study modes beyond straight flashcards.

**Get started:** quizlet.com — free tier is sufficient for most use cases.

### 8. Notion — Best for Note Organization

Notion is a flexible workspace app that functions as a note-taking app, task manager, database, and project planner in one. Students use it to organize class notes by course, track assignments with databases, and build a personal knowledge base that grows through college. Free for students.

**Best for:** Students who want a centralized hub for all their notes, readings, and tasks. Works especially well for humanities, business, and social science students who take a lot of notes.

**Get started:** notion.so — free personal plan, Education plan is also free with a student email.

## Study Tools Buying Guide

### Do you actually need a TI-84?

Check your school's course requirements and your planned standardized tests before buying. If your school uses TI-84 in class instruction, buy the TI-84. If you're in a school that allows any approved graphing calculator, the Casio fx-9750GIII saves $60. If you only need a scientific calculator for your current courses, buy a $12 Casio instead.

### Should you use Anki or Quizlet?

For most high school students: **Quizlet**. It's easier to use, has pre-made decks for popular courses, and the study modes are more varied.

For college students with heavy memorization requirements (pre-med, nursing, law): **Anki**. The spaced repetition algorithm is more rigorous, and the medical and science Anki decks available online are exceptionally good.

### Paper planner vs. digital?

Use what you'll actually use consistently. A paper planner left on your desk creates a passive visual reminder of what's coming. A digital app is always accessible from your phone. Many students use both: a paper planner for weekly scheduling and a digital app (Notion, Apple Reminders, or Google Calendar) for quick captures throughout the day.
