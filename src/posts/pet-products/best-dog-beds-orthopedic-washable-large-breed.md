# Best Dog Beds (2025): Orthopedic, Washable & Large Breed Options

A quality dog bed is one of the most impactful purchases you can make for your pet's health and comfort. Dogs spend 12–14 hours a day sleeping, and the right bed supports joint health, reduces pressure points, and gives your dog a dedicated space they can call their own.

This guide covers the best dog beds across three key needs: orthopedic support for older or arthritic dogs, easy-clean washable beds for muddy pups, and oversized options that actually fit large and giant breeds.

---

## What Makes a Great Dog Bed?

**Orthopedic support:** Memory foam or egg-crate foam relieves pressure on hips, elbows, and joints — especially important for dogs over 7 years old, large breeds prone to hip dysplasia (Labs, German Shepherds, Golden Retrievers), and post-surgery recovery.

**Washable construction:** Dogs track in mud, shed constantly, and have accidents. Beds with removable, machine-washable covers are significantly easier to maintain than fixed-cover beds. Look for covers that zip off cleanly and hold up to frequent washing without pilling.

**Size and fill depth:** Large breed dogs need beds with sufficient foam depth (3–4 inches minimum) to actually support their weight. A 150 lb Great Dane on a 2-inch foam pad is essentially sleeping on the floor.

**Waterproofing:** A waterproof inner liner protects the foam from accidents, sweat, and spills — critical for incontinent senior dogs or dogs in training.

**Bolsters and raised edges:** Some dogs prefer bolsters (raised sides) to rest their head on and feel secure. Others prefer flat mats. Know your dog's sleep style before buying.

## FAQ

**Q: How often should I wash my dog's bed?**
A: Wash the cover every 2–4 weeks, or immediately after accidents. Foam inserts can be spot-cleaned or aired out — most should not go in a washing machine.

**Q: How long do dog beds last?**
A: Quality orthopedic foam beds last 3–5 years before the foam loses its support. Budget fiber-fill beds may need replacement in 1–2 years.

**Q: Are raised dog beds or floor beds better for large dogs?**
A: Raised (cot-style) beds improve airflow and are easier for arthritic dogs to get up from. Orthopedic foam floor beds provide more support for joint relief.
