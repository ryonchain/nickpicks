## Wi-Fi vs. Dedicated Frequency — Pros and Cons

One of the first decisions you face is whether to go with a Wi-Fi monitor or a dedicated FHSS (Frequency Hopping Spread Spectrum) monitor. Each has real trade-offs worth understanding.

**Wi-Fi Monitors** connect to your home network and stream video through a companion app on your phone. The upside is remote viewing from anywhere in the world — you can check on your baby from the office, a restaurant, or another country. They also tend to support higher-resolution video, two-way audio, and smart home integrations with Alexa or Google Assistant. The downside: they depend on your router's reliability, introduce potential security vulnerabilities if not properly encrypted, and can lag or drop during network congestion. If your internet goes down, so does your monitor.

**Dedicated Frequency (FHSS/DECT) Monitors** create a direct, encrypted radio link between the camera and a handheld parent unit. There is no app, no internet dependency, and no monthly subscription. The signal is typically more stable in the immediate vicinity of your home, and latency is extremely low. The limitation is range — you are restricted to your home and immediate surroundings — and you cannot check in remotely. For parents who prioritize security and simplicity, dedicated frequency monitors remain the gold standard.

**Bottom line:** If remote access and smart features matter to you, go Wi-Fi. If reliability, privacy, and zero subscriptions are your priorities, choose a dedicated frequency monitor.

## Buying Guide — What to Look For

### Video Quality and Night Vision
Look for at least 720p resolution and infrared night vision that automatically activates in low light. Some monitors like the Nanit Pro offer color night vision, which makes it easier to assess skin tone and see if your baby has kicked off their blanket.

### Wi-Fi vs. Dedicated Frequency
As covered above, your lifestyle dictates this choice. Frequent travelers or parents who want remote access need Wi-Fi. Parents who prioritize stability and privacy should lean toward dedicated frequency.

### Range and Signal Reliability
Dedicated monitors typically advertise 1,000 ft of range, which covers most homes and yards. For large houses or those with thick walls, check user reviews specifically mentioning range issues before buying.

### Battery Life on the Parent Unit
This matters more than most people realize. Short battery life means constantly hunting for a charger at 2 AM. The Eufy SpaceView's 30-hour battery is exceptional; most others fall in the 8-12 hour range.

### Subscription Costs
Some of the best features on Wi-Fi monitors are locked behind monthly subscriptions. Factor ongoing costs into your budget — a $300 monitor with a $10/month subscription costs $420 in the first year alone.

## FAQ

**Q: Are Wi-Fi baby monitors safe from hackers?**
Any internet-connected device carries some security risk. Choose monitors from reputable brands that use end-to-end encryption (like Nanit or Owlet), enable two-factor authentication on your account, and keep your home router firmware updated.

**Q: Do I need a monitor with breathing tracking?**
For healthy full-term newborns, standard video monitoring is sufficient according to most pediatric guidelines. Breathing motion tracking (Nanit) or pulse ox monitoring (Owlet) adds reassurance, particularly for anxious parents, premature babies, or infants with specific medical histories. Always consult your pediatrician.

**Q: How far can baby monitors reach?**
Dedicated frequency monitors typically reach 800-1,000 feet in open air, but walls reduce this significantly. Wi-Fi monitors have effectively unlimited range as long as both devices have internet access.

**Q: Can I use a regular security camera as a baby monitor?**
Technically yes — cameras like the Wyze Cam work in a pinch. But purpose-built baby monitors offer two-way audio, lullaby playback, temperature alerts, and parent-unit screens that security cameras do not include. For a primary nursery solution, a dedicated baby monitor is the better investment.
