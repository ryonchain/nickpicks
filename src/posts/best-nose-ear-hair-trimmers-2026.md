## Why the Right Trimmer Matters

Nose and ear hair removal seems simple — but the wrong tool causes pulling, cuts, and ingrown hairs. Here's why it matters:

**Scissors:** Work but require good lighting and a steady hand. Easy to nick the inside of the nostril.

**Tweezers:** Effective but painful — pulling nose or ear hair removes the root and can cause ingrown hairs and infection.

**Wax:** Works for nose hair but risks wax going too deep. Not recommended by dermatologists.

**Rotary trimmers:** The right tool. A circular blade rotates inside a protective guard — it trims hair to the guard length without touching skin. Safe, fast, painless.

## How to Use a Nose Trimmer Properly

1. **Good lighting:** Natural light or a well-lit mirror — you need to see what you're doing.
2. **Tilt your head back slightly:** This gives the trimmer a clear path into the nostril.
3. **Insert 1/4–1/2 inch:** The trimmer tip should only go in far enough to trim visible growth at the nostril opening.
4. **Rotate slowly:** Let the trimmer do the work. Don't force depth.
5. **Ear hair:** Pull the ear lobe gently downward to open the ear canal opening. Trim only visible hair, never insert the trimmer deeply.
6. **Rinse after:** Rinse the trimmer head under running water immediately after use to prevent hair buildup.
