## Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Humidifier

### Cool Mist vs. Warm Mist
**Cool mist (ultrasonic):**
- More energy-efficient
- No burn risk — safe for children
- Requires more frequent cleaning to prevent bacteria
- Can produce white mineral dust in hard water areas

**Warm mist (steam):**
- Naturally sterile output
- Better for cold/congestion relief
- Higher energy use (heating element)
- Burns risk — not for children

**Verdict:** Most people: cool mist ultrasonic. For cold season congestion: warm mist or a dual-mode unit.

### Tank Size and Run Time
Match tank size to your filling frequency preference:
- **0.5 gal:** Fill every 1–2 days (small bedrooms)
- **1 gal:** Fill every 2–3 days (medium bedrooms)
- **1.5 gal:** Fill every 3–4 days (large bedrooms or infrequent refilling)

### White Dust Problem
Ultrasonic humidifiers vibrate water into mist — including dissolved minerals. Hard water produces white dust on surfaces. Solutions:
- Use distilled water (eliminates white dust completely)
- Buy evaporative humidifier (keeps minerals in filter)
- Use demineralization cartridges (some Levoit models include these)

### Ideal Bedroom Humidity
40–60% relative humidity is the healthy range. Below 30%: dry skin, static electricity, sinus irritation. Above 65%: mold growth risk. A humidistat (built into Levoit LV600HH) maintains optimal levels automatically.

### Cleaning
The #1 maintenance requirement. Clean tanks weekly with a white vinegar rinse to prevent mineral buildup and bacterial/mold growth. Neglected humidifiers spread mold spores — the opposite of the intended health benefit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do humidifiers help with dry skin?**
Yes. Increasing indoor humidity from 20% (typical heated winter air) to 40–60% significantly reduces transepidermal water loss — the main cause of dry skin in winter. Consistent overnight humidification shows measurable skin hydration improvement within days.

**Are cool mist humidifiers safe for babies and toddlers?**
Cool mist (ultrasonic or evaporative) is the recommended type for rooms with children — no burn risk from hot steam. Keep the unit out of reach and clean it religiously. AAP recommends humidifiers to maintain 40–60% humidity in baby rooms.

**How do I know what humidity level to set?**
The sweet spot is 40–60% relative humidity. Higher than 60% encourages mold and dust mites. Under 30% causes dry air symptoms. A cheap ($10) hygrometer tells you your room's current humidity so you can set the humidifier appropriately.

**Do humidifiers help with snoring?**
Yes, in many cases. Dry air dries out nasal passages, causing swelling that restricts airflow and worsens snoring. Humidified air keeps nasal tissue moist and reduces snoring in dryness-related cases. Not effective for snoring caused by anatomy or sleep apnea.

**How often should I clean my humidifier?**
Weekly for light use, every 3 days for daily use. Use white vinegar and water to dissolve mineral deposits. Rinse with clean water before refilling. Never let standing water sit in the tank for more than 2–3 days without cleaning.
