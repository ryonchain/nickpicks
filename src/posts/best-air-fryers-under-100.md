## Basket vs. Oven-Style: Which Air Fryer Type?

**Basket air fryers** are the original style — a pull-out drawer with a perforated basket inside. Food sits in the basket and hot air circulates from above and the sides. They're compact, heat up fast, and excel at foods you want crispy all around: fries, wings, nuggets, vegetables, shrimp.

Pros: Fast preheat (3–4 min), compact footprint, easier to shake/toss food mid-cook, simple operation  
Cons: Limited to one layer of food, smaller capacity

**Oven-style air fryers** look like a toaster oven with an air fry setting — and often, that's exactly what they are. Multiple rack positions allow cooking on two levels simultaneously. They're better for cooking larger items, sheet pan meals, toast, pizza, and baking.

Pros: More capacity, fits two trays, handles larger cuts of meat, doubles as a toaster oven  
Cons: Slower preheat (5–8 min), larger counter footprint, less crispy results on foods that need circulated air all around

**Size guide:**
- 2–3 qt basket: 1–2 people, small apartments
- 4–5 qt basket: 2–4 people, most popular size
- 5.8 qt basket: 4–6 people, families
- 10–26 qt oven: 4–8 people, full meal prep

## What Foods Air Fryers Actually Excel At

Air fryers work best for:
- **Frozen foods:** Fries, tater tots, nuggets, fish sticks — dramatic improvement over oven cooking
- **Crispy proteins:** Chicken wings, chicken thighs, shrimp, breaded fish
- **Roasted vegetables:** Broccoli, Brussels sprouts, asparagus, bell peppers
- **Reheating:** Pizza, fries, spring rolls — crispy again vs. microwave sogginess

Air fryers are not ideal for:
- Wet batters (tempura, beer batter) — drips and smokes
- Large roasts — better in a conventional oven
- Large batches — need multiple rounds
