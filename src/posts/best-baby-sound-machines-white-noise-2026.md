## White Noise Volume: The Safety Consideration

The AAP recommends keeping sound machines below **50 dB at baby's ear level** and **at least 7 feet from the baby** to avoid any risk of hearing damage. At the standard 6-foot crib distance, this means a volume setting of roughly 60–65 dB for the machine itself.

All machines on this list have volume controls that allow safe levels. Avoid running any sound machine at maximum volume or placing it directly in or attached to the crib.

---

## White Noise Types: Fan-Based vs Electronic

**Fan-based machines** (Marpac Dohm) use an actual fan to produce mechanical white noise — resulting in a sound that many parents and audiophiles find more natural and less "digital." The trade-off is fewer sound options and no volume fine-tuning.

**Electronic machines** (LectroFan, Hatch) generate white noise digitally, allowing precise volume control, a wider variety of sounds (brown noise, pink noise, rain, ocean, lullabies), and easier operation. Sound quality is excellent on modern machines.

**Our recommendation:** For pure sleep function, electronic machines offer more flexibility. Fan-based machines have devoted fans (no pun intended) who swear by the organic sound.
