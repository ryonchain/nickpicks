## Buying Guide: What Matters in Luggage

### Hardshell vs. Softshell
**Hardshell:**
- Protects fragile contents better
- Easier to clean
- More scratch-visible if uncoated
- Cannot expand to fit last-minute items

**Softshell:**
- Expands to fit overpacking
- Exterior pockets for quick access
- More forgiving under compression
- Absorbs impact differently (deforms vs. flexes)

Most travelers buying a set under $200 are buying hardshell — better durability per dollar at this price point.

### Shell Material
- **ABS plastic:** Most common in budget luggage. Lightweight, affordable, cracks under sharp impact.
- **Polycarbonate:** More durable, more expensive, flexes under impact without cracking.
- **Polypropylene:** Hardest material, used in higher-end budget sets. Best crack resistance.
- **ABS+PC mix:** Common in mid-range — balances cost and flexibility.

### Wheel Type
- **2-wheel (inline):** More durable, rolls better on outdoor surfaces; can't move sideways
- **4-wheel spinner (360°):** More maneuverable, easier in airports; wheels can stick on rough surfaces

For most travelers: 4-wheel spinner wins in airport use. For rough outdoor travel or cobblestones, 2-wheel is more reliable.

### Size Guide
- **20" Carry-on:** Fits in most overhead bins (check airline requirements — some budget carriers allow only 18")
- **24" Medium:** Standard checked bag, 50 lb limit in most US airlines
- **28" Large:** Oversized — may require oversize fees on some airlines

### TSA Locks
A built-in TSA-approved combination lock allows TSA to open the bag for inspection without breaking the lock. Practical if you check bags — standard luggage locks get cut off regularly by TSA.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should luggage last?**
Budget sets ($75–100): 2–4 years with regular travel. Mid-range ($150–200): 4–7 years. Premium ($300+): 10+ years. Frequency of travel matters more than time — a budget set used once a year outlasts one used weekly.

**Are luggage sets worth it, or should I buy pieces separately?**
Sets save 30–50% vs. buying pieces individually. The main downside: you're locked into the same brand's aesthetic and wheel type. If you already own a good carry-on, buying a checked bag separately may serve you better.

**What's the lightest luggage material?**
Polycarbonate and Makrolon (a polycarbonate variant) are the lightest hard materials. For softshell, ballistic nylon is lightest. The Samsonite Freeform above is notable for being lighter than ABS alternatives at this price.

**How do I know if a carry-on will fit in the overhead bin?**
Most US airlines accept 22"x14"x9" maximum. Southwest allows 24"x16"x10". Spirit and Frontier are strictest — check their policy for size limits. The 20" carry-ons above fit most major airline overhead bins.

**What does the warranty cover?**
Most budget luggage warranties cover manufacturing defects (broken handles, wheel mechanisms) but not airline damage. For airline damage, file a claim with the airline within 24 hours at the baggage counter. American Tourister and Samsonite offer 3–10 year warranties that are easier to use than budget alternatives.
