## Screen Time Guidelines for Toddlers

**AAP recommendations:**
- Under 18 months: avoid screen use except video chatting
- 18–24 months: high-quality programming only, with parent co-viewing
- 2–5 years: limit to 1 hour per day of high-quality programming
- 6+ years: consistent limits on time and types of media

The key variable: **passive vs. active screen time**. Educational tablets that require input, problem-solving, and decision-making are fundamentally different from passively watching videos. Interactive learning apps activate the same cognitive engagement as physical play.

## What Makes a Learning Tablet Actually Educational

**Adaptive curriculum:** The best tablets adjust difficulty based on your child's performance, keeping them in the learning "sweet spot" — not too easy (boring) and not too hard (frustrating).

**No ads or in-app purchases:** The moment a learning app can serve ads or prompt purchases, the incentive shifts from learning to engagement. Choose tablets with fully locked-down, parent-controlled environments.

**Parental controls and progress tracking:** Dashboards that show what skills your child has practiced and mastered give parents insight into development and help target areas that need more attention.

**Durability:** Toddlers drop things constantly. Look for drop-resistant construction, corner bumpers, and shatter-resistant screens. A 2-year warranty matters here.

## FAQ

**Q: Are learning tablets actually better than educational TV?**
A: Interactive learning (tablets requiring input) produces better learning outcomes than passive viewing. A child who taps through a phonics app is more engaged than one watching a letters song. That said, co-viewing and discussion during TV can approach the same engagement level. Both have their place.

**Q: When should my toddler start using a learning tablet?**
A: Most learning tablets are designed for ages 2+. Before age 2, focus on physical toys, books, and interactive play. Video chatting with family members is the exception the AAP allows before 18 months.

**Q: How do I choose between a general tablet and a dedicated learning device?**
A: Dedicated learning devices (LeapFrog, VTech) are simpler, more durable, and completely closed environments — good for ages 1–4. General tablets (Amazon Fire Kids) are better for ages 4+ when more content variety and adaptability are beneficial.
