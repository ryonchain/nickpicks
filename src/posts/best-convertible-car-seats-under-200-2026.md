## What "Convertible" Actually Means

A convertible car seat installs in **rear-facing mode** for infants and young toddlers, then **converts to forward-facing** once your child outgrows the rear-facing weight/height limits. Some models also convert to a **booster seat** (highback or backless) for older children — these are called "all-in-one" seats.

**Why rear-facing matters:** The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping children rear-facing as long as possible — ideally until they reach the seat's rear-facing height or weight limit. Rear-facing seats better distribute crash forces across the back, neck, and head.

## Key Specs to Compare

**Rear-facing weight limit:** Higher is better. Premium seats reach 40–50 lbs rear-facing. Budget seats may only go to 35–40 lbs. A higher rear-facing limit means your child stays in the safest configuration longer.

**Forward-facing weight limit:** Most convertible seats are rated to 65–80 lbs forward-facing. Some go to 100 lbs.

**Install type:** LATCH (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) makes installation more consistent than seat belt alone. Most convertible seats support both. Check LATCH weight limits — many LATCH systems are only rated to 40–65 lbs.

**Ease of installation:** A car seat installed correctly is infinitely safer than one installed incorrectly. Look for seats with clear level indicators, easy-to-route harness paths, and straightforward recline adjustments.

**Harness adjustability:** No-rethread harness adjustment means you can raise the harness height without rethreading webbing through slots — a significant convenience when layers change with seasons.

## Car Seat Safety Essentials

- **Never buy a used car seat** unless you know its full history and it has never been in a crash
- **Check expiration dates** — most seats expire 6–10 years from manufacture date (printed on the seat)
- **Register your seat** for recall notifications
- **Get your installation checked** — many fire stations and certified Child Passenger Safety Technicians offer free seat checks
- **Never add aftermarket accessories** (strap covers, head positioners) that didn't come with the seat

## FAQ

**Q: When should I switch from rear-facing to forward-facing?**
A: When your child exceeds the rear-facing height or weight limit of the seat, not at a specific age. Many toddlers remain rear-facing comfortably until age 2–4.

**Q: Can my child's feet touch the seat back while rear-facing?**
A: Yes — feet and legs touching the vehicle seat back is normal and safe. Leg fractures from this position are extremely rare, while rear-facing is significantly protective of the head and spine.

**Q: Do I need to buy a separate infant car seat first?**
A: Not necessarily. Many convertible seats start at 4–5 lbs and can be used from birth, eliminating the need for a separate infant seat. Check the minimum weight limit before deciding.
