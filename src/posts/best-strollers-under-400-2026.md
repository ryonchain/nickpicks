## Stroller Types Explained

**Full-Size Strollers** offer the most seat padding, storage, and suspension of any category. They handle daily errands and longer outings with ease but weigh 18–26 lbs and fold bulkier than alternatives. Best for parents who primarily use a car or have storage space.

**Lightweight and Umbrella Strollers** prioritize portability. Frames often dip under 15 lbs and fold into a compact column. The trade-off is a smaller canopy, less recline, and reduced under-seat storage. They are ideal for frequent travelers and parents navigating public transit.

**Travel Systems** bundle a stroller frame with an infant car seat and a LATCH-compatible base. Instead of waking a sleeping newborn to transfer from car to stroller, you click the carrier directly into the frame. The bundles reviewed here offer exceptional value because buying components separately would cost significantly more.

**Jogging Strollers** use a fixed or lockable front wheel, air-filled tires, and a hand brake to handle running pace and uneven trails. The City Mini GT2 in this list crosses over between jogging and all-terrain without the dedicated running frame, making it versatile for most parents who are not competitive runners.

## Buying Guide: What Actually Matters

**Weight and portability.** If you climb stairs, use public transit, or travel frequently, every pound matters. Set a weight budget before you shop — 13–17 lbs for lightweight, 18–23 lbs for full-size, 24+ lbs for travel systems with included car seats.

**Canopy coverage.** A full-coverage UPF 50+ canopy with a peekaboo window protects your child from sun and light rain while letting you check in without waking a sleeping baby. Look for canopies with ventilation panels in warm climates.

**Storage basket.** Underestimated by first-time buyers and immediately appreciated by experienced parents. Measure the opening width, not just the stated weight capacity. A narrow opening defeats a large basket.

**Folding mechanism.** One-hand folds change your life if you are frequently loading a stroller while holding a child. Test the fold in-store or watch unboxing videos before purchasing.

**Terrain.** Foam-filled and air-filled tires absorb bumps better but add weight. Hard plastic wheels are fine for smooth urban pavement and lighter. Know your daily environment before deciding.

**Car seat compatibility.** If you own or plan to buy an infant car seat, verify adapter compatibility before purchasing a stroller frame. Many brands work within their own ecosystem and offer universal adapters at an additional cost.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a $400 stroller last through multiple children?**
Yes. The UPPAbaby Cruz V2, Baby Jogger City Mini GT2, and Chicco Bravo Trio all use durable aluminum frames and replaceable fabric components. With normal use, all three will comfortably serve a second child.

**Q: What is the best stroller under $400 for twins?**
None of the strollers on this list are designed for twins. Twin-specific strollers (side-by-side or tandem) from Joovy, Baby Trend, and Baby Jogger start around $250 and are the better starting point for that search.

**Q: When do babies outgrow infant car seats used in travel systems?**
Most infant car seats, including the Chicco KeyFit 30, have a 30–35 lb weight limit. Most babies reach this around 12–18 months. The stroller frame continues to work as a regular stroller after the infant seat is retired.

**Q: Are umbrella strollers safe for newborns?**
The Summer Infant 3Dlite and most umbrella strollers require the baby to have independent head and neck control, typically around 6 months. Do not use an umbrella stroller for a newborn unless the product specifically states newborn compatibility.
