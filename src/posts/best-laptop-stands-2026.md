## Why a Laptop Stand Matters

A laptop screen sitting flat on a desk forces your neck down by 30 to 45 degrees. Over a full workday, that angle puts serious strain on your cervical spine and upper back. Research from ergonomics groups consistently puts eye level at the top third of your screen — which typically means raising your laptop 4 to 8 inches above the desk surface.

Beyond posture, stands improve **heat dissipation**. Laptops throttle performance when they overheat, and lifting the chassis off a flat surface dramatically improves airflow underneath. Users regularly see 5 to 10°C drops in CPU temperature after switching to a vented stand.

The ergonomic payoff is real: reduced neck and shoulder tension, better breathing posture (an upright torso opens the chest), and less wrist fatigue when paired with an external keyboard.

---

## Key Specs to Compare

- **Height range:** Look for at least 4 to 8 inches of lift. The more adjustment range, the better you can dial in your eye level.
- **Tilt angle:** Some stands offer 0 to 45 degrees of tilt. Useful if you occasionally need to type directly on the laptop.
- **Portability:** Folding or collapsible designs matter if you move between locations. Weight under 400g is the sweet spot for bag-friendly stands.
- **Compatibility:** Most stands fit 10 to 17-inch laptops. Check max weight rating if you use a large gaming or workstation machine.
- **Ventilation:** Open frame or perforated designs keep thermals better than solid shelf-style stands.

## Ergonomic Setup Tips

A stand alone solves the screen height problem, but a complete ergonomic setup requires a few more pieces:

- **Add an external keyboard and mouse.** Once your screen is at eye level, typing directly on the laptop puts your arms too high. A Bluetooth keyboard and mouse let your arms rest at a neutral 90-degree angle.
- **Sit back in your chair.** Most people hunch forward. Recline slightly and let the backrest support your lumbar spine.
- **Apply the 20-20-20 rule.** Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.
- **Check your lighting.** Position your screen so windows are to the side rather than behind or in front of you to minimize glare.

## FAQ

**Do laptop stands work with all laptops?**
Most stands support 10 to 17 inch laptops. Always check the weight rating — gaming laptops can exceed 3 kg, which pushes the limits of lighter stands like the MOFT.

**Is a fixed-height stand good enough?**
For most desk setups, yes. Fixed stands like the Rain Design mStand are simpler, more stable, and less prone to loosening over time. Adjustable stands make sense if multiple people use the same desk or if you switch between sitting and standing.

**Do I really need an external keyboard with a laptop stand?**
Yes. Once your screen is at proper eye level, the built-in keyboard will be too high for comfortable typing. A $30 to $50 Bluetooth keyboard completes the ergonomic equation.

**What is the best laptop stand for MacBook specifically?**
The Rain Design mStand wins on aesthetics and fit for MacBook users with permanent desks. The Nexstand K2 wins if you carry your MacBook daily.
