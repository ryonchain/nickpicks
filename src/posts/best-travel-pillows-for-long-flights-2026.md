## Buying Guide: Sleeping Position Matters Most

### The Core Problem
Standard horseshoe pillows sold everywhere solve only one problem: head falling sideways. They don't help if your head falls forward (chin to chest) or backward. Most people fall asleep with their head dropping forward — which is why most travel pillows disappoint.

### Match Pillow to Your Sleeping Position
- **Head tilts to one side:** Trtl Pillow, Cabeau, or BCozzy all work
- **Head falls forward (chin to chest):** BCozzy or Cabeau Evolution S3 (with forward-lean attachment)
- **Head falls backward:** Cabeau Evolution with headrest strap
- **All positions or unpredictable:** Cabeau Evolution is the safest choice

### Memory Foam vs. Inflatable vs. Fiber Fill
- **Memory foam:** Most comfortable, takes up more space, doesn't work well in very cold temperatures
- **Inflatable:** Most packable, least comfortable, can fail if punctured
- **Fiber fill:** Soft, more packable than foam, less precise support

### Size Compatibility
Tall travelers with longer necks may find standard-size pillows sit too low. BCozzy and Trtl Plus have adjustable sizing. Cabeau fits most adults comfortably.

### Hygiene and Washing
Travel pillows collect oil, sweat, and airplane germs. Look for removable, machine-washable covers. All picks above except the Klymit Drift have washable covers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why don't standard U-shaped horseshoe pillows work well?**
They only prevent lateral head tilt. Most people's heads drop forward (chin to chest) during deep sleep — horseshoe pillows can't prevent this. The pillows above all address specific forward or backward falling that cheaper options miss.

**What size travel pillow should I get?**
Measure from the base of your neck to your chin — this is your "neck height." Most travel pillows fit adults 5'2"–6'4" well. Taller travelers with longer necks should check BCozzy (comes in large) or Trtl Plus (adjustable).

**Can I use a travel pillow on a road trip?**
Yes — all of these work in a car seat as well as an airplane seat. The Cabeau's headrest strap also attaches to car headrests. BCozzy works well for longer car rides where you might switch sleeping positions.

**How do I clean my travel pillow?**
Most covers are removable and machine washable. Wash cold, air dry — do not machine dry memory foam. Inflatable pillows: wipe with antibacterial wipes, no machine washing.

**Is a travel pillow worth it for a 2-hour flight?**
Probably not for very short flights. The value is on flights 4 hours or longer, overnight transcons, or international flights where quality sleep is the difference between a functional and destroyed arrival day.
