## Buying Guide: Smart Plug Essentials

**Amperage matters.** Most smart plugs are rated 15A (1800W) — sufficient for most appliances. Govee's plug is rated 10A (1200W). Never use a smart plug with high-draw appliances (electric dryers, space heaters over 1500W, window ACs over 1200W) — check wattage before plugging in.

**Energy monitoring is worth it.** If you're curious what your devices actually cost to run, a monitoring plug like the Kasa EP25 pays for itself. A space heater running 8 hours/day at 1500W costs roughly $5–$7/month at average US electricity rates. Knowing this changes behavior.

**Surge protection.** Most single smart plugs don't include surge protection — they're just controlled switches. The Kasa KP303 power strip includes 1440J surge protection. For TVs, computers, and entertainment systems, pair a smart plug with a separate surge protector or use a smart power strip.

**Away mode.** Most smart plugs offer a vacation/away mode that randomly toggles the plug on and off to simulate occupancy. This is useful for lamps when traveling.

## FAQ

**Can smart plugs control any appliance?**
Smart plugs work for most standard appliances up to their rated wattage (typically 15A/1800W). Don't use them with EV Level 2 chargers, electric dryers, electric stoves, or large window ACs that draw more than their rated amperage.

**Can I set a smart plug to turn on at sunset?**
Yes — Alexa Routines, Google Home automations, and Kasa's app all support sunrise/sunset scheduling that adjusts daily based on your location.

**Do smart plugs work without WiFi?**
Most smart plugs require WiFi to function. If your WiFi goes down, you typically lose remote control and schedules may not execute. The physical device remains powered.
