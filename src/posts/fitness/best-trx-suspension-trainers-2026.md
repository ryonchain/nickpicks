# Best TRX and Suspension Trainers 2026: Full-Body Bodyweight Training at Home

Suspension trainers are the most versatile pieces of portable fitness equipment available. A set of straps with handles, anchored to a door, tree, or pull-up bar, enables 300+ exercises — rows, push-ups, pikes, single-leg squats, fallouts, planks, and more — all using bodyweight as resistance. They pack into a small bag and work anywhere.

The original suspension trainer — and still the benchmark — is the TRX. It costs $180–$200. Budget alternatives cost $30–$50 and are functional but feel less polished. Here's how they compare.

---

## What to Look For

**Build quality:** The straps take your full bodyweight repeatedly. Reinforced stitching, locking carabiners, and quality buckles prevent mid-exercise failure.

**Adjustability:** Length adjustment should be smooth and hold position under load. Cheap adjusters slip during rows or push-ups — a significant frustration.

**Anchor options:** Door anchor (convenient, requires a door that opens the right direction), pull-up bar mount, ceiling anchor. Most quality trainers include multiple mounting options.

**Handle grip:** Rubber or foam handles prevent slipping during sweaty workouts. Bare nylon handles cause blisters and slipping.

**Weight rating:** Suspension trainers should be rated for at least 350 lbs for safe training at any bodyweight.
