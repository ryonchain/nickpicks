# Best Cable Machines for Home Gym 2026: Functional Trainers and Cable Towers

Cable machines enable exercises that free weights can't replicate — constant tension through a full range of motion, chest flyes, cable rows at any angle, tricep pushdowns, lat pulldowns, and face pulls. They're what separates a gym from a weight room. The challenge is cost and space: a commercial cable station runs $2,000+; quality home versions start around $500 and go up to $2,000+.

The good news is that the price-performance threshold for home cable machines has dropped significantly in the last few years. Several brands now offer functional trainers under $1,000 that hold up to serious daily use.

---

## Types of Cable Machines

**Functional trainer (dual cable):** Two independent weight stacks or plates, adjustable pulleys on both sides. The most versatile cable machine — cables set at any height for any angle.

**Single cable tower:** One pulley, one weight stack. Usually has high/low cable positions. More compact, less versatile.

**Cable attachment to power rack:** Some power racks have cable pulley attachments. Adds cable functionality without a dedicated machine footprint.

**Resistance band cable alternative:** Bands attached to door anchors or rack uprights simulate cable tension. No weight stack, more limited tension control — but cost $30–$100 vs. $500–$2,000.
