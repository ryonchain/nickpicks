## What to Look For

**Weight capacity:** Aim for 600 lbs minimum. Your bodyweight + the weights you're pressing should have a safety margin. Cheap benches often list lower limits.

**Stability:** A good bench shouldn't wobble or flex during a heavy press. If it wobbles empty, it's a liability loaded.

**Pad density:** Too soft = your body sinks and you lose pressing leverage. Too hard = uncomfortable for long sessions. Medium-firm is correct.

**Adjustment range (FID):** Flat/Incline/Decline. Look for at least 5 position settings on incline. Decline is useful but not essential.

**Footprint:** Some adjustable benches fold for storage — useful for smaller spaces. Folding mechanisms add failure points but are generally fine for home use.
