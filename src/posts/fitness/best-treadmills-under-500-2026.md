# Best Treadmills Under $500 2026: Home Treadmills That Last

Budget treadmills are a gamble. At $300, you get walking-speed machines that struggle to handle regular running and tend to fail within 18 months. At $500, you hit the quality threshold where treadmills start to be genuinely usable for moderate running and deliver reasonable durability. Anything north of $1,000 is where you get serious motors and long warranties.

This guide focuses on what's actually good at the $500 ceiling — and when you should stretch the budget.

---

## What to Look For

**Motor (CHP):** Continuous Horsepower, not peak. 2.0 CHP minimum for walking + jogging. 2.5 CHP for regular running. Below 2.0 CHP, the motor strains and burns out faster.

**Belt size:** 48–54 inches long is standard for running. Shorter belts feel cramped above a jogging pace and can cause you to step off the back. Width should be 18–22 inches.

**Weight capacity:** Budget treadmills often rate 220–250 lbs. If you're above 200 lbs, verify this before buying.

**Incline:** Manual incline (set before you start) vs. automatic (adjusts while running). Automatic incline is better; manual is acceptable.

**Folding:** Most budget treadmills fold. Hydraulic-assisted folding is safer and easier than spring-tension folding.
