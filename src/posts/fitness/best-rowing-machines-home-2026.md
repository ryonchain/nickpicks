# Best Rowing Machines for Home 2026: Air, Water, and Magnetic Rowers

Rowing is the most complete single cardio exercise: it engages 86% of your muscles, loads both legs and upper body simultaneously, and burns more calories per minute than cycling or running at equivalent perceived effort — without the joint impact of running. A 30-minute row is a complete workout. The machine is also low-maintenance and long-lasting compared to treadmills.

The tradeoff is noise. Air rowers are loud (think gym noise). Water rowers are quieter and feel more natural. Magnetic rowers are nearly silent but sacrifice the natural resistance feel.

---

## Resistance Types

**Air:** Flywheel connected to a vent; resistance increases as you row harder. Natural, responsive feel. Loud. Concept2 is the standard.

**Water:** Paddle in a water tank; resistance works like real rowing. Quieter than air. Heavier and harder to move.

**Magnetic:** Magnets create resistance at set levels. Nearly silent. Less dynamic response but perfectly acceptable for steady-state cardio and HIIT intervals.

**Hydraulic:** Least expensive, most compact. Cylinders create resistance. Adequate for basic rowing, less natural feel. Usually found on the cheapest machines.
