# Best Exercise Bikes Under $500 2026: Upright, Recumbent, and Spin Bikes for Home

Exercise bikes are the most used home cardio equipment for good reason: low joint impact, effective calorie burn, and you can use them while watching TV or reading. The challenge is buying one that's actually durable and comfortable — cheap bikes fail at the flywheel, squeak within months, and have uncomfortable seats that kill motivation.

At $300–$500, you hit the quality threshold for a bike that lasts and feels decent to use daily.

---

## Types of Exercise Bikes

**Upright:** Most compact. Riding position similar to a regular bicycle — slightly forward lean. Engages core more than recumbent.

**Recumbent:** Larger footprint, but lower back support, horizontal seat, easier on joints. Better for older users or anyone with back issues.

**Spin/Indoor cycling:** Heavier flywheel, closer to road cycling position. Higher intensity, used for HIIT and structured training. Louder.
