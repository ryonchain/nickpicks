# Best Power Racks for Home Gym 2026: Squat Racks, Half Racks, and Full Power Cages

A power rack is the most important piece of safety equipment in a home gym. Squats and bench press done without a rack — or with a flimsy rack — are genuinely dangerous at moderate to heavy loads. The safety bars on a power cage mean you can train to failure alone without a spotter. A good rack also provides a chin-up bar, band pegs, and a mounting point for cable attachments.

The price range spans $200 (basic squat stands that wobble) to $700+ (commercial-quality cages that handle 1,000+ lbs). At $400–$600, you get the quality threshold where a rack is genuinely safe and stable for serious home training.

---

## Rack Types

**Squat stands (A-frame):** Two independent uprights, minimal footprint. Lower stability than a cage. Not recommended for very heavy lifts or pressing without a spotter.

**Half rack / folding rack:** Two uprights with a back cross member. More stable than stands. Some models fold against a wall for space efficiency.

**Full power cage (4-post):** Four uprights, front and back cross members, safety bars. Most stable. Best for squats, bench, overhead press, and pull-ups.
