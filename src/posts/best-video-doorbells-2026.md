## Buying Guide: Key Decisions

### Wired vs. Battery
Wired doorbells require existing doorbell wiring and offer continuous 24/7 recording options. Battery models require recharging every 1–6 months (depends on traffic volume) but install in minutes — no electrician needed.

### Subscription Costs
Factor subscription into your budget:
- **Ring Protect Basic:** $5/month ($50/year) — 60 days of video history for one device
- **Google Nest Aware:** $8/month — 60 days of event history
- **Arlo Secure:** $5/month — 30 days of history
- **Reolink:** $0 — local storage only

Over 3 years, subscriptions add $150–290 to the cost. Reolink and some Arlo models store locally for free.

### Smart Detection Without Subscription
Ring and Nest require subscriptions for AI-based detection features (person vs. package vs. animal). Google Nest Doorbell's on-device AI provides person/package/animal detection without Nest Aware — the best free tier in this category.

### Ecosystem Compatibility
- **Amazon Echo/Alexa users:** Ring integrates deepest
- **Google Home/Assistant users:** Nest Doorbell is the obvious fit
- **Apple HomeKit users:** Arlo has the strongest HomeKit support
- **No smart home ecosystem:** Any option works; Ring has the simplest setup

### Video Quality and Field of View
1080p is the minimum for clear facial recognition at your door. Look for:
- HDR for high-contrast lighting (bright sky + shaded porch)
- Wide vertical FOV (to see packages on the ground)
- Night vision or color night vision

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do video doorbells deter package theft?**
Studies and police reports suggest visible security cameras reduce theft. The deterrence value is meaningful — porch pirates often avoid homes with visible cameras. Ring's neighborhood network also enables sharing footage with local law enforcement.

**Do I need professional installation?**
Most homeowners can self-install a video doorbell in 15–30 minutes using a screwdriver. The included guides walk through wiring. If you don't have existing doorbell wiring, battery models are the easiest — no wiring at all.

**Can I see my doorbell camera when not home?**
Yes — all smart video doorbells provide live view from anywhere via their app. You can answer the doorbell, see who's there, and speak through two-way audio from anywhere in the world.

**How much Wi-Fi speed do I need?**
Video doorbells need an upload speed of 2–5 Mbps per device. Most home internet connections handle this easily. Ensure your router signal is strong near the front door — weak Wi-Fi is the most common installation problem.

**What happens if power goes out?**
Wired doorbells stop working. Battery-powered models continue until the battery drains. Cloud-connected doorbells lose remote access without internet. Local storage models (Reolink) continue recording to the SD card without internet.
