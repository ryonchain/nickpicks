## Rules for Sub-$10 Gift Success

**Choose consumables.** A gift that gets used up (food, body care, stationery) can't fail. A trinket they don't need adds clutter.

**Think in categories.** A $10 gift from L'Occitane, LEGO, or Pilot feels more valuable than a generic $10 purchase because of the brand quality signal.

**Add presentation.** A bow, a handwritten tag, or tissue paper adds $0 and raises the perceived value of anything. A $7 gift wrapped beautifully beats a $10 gift in a plastic bag.

**Match the person.** A $5 LEGO polybag for a LEGO-obsessed kid is a better gift than a $10 gift card they'll forget about.
