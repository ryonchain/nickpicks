## What Makes a Good Home Clipper?

**Blade sharpness:** A sharp blade cuts without pulling. Most professional-grade clippers use carbon steel or stainless steel blades that stay sharper longer. Consumer kits often use cheaper alloys.

**Motor type:** Rotary motors (used in higher-end models) are more powerful and better at cutting through thick or dense hair. Magnetic motors (entry-level) are lighter and quieter but can bog down on thick hair.

**Guards:** More guard lengths = more versatility. Professional kits come with 8+ guards. Basic kits include 3–4.

**Cordless vs. corded:** Cordless gives you full movement and is better for home use. Corded gives you unlimited runtime — useful if you cut hair for multiple family members in a session.

**Fade capability:** Some blades have zero-gap adjustment — you can close the blade completely to achieve very close fades. This is labeled as "5-star" or "fade" clippers in professional lines.
