## Buying Guide: Smart Bulbs Explained

**Hub or no hub?** Zigbee bulbs (Philips Hue) need a bridge or Zigbee hub (like an Amazon Echo 4th Gen) to function. WiFi bulbs (LIFX, Kasa, Govee, CYNC) connect directly to your router without extra hardware. For small setups, WiFi is simpler. For 20+ bulbs, Zigbee is more reliable and doesn't clog your WiFi.

**Lumens, not watts.** A 60W incandescent produces about 800 lumens. Most smart bulbs on this list are 800 lumens. LIFX is 1100 lumens — noticeably brighter for living rooms and kitchens.

**Color temperature.** "Warm white" (2700K–3000K) is cozy and relaxing — good for bedrooms and living rooms. "Cool daylight" (5000K–6500K) is energizing — good for home offices and kitchens. Tunable bulbs let you adjust between them; color bulbs let you do both plus full color.

**Schedules and routines.** All bulbs on this list support scheduling. Set them to auto-dim at sunset, turn off at bedtime, or gradually brighten in the morning as a wake-up light.

## FAQ

**Will smart bulbs work if the internet is down?**
Most WiFi bulbs lose remote app control during internet outages but continue working with local Bluetooth or hub-based control. Schedules programmed locally on the bulb may still execute.

**Do smart bulbs work in dimmer switches?**
No — standard dimmer switches interfere with smart bulb circuitry and can cause flickering or shorten bulb life. Replace dimmer switches with standard on/off switches when using smart bulbs.

**Can I control smart bulbs when I'm away from home?**
Yes — all WiFi bulbs on this list support remote control via their apps from anywhere with an internet connection.
