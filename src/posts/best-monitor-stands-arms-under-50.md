## Desk Clamp vs. Grommet vs. Freestanding: Which Do You Need?

**Desk clamp:** Mounts to the edge of your desk with a C-clamp. No drilling. Works on desks up to about 2.5 inches thick. This is the most common option and works for 90% of setups.

**Grommet mount:** Passes through an existing hole in your desk (usually the cable management hole). More secure, cleaner look, but you need the right hole in the right place.

**Freestanding stand:** Sits on the desk surface without clamping. Easy to move, no desk requirements, but takes up more desk real estate than a clamp or grommet mount.

For most home office setups, a desk clamp arm is the right call.

## What Makes a Good Budget Monitor Arm?

- **Weight capacity:** Your monitor's weight needs to be within the arm's rated range. Most 27-inch monitors weigh 8–15 lbs. Check your monitor specs.
- **VESA compatibility:** Nearly every modern monitor supports 75×75mm or 100×100mm VESA mounting. Verify before buying.
- **Range of motion:** Tilt, swivel, rotation (portrait mode), and height adjustment. Better arms give you full freedom.
- **Cable management channels:** Good arms route cables internally so your desk looks clean.
- **Build quality at the joint:** This is where cheap arms fail. Look for reviews mentioning sag over time.

## Installation Tips

**Before buying:** Measure your monitor's weight and check the back for VESA mounting holes (4 holes in a square pattern). Most monitors have them — some ultrawide monitors do not.

**During installation:** Don't fully tighten the arm joint screws until you've tested the range of motion. Position your monitor at seated eye height — the top of the screen should be roughly at eye level.

**After setup:** Route power and DisplayPort/HDMI cables through the arm's cable management channels and use Velcro ties at the desk to keep the remaining run neat.

## FAQ

**Q: Will a $30 monitor arm work as well as a $150 one?**
A: For most use cases, yes. The main difference at higher price points is build quality of joints (less sag over years), smoother gas spring mechanisms, and more cable management polish. For everyday home office use, budget arms like VIVO handle the job fine.

**Q: What's the maximum desk thickness for a clamp mount?**
A: Most desk clamps work on desks up to 2.5–3.5 inches thick. Check the specific product listing for the maximum clamp depth.

**Q: Can monitor arms damage my desk?**
A: They can leave small marks if the clamp is over-tightened. Put a thin piece of rubber or felt between the clamp and desk surface to protect the finish.

**Q: Do all monitors have VESA holes?**
A: Most do, but not all. Check your monitor's manual or product page for "VESA 75x75" or "VESA 100x100" compatibility before buying an arm.
