## What to Look for in Backyard Toys

**Safety certifications:** For trampolines, look for ASTM F381 safety standards compliance. Nets, padded springs, and weight ratings matter. Skip any trampoline without a proper safety enclosure.

**Weather durability:** Outdoor toys live outdoors. UV-resistant plastics, rust-resistant hardware, and solid construction determine whether a toy survives one season or five.

**Age appropriateness:** Water tables and sandboxes have a younger age range (1–6). Trampolines extend further (5–14). Buy for where your child is now, with a year or two of growth in mind.

**Setup and storage:** Some outdoor toys can be partially disassembled and stored for winter. Others are year-round fixtures. Know which you're buying.

## FAQ

**Q: How much sand do I need for a sandbox?**
A: The Step2 Naturally Playful Sandbox recommends 200 lbs (about 3 cubic feet). Generic sizing: fill the sandbox about 2/3 full to leave room for digging without overflow.

**Q: Is a trampoline enclosure net actually necessary?**
A: Yes. Full enclosure nets dramatically reduce injury risk by eliminating falls off the edge — the most common trampoline injury. Never purchase a trampoline without one.

**Q: How long does outdoor plastic furniture last?**
A: UV-stabilized HDPE and recycled plastic products (Step2, Lifetime) typically last 5–10+ years with minimal care. Standard polyethylene products fade and crack in 2–4 seasons. Look for UV-resistant materials.
