## Buying Guide: Cloud vs. Local Storage

**Cloud storage** saves video to the manufacturer's servers — accessible from anywhere, but requires a monthly subscription ($3–$10) after free trials end. Ring, Arlo, and Blink all use cloud-based models.

**Local storage** saves video to a microSD card, USB drive, or built-in memory — no monthly fees, no internet dependency. Wyze Cam v3 (microSD), Eufy SoloCam E40 (built-in 8GB), and Blink (Sync Module 2 + USB) all support local storage. Footage stays on-site, which some buyers prefer for privacy.

**Hybrid:** Several cameras support both — Eufy and Wyze store locally with optional cloud backup, giving you the best of both worlds.

## FAQ

**How many cameras do I need for a typical home?**
Most homes are well-covered with 3–5 cameras: front door, back door, garage, driveway, and one interior camera. Start with entry points and expand from there.

**Can outdoor cameras handle extreme cold or heat?**
IP65-rated cameras (most on this list) are designed to operate from -20°C to 45°C (-4°F to 113°F). Battery cameras may have reduced battery life in extreme cold.

**Do security cameras deter crime?**
Research suggests visible cameras reduce opportunistic crimes. Signs indicating surveillance and cameras at primary entry points are most effective.

**Can I view cameras on a TV?**
Yes — Ring and Arlo cameras display on Fire TV and Echo Show devices. Wyze Cam works on Alexa display devices. Arlo works on Apple TV via HomeKit.
