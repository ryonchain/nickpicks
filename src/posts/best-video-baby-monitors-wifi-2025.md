## Buying Guide: WiFi vs. Non-WiFi Baby Monitors

**WiFi monitors** let you view your baby from your phone anywhere in the world. They typically offer 1080p+ video, app notifications, and remote pan/tilt. The trade-offs: they require stable WiFi, some have subscription fees, and they're theoretically hackable if you use a weak WiFi password.

**Non-WiFi monitors** (like the Infant Optics DXR-8 Pro) use dedicated radio frequencies — private, hack-proof, and operational during internet outages. The trade-off: no remote viewing from your phone.

**Security.** WiFi baby monitors are hackable if your home network isn't secure. Use a strong password, enable WPA3 security, and keep monitor firmware updated. Manufacturers like Eufy use end-to-end encryption to protect the video stream.

**Subscriptions.** Nanit charges $5–$15/month for analytics history. Most other monitors (Eufy, VTech, Motorola) have no subscription fees for basic features.

## FAQ

**Can I use a baby monitor with multiple caregivers?**
Nanit and most app-based monitors allow sharing with multiple users (parents, grandparents). Nanit supports up to 8 viewers simultaneously.

**Do WiFi baby monitors work if my internet goes down?**
WiFi monitors lose remote access during outages. Most can still be viewed on the local network via LAN. Non-WiFi monitors (Infant Optics) work regardless of internet status.

**How do I keep my WiFi baby monitor private?**
Use a strong, unique WiFi password on your home router, enable WPA3 encryption, keep monitor firmware updated, and enable two-factor authentication in the monitor's app.
