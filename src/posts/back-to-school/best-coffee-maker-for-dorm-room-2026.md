## What Type of Coffee Maker Works in a Dorm?

**Single-serve pod machines (Keurig mini)** are the most dorm-friendly option. Fast brew time (about 2 minutes), no measuring, minimal cleanup, and quiet enough to not disturb a sleeping roommate. The cost per cup is higher than ground coffee, but the convenience factor is unmatched during finals.

**Compact drip coffee makers** brew 4–5 cups at a time, which works if you drink multiple cups in a sitting or share with a roommate. Slightly larger footprint than a Keurig mini but produce better-tasting coffee at lower per-cup cost.

**Pour-over setups** (a dripper + kettle) produce excellent coffee, cost almost nothing, and require no electricity for the brewer itself — just a kettle or microwave to heat water. Best for students who care about coffee quality and don't mind the manual process.

**Cold brew pitchers** work with cold water and 12–24 hours of steeping — no electricity at all. Perfect for students who plan ahead and like iced coffee.

## Dorm Policy Notes

Most residence halls prohibit:
- Appliances with **open coils** (toasters, some space heaters)
- **Hot plates** and induction cooktops
- Appliances over a specific wattage (typically 1,000–1,500W)

Standard drip coffee makers and Keurig-style pod machines are allowed in most dorms — they heat water internally with enclosed elements and typically draw 900–1,200W. When in doubt, email your RA or check your housing handbook before ordering. Single-serve machines like the Keurig K-Mini (1,500W) and compact drip makers like the BLACK+DECKER 5-cup (550W) are approved by the overwhelming majority of dorm policies.
