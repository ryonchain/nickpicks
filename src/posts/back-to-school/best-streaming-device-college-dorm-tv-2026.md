## Dorm Network Considerations

College dorms use shared enterprise Wi-Fi networks that can be restrictive in ways that affect streaming:

**MAC address registration:** Some universities require you to register device MAC addresses on the network before they connect. Check your IT department's website — there's usually a self-service portal. Most streaming devices display their MAC address during setup.

**Network isolation:** Some dorms isolate devices from each other on the network (no device-to-device communication). This affects casting from a phone to a Chromecast. Fire Sticks and Roku devices aren't affected because they stream directly from the internet, not from your phone.

**Download speed:** Streaming 4K requires 25+ Mbps. Campus networks are typically fast enough, but congested evening hours (9–11 PM) on shared networks can cause buffering. The Fire TV Stick 4K's Wi-Fi 6 support helps in congested environments.

If your dorm TV is near your router or ethernet port, consider a Fire TV Stick with an Ethernet adapter ($15) for a wired connection that bypasses congestion completely.
