## What Type of Printer Should a College Student Buy?

**Laser printers** are almost always the better choice for students who print mostly text — papers, readings, syllabi, and lab reports. Toner cartridges last far longer than ink cartridges, don't dry out if you don't print for weeks, and produce sharper black-and-white text. Laser printers cost more upfront but typically cost less per page over four years.

**Inkjet all-in-ones** make sense if you also need to scan (for submitting signed forms, photographing notes, turning in lab handouts) or if you need color printing for presentations, design coursework, or graphics. Color laser printers exist but cost significantly more.

**What to skip:** basic inkjet printers in the $30–$60 range. They lure you in with cheap hardware and then charge $30 per ink cartridge that runs out in 150 pages. Students who print 100 pages per semester pay more per year in ink than the printer cost.
