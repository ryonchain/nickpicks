## Carrier Types: Which Is Right for You?

**Structured carriers** (SSCs — Soft Structured Carriers) have padded straps, a waistband, and buckle closures. They're faster to put on than wraps, work well for both parents, and typically support higher weight limits. Best for: daily use, dads, parents who want speed.

**Wraps** are long pieces of stretchy or woven fabric that you wrap around your body and tie. They create an incredibly snug, womb-like hold that many newborns love. The learning curve is real — expect a week of practice before it feels natural. Best for: newborns, parents who want maximum snuggle contact.

**Ring slings** are a single loop of fabric threaded through two rings. They're the fastest carrier to put on and take off, making them ideal for quick transfers. Best for: quick errands, older babies who like to be up and down.

## What "Ergonomic" Actually Means

An ergonomic baby carrier supports the **M-position**: baby's knees are higher than their bottom, thighs are supported from knee to knee, and the spine is in a natural "C" curve. This position protects hip development and aligns with how pediatric orthopedists say infants should be positioned.

Look for the **HKKI (Hip-Healthy Institute)** or **Babywearing International** approval seal, which verifies the carrier has been tested to meet ergonomic standards.
