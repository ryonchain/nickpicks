## Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Topper

### Thickness and Depth
- **1–2 inches:** Minor cushioning adjustment, subtle change
- **3 inches:** Meaningful pressure relief, noticeable comfort improvement
- **4 inches:** Significant change — effectively a new feel, best for worn mattresses

### Density (Memory Foam)
- **2 lb density:** Softer, lighter, less durable. Good for lighter individuals.
- **3 lb density:** Balanced — best for most adults
- **4–5 lb density:** Firmer, more supportive, more durable. Best for heavier individuals or those needing more support.

### Materials at a Glance
| Material | Feel | Heat | Durability | Price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Memory foam | Slow sinking | Warm | 3–5 yrs | Low–mid |
| Gel memory foam | Slow sinking | Cooler | 3–5 yrs | Mid |
| Latex | Springy, responsive | Cool | 7–10 yrs | High |
| Fiber fill | Soft, fluffy | Cool | 2–3 yrs | Low |

### Sleeping Position Guide
- **Side sleepers:** 3–4 inch memory foam for hip and shoulder pressure relief
- **Back sleepers:** 2–3 inch medium-firm for spinal alignment
- **Stomach sleepers:** 2-inch firm — thick soft toppers create spinal flexion problems
- **Combination sleepers:** 3-inch medium with responsive foam or hybrid design

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will a mattress topper fix a sagging mattress?**
A topper masks mild sagging (under 1 inch) but doesn't fix structural sagging. If your mattress sags more than 1–1.5 inches, a topper alone won't restore proper support — you'll feel it through the topper after a few weeks.

**How long do mattress toppers last?**
Memory foam: 3–5 years with regular use. Latex: 7–10 years. Fiber fill: 2–3 years before flattening. Proper rotation (flip or rotate 180° every 6 months) extends life significantly.

**Can I put a mattress topper under the fitted sheet?**
Yes — and you should. Running the fitted sheet over the topper holds it in place and keeps it clean. For thicker toppers (4"), you may need deep-pocket fitted sheets.

**Do mattress toppers help with back pain?**
Yes, often. A topper that provides appropriate support for your sleeping position can significantly reduce morning back pain. Back and side sleepers benefit from 3-inch medium density. Stomach sleepers should use firmer, thinner toppers to prevent lumbar flexion.

**What's the difference between a mattress topper and a mattress pad?**
A mattress pad is thin (0.5–1") and primarily protects the mattress from spills and wear. A mattress topper (2–4") is primarily for comfort adjustment. A mattress protector is waterproof and has no comfort function.
