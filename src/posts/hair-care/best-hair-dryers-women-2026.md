## What to Look For in a Hair Dryer

**Wattage:** 1800–2000W is the sweet spot for home use. Above 2000W is professional-grade and dries faster; below 1800W means longer exposure time and more heat stress.

**Ionic technology:** Negative ions break up water molecules faster and reduce static frizz. Most dryers in the $40+ range include this.

**Heat settings:** At minimum, you want high/low heat and high/low airflow — plus a cool shot button to set your style.

**Concentrator nozzle:** Focuses airflow for smoothing. A diffuser attachment is essential for curly or wavy hair.

**Weight:** Most people overlook this until their arm aches after 10 minutes. Under 1.5 lbs is ideal for regular use.
