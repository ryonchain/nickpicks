# Best Purple Shampoo for Blondes 2026: Banish Brassiness for Good

Brassy, yellow tones in blonde hair happen for a simple reason: bleached hair is porous, and warm pigments deposit more easily than cool ones. Purple and blue pigments on the opposite side of the color wheel neutralize yellow and orange tones — which is exactly what purple shampoo does. Leave it on long enough and brassiness disappears. Leave it on too long and you get lavender hair.

This guide covers the best purple shampoos for natural blondes, highlighted hair, bleached blondes, and silver/gray hair — ranked by toning strength, hydration, and ease of use.

---

## How Purple Shampoo Works

Purple shampoo contains violet pigments suspended in a shampoo base. When left on hair long enough, those pigments deposit into the porous hair shaft and neutralize yellow undertones. The darker or more vibrant the purple in the shampoo, the stronger the toning effect.

**Light/medium brassiness:** 2–5 minutes in the shower, once a week
**Heavy brassiness:** 5–15 minutes, 2x per week until corrected, then maintenance 1x per week
**Risk of over-toning:** Any purple shampoo left on too long can turn very light hair lavender — easy to fix by washing with regular shampoo
