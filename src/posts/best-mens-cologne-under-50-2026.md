**Bottom line:** **Acqua di Gio by Giorgio Armani** (often found under $50 for 1oz) is the most universally appealing men's fragrance — clean aquatic with broad appeal for work and casual wear. ---

## How to Choose a Cologne

**Fragrance families:**
- **Aquatic/Fresh:** Clean, light, water or citrus notes. Best for daytime and office wear. Examples: Acqua di Gio, Nautica Voyage.
- **Woody/Earthy:** Sandalwood, cedar, vetiver. Versatile — work to evening. Examples: Polo Blue, Bleu de Chanel (budget alternatives included below).
- **Oriental/Spicy:** Warm, heavier notes like amber, vanilla, spice. Better for evening and cooler months.
- **Citrus:** Fresh, energizing, often light longevity. Best for summer and casual daytime.

**Longevity (from shortest to longest):** Eau de Cologne (EDC) < Eau de Toilette (EDT) < Eau de Parfum (EDP). Most budget fragrances are EDT.

**Application:** Pulse points — wrists, neck sides, chest. Don't rub wrists together (breaks down the top notes). Spray from 3–6 inches away.

## Application Tips

- **Less is more:** Two sprays is usually enough for EDT. Over-application is the most common cologne mistake.
- **Right spots:** Neck, chest, or wrist — never fabric (ruins the scent development).
- **Seasonal swaps:** Light aquatics/citrus for summer. Woody/oriental for fall and winter.
- **Storage:** Store away from sunlight and temperature swings. Bathroom cabinet is the worst location (humidity + heat). A drawer works fine.
