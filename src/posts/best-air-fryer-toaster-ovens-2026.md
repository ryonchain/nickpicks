## Air Fryer Toaster Oven vs. Standalone Air Fryer

A standalone air fryer is a countertop convection oven optimized for small quantities. An air fryer toaster oven adds the same convection/air fry function to a full toaster oven — you get a larger capacity, more cooking modes, and the ability to toast, bake, broil, and roast in addition to air frying.

**Get an air fryer toaster oven if:**
- You already have or are considering a toaster oven
- You cook for more than 2 people regularly
- You want to replace multiple appliances with one
- You have counter space for a larger footprint

**Stick with a standalone air fryer if:**
- You primarily air fry (it's more efficient for small quantities)
- Counter space is very limited
- You want faster preheating

---

## What to Look For

- **Capacity:** Measured in liters (10–32L typical). Larger is more versatile but takes up more counter space. Expect 10–15L for a toaster-oven footprint, 20L+ for something that holds a 12" pizza.
- **Convection fan:** A real convection fan circulates hot air evenly. Many cheaper models have only a bottom or top heating element with no true convection — avoid these for air frying.
- **Interior coating:** Dark non-stick interiors are easiest to clean. Stainless is durable but harder to maintain.
- **Controls:** Digital displays are easier to use precisely than analog dials. Look for individual time/temperature control.
- **Included accessories:** Air fry basket, baking pan, crumb tray as minimum. Wire rack, pizza stone, dehydrating rack for more versatile cooking.

## Cleaning Tips

Air fryer toaster ovens get greasy — regular cleaning prevents buildup that affects performance and can cause smoke.

- **After every use:** Wipe the interior while still slightly warm with a damp cloth.
- **Weekly:** Wash the air fry basket and pans in hot soapy water. Most are dishwasher-safe (check manual).
- **Monthly:** Remove the crumb tray and scrub. Clean the convection fan and heating elements with a soft brush if accessible.
