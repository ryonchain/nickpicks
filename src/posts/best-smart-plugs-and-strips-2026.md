## Buying Guide: Smart Plug Essentials

### Wi-Fi vs. Hub-Based (Zigbee/Z-Wave)
Most smart plugs use Wi-Fi directly — no hub required. These are easier to set up and don't need a separate controller. Advanced home automation users sometimes prefer Zigbee/Z-Wave hubs for more reliable mesh networks, but for most households Wi-Fi smart plugs are the right choice.

### Energy Monitoring
Smart plugs with energy monitoring (Kasa EP25) show real-time power draw and calculate monthly electricity cost. Useful for identifying high-draw appliances, verifying devices are truly off, and catching phantom power drain. Worth the small price premium.

### Matter Standard
Matter is a new smart home interoperability standard that lets you pair a device with multiple platforms (Alexa, Google, HomeKit) from a single setup. Kasa EP25 supports Matter. Wemo Mini uses older HomeKit protocol (still works, just slightly more friction). As Matter adoption grows, it's a future-proof feature to prefer.

### What Can You Control With a Smart Plug?
- **Lamps:** Turn on at sunset, turn off at bedtime
- **Fans and heaters:** Schedule for times you need them
- **Coffee makers:** Start brewing before you wake up
- **Holiday lights:** Schedule automatically without running extension cords inside
- **Old appliances:** Smart control without replacing the device
- **TVs/entertainment:** Auto-shutoff after midnight

### What CAN'T Smart Plugs Control
- Appliances with manual start buttons (washing machines, some microwaves)
- Devices that default to "off" when power is restored (some fans need a manual press after power cycle)
- High-draw appliances over the plug's amp rating (check spec — most are 15A/1800W max)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do smart plugs work without Wi-Fi?**
No — smart features (scheduling, voice control, remote access) require Wi-Fi. Physical button on the plug still works for manual on/off if Wi-Fi is down.

**Can smart plugs save money on electricity?**
Yes, in two ways: auto-scheduling prevents forgetting devices on, and energy monitoring identifies energy hogs. Studies suggest smart scheduling saves 5–15% on standby and always-on device costs.

**Is there a monthly fee for smart plugs?**
No subscription required for Kasa, Wemo, or Amazon smart plugs. The app is free, schedules are free, and all features work indefinitely. Some advanced platforms (like some Wink or SmartThings features) may have optional subscriptions, but the plugs themselves are no-subscription.

**What's the difference between a smart plug and a smart switch?**
Smart plugs work with any outlet without hardwiring. Smart switches replace your wall switch for hardwired lights. Plugs are for lamps and appliances; switches control ceiling lights and hardwired fixtures.

**Can I use smart plugs on 240V appliances (dryers, ovens)?**
No. Standard smart plugs are rated for 120V/15A circuits only. Special smart plugs exist for 240V/30A or 240V/50A circuits (for EV charging, etc.) but are different products at higher price points.
