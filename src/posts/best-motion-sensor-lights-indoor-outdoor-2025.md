## Buying Guide: Motion Sensor Light Essentials

**Solar vs. wired vs. battery.** Solar is the most convenient — no wiring, no batteries — but requires direct daily sunlight to stay charged. Wired provides the most reliable power and highest brightness. Battery is the middle ground — install anywhere, works in shade, but requires periodic replacement.

**Lumens matter.** 200 lumens is adequate for closets and small hallways. 400 lumens covers walkways and steps. 1,000+ lumens is needed for driveways and large areas. 2,000–3,500 lumens is for deterrence and security lighting of large outdoor spaces.

**Motion range and angle.** Most outdoor motion lights detect at 20–30 ft range and 90–180° angles. Position lights so the detection zone covers the approach path, not just directly below the light.

**Dusk-to-dawn vs. motion-only.** Dusk-to-dawn lights stay on all night — great for constant visibility, higher energy use. Motion-only lights activate on detection — lower energy, can miss slow-moving intruders.

## FAQ

**Do solar motion lights work in cloudy climates?**
Partially. Solar lights charge slower in overcast conditions but most have enough battery capacity to last through short cloudy stretches. In consistently cloudy climates (Pacific Northwest, UK), battery or wired lights are more reliable.

**How do I adjust motion sensor sensitivity?**
Most motion sensor lights have a small dial (on the unit itself) to adjust sensitivity and on-duration. Smart lights like Ring adjust sensitivity zones via app.

**Can I install a wired outdoor flood light myself?**
If you're comfortable working with household wiring and there's an existing outdoor junction box, yes — it's a standard wiring connection. If you need to run new wiring, hire an electrician.
