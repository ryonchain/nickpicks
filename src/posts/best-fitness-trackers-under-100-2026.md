## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### What Fitness Trackers Measure
**Standard features on all models:**
- Step counting
- Distance and calories (estimated)
- Heart rate (resting and during exercise)
- Sleep duration

**Mid-range additions (in picks above):**
- Sleep staging (light/REM/deep breakdown)
- SpO2 (blood oxygen)
- Stress level monitoring
- Workout auto-detection

**Premium additions (requires $200+ or Apple Watch):**
- Built-in GPS
- ECG (electrocardiogram)
- Body temperature
- Menstrual cycle tracking (some models)

### GPS: Built-in vs. Phone GPS
Built-in GPS: accurate pace and route tracking without phone. Required for runners and cyclists who leave their phone at home. Adds $50–100 to cost (Garmin Forerunner, Polar Pacer range).

Phone GPS: connects to phone's GPS for tracking. Works fine if you always have your phone on runs. Insufficient for phone-free workouts.

### Battery Life Trade-offs
- **5–7 days:** Apple Watch (2 days) to Garmin and Fitbit (5–10 days)
- **10–18 days:** Amazfit and Xiaomi — use simpler displays and lower processor
- **Trade-off:** Longer battery often means less capable health features or simpler displays

### App Ecosystem Matters
Your data is only as useful as what you can do with it. Fitbit and Garmin have the most comprehensive apps with historical trend analysis, workout recommendations, and health dashboards. Cheaper trackers often have functional but bare-bones apps.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are fitness trackers accurate?**
Steps: generally accurate (±10%). Heart rate: good at rest and moderate exercise, less reliable during high-intensity or wrist-motion-heavy activities. SpO2: less accurate than medical pulse oximeters. Sleep staging: approximate (technology is improving). Good for trend data and motivation, not for medical decisions.

**Do fitness trackers help you lose weight or exercise more?**
Research consistently shows activity trackers increase daily step counts and moderate exercise by 10–20%. The effect is strongest in the first 6 months before habituation. Users who review their data weekly see more sustained behavior change than those who ignore it.

**What's the difference between a fitness tracker and a smartwatch?**
Fitness trackers: smaller, longer battery, health-data focused, no apps. Smartwatches (Apple Watch, Samsung Galaxy Watch): run apps, take calls, GPS, larger display, short battery (1–3 days), more expensive. For health tracking only: fitness tracker. For full phone extension + health tracking: smartwatch.

**Can I wear a fitness tracker in the shower or pool?**
Most fitness trackers in this guide are waterproof to 50 meters (ATM5) — safe for showers and swimming laps. Check your specific model. "Water resistant" alone is not sufficient for swimming. Look for "swim-proof" or ATM5 rating.

**Which fitness tracker is best for sleep tracking?**
Fitbit Inspire 3 has the most detailed sleep staging and insights. Garmin Vivosmart 5 uses Body Battery (a sleep quality metric) effectively. Amazfit Band 7 tracks sleep well for the price. None match the sleep detection accuracy of a Withings watch, but all are sufficient for trend monitoring.
