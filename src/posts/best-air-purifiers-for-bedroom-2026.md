## Buying Guide: Key Concepts

### CADR Rating
Clean Air Delivery Rate — how fast the purifier cleans a room. Match CADR to your room size: multiply room sq ft by 2/3 to get the minimum CADR you need. A 300 sq ft bedroom needs at least 200 CADR.

### True HEPA vs. "HEPA-type"
True HEPA (H11–H14) captures 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger. "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like" is a marketing term with no standard — it may be much less effective. Always buy True HEPA.

### Noise Levels
- **17–24 dB:** Whisper-quiet, comparable to a soft breeze
- **25–30 dB:** Background hum, acceptable for light sleepers
- **35–45 dB:** Fan noise becomes noticeable, typical at higher speeds
- Most purifiers run 24–27 dB on their lowest sleep setting

### Ionizers
Ionizers can enhance particle removal but some produce trace ozone. People with asthma may be sensitive to ozone. The safest choice: buy a purifier with an ionizer you can turn off (Coway, Winix) so you have the option.

### Filter Cost
Factor in ongoing filter costs. Most HEPA + carbon combo filters cost $15–40 and last 6–12 months. Budget $30–60/year for filters.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do air purifiers help with allergies?**
Yes. Controlled studies show HEPA air purifiers significantly reduce allergen concentrations (pollen, dust mite particles, pet dander) in rooms. Allergy sufferers consistently report symptom improvement with daily use in bedroom.

**How long should I run a bedroom air purifier?**
Ideally, run it continuously. Most modern units have low-power sleep modes that cost under $5/month in electricity. Running it only while sleeping takes several hours to clean the air after you've spent the day with open windows or pets.

**Will an air purifier help with COVID or viruses?**
True HEPA captures particles down to 0.3 microns, which includes virus-laden aerosol droplets (not individual virions, which are smaller). HEPA purifiers do reduce viral aerosol concentration in rooms — multiple public health organizations recommend them for high-risk indoor settings.

**Where should I place a bedroom air purifier?**
Near the center of the room, or on a nightstand near the bed (for personal exposure focus). Avoid placing directly against a wall — allow 6–18 inches of clearance around the unit for air intake.

**Are air purifiers worth it if I have central HVAC?**
HVAC filters help with larger particles but most use MERV 8–11 filters that miss small allergens. Portable HEPA purifiers capture significantly more fine particles in bedroom air specifically.
