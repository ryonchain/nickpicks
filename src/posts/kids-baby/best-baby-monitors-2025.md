## WiFi vs. Dedicated Frequency Monitors

**WiFi monitors** stream video to your smartphone via your home network. The advantages are remote viewing from anywhere, app integration, and advanced features like breathing tracking and sleep analytics. The trade-off is dependence on WiFi stability and potential privacy concerns with cloud-stored footage.

**Dedicated RF monitors** (running on 900MHz or 2.4GHz FHSS bands) create an encrypted private connection between camera and parent unit. No WiFi needed, no cloud, no subscription fees. They're simpler, more reliable, and the go-to choice for most parents.

For most families, a **dedicated monitor** is the better daily driver. Choose WiFi if you need to monitor remotely or want AI-powered sleep features.

## What to Look For

**Resolution and night vision:** Minimum 720p with infrared night vision. 1080p makes a visible difference for reading details at night or across a larger room.

**Two-way audio:** Essential for soothing your baby with your voice without physically entering the room — critical during sleep training.

**Pan/tilt/zoom:** Motorized camera movement lets you follow a mobile baby without repositioning the camera.

**Battery life:** Parent unit should hit 8–10 hours on standby. Less than 6 hours means charging mid-night.

**Range:** Dedicated monitors typically cover 700–1,000 feet. More than enough for most homes.

## FAQ

**Q: WiFi or dedicated — which should I choose?**
A: Choose dedicated RF if you want simplicity and reliability without WiFi dependency. Choose WiFi if you need remote access from outside the home or want sleep analytics features.

**Q: Are baby monitors secure?**
A: Dedicated RF monitors can't be remotely hacked — they run on encrypted private signals. WiFi monitors can be vulnerable; use WPA3 security, enable two-factor authentication, and keep firmware current.

**Q: How long will I use a baby monitor?**
A: Most parents use monitors from birth through age 2–4. Many continue through preschool years for overnight monitoring.
