## FAQ

**Q: What makes a toy truly educational vs. just labeled that way?**
A: Look for toys that require active problem-solving or skill practice — not just passive button-pressing. Rush Hour makes kids think through sequences. Sum Swamp requires actual arithmetic. Zingo builds real phonics recognition. "Educational" stickers on toys that just play videos or music don't make the cut.

**Q: How long should educational play sessions be for ages 4–8?**
A: 20–30 minutes of focused educational play is more valuable than longer sessions where kids start going through the motions. Let kids take breaks and return to toys rather than pushing extended sessions.

**Q: Are educational board games better than educational apps?**
A: Both have value, but physical games add social skills (turn-taking, sportsmanship) and screen-free engagement. For ages 4–8, physical games are generally preferred for learning contexts.
