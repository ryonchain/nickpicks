## How to Choose Running Shoes

**Neutral vs. Stability:** If your feet roll inward when standing (flat arches visible), stability shoes reduce injury risk. If you have a neutral arch, stick with neutral shoes — stability shoes on a neutral foot cause unnecessary stiffness.

**Drop:** "Drop" is the heel-to-toe height difference. High drop (8–12mm) suits heel strikers; low drop (0–6mm) suits midfoot strikers and minimalist runners. Most beginners do better with moderate-to-high drop.

**Fit:** Running shoes should fit 1/2 to 1 full size larger than your casual shoes — your foot swells during running. Thumb's width between longest toe and end of shoe is the standard guideline.

**Rotation:** Running the same pair every day compresses the foam before the uppers wear out. Two-shoe rotation extends both pairs 30–40%.

---

## When to Replace Running Shoes

Most running shoes are designed for 300–500 miles. Signs of breakdown before that:
- You feel road impact through the midsole
- New knee, hip, or lower back soreness develops
- The heel counter compresses when you press it (no longer springs back)

**Tip:** Track mileage in Garmin, Strava, or Nike Run Club apps — they'll alert you at your mileage threshold.
