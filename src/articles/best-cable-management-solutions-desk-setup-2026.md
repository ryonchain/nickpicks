## Cable Management Strategy Guide

### The Right Order of Operations

1. **Plan your cable routes** before buying anything
2. **Install under-desk tray** for power strip and bricks
3. **Run raceways** along walls or desk edges for monitor/peripheral cables
4. **Use velcro ties** to bundle similar cables (USB group, power group, audio group)
5. **Cover power strip** with cable box if floor-standing
6. **Use cable clips** to route individual cables along desk edge

### Cable Management by Setup Type

**Minimal Desk (laptop + monitor + keyboard):**
- Under-desk velcro tie bundles
- 1-2 cable clips along desk rear
- JOTO Cable Box for power strip

**Full Workstation (desktop + multiple monitors + peripherals):**
- UPLIFT under-desk tray (required)
- J-channel raceway for monitor cables
- Labeled velcro ties by function
- Cable spine or sleeve for desktop cables

**Studio/Content Creator (audio gear + video + streaming):**
- UPLIFT tray + secondary horizontal tray
- Cable sleeve for desktop to floor run
- Labeled patch panel or USB hub centrally mounted

## FAQs

**Should I use zip ties or velcro ties?**
Always velcro. Zip ties can't be adjusted without cutting, damage cable sheathing over time, and leave a mess. Velcro ties are infinitely reusable.

**How do I manage cables for a standing desk?**
Use a cable management spine (flexible sleeve) between your desk and the floor — it extends and compresses as you adjust height. Pair with an under-desk tray that moves with the desk surface.

**Is cable management worth the time?**
Yes. Studies show visual clutter increases cognitive load. A clean desk improves focus, and proper cable management prevents tripping, accidental disconnects, and cable damage.
