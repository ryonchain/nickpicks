## Switch Guide

### Linear Switches (Best for Gaming)
- Smooth keypress with no tactile bump
- Faster actuation, quieter operation
- Examples: Cherry MX Red, Gateron Red, Razer Yellow, Logitech GL Linear
- Best for: FPS games, fast-paced typing, office use where noise matters

### Tactile Switches (Best for Typing + Gaming)
- Physical bump at actuation point (before bottom-out)
- Better typing feel, easier to register partial keypresses
- Examples: Cherry MX Brown, Gateron Brown, Gateron G Pro Brown
- Best for: Hybrid gamers/typers, people who prefer feedback without clicking

### Clicky Switches (Best for Typing)
- Tactile bump + audible click at actuation
- Satisfying typing feel, the loudest switch type
- Examples: Cherry MX Blue, Razer Green, Kailh BOX White
- Best for: Writers and typists; not recommended for competitive gaming

## FAQ

**Is a mechanical keyboard worth it for gaming?**
Yes — the faster actuation point (1.2–2.0mm vs. 4.0mm for most membrane keyboards), individual switch accuracy, and longevity (50+ million keypresses vs. 5–10 million for membrane) all benefit gamers. The impact on competitive play varies but the durability alone justifies the cost over 2–4 years.

**What's the best switch for FPS games?**
Linear switches with low actuation force (Cherry MX Speed Silver, Razer Yellow, Gateron Pro Yellow) for the fastest response without bottoming out every keypress. Actuation at 1.2–1.5mm with 45g force is the competitive sweet spot.

**Do I need RGB on a gaming keyboard?**
No — RGB is aesthetic. It has no performance impact. Buy it if you want it, but don't pay more than $20–$30 premium for it.
