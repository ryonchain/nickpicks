## Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Electric Scooter

### Range Reality Check

Manufacturer range ratings are tested at 15 mph on flat surfaces with a 165 lb rider. Real-world range is typically 20–30% lower with a heavier rider, hills, or higher speeds. Budget for real-world range = (rated range × 0.75) to find a realistic number.

### Legal Considerations

Electric scooter regulations vary dramatically by city and state. Most jurisdictions limit street/bike path scooters to 15–20 mph. Scooters over 25 mph may require registration, a license, or are restricted to roads. Check your local ordinances before purchasing a performance-tier scooter.

### Weight vs Range Trade-off

For transit users who carry the scooter on buses and trains, sub-30 lb scooters are the practical limit. For parking and riding, heavier scooters often deliver better range, ride quality, and braking.

### Tire Types

**Solid/Foam-filled tires** never go flat but transmit more road vibration. **Pneumatic tires** provide better ride comfort but require occasional flat repair. For city commuting on paved surfaces, solid tires are a reasonable choice for low-maintenance reliability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a license to ride an electric scooter?**
In most US states, scooters under 750W and 20 mph do not require a license for sidewalk or bike path use. Check your state and city regulations — laws vary significantly.

**How long do electric scooter batteries last?**
Most lithium batteries provide 500–1,000 full charge cycles before capacity degrades to 80%. At 1 charge per day, that's 1.5–3 years before noticeable range loss. Avoid charging to 100% daily — charging to 80–90% extends battery life.

**Can I ride in the rain?**
Light rain: any IPX4 or IP55 rated scooter handles light spray. Heavy rain and puddles: IPX5+ recommended. The Xiaomi 4 Pro and Apollo City Pro are the most weather-resistant in this comparison.

**What's the weight limit for electric scooters?**
Budget models: 220–220 lbs. Standard commuter: 220–265 lbs. Performance models: up to 330 lbs. Exceeding the rated load reduces range, performance, and motor longevity.
