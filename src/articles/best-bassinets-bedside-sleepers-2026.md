## Safe Sleep Guidelines (AAP 2022)

Before buying any bassinet, understand the basics of safe sleep:

1. **Always back to sleep** — every nap, every night
2. **Firm, flat surface** — no inclined sleepers (recalled), no padded inserts
3. **Nothing in the sleep space** — no pillows, blankets, bumpers, or stuffed animals
4. **Room-sharing, not bed-sharing** — baby's own sleep surface near your bed
5. **JPMA-certified products only** — confirms the product meets safety standards

The products on this list all comply with current AAP guidelines. Avoid any product marketed for "safer" inclined sleeping — these have been recalled and linked to infant deaths.

## Bassinet vs. Crib: What's the Difference?

**Bassinet:** Smaller, portable, used for 0–6 months until baby reaches weight limit (typically 15–30 lbs). Keeps newborn close to parents.

**Crib:** Larger, stationary, used from birth through toddlerhood. Required once baby outgrows bassinet.

Most families use both: bassinet for the first 3–6 months in the parents' room, then transition to a crib in the baby's room.

## FAQ

**Q: How long can a baby sleep in a bassinet?**
A: Until they reach the weight limit OR can push up on hands and knees, whichever comes first. Typically 3–6 months.

**Q: Is it safe for baby to sleep in a bassinet at night?**
A: Yes, provided the bassinet meets JPMA safety standards and you follow AAP safe sleep guidelines (back to sleep, firm surface, no soft objects).

**Q: Can I use a bassinet during the day for naps?**
A: Absolutely. Bassinets are safe for all sleep, day or night.

**Q: What's the difference between a bassinet and a pack-n-play?**
A: Pack-n-plays are larger and have a removable bassinet insert for newborns. They convert to a play yard as baby grows. Great for travel or as a dual-purpose item.
