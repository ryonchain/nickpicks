## What You Actually Need for Video Calls vs. Streaming

### For Video Calls (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet):
- **1080p/30fps** is sufficient — platforms compress video anyway
- **Autofocus** matters more than resolution
- **Low-light performance** matters if you work in a dim room
- Budget: C920x or Anker C200 handles this

### For Streaming (Twitch, YouTube):
- **1080p/60fps minimum** — motion blur at 30fps is noticeable to gaming audiences
- **Large sensor** — better for variable/dark lighting conditions
- **Uncompressed output** — if you're running OBS, you want what the sensor sees
- Budget: Razer Kiyo Pro or Elgato Facecam Pro

## Lighting Matters More Than Your Webcam

The most common webcam complaint — "I look washed out / too dark / shadows on my face" — is a lighting problem, not a webcam problem. Even a $40 webcam looks dramatically better with a ring light or a lamp positioned in front of you.

The basics:
- Face a window during daytime (natural front-lighting)
- Avoid having a window or bright light behind you
- A $25–$50 ring light transforms the quality of any webcam

## FAQ

**What resolution do I actually need?**
For video calls: 1080p. For streaming: 1080p/60fps or higher. 4K on a webcam is primarily useful for content creators recording locally — most platforms don't stream at 4K anyway.

**Will any webcam work with Mac?**
Yes — all models listed work on macOS without additional drivers. Logitech's software (Camera Settings) adds extra controls on Mac.

**Do webcams have built-in microphones?**
Yes, all models listed include built-in microphones. For video calls, these are fine. For streaming or content creation, a dedicated USB microphone is a meaningful upgrade (see our [best podcast microphones](#) article).
