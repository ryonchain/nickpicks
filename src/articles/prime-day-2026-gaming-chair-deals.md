## Gaming Chair vs. Ergonomic Office Chair

A common question: should you buy a gaming-branded chair or an ergonomic office chair?

**Get a gaming chair if:**
- You like the aesthetic (racing seat bucket, bold colors)
- You game in a reclined position (120–135°)
- Budget is $200–$600

**Get an ergonomic office chair if:**
- You work primarily in an upright typing position
- You sit 6–10 hours daily and have back issues
- Budget is $600–$2,000+

For long sessions, ergonomic chairs from Herman Miller, Steelcase (Leap V2), or Humanscale typically outperform gaming-branded chairs on lumbar support and long-term comfort.

## What to Look For

**Lumbar support:** Built-in adjustable lumbar is better than a pillow. The pillow slides, compresses, and tends to end up on the floor. Look for integrated 4-way lumbar (height + depth adjustment).

**Armrests:** Minimum 4D (height, width, depth, rotation). Cheap chairs have 1D or 2D armrests that don't match your desk setup.

**Seat depth:** Should be adjustable so you can sit back with 2–3 finger-widths between the seat edge and the back of your knees.

**Weight capacity:** Check the spec. Budget chairs claiming 250 lbs capacity often use that number as marketing; check reviews.

**Recline:** Most people prefer 100–110° for focused work, 120–135° for gaming. Full flat recline (170–180°) is a nice feature but rarely used.
