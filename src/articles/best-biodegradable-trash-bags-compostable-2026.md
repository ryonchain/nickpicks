## The Truth About "Biodegradable" Trash Bags

### What "Biodegradable" Actually Means

Any plastic will eventually break down — it just takes hundreds of years. "Biodegradable" has no legal definition in the US and is largely meaningless as a marketing claim. Most "biodegradable" bags break down in 3–5 years under specific conditions of heat, light, and moisture that landfills do not provide.

Some "oxo-biodegradable" bags break down into microplastic particles rather than organic matter — actively worse than regular plastic.

### What "Compostable" Actually Means

Certified compostable bags carry:
- **BPI certification** (Biodegradable Products Institute) — respected US standard
- **ASTM D6400 compliance** — the technical specification BPI uses
- **EN 13432** — European equivalent, widely accepted

These certify that the material breaks down in industrial composting conditions (58°C/136°F, controlled humidity, specific microorganisms). This is NOT:
- Home composting (most compost piles don't reach needed temperatures)
- Landfill conditions (oxygen-deprived, insufficient microbial activity)
- Ocean or soil (certified compostable bags can still harm marine environments)

### The Best Use Cases

**Use compostable bags for:**
- Lining a countertop compost collector headed to municipal organics pickup
- City programs that explicitly accept compostable bags (check your city's program)
- Backyard compost collection when you have a hot composting setup

**Use recycled-content bags for:**
- Regular household trash headed to landfill
- Any waste stream without composting access

**Skip greenwashed options:**
- Bags marketed as "biodegradable" without certification
- Oxo-degradable bags (fragment into microplastics)
- "Plant-based" claims without composting certification

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I put certified compostable bags in my home compost bin?

Most home compost systems don't get hot enough to break down certified compostable bags on any reasonable timeline (months vs. years). Use them in a countertop caddie for municipal pickup rather than trying to compost them at home. If you have a hot compost setup that regularly reaches 130°F+, some brands will break down in 3–6 months.

### Do compostable bags smell worse than plastic?

Some users notice a slight earthy smell from the plant-based material — more noticeable in warm weather or when used for wet food waste. It's generally not unpleasant, and using the bags for compostable waste specifically (rather than all trash) keeps the smell contained.

### Are recycled-content trash bags as strong as conventional bags?

Yes — the 65% recycled content in Seventh Generation bags doesn't meaningfully change performance. Recycled polyethylene has the same functional properties as virgin polyethylene for trash bag use.

### How long do compostable bags last before they start breaking down?

Stored at room temperature in dry conditions, most compostable bags last 12–18 months before degradation begins. Store them in a cool, dry location and buy quantities you'll use within a year. Don't store them in humid basements or hot garages.

### Does my city accept compostable bags in organics pickup?

This varies significantly by city. Many programs don't accept any bags in the organics bin (dump loose). Some accept BPI-certified bags specifically. Check your city's waste management website for your local program's exact requirements before buying.
