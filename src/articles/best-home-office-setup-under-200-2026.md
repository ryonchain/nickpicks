## How to Build This Setup in Order

1. **Buy the monitor riser first.** Set it up, adjust your monitor height, and notice the posture improvement immediately.
2. **Add the USB-C hub.** Connect your keyboard, mouse, and webcam through the hub. One cable from hub to laptop.
3. **Connect your keyboard and mouse.** Use the nano-receiver in one hub USB-A port.
4. **Mount the webcam.** Clip to the top of your monitor, align to eye level.
5. **Lay the desk mat.** Move everything to position, let the mat set your desk layout.
6. **Run the cable sleeve.** Bundle floor-to-desk cables from hub, webcam, and monitor.
7. **Add smart plugs.** Put the desk lamp and secondary power strip on smart plugs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you build a functional home office setup for under $200?**
Yes. A monitor riser ($25), wireless keyboard and mouse ($30), USB-C hub ($40), webcam ($70), and desk mat ($20) covers the five core categories for a functional, ergonomic, video-ready home office.

**What should I buy first for a home office under $200?**
Start with ergonomics: a monitor stand riser ($25) and a keyboard and mouse ($30) fix posture before anything else. Add a webcam ($70) if you're on video calls daily.

**Is the Logitech C920 still worth buying in 2026?**
Yes. The C920 delivers 1080p/30fps with autofocus and stereo microphones at around $70, which still beats most laptop cameras by a large margin. It's the baseline webcam for professional video calls.

**What's the difference between a monitor riser and a monitor arm?**
A monitor riser ($20–$30) is a fixed-height platform that lifts your screen 4–5 inches — best for most stationary setups. A monitor arm ($40–$150) offers full adjustability and frees up desk space, but costs more.

**Do I need a USB-C hub for my home office?**
If you're using a laptop as your main computer, almost certainly yes. Modern laptops strip ports to 1–2 USB-C connections. A 7-in-1 hub adds HDMI, USB-A, SD card, and power passthrough for around $40.
