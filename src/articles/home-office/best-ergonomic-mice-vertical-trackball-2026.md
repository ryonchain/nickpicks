# Best Ergonomic Mice 2026: Vertical, Trackball, and Standard Picks

A standard mouse keeps your palm face-down on the desk for hours, creating constant forearm pronation — a twisted position that stresses the tendons and muscles of the forearm and wrist. For most people, occasional use is fine. For remote workers clicking through documents, spreadsheets, or design tools for 6+ hours daily, it contributes to forearm fatigue and long-term wrist strain.

Ergonomic mice fix this by changing the grip posture. This guide covers vertical mice, trackballs, and contoured standard designs that reduce pronation and wrist deviation.

---

## Types of Ergonomic Mice

**Vertical mice:** Rotate your hand 90° into a "handshake" position. Eliminates forearm pronation entirely. Moderate learning curve (3–5 days) before speed is fully restored.

**Trackball mice:** The mouse doesn't move — your thumb or fingers roll a ball instead. Eliminates wrist movement almost entirely. The biggest adjustment in cursor control.

**Contoured ergonomic mice:** Standard movement but with a shape designed for a more natural grip — often an asymmetrical body that supports the palm and reduces lateral deviation.
