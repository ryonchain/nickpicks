# Best Ergonomic Keyboards 2026: Reduce Wrist Strain for Remote Workers

Standard keyboards force your hands into an unnatural inward-rotated position — palms face down, wrists bent toward the center. For most people this causes no issues for an hour of typing. For remote workers doing 6–10 hours daily, it's a slow accumulation of strain that manifests as wrist pain, forearm aches, and in chronic cases, repetitive strain injury (RSI).

Ergonomic keyboards address this by repositioning your hands in a more neutral posture. This guide covers the best options at every budget and commitment level.

---

## Types of Ergonomic Keyboards

**Split keyboards:** The keyboard is physically divided into two halves you can position independently. Your hands can be shoulder-width apart with wrists straight. The most ergonomic option — and the biggest learning curve.

**Curved/arched keyboards:** A single-piece board that's curved in the center to reduce wrist deviation. Lower learning curve than a split, moderate ergonomic benefit.

**Tented keyboards:** Angled so the inside edge is raised, rotating your forearms into a more neutral position. Often combined with split design.

**Low-profile mechanical keyboards:** Thinner keys reduce the wrist extension required. Not ergonomic in the split/tented sense but better than thick keycaps for some users.
