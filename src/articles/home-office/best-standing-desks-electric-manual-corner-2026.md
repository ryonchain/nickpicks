## Buying Guide

### Electric vs. Manual: Which Should You Choose?

**Choose electric if:**
- You transition 3+ times per day
- Multiple people share the desk
- You have back pain that requires frequent position changes
- You want programmable height memory

**Choose manual crank if:**
- You transition once or twice daily
- Budget is the primary concern
- You prefer mechanical simplicity (no motor to fail)

The real-world cost difference is ~$150–200 for comparable build quality.

### Height Range: What Matters for Your Body

For standing height, most ergonomics guides recommend elbows at ~90° with hands on the keyboard. Typical standing heights:
- 5'4" and under: 36–39 inches is comfortable
- 5'5"–5'11": 39–44 inches
- 6'0" and above: 44–48+ inches

Check maximum standing height carefully if you're over 6 feet tall — some budget desks cap at 45 inches, which forces a slight shoulder hunch.

### Frame Stability: The Most Important Spec Nobody Checks

A desk that wobbles at standing height is the most common complaint in standing desk reviews. Look for:
- **Dual-motor frames** — significantly more stable than single-motor
- **3-stage leg telescoping** — adds rigidity vs. 2-stage
- **Cross-beam** or lateral support bar — especially for wider desktops

If you're mounting monitors on an arm (rather than a stand), stability matters even more — any movement at the base amplifies at the top of a 24-inch arm.

### Desktop Size: What Most People Underestimate

A 48×24-inch desktop feels cramped with two monitors plus a keyboard. Most ergonomics setups work best with 60×30 inches minimum. For an L-shaped desk, target 60 inches on the primary side.

## FAQ

### How long do standing desks last?

Quality frames last 10–15+ years. The motor is the vulnerable component — premium brands like Uplift offer lifetime frame warranties and 5–10 year motor warranties. Budget desks typically cover 2–5 years on motors.

### Do I need an anti-fatigue mat with a standing desk?

Yes. Standing on a hard floor for 30+ minutes daily without cushioning contributes to foot and knee fatigue. An anti-fatigue mat (see our [Best Standing Desk Mats guide](/articles/best-standing-desk-mats)) dramatically reduces discomfort.

### Can a standing desk improve back pain?

For most people with lower back pain from prolonged sitting: yes, meaningfully. The benefit comes from alternating positions, not from standing all day. Prolonged standing without movement can actually worsen lower back discomfort.

### What's the right standing-to-sitting ratio?

Research suggests a 1:1 to 1:2 ratio (one hour standing per 1–2 hours sitting) is sustainable and beneficial. Most sit-stand reminder apps default to 30-minute intervals, which is a good starting point.
