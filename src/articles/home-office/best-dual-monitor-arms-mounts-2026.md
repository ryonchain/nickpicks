# Best Monitor Arms & Dual Monitor Mounts 2026: Single, Dual, and Ultrawide Picks

Monitor stands eat desk space. The average monitor stand takes 8–12 inches of desk depth — space that could be workarea, cable management, or nothing at all. A monitor arm frees that real estate, lets you position screens at the exact ergonomic height, and makes dual-monitor or multi-monitor setups dramatically cleaner.

This guide covers single-arm, dual-arm, and ultrawide monitor mounts at every budget.

---

## Before You Buy: Check These Numbers

1. **Monitor weight:** Most arms support 7–25 lbs. Weigh your monitor or check its spec sheet.
2. **VESA mount:** Confirm your monitor has a VESA 75×75 or 100×100 mounting pattern on the back.
3. **Desk thickness:** Most clamps work on desks 0.4–3.1 inches thick. Measure yours. Grommet mounts work on any desk with a 0.4"+ hole.
4. **Desk depth:** Arms extend 15–25 inches from the desk mount point. A too-shallow desk may not allow full range of motion.

## Monitor Arm vs. Built-In Stand: Is It Worth It?

Monitor arms pay off quickly:
- **Ergonomics:** Precise height positioning for neck alignment. Monitor stands rarely offer this.
- **Desk space:** Free up 8–12 inches of depth per monitor.
- **Flexibility:** Quickly switch between landscape and portrait, or share a monitor.
- **Cable management:** Route cables through the arm for a clean desk.

If your monitor is 14+ lbs or you have a glass desk, verify weight capacity and grommet compatibility before buying.
