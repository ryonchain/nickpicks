## Diamond Stud Earring Buying Guide

### The Four Cs for Stud Earrings

**Cut:** For round diamond studs, aim for "Excellent" or "Very Good" cut — this determines sparkle more than any other factor. Avoid "Good" or lower for any stud you'll wear frequently.

**Clarity:** For studs under 0.30 ct per ear, I1 clarity is eye-clean (inclusions invisible to the naked eye at normal viewing distance). For larger studs (0.50 ct+), go to SI1–VS2 to stay eye-clean.

**Color:** H–I is the sweet spot for yellow gold settings — the warm gold masks any slight yellow in the stone. For white gold or platinum, go D–G for a truly colorless appearance.

**Carat:** Per-ear carat weight (not total) is how professionals refer to stud size. 0.10–0.25 ct per ear = delicate/everyday. 0.50 ct per ear = visible and elegant. 1 ct per ear = statement jewelry.

### Lab vs. Natural Diamonds

**Lab-grown diamonds** are identical to mined diamonds in every measurable property (hardness, refractive index, chemical composition). They are certified by the same organizations (GIA, IGI). They cost 60–80% less than comparable natural diamonds.

**Natural (mined) diamonds** retain more resale and investment value, particularly for rare colors and large sizes. For studs under 1 ct, the resale value difference is minimal.

For maximum beauty per dollar: choose lab-grown. For sentimental or investment reasons: choose natural.

### Screw-Back vs. Push-Back Posts

**Screw-backs** (rotate to lock) are more secure — a strong choice for valuable studs or earrings worn during activity. The trade-off: slightly more effort to put on and remove.

**Push-backs** (butterfly backs) are easier to use but have a higher rate of accidental loss. Fine for lower-value studs worn primarily for dressy occasions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size diamond studs are most popular?**
0.25 ct total weight (0.125 ct per ear) is the most popular size for everyday delicate studs. 0.50 ct total (0.25 ct per ear) is the most popular size for a visible, versatile stud. 1 ct total (0.50 ct per ear) is the aspirational size for a statement piece.

**Are lab-grown diamond studs worth buying?**
Absolutely. Lab diamonds are physically identical to natural diamonds and cost 60–80% less. For earrings specifically — where resale value rarely matters — lab diamonds allow you to buy significantly higher carat weight and quality for the same budget. Most people cannot tell the difference visually.

**How do you clean diamond stud earrings at home?**
Soak for 15 minutes in a small bowl of warm water mixed with a few drops of mild dish soap. Use a soft toothbrush to gently scrub around the prongs and behind the stone where grime accumulates. Rinse under clean water and dry with a lint-free cloth. Do this monthly for brilliant, like-new sparkle.
