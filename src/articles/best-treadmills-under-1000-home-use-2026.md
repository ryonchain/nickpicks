## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Home Treadmill

### Motor: CHP vs. HP

Look for **continuous horsepower (CHP)** ratings, not peak horsepower. A 2.5 CHP motor runs continuously at that power — a "3.0 HP peak" motor may only sustain 2.0 CHP. For walking: 2.0+ CHP. For jogging: 2.5+ CHP. For sustained running: 3.0+ CHP.

### Belt Size

Minimum recommended belt dimensions:
- **Walking:** 16" wide × 46" long
- **Jogging:** 18" wide × 50" long
- **Running:** 20" wide × 55" long

Taller runners (6'+ feet) need at least 54-inch belt length.

### Incline

More incline means more calorie burn and hill training capability. 10% is standard, 15% is excellent. Under-desk treadmills often have fixed incline (no adjustment).

### Warranty

Minimum to look for:
- Frame: Lifetime
- Motor: 5+ years (or lifetime)
- Parts: 2+ years
- Labor: 1+ year

Horizon's lifetime frame/motor warranty at $699 is the best value in this category.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a $700 treadmill good enough for serious running?**
Yes — the Horizon T101 and NordicTrack T 6.5 Si both handle 45–60 minute running sessions without issue. You'll feel the difference vs. a $3,000 commercial machine, but the gap is smaller than you'd expect.

**How loud are home treadmills?**
Motor noise runs 60–75 dB at running speeds, similar to a conversation. Impact noise (footfall) depends on your flooring — a treadmill mat reduces transmitted sound to downstairs neighbors significantly.

**Do I need a treadmill mat?**
Strongly recommended. A mat protects your floor, reduces vibration and noise, and prevents dust from entering the motor. Standard mats cost $20–$40.
