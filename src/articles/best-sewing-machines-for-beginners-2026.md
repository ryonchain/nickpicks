## What to Look for in a Beginner Sewing Machine

**Automatic Needle Threader:** Threading a needle 10 times per session adds up. Every machine on this list above $100 includes one.

**Drop-In Bobbin:** Front-load bobbins (like on vintage machines) require more fiddling. Drop-in top bobbins are much easier to load correctly.

**Built-In Stitches:** You need maybe 6–10 stitches for 90% of beginner projects. More stitches = more creativity later.

**Free Arm:** The free arm lets you sew cuffs, sleeves, and pant hems by removing part of the machine base. Standard on all machines above.

**Machine Weight:** Lighter machines (6–10 lbs) are easier to move and store. Heavier machines (15+ lbs) tend to be more stable on the table.
