## Yoga Mat Buying Guide

### Thickness: What's Right for Your Practice

**1/8" (3mm):** Thin, excellent floor feel, lightweight and easy to pack. For experienced practitioners in studios with padded floors.

**1/4" (6mm):** Most common. Good balance of cushion and floor feel. Right for most beginners and intermediate practitioners.

**1/2" (12mm):** Maximum cushion. Best for meditation, stretching, gentle yoga, floor exercises, or anyone with significant joint sensitivity. Too thick for most yoga asanas.

### Material Guide

- **PVC:** Most common and affordable. Durable. Closed-cell. Less eco-friendly. Very durable if maintained.
- **Natural rubber:** Best grip. Eco-friendly. Requires more care. Not for latex allergies.
- **TPE:** Eco-friendly alternative to PVC. Good performance. Newer material with less long-term durability data.
- **Polyurethane top layer:** Wet-grip specialist. Premium cost. Best for hot yoga.

### Grip vs Cushion Tradeoff
Beginners often think they want maximum cushion — but too much thickness reduces balance and makes advanced poses harder. The 4–6mm range is the sweet spot for most yoga practitioners. Invest in natural rubber or polyurethane surfaces for grip before investing in more foam for cushion.

## FAQ

**Q: How long do yoga mats last?**
A: Quality yoga mats (Manduka PRO, Jade Harmony) last 5–10 years with proper care. Budget PVC mats typically last 2–4 years before the surface degrades and grip fails.

**Q: How do you clean a yoga mat?**
A: Wipe with a damp cloth after each use. For deeper cleaning, use a diluted soap spray (a few drops of dish soap in water) and rinse. Avoid soaking natural rubber mats. Air dry completely before rolling.

**Q: What size yoga mat do I need?**
A: Standard mats are 68" × 24" — enough for most people under 5'8". Taller users should look for 72" × 24" or 74" × 26" options (Manduka and Jade both offer extended sizes).

**Q: Is a non-slip yoga mat necessary?**
A: Yes for any dynamic practice. Slipping mid-pose is the primary injury risk in yoga. Natural rubber and polyurethane tops grip better than standard PVC — worth the investment if you practice regularly.

**Q: Can I use a yoga mat for other workouts?**
A: Yes — yoga mats work well for pilates, stretching, bodyweight exercises, and foam rolling. Thicker mats (6mm+) provide better cushion for floor exercises. Avoid using yoga mats for heavy kettlebell or dumbbell work (weight can damage the surface).
