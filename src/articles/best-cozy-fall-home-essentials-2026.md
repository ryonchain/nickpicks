## Tier 1: Maximum Cozy Impact

### Weighted Blankets — The Cozy Upgrade

A weighted blanket uses evenly distributed pressure (7–20 lbs typically) that creates a "deep pressure stimulation" effect — similar to a firm hug. For most people, this reduces anxiety, improves sleep quality, and makes the act of sitting on the couch feel noticeably more relaxing.

For fall specifically: weighted blankets pair perfectly with the season's indoor-focused, lower-energy vibe.

**Weight guide:** Most recommendations suggest 10% of your body weight. A 150-lb person typically enjoys a 15-lb blanket. 20 lbs feels more like a heavy compression; 10 lbs is a lighter, more subtle effect.

- Multiple size/weight combinations available

## Tier 3: The Finishing Touches

### Hot Drink Station

Fall is the season of warm drinks. A designated spot for coffee, tea, hot chocolate, and cider makes the ritual consistent.

**Kettle:**
- Electric Gooseneck Kettle (Fellow Stagg EKG or COSORI Electric Kettle): ~$25–70 depending on brand
- Variable temperature for tea (175°F for green, 212°F for black/herbal)

**Mugs:** Oversized (14–20oz) ceramic or double-wall insulated mugs keep drinks warm longer. The Lodge Stoneware Mug (~$12) or Yeti Rambler 14oz Mug (~$22) for insulated.

**Hot chocolate:** A small frother (Zulay Milk Frother ~$10) creates coffee-shop hot chocolate at home — froth warm milk and mix with good cocoa.

### Area Rug

Cold floors kill cozy. An area rug in the living room and bedroom adds:
- Visual warmth (texture underfoot)
- Actual warmth (your feet on cold wood or tile is jarring in fall/winter)
- Sound dampening (rugs make rooms feel quieter and more intimate)

**Best value living room rug:** Rugshop Abstract Shaggy Rug (~$40–80 for 5×7) — plush pile, good fall tones available, significant texture underfoot

**Best bedroom rug:** A smaller rug ($30–50, 3×5 or 4×6) placed where your feet land when getting out of bed. No cold floor shock in the morning.
