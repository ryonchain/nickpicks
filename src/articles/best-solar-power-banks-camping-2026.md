## FAQs

**Can a solar power bank actually keep you charged on a multi-day backpacking trip?**
It depends on usage. A 10,000 mAh power bank with a 5W solar panel can keep a single phone alive on a 3–5 day trip if you use your phone sparingly and get 4+ hours of direct sun daily. For heavier use or multiple devices, pair a larger capacity bank with a dedicated foldable solar panel (15W+) for meaningful daily recharging.

**What's the best way to maximize solar charging?**
Angle the panel perpendicular to the sun (not flat), keep it clean of dust and debris, and avoid partial shading — even a small shadow over one panel cell can cut output by 50–80% on monocrystalline panels. Amorphous panels (like the BigBlue) handle shade better.

**Do solar power banks work in cloudy weather?**
Yes, but with significantly reduced output — typically 10–25% of rated wattage under light cloud cover, and as low as 5% under heavy overcast. For reliable emergency preparedness, always keep your power bank fully charged before a trip and treat solar as a backup top-up, not the primary charging method.
