## What to Look For

- **Match capacity to your collection:** Measure or roughly count your pieces first. Undersized jewelry boxes cause jewelry to pile up and tangle — defeating the purpose of organizing.
- **Velvet or felt lining:** Bare plastic or cardboard interiors will scratch metals and stones. Quality boxes use velvet or felt lining throughout — check product photos carefully.
- **Necklace-specific storage:** Long necklaces need hooks or bars to hang; short ones can lie flat. If you have more than 5–6 necklaces, look for a box or organizer with dedicated hanging space.
- **Mirror quality:** Built-in mirrors are useful if they're large enough to actually see your jewelry when wearing it. Small mirrors in box lids are more decorative than functional.
- **Travel vs. home:** If you travel frequently, invest in a dedicated travel roll separately — trying to travel with your main home jewelry box leads to damaged pieces.
- **Gift considerations:** Jewelry armoires and mirrored boxes present beautifully and make excellent gifts for graduations, birthdays, and Mother's Day. Check whether the item ships in gift-friendly packaging.
