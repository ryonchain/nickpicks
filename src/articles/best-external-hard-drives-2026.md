## Buyer's Guide: HDD vs. SSD External Drives

### Choose HDD When:
- You need maximum capacity per dollar (photo archives, document backup)
- Transfer speed doesn't matter (automated backup runs overnight)
- Budget is the primary constraint

### Choose SSD When:
- You edit video or photos directly from the drive
- You move large files frequently
- Durability matters (no moving parts = drop-resistant)
- You work in extreme temperatures or dusty environments

### Speed Comparison
| Drive Type | Typical Speed | Best Use Case |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| HDD (USB 3.0) | 100–150 MB/s | Backup, archiving |
| SATA SSD (USB 3.2) | 500–550 MB/s | Active editing, fast backup |
| NVMe SSD (USB 3.2 Gen 2) | 900–1,100 MB/s | Video editing, content creation |
| NVMe SSD (Thunderbolt 3/4) | 2,000–3,000 MB/s | Professional 4K/8K workflows |

### Capacity Planning Guide
| Use Case | Recommended Capacity |
|----------|---------------------|
| Single laptop backup | 1–2TB |
| Family photo/video archive | 4–8TB |
| Video editor working drive | 2–4TB SSD |
| Network-attached storage | 8–16TB |
| Professional production | 4TB+ NVMe SSD |

## FAQ

**How long do external hard drives last?**
HDDs: 3–5 years of regular use. SSDs: 5–10 years. Neither should be your only backup. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite.

**Do I need a special cable?**
Most drives include their cable. For highest speeds, ensure your cable is USB 3.2 compliant — older USB 3.0 cables may bottleneck SSD speeds.

**Can I use an external drive for PS5/Xbox?**
Yes — any USB drive works for backward-compatible PS4/Xbox One games. For PS5/Xbox Series X native games, you need a specific expansion SSD (PS5: M.2 NVMe; Xbox: Seagate expansion card).

**Should I format my drive for Mac or Windows?**
If you only use one platform, format for it. If you switch between both, use exFAT — compatible with both without performance penalty.
