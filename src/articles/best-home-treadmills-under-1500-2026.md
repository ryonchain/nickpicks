## What to Look for in a Home Treadmill

### Motor Power (CHP)
- **1.5–2.0 CHP**: Walking only. Will strain under jogging.
- **2.5–3.0 CHP**: Everyday runners up to 5K pace.
- **3.5+ CHP**: Serious training, heavy users, long duration workouts.

### Incline Range
A 0–12% incline covers most training needs. Go for 15%+ if you want HIIT incline intervals or simulate mountain training.

### Deck Size
Standard home decks are 20" × 55". If you're over 6'2" or run with a long stride, look for 22" × 60".

### Folding vs. Non-Folding
Folding treadmills save space but add mechanical complexity. Most under $1,500 fold — check how compact the folded footprint is, and whether the hydraulic assist makes it safe to lower alone.

### Weight Capacity
Most home treadmills support 250–325 lbs. If you're near the limit, the motor and frame will wear faster — go to the next weight class.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a home treadmill worth it vs. a gym membership?**
At $900–$1,500, you break even on a $30/month gym membership in 2.5–4 years — but you get unlimited access 24/7, no commute, and no waiting. If you run consistently, it pays off within the first year.

**How much space does a treadmill need?**
Plan for at least 7 feet of clearance behind the treadmill and 2 feet on each side for safety. Most folding models store in a 3.5–4 square foot footprint.

**Do I need a warranty?**
Yes. Look for at minimum: **lifetime frame, 2+ years motor, 1 year parts**. The NordicTrack EXP 7i (10-year frame, 2-year parts) is exceptional for the price.

**What's the quietest home treadmill?**
The Horizon 7.0 AT consistently ranks as one of the quietest treadmills in this price range due to its 3-zone cushioning and variable-response motor.
