## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the quietest desk fan for a home office?**
The Dyson Cool AM06 is the quietest desk fan available, running at 43dB on the lowest setting — quieter than a whisper (about 40dB). The Dreo Nomad One tower fan and Vornado 460 are also notably quiet at low speeds, running around 25–35dB.

**Are USB desk fans powerful enough to cool a room?**
No. USB-powered fans (5V) are designed for personal cooling — they move air past you, not the whole room. For room cooling, you need a fan with a standard AC adapter drawing at least 15–20W. USB fans excel at targeted cooling in air-conditioned rooms.

**What's the difference between a desk fan and an air circulator?**
A desk fan blows air directly at you in a straight column. An air circulator (like the Vornado) moves air in a vortex pattern that circulates the whole room — better at lowering ambient temperature but less effective at personal cooling compared to a fan pointed at your face.

**How loud is too loud for a WFH desk fan?**
For video calls, any fan below 40dB is generally safe — most microphone noise gates and background noise suppression (built into Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet) will mute fan noise. Above 50dB, the fan may be audible on calls. The Dyson AM06 and Vornado 460 are both safe for open-mic calls.

**Is the Dyson bladeless fan worth the price for a home office?**
For WFH professionals who value quiet and air quality, yes. At $300, the Dyson AM06 is significantly more expensive than the Vornado 460 ($40). The Dyson's main advantages are near-silent operation, precise airflow control, and bladeless design that's easier to clean.
