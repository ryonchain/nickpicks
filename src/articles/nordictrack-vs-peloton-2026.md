NordicTrack vs Peloton — two premium home fitness brands that dominate the connected cardio market. Peloton built the category. NordicTrack has risen as a technically strong challenger at a lower price point. Here's how they compare.

---

## Quick Verdict

- **Buy Peloton** if: You want the best live and on-demand class experience, the most established instructor roster, and the premium brand that built connected fitness.
- **Buy NordicTrack** if: You want comparable hardware with a wider range of content (including interactive trails via iFIT) at a meaningfully lower upfront price.

---

## Hardware Comparison: Bikes

**Peloton Bike (~$1,445):** 22-lb flywheel, 32-level magnetic resistance, 21.5" HD touchscreen, 2-channel speakers, rear-facing camera. Industry-defining design that set the standard for connected exercise bikes.

**Peloton Bike+ (~$2,495):** 23.8" rotating touchscreen (rotates for floor workouts), auto-resistance, 3-channel speakers. The premium upgrade.

**NordicTrack S22i (~$1,999):** 22" touchscreen, -10 to +20% incline/decline capability (unique feature), 24 resistance levels, Bluetooth headphone support. The incline feature changes the workout quality significantly — simulating hill climbs adds real challenge.

**Winner:** Tie. Peloton Bike is better value at $1,445 vs S22i at $1,999. But the S22i's incline/decline is a meaningful functional advantage.

---

## Hardware Comparison: Treadmills

**Peloton Tread (~$2,995):** 23.8" touchscreen, 0–12.5 MPH, 0–12% incline, 59" x 20" belt. Premium build quality.

**NordicTrack Commercial 1750 (~$1,999):** 10" touchscreen, 0–12 MPH, -3 to +15% incline/decline, 60" x 22" belt, iFIT-powered. The decline capability is unique in this class, and the 22" wide belt is more comfortable.

**Winner:** NordicTrack 1750 for hardware value. Peloton Tread for software/class experience.

## Content Library and Classes

**Peloton:** ~10,000 on-demand classes across cycling, running, strength, yoga, meditation, and stretching. Instructor-led classes with live leaderboard. 20+ new live classes per day. The instructor roster (Robin Arzon, Cody Rigsby, Alex Toussaint) has developed celebrity-level followings. Best-in-class class production quality.

**NordicTrack (iFIT):** 16,000+ on-demand workouts. Includes Google Maps-powered interactive scenic routes — your bike or treadmill auto-adjusts resistance/incline to match real-world terrain from global trails and routes. If you're into running in the Swiss Alps or cycling in Colorado, iFIT offers that in a way Peloton doesn't.

**Winner:** Peloton for live class community and instructor following. iFIT for content volume and interactive trail experience.

## Subscription Cost

**Peloton All-Access Membership:** $44/month per household
**iFIT Premium (NordicTrack):** $39/month per household (family plan)

Both require subscriptions to access full content. Hardware functions in limited capacity without subscription.

**Winner:** iFIT slightly cheaper.

## Resale Value

Peloton equipment holds resale value significantly better than NordicTrack. Used Peloton bikes sell for 60–70% of original price; NordicTrack equipment depreciates faster.

**Winner:** Peloton.

## Community

Peloton's community is a genuine differentiator. The leaderboard, tags system, high-five interactions, and milestone badges create real social accountability. NordicTrack's community features are less developed.

**Winner:** Peloton.

## Which Should You Buy?

**Buy Peloton Bike (~$1,445)** if: Community motivation, class quality, and instructor loyalty are important. You want the connected fitness leader.

**Buy NordicTrack S22i (~$1,999)** if: The incline/decline feature appeals to you and you want the most challenging stationary bike ride possible, or if iFIT's global routes are more appealing than Peloton's studio format.

**Buy Peloton Tread (~$2,995)** if: You want the premium connected treadmill experience with class depth. Budget is less of a concern.

**Buy NordicTrack 1750 (~$1,999)** if: You want a full-featured connected treadmill at $1,000 less than Peloton's offering, with a wider belt and decline capability.

## Bottom Line

Peloton wins on community, class quality, and brand cachet. NordicTrack wins on hardware value and iFIT's unique content angles. If you've been motivated by Peloton's instructor energy, buy Peloton.
