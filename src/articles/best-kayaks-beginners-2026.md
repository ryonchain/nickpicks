## Kayak Buying Guide

**Sit-on-top vs. sit-inside:**
- **Sit-on-top:** Easier entry/exit, self-bailing, better for warm weather, forgiving for beginners. You will get wet.
- **Sit-inside:** Dryer, warmer, better in cooler conditions and light chop. Requires wet exit practice in case of capsize.

**Length and tracking:** Longer kayaks track straighter (easier to paddle in a line) and are faster, but less maneuverable. For lakes and slow rivers: 10–12 feet. For tighter rivers and ponds: 8–10 feet. For touring and longer distances: 12–14 feet.

**Weight matters more than beginners expect:** Kayak weight affects every car-top loading and launching session. A 50-lb kayak feels fine in the parking lot and terrible after the 200-yard carry back. For solo use without a cart or helper, look for 40 lbs or under.

**Essential gear:** Life jacket (required by Coast Guard for anyone on board), paddle, bilge pump (sit-inside), dry bags for phone and valuables. Budget $50–$100 for accessories on top of the kayak price.
