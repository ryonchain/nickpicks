# Best Impact Drivers in 2026

A cordless drill and an impact driver look similar, but they work differently. A drill rotates continuously; an impact driver rotates and simultaneously hammers in the rotational direction — delivering up to 1,800 in-lbs of torque without stripping screws or stalling on resistance. For driving long screws into hardwood, lag bolts into framing, or working in tight spaces, an impact driver is dramatically more effective than a drill.

## Impact Driver Buying Guide

**Impact driver vs. drill/driver:** Use a drill for drilling holes and driving occasional screws. Use an impact driver for high-volume screw driving, long screws, and lag bolts. Many serious DIYers own both — a drill for precision holes and an impact driver for fastening.

**Torque requirements:**
- Light tasks (furniture, trim, light framing): 1,200–1,500 in-lbs
- Standard DIY (decks, framing, shelving): 1,500–1,800 in-lbs
- Heavy tasks (lag bolts, structural screws): 1,800+ in-lbs

**Battery compatibility:** The biggest reason to pick a specific brand is battery ecosystem. If you own DEWALT tools, the DEWALT impact driver uses the same 20V MAX batteries. Starting fresh? DEWALT or Milwaukee have the widest tool ecosystems.

**Bit selection:** Impact drivers require impact-rated bits (usually black or colored hex bits). Standard screwdriver bits break under the rotational impacts. Invest in a quality impact bit set ($20–$40) to avoid frustration.
