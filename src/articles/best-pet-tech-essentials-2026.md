### The Problem-Solution Map

|---|---|

### GPS Tracker Subscription Costs

Both Whistle and Fi require monthly subscriptions for cellular GPS functionality. Budget for:

- **Whistle Go Explore:** $9.99–$15.99/month or $99–$119/year
- **Fi Series 3:** $99/year

Factor the subscription into the total cost of ownership — the device price alone understates what you'll spend annually.

### Automatic Feeder: Dry Food Only

All consumer automatic feeders in this price range are designed for dry kibble. Wet food requires refrigeration and different dispensing technology not available in this category. For wet food, look at heated automatic feeders specifically designed for wet food (significantly more expensive, fewer reliable options).

**Q: Do automatic pet feeders work reliably?**
Yes, from reputable brands like PETLIBRO with battery backup. Test for 1–2 weeks while home before relying on them during travel. Very small kibble can cause dispensing issues.

**Q: How accurate is pet GPS?**
10–30 feet in open areas — same GPS tech as your phone. Dense urban areas and indoor environments reduce accuracy. Both Whistle and Fi use LTE cellular for nationwide coverage.

**Q: Can Furbo work for cats?**
Yes, but cats respond less to voice calls and treat tossing than dogs. For cat-only monitoring, a simple Wyze Cam at $35 may be sufficient.

**Q: What GPS tracker works for cats?**
Tractive GPS Cat Tracker is designed for cats. Whistle and Fi are sized for dogs. Look for under 30g for a comfortable cat collar attachment.

**Q: Do smart pet toys actually help?**
For fetch-motivated dogs: yes, the ball launcher provides real exercise and mental stimulation. Puzzle feeders work for most dogs regardless of play style.
