## Why a Desk Converter Instead of a Full Standing Desk?

Full standing desks run $400–$1,200+. A converter drops on top of your existing desk, raises your monitor and keyboard to standing height, and costs a fraction of that. The ergonomic case is real: alternating between sitting and standing reduces lower back fatigue, improves circulation, and helps with afternoon energy dips. You don't need a $1,000 desk to get there.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do standing desk converters fit any desk?**
Most platform-style converters sit on top of any flat surface, so yes — they fit virtually any desk. Clamp-style converters require a desk edge of at least 1" thickness and under 4" depth.

**How long should I stand per day?**
Research suggests alternating 30–45 minutes sitting with 15–30 minutes standing, totaling 2–4 hours of standing per 8-hour workday. Start with less and build up — standing fatigue is real initially.

**Will a standing desk converter fix my back pain?**
It can help reduce lower back strain from prolonged sitting, but it's not a fix for poor posture or pre-existing conditions. Pair it with ergonomic monitor height alignment (eye level at the top third of the screen) for best results.
