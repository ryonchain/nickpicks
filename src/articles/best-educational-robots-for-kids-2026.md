## Educational Robot Buyer's Guide

### Choosing by Age and Experience Level

| Age | Experience | Best Robot |
|-----|-----------|-----------|
| 4–7 | Beginner (first robot) | Root (Level 1), Dash (Go app) |
| 6–9 | Early learner | Dash, Botley 2.0 |
| 8–12 | Intermediate | Sphero BOLT, mBot2, Root |
| 10–16 | Advanced | LEGO Mindstorms, mBot2 |

### Screen-Based vs. Screenless Robots

Most high-ceiling educational robots require a tablet app for programming. Screenless robots (Botley 2.0, Coding Critters) are excellent for young children but have lower ceilings. For ages 8+, tablet-connected robots provide the most genuine STEM depth.

### Key Features to Evaluate

**Sensor variety:** More sensors = more kinds of problems kids can solve. Distance, color, and tilt sensors enable the most interesting projects.

**Coding progression:** The best robots have a clear path from beginner to advanced — blocks to Python, not just blocks.

**Expandability:** Systems that support add-on sensors and modules (mBot2, LEGO Mindstorms) have much longer lifespans.
