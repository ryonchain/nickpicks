## What Makes a Work Headphone Worth Buying

Four things separate genuinely useful work headphones from noise-shaped earwear:

- **ANC Depth:** True active noise cancellation uses microphones and inverse sound waves to cancel ambient noise in real time. Look for headphones rated to reduce noise by 25–35 dB — enough to block HVAC hum, keyboard clatter, and open-office chatter.
- **Microphone Quality:** For calls, a dedicated boom mic or multi-mic array with beamforming and wind suppression matters more than audio playback quality. Check call reviews, not just music reviews.
- **Battery Life:** An 8-hour workday requires at least 20 hours rated battery to account for ANC drain and volume levels. 30+ hours means you charge weekly instead of daily.
- **Comfort During Long Wear:** Memory foam ear cushions, adjustable headbands, and balanced weight distribution (under 9 oz) prevent fatigue during 4–6 hour sessions.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### ANC vs. Passive Isolation
True ANC uses electronics to cancel low-frequency noise (HVAC, engines, crowd murmur). Passive isolation comes from ear cup padding blocking mid-to-high frequencies. The best headphones combine both — don't choose one over the other.

### Multipoint Bluetooth
Multipoint lets you stay connected to your laptop and phone simultaneously. When a call comes in on your phone, the headphones switch automatically. Nearly every work headphone above $100 now supports multipoint — confirm before buying.

### Codec Support for Music Quality
For music between meetings, LDAC (Sony) delivers Hi-Res audio up to 990 kbps over Bluetooth. AAC is the standard for iPhone users. SBC is the universal fallback. If you're an Android user who cares about audio quality, LDAC makes a meaningful difference.

### Boom Mic vs. Built-In Mic
Consumer headphones use built-in mics that sound acceptable on calls. Professional headphones (Jabra, Poly) use dedicated boom or flex mics that sound significantly better for extended call sessions. If calls are your primary use, the mic quality difference is real.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does ANC work on phone calls?**
Most headphones deactivate ANC's microphone during calls to use those same mics for voice pickup. The ANC effect on the caller's end comes from mic beamforming (blocking background noise) rather than traditional ANC.

**Will ANC headphones help with tinnitus?**
Some users find ANC helpful by reducing ambient noise, which lessens the sensation of tinnitus. However, some people find the ANC signal itself uncomfortable — try before buying if this is a concern.

**How long does it take to adjust to ANC?**
Most people notice a slight pressure sensation when first using strong ANC — this typically fades after 20–30 minutes as your ears adjust. If it persists after a week, the ANC level on that headphone may not suit you.

**Can I use these on airplanes in airplane mode?**
Yes — Bluetooth works independently from cellular data. Enable airplane mode on your phone and re-enable Bluetooth. Your headphones will connect normally.
