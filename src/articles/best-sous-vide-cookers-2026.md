## How Sous Vide Works

1. Fill a container (pot, cambro, Rubbermaid bin) with water
2. Clip the immersion circulator to the edge
3. Set temperature and time for your protein
4. Seal food in a vacuum bag (or Ziploc with water displacement)
5. Submerge bag, clip to container edge
6. After cooking time, sear in a cast iron pan for crust

The circulator heats and circulates water to maintain exact temperature. The food can't overcook — it hits target temperature and stays there indefinitely.

## Equipment You'll Need Beyond the Circulator

**Container:** A 12-quart Rubbermaid container ($15) or large stockpot. Get a lid to reduce evaporation on long cooks (6+ hours).

**Vacuum sealer:** Optional but recommended. Ziploc freezer bags with the water displacement method work for most proteins. A FoodSaver is $60–$80 and eliminates air properly for longer cooks.

**Cast iron pan:** For the post-cook sear. A 10"–12" pan at maximum heat for 90 seconds per side creates the crust that sous vide alone can't provide.

**Probe thermometer:** Optional but useful for verifying your circulator's accuracy and for checking the final sear.
