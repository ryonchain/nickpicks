## What Smart Sleep Trackers Actually Measure

Modern sleep trackers use accelerometers, heart rate sensors (PPG), SpO2 sensors, and skin temperature sensors to estimate:

- **Sleep stages:** Light, REM, and deep sleep duration
- **HRV (Heart Rate Variability):** Recovery quality indicator
- **Resting Heart Rate:** Baseline cardiovascular health
- **SpO2 (Blood Oxygen):** Flags potential sleep apnea patterns
- **Sleep latency:** Time to fall asleep
- **Sleep efficiency:** Percentage of time in bed actually sleeping

No consumer device is as accurate as a clinical polysomnography sleep study. But consistent relative tracking — seeing trends over weeks — provides genuine insight into sleep quality.

## How to Choose the Right Sleep Tracker

**Wear comfort:** If you forget to wear a wristband, get a ring. If you take off all jewelry at night, consider the Withings under-mattress option.

**Subscription tolerance:** Oura ($5.99/mo) and Whoop ($19.99/mo) are subscription-dependent. Samsung, Garmin, Withings, and Ultrahuman have no recurring fees.

**Ecosystem:** Garmin works with any phone. Samsung Ring requires Samsung Health (Android only). Apple Watch works only with iPhone.

**Data depth vs. simplicity:** Whoop and Oura provide the deepest data. Withings and Samsung provide simpler summaries. Choose depth if you'll actually act on the data.
