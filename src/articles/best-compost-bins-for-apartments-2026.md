## Apartment Composting Buying Guide

### Which Method Is Right for Your Apartment?

**You want zero effort and no outdoor access → Lomi Electric.** Just drop scraps in, press a button, collect dirt. The highest upfront cost but the most convenient.

**You have green waste pickup or community composting nearby → OXO or Full Circle collector.** Collect scraps on your counter and drop them off. Simple and cheap.

**You cook lots of meat and dairy but have some outdoor space → Bokashi.** The only method that handles all food types and works in sealed, odor-free under-sink storage.

**You have houseplants and want premium fertilizer → Worm Factory.** The output (worm castings) is the best plant fertilizer money can buy — and it's free once the system is established.

### Managing Odor in Apartments

Odor is the biggest barrier to apartment composting. Here's how each method controls it:

- **Electric (Lomi):** Heating and carbon filters neutralize odors during operation. Very effective.
- **Countertop collectors:** Charcoal filters absorb odors. Works for 3–5 days; empty before scraps decompose significantly.
- **Bokashi:** Sealed bucket, fermentation rather than decomposition — low odor when properly sealed.
- **Vermicomposting:** Minimal earthy smell when correctly managed. Avoid meat/dairy and don't over-wet the bin.

### What You Can (and Can't) Compost in an Apartment

**Safe for all methods (excluding worm bins):**
- Fruit and vegetable scraps
- Coffee grounds and tea leaves
- Eggshells
- Bread and grains (in moderation)

**Safe for Lomi and Bokashi only:**
- Meat and fish
- Dairy products
- Cooked food

**Never compost:**
- Oils and fats (large amounts)
- Pet waste
- Diseased plant matter

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does apartment composting actually smell bad?

With the right system, no. Countertop collectors with charcoal filters control odor effectively for 2–4 days. Bokashi is fermentation-based and stays nearly odorless when sealed. Electric composters (Lomi) operate almost odor-free. The smell comes from leaving scraps too long without the right system — the systems above are designed to prevent this.

### What do I do with the compost output if I don't have a garden?

Options for urban apartment dwellers: community garden drop-off, local farmers market compost programs, parks department composting programs, neighborhood compost sharing groups, houseplant soil amendment (worm castings are especially good), or check if your city has a residential food scrap pickup program.

### How long does it take to see compost results?

Lomi: 4–16 hours per cycle. Bokashi: 2 weeks of fermentation, then 2–4 weeks to finish in soil. Worm Factory: 3–6 months to reach full productivity. Countertop collectors: you're just storing scraps, not creating compost — output depends on where you take them.

### Is it worth composting in an apartment?

Yes. Even collecting scraps for community composting or municipal green waste programs diverts food from landfill, where it would generate methane. If you have houseplants, the fertilizer output of a worm bin or bokashi system is valuable. The environmental return is real regardless of the method.

### Can I compost if my apartment has no outdoor space at all?

Yes. Lomi operates entirely indoors with no outdoor access. Worm bins can live in a closet or under a sink. Bokashi works under the sink but requires some soil for finishing — a balcony planter or regular trips to a community garden cover this.
