## Why D3 + K2 Together?

Vitamin D3 dramatically increases calcium absorption. Without Vitamin K2 (specifically MK-7 form), that calcium can deposit in arteries rather than bones. K2 activates the proteins (osteocalcin and Matrix Gla Protein) that shuttle calcium into bone matrix and prevent arterial calcification.

**Bottom line:** Take D3 with K2. MK-7 is the preferred K2 form (longer half-life than MK-4, active at lower doses).

## What's the Right Vitamin D Dose?

The optimal Vitamin D dose is highly individual based on current serum levels, body weight, sun exposure, and age. General guidance:

| Serum 25-OH-D Level | Recommended D3 Dose |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Below 20 ng/mL (deficient) | 5,000–10,000 IU/day (medical supervision) |
| 20–30 ng/mL (insufficient) | 2,000–5,000 IU/day |
| 30–50 ng/mL (adequate) | 1,000–2,000 IU/day |
| Above 50 ng/mL | Maintenance or consult doctor |

**Target range:** Most integrative health practitioners target 40–60 ng/mL as optimal.

**Test before supplementing:** A 25-OH-D blood test costs $30–50 and removes all guesswork. LabCorp, Quest Diagnostics, and at-home kits are all widely available.

## FAQ

**Should I take vitamin D3 with K2?**
Yes — K2 (specifically MK-7 form) directs calcium to bones and away from arteries when D3 increases calcium absorption. D3 alone without K2 may increase arterial calcification risk at higher doses.

**What is the best form of K2 in supplements?**
MK-7 (menaquinone-7). It has a longer half-life than MK-4 and is effective at 100–200 mcg/day. MK-4 requires much higher doses (1,500 mcg) for similar effects.

**Can I take too much Vitamin D?**
Yes — Vitamin D toxicity occurs above sustained 10,000 IU/day without medical supervision. Symptoms include hypercalcemia (nausea, weakness, frequent urination). At 5,000 IU/day for most adults, toxicity risk is extremely low but testing annually is recommended.
