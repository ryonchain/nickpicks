## Microscope Buying Guide

**By Age:**
- Ages 5–7: My First Lab Duo-Scope (simple, durable, low magnification)
- Ages 8–12: National Geographic Dual LED (fun accessories, good range)
- Ages 12+/Students: AmScope M150C (real lab-quality optics)
- High School/AP Bio: AmScope B120C-E (professional grade)

**Compound vs. Stereo:**
- **Compound microscope** — looks at thin, transparent specimens on slides (cells, pond water, bacteria)
- **Stereo microscope** — looks at 3D opaque objects (coins, insects, flowers, rocks)
- **Dual-function** (My First Lab) — does both at low magnification

**Key Specs Explained:**
- **Magnification:** 40x–400x covers most student biology. 1000x requires immersion oil and practice.
- **Optics:** All-glass optics > plastic lenses. All-glass transmits light more clearly and lasts longer.
- **Build:** All-metal frame lasts 20+ years. Plastic frames crack with regular student use.
