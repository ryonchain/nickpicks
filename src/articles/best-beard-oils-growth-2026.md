## Why Beard Oil Matters

Beard itch and skin flaking (beardruff) are caused by dry skin underneath the beard, not the beard hair itself. Facial skin produces sebum to naturally condition hair, but beard hair grows faster than the sebum supply can keep up with — especially in the first 1–4 weeks of growth, when itch is worst.

Beard oil replaces and supplements that sebum, conditioning both the hair and the underlying skin. Secondary benefits include:

- **Reduced breakage:** Conditioned hair is more flexible and less prone to splitting at the tips
- **Manageable texture:** Coarse, wiry hairs soften, making combing and styling easier
- **Better appearance:** Hydrated hair lays flatter and looks fuller than dry, frizzy beard hair

## What to Look For When Buying

**Carrier oils matter more than essential oils:** Essential oils provide scent; carrier oils do the conditioning. Prioritize jojoba, argan, sweet almond, and avocado oil in the top positions on the ingredient list.

**Avoid mineral oil:** It coats hair rather than penetrating it, creating a superficial softness that washes away immediately. Check that "mineral oil" isn't in the first three ingredients.

**Start with less than you think you need:** Most men over-apply, leaving the beard greasy. 3–4 drops is the right amount for a short beard. Add more gradually.

**Match scent to your environment:** If you work in close proximity to clients or colleagues, choose a light or unscented option. Strong beard oil scents in an office setting can be distracting.
