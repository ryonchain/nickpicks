### Eye Strain and Bias Lighting Science

Bias lighting works by raising the ambient brightness around your monitor, which reduces the perceived contrast ratio between your bright screen and the dark room. When the brightness differential is high, your pupils constantly adjust — leading to eye fatigue over hours. A bias light sitting behind the monitor at roughly 10–15% of the monitor's max brightness is the ergonomically optimal setup.

For maximum benefit:
- Place the bias light strip around the perimeter of the monitor's back
- Set bias light brightness to ~10% of your monitor's brightness
- Use a warm color temperature (3000K–4000K) for evening use

## FAQs

**Do LED strips need to go under the desk or behind the monitor?**
Both placements serve different purposes. Under-desk strips (or shelf lighting) add ambient room glow and aesthetic. Behind-monitor strips (bias lighting) specifically reduce eye strain during extended screen use. For best results, combine both.

**Is smart lighting (app control) necessary?**
Not strictly — basic LED strips with a remote work fine for static setups. Smart control adds scheduling (auto-on at sunset, off at midnight), scenes, and music sync, which significantly improves the day-to-day experience. At the price gap between smart and basic strips, smart is usually worth it.

**Can LED strips damage my desk finish?**
3M adhesive backing on most LED strips can strip paint or veneer from finished desks when removed. To protect your desk, apply strips to a cable raceway or mounting channel rather than directly to the desk surface, or use Command Strip-style mounting clips.
