## TV Buying Guide: What Actually Matters

**Panel technology matters most.**
- **OLED:** Perfect blacks, best contrast, moderate brightness. Best for dark rooms.
- **QD-OLED:** Better brightness than W-OLED, still perfect blacks. Best for gaming + movies.
- **Mini-LED:** High brightness, good local dimming, no burn-in risk. Best for bright rooms.
- **QLED:** Standard LED with quantum dot color enhancement. Good mid-range value.
- **LED:** Basic. Fine for casual use.

**HDMI 2.1 matters for gaming.**
If you own a PS5 or Xbox Series X, you need at least one HDMI 2.1 port for 4K at 120fps. Most budget TVs only have HDMI 2.0 (limits gaming to 4K at 60fps or 1080p at 120fps).

**Refresh rate: 60Hz vs 120Hz vs 144Hz.**
60Hz is fine for streaming. 120Hz improves sports and gaming. 144Hz is premium and mainly benefits gaming.

**Sound quality: buy a soundbar.**
Every TV in this guide has mediocre built-in speakers. Budget $100–200 for a soundbar and your experience improves dramatically.
