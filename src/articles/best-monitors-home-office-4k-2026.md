  - question: "Is 4K worth it for a home office monitor?"
    answer: "Yes, if your monitor is 27 inches or larger. At 27 inches, 4K resolution delivers 163 PPI — meaning text is noticeably sharper than 1080p or 1440p. You'll spend less time zooming, scrolling, and squinting. For spreadsheets, code, and document work, the extra pixel density makes a real quality-of-life difference. If your monitor is 24 inches or smaller, 1440p is the sweet spot — 4K on a small screen requires heavy scaling that defeats the purpose."
  - question: "What panel type is best for home office use?"
    answer: "IPS (In-Plane Switching) is the best home office panel. IPS provides wide viewing angles (important if you share your screen in meetings), accurate color reproduction (important for any visual work), and comfortable brightness levels for all-day use. VA panels have better contrast (good for movies) but narrower viewing angles that cause color shift when you're not sitting dead-center. OLED is stunning but expensive and not ideal for static document work due to burn-in risk."
  - question: "What does 'eye-care' mean in monitor marketing?"
    answer: "Eye-care features vary by manufacturer but generally include: Flicker-Free (DC dimming instead of PWM, which eliminates invisible screen flicker that causes eye strain), Low Blue Light filtering (reduces the blue light spectrum that causes sleep disruption and eye fatigue), and Anti-Glare coating (reduces reflections in bright rooms). All three are worth having for all-day home office use. Look for these in BenQ's Eye-Care lineup and LG's NanoIPS monitors."
  - question: "How far should I sit from a home office monitor?"
    answer: "The general rule: sit at arm's length from your monitor, with the screen top at or slightly below eye level. For a 27\" monitor, that's typically 60–90 cm (24–36 inches). For a 32\" monitor, sit 80–100 cm away. Too close causes eye strain from constant focus adjustment; too far makes you squint at small text. Pair proper viewing distance with appropriate text scaling (125–150% on Windows 4K monitors) for the most comfortable setup."

## Home Office Monitor Setup Guide

**Single vs dual monitor:**
- One 27–32" 4K > two 24" 1080p for most tasks
- One ultrawide (34") ≥ two 27" monitors for side-by-side work
- Dual 27" 4K is the highest-productivity setup if desk space allows

**Refresh rate for home office:**
60Hz is perfectly adequate for document work, coding, video calls, and content creation. Only gaming and video editing with playback review benefit from 120Hz+.

**Essential features for all-day use:**
1. Flicker-Free (Flicker-free certification, not marketing)
2. USB-C with 60W+ for single-cable laptop connection
3. Height-adjustable stand or VESA mount compatibility
4. 99% sRGB minimum color accuracy
