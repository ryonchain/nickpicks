## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### Engine Power (HP)
- **Up to 1 acre:** 17–18 HP is sufficient
- **1–2 acres:** 18–22 HP recommended
- **2+ acres or hills:** 22+ HP, preferably a V-Twin

### Cutting Deck Width
Wider decks mow faster but are harder to maneuver in tight spaces:
- **42 inches:** Best for most residential lots
- **48–54 inches:** Better for larger, open yards

### Transmission Type
- **Manual (gear drive):** Cheapest, most durable, less convenient
- **Hydrostatic (pedal):** Most popular — smooth, easy to learn
- **Zero-turn (lap bars):** Fastest and most maneuverable, slight learning curve

### Deck Material
Stamped steel decks (budget) dent easier than fabricated steel decks (premium). For commercial use or durability, spend up for fabricated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I change the oil on a riding mower?**
Every 50 hours of operation or at the start of each season, whichever comes first.

**What size yard needs a riding mower?**
Most experts recommend a riding mower once your yard exceeds 0.5 acres. For under 0.5 acres, a self-propelled walk-behind is usually sufficient.

**Can riding mowers handle wet grass?**
It's better to mow dry — wet grass clumps, clogs decks, and can cause wheel slip on slopes. If you must mow wet, reduce speed and raise the cutting height.
