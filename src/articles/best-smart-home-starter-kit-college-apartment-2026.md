Moving into your first apartment is a great time to set up a smart home. The key difference from a college dorm: you actually have a home to automate. But apartment constraints still apply — no holes in walls, no permanent wiring changes, and the setup should move with you when the lease ends. Here's the best renter-friendly smart home setup for a college apartment in 2026.

## Renter Rules: What You Can and Can't Do

Before buying anything, the apartment reality check:

**Safe to do:**
- Smart bulbs (screw into existing fixtures)
- Smart plugs (plug into existing outlets)
- COMMAND strip-mounted devices (remove cleanly)
- Standalone security cameras (no wiring)
- Smart speakers (sit on a shelf)
- Smart thermostats (requires landlord permission in most cases)

**Needs landlord permission:**
- Replacing light switches (wiring required)
- Smart locks (replaces existing hardware)
- Smart doorbells (replaces existing hardware)
- Smart thermostats (wiring and landlord's HVAC system)

The first group is all you need for an excellent college apartment smart home.

## Optional Upgrades

### Smart Lock — If Your Landlord Allows It

If your landlord permits smart lock installation, the Schlage Encode Plus (~$250) works with Apple HomeKit and never requires a key. More practically, it means no lost keys and you can let someone into your apartment remotely. But it requires replacing the existing deadbolt — need landlord OK first.

### Smart Thermostat — Energy Savings

A Google Nest Thermostat ($130) or ecobee ($180) can cut HVAC costs by 10–23% per year by learning your schedule. But it requires wiring into the existing thermostat, which is a landlord call. Some landlords will allow it if you agree to reinstall the original before moving out.

## Setting Up Routines (Where Smart Home Gets Good)

The real value of a smart home isn't individual device control — it's routines. In the Alexa app, you can create automated sequences:

**Morning Routine (7:00 AM)**
→ Echo Dot plays your morning playlist at 30% volume
→ Smart plugs turn on the coffee maker
→ Smart bulbs switch to cool daylight (6500K, 100% brightness)

**Study Mode**
→ Echo Dot switches to white noise
→ Bulbs change to neutral 4000K at 80% brightness
→ Strip lights set to steady white

**Night Mode (11:00 PM)**
→ Bulbs dim to 20% warm yellow
→ TV plug turns off automatically
→ Coffee maker plug turns off

**Goodbye Routine (triggered when you say "Alexa, goodbye")**
→ All lights turn off
→ All plugs turn off except the fridge
→ Camera activates recording mode

The whole setup — Echo Dot, 4 smart plugs, 4 smart bulbs, one camera, LED strips — runs about $150. That's a smart apartment on a college budget.
