## Understanding Retinol Before You Buy

### The Retinoid Spectrum
Not all retinoids are equal in strength or speed:
- **Retinyl esters (retinyl palmitate/acetate):** Weakest. Two conversion steps needed. Good for 20s prevention.
- **Retinol:** OTC gold standard. One conversion step. Takes 8–12 weeks for visible results.
- **Retinaldehyde:** One step stronger than retinol. Faster results, less irritation than prescription.
- **Tretinoin (retinoic acid):** Prescription only. Direct activity, fastest results, highest irritation potential.

OTC products can only sell up to retinaldehyde. Everything marketed as "retinol" is actually retinol unless labelled retinaldehyde specifically.

### Concentration Guide
- **0.01–0.03%:** Beginner/sensitive. Minimal irritation, slow onset.
- **0.1–0.3%:** Standard. Visible results in 12 weeks. Tolerable for most skin types.
- **0.5%:** Moderate clinical strength. Results in 8 weeks. Some initial peeling.
- **1%:** Maximum OTC strength. Clinical results in 4–6 weeks. Requires tolerance buildup.

### The Retinization Phase
Weeks 1–4 typically include flaking, dryness, and sensitivity. This is the skin adjusting to accelerated cell turnover, not the product damaging your skin. Start with 2x/week, increase to nightly as tolerated over 6–8 weeks.

## Retinol Protocol for Beginners

**Week 1–2:** Apply 2x/week on clean, dry skin at night. Apply moisturizer on top immediately.

**Week 3–4:** Increase to every other night if tolerating well. Continue moisturizer on top.

**Week 5–8:** Advance to nightly use. Some flaking normal; address with barrier cream, not by stopping.

**Months 2–3:** Results begin appearing — fine line softening, skin texture improvement, brightening.

**Month 3+:** This is the timeline for real anti-aging collagen remodeling activity.
