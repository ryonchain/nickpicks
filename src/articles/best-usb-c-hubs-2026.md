## Buying Guide

### USB-C vs. Thunderbolt: What's the Difference?

**USB-C** is a connector shape. Speeds range from USB 2.0 (480Mbps) to USB 3.2 Gen 2×2 (20Gbps).

**Thunderbolt 4** uses the same USB-C connector but runs at 40Gbps, supports dual 4K displays, and daisy-chains devices. It's backward compatible with USB-C devices.

If your laptop has a Thunderbolt 4 port (Intel Macs, M-series Macs, some ThinkPads), a TB4 dock unlocks maximum performance. If not, a high-quality USB-C hub (USB 3.2 Gen 2) is the right choice.

### Power Delivery: What Wattage Do You Need?

| Laptop | Minimum PD | Ideal PD |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| MacBook Air (M1/M2/M3) | 30W | 65W |
| MacBook Pro 14" | 67W | 96W |
| MacBook Pro 16" | 96W | 140W |
| Dell XPS 13/15 | 45W | 90W |
| Most Windows ultrabooks | 45W | 65W |

A hub with 100W PD covers most MacBook Pro and Windows laptop charging scenarios.

### Display Output: 4K@30Hz vs. 4K@60Hz

4K@30Hz works fine for productivity (documents, coding, web browsing). 4K@60Hz is noticeably smoother for video editing, photo editing, and any animated work. If you're doing creative work on an external display, confirm 60Hz support before buying.

### Single-Cable Convenience vs. Multi-Port Hubs

A Thunderbolt dock delivers power, data, and display from one cable. A USB-C hub does the same but at lower total bandwidth, which can be shared across ports. For most users, a good USB-C hub is sufficient. Professionals with 40Gbps storage, external GPUs, or dual high-refresh displays should invest in Thunderbolt.

## FAQ

**Do USB-C hubs work with iPad Pro?**
Yes — iPad Pro M-series supports external display and USB-C accessories. Not all hubs deliver 4K output reliably on iPad; verify compatibility with the specific hub's specs before buying.

**Why does my hub run hot?**
Plastic hubs have poor thermal dissipation. Running 4K HDMI, power delivery, and multiple USB-A devices simultaneously generates significant heat. Aluminum hubs (Satechi, Belkin Connect Pro) handle sustained use much better.

**Will a USB-C hub slow down my laptop?**
On USB-C hosts (non-Thunderbolt), total bandwidth is shared across all ports — heavy use on USB-A while outputting 4K can cause display artifacts. On Thunderbolt 4 docks, bandwidth is allocated more intelligently. For most office tasks, you won't notice a difference.

**Can I run dual monitors from a USB-C hub?**
On Thunderbolt 4 laptops: yes, with a TB4 dock. On non-Thunderbolt USB-C laptops: dual HDMI hubs work, but require DisplayLink technology for some configurations — check your laptop's spec sheet for display output limitations.

**What's the best USB-C hub for travel?**
The Anker 7-in-1 is compact, covers the core ports, and doesn't require external power. It's the best portable option. For daily desk use, a larger dock makes more sense.
