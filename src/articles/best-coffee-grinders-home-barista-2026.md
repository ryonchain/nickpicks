## Why Grind Quality Matters More Than the Brewer

The single biggest improvement most home coffee drinkers can make is switching from a blade grinder (or pre-ground coffee) to a burr grinder. Here's why:

**Consistency is everything.** Coffee extraction depends on particle size. Uniform particles extract at the same rate, producing balanced flavor. Inconsistent particles — the inevitable result of blade grinding — extract unevenly: the fines over-extract (bitter), the large chunks under-extract (sour). You taste both simultaneously.

**Freshness.** Pre-ground coffee goes stale within days of opening. Whole beans stay fresh for weeks. Grinding immediately before brewing captures volatile aromatic compounds that disappear within minutes of grinding.

**What to evaluate in a grinder:**
- **Burr type:** Conical burrs (most home grinders) are quieter, produce less heat, and handle a wide range of grind settings. Flat burrs (pro/prosumer machines) produce more uniform grounds but are typically more expensive.
- **Grind settings:** More settings = more precision. Stepless grinders (continuous adjustment) offer the most control but have a steeper learning curve than stepped designs.
- **Retention:** Coffee grounds that stay inside the grinder between uses go stale. Low-retention grinders minimize waste and stale taste.
- **RPM:** Lower RPM = less heat generated during grinding = better flavor preservation. Hand grinders win here.
