## What to Look for in an Electric Toothbrush

Electric toothbrushes outperform manual brushes in plaque removal — the American Dental Association confirms this. But not all electrics are created equal:

- **Brush modes:** Most people only need two: Daily Clean and Sensitive. Whitening, Gum Care, and Tongue Cleaning modes are meaningful additions if you have specific concerns. More modes only help if you actually use them.
- **Pressure sensor:** The most important safety feature. Brushing too hard causes gum recession and enamel erosion — damage that's irreversible. A sensor that slows the brush or alerts you when pressure is excessive prevents long-term damage.
- **Battery life:** Rechargeable lithium-ion batteries (7–14 days per charge) are far better than AA-powered models for consistent torque. Longer battery life matters for travel.
- **Replacement head cost:** This is the long-term cost that matters more than the initial price. Generic-compatible heads are significantly cheaper than brand-name replacements for most models.
- **ADA Acceptance:** The ADA Seal of Acceptance indicates the toothbrush has been tested for safety and effectiveness.

## FAQ

**Does the brush mode actually matter?**
For most people, no. The daily clean mode on any quality toothbrush removes plaque effectively. Sensitive mode matters if you have gum recession or hypersensitive teeth. Whitening modes are mild abrasives — less effective than whitening treatments but add value if you're between whitening appointments.

**How often should I replace the brush head?**
Every 3 months, or when bristles visibly splay. Frayed bristles lose effectiveness. Generic replacement heads (not brand-name) are often identical in quality and significantly cheaper — check Amazon for compatible options.

**Is an electric toothbrush worth it vs. manual?**
Yes, clinically. Multiple ADA-reviewed studies show electric brushes (especially oscillating-rotating types) remove more plaque and reduce gingivitis more effectively than manual brushes. The difference is most significant for people who brush too briefly or with poor technique.
