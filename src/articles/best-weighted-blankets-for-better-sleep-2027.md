That's the science behind what feels like a full-body hug.

Whether you're dealing with anxiety, insomnia, sensory processing differences, or just want better sleep, the right weighted blanket can make a real difference. Here's what actually works.

## How to Choose the Right Weighted Blanket

### The 10% Body Weight Rule

The most commonly cited guideline is to choose a weighted blanket that's approximately 10% of your body weight. A 150-pound person would ideally use a 15-pound blanket. This isn't a strict rule, but it's a good starting point. If you prefer more or less pressure, adjust accordingly.

### Hot vs. Cold Sleepers

This is probably the most important factor after weight. If you sleep hot:
- Look for knit or bamboo materials (Bearaby, YnM bamboo)
- Avoid microfiber and minky options

If you sleep cold:
- Minky and flannel options work well (Luna, Layla warm side)
- Thicker quilted constructions trap more heat

### Glass Beads vs. Poly Pellets

Glass beads are the premium choice — they're smaller, denser, distribute more evenly, and don't make noise. Poly pellets (plastic) are lighter per volume and sometimes used in kids' products, but can shift and make crinkling sounds over time.

### Single or Shared

Weighted blankets don't work well as shared blankets — the weight that helps you might not be right for your partner. Consider buying individual twin or throw-size options for couples rather than sharing a full or queen.
