A video baby monitor is one of the most-used baby products in the first two years — you'll check it dozens of times a night. Getting the right one matters.

---

## WiFi vs Non-WiFi: Which Should You Buy?

**WiFi Baby Monitors**
- View from anywhere via smartphone (grocery store, travel, work)
- Better resolution (1080p HD vs standard non-WiFi quality)
- Smart features: sleep analytics, two-way audio, room temp/humidity
- Risk: internet-connected devices can potentially be accessed if improperly secured
- Best brands: Nanit, Owlet, Infant Optics (newer WiFi models)

**Non-WiFi (DECT/FHSS) Baby Monitors**
- Dedicated parent unit receiver — no smartphone required
- No internet connection = no hacking vulnerability
- Typically limited to 500–1,000 ft range (within home)
- Simpler to set up and use
- Best brands: Infant Optics DXR-8 Pro, VTech, Motorola MBP series

**My recommendation:** WiFi monitor if you want to check the camera from your phone and want sleep insights. Non-WiFi if you want simplicity, lower monthly costs, and no privacy concerns.
