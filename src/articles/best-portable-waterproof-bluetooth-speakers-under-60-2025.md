## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Waterproof Bluetooth Speaker Under $60

### IP Rating: Know What "Waterproof" Actually Means
Not all waterproof claims are equal. Look for at minimum **IPX5** (protected against water jets) for poolside use. For kayaking, rafting, or beach use where the speaker might get submerged, hold out for **IPX7** (submersible up to 1 meter for 30 minutes) or better. Avoid vague "water-resistant" claims without an official IP rating.

### Battery Life
Most portable speakers in this range offer 10–24 hours of playtime. If you're taking weekend camping trips, prioritize 20+ hours. For daily commutes or desk use, 10–12 hours is plenty. Note that battery ratings are usually measured at 50–60% volume — real-world listening time at full blast can be 30–40% shorter.

### Sound Quality: Bass, Mids, and Highs
At this price, don't expect audiophile sound. But look for features like passive bass radiators, dual drivers for stereo separation, or manufacturer sound modes (XBass, BassUp). Reading Amazon verified reviews about "tinny sound" or "great bass" is often more reliable than spec sheets.

### Portability: Size, Weight, and Mounting
Think about how you'll carry it. Clip-style speakers (JBL Clip 4, StormBox Micro 2) attach directly to bags and gear. Cylindrical speakers like the Soundcore 2 fit in cup holders and side pockets. Rugged brick-style speakers trade portability for durability.

### Connectivity
All speakers here use Bluetooth 5.0 or later for stable, low-latency connections. Some offer AUX input as a fallback. TWS (True Wireless Stereo) pairing lets you pair two identical units for real stereo separation — a nice bonus if you'll ever buy a second unit.
