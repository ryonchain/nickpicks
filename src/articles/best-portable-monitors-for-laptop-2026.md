## How to Choose

**Which connection type do you need?**
- **USB-C only laptop (MacBook, newer Dell/HP):** Any USB-C monitor works. Single-cable setup is cleanest.
- **Older laptop with HDMI:** Look for USB-C + Mini HDMI (ViewSonic VX1655, Lepow H1).
- **Gaming console or streaming stick:** Mini HDMI input.

**What's your primary use case?**
- **Documents / productivity:** 1080p IPS is sufficient (ViewSonic, Lepow)
- **Video / photo editing:** Get 2.5K+ IPS (ASUS MB16QHQ) or OLED (ASUS MQ16AH)
- **True dual-screen anywhere:** Mobile Pixels Trio Max
- **Touch input / tablet workflow:** Lenovo ThinkVision M14t

## FAQ

**Do portable monitors need power?**
Most draw power from your laptop via USB-C. The ASUS ZenScreen OLED MQ16AH has an internal battery for standalone use. Budget models with Mini HDMI need a separate USB-A cable for power.

**Will a portable monitor drain my laptop battery faster?**
Yes, typically 15–30% faster depending on brightness. If battery life is critical, look for monitors with their own power source.

**What size portable monitor should I buy?**
15.6" is the most popular — it fits in a 15" laptop sleeve, gives meaningful extra space, and isn't too heavy. If you travel with a 13" laptop, a 14" portable monitor is the better balance.

**Does my laptop support USB-C display output?**
Most laptops from 2019+ with USB-C support DisplayPort Alt Mode. Check your laptop specs for "USB-C with DisplayPort Alt Mode" or "Thunderbolt." MacBooks with USB-C always support external displays.
