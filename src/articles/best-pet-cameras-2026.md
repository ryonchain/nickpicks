## Buyer's Guide: Choosing a Pet Camera

### Key Features to Evaluate

**Treat dispenser:** Essential for positive reinforcement training remotely. Most use cylindrical treats (like Milk-Bone mini treats). Check compatibility before buying treat bags.

**Pan/Tilt:** Fixed cameras have a blind spot — your pet will inevitably be out of frame when you want to check. Pan/tilt (motorized or manual) solves this.

**Night vision:** Standard infrared night vision produces black-and-white footage. Color night vision (Wyze Cam v4) produces color footage in low light.

**Cloud storage vs. local storage:** Cloud storage ($5–10/month) provides remote access to recordings. Local MicroSD is free but requires physical access to pull footage.

**AI detection:** Worth paying for if you have a dog with separation anxiety — bark alerts, activity summaries, and distress detection are genuinely useful.

## FAQ

**Do pet cameras require a subscription?**
Basic live view works without subscription on most models. Cloud recording, AI detection, and extended history typically require $7–10/month.

**What treats work in Furbo and Petcube dispensers?**
Small cylindrical treats: Zuke's Mini Naturals, Wellness Soft WellBites, Milk-Bone Mini Original. Avoid hard, flat, or irregularly shaped treats — they jam the mechanism.

**Can pet cameras work when I'm not on Wi-Fi?**
Yes — the camera connects to your home Wi-Fi, and you view it on your phone via cellular data from anywhere. The camera works 24/7 even when you're away.

**How high should I mount a pet camera?**
3–4 feet for dogs (eye level gives more engaging interaction during treat time). 5–6 feet for monitoring the full room. Wall mount brackets are included or available for most models.
