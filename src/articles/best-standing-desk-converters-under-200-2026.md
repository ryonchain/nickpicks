## Buying Guide: Z-Lift vs. X-Lift vs. Electric

### Z-Lift (Most Common)
The keyboard tray and monitor platform move up/down together with offset between them. The Z-shape keeps keyboard below monitor at all heights.

**Best for:** Most users. The integrated keyboard tray maintains proper ergonomic positioning at any height.

### X-Lift (Scissor)
Single flat platform raises and lowers like a scissor. Simpler mechanism, more compact.

**Best for:** Single monitor setups where keyboard can sit on the desk surface. Less ideal ergonomically if your keyboard needs to be lower than your monitor.

### Electric (Motorized)
Motor drives height adjustment at the press of a button.

**Best for:** Users who adjust height frequently throughout the day, shared workstations with multiple users, anyone with wrist/shoulder issues that make spring adjustment painful.

## Ergonomics: The Right Setup

A standing desk converter only helps if configured correctly. Key positioning rules:

1. **Monitor height:** Top of the screen at or slightly below eye level
2. **Arm position:** Elbows at 90°, forearms parallel to floor when typing
3. **Keyboard/mouse:** Keyboard tray should be 8–12 inches lower than monitor platform
4. **Standing duration:** Start at 20 minutes standing per hour, increase gradually to 30–45 minutes

## FAQ

**Q: Do standing desk converters hurt your back?**
A: Standing incorrectly for too long does. Standing desk converters are tools for alternating between sitting and standing — not replacing sitting entirely. Gradually build up standing time, and use an anti-fatigue mat (highly recommended).

**Q: What's the maximum weight a standing desk converter can hold?**
A: Most handle 25–35 lbs. A typical 27-inch monitor weighs 8–12 lbs. Two monitors plus monitor arms typically total 20–30 lbs. Check your specific monitor weights before buying.

**Q: Do I need to drill into my desk to install a standing desk converter?**
A: No. All standing desk converters sit on top of your existing desk surface without any mounting. They're purely resting on the desk surface.

**Q: Will a standing desk converter fit on any desk?**
A: Check the width — most converters need 28–36 inches of desk width. Also check depth: converters typically extend 15–22 inches back on the desk, which requires a desk depth of at least 20–24 inches.

**Q: What's the difference between a desk converter and a full standing desk?**
A: A desk converter sits on top of your existing desk and raises a portion of the surface. A full standing desk is a complete desk replacement with motor-driven height adjustment for the entire surface. Full standing desks are better for dual-monitor heavy use and offer the full desk surface; converters are better for budget, renters, or users happy with their current desk.
