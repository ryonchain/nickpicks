## Leather Quality: The Most Important Factor

### The Four Grades of Leather

**1. Full-grain leather (best)**
The outermost layer of the hide, with the grain and all natural markings intact. No sanding, no buffing. Develops a unique patina with use. Most durable — the grain has not been weakened. Correct choice for wallets you want to last 10–20+ years.

**2. Top-grain leather (second best)**
Top layer of the hide, but sanded to remove imperfections and create a uniform appearance. Slightly less durable than full-grain but more consistent looking. Found in most designer leather goods.

**3. Genuine leather (misleading term)**
Lower layers of the hide — what remains after full-grain and top-grain are removed. Sanded and painted to look like full-grain. Inferior durability, peels and cracks within 2–4 years. Despite the name, this is lower quality than both grades above.

**4. Bonded leather (avoid)**
Leather scraps and fiber shredded, reconstituted with polyurethane adhesive into sheets. Looks like leather briefly, falls apart within 1–2 years. Found in budget wallets and some "vegan leather" products.

### What to Look For
- **Tanning method:** Vegetable-tanned leather develops the richest patina; chrome-tanned is softer initially
- **Thread:** Waxed thread holds better than standard thread; check stitching tightness at corners
- **Hardware:** Solid brass or stainless steel, not plated zinc

## Wallet Maintenance

**Conditioning:** Apply a quality leather conditioner (Leather Honey, Bickmore Bick 4) every 6 months. Prevents cracking and feeds the leather's natural oils.

**Avoid overstuffing:** A wallet carrying 12+ cards constantly will develop irreversible fold stress. Carry what you need (6 cards maximum for slim carry).

**Breaking in new leather:** New vegetable-tanned leather is stiff. Flex the wallet gently, carry it daily, and it will soften to a perfect fit within 2–4 weeks.

**Water exposure:** Full-grain and bridle leather can handle incidental water exposure. Allow to dry naturally — never use heat. Apply conditioner after drying.
