## What to Look For

**Preamp quality** is the most important spec. A preamp that adds noise or distorts at high gain settings will affect every recording you make. Focusrite and UA are the safe choices for clean, quiet gain.

**Input count:** Most home recordists need 2 inputs. If you record drums or live bands, you need 8+.

**Latency:** Look for "round-trip latency" under 10ms — most modern interfaces achieve this. Anything above 20ms makes real-time monitoring feel "wrong."

**Software bundle:** Most interfaces ship with a DAW. Ableton Live Lite is the most popular. PreSonus ships with Studio One Professional, which is a full commercial license worth $400.

**Bus power vs. wall power:** Bus-powered interfaces (USB-powered) are more portable. Interfaces with many inputs require wall power due to higher current draw.
