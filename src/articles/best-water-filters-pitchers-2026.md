## What a Filter Pitcher Actually Does (And Doesn't)

Tap water in most U.S. cities is legally safe to drink, but "safe" and "good-tasting" are different standards. Chlorine, chloramine, and trace minerals create the flat, chemical aftertaste that drives most people toward filtered or bottled water. A filter pitcher solves that — cheaply and simply.

The difference between a $25 Brita and a $95 Clearly Filtered isn't just price. It's what gets through. Carbon filters remove chlorine and improve taste. More advanced pitchers add ion exchange resin to capture lead, mercury, PFAS, and dissolved heavy metals that basic carbon misses entirely.

---

## Key Buying Factors

**Certifications:** NSF/ANSI 42 = taste/odor improvement. NSF/ANSI 53 = health contaminants (lead, mercury). NSF P473 = PFAS. No certification = unverified claims.

**Filter lifespan and cost:** Calculate annual cost, not just the pitcher. Brita Standard: $6/filter × 6 filters/year = $36/yr. ZeroWater: $16/filter × 5/year = $80/yr. Clearly Filtered: $30/filter × 3/year = $90/yr. Waterdrop Chubby: $15/filter × 2/year = $30/yr.

**Flow rate:** ZeroWater's 5-stage system is noticeably slower than Brita. Slow pitchers frustrate users into skipping filtration.

**Capacity:** 10-cup pitchers weigh 5–7 lbs when full. A lighter 5-cup pitcher requires more refills but is easier for elderly or smaller users.

Most pitchers reduce chlorine taste and odor, some heavy metals, and particulates. The ZeroWater 5-stage filter reduces virtually all dissolved solids to 0 ppm. The Clearly Filtered pitcher is the only pitcher with documented PFAS (forever chemical) removal. Standard Brita filters do not remove lead or PFAS.

**Which water filter pitcher removes PFAS?**
