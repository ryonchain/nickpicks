## What to Look for in a Smart Bulb

**Hub vs. hub-free:** Philips Hue uses a Zigbee hub (included in starter kits) for rock-solid local control. Govee, LIFX, and Wyze connect directly via Wi-Fi — simpler setup, but dependent on a stable router.

**Brightness (lumens):** Standard 60W replacement = 800 lumens. Look for 800-1100 lumens for main room lighting. Dim smart bulbs waste money.

**Color temperature:** Warm white (2700K) suits bedrooms. Cool white (4000-5000K) suits offices. Tunable white lets you adjust. RGBWW adds full color plus tunable white.

**Dimming range:** Cheap smart bulbs only dim to 10-20%. Quality bulbs dim to 1%, which matters for night lights and ambiance.

**App reliability:** Research the brand's app history. Brands acquired by larger companies or with a history of discontinuing products (Wink, Insteon) have bricked bulbs mid-lifecycle.

## FAQ

**Do smart bulbs need a hub?**
Wi-Fi bulbs (Govee, LIFX, Wyze) connect directly to your router. Zigbee bulbs (Sengled, Hue) need a hub but are more reliable at scale.

**Can I dim smart bulbs with a regular dimmer switch?**
No — smart bulbs need constant power. Standard dimmer switches interfere with them. Use smart switches or the app to dim.

**How many smart bulbs can I have on one Wi-Fi network?**
Most home routers handle 20-50 devices comfortably. Beyond that, consider Zigbee or a mesh Wi-Fi upgrade.
