    answer: "Single-motor desks drive both legs from a single motor via a crossbar, which limits weight capacity (typically 150–200 lbs) and can cause uneven lift under heavy loads. ## Electric Standing Desk Buying Guide

### Frame vs. Complete Desk
Most standing desk manufacturers sell the **frame only** — you source your own desktop. This saves money and lets you customize tabletop material (wood, bamboo, laminate). Complete desk packages cost slightly more but are faster to deploy.

### Weight Capacity Planning
Add up the weight of everything on your desk: monitors, arms, equipment, accessories. Most users with a standard dual-monitor setup need 80–120 lbs of capacity. Buy at least 50% more capacity than you think you need.

### Height Range Matters for Both Sitting and Standing
The right desk must go **low enough for seated use** and **high enough for standing**. Check both the minimum and maximum height. Taller users (6'+) need desks with higher maximum height; shorter users need lower minimums. The ideal standing height = elbow height when standing relaxed.

### Stability: Single vs. Dual vs. Four-Leg
- **Single motor, 2-leg**: budget-friendly, adequate for light use
- **Dual motor, 2-leg**: better stability, higher weight capacity
- **4-leg**: premium stability, handles heavy equipment with zero wobble

### Programmable Presets
At minimum, look for 2 presets (sitting + standing). Four presets are ideal — you can set morning standing height, afternoon sitting height, and two custom positions for video calls or focused work.
