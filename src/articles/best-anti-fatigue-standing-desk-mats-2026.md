## Anti-Fatigue Mat Buying Guide

### What Thickness Do You Actually Need?

| Thickness | Best For | Feel |
|-----------|----------|------|
| 1/2" (12mm) | Light standing, budget option | Minimal cushion |
| 3/4" (19mm) | Most users — daily standing | Balanced cushion and support |
| 1" (25mm) | Long sessions, heel support, heavy users | Maximum cushion |

### Foam vs. Rubber vs. Gel: Which Material?

**PU Foam** — Best balance of comfort and price. Rebounds well. Can compress over time.

**Nitrile Rubber** — Most durable. Commercial-grade. Firmer underfoot. Ideal for high-traffic areas.

**Gel Hybrid** — Added heel cushioning. Good for shoe wearers. Can develop odor over time.

### Key Features to Prioritize

**1. Beveled edges** — Critical for safety. A mat without beveled edges is a tripping hazard, especially when stepping off while distracted.

**2. Non-slip bottom** — Check this carefully. On hardwood and laminate floors, even mats with "grip" bottoms can wander. Look for nubs or deep-texture patterns.

**3. Flat vs. textured surface** — Flat surfaces clean easily. Textured surfaces (like the Topo) provide standing variety but collect more debris.

**4. Size matching your standing position** — Make sure the mat is wide enough for your natural standing stance. Most people need at least 20" wide.

## FAQs

**How long does an anti-fatigue mat last?**
Quality PU foam mats last 2–4 years with daily use before noticeable compression occurs. Rubber and nitrile mats can last 5–10 years. If you notice the mat feels "flat" under your feet, it's time to replace it — a compressed mat provides no benefit.

**Can I use an anti-fatigue mat on carpet?**
Most standing mats perform best on hard floors. On carpet, the mat can shift and the cushioning effect is partly absorbed by the carpet underneath. If you must use one on carpet, look for a mat with a deeply textured or spiked bottom for grip, or use a carpet anchor pad underneath.

**Do I need an anti-fatigue mat if I already have a thick rug?**
A decorative rug doesn't substitute for an anti-fatigue mat. Anti-fatigue mats use specific foam or rubber densities calibrated to promote micro-movements — a standard rug provides surface softness but no ergonomic benefit and can actually be unstable underfoot.
