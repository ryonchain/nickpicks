  - question: "What's the difference between linear, tactile, and clicky switches?"
    answer: "Linear switches (Red, Speed) have smooth travel with no bump — great for fast gaming keypresses. Tactile switches (Brown, Clear) have a bump you feel mid-keystroke without the click sound — the most versatile for mixed gaming and typing. Clicky switches (Blue, Green) have both a tactile bump and an audible click — excellent typing feel but loud in shared spaces."
  - question: "Are hotswap keyboards worth it?"
    answer: "Yes, especially if you're new to mechanical keyboards. Hotswap sockets let you change switches without soldering — you can try tactile, linear, and clicky switches to find your preference without buying a new board. Most keyboards under $100 now offer hotswap."

Go TKL or 65% if desk space or wrist travel to mouse is a concern."

## How to Choose a Mechanical Keyboard

**Pick your layout first.** Numpad users need full-size. Most gamers prefer TKL — it's the right balance of keys and desk space. Writers who don't need arrow keys love 65% or 60% for the compact footprint.

**Try before you commit to a switch type.** If you've never used mechanical, get a switch tester from Amazon before buying a full board. Spending $10 to find your preference saves $150 later.

**Hotswap is worth prioritizing.** Most keyboards at $80+ now offer it. Being able to swap switches without soldering makes experimenting cheap and easy.
