## Coffee Table Sizing

**Rule of thumb:** Coffee table should be 2/3 the length of your sofa.

| Sofa Length | Coffee Table Width |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 72–84" (3-seat) | 48–56" |
| 84–96" (large 3-seat) | 56–64" |
| Sectional | Round table or two smaller ottomans |

**Height:** Coffee table should be within 1–2 inches of sofa seat height. Standard sofa seat is 17–19"; standard coffee table is 16–18".

**Clearance:** Leave 12–18 inches between sofa and coffee table for comfortable legroom.

## What to Consider When Buying

**Height relative to sofa:** The standard rule is within 1–2 inches of your sofa seat height. Too low makes reaching uncomfortable. Too high looks awkward and creates a formal, disconnected feel.

**Material and durability:**
- Glass: Sleek, easy to clean, shows fingerprints, breaks if struck
- Solid wood: Durable, ages beautifully, can scratch
- Metal: Industrial look, nearly indestructible, cold to the touch
- MDF/laminate: Budget-friendly, chip-resistant to impacts but not scratch-resistant

**Traffic flow:** In smaller rooms, the coffee table can obstruct movement. Round tables and ottomans are easier to navigate around than sharp rectangular corners.

**Storage need:** If your living room lacks shelving or cabinetry, a coffee table with a shelf or drawer adds meaningful organization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the correct height for a coffee table?**
The coffee table should be within 1–2 inches of your sofa seat height. Most sofa seats are 17–19" high, making standard coffee table height 16–18". For very low sofas (floor couches or sectionals with a 14" seat), look for a coffee table in the 12–15" range.

**Is an ottoman better than a coffee table?**
Ottomans are better when: you have children (no sharp corners), want additional seating flexibility, or prefer a soft landing for feet. Coffee tables are better when you want a stable surface for drinks, books, or decorative objects without a tray.

**How do I style a coffee table?**
Use the "rule of three": anchor with one large object (book stack or plant), add one mid-height object (vase or lantern), and one small accent (candle or decorative object). A tray unifies small objects and keeps the surface organized. Leave 40% of the table surface empty for practical use.

**What size coffee table for a sectional sofa?**
A round coffee table (36"–42" diameter) works well with most L-shaped sectionals, positioned at the interior corner. For long straight sectionals (over 100"), two smaller rectangular tables side-by-side (24" x 24" each) is more practical than a single large table.
