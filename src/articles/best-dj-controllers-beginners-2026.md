## Software: What Comes Included

Almost every controller ships with DJ software. Here's what the major options offer:

**Rekordbox (Pioneer):** Free plan for basic mixing. Plus plan ($10/month or with DDJ-400 subscription) adds cloud library sync, video mixing, and advanced effects.

**Serato DJ Lite:** Free with compatible hardware. Limited to 2 decks and basic effects. Pro upgrade ($15/month or included with some controllers) adds 4 decks, FX, and Serato Flip for custom rearrangements.

**Traktor (Native Instruments):** Powerful stems mode that separates any track into 4 stems (drums, bass, melody, vocals) in real time. Requires compatible hardware for full features.

**DJUCED (Hercules):** Free, designed for learning. Best interactive tutorial system of any DJ software.

## What Beginners Actually Need

- **Jog wheels:** Every controller has them. Size and weight matter more than brand name.
- **Built-in audio interface:** Essential. Lets you plug speakers directly into the controller without buying a separate interface.
- **Software integration:** Stick to the software your controller ships with until you outgrow it.
- **Headphone output:** Needed for pre-cueing (previewing the next track in your headphones before mixing it in). All controllers listed have one.
