## What to Look For in a Handheld Vacuum

### Suction Power

Look for airwatts (AW) or kPa specifications. As a rough guide: 10-13 kPa is adequate for light use, 15+ kPa handles everyday messes, and 20+ kPa (or 100+ AW for Dyson's measurement) handles embedded debris and pet hair.

### Battery Life

Most handheld vacuums offer 15-40 minutes of cordless use. For light daily use, 20 minutes is sufficient. If you're doing car detailing or whole-house spot cleaning sessions, aim for 30+ minutes.

### Dustbin Size

A small dustbin means frequent interruptions to empty. For quick daily use, this is fine. For bigger jobs, look for larger capacity or at least a tool-free emptying mechanism so the process isn't tedious.

### Filtration

If allergies are a concern, look for HEPA filtration. A HEPA filter captures 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger — including most allergens, dust mites, and pet dander.

### Weight

This matters more than people think. A 5-pound vacuum isn't heavy to carry, but it's awkward to hold at arm's length while cleaning a car ceiling. Lighter is better for handheld use specifically.
