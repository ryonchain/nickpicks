## What to Look for in a Smart LED Desk Lamp

**Color temperature range**: 2700K is warm yellow (evening reading). 5000K–6500K is cool blue-white (daytime focus). A range of at least 2700K–5000K covers most scenarios. Narrower ranges limit flexibility.

**Color rendering (CRI)**: CRI measures how accurately the lamp renders colors. RA90+ is good; RA95+ is excellent. Important for design work, photography, and anyone who needs to accurately judge colors.

**Flicker rate**: Cheap LED drivers flicker at a rate invisible to the eye but registered by the brain — contributing to eye fatigue and headaches. Look for lamps that specify "flicker-free" — all picks on this list qualify.

**Brightness in lux**: 500–800 lux is adequate for casual reading. 1000+ lux is better for focused work. 1500+ lux is professional/studio grade.

**Smart features you'll actually use**: App control is valuable if you use smart home routines. Voice control is genuinely useful hands-free. Music sync is fun for 20 minutes. Be honest about which features you'll use in month 6, not month 1.
