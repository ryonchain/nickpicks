## Kindle vs. Kobo vs. Phone — Why Kindle Wins on Prime Day

**Phone reading problems:** Phone screens backlit at blue wavelengths disrupt sleep, cause eye strain, and compete with notifications for your attention. E-ink screens reflect light like paper — you can read in sunlight, in the dark without eye strain, and your phone stays where it belongs.

**Kobo vs. Kindle:** Kobo makes excellent e-readers with better library integration (OverDrive/Libby) and no locked-in ecosystem. But Kobo doesn't discount on Amazon Prime Day — you won't find Kobo deals in this window. If you want the best deal on an e-reader right now, Kindle wins by default.

**Library books on Kindle:** You can borrow library books on Kindle via the Libby/OverDrive app — the Kobo advantage disappears once you install Libby.
