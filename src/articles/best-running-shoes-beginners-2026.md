## Buying Guide for Beginner Runners

### Do You Need Neutral or Stability?

**Neutral shoes:** Correct for runners with normal or high arches. Foot doesn't roll significantly inward. Most neutral cushioned shoes work.

**Stability shoes (medial post):** For overpronators — runners with flat feet whose foot rolls inward excessively. Signs: inner edge shoe wear, knee pain on long runs, collapsed arch.

**Motion control shoes:** For severe overpronation. Stiffer and more structured than stability shoes. Usually unnecessary unless prescribed by a podiatrist.

**Quick test:** Wet your foot and step on a paper bag. A normal arch shows a band connecting heel to forefoot. Flat feet show nearly the full footprint. High arches show only the outer edge and heel/forefoot.

### Stack Height and Drop Explained

**Stack height:** Total midsole thickness. Higher = more cushion and impact absorption. Maximum cushion shoes (HOKA) have 35–40mm. Standard is 25–28mm.

**Drop (heel-to-toe offset):** Heel height minus forefoot height. Higher drop (10–12mm) promotes heel striking — fine for beginners transitioning from walking. Lower drop (4–8mm) encourages midfoot striking — better for form, but requires calf/Achilles conditioning.

**Beginner recommendation:** 8–12mm drop. Don't try to force low-drop or zero-drop shoes as a beginner — it's a common cause of Achilles tendinitis.

### Breaking In New Running Shoes

- Week 1–2: Short runs only (20–30 minutes). Let your feet adapt.
- Week 3–4: Gradually extend distance.
- Replace at 300–500 miles — cushioning degrades before uppers show visible wear.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do expensive running shoes prevent injuries?**
Research shows that the "right fit" matters more than price. A $90 shoe that fits your foot type and gait correctly prevents more injuries than a $180 shoe in the wrong category. That said, premium midsole foams (PWRRUN, DNA Loft) do provide measurably better cushioning.

**Should I get fitted at a running store?**
Strongly recommended for your first pair. A good running store will watch you walk/run, analyze your gait, and recommend the right category (neutral/stability/max cushion). Free service, no obligation to buy.

**How many miles do running shoes last?**
300–500 miles for most running shoes. Track your miles (most running apps do this automatically). Signs of worn cushion: increased soreness after runs, visible midsole compression, reduced "bounce" feel.
