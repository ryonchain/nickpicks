## What to Look For in a Home Office Monitor

### Panel Type

**IPS** panels offer the best color accuracy and viewing angles — the default for office use. **VA** panels have higher contrast ratios but worse off-angle color. **TN** panels are the fastest for gaming but worst for color — avoid for office work.

### Resolution

- **1080p (FHD):** Fine at 24 inches, cramped at 27+.
- **1440p (QHD):** The sweet spot for 27-inch monitors.
- **4K (UHD):** Ideal at 27–32 inches. Text is razor sharp.

### USB-C Power Delivery

If you use a MacBook, ThinkPad, or other USB-C laptop, prioritize a monitor with USB-C PD (Power Delivery). This lets you run one cable for video, data, and charging.

### Ergonomics

Adjustable height, tilt, and swivel matter more than most buyers realize. Standing desks change your monitor height constantly — ensure the stand accommodates that range.

## FAQ

**What is the best monitor size for a home office?**
27 inches at 1440p or 4K is the sweet spot for most desks. 32 inches works well in larger spaces.

**Do I need 4K for a home office monitor?**
Not required, but 4K sharpens text meaningfully for long reading sessions and makes spreadsheets much more comfortable.

**What refresh rate do I need for office work?**
60Hz is sufficient for office work. Only get 144Hz+ if you also game on the monitor.

**Is ultrawide better than dual monitors?**
For most users, yes. One ultrawide eliminates the center bezel, reduces cable clutter, and uses one display input.
