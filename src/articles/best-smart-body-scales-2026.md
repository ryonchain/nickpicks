## Do Smart Scales Accurately Measure Body Fat?

Honest answer: bioelectrical impedance (BIA) — the technology used in all consumer smart scales — has a ±3–5% margin of error vs. DEXA scan (medical gold standard). However:

- Consistency matters more than absolute accuracy for tracking trends
- Measuring at the same time each day (morning, after bathroom, before food) significantly reduces variation
- Premium scales (Withings, Garmin) have more electrode points and better algorithms

Use the numbers as trend data, not gospel. A scale saying 22% vs. DEXA's 19% is fine as long as your scale consistently shows the trend over time.

## What to Look For

**Sensor count:** 4 sensors (2 foot + 2 heel) provide better body composition accuracy than 2-sensor designs.

**Wi-Fi vs. Bluetooth:** Wi-Fi scales sync automatically in the background; Bluetooth requires opening the app within range. Wi-Fi is worth paying for.

**Ecosystem:** Check if your scale syncs with the apps you use (Apple Health, Garmin Connect, MyFitnessPal, Strava).

**Weight limit:** Most scales go to 330–400 lbs. Check the limit before purchasing.

**Number of users:** If multiple household members will use the scale, ensure it has enough user profiles.
