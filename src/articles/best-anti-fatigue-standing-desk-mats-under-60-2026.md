## FAQ

**How thick should an anti-fatigue mat be?**
Aim for at least 3/4 inch (0.75") of thickness. That's the minimum needed to meaningfully reduce joint compression on hard floors. Mats thinner than 1/2 inch provide little real cushioning and compress flat within months. Mats over 1 inch can feel unstable underfoot. Every pick on this list hits the 3/4-inch sweet spot.

**Are flat mats or contoured mats better for standing desks?**
It depends on how long you stand. Flat mats work well for sessions under 30–45 minutes — they cushion the floor and reduce joint compression. Contoured mats like the Topo Mini have raised ridges and slopes that encourage subtle micro-movements, which is more effective for sessions lasting an hour or more. If you stand for short bursts throughout the day, a flat mat is perfectly adequate and easier to clean.

**How do you clean a standing desk mat?**
For smooth-top mats (Gorilla Grip, Sky Mat, Amazon Basics), wipe down with a damp cloth and mild dish soap — most spills clean up in seconds. For textured foam surfaces, use a soft brush to dislodge debris before wiping. Avoid harsh solvents or bleach, which degrade the foam. Never put a standing mat in a washing machine.
