## Premium Diffuser Buying Guide

### Ultrasonic vs Nebulizing: The Core Choice

| Factor | Ultrasonic | Nebulizing |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| Water required | Yes | No |
| Aroma strength | Moderate | High |
| Oil consumption | Low | High |
| Coverage | Up to 400 sq ft | 500–1000 sq ft |
| Maintenance | Clean weekly | Clean monthly |
| Noise level | Very low | Low to moderate |
| Cost | $ to $$$ | $$$ |

For most people, ultrasonic is the right choice. For therapeutic use or large spaces, consider nebulizing.

### Coverage Area Reality Check
Manufacturers test coverage in ideal conditions (no ventilation, sealed room). Practical coverage is typically 30–40% less. For an open floor plan, choose a diffuser rated at 2x your target area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I choose between ultrasonic and nebulizing?**
If you want ambient scent for a room, choose ultrasonic. If you're using oils therapeutically (for respiratory support, sleep, mood management) and want maximum concentration, choose nebulizing. Most home users are satisfied with ultrasonic.

**Can I use any essential oil brand with these diffusers?**
Yes, for all ultrasonic and nebulizing diffusers above. Avoid using fragrance oils (synthetic) in nebulizing diffusers — they can damage the glass atomizer. The Pura smart diffuser is the exception; it uses only Pura's proprietary fragrance pods.

**How often should I clean my diffuser?**
Ultrasonic diffusers: clean weekly to prevent oil residue buildup on the ultrasonic plate. Use a cotton swab with white vinegar or a few drops of rubbing alcohol. Nebulizing diffusers: clean monthly by running pure alcohol through the glass reservoir.

**Is diffusing essential oils safe around pets?**
Many oils are toxic to cats and some are harmful to dogs. Specifically avoid: eucalyptus, tea tree, clove, and cinnamon around cats. Consult a veterinarian before diffusing in spaces shared with pets. Always provide ventilation.
