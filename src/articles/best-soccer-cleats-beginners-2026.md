## Soccer Cleat Types: What Beginners Need to Know

Before buying, understand the surface you're playing on:

| Surface | Cleat Type | What to Look For |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Natural grass | FG (Firm Ground) | Molded conical or blade studs |
| Artificial turf (AstroTurf) | TF (Turf) | Rubber nubs instead of studs |
| Indoor courts | IC (Indoor) | Flat rubber outsole |
| Soft/wet grass | SG (Soft Ground) | Removable metal studs |

**Most beginners want FG cleats** — they work on dry natural grass and firmer artificial grass. Don't buy SG (metal studs) for a beginner.

## Cleat Fit Guide

### How Soccer Cleats Should Fit

- Toe should have 1/4" – 1/2" of space from the front of the cleat
- Heel should sit securely — no slipping
- Width: cleats run narrower than regular shoes; go up half a size if you're between sizes

### Nike vs. Adidas Sizing

| Brand | Fit Tendency |
|-------|-------------|
| Nike | Narrow to medium; go up half a size if you're wide |
| Adidas | Medium to wide; Copa line is widest |
| PUMA | Narrow to medium |
| Under Armour | Medium; good for standard feet |
