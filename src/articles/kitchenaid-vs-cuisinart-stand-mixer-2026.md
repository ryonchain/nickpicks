## Head-to-Head: Key Categories

### Power and Performance

For bread dough, the Cuisinart SM-50's 500W motor vs the Artisan's 325W is a real difference. Stiff doughs (pizza, bagels, dense whole wheat) benefit from higher wattage. That said, KitchenAid's motor is engineered for sustained performance — it may be lower wattage but handles strain without overheating.

**Winner: Cuisinart (for heavy doughs), KitchenAid (for consistency)**

### Attachments

This is KitchenAid's defining advantage. 80+ attachments vs Cuisinart's limited lineup. If you want to use your mixer as a pasta maker, meat grinder, or ice cream churner, KitchenAid wins by default.

**Winner: KitchenAid (no contest)**

### Bowl Design

Both use stainless steel bowls. KitchenAid uses a tilt-head (Artisan) or bowl-lift (Pro 600) — both work well. Cuisinart uses a tilt-back head on most models.

**Winner: Tie**

### Noise Level

Both produce significant motor noise at high speeds. KitchenAid Artisan is generally rated as quieter for everyday use. Cuisinart can be louder under heavy load.

**Winner: KitchenAid (marginally)**

### Longevity

KitchenAid's proven 25+ year track record is unmatched. Replacement parts — bowls, beaters, gaskets — are available everywhere. Cuisinart stand mixers are durable but have a shorter proven product history.

**Winner: KitchenAid**

### Value

At $220–280, the Cuisinart SM-50 delivers 85% of KitchenAid's everyday functionality at 60% of the price.

**Winner: Cuisinart**
