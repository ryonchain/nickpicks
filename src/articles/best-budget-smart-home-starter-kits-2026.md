## Choosing an Ecosystem Before You Buy

This is the most important decision in smart home shopping. Your starter kit determines which devices you can add later without friction.

**Alexa (Amazon):** The widest device compatibility. Nearly every smart home device works with Alexa. Best if you shop on Amazon frequently and don't care about deep automation.

**Google Home:** Strong Android integration. Assistant routines are powerful. Best if you use Android phones, Google Workspace, or Chromecast devices.

**Apple HomeKit:** Privacy-focused, local processing, works with Siri. More limited device selection but premium experience. Best for iPhone-heavy households.

**SmartThings (Samsung):** The most powerful hub-based option. Runs automations locally, works across Zigbee, Z-Wave, Wi-Fi, and Matter. Best for users who want to build a serious automation system.

**Matter:** The new universal standard. Matter devices work across Alexa, Google, Apple, and SmartThings. Buy Matter-compatible devices and you're not locked in.

## FAQ

**Do I need a hub to start a smart home?**
No — Wi-Fi devices (Amazon, Kasa, Govee) connect directly to your router. A hub (SmartThings, Hue Bridge) adds local control and multi-protocol support but isn't required to start.

**What's the first smart home device I should buy?**
A smart speaker (Echo or Nest) + one smart plug. This gets you voice control and a useful automation (turning off a lamp) without commitment.

**Are Matter devices worth it in 2026?**
Yes, if you're starting fresh. Matter devices work across Alexa, Google, Apple, and SmartThings — future-proofing your purchase.
