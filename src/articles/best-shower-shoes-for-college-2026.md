## What Makes a Good Dorm Shower Shoe

### Non-Slip Sole (Non-Negotiable)

Wet tile is a fall hazard. Look for:
- Deep tread grooves on the outsole
- Rubber (not foam) sole material
- Heel strap or closed back for extra stability

Flat foam slides look great but slip on wet tile. The extra second of stability is worth it.

### Drainage Design

Shoes that hold water breed bacteria. Good drainage means:
- Open-toe or open-side design
- Drainage holes in the footbed
- Materials that don't absorb water (rubber, EVA foam, synthetic)

Avoid canvas or fabric shower shoes entirely — they stay wet for hours and become mold magnets.

### Size and Fit

Dorm shower shoes should fit snugly but not tight. Flip flops that are too loose flap against your heel and trip you on wet floors. Aim for a half-size down from your street shoe if between sizes.

## Shower Shoe Care Tips

**After each shower:**
- Shake out water
- Leave outside your shower caddy to air dry (not inside your room where they stay wet)

**Weekly:**
- Rinse with warm water and mild soap
- Let dry in sunlight when possible (UV kills bacteria)

**Replace when:**
- Sole shows deep wear through the grip pattern
- Straps start to crack or separate from footbed
- They have any persistent odor after cleaning
