## Buyer's Guide: How to Style Midi Dresses

### By Occasion
- **Work/professional:** Bodycon or fit-and-flare midi + blazer + pointed-toe heels or kitten mules
- **Casual/weekend:** Smocked, floral, or jersey midi + sneakers or sandals + denim jacket
- **Evening/going out:** Flowy V-neck or satin midi + strappy heels + minimal jewelry
- **Vacation/resort:** Floral or linen midi + flat sandals + sun hat

### Shoe Pairings
- **Midi + sneakers:** Casual, effortless, youthful
- **Midi + flat sandals:** Relaxed summer, beach, bohemian
- **Midi + kitten heels:** Office-appropriate, refined
- **Midi + block heels:** Work-to-evening versatility
- **Midi + strappy heels:** Evening, special occasions

### Styling for Height
- **Petite:** Choose midi styles with waist definition; avoid very full skirts that overwhelm; high-waisted styles elongate the leg
- **Tall:** Full-length midi and maxi dresses look especially elegant; avoid cuts that make the midi look like an awkward mini
- **Curvy:** Wrap-style or smocked midi are most adjustable; A-line silhouettes balance proportions

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What length is a midi dress?**
Typically 38–48 inches from shoulder to hem, landing between knee and ankle. True midi should hit mid-calf.

**Are midi dresses appropriate for the office?**
Yes — structured fabrics in neutral colors with appropriate necklines work for most professional environments. Pair with a blazer for formal settings.

**Can petite women wear midi dresses?**
Absolutely. High-waisted styles and defined silhouettes work beautifully on petite frames. Avoid very voluminous skirts that can overwhelm a smaller frame.

**How do you accessorize a midi dress?**
For casual: minimal jewelry, simple bag, sandals. For evening: statement earrings, clutch, strappy heels. Belting at the waist adds definition to flowy silhouettes.
