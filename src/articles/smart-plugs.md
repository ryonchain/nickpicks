## Smart Plug Buying Guide

### Ecosystem Compatibility

**Amazon Alexa ecosystem:** Kasa EP25 (best features) or Amazon Smart Plug (deepest Alexa integration)
**Google Home ecosystem:** Kasa EP25 or Govee (budget)
**Apple HomeKit / Siri:** Eve Energy — nothing else comes close for HomeKit quality
**All ecosystems / future-proof:** Eve Energy (Matter + Thread)

In 2026, **Matter** is the standard to look for if you're uncertain about your long-term ecosystem. Matter smart plugs work with Alexa, Google Home, HomeKit, and SmartThings without manufacturer lock-in.

### Energy Monitoring: Is It Worth It?

Yes — for most users. Energy monitoring reveals surprising data: a gaming PC left on standby draws 50–80W constantly. An old refrigerator in the garage draws $15–20/month. A space heater costs more per hour than expected. The Kasa EP25's monthly cost estimate in the app makes this tangible and actionable. For lamps and low-draw devices, energy monitoring is less useful. For appliances, it's often the most valuable feature.

### Amperage and Load Rating

Standard smart plugs are rated for 10–15 amps. Check your device before plugging in:
- Lamps: 60–200W (fine for all smart plugs)
- Fans: 100–400W (fine for all)
- Space heaters: 1500W — requires 15A rating, don't use 10A plugs
- Window AC units: 500–1500W — check rating before use
- Hair dryers: 1200–1875W — fine for 15A plugs, not 10A

### 2.4 GHz vs. 5 GHz WiFi

All smart plugs on this list require 2.4 GHz WiFi. Most home routers broadcast both bands under the same name — if you're having trouble connecting a smart plug, temporarily separate your 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz networks during setup, connect your phone to 2.4 GHz, and complete setup. After setup, both can be on the same network name again.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do smart plugs work without internet?**
Basic on/off via the physical button always works. Scheduling set before the outage typically continues. Voice control and remote app access require internet. Some smart plugs (Eve Energy with Thread, some Lutron products) support local processing that works without cloud connectivity — but these are exceptions. For most smart plugs, assume internet is required for voice and app control.

**Are smart plugs safe for high-draw appliances like space heaters?**
Yes, if the smart plug is rated for the appliance's amperage. Most space heaters draw 12.5A (1500W ÷ 120V). Use smart plugs rated for 15A (like the Kasa EP25). Never use a 10A plug with a 1500W heater — fire risk. Always check the manufacturer's max load rating. Using smart plugs to automate space heaters is common and safe when properly rated.

**What is Matter and why does it matter?**
Matter is an open-source smart home connectivity standard backed by Apple, Google, Amazon, and Samsung. Matter devices work with any Matter-compatible hub or app — no ecosystem lock-in. If you buy a Matter smart plug today and switch from Alexa to Google Home next year, the plug still works. In 2026, Matter is becoming the standard for new smart home devices.

**Can smart plugs save money on electricity?**
Yes, in specific ways. Smart plugs eliminate "phantom load" — the energy devices draw while on standby. The average US home has 40+ devices on standby drawing 5–10% of total electricity. Smart plugs on TVs, gaming consoles, and desktop PCs can cut standby consumption. The Kasa EP25's energy monitoring makes it easy to identify the biggest offenders and schedule them off overnight.
