## How to Tell Real Silk from Fake

**Burn test:** Real silk burns slowly, smells like burning hair, and leaves a crushable ash. Synthetic "silk" melts into a plastic bead.

**Price reality:** Authentic mulberry silk pajama sets under $80 almost certainly aren't real silk. Silk costs money. If the price seems impossibly low for "100% silk," read the materials label closely.

**Momme weight:** Look for this spec. Real silk brands list momme weight. If a seller doesn't disclose it, that's a red flag.

**"Silk satin" vs "silk":** "Silk satin" means a silk weave pattern applied to polyester. "Silk" or "mulberry silk" should mean the real fiber.

## Caring for Silk Pajamas

- **Machine washable silk** (Lunya): Use cold water, delicate cycle, mesh laundry bag, skip the dryer — line dry.
- **Standard silk** (LilySilk, Fishers Finery): Hand wash in cool water with a gentle detergent (Woolite or The Laundress Delicate Wash). Never wring. Roll in a towel to absorb water, then lay flat to dry.
- **Avoid:** Hot water, bleach, direct sunlight for extended periods, and the dryer.
