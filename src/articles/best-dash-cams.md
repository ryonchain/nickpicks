## Key Specs That Matter

**Resolution** — 1080p is the minimum for clear license plate reading at highway speeds. 1440p (QHD) is the sweet spot. 4K captures more detail but requires larger storage cards and generates significantly larger files.

**Night vision** — "Starvis" and "Starvis 2" Sony sensors perform dramatically better in low light than standard sensors. This matters more than resolution for nighttime incidents.

**GPS** — records your speed and location alongside video. Critical for insurance claims and disputes about speed.

**Parking mode** — records while parked. Requires hardwiring to your car's fuse box (or a capacitor buffer) to avoid draining your car battery.

**Storage** — dash cams loop-record onto microSD cards. Most cards fail after 6–12 months of continuous read/write. Use cards rated for high-endurance (Samsung Pro Endurance, Transcend High Endurance).

**Capacitor vs battery** — premium dash cams use supercapacitors rather than lithium batteries. Capacitors handle extreme temperatures (-40°C to 85°C) better than batteries and last longer.

## Installation Guide

**Plug-and-play (cigarette lighter):** Powers off when you turn off the car — no parking mode. Easiest installation.

**Hardwired (fuse box):** Powers from the car's fuse box. Enables parking mode. Requires basic electrical knowledge or a 1-hour installation at a car stereo shop (~$50–75).

**OBD-II power adapter:** Middle ground — powers from OBD port, supports basic parking mode, no permanent wiring. Limited amperage for some cameras.

**MicroSD card recommendation:** Use Samsung Pro Endurance or Transcend High Endurance cards rated for continuous read/write. Standard cards fail faster in dash cam use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need front and rear coverage?**
Rear coverage protects against rear-end collisions and brake-checking disputes. Front-only is adequate for most situations, but if you want comprehensive protection, front+rear is worth it.

**Does a dash cam drain my battery?**
In parking mode, yes — gradually. Hardwired cameras typically draw 100–200mA in parking mode. After 48–72 hours of non-operation, most cars will show reduced battery. Use a voltage cutoff relay (included in most hardwire kits) set to cut power when battery drops to 12V.

**Can dash cam footage be used in court?**
In most US states, yes. Footage with metadata (GPS, timestamp) is generally admissible and respected by insurance companies. Check your state's laws for recording consent requirements.

**Will the dash cam be visible to thieves?**
Yes, if not hidden. Consider hiding the cable cleanly and positioning the camera high on the windshield. Some cameras have a "stealth mode" that disables the LED recording indicator.
