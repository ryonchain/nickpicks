## What to Look for When Buying Lion's Mane

**Non-negotiable:**
1. **Fruiting body, not mycelium** — Look for explicit "fruiting body" labeling. "Mushroom extract" alone is ambiguous.
2. **Beta-glucan content disclosed** — ≥20% is good; ≥25% is excellent. Published testing is the gold standard.
3. **Low starch content** — High starch indicates grain filler from mycelium substrate. Look for <5% starch.

**Red flags:**
- "Proprietary blend" without individual component doses
- "Mycelium" without fruiting body content specified
- No beta-glucan percentage on label or website
- Very low price per mg without quality testing

**Dosing guidance:**
- Start at 500–1,000mg/day
- Work up to 2,000mg/day if well-tolerated
- Take consistently for at least 4 weeks before evaluating effects
- Time of day doesn't matter much — morning is popular for cognitive focus benefits

Lion's mane is one of the few "brain supplements" with a plausible mechanism (NGF stimulation) and early human data. It's not a pharmaceutical-grade cognitive enhancer, but as an adaptogen for long-term brain health support, the evidence is more credible than most nootropic supplements on the market.
