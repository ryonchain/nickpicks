## 10-Inch vs. 18-Inch: Which Size Do You Need?

Ring light diameter directly determines the working distance and fill area:

- **10-inch ring lights:** Ideal for desk use — video calls, podcasting, makeup, close-up content creation. Works well 2–4 feet from the subject. The catch: at 6+ feet, light intensity drops significantly and the ring reflection in eyes becomes a small dot rather than an appealing circle.
- **18-inch ring lights:** Floor-standing setups for full-body shots, stand-up content, or interview setups. Maintains strong fill at 5–8 feet. Required if you're shooting from across a room or capturing more than head and shoulders.

For 90% of home office users and Zoom-call improvers: 10-inch is sufficient. For YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram content where you're visible from the waist up: 18-inch is the better tool.

Additional specs to evaluate:

- **Color temperature range:** Look for 3200K–5600K or wider. 3200K (warm/tungsten) works in naturally warm-lit rooms. 5600K (daylight) is the default for bright, clean video. Mixing color temperatures between your ring light and room lights creates unflattering skin tones.
- **Brightness control:** Stepless (continuous) dimming is better than step-based control — allows precise matching to ambient light levels.
- **Phone holder vs. camera mount:** Cheap phone holder clips work for vertical phone video. For horizontal camera mounting or mirrorless cameras, you need a standard 1/4-20 screw mount.
- **Remote control:** A wireless remote lets you adjust brightness and color temperature without touching the light between takes — useful for solo creators.
- **Tripod height range:** Many 10-inch kits include short desktop tripods (12–18 inches), which limits placement options. Look for a kit with a full-height floor tripod if you need flexibility.

## What to Look For When Buying

**CRI matters for skin tones:** Color Rendering Index measures how accurately a light source renders colors. CRI >95 produces natural, accurate skin tones. Most budget ring lights sit at CRI 85–90 — acceptable but noticeably different from the GVM's CRI >97 for close-up face content.

**Power source:** USB-powered ring lights (10-inch and under) are convenient but limited in output. AC-powered lights (18-inch) deliver significantly more brightness and don't tax your USB ports. For serious studio use, AC power is worth the tether.

**Stand stability:** A 75-inch metal tripod with a heavy ring light is a tipping hazard, especially with a phone or camera mounted. Look for stands with rubber feet and wide base leg spread, or sandbag the legs if you're working in a high-traffic area.

**Color temperature matching:** Your ring light's color temperature should match your room's ambient light. Shooting in a room with warm incandescent bulbs? Set your ring to ~3200K. Working under daylight-balanced LED room lights? Match at 5600K. Mismatched color temperatures create awkward green or orange casts on skin.
