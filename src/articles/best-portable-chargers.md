A portable charger is one of the most practical travel accessories you can own — it means your phone, tablet, or laptop never hits zero on a long flight, a day of meetings, or a hiking trip. The challenge is matching capacity and charge speed to your actual needs without buying a brick that weighs more than your laptop.

The key specs: mAh (capacity), output wattage (charge speed), input wattage (recharge speed), and port configuration. Here are the best portable chargers and power banks for 2026.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose

**Match capacity to use case.** For daily commuting and one phone: 10,000 mAh is sufficient and light. For all-day travel with a tablet: 20,000 mAh. For laptop-dependent travel: 26,000+ mAh with 65W+ USB-C output.

**Check the output wattage, not just mAh.** A 20,000 mAh bank with 5W output charges your phone slowly and may not keep up with active use. Look for USB-C Power Delivery at 18W minimum for phones; 45W+ for tablets; 65W+ for laptops.

**TSA limits.** Power banks above 100Wh (~27,000 mAh at 3.7V) are not allowed in carry-on or checked baggage. Check the Wh rating printed on the bank before flying.

**Recharge speed matters.** A bank that takes 8 hours to recharge is inconvenient for frequent travel. Look for 18W+ input for 10,000 mAh banks; 60W+ input for 20,000+ mAh banks.

A quality [travel accessories kit](/articles/best-travel-accessories-carry-on-2026/) for frequent flyers should include a power bank, a multi-port charging adapter, and a cable organizer — the three items that solve most airport charging problems.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many mAh do I need in a portable charger?**
For smartphones: 10,000 mAh provides 2–3 full charges. For tablets: 20,000 mAh provides 1–2 full charges. For laptops: 26,000 mAh (65Wh) is the TSA carry-on limit and powers most laptops once or twice. For daily travel, 10,000 mAh is enough. For long trips, go to 20,000+.

**What is Power Delivery and why does it matter?**
Power Delivery (PD) is a USB-C charging standard that adjusts voltage and current to charge devices faster and more efficiently. A 65W PD power bank can charge a laptop in roughly the same time as a wall adapter. Without PD, USB-C charging falls back to slower 5W or 9W speeds.

**Are power banks allowed on planes?**
Power banks are allowed in carry-on baggage only — never checked luggage. TSA limits lithium ion batteries to 100Wh per bank (roughly 27,000 mAh at 3.7V). The Anker 737 at 24,000 mAh is within this limit. Always check the Wh rating on the battery, not just mAh.

**How long does it take to recharge a power bank?**
Recharge time depends on the power bank's input wattage and capacity. A 10,000 mAh bank with 18W input takes about 2 hours. The Anker 737 at 65W input recharges its 24,000 mAh in about 2 hours as well. Lower-end banks with 5W input can take 8+ hours for the same capacity.

**Can I charge my MacBook with a portable charger?**
Yes, if the power bank supports USB-C Power Delivery at 65W or higher. The Zendure SuperTank (100W output) and Anker 737 (65W output) both charge MacBook Air and most MacBook Pro models. Check your MacBook's watt requirement — M-series MacBook Airs charge efficiently at 30W–67W.
