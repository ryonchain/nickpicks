## How to Choose a Video Doorbell

### Wired vs. battery: which installation type is right for you

The most important first question is whether you have existing doorbell wiring. If you do, both wired and battery doorbells work — wired operation delivers continuous power and eliminates charging. If you don't have wiring, battery-powered models (Ring 4, Nest Doorbell Battery, Eufy Dual, Arlo Essential) install without any electrical work. The Ring Pro 2 is the only model on this list requiring hardwired installation. Most homes built after 1970 have doorbell wiring; check by locating your existing doorbell button and following the two wires behind it.

### Subscription costs: the real price of a video doorbell

The sticker price of a video doorbell is only part of the cost. Ring and Arlo both provide live view for free but save no motion clips without a subscription. Google Nest's 3-hour free buffer is the best compromise in the industry. Eufy eliminates subscriptions entirely with local storage. Over three years of ownership, a Ring doorbell with Ring Protect ($4.99/month) costs ~$180 in subscriptions alone — more than the hardware. Factor this into your decision, especially if you're considering multiple cameras on the same account.

### AI detection vs. basic motion sensing

Basic motion sensing triggers on any change in the camera's frame — passing cars, blowing shadows, and leaves generate the same alert as a person approaching. AI detection, available on Google Nest and Arlo (with subscription), identifies the type of subject and sends categorized alerts: "Person detected," "Package delivered," "Animal detected." The practical difference is alert fatigue: homes on busy streets can receive dozens of false alerts per day without AI filtering. Google Nest's on-device AI is the most accurate, requiring no cloud round-trip to process detections.

### Ecosystem compatibility before you commit

Each doorbell has a natural home ecosystem. Ring integrates deepest with Alexa and Amazon devices. Google Nest integrates with Google Home and Google Assistant only — no Alexa support. Arlo Essential supports the broadest range (Alexa, Google, HomeKit, SmartThings). Eufy supports Alexa and Google. Before purchasing, confirm which voice assistant and hub you'll use, and verify the doorbell supports it natively rather than through a third-party workaround.
