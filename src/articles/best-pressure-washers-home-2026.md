## Pressure Washer Buying Guide

**Understanding PSI, GPM, and Cleaning Units:**
- **PSI** (pounds per square inch): water pressure force — what cuts through grime
- **GPM** (gallons per minute): water volume — what rinses material away
- **Cleaning Units** = PSI × GPM: the combined measure of real-world cleaning power
- For residential use: 1,600–2,400 PSI / 1.2–1.8 GPM is sufficient for vehicles, decks, siding, and patios
- For heavy-duty: 3,000+ PSI for concrete, paint stripping, oil stains

**Surface-appropriate pressure:**
| Surface | Max PSI |
|---------|---------|
| Car/vehicle | 1,500 PSI |
| Wood deck | 1,500–1,800 PSI |
| Vinyl siding | 1,500–2,000 PSI |
| Concrete/driveway | 2,500–3,000 PSI |
| Brick/masonry | 2,000–2,500 PSI |

Too much pressure on wood or soft surfaces causes fiber damage and etching. Start low and increase as needed.

**Electric vs. gas for 2026:** For 90% of homeowners — vehicles, patios, decks, siding on properties with outdoor outlets — electric is the better choice. No maintenance, quieter, lower purchase price, and 2,000–2,300 PSI is genuinely adequate. Gas makes sense for large properties without outlet access, heavy concrete cleaning, and pre-paint stripping.
