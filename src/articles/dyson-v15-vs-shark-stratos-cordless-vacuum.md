Two brands dominate the cordless vacuum market: Dyson and Shark. One is a premium engineering statement. The other delivers near-identical performance for $200 less.

This comparison breaks down exactly where each wins — and which one you should actually buy.

## Quick Verdict

| | Dyson V15 Detect | Shark Stratos |
|---|---|---|
| Suction power | 230 AW (max) | 150 AW (tested comparable) |
| Runtime | Up to 60 min (eco) | Up to 60 min (regular) |
| Filtration | 5-stage, whole-machine | HEPA + anti-allergen |
| Laser detection | Yes — reveals hidden dust | No |
| Auto mode | Particle sensor | CleanSense IQ |
| Weight | 6.8 lbs | 8.6 lbs |
| Price | $700–750 | $400–500 |
| **Best for** | **Allergy sufferers, detail cleaners** | **Budget-conscious, pet owners** |

**Bottom line:** Dyson V15 wins on technology and filtration. Shark Stratos wins on value. Most households should buy the Shark.

---

## Where Dyson V15 Wins

### Laser Detection (It's Not Gimmicky)
The V15's green laser on the fluffy head illuminates fine dust particles invisible to the naked eye. This sounds like marketing, but it genuinely reveals what you're missing — and once you see it, you can't unsee it.

The built-in particle counter LCD shows you how much debris is being sucked up in real time. When it drops to zero, you know you're done.

### Filtration System
Five-stage filtration captures 99.99% of particles as small as 0.3 microns. The V15 expels air that's measurably cleaner than what it took in. For asthma and allergy sufferers, this distinction is meaningful.

### Weight and Balance
At 6.8 lbs, the V15 is lighter than the Stratos and handles overhead work (stairs, ceiling fans, drapes) without fatigue.

### Brand Ecosystem
If you already own Dyson attachments or plan to buy into the ecosystem (Dyson tools use the same battery platform), V15 compatibility adds value.

---

## Where Shark Stratos Wins

### Price
The Shark Stratos costs $200–300 less than the Dyson V15. That's a meaningful difference, especially given that the performance gap on actual carpet and hard floors is minimal.

### CleanSense IQ Mode
Shark's CleanSense IQ senses debris and automatically adjusts suction. Similar concept to Dyson's auto mode, but without requiring the particle counter technology that inflates the V15's price.

### Odor Neutralizer
The Shark Stratos has an odor-neutralizing filter system that Dyson lacks. If you have pets, this matters — especially on upholstery and rugs where pet odors accumulate.

### Attachments
The Stratos comes with more attachments out of the box and the DuoClean PowerFins head handles both carpet and hard floors in a single pass without switching tools.

---

## Head-to-Head Tests

### Carpet Performance
Both pick up pet hair and embedded dirt effectively. Independent lab tests show the Dyson edges out the Shark on fine particle pickup. In real-world use: both clean carpets well.

**Edge: Dyson (marginal)**

### Hard Floors
The Shark DuoClean head has a soft roller integrated into the main head — it picks up large debris and fine dust simultaneously. The Dyson requires the soft roller head to be attached separately.

**Edge: Shark**

### Runtime
Both advertise up to 60 minutes, but at max suction both drop significantly. At max power:
- Dyson V15: ~10 minutes
- Shark Stratos: ~15 minutes

Real-world cleaning at medium power: both manage a full medium-sized home in a single charge.

**Edge: Shark (marginal)**

### Noise
Dyson is quieter. Shark Stratos runs louder at comparable suction levels.

**Edge: Dyson**

### Maintenance
Both require filter cleaning every 1–3 months. Dyson filters are washable and easier to access. Shark filters require more steps.

**Edge: Dyson**

## Who Should Buy Each

**Buy the Dyson V15 Detect if:**
- You or someone in your home has allergies or asthma
- You want the absolute best filtration available in a cordless vacuum
- The laser detection feature genuinely interests you (it is useful)
- You have a premium home and want a premium tool
- Budget isn't a significant constraint

**Buy the Shark Stratos if:**
- You want to spend $200–300 less without sacrificing cleaning performance
- You have pets and care about odor neutralization
- You primarily clean hard floors (DuoClean head wins here)
- You need more runtime at medium suction

---

## What About the Dyson V12 or Shark IZ?

**Dyson V12 Detect Slim:** Lighter, less suction, smaller bin. Best for smaller homes or light use. Currently ~$500–580.

**Shark IZ462H:** The IZ series is the affordable Shark tier. Less suction, no CleanSense IQ, but excellent for basic cleaning at $250–350.

---

## Final Recommendation

For most households: **buy the Shark Stratos.** It cleans nearly as well as the Dyson V15, handles hard floors better, neutralizes pet odors, and costs meaningfully less.

For allergy sufferers and people who clean obsessively: **buy the Dyson V15.** The filtration system is genuinely superior, and the laser detection provides real feedback that changes how you clean.

Neither choice is wrong. The gap between them is smaller than the price difference suggests.
