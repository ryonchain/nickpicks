# Prime Day 2026 Prep: 10 Things to Do Before July 16

**Amazon Prime Day 2026 starts July 16 at midnight PT** and runs 48 hours through July 17. That's 3 days away. If you do nothing now, you'll spend Prime Day frantically clicking through pages of garbage deals looking for the good ones.

If you spend 20 minutes on this checklist today, you'll know exactly what to buy the moment deals drop — and avoid the pressure-buying traps Amazon sets every year.

---

## 1. Confirm Your Prime Membership Is Active

Deals are Prime-exclusive. If your membership lapsed, you need to renew before July 16 — and a 30-day free trial counts.

[Check your Prime membership status →](https://www.amazon.com/prime?tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"}

If you share a household, [Amazon Household](https://www.amazon.com/myh/manage?tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"} lets you share Prime benefits with one adult and up to 4 teens — they can all shop the deals.

---

## 2. Build Your Wishlist Now (Not During the Sale)

The biggest Prime Day mistake: trying to research products while deals are live. You end up impulse-buying things you didn't need because "it's on sale."

**How to build a smart wishlist:**
1. Open your [Amazon Wishlist](https://www.amazon.com/hz/wishlist/ls?tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"}
2. Add everything you've been considering for the past 3 months
3. Check current prices now and note them
4. On July 16, sort by "Price: Low to High" and compare

If the price on July 16 isn't at least 20% below the price you see today, skip it.

---

## 3. Set Price Drop Alerts

Amazon's price tracking can help you catch deals the moment they go live. Use browser extensions like [CamelCamelCamel](https://camelcamelcamel.com) to set email alerts when items hit your target price.

**How to use it:**
1. Find the product's Amazon URL
2. Paste it into CamelCamelCamel
3. Set a target price (typically 20–30% below current)
4. Get emailed the moment the price drops

This is especially useful for expensive items like TVs, laptops, and robot vacuums where the difference between a real deal and a manufactured discount isn't obvious at a glance.

## 4. Know Which Categories Actually Deliver Real Deals

Not all categories are created equal on Prime Day. Based on historical data:

### Almost Always Excellent (40–60% off is typical):
- **Amazon's own devices**: Echo Dot, Echo Show, Kindle, Fire TV Sticks, Fire tablets, Ring cameras, Blink cameras, Eero routers — Amazon uses Prime Day to push hardware. These discounts are real.
- **Robot vacuums**: Roomba, Roborock, Ecovacs — these hit their annual lows on Prime Day. $200–$400 robots frequently drop to $130–$250.

### Usually Good (20–40% off):
- **Wireless headphones**: Sony XM5, Bose QuietComfort, Beats — large inventory, aggressive discounts
- **Smart home**: Philips Hue, Govee, smart plugs, smart locks
- **Kitchen appliances**: Instant Pot, Ninja, Keurig, air fryers

### Variable — Check the Price History:
- **Laptops and tablets**: Apple rarely discounts significantly. Third-party sellers on Apple products can be real or fake deals. Samsung, Lenovo, ASUS are more reliably discounted.
- **TVs**: LG and Samsung do real cuts. Many "deal" TVs are rebranded house brands.

### Usually Skip:
- **Clothing and fashion**: Deep discounts on things you don't need at full price aren't savings.
- **Grocery**: Deals exist but selection is thin. Better to use Subscribe & Save.
- **Power tools**: Real deals exist but only on name brands. No-name tools with big discounts are rarely worth buying.

## 5. Download the Amazon App (Deals Are Often App-Only)

Amazon routinely makes select deals available only through the app. [Download the Amazon app](https://www.amazon.com/gp/browse.html?node=2350149011&tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"} and enable deal notifications.

During Prime Day:
- Open the app → Deals → "Expiring Soon" filter
- Lightning deals have countdown timers — some run out in minutes

## 6. Look Up Last Year's Prime Day Prices

Before Prime Day 2026 drops, check what Prime Day 2025 prices were for items on your list. [CamelCamelCamel](https://camelcamelcamel.com) shows all-time price history including last year's event.

If the "Prime Day 2026 deal" is the same price as last year's Prime Day deal — it's not a special price, it's just their event price. Pass on urgency, but buy if it's the right time for you.

## 7. Set a Budget — Seriously

Amazon is exceptional at manufacturing urgency. Lightning deals with countdown timers, "Only 3 left!", and 48-hour windows are all designed to trigger impulse buying.

Before July 16, set a hard budget for each category:
- Home/kitchen: $____
- Electronics: $____
- Kids/toys: $____
- Personal care: $____

When your category budget is spent, close the tab. The deals will still be there (or better alternatives will appear in August).

## 8. Know What To Skip Until Fall/Holiday Season

Some categories get equal or better discounts during Black Friday and Cyber Monday:
- **Toys and games**: Toy manufacturers push harder discounts in November. Prime Day toy deals are good, but Black Friday is usually better.
- **Video games and consoles**: Black Friday wins this one almost every year.
- **Large appliances**: Labor Day and Black Friday sales often match or exceed Prime Day.

If you don't urgently need something in one of these categories, wait.

## 9. Check These Categories That Don't Get Hyped But Deliver

Beyond the obvious, these niche areas consistently deliver real deals on Prime Day:

- **Outdoor gear**: Hammocks, folding chairs, portable grills — well-stocked brands discount aggressively in mid-July before inventory peaks for fall.
- **Office supplies and organization**: Desk organizers, cable management, storage — less sexy, deeply discounted.
- **Baby and toddler**: Car seats, strollers, and monitors often see their best prices of the year.
- **Pet supplies**: Dry food, chews, and pet tech (GPS trackers, fountains) see real cuts.

## 10. Use "Add to Cart" as a Price Lock (But Read the Fine Print)

Amazon lets you add items to your cart at the sale price even if the deal expires before you check out — but this only works for Lightning Deals, not all Prime Day prices.

The trick: add to cart the moment you see a good deal, then check out all at once at the end of the day. The items stay at the sale price in your cart until the deal window expires (usually 15 minutes for Lightning Deals after you add to cart).
