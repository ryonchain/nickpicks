## Buying Guide: What 9 Year Olds Actually Want

**Independence signals:** 9-year-olds want to feel capable. Gifts that let them make something, build something, or succeed at something independently land very well.

**Reading level:** Full chapter books at 200-400 pages. Graphic novels (Big Nate, Dog Man, Phoebe and Her Unicorn) for reluctant readers. Fantasy series for confident readers.

**Screen time balance:** If parents want to manage screen time, non-screen STEM kits (Snap Circuits, crystal growing) and outdoor toys (walkie-talkies, hover balls) are the answer.

**Roblox and Minecraft:** Still extremely popular at 9. A gift card ($25) to either platform is always right if they play these games.

**What to avoid:** Baby-skewing toys (anything marketed as "ages 5+"), toys with too many tiny pieces and no storage, toys that require 20+ minutes of setup.
