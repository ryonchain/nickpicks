## Why a Dutch Oven Belongs on Every Wedding Registry

A Dutch oven is the single most versatile kitchen vessel a couple can own. Couples who own a quality Dutch oven cook with it 2–3 times per week across every season.

The enameled cast iron versions (Le Creuset, Staub, Lodge) require no seasoning, work on every cooktop type including induction, go from stovetop to oven seamlessly, and can last 50+ years.

## How to Use a Dutch Oven on Your Registry

**Register for the 5.5–6 Qt:** This is the sweet spot for most couples. Big enough for a whole chicken (4–5 lbs), a large pot roast, or a double batch of soup. Small enough to fit in most ovens without issue.

**Choose a color thoughtfully:** Le Creuset and Staub both offer 20+ colors. Neutral colors (White, Black, Blue) age well. Statement colors (Volcanic Orange, Cherry) become the focal point of the kitchen — only register for bold colors if you love them.

**Add attachments:** Both Le Creuset and Staub sell matching trivets, serving spoons, and lids as separate registry items.
