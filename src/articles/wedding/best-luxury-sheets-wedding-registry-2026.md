## The Sheet Buying Framework

Three things matter more than thread count:

1. **Fiber origin** — Long-staple Egyptian, Supima/Pima, or Turkish cotton produces far superior sheets to standard cotton. The long fiber length means fewer fiber ends, less pilling, and a silkier feel.

2. **Weave type** — Percale (crisp, cool, matte) vs. sateen (smooth, silky, slightly warmer). This affects feel more than any other single variable.

3. **Construction quality** — Reinforced seams, even stitching, properly fitted deep pockets (fits mattresses 14" or deeper). Quality sheets stay on the bed; cheap ones pop off corners constantly.

## The Practical Registry Setup

**Register for 2 complete sets:** One in use, one in the wash. Allows fresh sheets twice weekly without waiting for laundry to dry.

**Register same color across both sets:** Mixing sets is fine; mixing colors creates visual chaos in a linen closet.

**Add pillowcase extras:** Register for 4 extra pillowcases (beyond the set) — they wear faster than flat and fitted sheets and having spares extends the set's lifespan.

**Don't register for specialty pillowcases yet:** Silk pillowcases, cooling pillowcases, and printed pillowcases are great additions but secondary to a quality matched set.
