## Cookware Material Guide: What Actually Matters

**Stainless steel (clad/tri-ply):** Best heat retention, works on all surfaces including induction, dishwasher-safe, lasts forever. Requires more fat to prevent sticking, needs proper preheating technique. The "professional" choice.

**Hard-anodized non-stick:** Durable non-stick surface that's twice as hard as stainless steel. Good for everyday cooking. Coating eventually degrades (5–10 years of regular use). Easier for beginners.

**Ceramic non-stick:** PTFE-free and PFAS-free — zero toxic coatings. Good for health-conscious cooks. Requires lower heat and gentle handling. Shorter coating lifespan than hard-anodized.

**Cast iron:** Incredible heat retention, naturally non-stick when seasoned, lasts generations. Heavy, requires seasoning, not dishwasher-safe. The piece you keep forever.
