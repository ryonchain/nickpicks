## Why Knife Quality Matters More Than Any Other Kitchen Tool

A sharp, well-balanced knife makes every cutting task faster, safer, and more enjoyable. Dull knives cause more accidents than sharp ones (more force = more chance of slipping). Good knives reduce prep time significantly — a quality chef's knife processes a full meal's vegetables in half the time of a cheap knife.

Most couples enter marriage with knives they've accumulated rather than chosen — a hodgepodge of discount block sets, hand-me-downs, and impulse purchases. A wedding registry is the natural moment to build a quality knife collection that lasts 20+ years.

## Registry Build: The Complete Knife Collection

**Essential (register for these first):**
- 8-inch chef's knife (the workhorse — 80% of all tasks)
- 3.5-inch paring knife (peeling, trimming, small cuts)
- 8-inch serrated bread knife (bread, tomatoes, cakes)

**Important additions:**
- 6-inch boning/filet knife (meat, fish)
- 10-inch slicing knife (carving roasts, turkey)
- Honing steel (NOT a sharpener — realigns edge before each use)
- Whetstone (actual sharpening, 2-4x per year)

**One block or individual knives?** A quality block set is more cost-effective and creates a visual set for the kitchen. Individual knives allow mixing brands by type (German for chef's knife, Japanese for paring) — the professional chef's approach.
