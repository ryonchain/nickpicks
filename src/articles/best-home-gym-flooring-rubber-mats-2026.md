## How to Choose Home Gym Flooring

**By use case:**

| Activity | Minimum Thickness | Recommended |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Yoga / stretching | 1/4" foam | 1/2" foam |
| Cardio equipment | 1/4" PVC mat | 3/8" rubber tile |
| Light dumbbell training | 3/8" rubber tile | 1/2" rubber tile |
| Barbell training (no drops) | 1/2" rubber | 3/4" rubber |
| Olympic lifting / drops | 3/4" rubber | 3/4" rubber + platform |
| Powerlifting deadlifts | 3/4" rubber | Matador platform |

**Flooring types compared:**

**Foam tiles:** Cheapest ($0.30–$1/sq ft). Good for yoga, stretching, and bodyweight work. Not suitable for barbell training or heavy equipment — compresses under point loads.

**Rubber interlocking tiles:** Most versatile ($1–$2.50/sq ft). Available in 3/8" to 3/4". Good for most home gym applications. Easy to install/reconfigure.

**Rubber rolls:** Best for coverage areas (full room, garage gym). More cost-effective per sq ft than tiles for large areas. More labor-intensive to install.

**Vulcanized rubber:** Commercial standard. Available as rolls or horse stall mats. 10+ year lifespan under heavy use.

## Coverage Calculator

To estimate how much flooring you need:

2. Add 10% for cuts and waste

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need flooring if my garage has concrete?**
Yes — concrete is hard on joints, cold in winter, and slippery when wet. More importantly, dropping a barbell on concrete can damage both the concrete and the barbell's knurling and finish. Rubber flooring is essential for any regular barbell work.

**Can I install rubber flooring over carpet?**
Possible but not recommended for weight training. Carpet compresses under point loads (squat rack feet, dumbbell rack) and creates an unstable surface for heavy lifting. For cardio-only, equipment mats over carpet are fine.

**Does rubber flooring smell?**
New rubber flooring off-gasses (that tire-smell) for 24–72 hours. Ventilate the space and leave it open for a day before use. The smell dissipates completely within a week.
