## Ergonomic Chair Buying Guide

### What Actually Matters in Lumbar Support

Lumbar support prevents the lower back from collapsing into a C-curve (kyphosis) during long sitting sessions. The best support systems:

1. **Support the sacrum and lumbar independently** (Herman Miller PostureFit SL)
2. **Adapt to movement** (Steelcase LiveBack, Humanscale pivoting back)
3. **Are height and depth adjustable** (to accommodate different torso dimensions)
4. **Maintain contact during recline** (most chairs lose lumbar contact when you lean back)

Foam lumbar pillows added to a chair work temporarily but don't adapt to movement and compress over time. A chair with a built-in system is worth more.

### Seat Depth Matters More Than Most People Know

Seat depth should leave 2–4 fingers between the front edge of the seat and the back of your knee. Too shallow = pressure on thighs; too deep = forces forward perching. Most chairs are designed for average height (5'8"–5'11") men. Short users and women often need smaller seat depths — Aeron Size A or Branch's adjustable depth.

### Armrests: Underrated Feature

Good armrests reduce neck and shoulder tension by supporting your forearms at keyboard height. They should be adjusted so your shoulders are relaxed, not elevated. 4D armrests (height, depth, width, pivot) offer the most flexibility. Fixed armrests are a dealbreaker for serious ergonomics.

### The Real Cost of a Premium Chair

Herman Miller Aeron: ~$1,600. At 8 hours/day, 250 working days/year, over a 12-year warranty period = $0.07/hour for your seating. If it prevents one back surgery (average cost: $100,000+) or a month of physical therapy ($3,000–$5,000), the math is obvious. Buy the best chair you can.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Herman Miller Aeron worth the price?**
For people who work 8+ hours/day at a desk, yes — the 12-year warranty and proven lumbar system make it cost-effective over time. For occasional or part-time desk use, the Branch or Sihoo M57 offer 80% of the benefit at 30% of the price.

**What's the difference between the Aeron and Steelcase Leap V2?**
The Aeron has a better fixed lumbar support system; the Leap V2 has a better dynamic lumbar system that adapts to movement. If you stay relatively still while working, choose the Aeron. If you move around frequently, choose the Leap V2.

**Can an ergonomic chair fix existing back pain?**
A good ergonomic chair reduces pain caused by poor sitting posture but won't fix a herniated disc or structural issue. Combine a good chair with regular movement breaks (stand up every 30–45 minutes) and core strengthening for best results.

**Do I need a chair mat?**
For hardwood or tile floors, yes — chair mats protect your floor from caster damage and make rolling easier. For carpeted floors, look for casters designed for carpet (most chairs include them).

**What size Aeron should I get?**
- Size A: Under 5'3" or under 130 lbs
- Size B: 5'3"–5'11", 130–230 lbs (fits most people)
- Size C: Over 6'0" or over 230 lbs
