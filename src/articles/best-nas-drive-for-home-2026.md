Your photos stream to your phone. Your movies play on any device on your home network — or remotely. Your files back up automatically.

## What Hard Drives to Buy for Your NAS

NAS devices require **NAS-specific hard drives** — not the consumer desktop drives you'd buy for an external backup. NAS drives are designed for 24/7 operation, higher vibration tolerance (multiple spinning drives in close proximity), and RAID workloads.

**Top NAS hard drive options:**
- **WD Red Plus 4 TB** (~$80): Most popular choice for 2-bay NAS, CMR recording, 5,400 RPM, rated 180 TB/year workload
- **WD Red Plus 8 TB** (~$130): Step up for larger libraries without step-change in cost
- **Seagate IronWolf 4 TB** (~$75): Direct competitor to WD Red; reliable track record
- **Seagate IronWolf Pro 8 TB** (~$149): Higher workload rating for demanding setups

**Total cost estimate for a complete 2-bay setup:**
- Synology DS223j + 2× WD Red Plus 4 TB = ~$330 (8 TB usable in RAID 1)
- Synology DS223j + 2× WD Red Plus 8 TB = ~$430 (16 TB usable in RAID 1)

## What Can a Home NAS Do?

**Photo backup:** Replace Google Photos. Set up Synology Photos or QNAP's QuMagie to automatically back up every photo from every family member's phone. Private, unlimited, yours.

**Plex media server:** Rip your DVD and Blu-ray collection to the NAS. Stream any movie to any TV in the house (and remotely) via Plex. No subscription required for local streaming.

**File sync:** Sync folders across all your computers like Dropbox — but hosted on your hardware with no monthly fee and no file size limits.

**Automatic computer backup:** Set up Time Machine (Mac) or Synology Active Backup for Endpoint (PC) to back up all your computers to the NAS automatically.

**Remote access:** Access any file on your NAS from anywhere in the world via your phone or browser. Synology's QuickConnect makes this as simple as a one-click setup.

## FAQ

**Is a NAS worth it vs. cloud storage?**
If you pay $10–$30/month for cloud storage and have a decent photo/file library, a NAS pays for itself in 12–24 months and provides unlimited storage for the life of the hardware. The break-even math strongly favors NAS for families with large photo/video libraries.

**Do I need RAID?**
RAID 1 (mirroring) means your data is written to two drives simultaneously — if one drive fails, you lose nothing. For a home NAS with important photos and files, RAID 1 is strongly recommended. Note: RAID is not a backup — it protects against drive failure but not against accidental deletion, ransomware, or device theft. Maintain a separate backup.

**Can I access my NAS from outside my home?**
Yes. Synology's QuickConnect and QNAP's myQNAPcloud create a secure tunnel to your NAS accessible via web browser or phone app without complex networking configuration.
