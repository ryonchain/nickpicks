## Kids Headphone Buyer's Guide

### Why 85dB Volume Limiting Matters

The WHO recommends that children limit headphone exposure to 85dB maximum. At 85dB, children can safely listen for up to 8 hours per day without risk. At 94dB, that safe limit drops to under 1 hour. Many cheap kids headphones claim volume limiting but deliver it inconsistently — look for brands like Puro, JLab, and BuddyPhones that have had their limits independently tested.

### Wireless vs. Wired for Kids

| Feature | Wireless | Wired |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Convenience | Higher | Lower (cable management) |
| Battery worry | Yes | No |
| School use | Varies by policy | Universally accepted |
| Durability | Good | Excellent |
| Price | Higher | Lower |

**Recommendation:** Wireless for home and travel; wired for school use with shared devices.

### Fit for Growing Kids

Look for headphones with multiple size settings. Kids' heads grow quickly — adjustable headbands with clear size indicators (and more than 2–3 settings) provide longer useful life before replacement is needed.
