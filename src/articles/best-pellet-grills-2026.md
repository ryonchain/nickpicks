## How to Choose a Pellet Grill

**Cooking area:** 500–700 sq in handles most family cookouts. Go 1,000+ sq in for entertaining large groups or cooking multiple proteins at once.

**Hopper size:** Larger hoppers (30+ lbs) mean fewer refills during long smokes. A 10-lb hopper burns through on an 8-hour brisket — you'll need to refill.

**Temperature range:** Look for 160°F–500°F minimum. Premium grills reaching 600°F+ can sear as well as smoke.

**Temperature accuracy:** PID controllers (like Recteq) maintain ±5°F. Basic digital controllers vary ±25°F — adequate for smoking but not ideal for precise baking.

**Connectivity:** Wi-Fi control via app is now standard on mid-range grills and above. Useful for overnight smokes — check temp from bed without going outside.
