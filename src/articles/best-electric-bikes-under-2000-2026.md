**Pros:**

## E-Bike Buying Guide

**Class 1 vs. 2 vs. 3:**
- Class 1: Pedal assist only, 20 mph max. Allowed on most bike paths.
- Class 2: Throttle + pedal assist, 20 mph max. Allowed on most bike paths.
- Class 3: Pedal assist up to 28 mph. Restricted from some paths; helmet required in most states.

**Range reality:** Manufacturer range claims assume ideal conditions (flat terrain, light rider, low assist). Expect 60–70% of claimed range in real mixed riding.

**Motor placement:** Rear hub motors provide a more "normal" bike feel. Mid-drive motors (like Bosch/Shimano) handle hills better but cost significantly more. Front hub motors are cheapest but least natural.

**Battery Wh:** Battery capacity in watt-hours (Wh) is the most honest range predictor. 400 Wh = ~25–35 miles real. 700 Wh = ~45–55 miles real.

**Weight:** Most e-bikes weigh 50–70 lbs. This matters for: apartment stairs, loading into a car, and handling at low speeds.
