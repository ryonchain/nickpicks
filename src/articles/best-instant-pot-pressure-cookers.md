## Reviews

### Instant Pot Duo 7-in-1 — Best Overall

Seven years running, the Instant Pot Duo remains the bestselling electric pressure cooker on Amazon — and for good reason. It pressure cooks, slow cooks, sautés, steams, makes rice, warms, and sterilizes. The 6-quart size feeds 4–6 people comfortably.

**Pros:** Proven reliability; huge recipe community; simple interface; affordable; easy to clean.
**Cons:** No air fry function; lid sealing takes practice initially; doesn't keep warm as long as a dedicated slow cooker.

**Verdict:** The right first pressure cooker for most households.

### Ninja Foodi 14-in-1 — Best Multi-Cooker

The Foodi does everything the Instant Pot does PLUS air frying, roasting, dehydrating, and broiling with a crisping lid. This is genuinely two appliances in one — you can pressure cook chicken and then switch to the air fry lid to crisp the skin without dirtying another pan.

**Pros:** Air fry lid is excellent; pressure cook performance rivals Instant Pot; large capacity.
**Cons:** Expensive; two lids require storage; heavier and bulkier.

**Verdict:** Buy it if you want to eliminate a separate air fryer and don't mind the price premium.

### Instant Pot Pro 10-in-1 — Best for Serious Cooks

The Pro adds sous vide, sterilize, and bake functions to the standard lineup. More importantly, it has a rotating knob instead of buttons — the interface is faster and more intuitive. Built-in altitude adjustment is a genuinely useful feature for high-elevation households.

**Pros:** Excellent interface; altitude adjustment; sous vide built in; brushed stainless body.
**Cons:** Premium price for incremental gains over the Duo; still no air fry.

**Verdict:** The best non-Foodi option if you cook regularly and want finer control.

### Crock-Pot Express — Best Budget Alternative

The Crock-Pot Express gives you core pressure cooking plus slow cooking and sauté for around $80. It's not as refined as the Instant Pot (slightly louder pressure release, basic display), but it cooks identically well for soups, stews, and grains.

**Pros:** Significantly cheaper; reliable core performance; familiar Crock-Pot brand.
**Cons:** Interface less intuitive; fewer preset programs; lid seal less consistent.

**Verdict:** A solid pick if you're not ready to spend $100 on an Instant Pot.

## How to Choose

**Size guide:**
- 3 qt: Singles, couples, college students
- 6 qt: Families of 4–6 (most popular, recommended for most)
- 8 qt: Large families, batch cooking, big cuts of meat

**Do you want air fry?** If yes, go straight to the Ninja Foodi. If no, the Instant Pot Duo or Pro are better pressure cookers for the price.

**How often will you use it?** Daily cooks should invest in the Pro or Foodi. Occasional users will be happy with the Duo.

## Bottom Line

Start with the **Instant Pot Duo** — the community support alone (recipes, troubleshooting, tutorials) makes it the safest choice. Upgrade to the **Ninja Foodi** if you also want to eliminate your air fryer.
