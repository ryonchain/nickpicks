## Navigation: iRobot OS vs Matrix Mapping

**Roomba:** iRobot's proprietary navigation system has evolved significantly. The j-series uses a camera for real-time object avoidance, mapping room layouts with high accuracy. It learns your home over multiple cleans, getting more efficient over time. The app lets you name rooms and create cleaning schedules by zone.

**Shark:** Shark's Matrix Mapping uses a combination of LiDAR (on AI Ultra models) or a camera to map your home. Performance has improved significantly — the newer AI Ultra can detect and avoid obstacles in real time, including pet waste on premium models.

**Winner:** Roomba edges out Shark in navigation precision and AI-driven adaptability, but Shark has closed the gap significantly on mid-to-high-end models.

---

## Cleaning Performance

Both brands deliver strong suction on hard floors. On carpet, Roomba's rubber dual-extractor brushes and Shark's self-cleaning brushroll both handle debris well.

**Pet hair:** Both handle pet hair well at similar price points. Roomba's rubber extractors resist tangling from long fur. Shark's self-cleaning brushroll strips hair off continuously during operation.

**Winner:** Tie at comparable price points. Roomba's j9+ Combo is the best pet hair performer overall; the Shark IQ XL delivers similar results for $200 less.

---

## Auto-Empty Base

Both brands offer bagless self-empty bases on their premium models. Roomba's Clean Base on the j-series holds 60 days of debris. Shark's Bagless XLTM base also holds 60 days and doesn't require replacement bags.

**Winner:** Tie — Shark wins on cost (no bags required on newest models); Roomba wins on the overall ecosystem integration.

## Price

Shark consistently undercuts Roomba by $100–$300 at equivalent feature levels. For buyers who want auto-empty + mapping + pet hair performance, Shark delivers that combination at a lower entry price.

**Winner:** Shark for value.
