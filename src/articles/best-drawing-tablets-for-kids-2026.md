## Drawing Tablet Buyer's Guide

### Screen vs. Non-Screen Tablets

| Type | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|------|------|------|---------|
| Non-screen pen tablet (Wacom, Huion, XP-Pen) | Affordable, professional grade | Requires hand-eye coordination adjustment | Ages 10+ serious learners |
| Screen tablet (iPad + Pencil) | Most natural drawing feel | Premium price | Ages 6+ with budget |
| LCD eWriter (Boogie Board) | Youngest kids, zero mess | Not real digital art | Ages 4–8 |

### Best Free Drawing Apps for Kids

- **Krita** (Windows/Mac, free) — Most capable free drawing app
- **Autodesk SketchBook** (free) — Clean interface, excellent for beginners
- **Procreate** (iPad only, $12.99 one-time) — Best iPad drawing app
- **MediBang Paint** (all platforms, free) — Popular with manga/anime artists

### Do Kids Need High Pressure Sensitivity?

For casual drawing and learning, 4096 pressure levels (Wacom entry, Huion H430P) is perfectly sufficient. 8192 levels (XP-Pen Deco, Wacom Pro) matter more for professional digital painters working with subtle brush pressure. For children, the difference is negligible.
