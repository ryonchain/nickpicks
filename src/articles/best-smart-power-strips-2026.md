## What to Look for in a Smart Power Strip

**Individual outlet control:** The core feature. Make sure each outlet can be toggled independently — not just the whole strip.

**Surge protection rating (joules):** Higher is better. Look for 1080J minimum; 2760J+ is preferred for electronics protection. Cheap strips list no joule rating.

**USB ports:** Quality strips include 2-4 USB ports (look for USB-A and USB-C fast charging). These are often always-on even when the outlet is off.

**App ecosystem:** Kasa (TP-Link) has the most reliable smart home app in this category. Govee is solid. Avoid no-name brands with apps that require account creation on sketchy overseas servers.

**Energy monitoring:** Some models track real-time wattage per outlet. This is the most useful feature for identifying phantom load from idle electronics.

**Build quality:** Check for UL listing and actual surge protection circuitry. Many cheap "surge protectors" are just power strips with no genuine protection.

## FAQ

**Can I control individual outlets by voice?**
Yes — with Alexa or Google Home, name each outlet in the app (e.g., "TV," "Monitor") and say "Alexa, turn off the TV."

**Do USB ports turn off when I switch the outlet?**
Most smart strips keep USB ports always-on (charging ports). Check the spec sheet — Kasa EP40 has independent USB power.

**Is surge protection in smart strips real?**
Look for the joule rating and UL 1449 certification. Kasa and Meross include genuine surge protection. No-name strips often lack it.
