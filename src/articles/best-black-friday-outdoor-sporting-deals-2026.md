Outdoor and sporting goods Black Friday deals are strong in specific categories and unreliable in others. ## Where to Shop for Outdoor Black Friday Deals

**Amazon:** Best for portable power stations, camping electronics, small gear, and fitness accessories. Wide selection, fast shipping, easy returns.

**REI Co-op:** REI closes on Black Friday (company tradition) but runs a "REI Black Friday Sale" online in the days before and after. Members (REI Co-op) get early access. Best for technical gear, apparel, and footwear.

**Dick's Sporting Goods:** Strong on fitness equipment (treadmills, bikes, weightlifting), sports equipment, and outdoor apparel. Black Friday deals often better in-store.

**Bass Pro Shops / Cabela's (same company):** Best for hunting, fishing, and serious camping. Their BF circular launches weeks early — download it and plan ahead.

**Academy Sports:** Regional but strong deals on fitness equipment and outdoor basics.

### Camping Gear — Better in Summer Clearance, But Some Good BF Deals

Camping gear is tricky for BF. Retailers want to clear seasonal inventory in August–September when it's still warm, not in November when nobody's thinking about camping. As a result:

- **Best deals on camping gear:** July–September (post-summer clearance)
- **Still good at BF:** Non-seasonal camping items (cookware, lanterns, sleeping bags)

**Expected BF camping deals:**

### Bikes — Minimal BF Discounts, Better at End of Season

**Bicycles are not a BF deal category.** Bike shops move bikes in spring and summer; fall inventory is limited and BF discounts on bikes are minimal or nonexistent.

**Better timing for bike purchases:** March–April (new model year arrives, previous year discounts appear) or August–September (end of season clearance, especially at local bike shops).

**Exception:** Electric bikes (e-bikes) occasionally see Amazon BF deals. Rad Power Bikes and Lectric eBikes have run BF promotions (accessories bundled, small percentage off). Watch their brand pages specifically, not general retailers.

---

### Kayaks and Water Sports — Not a BF Category

Similar to bikes — water sports equipment clears in August/September, not November. Don't expect meaningful BF kayak discounts. The right time to buy a kayak is August or early spring.

## Black Friday Outdoor Shopping Strategy

### For Fitness Equipment (Best BF Category)
1. Know your specific model before BF week
2. Set a Best Buy and Amazon price alert
3. Check manufacturer websites — Peloton, NordicTrack, and Bowflex often run their own BF promos
4. Compare financing offers (0% APR for 12–24 months is often the real "deal" for big-ticket fitness purchases)

### For Portable Power Stations
1. Jackery and EcoFlow post deals the week before BF — early access matters
2. Previous generation models see deeper discounts; verify specs still meet your needs
3. The 1000–1500Wh range is the sweet spot for most users — enough for camping appliances + device charging

### For Garmin/GPS Watches
1. Set a CamelCamelCamel alert for your specific model
2. Older Forerunner models drop most when new model launches — BF is often timed with new releases
3. REI members get early access and dividend on sporting electronics

### What to Skip and Buy in Summer Instead
- Tents
- Kayaks and canoes
- Bikes
- Technical outdoor apparel (better during REI Gear Up Sale in August)
- Camping chairs and tables (Amazon warehouse deals all summer)
