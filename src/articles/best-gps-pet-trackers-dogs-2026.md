## Why a GPS Tracker for Your Dog Is Worth Every Penny

The average cost of finding a lost dog — between vet bills, shelter fees, search efforts, and time — can reach $500–$1,500. A GPS tracker costs $50–$150 upfront plus a subscription. It's the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy.

More importantly: the emotional cost of a missing pet is not something you want to find out about. This guide ranks the five best GPS dog trackers available on Amazon in 2026.

---

## What to Look for in a GPS Dog Tracker

**Coverage:** GPS-only trackers work anywhere with satellite signal. Bluetooth-only trackers (like Tile) only work within ~100–200 feet. For a dog that can run, GPS is non-negotiable.

**Subscription cost:** Most GPS trackers require a monthly data subscription ($5–$15/month). Factor this into the real cost of ownership.

**Battery life:** A tracker that dies after 2 days gives you a false sense of security. Look for at least 5–7 days with tracking enabled.

**Water resistance:** Dogs get wet. Look for IPX7 or better (submersible to 1 meter).
