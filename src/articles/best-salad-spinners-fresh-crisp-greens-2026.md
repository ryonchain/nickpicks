## Why a Salad Spinner Is Worth the Cabinet Space

Wet greens are the enemy of good salad. Water on lettuce leaves:
- **Dilutes dressing** — vinaigrette slides off wet leaves instead of coating them
- **Causes sogginess** within minutes of dressing
- **Shortens storage life** — excess moisture accelerates bacterial growth and wilting

A salad spinner removes 90%+ of surface water in 30 seconds, making dressings adhere properly and extending prepped greens' refrigerator life from 1–2 days to 4–5 days (stored dry in the spinner bowl with a paper towel).

For anyone who eats salad more than once a week, a salad spinner pays for itself in reduced food waste inside a month.

## What Makes a Good Salad Spinner?

- **Spin mechanism:** Push-button pump mechanisms are the most ergonomic — one-hand operation, smooth spin, and simple locking for storage. Pull-cord and hand-crank designs work but require more effort.
- **Capacity:** 5-quart is right for 1–2 people; 7–11 quart for families or meal prep.
- **Bowl quality:** The spinning bowl should be usable as a serving bowl. Look for straight sides (easier to toss salad) and clear construction to see contents.
- **Lid lock:** A lid that locks the basket inside the bowl turns the spinner into covered storage — greens go straight from spinner to fridge without transferring.
- **Non-slip base:** Critical — spinning at speed on a slippery countertop launches the bowl off the counter.

## How to Get the Most Out of Your Salad Spinner

**Spin twice:** After the first spin, dump the water, redistribute the leaves, and spin again. The second pass removes the water the centrifuge missed on the first.

**Store in the spinner:** After spinning, keep greens in the spinner bowl with the lid locked and a paper towel layer on top. Refrigerate directly in the bowl — greens stay crisp for 4–5 days.

**Wash whole heads, then tear:** Washing whole lettuce heads before tearing removes more surface contamination. Tear into serving pieces after washing and spinning.

**Don't overfill:** A half-filled basket spins more effectively than a packed one — the leaves need room to move.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I put a salad spinner in the dishwasher?**
Most plastic salad spinners (OXO, Zyliss, KitchenAid) are top-rack dishwasher safe for the bowl and basket. The lid/mechanism component should be hand-washed to preserve the mechanism. Stainless steel models (RSVP Endurance) are fully dishwasher safe.

**How do I get my salad greens to stay crisp longer?**
Spin thoroughly to remove excess water, store with a dry paper towel to absorb remaining moisture, and keep in an airtight container or the locked spinner bowl. Properly dried and stored greens stay crisp for 4–5 days vs. 1–2 days when wet-stored.

**Does a salad spinner work for herbs?**
Yes — delicate herbs like parsley, cilantro, and basil spin dry without damage. Particularly useful for herbs that go limp quickly when stored wet.

**What size salad spinner is right for me?**
2–4 qt: Solo cooks and couples. 5 qt: 2–4 person households. 7–11 qt: Large families, meal preppers, or frequent entertaining.
