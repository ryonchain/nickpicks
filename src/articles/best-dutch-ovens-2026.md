## Buyer's Guide: What Size Dutch Oven Do You Need?

### Capacity Guidelines

| Household Size | Recommended Capacity | Best Use |
|----------------|---------------------|----------|
| 1–2 people | 3.5–4 quarts | Soups, small braises |
| 2–4 people | 5–6 quarts | Most recipes, bread baking |
| 4–6+ people | 7+ quarts | Large batches, stock, big roasts |

The 5.5–6 quart range covers the majority of recipes. If you only buy one Dutch oven, this is the size.

### Bare vs. Enameled Cast Iron

**Bare cast iron (Lodge Camp Dutch Oven):**
- Requires seasoning (oil + heat treatment)
- Reacts with acidic foods (tomatoes, wine)
- Better for camping/outdoor cooking
- Cheaper

**Enameled cast iron (all models above):**
- No seasoning required
- Safe for acidic dishes
- Easy cleanup
- Better for stovetop + oven cooking
- More expensive

## FAQ

**Can Dutch ovens go in the dishwasher?**
Technically yes for enameled models, but hand washing is recommended. Dishwasher detergent can dull the enamel gloss over time.

**What's the best Dutch oven for baking bread?**
Any Dutch oven with an oven-safe lid works. The Lodge 6-quart or Lodge Combo Cooker (~$55) is the budget-friendly bread-baking choice. The tight-fitting lid traps steam in the first 20 minutes of baking — critical for the crust.

**Why does bread baked in a Dutch oven taste better?**
The enclosed pot creates a steam environment in the first 15–20 minutes of baking that mimics professional steam-injected ovens. This keeps the surface extensible while oven spring happens, then the steam escapes and the crust forms.
