## Resistance Types Explained

### Air Resistance
- **How it works:** Fan flywheel; the harder you pull, the more resistance you get
- **Feel:** Most like actual on-water rowing; infinite resistance range
- **Sound:** Loudest of all types (whooshing fan)
- **Best for:** Performance athletes, serious training
- **Top pick:** Concept2 Model D

### Water Resistance
- **How it works:** Paddles in a water tank create drag as they move
- **Feel:** Natural, smooth, most satisfying sound (gentle splash)
- **Sound:** Quiet—the water sound is calming, not disruptive
- **Best for:** Home gyms near bedrooms, aesthetics-first buyers
- **Top pick:** WaterRower Club

### Magnetic Resistance
- **How it works:** Electromagnets create drag; resistance set digitally
- **Feel:** Smooth, consistent, but less "real" than air or water
- **Sound:** Near-silent
- **Best for:** Apartments, early morning workouts, beginners
- **Top pick:** Sunny Health SF-RW5515 (budget), NordicTrack RW900 (premium)

### Space Calculator
Before buying, measure your available floor space. Most rowing machines are 8–9 feet long fully extended. Almost all fold or store upright when not in use. The WaterRower and Concept2 store vertically; the Sunny and NordicTrack fold forward.

### Maintenance
- **Magnetic:** Nearly maintenance-free (occasional wipe-down)
- **Air:** Clean fan cage monthly, lightly oil the chain/monorail
- **Water:** Treat water with purification tablets every 12 months; refill as needed
