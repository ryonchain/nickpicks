## Circular Saw Buying Guide

**Corded vs. cordless:** Corded saws deliver unlimited runtime and typically more power. Cordless offers portability and works on battery-powered job sites. For most DIYers with a garage workshop, either works fine — pick based on your existing battery ecosystem.

**6-1/2 in vs. 7-1/4 in blades:** 7-1/4 in is the standard for framing and sheet goods. 6-1/2 in cordless saws handle most DIY cuts and weigh less. Unless you're cutting thick lumber regularly, a 6-1/2 in cordless saw is sufficient.

**What to skip:** Avoid saws without electric brakes — they cost less but the blade coasting to a stop after the trigger releases is a safety hazard. All picks above include an electric brake.
