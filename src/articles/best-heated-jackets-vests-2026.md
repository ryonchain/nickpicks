## Buying Guide: Choosing a Heated Jacket or Vest

### Standalone Battery vs. Power Tool Ecosystem
**Standalone battery (ORORO, ActionHeat):** Works for anyone, included battery is USB-chargeable, simpler. Best for casual users, hikers, daily wear.

**Power tool battery (Milwaukee, Dewalt, Bosch):** Best for trades workers who already own the battery ecosystem. Higher battery capacity means longer runtime, and you're not managing a separate battery system.

### Jacket vs. Vest
**Jacket:** More warmth, full coverage, better for extreme cold.
**Vest:** Greater arm mobility, easier to layer, lighter weight, useful in shoulder seasons or under a heavy shell.

### Heating Zones: What Matters
Core zones that actually warm the body:
- **Chest:** Warms core and blood supply — highest priority
- **Upper back:** Combined with chest creates wraparound warmth
- **Collar/neck:** Surprisingly effective — neck warmth changes perceived body temperature significantly

Less essential:
- **Pocket heating:** Nice for cold hands, but not a core warmth driver
- **Sleeve heating:** Rare, adds cost and complexity

### Heat Levels & Battery Life Rule of Thumb
Most heated jackets follow this pattern:
- High: 2–3 hours | Maximum warmth for extreme cold
- Medium: 4–6 hours | Balanced warmth for typical winter
- Low: 6–10 hours | Mild warmth for shoulder seasons or layering

## FAQ

**Q: Can heated jackets be washed?**
A: Yes — with the battery removed. All quality heated jackets are machine washable. Remove the battery, disconnect the wiring connector, zip closed, wash on gentle/cold, air dry or low tumble dry.

**Q: Are heated jackets safe?**
A: Yes. Quality heated jackets use carbon fiber heating elements with multiple safety features including auto-shutoff if temperature exceeds safe limits. Buy from established brands to ensure safety compliance.

**Q: How warm do heated jackets actually get?**
A: Most heated jackets reach 110–130°F at high setting on the heating elements. This provides whole-body warmth even in sub-freezing temperatures when paired with a base layer.

**Q: Can I use a heated jacket without activating the heat?**
A: Yes — heated jackets work as standard insulated jackets without power. The heating is an addition to the jacket's passive insulation.

**Q: Do heated jackets work for skiing or snowboarding?**
A: Yes, but check waterproofing ratings. Most heated jackets are water-resistant (light rain), not waterproof. For snow sports, look for jackets with higher waterproof ratings or wear them as a mid-layer under a waterproof shell.
