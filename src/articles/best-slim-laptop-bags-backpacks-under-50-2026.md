## FAQ

**What makes a laptop bag TSA-friendly?**

A TSA-friendly laptop bag has a dedicated compartment that unfolds completely flat — often called a "checkpoint-friendly" design — so your laptop can be X-rayed inside the bag without you having to remove it. TSA guidelines require laptops to be placed separately in a bin, but bags certified as checkpoint-friendly are exempt from that rule provided the laptop compartment contains no other items and the bag lies flat on the X-ray belt. Look for a panel-style opening that splays to 180 degrees, and make sure nothing else is stored in that specific pocket.

**Sleeve vs. backpack: which is better for commuting?**

It depends on how much you carry. A sleeve is lighter, more compact, and slips inside a tote or roller bag — ideal if your laptop is essentially all you're bringing. A backpack distributes weight evenly across both shoulders, offers dedicated pockets for chargers, water bottles, and notebooks, and keeps your hands free during a commute. For anything beyond a laptop and a single cable, a slim backpack is almost always the more practical choice for daily use. If you commute by car and your laptop goes straight from bag to desk, a sleeve is perfectly sufficient and easier to carry.

**How do I measure my laptop for a bag?**

Measure the laptop's physical chassis — width, depth, and height — with the lid closed. Don't rely on screen size alone; a 15.6-inch display laptop can vary by more than an inch in chassis width depending on the manufacturer and model year. Compare your laptop's actual measurements to the bag's listed compartment dimensions, and look for at least a quarter-inch of clearance on each side so the laptop slides in and out without forcing. When in doubt, size up: a compartment rated for 16 inches will comfortably fit a 15.6-inch laptop, but a 15.6-inch slot may not close properly over a thick 16-inch chassis.
