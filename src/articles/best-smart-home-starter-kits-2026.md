## What to Know Before Buying

### Alexa vs. Google Home vs. Apple HomeKit

**Alexa (Amazon):** Largest device compatibility. Best if you shop on Amazon regularly or already own Echo devices. Works with Kasa, Govee, Meross, and virtually every smart home brand.

**Google Home:** Best if you're an Android user or heavy Google user (Calendar, Maps, Gmail integrations). Slightly fewer compatible devices than Alexa.

**Apple HomeKit:** Best for iPhone/iPad/Mac households. Strictest certification process = most secure. Fewer compatible devices; typically costs more.

### Hub vs. Hubless

**Hubless (Wi-Fi direct):** All Govee and Kasa devices connect directly to your router. Easier setup, but can be slow or unreliable if Wi-Fi is congested with many devices.

**Hub-based (Philips Hue):** A dedicated hub runs local processing. Faster, more reliable automations, works when internet is down. Worth it for a whole-home lighting system.

**Q: What do I need to start?**
Wi-Fi, a smartphone, and one smart plug or smart bulb. An Echo Dot or Google Nest Mini adds voice control. No hub, no wiring, no professional installation required.

**Q: Alexa vs. Google Home — which should I start with?**
Alexa if you shop on Amazon or want the widest device compatibility. Google Home if you're an Android user or tied to Google services. Both work with most brands.

**Q: Do smart bulbs work if Wi-Fi goes down?**
Budget Wi-Fi bulbs (Govee) lose app control but work with a wall switch. Philips Hue with a Bridge hub maintains local control via the Hue app on your home network.

**Q: Can I mix Alexa and Google Home in the same house?**
Yes — most smart devices work with both simultaneously. Each voice assistant controls the same devices independently.

**Q: Are these devices secure?**
Major brands (Amazon, TP-Link, Philips Hue) use encrypted connections and push firmware updates. Keep devices updated and use a strong router password.
