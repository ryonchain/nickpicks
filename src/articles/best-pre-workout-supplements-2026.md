## Pre-Workout Buying Guide

**Caffeine tolerance matters:** If you're sensitive to caffeine, start with 150–200mg products. Heavy coffee drinkers may need 300mg+ to notice an effect.

**Watch for proprietary blends:** Any pre-workout that lists a "blend" with a combined weight rather than individual ingredient doses is hiding underdosing. Choose fully transparent labels.

**Timing:** Take pre-workout 20–30 minutes before training. Caffeinated formulas taken within 6 hours of sleep can disrupt sleep quality.

**Cycling:** Regular pre-workout users often cycle on/off to prevent caffeine tolerance buildup — 6–8 weeks on, 2 weeks off.

## FAQ

**How much caffeine is in pre-workout?**
Most pre-workouts contain 150–350mg of caffeine per serving. Entry-level products like C4 Original have 150mg. High-stimulant formulas like Gorilla Mode contain up to 350mg. Always check the label and account for caffeine from other sources throughout the day.

**What does beta-alanine do in pre-workout?**
Beta-alanine buffers lactic acid buildup in muscles, which can delay fatigue during high-intensity exercise. It causes a harmless tingling sensation (paresthesia) in many people. Research supports doses of 3.2–6.4g/day for endurance improvements.

**Is stimulant-free pre-workout worth it?**
Yes — for afternoon workouts, caffeine-sensitive individuals, or those who already consume significant caffeine. Stimulant-free formulas like pump-focused products still deliver nitric oxide boosters, creatine, and other performance ingredients without the energy crash or sleep disruption.

**When should I take pre-workout?**
Most pre-workouts should be taken 20–30 minutes before training. Caffeine peaks in the bloodstream around 30–60 minutes after ingestion. Avoid taking caffeinated pre-workout within 6 hours of sleep to prevent disruption.
