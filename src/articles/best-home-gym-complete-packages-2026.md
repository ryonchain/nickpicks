## Before You Buy: The Fundamentals

**Flooring first.** Don't put gym equipment on bare concrete or carpet. Rubber gym mats protect your floor and equipment, reduce noise, and provide traction. Budget at least $100–200 for flooring regardless of your total budget.

**Ceiling height matters.** Pull-ups and overhead pressing require at least 8' ceilings. Rack height varies (most power racks are 83"–90"). Measure before buying.

**Space requirements:**
- 8'x8' minimum for a functional training space (kettlebells, resistance bands)
- 10'x10' for a basic weight bench + dumbbells setup
- 12'x20' for a power rack + barbell + full functional training area

## Space-Saving Tips

**Fold-down wall bench:** If floor space is limited, fold-down benches (Goplus, Wall Mounted Folding Bench) mount to the wall and swing up when not in use.

**Wall-mounted pull-up bar:** Eliminates the floor footprint of a pull-up tower. Titan Fitness wall-mounted pull-up bar holds 400+ lbs and mounts in 30 minutes.

**Vertical dumbbell storage:** A vertical dumbbell rack takes 80% less floor space than a horizontal rack for the same dumbbell count.
