## How to Choose a Student Desk Lamp

**Color temperature guide:**
- **2700K–3000K (warm white):** Relaxing, evening study, reading fiction
- **4000K–4500K (neutral white):** Best for general studying and note-taking
- **5000K–6500K (cool white/daylight):** Alert, focused; best for early morning work

**Brightness guide:**
- Under 500 lux: Ambient lighting, not for primary task work
- 600–800 lux: Adequate for most study tasks in a reasonably lit room
- 1000+ lux: Good for detail work and studying in dark rooms

**Flicker-free matters:** Fluorescent and cheap LED lamps flicker at 100/120Hz — invisible to the eye but registered by the brain, causing fatigue over hours. Look for "flicker-free" certification (BenQ guarantees it; TaoTronics meets this standard).

## FAQ

**Should I use warm or cool light for studying at night?**
For night studying, 3000K–4000K (neutral warm) is the best balance — alert enough for focus without disrupting circadian rhythms the way 6500K cool light does. Avoid very cool blue-white light after 9 PM.

**Is it okay to use a desk lamp in a dorm room?**
Yes — most university housing allows desk lamps. Halogen and incandescent bulbs may be prohibited (fire risk), but LED lamps are universally allowed. Verify your housing policy if unsure.

**How much brightness do I need for reading without eye strain?**
For comfortable reading, 500–800 lux is sufficient in a normally lit room. In a dark room (late night, lights off), 800–1200 lux is more comfortable. The BenQ at 1600 lux handles any condition.
