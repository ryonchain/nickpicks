## Energy Monitoring Buying Guide

### Understanding Phantom Load

When you measure idle power draw of typical home devices, the numbers are surprising:

| Device | Typical Idle Draw |
|---|---|
| Cable/satellite box | 15–20W |
| Gaming console (standby) | 1–15W |
| Large flat-screen TV | 5–15W |
| Desktop computer (sleep) | 5–10W |
| Coffee maker (clock) | 3–5W |
| Microwave (clock) | 1–3W |
| Phone charger (idle) | 0.1–0.5W |

A cable box running 24/7 at 17W uses about 149 kWh per year — around $18–25 at average US electricity rates. A gaming console in standby mode for 20 hours a day uses $5–$10 per year. Multiply across a full household and the savings from smart scheduling are meaningful.

### What to Monitor First

High-impact devices to plug into monitoring smart plugs first:
1. **Entertainment center**: TV + cable box + receiver + game console — often the biggest phantom load cluster
2. **Home office**: desktop PC + monitors + printer + speakers
3. **Kitchen counter appliances**: coffee maker, toaster, microwave
4. **Older appliances**: older refrigerators, chest freezers, window AC units

### Schedules That Save Money

Once you identify phantom load sources, set schedules to cut power automatically:
- **Entertainment center**: off from midnight to 7am
- **Office equipment**: off from 8pm to 7am
- **Phone charger hub**: on from 9pm to 6am (stops charging after full anyway)
- **Coffee maker**: on 6–9am only

The average household with energy monitoring smart plugs on their main phantom load sources saves $100–$200/year on their electricity bill.

### Electricity Rate Matters

Savings from phantom load elimination depend on your electricity rate. California, New York, and New England pay $0.20–$0.35/kWh — three times the national average of $0.12/kWh. For high-rate states, the ROI on energy monitoring smart plugs is weeks to months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will smart plugs work during a power outage?

Smart plugs lose their Wi-Fi connection during a power outage and most will return to their "last state" when power resumes. Some (like Kasa) can be configured to return to on, off, or previous state after an outage. Check your plug's settings for power-restoration behavior.

### How accurate is the energy monitoring on these plugs?

Accuracy varies: Kasa and Meross are accurate to within 5–10% for typical loads, which is sufficient for identifying phantom load and tracking consumption trends. The Shelly Plus 1PM is accurate to within 1–2%, approaching professional measurement equipment.

### Do energy monitoring smart plugs work with 240V appliances?

Standard US smart plugs are rated for 120V circuits only. 240V appliances (dryers, electric stoves, EV chargers) require dedicated 240V smart switches or whole-home monitoring solutions like the Emporia Vue 2.

### Is there a privacy concern with cloud-connected smart plugs?

Yes — most consumer smart plugs require a cloud connection and send usage data to the manufacturer's servers. If this concerns you, the Shelly Plus 1PM with local-only configuration sends zero data to the cloud. Home Assistant users can run entirely local smart home setups.

### Can smart plugs be set to turn on automatically at sunrise and off at sunset?

Yes — all the plugs listed above support sunrise/sunset scheduling tied to your home's location. This is particularly useful for outdoor lighting, porch lights, and seasonal decorative lighting.
