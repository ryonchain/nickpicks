## What Oily Skin Actually Needs

The instinct with oily skin is to wash aggressively and strip all oil. That backfires. Over-cleansing damages the skin barrier, which triggers more sebum production as a defense response — making oiliness worse.

The goal is to:
1. Remove excess surface oil without stripping baseline moisture
2. Clear pores of the mixture of sebum and dead skin cells that cause blackheads and breakouts
3. Use actives (salicylic acid, niacinamide) that regulate oil production over time

Gel and foaming formulas work better for oily skin than creamy or milky cleansers. Avoid any cleanser that leaves skin feeling "squeaky clean" — that sensation means you've stripped protective oils and the barrier is compromised.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Gel or foam formulas over cream:** Cream cleansers are formulated for dry skin. Gels and foams emulsify oil more effectively and rinse clean without residue.

**Non-comedogenic is a baseline requirement:** If a face wash doesn't say non-comedogenic, skip it. Pore-clogging ingredients in a cleanser are counterproductive.

**Fragrance-free is safer:** Added fragrance is the most common cause of contact dermatitis in skincare. Oily skin is often also sensitive — reduce unnecessary irritant exposure.

**Active ingredients need time:** Salicylic acid, niacinamide, and benzoyl peroxide all require 4–6 weeks of consistent use to show meaningful results. Don't switch after two weeks.
