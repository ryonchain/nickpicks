## How to Choose the Right Yoga Mat

### Thickness Guide

| Thickness | Feel | Best For |
|-----------|------|----------|
| 1–2mm | Minimal; close to the floor | Travel, hot yoga overlay |
| 3–4mm | Balanced cushion + stability | Most yoga styles, experienced practitioners |
| 5–6mm | Maximum cushion | Restorative yoga, beginners, sensitive joints |

Thicker isn't always better — very thick mats can make balancing poses harder (wobbly surface).

### Grip: Material Comparison

| Material | Dry Grip | Wet Grip | Eco-Friendly | Durability |
|----------|---------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Natural rubber + PU | Excellent | Excellent | Good | 5–10 years |
| Natural rubber | Very Good | Good | Good | 5–10 years |
| PVC (Manduka PRO) | Good (after break-in) | Moderate | Low | 10+ years |
| TPE/PER | Good | Moderate | Better than PVC | 2–4 years |
| Budget PVC | Adequate | Poor | Low | 1–3 years |

### Eco-Friendly Considerations

For an environmentally conscious purchase:
- **Best:** Natural rubber (biodegradable, sustainable sourcing)
- **Good:** TPE or PER (non-toxic, some biodegradability)
- **Avoid:** Standard PVC if eco-credentials matter to you

Manduka's PVC mats use OEKO-TEX certified, non-toxic PVC — a cleaner version of an otherwise non-eco material.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How thick should a yoga mat be for beginners?**
5–6mm is ideal for beginners — extra cushioning makes floor poses, kneeling, and seated positions more comfortable while your body adapts. As you progress, many practitioners prefer thinner mats (3–4mm) for better ground feel and balance stability.

**How do I clean a yoga mat?**
Wipe down with a diluted spray (water + mild dish soap or dedicated mat cleaner) after each use. Air dry completely before rolling. Deep clean monthly with a full wash — hand wash or machine wash on gentle for most mats. Never put in a dryer.

**How long do yoga mats last?**
Budget PVC mats: 1–3 years with regular use. Mid-range natural rubber: 3–5 years. Premium mats (Liforme, Manduka PRO) with proper care: 7–10+ years. Manduka PRO carries a lifetime guarantee.

**What's the difference between a yoga mat and a Pilates mat?**
Pilates mats are typically thicker (8–10mm) to cushion exercises like rolling on the spine. Yoga mats at 4–6mm prioritize grip and stability. A 6mm yoga mat works well for both practices.

**Do I need a yoga mat towel?**
If you practice hot yoga or sweat heavily, a yoga mat towel (placed on top of the mat) dramatically improves grip when wet. Natural rubber + PU surfaces like the Liforme and Lululemon Reversible improve grip with moisture — but most PVC surfaces get slippery.
