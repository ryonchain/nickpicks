## How IPL Hair Removal Works

IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) emits broad-spectrum light that's absorbed by the melanin (pigment) in hair. This converts to heat, which destroys the hair follicle's ability to regrow hair.

**Key point:** IPL works best on **light to medium skin tones with dark hair** — the contrast between skin and hair allows targeted melanin absorption. Devices now include skin tone sensors that auto-adjust intensity for safety.

**Typical results:** 70–90% reduction in hair growth after 6–8 sessions (performed weekly).

## What to Look For

### Skin Tone Compatibility
All devices work on skin tones Fitzpatrick I–IV (pale to medium-dark). **No IPL device is currently safe for Fitzpatrick V–VI (dark to very dark skin)** — melanin concentration in skin absorbs too much light and can cause burns. Consult a dermatologist for darker skin tones.

### Flash Count
- 300,000–500,000 flashes: Budget range
- 500,000+ flashes: Premium devices — lifetime use without cartridge replacement

### Treatment Window Size
Larger window = faster session. Small windows are better for face/precision work but slow for legs/body.

### Cooling
A cooled treatment window makes sessions significantly more comfortable, especially in sensitive areas. Worth the premium.
