## Why Linen Beats Cotton for Most Sleepers

Cotton is reliable. Linen is exceptional — for the right person. Here's the functional difference: linen is made from flax fibers, which are thicker and more moisture-wicking than cotton at the fiber level. That translates to sheets that absorb sweat quickly and release it into the air rather than holding dampness against your skin. In summer, linen sleeps 2–3°F cooler than equivalent-weight cotton. In winter, the looser weave traps just enough air to stay cozy.

The other advantage is durability. Quality linen sheets get softer and more beautiful with each wash for the first two years of use. Heirloom-quality sets from European mills routinely last 10–15 years with basic care, compared to 3–5 years for mid-range cotton. The math favors linen even before you account for the comfort difference.

**What to look for when buying linen sheets:**
- **European Flax certification:** Belgian and French flax is woven tighter and softer than lower-grade alternatives
- **GSM (grams per square meter):** 165–175 GSM is the sweet spot — heavy enough to feel substantial, light enough to breathe
- **Stone washing:** Pre-washed and stone-washed linen is immediately soft; unwashed is stiffer and takes 3–5 washes to break in
- **OEKO-TEX certified:** Ensures no harmful chemicals in the fabric or dye
- **Deep pockets:** 15"+ for queen/king mattresses with toppers

## Buying Guide

**How to wash linen sheets.** Machine wash cold on a gentle cycle. Tumble dry low — remove while slightly damp and let finish drying flat or on the bed. High heat shrinks linen. After 5–6 washes, you'll notice the sheets feel noticeably softer each time.

**The first-night scratch.** Fresh-from-the-box linen often feels coarser than expected if you're used to percale or sateen. This is normal. Wash once before first use, then give it two or three more nights. The break-in period is real and worth pushing through.

**Sizing advice.** Buy one size up if your mattress is deeper than 14 inches or you use a thick topper. Most linen sheets list pocket depth on the listing — 15"+ handles most applications.

**Wrinkles.** Linen wrinkles. Always. This is not a defect; it's an inherent property of flax fibers and part of the aesthetic. Smooth the sheets after making the bed and they look intentionally relaxed, not messy. If wrinkles bother you, linen is not the right choice.

## FAQ

**Are linen sheets worth the price?**
For hot sleepers, yes — unambiguously. The breathability difference versus cotton is significant. For people who already sleep cool on cotton percale, the benefit is smaller and more about texture preference.

**How long do linen sheets last?**
Quality linen sheets last 8–15 years with basic care. The fiber actually gets stronger after washing. The main failure mode is thin spots from pulling or friction, not pilling.

**Can linen sheets cause allergies?**
Linen is naturally hypoallergenic and resistant to dust mites. OEKO-TEX certified linen is free of harmful dyes or processing chemicals. It's generally a better choice than synthetic materials for allergy sufferers.

**What thread count should linen have?**
Thread count doesn't apply to linen the way it does to cotton. Focus on GSM (150–180 is ideal) and flax source (European flax is higher quality) instead.
