## What to Avoid

- **Iron in a men's multi:** Men rarely need supplemental iron; excess iron is stored in organs and linked to long-term health issues.
- **Megadose formulas:** 1,000% daily value of B vitamins is mostly marketing — water-soluble excess is excreted.
- **Oxide forms:** Magnesium oxide and zinc oxide are cheap but poorly absorbed. Look for citrate, glycinate, or picolinate.
- **Synthetic folic acid:** Active men and those with MTHFR gene variants absorb methylfolate far better.

The best multivitamin is the one you take consistently. Pick the form (capsule, tablet, gummy — though gummies are lower quality), the dose frequency that fits your routine, and a brand with third-party testing. Then stick with it.
