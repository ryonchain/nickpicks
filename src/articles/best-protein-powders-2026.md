## Protein Powder Buyer's Guide

**Whey vs. Plant:** Whey is more bioavailable and delivers more leucine per gram of protein — leucine is the amino acid that triggers muscle protein synthesis. Plant proteins require combining sources (pea + rice) to create a complete amino acid profile. For most people, either works if total daily protein is adequate.

**Isolate vs. Concentrate:** Isolate is filtered to remove more fat and lactose (90%+ protein content). Concentrate retains more fat and lactose (70–80% protein content). Isolate costs more but works better for lactose-sensitive individuals and for keeping calories tight.

**Third-Party Testing:** Look for NSF Certified for Sport, Informed Sport, or Informed Choice logos. These programs test for banned substances and protein accuracy. Labdoor ratings (labdoor.com) are also valuable for checking if a product contains what the label claims.

**Protein Targets:** Most research supports 0.7–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight for muscle building. A 170 lb person needs 119–170g of protein daily. If diet provides 80–120g, one or two protein shakes close the gap.
