## What PS5 and Xbox Series X Need from a Monitor

Both PS5 and Xbox Series X output up to 4K at 120Hz, but they require specific features to do it:

**Must-have:**
- **HDMI 2.1 port** — required for 4K 120Hz. HDMI 2.0 maxes out at 4K 60Hz or 1440p 120Hz
- **4K or 1440p resolution** — to take advantage of console output
- **120Hz refresh rate** — for the smoothest gameplay

**Nice to have:**
- **VRR (Variable Refresh Rate)** — reduces screen tearing; PS5 and Xbox both support it
- **HDR (HDR10 or Dolby Vision)** — improves contrast and color in supported games
- **Auto Low Latency Mode (ALLM)** — monitor switches to gaming mode automatically when console is detected
- **Low input lag** — ideally under 5ms in game mode

---

## The 4K vs. 1440p Decision

**4K (3840x2160):** The PS5 and Xbox Series X are built around 4K. A 4K monitor gets the most from the console's hardware in games that support 4K output.

**1440p (2560x1440):** Most monitors only support up to 1440p natively, but the PS5 and Xbox Series X now support 1440p output (after software updates). A 1440p 120Hz monitor is a solid choice if you want a higher refresh rate at a lower price point.

**For console gaming specifically:** 4K at 60Hz vs. 1440p at 120Hz is a real trade-off in many games. Games with 60fps caps benefit from 4K. Games with 120fps modes benefit from 1440p. Know your game list.

## HDMI 2.0 vs. HDMI 2.1: Why It Matters

This is the single most important spec to verify before buying a console gaming monitor:

| Connection | Max Bandwidth | PS5/Xbox Capability |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|
| HDMI 2.0 | 18 Gbps | 4K 60Hz or 1440p 120Hz |
| HDMI 2.1 | 48 Gbps | 4K 120Hz, 4K VRR |

If you want 4K 120Hz on PS5 or Xbox Series X, you **must** have HDMI 2.1 on the monitor. This is the most common spec mistake made when buying console monitors — many monitors advertise "4K 120Hz" but their HDMI port is only 2.0, which can't actually output 4K 120Hz from a console.

**How to verify:** Check the spec sheet for "HDMI 2.1" specifically, not just the max resolution or refresh rate.
