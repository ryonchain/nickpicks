## What to Know Before Buying an Electric Pressure Washer

### PSI vs. GPM — Which Matters More?

PSI (pounds per square inch) measures the pressure of the water. GPM (gallons per minute) measures the volume. Cleaning power is actually a function of both — multiply them together to get cleaning units (CU). A 2000 PSI / 1.76 GPM machine (3520 CU) will clean better than a 2500 PSI / 0.9 GPM machine (2250 CU) despite the lower pressure number.

### Nozzle Selection

Most machines include 4-5 quick-connect nozzles:
- **0-degree (red):** Concentrated stream for stubborn stains — use with caution
- **15-degree (yellow):** Heavy-duty cleaning on hard surfaces
- **25-degree (green):** General cleaning — the workhorse nozzle
- **40-degree (white):** Delicate surfaces, cars, windows
- **65-degree/soap (black):** Low pressure for applying detergent

Always start with the widest nozzle and work toward narrower if needed.

### Electric vs. Gas

Gas pressure washers offer higher pressure and don't need an outlet nearby. For most homeowners, though, electric wins: quieter, lower maintenance, no exhaust, and 2000+ PSI is enough for 95% of home tasks. The only scenario where gas makes sense is professional use or regularly cleaning very large concrete areas.

### Hose Length

A 25-foot hose is the practical minimum. A 35-foot hose gives you meaningful freedom of movement. If you have a large property, budget for a 50-foot hose extension rather than constantly repositioning the machine.
