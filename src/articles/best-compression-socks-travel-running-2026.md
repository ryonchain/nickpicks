## Compression Sock Buying Guide

### Understanding mmHg Ratings
- **8–15 mmHg (light):** General fatigue, mild swelling, prevention
- **15–20 mmHg (moderate):** Travel, mild varicose veins, prolonged standing
- **20–30 mmHg (firm):** Athletic recovery, post-surgery, moderate varicose veins, DVT prevention
- **30–40 mmHg (extra firm):** Medical-grade, typically requires prescription

For most healthy adults traveling or exercising, 15–20 or 20–30 mmHg is appropriate. Consult a physician for 30+ mmHg.

### Sizing Matters
Compression socks must fit correctly to work. Too large = no compression benefit. Too small = circulation constriction. Measure:
1. **Shoe size** (for length)
2. **Calf circumference** at the widest point (usually ~3–4 inches below the back of the knee)
3. **Ankle circumference** at the narrowest point

Most brands provide size charts based on these two measurements.

### Care Instructions
- **Wash inside-out** on gentle cycle or hand wash
- **Air dry only** — dryer heat degrades elastic fibers and destroys compression
- **Replace every 3–6 months** with regular use — compression diminishes over time regardless of visible wear

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can compression socks prevent DVT on flights?**
Compression socks reduce DVT risk but don't eliminate it. 20–30 mmHg graduation, combined with walking every hour and staying hydrated, significantly reduces clot risk on flights over 6 hours. Consult a doctor if you have clotting history.

**Should compression socks be tight or uncomfortable?**
Graduated compression should feel firm but not painful. Tightness at the ankle (the highest compression point) is normal. Pain, numbness, or tingling means the socks are too small or you have a circulation issue — remove them and consult a physician.

**Can I sleep in compression socks?**
Generally not recommended unless prescribed. Compression works with the muscle pump mechanism during movement. While lying down, gravity isn't fighting circulation, so the benefit is minimal and prolonged wearing can cause problems for some people.
