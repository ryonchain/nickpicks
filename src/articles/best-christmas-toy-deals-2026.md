Christmas is the biggest toy-buying event of the year — but the **best toy deals happen before Christmas, not during it**. Here's when prices peak, which toys to prioritize, and how to avoid the last-minute shortage scramble.

## When Do Christmas Toy Deals Peak?

| Date | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| Black Friday (Nov 28) | Deepest discounts — LEGO, Hot Wheels, Nintendo bundles |
| Cyber Monday (Dec 1) | Online toy extensions, Amazon Lightning Deals on toys |
| Dec 1–14 | Second wave — major toy sets go on sale |
| Dec 15 | Prime two-day shipping window closes for Christmas |
| Dec 18–22 | Last-chance week — depleted stock, higher prices on popular items |
| Dec 26 | Toy clearance begins — up to 70% off remaining stock |

**Key rule:** Popular toys (LEGO flagship sets, Pokémon trading cards, Nintendo bundles) sell out between December 10–15. Buy by Cyber Week for guaranteed selection.

## Christmas Toy Shopping Tips

1. **The 12 Days Before Christmas are the most dangerous for inventory** — popular toys sell out December 10–15 and don't restock. Buy early.
2. **Price track before you buy** — use CamelCamelCamel to confirm Amazon's "Christmas deal" is actually lower than the year-round price.
3. **Amazon "Buy Again" items stay stocked longer** — household names like LEGO and Hot Wheels core sets maintain inventory well; boutique brands and niche toys do not.
4. **Check Target Circle deals** — Target frequently runs 20–30% off toy events in early December, sometimes deeper than Amazon.
5. **After-Christmas is real for toys** — if your child won't notice a slightly late delivery, the week of December 26 brings toy clearance of 50–70% at Target, Walmart, and Kohl's.
