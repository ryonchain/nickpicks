## Hammock Types

### Single Hammock
- Capacity: 1 person (typically 250–350 lb limit)
- Pack size: Fist-sized; ultra packable
- Best for: Solo backpackers, weight-conscious hikers
- Tradeoff: Not comfortable for couples; limited lounging/sitting width

### Double Hammock
- Capacity: 1–2 people (typically 400–500 lb limit)
- Pack size: Larger (softball to football size)
- Best for: Car camping, backyard, couples
- Tradeoff: Slightly heavier; overkill for solo ultralight

### Gathered-End vs. Spreader Bar
- **Gathered-end (banana shape):** The standard for camping. Natural cocoon position. Packs small.
- **Spreader bar (flat):** Stays flat. Better for lounging/reading. Harder to sleep in comfortably. Common in resort/beach hammocks, not camping.

For sleeping, gathered-end hammocks are overwhelmingly preferred. The key is diagonal lying—shift your body 30° from the centerline and the hammock flattens into a genuinely flat sleeping surface.

## Hammock Setup Guide

### Tree Selection
- Find trees 12–15 feet apart
- Trees should be at least 8–10 inches in diameter (smaller trees can be damaged and may not hold weight)
- Healthy trees only—dead standing trees may fall

### The 30° Rule
Hang straps so the suspension angle is approximately 30° from horizontal. Flatter = more tension on straps and trees (risk); steeper = too much hammock sag (uncomfortable). 30° is the sweet spot confirmed by both physics and experienced hammock campers.

### Diagonal Lie Position
Don't lie straight along the centerline—you'll feel like a burrito. Instead, shift your body 30° off center (feet toward one corner, head toward the other). The hammock flattens dramatically and back-flat sleeping becomes possible.

### Cold-Weather Hammocking
Hammocks lose heat rapidly through the bottom (no insulation below you). Solutions:
1. **Underquilt:** Hangs beneath hammock exterior; most effective but adds weight
2. **Foam sleeping pad:** Slide underneath your sleeping bag inside the hammock
3. **Winter jacket under your back:** Emergency approach; works for mild temperatures
