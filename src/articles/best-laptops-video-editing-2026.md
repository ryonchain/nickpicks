## Buyer's Guide

### Minimum Specs for Video Editing

| Resolution | CPU | RAM | GPU | Storage |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|---------|

| 4K (H.264/H.265) | Intel Core Ultra / M3 | 32GB | RTX 4060 / M3 GPU | 1TB+ |
| 4K ProRes/RAW | Intel Core Ultra 9 / M3 Pro | 32–64GB | RTX 4070 / M3 Pro | 2TB+ |
| 8K/Cinema RAW | M3 Max / Intel Core Ultra 9 HX | 64–128GB | RTX 4080 / M3 Max | 4TB+ |

### Apple Silicon vs. Intel/AMD for Video Editing

**Apple M3/M3 Pro/M3 Max:** Hardware media engines for ProRes and HEVC make them dramatically faster for Final Cut and optimized DaVinci workflows. Unified memory is faster than discrete GPU. Best battery life.

**Intel Core Ultra + NVIDIA RTX:** Best for GPU-accelerated effects in Premiere Pro (CUDA performance), After Effects, 3D rendering. Required for some Windows-only plugins and software.

### Most Important Specs for Editors

1. **RAM**: 32GB is minimum for 4K; 64GB+ for RAW workflows
2. **SSD Speed**: NVMe SSD; 3,000+ MB/s for 4K; 7,000+ MB/s for 8K
3. **Display color accuracy**: DCI-P3 coverage essential; Delta E < 2 preferred
4. **GPU VRAM**: 8GB+ for After Effects/3D; 12GB+ for 8K
5. **CPU**: MediaEngine support (Apple) or Core Ultra H-series (Intel) for hardware encoding

### Best Video Editing Software by Platform
- **Mac**: Final Cut Pro ($299 one-time) — fastest for ProRes/HEVC
- **Cross-platform**: DaVinci Resolve (free + paid Studio) — best color grading
- **Windows/Cross-platform**: Adobe Premiere Pro (subscription) — most plugins, industry standard

## FAQ

**Is a MacBook Pro really better than Windows for video editing?**
For Final Cut Pro users: significantly yes. For Adobe Premiere: competitive, with some CUDA-dependent effects favoring Windows + RTX. For DaVinci Resolve: M3 Pro/Max is the fastest laptop available for the price.

**How much RAM do I need for 4K video editing?**
32GB is the current practical minimum for 4K H.264/H.265. For 4K RAW (RED, ARRI, BlackMagic): 64GB+. For 8K workflows: 128GB.

**Do I need a dedicated GPU for video editing?**
For YouTube (H.264/H.265 codecs): a good CPU handles it. For effects-heavy work (motion graphics, 3D, color grading): a dedicated GPU speeds up rendering significantly. Apple M-series GPUs are the exception — they're very capable despite being "integrated."

**What external storage should I get for video editing?**
- Internal NVMe SSD for active projects
- External SSD (Samsung T7 Shield, WD My Passport SSD) for backups
- Large HDD (4–8TB) for archival storage

**Is a 1TB SSD enough for video editing?**
For one or two active projects: yes. 4K H.264 is about 15GB/hour; 4K ProRes is about 200GB/hour. Manage by keeping only active projects internal and archiving to external drives.
