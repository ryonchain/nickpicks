## The Rule for Kitchen Gadgets

Before buying any kitchen tool, ask two questions:
1. Will I use this at least once a week?
2. Does it do something I can't do without it — or do substantially better?

If both answers are yes, buy it. If you're only nodding yes to one, you'll have another drawer orphan within six months.

Everything on this list passes both tests for the average home cook.

## Gadgets to Skip

**Avocado slicer:** A knife and a spoon do the same thing. The avocado slicer only saves 5 seconds and creates a single-purpose drawer occupant.

**Electric can opener:** Unless hand strength is a genuine limitation, a good manual can opener is faster to set up, faster to use, and takes no counter space.

**Strawberry huller:** Just cut the top off with a knife. Takes three seconds, zero cleanup.

**Herb scissors:** A good chef's knife and cutting board are faster than the cleanup on multi-blade herb scissors.
