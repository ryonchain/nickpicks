## What to Look for in Carry-On Luggage

### Dimensions: Universal Compliance
The most common carry-on size limit for US carriers is 22" x 14" x 9" (total 45 linear inches). International budget carriers (Ryanair, EasyJet, AirAsia) have stricter limits — 20" x 15.5" x 7.9" for Ryanair. If you fly internationally on budget carriers, prioritize bags in the smaller compliance range.

### Hardside vs. Softside
**Hardside:** Better for fragile contents, maintains shape, easier to wipe clean. More durable shell but less packable. Polycarbonate is the best material at this price range; aluminum is the lifetime investment.

**Softside:** More capacity in the same dimension (flexible shell compresses slightly), easier overpacking, exterior pockets for quick-access items. Better for overstuffed packers.

### Spinner vs. Two-Wheel
Spinner wheels (4-wheel, swiveling) roll in any direction — easier to maneuver in crowds. Two-wheel bags (inline skate style) roll only forward/back — better on uneven surfaces and cobblestones. Most travel is best served by spinners.

### Weight
US airlines generally don't enforce carry-on weight limits; international carriers often enforce 15 lb (6.8 kg) limits strictly. A 7-lb bag leaves 8 lbs for contents under a 15 lb limit — workable. A 9-lb bag leaves only 6 lbs, which severely limits packing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size carry-on fits in all overhead bins?**
No single size guarantees universal bin access. Standard US airline limit is 22" x 14" x 9" — bags at or under this size fit most domestic overhead bins. Budget carriers (Spirit, Frontier) have stricter limits. Always check your specific airline's published limits before your trip.

**Is hardside or softside more durable?**
Both can be durable — it depends on the material and brand more than the shell type. Aluminum (Rimowa) is the most durable. High-quality polycarbonate (Away) lasts 5–10 years with regular use. High-denier nylon (Travelpro) lasts equally long. Budget polycarbonate or nylon from no-name brands fails faster regardless of style.

**Can I bring a carry-on and a personal item?**
Yes — most airlines allow one carry-on (overhead bin) and one personal item (under seat in front of you). Personal item size limits vary by carrier but are typically around 18" x 14" x 8". A personal item bag or daypack that fits in the carry-on can substitute on routes where you want to travel light.

**How do I protect fragile items in a carry-on?**
Pack fragile items in the center surrounded by clothing. Hardside shells provide more impact protection than softside. For extremely fragile electronics or breakables, carry them on your person or in a padded camera bag rather than in a suitcase.
