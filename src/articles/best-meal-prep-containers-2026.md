## Glass vs. Plastic vs. Stainless: Which Should You Choose?

**Glass:**
- Pros: No chemical leaching, oven-safe, doesn't stain or absorb odors
- Cons: Heavier, breakable
- Best for: Home meal prep, reheating in oven or microwave

**Plastic:**
- Pros: Lightweight, durable, affordable
- Cons: Can leach chemicals if old or scratched; stains with tomato sauces
- Best for: Office lunches, when weight matters
- Look for: BPA-free, phthalate-free labels

**Stainless Steel:**
- Pros: Zero plastic contact, durable, no odor/flavor absorption
- Cons: Not microwave-safe, heavier, pricier
- Best for: Cold food storage, zero-plastic household goals

## Meal Prep Container Sizes: What You Actually Need

For a standard meal prep setup:
- **Small (1–2 cups):** Snacks, sides, sauces
- **Medium (3–4 cups):** Single meals (salads, grain bowls)
- **Large (5–7 cups):** Batch cooking storage (soups, chili, pasta)

A good 10-piece set should include 2–3 sizes. Buying all the same size limits flexibility.
