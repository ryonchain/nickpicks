## Why Luxury Hair Care Is Worth It (Sometimes)

Hair care doesn't have the same clinical backing as skincare — the FDA doesn't regulate hair care claims the way it does cosmetics. But genuine chemistry differences exist:

**Worth the premium:**
- Bond repair treatments (Olaplex) — real, patented chemistry
- Keratin and ceramide concentrations in repair lines (Kérastase, Redken)
- Argan oil purity and concentration (real Moroccanoil vs. imitators)
- Formulas free of damaging sulfates, parabens, and silicones that build up

**Harder to justify:**
- Luxury shampoos that rinse out before actives absorb
- Marketing claims around "shine" and "smoothness" without specific actives
- Expensive packaging inflating the price of a basic formula

The guide below focuses on products where the chemistry genuinely outperforms the price.

## The Non-Negotiable Rule

No luxury hair care product repairs damage faster than a trim. Split ends cannot be "repaired" — they can only be temporarily smoothed with coatings. If breakage or splits are your concern, book a trim first, then invest in prevention with Olaplex and a quality oil.

Apply products to mid-lengths and ends, not roots (except shampoo). The ends are where damage lives; the roots produce their own natural oils and don't need additional product weight.
