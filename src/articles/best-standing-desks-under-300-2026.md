## Types of Standing Desks

**Electric sit-stand desks (motorized):** Press a button to move between sitting and standing height. The best option if you'll actually use the standing feature daily. Requires a power outlet. Most have programmable height presets.

**Manual crank desks:** Turn a crank to adjust height. Cheaper, no power needed, but you'll use the standing feature less often if you have to crank it up every time.

**Desktop converters:** Sit on top of your existing desk and raise to standing height. Cheapest option, but takes up desk space and can feel unstable at full height.

For regular use, an electric desk is worth the extra cost over a converter.

## Key Specs Explained

### Height Range

Your desk height should let you stand with your elbows at 90° — typically 38-44" for most adults. Sit height should match your seat height with feet flat on the floor, typically 26-30" for most chairs.

Check the desk's height range covers both your sit AND stand positions before buying. Some budget desks don't go low enough for short users or high enough for very tall users.

**Height guide by height:**
- Under 5'4": Look for minimum height of 25" or less
- 5'4" to 6'0": Standard range (28-47") covers you
- Over 6'0": Look for max height of 48"+ 

### Desktop Size

Under $300, you're typically looking at 48"x24" desktops. That comfortably fits:
- One 27" ultrawide monitor + laptop
- Two monitors (24" each)
- Monitor + laptop + accessories

If you need more space, step up to the 55" options (SHW at ~$240) or budget up to $350 for 60" desktops.

### Motor Stability

Wobble is the #1 complaint with budget standing desks. At standing height (45"+), desks flex side-to-side when you type or use a mouse. Test factors:
- Dual-motor designs (motors on each leg) are more stable than single-motor designs
- Cross-bracing between legs improves stability significantly
- Heavier steel construction reduces sway

## How to Actually Use a Standing Desk

The goal isn't standing all day — it's alternating. Research suggests:
- 20-30 minutes sitting, 8-10 minutes standing is optimal for most people
- Standing while on calls is the easiest habit to start
- A programmable reminder (your phone alarm) helps build the sit/stand rhythm

Most standing desk users find the $200+ investment pays for itself in reduced afternoon back pain and improved focus during standing periods.
