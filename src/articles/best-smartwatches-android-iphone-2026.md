## What Makes a Smartwatch Worth Buying

Four factors determine whether a smartwatch actually earns daily wrist space:

- **Platform Compatibility:** Apple Watch only works with iPhone. Samsung Galaxy Watch works with Android (optimal) and iPhone (limited). Garmin and Fitbit devices work with both iOS and Android equally well.
- **Health Sensors:** Look for continuous heart rate monitoring, SpO2 (blood oxygen), and skin temperature sensors for sleep tracking accuracy. ECG capability requires FDA clearance — not all countries support it.
- **Battery Life:** The Apple Watch and Galaxy Watch need nightly charging. Garmin and Amazfit watches last 7–20 days on a charge — a real differentiator for travel and endurance athletes.
- **GPS Accuracy:** For runners and cyclists, on-board GPS eliminates phone dependency. Multi-band GPS (L1+L5) dramatically improves accuracy in cities and under tree cover.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### Platform Lock-In
Before buying, understand what you're committing to. Apple Watch cannot pair with Android. Galaxy Watch's health features are reduced outside Samsung phones. Garmin and Fitbit are genuinely platform-agnostic — switch phones without losing your health data history.

### Sleep Tracking Quality
Skin temperature sensors (Apple Series 10, Fitbit Sense 3) dramatically improve sleep stage accuracy compared to heart rate alone. If sleep health is your primary motivation, prioritize watches with temperature sensing.

### Wrist-Based vs. Chest Strap Heart Rate
All these watches use optical (PPG) wrist-based heart rate, which works well at rest and light activity. For high-intensity training where accuracy matters for training zones, pair with an ANT+/Bluetooth chest strap — most Garmin watches support this.

### Display Technology
AMOLED displays offer vivid color and deep blacks but consume more battery. MIP (Memory-in-Pixel) displays used by older Garmin models are barely readable indoors but last weeks on a charge and are clear outdoors. Newer Garmin AMOLED models (Forerunner 265) compromise intelligently between the two.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use Apple Watch with an Android phone?**
No. Apple Watch requires an iPhone (iPhone XS or newer) for setup and sync. There is no Android-compatible mode.

**Do I need a phone plan for my smartwatch?**
LTE-enabled versions (Apple Watch with Cellular, Samsung Galaxy Watch LTE) support standalone calling and data without your phone nearby — but require adding a line to your carrier plan (typically $10–15/month).

**How accurate is wrist-based heart rate for workouts?**
At steady-state cardio (running, cycling), wrist heart rate is accurate within 2–5 BPM of a chest strap. During HIIT, heavy lifting, or sports with wrist movement, accuracy degrades. For training zone precision, use a dedicated chest strap.

**Will my smartwatch work in airplane mode?**
Yes. GPS, heart rate tracking, and offline workouts work without cellular or Wi-Fi. Smartwatch functions that require connectivity (notifications, streaming music) pause until you reconnect.
