## Men's Down Jacket Buying Guide

**Down vs. synthetic insulation:**
- **Down:** Warmer per weight, more packable, premium feel. Loses insulating power when wet.
- **Synthetic:** Remains warm when wet, cheaper, easier to care for. Heavier and less packable.
- **Hybrid:** Down body + synthetic shoulders/underarms (Patagonia Nano Puff, Arc'teryx). Best of both.

**Fill power ratings:**
- **550–600 fill:** Entry-level down. Good warmth, not the most packable.
- **650–700 fill:** Good mid-range. Most Eddie Bauer and TNF jackets.
- **750–800 fill:** High performance. Excellent warmth-to-weight ratio.
- **850+ fill:** Premium. Lightest, warmest, most packable. Arc'teryx, Western Mountaineering.

**When to choose down vs. puffer:**
- **Down jacket (packable):** Great for travel, mid-layers, and dry conditions.
- **Full puffer (water-resistant):** Better for daily urban use where brief rain is likely.
- **Expedition parka:** For below -20°F conditions; much bulkier.

**Care instructions:**
- Down jackets require periodic washing (down clumps when dirty, reducing insulation)
- Wash on gentle with down-specific detergent (Nikwax Down Wash)
- Tumble dry on low with 2-3 dryer balls to re-loft the down
- Store uncompressed when possible
