# Best Sourdough Baking Supplies (2026)

Sourdough baking has a steep initial equipment investment that beginners often get wrong — buying the wrong things and struggling, or spending far more than necessary. We break down exactly what you need, what's worth spending money on, and what you can skip.

The good news: a $50–80 investment covers the essentials, and a great loaf of sourdough from proper equipment is genuinely better than anything you can buy at a bakery.

## Bottom Line

**The non-negotiables:** Dutch oven, digital kitchen scale, banneton proofing basket, scoring lame.
**Most impactful single upgrade:** A quality Dutch oven. The steam-trapping lid is responsible for the ear (that iconic crust rupture) and open crumb structure that defines artisan sourdough.
**Skip for now:** Bread proofer/warming box, fancy mixers, and most specialized gadgets — master the basics first.

## Essential Sourdough Equipment

### Dutch Oven (The Most Important Tool)

Sourdough baked in a Dutch oven steams in its own moisture for the first 20 minutes (lid on), then browns with the lid off. This two-stage baking process creates:
- **Oven spring**: maximum rise before the crust sets
- **The ear**: the scored ridge that opens dramatically
- **Crackling crust**: the signature of a properly baked sourdough

**What to look for:** 4–6 quart capacity (standard loaf size), enameled interior (prevents rust), flat or shallow lid (easier to maneuver), oven-safe to 500°F.

### Digital Scale (Most Beginner-Skipped Essential)

Sourdough baking uses baker's percentages — all ingredients are measured by weight relative to flour. Measuring by volume (cups) introduces 15–25% measurement error that makes results inconsistent. A digital scale with 1-gram precision costs $15–25 and immediately improves your baking.

### Banneton (Proofing Basket)

A banneton shapes the dough during its final rise and creates the characteristic spiral ring pattern on the crust. The rattan coils wick away surface moisture, helping the dough develop the skin needed for clean scoring.

**Shapes:** Round (boule) for round loaves, oval (batard) for torpedo-shaped loaves. A round banneton handles most beginner recipes.

**Size:** 9" round fits most standard 1,000g dough recipes (500g flour).

### Scoring Lame

Scoring (cutting the surface of shaped dough before baking) controls where the loaf expands. Without scoring, dough bursts unpredictably. A proper lame (curved blade on a handle) enables the clean, controlled cuts that create the ear. A double-edge razor blade works fine — the handle just provides control.

## Sourdough Starter: What You Actually Need

A sourdough starter requires only flour, water, and time — no equipment purchase necessary. Mix equal parts flour and water by weight (50g each), leave at room temperature, and feed daily for 5–7 days. By day 5–7, it should be active enough to leaven bread.

To skip the 7-day startup: **Cultures for Health** and **King Arthur** sell established, dehydrated starters that activate within 3–5 days ($8–15).
