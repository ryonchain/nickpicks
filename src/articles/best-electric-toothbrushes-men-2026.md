## Why Electric Beats Manual for Most Men

Independent clinical studies consistently show electric toothbrushes remove 21% more plaque than manual brushes over a 3-month period. But the real gap is behavioral: the timers, pressure sensors, and quadrant prompts on quality electric toothbrushes correct the two biggest manual brushing mistakes men make — brushing too short and brushing too hard.

The right electric toothbrush for you depends primarily on three things:
1. **Gum sensitivity:** Sonicare's sonic technology is gentler on inflamed or receding gum tissue
2. **Plaque priority:** Oral-B's oscillating head generates more mechanical contact per stroke
3. **Feature preferences:** Premium models add AI coaching, Bluetooth tracking, and whitening modes that lower-tier models skip

## What to Look For When Buying

**Oscillating vs. sonic:** This is the primary choice. Oral-B (oscillating-rotating) for maximum plaque removal efficacy. Sonicare (sonic) for gum-safe cleaning and comfort. Both dramatically outperform manual brushing.

**Pressure sensor type:** Basic pressure sensors add a warning light when you brush too hard. Advanced pressure sensors (Sonicare DiamondClean 9300) actually reduce motor speed. If you're a hard brusher, the active pressure reduction is worth paying for.

**Brush head cost over time:** Budget the replacement head cost into your purchase decision. Both Oral-B and Sonicare have subscription programs that reduce per-head cost to $5–8 per head per quarter.

**Modes vs. simplicity:** Multiple cleaning modes are genuinely useful if you use them. If you'll only ever use Daily Clean, save money and buy the Pro 1000.
