## Burr Grinders vs. Blade Grinders: Why It Matters

A blade grinder is a spice chopper. It chops beans randomly — producing a mix of fine dust (over-extracts) and coarse chunks (under-extracts) in the same batch. The result is coffee that tastes both bitter and sour simultaneously.

A **burr grinder** passes beans between two abrasive surfaces at a controlled distance. Every particle exits at approximately the same size. Consistent grind size = consistent extraction = coffee that tastes the way it's supposed to.

**Conical burrs** (most home grinders) stack one cone-shaped burr inside a ring burr. They're quieter, generate less heat, and handle a wide range of brew methods. **Flat burrs** (prosumer and commercial) use two parallel disc burrs — they produce more uniform particle distribution and are preferred for espresso, but generate more heat and are louder.

**Grind retention** (grounds stuck inside the grinder) is an important spec often overlooked in product listings. High-retention grinders waste 1–3g per dose (significant at specialty coffee prices) and produce stale grounds mixed with fresh. Single-dose grinders and designs with short grind paths minimize retention.

## Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Burr Grinder

### Match the Grinder to Your Brew Method

**Espresso** demands the finest, most consistent grind — particle variation causes channeling in the puck. Use only grinders with 50+ adjustment steps and a proven track record for espresso (Baratza Encore ESP, 1Zpresso JX-Pro, Niche Zero).

**Pour-over and drip** work well with 10–20 settings — medium grind ranges where consistency matters but exact particle size has more tolerance. The Oxo Brew and Capresso Infinity Plus are excellent here.

**French press and cold brew** use coarse grinds where almost any burr grinder performs adequately. Even the budget Capresso handles coarse grinding well.

### Grind Retention and Single Dosing
If you drink specialty coffee by origin and roast frequently, high retention wastes expensive beans and introduces stale grounds. Single-dose grinders (Niche Zero, 1Zpresso) optimize for zero waste and maximum freshness at the cost of convenience for high-volume households.

### Manual vs. Electric Trade-offs
Manual grinders offer better grind quality per dollar and near-zero retention. Electric grinders offer speed and consistency without effort. For households making 2+ cups daily with different roasts, electric wins on practicality. For single-cup specialty enthusiasts who enjoy the ritual, manual wins on quality per dollar.

### Grinder Maintenance
Steel burrs should be cleaned monthly with a grinder brush and quarterly with Urnex Grindz cleaning tablets. Ceramic burrs need less frequent cleaning but should be brushed after each use to prevent oils building up on the surface.
