## Burr Coffee Grinder Buying Guide

### Conical vs. Flat Burrs
**Conical burrs** produce slightly varied particle sizes that many find adds complexity to filter coffee. **Flat burrs** produce extremely uniform particle sizes, ideal for espresso clarity and precision. Both make excellent coffee — the difference is subtle and matters more to enthusiasts than casual drinkers.

### Grind Consistency Is Everything
The whole point of a burr grinder vs. a blade grinder is **consistency**. Inconsistent grinds produce uneven extraction — some coffee grounds over-extract (bitter) while others under-extract (sour). Look for "low fines" in reviews as a measure of grind quality.

### Grind Retention Matters for Freshness
Retention refers to how much ground coffee stays inside the grinder after each use. Low-retention grinders waste less coffee and prevent stale grounds from mixing with fresh ones. Single-dose grinders typically have the lowest retention.

### Match Your Grinder to Your Brewing Method
- **Espresso**: needs ultra-fine, consistent grind with micro-adjustable settings
- **Pour-Over**: needs medium-fine to medium, flat burrs excel here
- **French Press**: needs coarse, consistent chop — most grinders handle this well
- **AeroPress**: medium-fine works great, very forgiving

### Noise and Speed
Electric burr grinders make noise. Expect 65–85 dB — similar to a food blender. Manual grinders are silent but require effort. Grinding speed doesn't affect quality, but 5–10 second grind times are faster and more convenient for daily use.
