## What VESA Means and Why It Matters

VESA is a standardized hole pattern on the back of monitors for mounting arms and brackets. The most common patterns are 75×75 mm and 100×100 mm — nearly all modern monitors have one of these.

**Before buying:** look at the back of your monitor for four holes arranged in a square. If the holes exist, the monitor is VESA-compatible. If the back is flat/sealed, the monitor is not VESA-compatible and cannot be arm-mounted.

## FAQ: Monitor Arm Questions

**What monitor size is too heavy for a standard arm?**
Most single-arm mounts handle up to 25–32 lbs. A 27" 4K monitor typically weighs 12–16 lbs. A 34" ultrawide weighs 15–22 lbs. A 49" super-ultrawide can exceed 30 lbs — check before buying.

**Does a monitor arm improve ergonomics?**
Significantly. Most built-in monitor stands don't adjust high enough — screens end up below eye level, causing neck strain. A monitor arm gets the center of the screen exactly at eye level.

**Can I use a monitor arm on a glass desk?**
Yes, with the grommet mount (hole-through-desk). C-clamps may crack thin glass. Check your desk thickness and glass rating before applying any clamp.
