## What to Look For When Buying

**Dedicated vs. kit attachment:** A dedicated $20 nose trimmer outperforms most kit attachments in comfort and effectiveness. If you use a kit attachment and it pulls, the standalone upgrade is worth the cost.

**Waterproof for easy cleaning:** Nose hair trimmers accumulate debris quickly. Waterproof models (Panasonic, Philips NT5600) take 10 seconds to rinse clean; non-waterproof models require brush cleaning, which takes longer and leaves more residual debris.

**Battery type:** All trimmers reviewed here use AA batteries. Rechargeable AA batteries (Eneloop Pro) reduce the ongoing battery cost significantly and are recommended for any battery-powered grooming tool.

**Rotary vs. reciprocating:** Rotary blades (Panasonic, Philips) cut from all angles simultaneously — more effective per insertion. Reciprocating blades (some older models) cut in one plane — requires more repositioning. Rotary is the clear choice for nose and ear use.
