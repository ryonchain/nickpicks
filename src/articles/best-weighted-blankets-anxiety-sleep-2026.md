## How Weighted Blankets Work

The "10% of body weight" guideline is a starting point — most adults find 15–25 lbs effective. The weight should feel snug but not restrictive. Deep pressure stimulation (DPS) triggers the release of serotonin and melatonin while reducing cortisol levels. Studies show measurable reductions in anxiety and improved sleep onset in populations ranging from children with ADHD to adults with generalized anxiety disorder.

**Choosing weight:**
- Under 120 lbs: 10–15 lb blanket
- 120–180 lbs: 15–20 lb blanket
- Over 180 lbs: 20–25 lb blanket
- Children: consult a pediatric OT; start at 5–7 lbs for ages 5+

## Buying Guide: Choosing a Weighted Blanket

### Weight Selection

The 10% of body weight guideline is a starting point, not a rule. Many users prefer going heavier — a 180 lb person might prefer a 20–25 lb blanket over the guideline's 18 lbs. Start conservatively if you're new to weighted blankets; you can always size up. Never use weighted blankets on infants or children under 2 years old.

### Fill: Glass Beads vs. Yarn

**Glass microbeads**: Dense, quiet, even distribution. The standard fill. Better for precise weight targeting. Can trap heat.

**Yarn/knit construction** (Bearaby): Breathable, aesthetic, no bead migration. Weight is less precise but sufficient for most users. More breathable in warm weather.

### Cover Material

**Fleece**: Warm, soft, good for winter. Can overheat sensitive sleepers.  
**Cooling fabric**: Moisture-wicking, summer-friendly. Noticeably cooler than fleece.  
**Cotton**: Breathable middle ground. Best year-round option for most climates.

### Washing Considerations

All weighted blankets require careful washing — the weight can damage home machines above certain thresholds. Most 20 lb and under blankets are machine-washable in standard home machines. 25 lb and heavier often require commercial washers. Check manufacturer instructions carefully.

## FAQ

**Do weighted blankets help with anxiety?**
Multiple studies show reductions in anxiety symptoms in clinical and non-clinical populations. A 2020 study in the Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine found weighted blankets significantly reduced anxiety and improved sleep in psychiatric populations. Individual results vary.

**Can I sleep with a weighted blanket all night?**
Yes. Weighted blankets are designed for full-night use. Most users find they get used to the weight within 1–2 weeks and wouldn't sleep without it.

**Is it safe to use a weighted blanket with a partner?**
For couples who share a bed, two twin-size weighted blankets work better than one shared blanket — each person can choose their preferred weight.

**How long do weighted blankets last?**
Quality weighted blankets last 5–10 years with proper care. Signs of replacement: significant bead migration, broken stitching allowing bead escape, or loss of weight distribution evenness.
