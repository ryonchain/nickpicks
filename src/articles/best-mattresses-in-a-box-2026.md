## How to Choose the Right Mattress

### Step 1: Know your sleep position

| Sleep Position | Recommended Firmness | Why |
|---------------|----------------------|-----|
| Side | Medium to Soft | Cushions shoulder and hip pressure points |
| Back | Medium-Firm | Supports lumbar curve without sinking |
| Stomach | Firm | Prevents lower back from arching |
| Combination | Medium | Balances all positions |

### Step 2: Understand foam vs. hybrid vs. innerspring

- **All-foam:** Excellent motion isolation, good pressure relief, tends to sleep warmer. Best for light sleepers easily disturbed by a partner's movement.
- **Hybrid (foam + coils):** Combines pressure relief with bounce and airflow. The best all-around option for most people.
- **Innerspring:** Traditional bounce feel, good airflow, strong edge support. Best for those who dislike the "sinking" feeling of foam.

### Step 3: Use the trial period — it exists for a reason

Every mattress on this list offers at least a 100-night trial with free returns. The reason: your body genuinely needs 2–4 weeks to adjust to a new sleep surface. Don't judge a mattress in the first week.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should a mattress last?**
Quality mattresses last 7–10 years. Memory foam typically degrades around 8 years; quality hybrids and innersprings can last 10+. If you're waking up stiff or seeing visible sagging, it's time for a replacement.

**Is it worth spending more than $1,000 on a mattress?**
Yes, if you have chronic back pain, sleep hot, or share your bed with a partner who moves frequently. The Casper Wave Hybrid and Purple Plus solve specific problems that budget mattresses cannot. If you sleep fine on your current mattress and are just upgrading, the Nectar or DreamCloud deliver excellent quality at lower prices.

**When is the best time to buy a mattress?**
Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, Labor Day, and Black Friday/Cyber Monday. Most bed-in-a-box brands run 30–50% off during these windows. Avoid buying at full price — sales are the norm, not the exception.

**Can I put a mattress-in-a-box on a platform bed without a box spring?**
Yes. All mattresses on this list are designed for platform beds, adjustable bases, or slatted frames. A box spring is not required and can actually void the warranty on some models.
