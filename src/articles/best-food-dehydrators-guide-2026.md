## Horizontal vs Vertical Airflow Explained

The biggest quality difference in food dehydrators is airflow direction — it determines how evenly your food dries.

**Horizontal (rear-mounted fan):** Fan pushes air from back to front across all trays simultaneously. Every tray gets the same airflow and temperature. No tray rotation needed. Used in Excalibur models and most professional-grade dehydrators. Best for jerky, fruit leather, and large batches.

**Vertical (bottom or top fan):** Fan pushes air up or down through the tray stack. Bottom trays get more airflow than top trays (or vice versa). Requires rotating trays every few hours for even drying. Lower cost. Common in NESCO, Presto, and budget models. Fine for casual use; inconvenient for overnight 12-hour drying runs.

## Temperature Guide by Food Type

| Food | Temperature | Time |
|------|------------|------|
| Beef jerky | 160°F (USDA safe) | 4–8 hours |
| Fruit slices | 135°F | 6–12 hours |
| Vegetables | 125°F | 6–10 hours |
| Herbs | 95–115°F | 1–4 hours |
| Fruit leather | 135°F | 6–8 hours |
| Fish | 145°F (USDA safe) | 8–12 hours |

**Food safety note:** USDA recommends pre-heating meat to 160°F (165°F for poultry) before dehydrating. Many dehydrators reach this temperature; verify your model's max temp before making jerky.

## What to Look for in a Food Dehydrator

**Airflow direction** is the most important quality factor. Horizontal (rear fan) = no rotation needed. Vertical (bottom/top fan) = rotate every few hours.

**Temperature range:** Minimum 160°F (165°F for poultry) for USDA-safe meat jerky. Models that max at 155°F should not be used for raw meat without pre-cooking.

**Tray count:** 5–6 trays for occasional use; 9 trays for batch processing. Expandable models offer flexibility.

**Timer:** Valuable for overnight drying runs — set it and wake up to finished product.

**Tray material:** Stainless steel trays resist odor absorption and are more durable than plastic mesh. Mesh/screen trays are fine for most foods.
