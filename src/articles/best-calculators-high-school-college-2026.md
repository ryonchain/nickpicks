## FAQ

**Is the TI-84 still worth buying in 2026?**
Yes, if your school or course specifies it. The TI-84 Plus CE remains the most common required graphing calculator in US education. If your courses don't specify TI, the Casio fx-9750GIII delivers equivalent features at half the cost.

**What's the difference between a scientific and graphing calculator?**
Scientific calculators handle algebraic, trigonometric, logarithmic, and statistical functions but can't plot graphs. Graphing calculators add the ability to graph functions, which is required for calculus, statistics, and many standardized tests.

**Can I use a calculator app on my phone instead?**
Most exams and standardized tests (SAT, AP, finals) prohibit phone use. Invest in a physical calculator for test-taking. Phone apps work fine for homework — Wolfram Alpha and Desmos are excellent free tools for learning.
