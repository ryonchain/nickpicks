### 4. Sennheiser HD 600 — Best Open-Back Headphones for Mixing

**Price: ~$300**

Open-back headphones have drivers that vent to the outside — air pressure equalizes, removing the resonance that makes closed-back headphones sound slightly boomy or compressed. The HD 600 is the reference standard for open-back monitoring: neutral frequency response, massive soundstage, and accurate stereo imaging that reveals mix placement problems closed-back headphones hide.

**Open-back advantages:**
- **No resonance buildup** from sealed ear cups — more accurate spatial representation
- Wide, natural soundstage — easier to hear where instruments sit in the stereo field
- Less listening fatigue over long mixing sessions
- Neutral tonality: HD 600 is considered the closest thing to a "perfectly flat" headphone

**Critical limitation:** Open-back headphones provide no isolation. They leak sound freely in both directions — you can hear your environment, people can hear what you're playing. Completely unusable for tracking (bleed into microphone). Dedicated home studio use only.

**Best for:** Engineers who mix in a dedicated studio room and want the most accurate stereo image possible.

## Open-Back vs. Closed-Back

| | Open-Back | Closed-Back |
|---|---|---|
| **Sound character** | Spacious, natural, accurate | More bass, slightly compressed |
| **Isolation** | None — ambient noise bleeds in/out | 24–35 dB isolation |
| **Mixing accuracy** | Better stereo image | Less accurate soundstage |
| **Tracking use** | No (bleeds into microphone) | Yes |
| **Best for** | Final mix reference, home studio | Tracking, travel, home practice |
| **Examples** | Sennheiser HD 600, HD 560S | ATH-M50x, DT 770, MDR-7506 |

**Practical recommendation:** Most home producers need closed-back first. If you have studio monitors for primary mixing, use closed-backs as a secondary check. Open-backs become valuable when you mix primarily on headphones.

## Impedance Guide

Headphones are rated in ohms (Ω). Higher impedance headphones need more voltage to reach the same volume.

- **32Ω:** Designed for phones and consumer devices. Works with anything.
- **80Ω:** Designed for audio interfaces and professional gear. May be quiet from a phone.
- **150Ω / 250Ω / 600Ω:** Requires a dedicated headphone amplifier. Produces cleaner audio from appropriate gear.

For audio interface monitoring (Focusrite Scarlett, PreSonus, etc.): **80Ω** is the ideal impedance.
