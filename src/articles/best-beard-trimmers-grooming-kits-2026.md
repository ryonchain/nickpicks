## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between a beard trimmer and a body groomer?**
Beard trimmers use a reciprocating blade designed for facial hair, with attachments measured in millimeters for precise length control. Body groomers typically have wider, duller blades to prevent skin nicking on larger areas. Some trimmers (like the Philips OneBlade) are rated for both face and body use.

**How often do trimmer blades need to be replaced?**
Most trimmer blades are designed to last 2–4 years with regular use and proper cleaning. Signs they need replacement: pulling instead of cutting cleanly, requiring multiple passes over the same area, visible blade chipping. Philips OneBlade replacement blades are $12–15 each; Braun and Wahl replacements vary by model.

**Can you use a beard trimmer on wet hair in the shower?**
Only if the trimmer is rated for wet use. The Philips OneBlade 360 and Panasonic ER-GB80-S are fully waterproof (IPX7) and designed for wet or dry use. Many trimmers are splash-resistant (IPX5) but shouldn't be fully submerged. Check the rating before using any trimmer in the shower.

**What length guard should I start with for a new beard style?**
Start longer than you think you need — you can always trim shorter, but you can't put hair back. For stubble (1–3mm): use a bare blade or 1–2mm guard. For short beard (5–10mm): start with a 10mm guard and work down. For a full beard trim: start at your current length and go one guard shorter to see the effect before committing.

**Do cordless trimmers have enough power for thick beards?**
Modern lithium-ion cordless trimmers deliver consistent power throughout the battery cycle, unlike older NiCad batteries that weakened near the end of charge. The Panasonic ER-GB80-S is specifically rated for thick beards at full cordless power. If you have a dense, coarse beard, look for motors rated 7,200+ RPM and wide blade gaps.
