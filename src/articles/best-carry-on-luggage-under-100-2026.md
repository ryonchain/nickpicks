## Hardside vs Softside: Which Should You Choose?

**Choose hardside if you:**
- Pack fragile items (electronics, glass, souvenirs)
- Want a TSA lock integrated into the shell
- Prefer low-maintenance cleaning (wipe with damp cloth)
- Travel frequently through rough handling environments

**Choose softside if you:**
- Prioritize weight savings (every lb counts on weigh-in flights)
- Need exterior pockets for documents and small items
- Travel to destinations where luggage may get compressed (tight storage)
- Prefer flexibility if carry-on space is tight

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size carry-on fits in most airline overhead bins?**
Most domestic US airlines allow carry-ons up to 22 x 14 x 9 inches (including wheels and handles). International airlines can be stricter — budget carriers like Spirit and Frontier impose smaller limits. Always check your specific airline's current policy before flying.

**Is hardside or softside luggage better for carry-on?**
Hardside is better for protecting fragile items and resists exterior moisture, but cannot compress to fit tight overhead bins. Softside expands slightly and often has exterior pockets for quick access. For most travelers, hardside polycarbonate is the right choice under $100 for its durability and scratch resistance.

**Are spinner wheels worth it on a carry-on?**
Yes — four 360-degree spinner wheels make navigating airports dramatically easier, especially in busy terminals. The tradeoff is a slightly heavier suitcase (typically 0.3–0.5 lbs more than two-wheel rollaboards). Under $100, look for double spinner wheels that share the load across two wheels per corner for longer lifespan.

**Can I bring a personal item in addition to a carry-on?**
Most major airlines allow one carry-on bag plus one personal item (under-seat bag, backpack, or purse) at no additional charge. Budget airlines typically charge for carry-ons above the personal item allowance. A compact backpack or tote paired with a carry-on maximizes your luggage allowance.
