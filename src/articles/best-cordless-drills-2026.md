## Buying Guide: Choosing Your First (or Next) Cordless Drill

**Voltage:** 12V for light work (trim, cabinetry, tight spaces). 18V/20V for general all-around use. More voltage = more torque capacity, but also more weight. 20V MAX (DeWalt) and 18V (Milwaukee/Ryobi/Makita) are interchangeable in practice — the voltage difference is marketing, not functional.

**Brushless vs. brushed:** Brushless motors are 30% more efficient, run cooler, last longer, and make the drill more expensive by ~$20–$40. Always choose brushless if staying in a tool family long-term.

**Battery platform:** This decision is more important than the drill itself. Once you own 3+ tools on a platform, switching is expensive. Ryobi ONE+ is best for homeowners (cheapest ecosystem). Milwaukee M18 is best for tradespeople. DeWalt 20V MAX and Makita 18V LXT are excellent for both.

**Hammer vs. drill:** Standard drills bore holes in wood, metal, and drywall. If you're drilling into brick, concrete, or masonry, you need a hammer drill (the chuck hammers as it rotates). Confirm you're buying a hammer drill if masonry work is in your plans.

**Clutch settings:** More clutch settings = finer control when driving screws. 15–24 settings is standard for a quality drill. Fewer settings make it harder to drive small screws without stripping.

## FAQ

**DeWalt vs. Milwaukee — which is better?**
For professional daily use, Milwaukee M18 FUEL wins on torque and ecosystem depth. For homeowners and light commercial use, DeWalt 20V MAX wins on value, accessory availability, and two batteries included in most kits.

**What is the difference between a drill and a drill/driver?**
A drill/driver has a clutch mechanism for driving screws without stripping them — it disengages when resistance reaches the set level. Most "cordless drills" sold today are drill/drivers. A pure drill (without clutch) is less common and suited for metalwork where slipping isn't desirable.

**Do I need 18V or is 12V enough?**
For most homeowners doing furniture assembly, picture hanging, and basic repairs: 12V is sufficient and significantly lighter. For drilling into lumber, driving deck screws, or any sustained DIY work: 18V/20V is the right call.

**Can I use DeWalt 20V batteries on older 18V DeWalt tools?**
No — 20V MAX and 18V are different battery systems. DeWalt discontinued the 18V platform. If you have old 18V tools, you'll need an adapter or upgrade to 20V MAX tools.

**How long do cordless drill batteries last?**
Quality lithium-ion batteries last 3–5 years with regular use before capacity drops noticeably. Storing batteries at partial charge (50%) and cool temperatures extends life. Never store at full discharge or in a hot car.
