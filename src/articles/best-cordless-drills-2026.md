## Cordless Drill Buying Guide

**Battery voltage and compatibility:** 18V (Milwaukee M18, Makita LXT) and 20V MAX (DeWalt) are equivalent in real-world performance — "20V MAX" is peak voltage, not sustained. 12V (Bosch) is genuinely lower power. Once you own one platform's batteries, stick with that ecosystem for future tool purchases.

**Brushless vs. brushed motors:** Brushless motors use electronic commutation instead of physical carbon brushes. They're more efficient (20–35% longer runtime per charge), run cooler, last longer, and require no brush replacement. The price premium over brushed is typically $20–40 and worth it for any drill used regularly.

**Torque output:** More torque = more capability. 400–600 UWO covers homeowner tasks (drywall, wood studs, decking). 800–1,200 in-lbs covers professional contractor applications (framing, metal). Match torque to your typical applications.

**Battery capacity:** Ah (amp-hour) rating determines runtime. 2.0Ah for occasional use; 3.0–4.0Ah for regular weekend projects; 5.0Ah for full professional daily use. Higher Ah batteries also provide better sustained power for demanding applications.

**Clutch settings:** More clutch settings = more precise screw depth control. For drywall and cabinetry, you need low clutch settings to avoid crushing material. Professional drills typically offer 20+ settings; consumer drills offer 10–15.
