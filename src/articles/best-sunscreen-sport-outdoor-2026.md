## SPF Guide

| SPF | UVB Protection | Reapplication |
|-----|---------------|--------------|
| SPF 30 | 97% UVB blocked | Every 2 hours |
| SPF 50 | 98% UVB blocked | Every 2 hours |
| SPF 100 | 99% UVB blocked | Every 2 hours |

**The real difference:** SPF 30 vs. 100 is marginal on paper. What matters: how much you apply (most people use 25–50% of the recommended amount) and whether you reapply. SPF 50 is the sweet spot for sport use.

**Broad spectrum:** Always look for "broad spectrum" on the label — this means UVA and UVB protection. SPF alone only measures UVB.

## Application Guide for Sport Use

- **Amount:** 1 oz (about a shot glass) for full body. Most people apply 25–50% of this — the main reason sunscreen underperforms.
- **Face:** 1/4 teaspoon for just the face
- **Timing:** Apply 15–30 minutes before sun exposure (mineral sunscreens work immediately; chemical filters need 15–20 min to activate)
- **Reapplication:** Every 80 minutes maximum, and immediately after toweling off or heavy sweating
- **Don't miss:** Ears, back of neck, tops of feet, bald spots
