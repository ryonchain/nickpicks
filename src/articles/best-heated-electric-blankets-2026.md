## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Heated Electric Blanket

**Heat settings.** More settings give finer warmth control. 10-setting models let you find the precise temperature between "cool" and "too hot." Budget models with 3–5 settings work fine but may leave you choosing between slightly too warm and slightly too cool.

**Auto-shutoff duration.** For safety, choose a blanket with at least a 10-hour auto-shutoff for overnight use. 3-hour shutoffs are fine for couch throws but require manual reset for all-night sleeping. Never use an electric blanket with a disabled or missing auto-shutoff.

**Wire technology.** ThermoFine and similar auto-regulating wire systems adjust heat output as external temperature changes — maintaining consistent warmth without manual adjustment. Non-auto-regulating blankets output fixed heat regardless of room temperature.

**Fabric.** Plush and Royalmink fabrics are softer but show more lint. Knit fabrics breathe better and are more durable. For bed use, match the blanket's softness to your sheet fabric preferences.

**Washability.** All blankets on this list are machine washable — this is now an expected standard. Check that your specific blanket is also dryer safe; some require air drying to protect heating wires.

**UL listing.** Always purchase UL-listed electric blankets. All picks on this list are UL certified. Avoid generic blankets without safety certifications.

## FAQ

**Is it safe to sleep with an electric blanket on all night?**
Yes, with a blanket that has an auto-shutoff of 8+ hours. All blankets on this list are designed for overnight use. The auto-shutoff is a redundant safety feature — modern electric blankets don't pose fire risk under normal use with intact wires.

**Can electric blankets be washed?**
Yes — all picks on this list are machine washable. Always detach the controller before washing. Use cold water, gentle cycle, and low-heat drying. Never dry clean or use the iron on an electric blanket.

**How long do heated electric blankets last?**
5–10 years with proper care. Never fold an electric blanket with sharp creases (roll to store instead, as repeated sharp folds can damage heating wires). Signs of wire damage include hot spots, cold spots, or burning smell — replace immediately.

**Are heated blankets safe for children?**
Not for infants or toddlers who can't control their environment. For older children (5+), use the lowest heat settings and ensure the child can remove or turn off the blanket independently. Consult your pediatrician for specific guidance.

**Can I use an electric blanket with an adjustable bed?**
Yes, for flex flat or minor incline. Avoid using heated blankets with adjustable beds that fold at extreme angles — repeated sharp bending can damage heating wires over time.
