## Choosing the Right Size

**4x4 beds** are the most popular starting size — reachable from all sides without stepping in (avoiding soil compaction), big enough to mix crops, small enough to fill with quality soil for under $50.

**4x8 beds** are the next step for serious vegetable gardens. One 4x8 bed with 12-inch depth holds 32 cubic feet of soil — enough for 8-12 tomato/pepper plants or a full salad garden.

**Soil cost planning:** A standard 4x8 x 12-inch bed requires ~32 cubic feet of soil. At $8-10 per cubic foot for quality garden mix, that's $250-320 in soil. Factor this into your budget — the soil often costs more than the bed itself.

## What to Plant in Raised Beds

**Highest ROI crops for beginners:**
- Tomatoes (1 plant per square foot, $4 plant = $30-80 in tomatoes)
- Lettuce / salad greens (cut-and-come-again = continuous harvest)
- Herbs (basil, parsley, cilantro — expensive to buy, cheap to grow)
- Peppers (prolific producer, long season)
- Zucchini (very high yield, easy to grow)

**Avoid in shallow beds (under 12 inches):**
- Carrots, parsnips, beets (need 12-18 inches)
- Potatoes (need 12-18 inches to hill properly)
- Corn (wind-pollinates; needs large block planting)
