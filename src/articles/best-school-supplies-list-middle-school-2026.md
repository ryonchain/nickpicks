## The Non-Negotiable Core Supplies

These items you need before day one, no exceptions.

### Notebooks and Binders

Middle school students typically have 4-7 classes, each with separate materials. The question is: one big binder vs. separate notebooks.

**Option 1: 1.5" or 2" 3-ring binder (one per subject) — $2-4 each**
Most flexible, works with loose-leaf paper and printed handouts. Buy one per subject.

**Option 2: Mead Five Star 1-Subject Spiral Notebooks — $3 each**
Faster access, sturdier covers. Works for classes with less handout volume.

**Recommended setup:** One 2" binder for each core class (math, English, science, social studies) plus spiral notebooks for electives.

3M Post-it Notes are the gold standard. Buy the 3x3 inch size in a value pack (~$10 for 10 pads). Middle schoolers use these for study notes, bookmarks, and reminders.

## What to Skip

**Supplies teachers say you need but you probably don't:**

- **Full sets of colored pencils:** One basic 8-pack covers middle school art use. Skip the 100-count set.
- **Poster board (multiple sheets):** Usually available in class or at school. Buy if needed, not preemptively.
- **Fancy pencil sharpeners:** If you're using mechanical pencils, you don't need one.
- **Index cards (huge packs):** Buy a 100-count pack, not 500. You'll use fewer than you think.
- **Ruler set (multiple sizes):** One 12-inch ruler handles all middle school needs.
