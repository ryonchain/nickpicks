## Types of Bidet: Which Is Right for You?

**Cold-water attachment (~$35–$90):** Clamps under your existing seat, connects to the toilet water supply. Installation: 15 minutes. No electricity, no warm water. Best for: trying bidets without commitment, tight budgets, rental situations.

**Electric bidet seat (~$200–$850):** Replaces your existing toilet seat. Requires an electrical outlet near the toilet (GFCI). Includes heated seat, warm water wash (tankless or tank), air dryer, remote. Best for: daily serious use, homes you own.

**Standalone bidet:** A separate fixture next to the toilet. Requires plumbing installation. Common in Europe but rare in American bathrooms due to space constraints.

## Installation: What You Need to Know

**Cold water attachment:** All you need is a wrench and 15 minutes. The T-valve connects between the fill valve and toilet water supply.

**Electric bidet seat:** You need a GFCI electrical outlet within 4 feet of the toilet. Most bathrooms in homes built after 1970 have one. If not, an electrician can add one for $150–$250. The seat itself replaces your existing toilet seat — no plumber needed.

**Warm water without electricity:** You need to connect to the hot water supply under your bathroom sink. Warm-water non-electric attachments like the Tushy Spa connect via a supply hose. Requires your sink to be near the toilet.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will a bidet work with my toilet?**
Most bidet attachments and seats fit standard round (16.5" bowl length) and elongated (18.5" bowl length) toilets. The main exceptions: French curve toilets (angled bowl), some one-piece toilets with limited clearance at the back, and pressure-assist toilets.

**Do bidets use a lot of water?**
A typical bidet wash uses about 0.5–0.8 liters of water — far less than the 37 gallons per roll of toilet paper production requires. They're an environmental upgrade, not a burden.

**Does a bidet require a plumber?**
No — cold water attachments and most electric bidet seats are DIY installs. You only need a plumber if you need a new electrical outlet or want to install a standalone bidet fixture.
