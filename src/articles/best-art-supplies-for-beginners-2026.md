## Drawing & Sketching

### 1. Staedtler Mars Lumograph Pencil Set — Best Drawing Pencil Set

12-piece set from 6H (hard/light) to 6B (soft/dark), smooth German graphite, and break-resistant leads. Staedtler is the standard recommendation for technical drawing, figure drawing, and general sketching. Used by design students and professional illustrators alike.

**Price:** ~$12  
**Best for:** Anyone starting drawing, sketching, portraiture, architecture

---

### 2. Canson XL Sketch Pad 9x12 — Best Sketch Pad

100 sheets, 60 lb weight, acid-free, and fine-tooth surface that accepts pencil, charcoal, and light marker. The most consistently recommended sketch pad at any art school or studio. Don't overthink paper at the beginning — Canson XL is reliable and affordable.

**Price:** ~$10  
**Best for:** Daily drawing practice, figure studies, thumbnail sketches

## Watercolor

### 3. Winsor & Newton Cotman 45-Pan Set — Best Watercolor Starter Set

The most recommended beginner watercolor set in the world, and for good reason: real pigment, real watercolor behavior, and enough colors to learn color mixing without buying individual tubes. Students and hobbyists use Cotman for years before upgrading.

**Price:** ~$45  
**Best for:** Watercolor beginners, anyone testing the medium

---

### 4. Strathmore 400 Watercolor Pad 140 lb — Best Watercolor Paper

Cold-press, 140 lb, acid-free. The difference between sketch paper and proper watercolor paper is enormous — Strathmore 400 handles wet washes without buckling and accepts multiple layers without tearing. A non-negotiable investment if you're doing watercolor.

**Price:** ~$18  
**Best for:** Watercolor practice, all skill levels

---

## Acrylic Painting

### 5. Arteza Acrylic Paint Set 60 Colors — Best Acrylic Set

60 colors in 22ml tubes, heavy-body consistency, smooth blending, and vibrant pigment for the price. Arteza's acrylics dry true to color and don't separate in the tube. Significantly better than drugstore alternatives while remaining accessible to beginners.

**Price:** ~$40  
**Best for:** Acrylic painting beginners, canvas painters, mixed-media artists

---

### 6. Princeton Select Artiste Mixed Media Brush Set — Best Brush Set

8 brushes in rounds, flats, and filberts — covers every basic brush stroke needed for acrylic, watercolor, and gouache. Princeton Select is recommended consistently in art schools because the synthetic hair is durable, springy, and doesn't shed. Works across all water-based media.

**Price:** ~$25  
**Best for:** Anyone starting painting (any water-based medium)

## Colored Pencils

### 7. Crayola Signature Premium Colored Pencils 50-pack — Best Beginner Colored Pencils

Pre-sharpened, vibrant pigment, and smooth laydown for the price. Crayola Signature (not regular Crayola) is a genuine quality step up — suitable for sketchbook coloring, zentangle, and illustration. The best colored pencils under $20.

**Price:** ~$15  
**Best for:** Beginners, adult coloring book users, casual illustration

---

### 8. Prismacolor Premier 48-pack — Best Colored Pencils (Upgrade)

Wax-based, soft lead that blends, layers, and burnishes. Prismacolor Premier is the industry standard for colored pencil illustration — vibrant, richly pigmented, and capable of photorealistic rendering in skilled hands. The upgrade most colored pencil artists make after 3–6 months.

**Price:** ~$40  
**Best for:** Intermediate+ colored pencil artists, portrait and illustration work

## Markers

### 9. Tombow Dual Brush Pen Set 96-color — Best Markers for Beginners

Dual-tipped with a flexible brush tip and a fine bullet tip. Water-based (blendable), acid-free, and available in 96 colors. Tombow markers are used by hand-lettering artists, illustrators, and journalers — highly versatile, excellent build quality, and long-lasting.

**Price:** ~$80 for 96 colors  
**Best for:** Hand lettering, illustration, journaling, color studies

---

### 10. Copic Sketch Marker 72-piece Set — Best Illustration Markers (Advanced Beginner)

Refillable, alcohol-based markers with dual nibs (brush + chisel). Industry-standard for manga, fashion illustration, and professional sketching. Expensive upfront but refillable (markers last years, refills cost ~$3–4). The highest-quality marker available.

**Price:** ~$350  
**Best for:** Serious illustrators, manga artists, fashion designers, advanced beginners
