The Hoya Pro1 Digital uses a slim profile that prevents vignetting on wide-angle lenses. The low-profile ring fits in tight situations where standard-profile CPLs can't thread on.

## Do I Actually Need These Filters?

### Filters Worth Buying

- **UV protector:** Yes — if the lens is worth protecting, a quality UV filter is cheap insurance
- **Circular polarizer:** Yes — the only effect that can't be replicated in Lightroom or Photoshop
- **ND filter:** For video or long exposures — essential for maintaining exposure control in daylight

### Filters You Don't Need

- **Skylight/1A filters:** Marginal effect, now replaced by white balance control
- **Star/Cross-screen filters:** Niche effect, replicable in post
- **Warming/cooling filters:** White balance and Lightroom handle this better

---

## Buying Guide: Thread Size and Compatibility

Every lens has a thread size printed on the barrel or cap (e.g., "Ø77" or "77mm"). Buy filters matching your lens thread diameter exactly. For a multi-lens kit, step-up rings allow one large filter to fit multiple smaller lenses.
