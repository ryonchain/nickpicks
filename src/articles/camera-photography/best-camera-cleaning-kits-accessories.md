## Cleaning Technique: What Not to Do

**Never use:**
- Compressed air cans (propellant deposit risk, freezing risk on sensors)
- Paper towels or tissues (scratch coatings with microscopic fibers)
- Breath (moisture damage risk, salt deposits)
- Rubbing alcohol (strips anti-reflection coatings)
- Cotton swabs (leave fibers, unsafe pressure for sensors)

**Always do:**
1. Start with air blower on lens and sensor
2. If dust remains, use lens cleaning fluid + microfiber (lenses) or sensor swab (sensors)
3. Store cleaning tools in a clean case to prevent them from becoming contamination sources
