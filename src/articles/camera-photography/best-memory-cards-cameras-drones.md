# Best Memory Cards for Cameras & Drones (2026)

The memory card is the one component that, if too slow, limits everything else. Shoot RAW on a card that can't keep up and you're waiting for the buffer to clear. Shoot 4K video with a V10 card and it stops mid-clip. The right card for your camera and workflow is a small purchase with a big impact.

## Best SD Cards for Cameras

### 1. SanDisk Extreme Pro SDXC — Best Overall SD Card

**Price:** ~$20–$100 | [Check price on Amazon]([AMAZON_LINK])

The SanDisk Extreme Pro is the most widely used SD card among professional photographers for good reason. Read speeds of 200MB/s and V30 (minimum 30MB/s sustained write) make it reliable for 4K video and burst shooting. Available from 64GB to 1TB.

The UHS-I interface (not UHS-II) means it won't saturate the fastest camera slots, but most cameras don't have UHS-II slots anyway. For cameras without CFexpress slots, the Extreme Pro is the benchmark card.

**Pros:**
- 200MB/s read speeds — fast file transfers to computer
- V30 rated — reliable for 4K video recording
- Wide availability in 64GB–1TB capacities
- Temperature and X-ray proof rated
- Lifetime limited warranty

**Cons:**
- UHS-I interface tops out around 200MB/s — UHS-II cameras need UHS-II cards
- Write speed of 90MB/s is not the highest available
- Price per GB is higher than off-brand alternatives

**Best for:** Mirrorless and DSLR shooters needing reliable 4K video and burst shooting capability.

### 2. Sony TOUGH-M SD Card — Most Durable SD Card

**Price:** ~$40–$120 | [Check price on Amazon]([AMAZON_LINK])

Sony's TOUGH-M is the most physically rugged SD card available. Rated for 18,000 bend cycles, waterproof to 5 meters, dustproof, and tested for shockproof drops to 5 meters. The one-piece molded design eliminates the write-protect switch that breaks on standard SD cards in rugged conditions.

Write-protect switch breakage is the most common SD card failure mode. Sony TOUGH's switchless design eliminates this failure point entirely.

**Pros:**
- 18,000 bend-cycle rating — survives being bent and flexed
- No write-protect switch — eliminates the most common breakage point
- V60 video speed class for higher video bitrate cameras
- Waterproof, dustproof, shockproof
- 277MB/s read speed (with UHS-II compatible reader)

**Cons:**
- V60 rating means higher per-unit cost than V30 cards
- UHS-II speed requires UHS-II card reader to achieve full speed
- More expensive than SanDisk equivalent

**Best for:** Wildlife photographers, adventure photographers, anyone shooting in rain, dust, sand, or drop-prone environments.

## Best CFexpress Cards

### 4. Sony CFexpress Type A — Best for Sony Alpha/FX Cameras

**Price:** ~$130–$400 | [Check price on Amazon]([AMAZON_LINK])

CFexpress Type A is Sony's proprietary high-speed format used in the Sony A7 IV, A7S III, A1, FX3, FX6, and FX30. These cameras can use both SD and CFexpress Type A in the same slot, but CFexpress A unlocks full RAW video and burst buffer capability. Read speeds of 800MB/s and write speeds of 700MB/s allow sustained high-bitrate RAW video recording that standard SD cannot sustain.

**Pros:**
- 800MB/s read / 700MB/s write — handles 4K RAW and 8K recording
- Compatible with Sony CFexpress A + SD combo slots
- Required for sustained Sony A1 burst and RAW video
- Sony-manufactured — reliability and consistency

**Cons:**
- Only compatible with specific Sony cameras (CFexpress A is Sony's format)
- Significantly more expensive than SD
- Requires CFexpress Type A card reader for fast computer transfers

## Buying Guide

### Speed Classes Explained

| Class | Minimum Write Speed | Best For |
|-------|---------------------|----------|
| V10 | 10MB/s | 1080p video, basic shooting |
| V30 | 30MB/s | 4K video, burst shooting |
| V60 | 60MB/s | High-bitrate 4K, some 6K cameras |
| V90 | 90MB/s | 6K/8K RAW video |

### Check Your Camera's Recommended Cards

Every major camera manufacturer publishes a "recommended memory card" list. Check your specific camera model's list before buying — some cameras have buffer issues with cards below specific thresholds even if the card is technically rated high enough.

### How Much Capacity to Buy

| Shooting Style | Minimum Capacity |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Casual photographer (JPEG) | 64GB |
| RAW shooter, day trips | 128GB |
| Video (4K, daily) | 256GB |
| Professional events | 2x 256GB cards |
