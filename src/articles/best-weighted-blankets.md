# Best Weighted Blankets for Anxiety and Sleep: Top Picks for 2026

A weighted blanket applies gentle, even pressure across your body — a technique called deep pressure stimulation — that signals your nervous system to shift from fight-or-flight mode into rest-and-digest. The science is real: multiple clinical studies have found that weighted blankets reduce anxiety, improve sleep onset, and increase nighttime melatonin production. For people with anxiety, insomnia, sensory processing differences, or ADHD, they can be genuinely life-changing.

The right weight matters: the general rule is 10% of your body weight, plus or minus a pound. Too light and you lose the pressure effect; too heavy and it feels restrictive. This guide covers the five best weighted blankets on Amazon — from premium gravity-defying options to budget-friendly picks — so you can find the one that actually helps you sleep.

## How to Choose the Right Weighted Blanket

**Weight:** Aim for 10% of your body weight. A 150 lb person does well with a 15 lb blanket; a 200 lb person with a 20 lb blanket. Children should use blankets supervised by a parent and sized to their weight.

**Fill:** Glass microbeads are quieter and smoother than plastic poly pellets. Organic cotton construction (Bearaby) eliminates beads entirely for a different tactile experience.

**Temperature:** If you sleep warm, prioritize breathable fabrics or Bearaby's open-knit design. If you sleep cold, a standard bead-filled blanket with a fleece cover works well.

**Washing:** Blankets over 15 lb typically need a commercial washer. Factor this into your decision if home laundry is important to you.

**Try before committing:** Start with a mid-weight option (15 lb for most adults). You can always go heavier if you want more pressure — going too heavy makes it hard to reposition during the night.
