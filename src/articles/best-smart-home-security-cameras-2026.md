## What to Evaluate in a Smart Home Camera

Security cameras vary widely in capability. Here's what matters:

- **Resolution:** 1080p is the functional minimum for identifying faces at 10–15 feet. 2K (1440p) and 4K provide license plate and face recognition at 30+ feet. Higher resolution increases storage requirements.
- **Night vision:** IR night vision (infrared) produces black-and-white footage. Color night vision uses a starlight sensor or built-in spotlight to show color in near-darkness — significantly more useful for identification. IR range (20–100 feet) varies widely.
- **Storage:** Local SD card storage avoids monthly fees but requires physical recovery if the camera is stolen. Cloud storage is accessible anywhere but involves recurring costs. Many cameras support both.
- **Motion zones:** Adjustable zones let you exclude the sidewalk or a tree from triggering alerts. Without zones, every passing car triggers a notification. Polygon zone support (precise custom shapes) is better than grid-based zones.
- **Smart home integration:** Alexa and Google Home allow voice control and viewing on Echo Show or Nest Hub screens. Matter support means the camera can work across platforms without vendor lock-in.
- **Power source:** Wired cameras have no battery anxiety. Wire-free (battery-powered) cameras install anywhere but require recharging every 1–6 months depending on activity level.

## FAQ

**Do I need a subscription?**
Not for basic functionality with most cameras. Wyze, Eufy, and Reolink all offer free local storage. Subscriptions add cloud history, AI detection improvements, and cross-device sharing. Evaluate based on your specific use case.

**Is 4K necessary for home security?**
4K is useful for wide-angle cameras covering large driveways or yards — the extra resolution lets you crop and zoom into details. For close-range monitoring (doorbell, indoor), 1080p or 2K is sufficient.

**Can security cameras work across Alexa and Google?**
Most cameras (Reolink, Arlo, Eufy, Ring) support both Alexa and Google Home via their respective skills/actions. Matter-compatible cameras offer the broadest cross-platform compatibility without vendor lock-in.
