## What to Look for in a Cycling Computer

**GPS system:** Dual-band GPS (L1/L5) is the new standard on premium units. It provides better accuracy in challenging conditions (urban canyons, tree cover) and during fast cornering. For most recreational cyclists, standard GPS is sufficient.

**Power meter compatibility:** All modern cycling computers support ANT+ and Bluetooth power meters. If you train with power (watts), verify the head unit displays the metrics you need: 3-second power, normalized power, Training Stress Score (TSS), and Intensity Factor (IF).

**Navigation:** Turn-by-turn navigation with color maps is now available on mid-range units. Breadcrumb navigation (showing a route line without street maps) works fine for familiar routes. For exploring new areas, color maps are worth the price jump.

**Battery life:** Standard rides: 12-20 hours is standard and sufficient. Ultraendurance events: look for 24+ hours or solar charging (Garmin Edge 1040 Solar).

**Display readability:** Transflective MIP (Memory in Pixel) displays are the standard for outdoor cycling — readable in direct sunlight and low-power. AMOLED displays offer richer color and indoor readability but consume more power and can washout in bright sun.

**ANT+ sensors:** Most cycling speed/cadence sensors, power meters, and heart rate monitors use ANT+. All Garmin and Wahoo computers support ANT+/Bluetooth dual connectivity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a power meter to use a cycling computer?**
No. GPS, speed, cadence, and heart rate work without a power meter. A power meter is a separate sensor you add to your bike — many serious cyclists add one after buying the head unit. If you plan to train with power, confirm your chosen computer supports the power meter protocol you intend to use.

**Can I use my phone instead of a cycling computer?**
Phone GPS cycling apps (Strava, Garmin Connect) work but drain phone battery in 2–3 hours, aren't sunlight-readable, and require a handlebar mount that's vulnerable to drops. For casual rides, fine. For regular training, a dedicated cycling computer pays for itself in reliability and data quality.

**Garmin vs Wahoo: which should I choose?**
Choose Garmin if: you want deep training analytics, use Garmin Connect and Running/Triathlon ecosystem, or need long battery life. Choose Wahoo if: you prioritize setup simplicity, use TrainingPeaks for analytics, or want the cleanest navigation interface.
