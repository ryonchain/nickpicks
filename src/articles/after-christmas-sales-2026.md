The week after Christmas is one of the most underrated shopping windows of the year. Retailers clear excess holiday inventory fast — and what didn't sell at full price in December often gets marked down 40–70% starting December 26. Here's where to look and what to expect.

## When Do After-Christmas Sales Start?

For most retailers, after-Christmas clearance begins the moment Christmas Day ends — even during Christmas Day itself for online stores.

| Date | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| Dec 25 (Christmas Day) | Amazon and online retailers begin clearance immediately |
| Dec 26 | Target, Walmart, Best Buy open early with in-store clearance events |
| Dec 27–28 | Full clearance inventory online; in-store shelves reset |
| Dec 29–31 | Final year-end markdowns; "New Year" sale labeling begins |
| Jan 1–7 | "New Year" deals on fitness, health, organization products |
| Jan 8–15 | CES 2027 effect — 2026 electronics begin deeper clearance as new models announce |

### Appliances — Clearance on Floor Models

After Christmas, home improvement stores (Lowe's, Home Depot) clear display and floor models to make room for spring inventory.

**What to look for:**
- Floor model dishwashers, ranges, and refrigerators: 20–40% off retail
- Open-box washers/dryers: 15–30% off with delivery available
- Last year's refrigerator models as 2027 models are announced

**Pro tip:** Ask the appliance department for "manager special" floor models. These aren't always listed online — in-store conversation gets you a better deal than what's on the website.

## After-Christmas Shopping Tips

1. **Shop online on Christmas Day** — Amazon's clearance begins before stores open. The best selection goes online first.
2. **Check store apps before driving** — Target and Walmart apps show clearance by your local store. Save yourself a trip for a category with nothing left.
3. **Open-box electronics are low-risk at Best Buy** — "Certified" open-box comes with the same in-store warranty as new, and Best Buy inspects and repackages them. Worth considering over new for TVs, laptops, and tablets.
4. **Decor is the highest-ROI after-Christmas buy** — a $50 artificial Christmas tree bought at 75% off post-Christmas saves you $150 compared to buying it new next November.
5. **Stack coupons and cash-back on clearance** — Kohl's Cash, Target Circle, and Discover/Chase portal cash-back often apply on top of clearance prices. Check before checkout.
6. **Gift card arbitrage** — if you received gift cards, use them during clearance events. A $100 Target gift card spends at 2–3x value on clearance items vs. regular price.

## What's NOT Worth Buying After Christmas

- **Gift wrap and tissue paper** — you can't predict what you'll need next year; it's not worth the storage
- **Food and perishables** — Christmas candy and baked goods clearance isn't worth the shelf space
- **Dated technology** — after-Christmas "deals" on prior-generation electronics can be misleading; CES in early January often announces the next generation, making the "deal" actually overpriced
