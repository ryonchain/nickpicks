## How to Choose Noise-Cancelling Headphones for Studying

**ANC strength tiers for dorm use:**
- Sony XM5 / AirPods Max / Bose QC45: Cancel HVAC, bass through walls, conversation
- Anker Q45: Cancels most ambient noise — occasional bass breakthrough
- JBL Tune 660NC: Reduces noise substantially but doesn't fully block deep bass

**Over-ear vs on-ear:** Over-ear cups (Sony, Bose, Anker) create a seal that improves both passive noise isolation and ANC effectiveness. On-ear cups (JBL) are lighter but let more sound through and cause more ear fatigue over long sessions.

**Microphone quality for Zoom:** If you take a lot of online classes or have remote work, mic quality matters. Sony XM5 and Bose QC45 have the best mics here. Anker Q45 is adequate; JBL Tune 660NC struggles in noisy environments.

## FAQ

**Will noise-cancelling headphones block everything in a dorm?**
No. ANC is most effective on constant, low-frequency sounds (HVAC, traffic, bass music). It's less effective on sharp, sudden sounds like voices or door slams. Combined with passive isolation from over-ear cups, you'll get a significant reduction in distraction — but not total silence.

**Can I use ANC headphones for online classes?**
Yes — all picks above include a microphone for calls. For Zoom and online classes, Sony XM5 and Bose QC45 have the best mic quality. Use the headphone mic over your laptop's built-in mic for better call clarity.

**Is it safe to study with ANC headphones for long hours?**
ANC itself is safe — it doesn't damage hearing. Keep your listening volume below 75–80 dB (most phones have a volume warning). Using ANC to reduce ambient noise actually allows you to listen at lower volumes, which is healthier long-term.
