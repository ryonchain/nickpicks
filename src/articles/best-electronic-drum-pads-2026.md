## Volume Comparison

One of the main reasons people buy electronic drums is volume control. Here's the realistic noise output:

- **Rubber pads:** Loud impact noise on the pad surface, plus kick pedal thud through the floor. Still loud enough to disturb neighbors below you.
- **Mesh pads (Roland, mid-range):** Significantly quieter pad strike, but kick pedal remains an issue.
- **Noise isolation feet + mesh + riser mat:** The combination most apartment drummers use — reduces floor transmission significantly.
- **Practice pads (non-powered):** Silent except for stick impact on pad surface. Quietest option. No sound output.

If floor noise is the primary concern, look for drum riser platforms (Gear4music, Lemon Drum riser) — they reduce floor-transmitted sound from kick pedal by 60–80%.
