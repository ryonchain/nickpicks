The question is no longer whether to buy OLED, but which OLED to buy.

## #2 Brightest OLED: Samsung S95D QD-OLED

**Price (65"):** ~$2,200 | [Check price on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Samsung+S95D+QD-OLED+65+inch){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"}

Samsung's QD-OLED technology works differently from LG's WOLED. Instead of using white subpixels filtered through color layers, QD-OLED uses blue OLED emitters converted by quantum dot layers — the result is significantly higher brightness and purer color saturation. The S95D peaks at approximately 2,000 nits in a 10% window, making it the brightest OLED TV available in 2026.

In a bright living room, this matters enormously. Where LG's panels can look slightly washed out in direct sunlight, the S95D holds its blacks and colors with authority. The anti-reflective coating is also among the best on any TV, further improving real-world performance in lit rooms.

**Key specs:**
- Panel: 65" QD-OLED, 4K (3840×2160)
- Peak brightness: ~2,000 nits (10% window, HDR)
- HDMI 2.1: 4× ports
- Input lag: 1.5ms (4K/144Hz game mode)
- VRR: Yes (FreeSync Premium Pro, G-Sync Compatible)
- Tizen OS with Samsung Gaming Hub built-in
- HDR formats: HDR10, HDR10+, HLG (no Dolby Vision)

**Pros:**
- Best brightness of any OLED on the market
- Exceptional anti-reflective coating for bright-room viewing
- Samsung Gaming Hub for cloud gaming without a console
- Stunning color volume — DCI-P3 coverage exceeds 99%

**Cons:**
- No Dolby Vision — HDR10+ instead (fewer supported titles)
- Premium price over LG C4 for a narrower use-case advantage
- Tizen OS has more bloatware than webOS

**Best for:** Buyers with bright, window-filled living rooms where TV brightness is the limiting factor on picture quality.

## How to Choose the Right OLED TV

**Room brightness is the most important factor.** In a dark or dim room, the LG B4 and C4 both look extraordinary. In a bright room with windows, the Samsung S95D's extra brightness makes a real, visible difference.

**Gaming vs. movies changes the calculus.** The LG C4's four HDMI 2.1 ports and sub-2ms input lag make it the definitive gaming TV. The Sony A95L's processing and Acoustic Surface Audio make it the definitive movie TV.

**Budget:** The LG B4 at ~$1,000 for 65" is the most compelling value in consumer TVs today. If you can't justify the C4 premium, the B4 won't disappoint.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is OLED worth it over QLED in 2026?**
For most living rooms, yes. OLED delivers perfect blacks, infinite contrast, and wider viewing angles that QLED cannot match at any brightness level. The gap has narrowed — Samsung's QD-OLED and Hisense's Mini-LED panels are genuinely excellent — but OLED still wins for dark-room movie watching, gaming in any lighting, and overall image consistency.

**What size OLED TV should I buy?**
55 inches is the sweet spot for bedrooms and smaller living rooms. 65 inches is the most popular size for main living rooms with 8–12 feet of viewing distance. 77 inches and up makes sense if you sit more than 12 feet from the screen or want a cinema-like experience. OLED remains more expensive per inch than LCD at larger sizes.

**Do OLED TVs still burn in?**
Burn-in risk is real but low for typical TV viewing habits. Static HUD elements in video games, news tickers, and sports channel logos are the primary risk factors. LG, Samsung, and Sony all include pixel-refresher routines and automatic brightness limiters to reduce risk. For normal mixed-use viewing, burn-in is unlikely to occur within 5–7 years of typical use.

**Which OLED TV is best for gaming in 2026?**
The LG C4 OLED is the top gaming TV — it has four HDMI 2.1 ports, supports 4K/144Hz, VRR, ALLM, G-Sync, and FreeSync Premium Pro, and has an input lag of under 1.5ms. The Samsung S95D is a close second with better brightness for gaming in bright rooms.
