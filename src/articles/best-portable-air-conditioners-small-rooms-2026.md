## The BTU Confusion Explained

The portable AC industry has had a BTU labeling problem for years. Here's what to know:

**Old standard (pre-2017):** BTU ratings measured in optimal lab conditions, often 30–50% higher than real performance.

**New DOE standard (SACC — 2017+):** Measures actual cooling capacity in a real room, accounting for the heat the machine itself adds to the room.

**Rule of thumb:** An 8,000 BTU SACC unit (new rating) is roughly equivalent to an old-label "10,000–12,000 BTU" unit.

When comparing models: **always look for the SACC rating**, not the gross BTU.

---

## Single Hose vs. Dual Hose

**Single hose:** One hose exhausts hot air outside. This creates negative air pressure in your room, which draws warm outside air in through cracks and gaps.

**Dual hose:** One hose intakes fresh outside air; one exhausts hot air. Maintains room pressure equilibrium. More efficient.

**Verdict:** Dual hose models cool faster, maintain temperature better, and use less energy. Worth the $50–100 premium.
