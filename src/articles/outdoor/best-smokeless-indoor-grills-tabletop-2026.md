## Tips for Getting the Most from an Indoor Smokeless Grill

**Pat meat dry before grilling.** Surface moisture creates steam, which inhibits searing and increases smoke. A 60-second pat-down with paper towels makes a measurable difference.

**Preheat fully.** Indoor grills need 3–5 minutes to reach maximum temperature. Don't put food on a cold or warming grill — the difference in sear quality is dramatic.

**Manage fat content.** The more fat drips during cooking, the more smoke is generated. Trim excess fat from chicken thighs and steaks before grilling indoors.

**Use the ventilation.** Run your kitchen exhaust fan on high during cooking. Even the best smokeless grills produce some vapor that benefits from exhaust.
