## Beginner Charcoal Grilling Tips

**Always use a chimney starter.** Lighter fluid imparts chemical flavors and is harder to control. A chimney starter ($20–$30) gives you reliably lit coals in 15–20 minutes every time.

**Master two-zone cooking.** Put all your coals on one side. High heat on the coal side; indirect heat on the other. Sear over direct heat, finish over indirect — the most important technique in charcoal grilling.

**Vent management = temperature control.** Open vents let air in, raising temperature. Closing them starves the fire. Never close vents completely during a cook or you'll extinguish the coals.

**Wood chunks, not chips, for smoking.** Chunks (fist-sized pieces of hardwood) smolder for 45–60 minutes. Chips burn up in 5–10 minutes. For any cook longer than 20 minutes, use chunks.
