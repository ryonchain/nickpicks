## What Makes a Great BBQ Tool

**Tongs:** Look for scalloped or silicone-tipped heads that grip without piercing. 16–18 inches is the ideal length. Locking mechanism for storage is a practical bonus.

**Spatula:** Wide, thin head for easy sliding under food. An offset angle reduces wrist strain for long flipping sessions. A serrated edge helps cut burgers in half on the grill.

**Fork:** Use sparingly — piercing meat lets juices escape. Mostly useful for large roasts that need repositioning.

**Grill Brush:** The most important safety factor. Brass-bristle or stainless coil brushes are safer than wire brushes — lose bristles can stick in food and cause injury. Replace your brush every season.

**Basting Brush:** Silicone always. Cheap natural-bristle brushes absorb bacteria, melt at high temps, and shed into food.
