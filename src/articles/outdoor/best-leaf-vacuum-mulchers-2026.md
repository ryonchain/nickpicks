## Mulch vs. Bag: What to Do with the Mulched Leaves

Once shredded, mulched leaves are more valuable than you might think:

**Garden beds:** A 2-3 inch layer of shredded leaf mulch suppresses weeds, retains moisture, and breaks down over winter into organic matter that improves soil. Apply directly after fall cleanup.

**Compost bin:** Shredded leaves are the ideal "brown" carbon source in a compost pile — alternate with green material (grass clippings, food scraps) in a 3:1 ratio. Shredded leaves break down 4–5× faster than whole leaves.

**Lawn top-dressing:** Fine-mulched leaves can be spread thinly across the lawn (visible soil shows through the layer) and left to break down. This "leaf mulching" technique adds nutrients and improves soil over time.

**Yard waste disposal:** Even if you're bagging, the 10–16:1 volume reduction means dramatically fewer bags to carry and less yard waste cost.
