## What to Consider Before Buying

**Cooking area:** For 2–4 people, 300–400 sq in is sufficient. For parties of 6–10, look for 450+ sq in.

**BTU per square inch:** Aim for 80–100 BTUs per square inch of primary cooking area. Higher total BTUs don't help if the cooking surface is large.

**Grate material:** Cast iron holds heat best and delivers great sear marks. Stainless steel grates are easier to clean. Wire grates are the lowest tier — food sticks and they rust faster.

**Ignition reliability:** Push-button electronic ignition (battery-powered) is more reliable than piezo (press-spark) systems, especially in wet or cold conditions.

**Burner material:** Stainless steel burners last significantly longer than aluminized steel or cast iron burners. Worth paying extra for.
