## Types of Backyard Fire Pits

**Wood-burning fire pits** are the classic choice — real fire smell, crackling wood, and ambiance that gas can't fully replicate. Trade-offs: you need to source and store wood, tend the fire, and manage smoke (which typically blows toward wherever you're sitting).

**Smokeless wood-burning fire pits** (Solo Stove design) use a double-wall construction that preheats secondary air and injects it at the top of the fire, burning the smoke before it escapes. They eliminate 80–95% of smoke. Still requires wood, but far more comfortable for people sitting around the fire.

**Propane/natural gas fire pits** light with a valve twist, reach full heat in seconds, and turn off completely with no ember management. Clean and convenient — perfect for patios close to the house. Trade-off: the flame looks different from wood (no crackling, no smell), and propane tanks need periodic replacement/refill.

**Tabletop fire pits** are compact enough for small patios, apartments, or camping. Solo Stove Mesa and Tiki Brand tabletop models deliver a real wood fire in a form factor that fits on any outdoor table.

## Buyer's Guide

### Wood Fire Pit Safety Checklist

- Check local burn ordinances (many municipalities restrict wood burning on high-ozone or red-flag days)
- Keep fire pit at least 10 ft from structures, fences, and dry vegetation
- Always use a spark screen on wind-exposed sites
- Have water or a fire extinguisher accessible
- Never leave an active fire unattended

### Best Fuel for Wood Fire Pits

- **Hardwoods** (oak, hickory, maple, ash): Burn hot, produce less smoke, and last longer — ideal for smokeless fire pits
- **Softwoods** (pine, cedar, fir): Burn fast and fragrant but produce more smoke and sap deposits — avoid in Solo Stove-style smokeless pits
- **Kiln-dried wood**: Lower moisture content burns cleaner — the best choice for any fire pit

### Gas Fire Pit vs. Wood: At a Glance

| Factor | Wood Fire Pit | Gas Fire Pit |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Startup time | 10–15 min | 3 seconds |
| Maintenance | Ash cleanup, wood storage | Propane tank refills |
| Ambiance | Crackling, real wood smell | Clean flame, no smell |
| Heat output | High (variable) | Consistent (BTU rated) |
| Smoke | Yes (minimal with smokeless) | None |
| Deck safety | Spark screen required | CSA-certified models OK |
