# Best Pellet Grills & Smokers 2026: Traeger vs. Pit Boss vs. Camp Chef

In 2026, three brands dominate the market: Traeger (the inventor), Pit Boss (the value challenger), and Camp Chef (the innovation disruptor). Here's how they compare.

## Detailed Reviews

### 1. Traeger Pro 780 — Best Overall Pellet Grill

The Traeger Pro 780 is the best entry into serious pellet grilling. WiFire app integration lets you monitor and adjust temperature from your phone. The D2 Direct Drive controller holds temperature to within ±15°F — tight enough for competition-quality BBQ.

**Key specs:**
- Cooking area: 780 sq in (main) + 272 sq in (second shelf)
- Temperature range: 165°F–500°F
- Controller: D2 with WiFire app (iOS/Android)
- Hopper capacity: 18 lbs
- Construction: Powder-coated steel body, stainless grates

**What we love:** The WiFire integration is genuinely useful — you can monitor a 12-hour brisket from inside without opening the lid every hour. Traeger's pellet ecosystem is the most widely available (Home Depot, Costco, online) making resupply easy. The Pro 780 is the entry point where Traeger reliability and the D2 controller come together.

**Where it falls short:** No direct-flame searing. At $699, it's not cheap — but it's the most thoroughly proven pellet grill on the market for the price.

**Vs Pit Boss at this price:** Traeger wins on build quality, app quality, and long-term support. Pit Boss wins on cooking area per dollar.

**Vs Camp Chef:** Camp Chef's Woodwind adds direct flame searing but costs $200 more.

### 2. Pit Boss 700FB — Best Budget Pellet Grill

The Pit Boss 700FB delivers 700 sq in of cooking area and real wood-pellet smoking at $399 — a price that's hard to beat for the feature set. The 700FB's defining feature is its slide-plate flame broiler, which opens a direct-heat zone for searing.

**Key specs:**
- Cooking area: 700 sq in
- Flame Broiler: Yes (opens for direct flame access)
- Temperature range: 180–500°F (sear up to 1,000°F with Flame Broiler open)
- Hopper capacity: 21 lbs
- WiFi: No

**What we love:** The Flame Broiler is a big deal — open it to expose direct flame for searing steaks, close it for indirect smoke. At this price, that's a differentiating feature. The 21 lb hopper handles a long cook without refilling.

**Where it falls short:** No WiFi monitoring. The PID controller is less precise than Traeger's D2 — expect ±25–30°F temperature swings. Build quality is noticeably lighter than Traeger.

**Who it's for:** Budget-minded grillers who want pellet convenience and direct-flame searing under $400.

### 3. Camp Chef Woodwind Pro 24 — Best Innovation

Camp Chef's Woodwind Pro 24 introduced Smoke Control — a dial that adjusts smoke output from level 1 (clean, mild) to level 10 (heavy smoke), independent of temperature. This solves the pellet grill community's biggest complaint: too little smoke flavor at high cooking temps.

**Key specs:**
- Cooking area: 811 sq in (with second shelf)
- Smoke Control: 10-level adjustment system
- Slide & Grill: Direct flame access (up to ~650°F)
- WiFi: Yes (Camp Chef Connect app)
- Sear Box: Compatible with add-on Sidekick burner

**What we love:** Smoke Control is genuinely unique and works — you can push maximum smoke at 225°F or pull it back for a cleaner finish at 400°F. The Slide & Grill direct flame access competes with Pit Boss's Flame Broiler. The modular Sidekick add-on (sold separately) adds a propane sear burner for finishing at 900°F+.

**Where it falls short:** At $899, you're competing with entry-level Traeger Ironwood. The Camp Chef Connect app is solid but not as polished as Traeger's WiFire.

**Who it's for:** Experienced grillers who want maximum smoke control and searing flexibility.

### 4. Traeger Ironwood 885 — Best Premium

The Traeger Ironwood 885 adds Downdraft Exhaust (pulls smoke through food rather than over it), Super Smoke mode (maximum smoke at low temps), and a double-wall insulated body to the Pro 780 platform. Serious competition BBQ cooks use Ironwoods.

**Key specs:**
- Cooking area: 885 sq in
- Downdraft exhaust: Yes
- Super Smoke mode: Yes (max smoke at 165–225°F)
- Temperature range: 165°F–500°F
- WiFi: WiFire with Alexa integration

**What we love:** Super Smoke mode is noticeably better than the Pro 780's smoke output — the brisket, ribs, and chicken results at low temps are genuinely more flavorful. Downdraft exhaust creates a more even smoke distribution across the cooking surface.

**Where it falls short:** $1,299 is serious money. The jump from Pro 780 to Ironwood is meaningful but not transformative — it's for enthusiasts.

**Who it's for:** Dedicated pitmasters who smoke regularly and want the best Traeger without going to the flagship Timberline.
