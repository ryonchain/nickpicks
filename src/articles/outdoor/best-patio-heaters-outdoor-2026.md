## Propane vs. Electric: Which Is Right for You?

**Propane patio heaters** are freestanding, require no electrical wiring, and produce high BTU output (40,000–50,000 BTU) that heats a wide outdoor area. The trade-off: propane tank cost, refilling/swapping 20 lb tanks, and wind can blow out the pilot light. Best for large uncovered patios and areas without electrical access.

**Electric infrared patio heaters** mount permanently to walls or ceilings and direct heat at people rather than trying to heat outdoor air. They reach full heat instantly, have no open flame, and work in covered spaces (pergolas, porches) where propane heaters can't be used safely. The trade-off: installation cost and permanent placement. Best for covered patios and restaurants.

**Tabletop propane heaters** are compact and portable for small seating areas, camping, or supplemental heating alongside a larger unit.

## Buyer's Guide

### How Many BTU Do You Need?

| Space | Recommended BTU |
|-------|----------------|
| Intimate table (4 people) | 10,000–15,000 BTU |
| Small patio (6 people) | 30,000–40,000 BTU |
| Large open patio (10+ people) | 40,000–50,000 BTU |
| Covered pergola | Electric infrared (BTU equivalent 10,000+) |

Open-air spaces lose heat to wind — if your patio is exposed, increase BTU estimate by 20%.

### Propane Safety Rules

- Never use propane heaters in fully enclosed spaces (carbon monoxide risk)
- Always check for gas leaks before lighting (soapy water on connections)
- Keep heater 3 ft minimum from combustible materials
- Use CSA/UL-certified heaters only

### How Long Does a Propane Tank Last?

A standard 20 lb propane tank holds 4.7 gallons of fuel. At 46,000 BTU:
- High setting: ~7–8 hours
- Medium setting: ~10–12 hours
- Low setting: ~14–16 hours

A 20 lb refill costs $20–25 at most hardware stores, grocery chains, and gas stations.
