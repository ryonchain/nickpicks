## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Weighted Blanket

**The 10% rule:** The general guidance is to choose a blanket that's approximately 10% of your body weight — a 150 lb person would use a 15 lb blanket. This is a starting point, not a rule. Some people prefer slightly heavier (10–12%), some slightly lighter (8%). If you've never used a weighted blanket, start at 10% and adjust from there.

**Fill types:** Glass beads are the standard in quality blankets — they're quiet, small, and stay evenly distributed. Plastic poly pellets are cheaper and louder. Steel shot beads are dense and expensive. Avoid plastic pellet fills if possible.

**Temperature regulation:** If you sleep warm, prioritize cotton or natural fiber construction (Baloo, Bearaby). If you sleep cold, minky fleece (Gravity, Degrees of Comfort fleece side) provides more insulation.

**Machine washability:** Heavier blankets (17+ lbs) often require commercial-grade washing machines or dry cleaning. Lighter blankets (12–15 lbs) are usually manageable in a standard home machine on gentle cycle. Check the specific weight limit in care instructions.

## FAQ

**Do weighted blankets actually help with anxiety and sleep?**
Controlled studies show mixed results — the effects appear real but vary significantly by individual. The pressure stimulus (deep touch pressure) mirrors the calming effect of a firm hug. Many users report faster sleep onset and fewer nighttime wakings. They don't work for everyone, but the mechanism (pressure sensation reducing cortisol) is plausible.

**Are weighted blankets safe for children?**
Weighted blankets are generally recommended for children age 3 and over, with weight chosen at 10% of the child's body weight. They should not be used for infants or toddlers without pediatric guidance — there is a risk of suffocation for children who cannot remove the blanket independently.

**Can two people share a weighted blanket?**
Standard weighted blankets are sized for one person — the weight is calibrated per user. For sharing, you'd need either a King-size blanket at the combined recommended weight (impractical) or the Degrees of Comfort dual-zone model for separate-side temperature management.

**How long do weighted blankets last?**
With proper care, a quality weighted blanket lasts 3–5 years minimum. Glass bead fill maintains its weight distribution when washing is done correctly (gentle cold, no heat dry). The stitching and cover material are the first things to show wear — higher thread counts and tighter pocket stitching last longer.
