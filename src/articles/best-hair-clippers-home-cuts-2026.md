## What to Know Before Your First Home Cut

Home haircuts have become the norm for millions of men, and the quality gap between a salon cut and a home cut has closed significantly — but only with the right tool and the right technique.

The biggest home-cutting mistakes:

- **Starting too short:** Always go longer than you think. You can always take more off; you cannot put hair back. Start a full guard size above your target length.
- **Skipping the taper comb:** The hardest part of a home cut is the transition zone between short sides and longer top. Taper combs and blending guards exist specifically for this.
- **Dull blades:** A clipper that tugs instead of cutting cleanly is the fastest path to an uneven result. Oil your blades and replace them when performance drops.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Corded for power, cordless for convenience:** Thick or long hair benefits from consistent corded power. Short maintenance cuts work well with cordless. The Wahl 5-Star Magic Clip gives you both.

**Guide comb count:** More combs allow finer gradations between lengths. 12 combs (Wahl Elite Pro) vs. 4 combs (Oster Fast Feed) represents a different intended use — kit vs. professional technique.

**Motor type matters for thick hair:** Pivot motors (Oster) maintain torque under load. Magnetic motors (Andis) run fast but can slow under thick hair. Rotary motors (Wahl) are the consumer standard with reliable all-around performance.

**Blade replacement vs. self-sharpening:** Wahl's self-sharpening blades reduce long-term cost. Andis and Oster use replaceable blades that can be swapped for specialty fade blades — relevant if you're building technique.
