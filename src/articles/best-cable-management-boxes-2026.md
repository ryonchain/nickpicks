The result is a nightstand, TV console, or desk that looks intentional rather than improvised.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Cable Management Box

**Measure your power strip first.** This is the most important step. Standard cable management boxes fit power strips up to 13–15 inches. If you have a 20-inch smart surge protector or a UPS unit, you need the D-Line Large or a similar oversize box. Don't buy the box before measuring.

**Ventilation is a safety requirement.** An enclosed box with no airflow is a fire hazard — power strips generate heat, and that heat needs somewhere to go. All the picks in this guide have adequate ventilation. Avoid boxes with solid, completely sealed tops and sides.

**Cable exit placement.** Think about where your device cables need to run. Standard boxes have two cable exits on the short ends, which works for most linear desk setups. If devices are positioned at different angles or heights, a box with side exits or the four-sided SimpleCord gives you more flexibility.

**Material and aesthetics.** White and black ABS plastic boxes are functional and blend into most setups. Bamboo boxes are the right choice when you're trying to match natural wood furniture or a warm home-office aesthetic. The material choice is about visual integration, not functional performance.

**Size up if in doubt.** A cable management box that doesn't fit your power strip is useless. A box that's slightly larger than needed is just fine — the extra space inside makes cable management easier, not worse.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are cable management boxes safe for power strips?**
Yes, provided the box has adequate ventilation. All picks in this guide have perforated lids, mesh sides, or slatted openings that allow heat dissipation. Never pack a box so tightly with cables that it blocks the ventilation openings.

**What size cable management box do I need?**
Measure your power strip's length. Standard boxes fit up to 13–15 inches. For large surge protectors or UPS units, buy the D-Line Large (19.5" internal length). When in doubt, size up — a box with extra space is easier to use than one that's barely large enough.

**Can cable management boxes go under desks?**
They can be placed on the floor under a desk, but they aren't designed for under-desk mounting. For mounted under-desk cable management, look for under-desk cable management trays or raceways instead of freestanding boxes.

**Do cable management boxes need to be plugged in?**
No — they're passive containers. The power strip goes inside; the power strip's cord routes out through the box's cord opening to the wall outlet; device cables route through the box's cable exits to your devices.

**How do I route cables through a cable management box?**
Place the power strip inside. Thread its power cord out through the back opening to the wall. Run device cables in through the front or side openings and plug them into the power strip. Use velcro ties to manage excess cable length inside the box before closing the lid.
