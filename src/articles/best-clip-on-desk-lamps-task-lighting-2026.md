## Task Lighting Buying Guide

### Color Temperature for Different Tasks

| Task | Recommended Color Temp |
|------|----------------------|
| Creative/design work | 5000K–6500K (daylight) |
| General computer work | 4000K–5000K (cool white) |
| Reading paper documents | 3000K–4000K (neutral-warm) |
| Evening work | 2700K–3000K (warm white) |

### Lux vs Lumens: What You Need

**Lumens** is total light output. **Lux** is lumens per square meter — the useful measure for task lighting. A focused 400-lumen lamp close to your work surface achieves 400+ lux at 18 inches, which is the recommended level for detailed task work. A scattered 800-lumen lamp at arm's length might only achieve 100–200 lux on the surface.

For focused task lighting: 300–500 lumens from a directed source at 18–24 inches is sufficient for most desk work.

### Clamp vs Monitor Mount

**Clamp (desk edge):** Flexible placement on any flat edge. Better for lighting a wider area of the desk or directing light at specific angles. The gooseneck arm on most clamp lamps gives full 3D positioning.

**Monitor mount:** Designed to sit on top of the monitor and illuminate the area directly in front of it. Better for keyboard and document illumination without screen glare. Limited to monitor-adjacent positioning.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can task lighting improve video call appearance?**
Yes — a lamp positioned to the left or right of your monitor (45-degree angle) acts as a key light that illuminates your face for video calls. 4000K–5000K color temperature looks natural on camera. This setup significantly improves how you appear in Zoom and Teams calls compared to relying on overhead room lighting.

**How do I reduce glare from a desk lamp on my monitor?**
Position the lamp so the beam is directed at your work surface, not at the monitor face. Monitor-top light bars are specifically designed for this — their asymmetric beams point downward at the keyboard and desk. For clamp lamps, angle the head toward your desk surface at approximately 30–45 degrees from horizontal.

**Should I use LED or CFL for a desk lamp?**
LED exclusively. CFL lamps take time to warm up, have visible flicker, generate heat, and are inefficient. All lamps in this guide are LED.
