The challenge: most air purifiers aren't built with bedrooms in mind. They're rated for coverage area but not noise level. This guide focuses on units that actually work at night — quiet first, powerful second.

## How to Pick the Right Bedroom Air Purifier

### Noise level matters more than coverage

In a bedroom, sleep mode noise level is the #1 specification. A purifier covering 500 sq ft means nothing if it keeps you awake at 40 dB on low. Always check the dB rating on the lowest or Sleep setting — not the "quiet mode" marketing claim.

**Target: 30 dB or lower on Sleep/low setting**

### CADR and room size

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) measures cubic feet of clean air per minute. As a rule of thumb, your CADR should match your room's square footage:

- 150 sq ft bedroom → CADR 100+
- 250 sq ft bedroom → CADR 150+
- 350+ sq ft master bedroom → CADR 200+

### True HEPA vs. "HEPA-type"

Always buy True HEPA (or H13 HEPA). These filter 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger — including dust mites, pollen, mold spores, pet dander, and fine smoke particles. "HEPA-type" filters typically capture only 85–90% and miss the smallest, most harmful particles.

### Avoid ozone-generating purifiers for bedrooms

Ionizers and UV-C purifiers can produce trace amounts of ozone — a respiratory irritant that's problematic in enclosed sleeping spaces. If your purifier has an ionizer (Coway, Winix), disable it for nighttime bedroom use.
