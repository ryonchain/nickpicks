## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Suction Power (Pa):** 2,000–2,500 Pa handles hardwood and low-pile carpet well. For thick carpet or heavy debris, look for 4,000 Pa+. Self-emptying premium models hit 6,000 Pa.

**Navigation Type:** Gyroscope (random/bouncing) is cheaper but less efficient. LiDAR maps your home precisely and cleans in methodical rows. Camera-based AI (Roomba) sits in between — good pattern recognition but less precise room geometry.

**Self-Emptying Dock:** Worth the premium if you run the robot daily. The dock stores weeks of debris so you're not emptying a tiny dustbin every day. Some premium docks also wash the mop pad and refill water automatically.

**Pet Hair Performance:** Look for rubber brushrolls (not bristle) — they resist tangling. AI obstacle avoidance matters more than suction if you have pets that leave surprises.

**Mopping:** Useful for hardwood homes. Basic mopping is just dragging a damp pad. Sonic vibrating mopping (Roborock) actually scrubs. Mop-lifting (for carpet transition) is essential if you have both carpet and hardwood.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do robot vacuums work on thick carpet?**
Yes, but they work best on low-pile and medium-pile carpet. Very thick, high-pile carpet (shag rugs) can overwhelm most robots. Choose models with 4,000+ Pa suction for thick carpets.

**How often should I run a robot vacuum?**
Daily for pet owners; every 2–3 days for average homes. The more frequently you run it, the less debris accumulates and the more effective each run is.

**Are self-emptying docks worth the cost?**
Yes, if you run the robot daily or near-daily. The convenience of not emptying a tiny bin after every single run is significant. Budget 1–2 years of use and the dock pays for itself in time saved.

**Can robot vacuums replace upright vacuums?**
For maintenance cleaning, yes — a daily robot vacuum keeps floors visibly clean. For deep cleaning (under cushions, stairs, edges), you'll still need an upright or handheld occasionally.
