## Hub Requirement

**Philips Hue** requires the Hue Bridge (~$60 sold separately or included in starter kits). The Bridge connects to your router and enables Zigbee communication, local processing, and out-of-home control. The upside: Zigbee is reliable, fast, and doesn't strain your Wi-Fi network. With 50+ bulbs, you won't clog your router.

**LIFX** bulbs connect directly to Wi-Fi — no hub required. Simpler setup: screw in, download app, connect. The downside: each LIFX bulb is a Wi-Fi device. In homes with 20+ bulbs, this can strain routers. Performance also depends on Wi-Fi signal strength — distance from the router matters.

**Winner:** LIFX for initial simplicity; Hue for reliability at scale.

## Brightness and Color

**LIFX** is objectively brighter. The LIFX Color A19 outputs 1100 lumens; comparable Philips Hue White and Color A19 outputs 800 lumens. For rooms where maximum brightness matters (kitchens, workspaces), LIFX has a practical advantage.

LIFX color reproduction is also considered more vibrant. Reds, greens, and blues appear more saturated. If color mood lighting is important to you, LIFX produces the more striking results.

**Winner:** LIFX for brightness and color vibrancy.
