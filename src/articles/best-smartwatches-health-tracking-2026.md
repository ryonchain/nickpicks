## What to Look For

**ECG capability** is the most important health feature to prioritize — it can detect atrial fibrillation, a serious arrhythmia that affects 6 million Americans and significantly raises stroke risk. All five watches above include ECG.

**Battery life** should match your lifestyle. If you can't remember to charge nightly, the Withings ScanWatch 2 (30 days) or Garmin Venu 3 (14 days) are dramatically more practical than daily-charge competitors.

**Platform compatibility** matters more than most buyers realize. Samsung Galaxy Watch 7's blood pressure and body composition features only work with Samsung smartphones. Apple Watch only works with iPhone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which smartwatch is best for heart health monitoring?**
The Withings ScanWatch 2 is the most medically validated for heart health — its ECG and SpO2 sensors are FDA-cleared, meaning they meet clinical standards for atrial fibrillation and sleep apnea detection. For general heart rate accuracy during exercise, Polar Vantage V3 leads the category.

**Can a smartwatch replace a medical heart monitor?**
No. Smartwatch ECGs are screening tools, not diagnostic devices. They're excellent at flagging potential arrhythmias for follow-up with a cardiologist, but a clinical Holter monitor or in-office ECG is required for diagnosis.

**Do smartwatches work for sleep tracking?**
Yes — modern smartwatches track sleep stages (light, deep, REM) with accuracy within 80–85% of clinical sleep studies. They're best used for trend tracking over weeks rather than single-night precision.
