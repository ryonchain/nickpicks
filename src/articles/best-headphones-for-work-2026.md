## ANC vs. Passive Noise Isolation: Which Do You Need?

**Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)** uses microphones to pick up ambient noise and generates an inverse sound wave to cancel it. It works best for steady, low-frequency noise: HVAC systems, airplane engines, open office ambient buzz, road noise.

**Passive noise isolation** is physical blocking of sound through the earcup seal. It works better for high-frequency, variable noise: conversations, keyboard clatter, barking dogs.

The best WFH headphones use both: ANC for the low-frequency ambient rumble, passive isolation via a good seal for higher-frequency intrusions. That's why over-ear designs with good cushion seal outperform open-back headphones in noisy environments.

## Microphone Quality: Why It Matters More Than ANC

Most buyers focus on ANC quality for headphones, but microphone quality is equally important for WFH. In a Zoom call, your colleagues hear you — not the ambient noise your ANC is blocking. A headphone with great ANC but mediocre microphone still sounds bad to your colleagues.

**Best microphone options:**
1. **Jabra Evolve2 55** — best professional mic, 10-mic array

3. **Bose 700** — best microphone of the consumer Bose lineup (not included in our main picks because it's older, but the mic is exceptional)

If your microphone quality is currently a constant complaint from colleagues ("Can you say that again?" is a sign), upgrade to the Jabra or Bose 700 specifically for the mic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Sony WH-1000XM5 vs. WH-1000XM4 — is the upgrade worth it?**
The XM5 has better ANC (especially for mid-frequency noise like voices), improved microphone quality, and slightly better call performance. The XM4 has better fold-ability for travel and often sells for $100 less. For WFH, the XM5's microphone improvement is the meaningful upgrade. If price matters, the XM4 at $279 is still excellent.

**Do noise-canceling headphones work for open offices?**
Yes, for the ambient buzz. No, for loud conversations directly next to you — no ANC eliminates human speech volume when someone is 2 feet away. For open offices with lots of conversation, a combination of ANC + white noise through the headphones works best.

**Wired vs. wireless for WFH?**
Wireless wins for WFH. The freedom to grab coffee, walk to the kitchen, or stand up without unplugging is genuinely quality-of-life improving. Wireless audio latency has improved to the point that it's imperceptible for video calls. Only audio professionals who need sample-accurate monitoring should choose wired.

**Can I use these for phone calls without my computer?**
Yes — all picks support Bluetooth connection to smartphones. Multipoint models (Sony XM5, Anker Q45) can pair to laptop and phone simultaneously so call audio automatically routes to the device with an active call.
