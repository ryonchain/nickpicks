## Stubble vs. Short Beard: Different Needs

**Stubble (0.5–5mm)** is about precision and skin comfort. At this length, any trimmer that pulls or irritates is a deal-breaker. The blade design and guard tightness matter more than raw motor power.

**Short beards (5–25mm)** are about consistent length maintenance and crisp edge definition. You need a trimmer with accurate guards that deliver repeatable results across the full beard.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Guard precision matters more than range:** A trimmer advertising "0.5–25mm" is only useful if the 3mm and 5mm guards are accurately sized. Cheap guards drift — your 5mm trim becomes a 6mm trim the next time. Wahl and Braun guard tolerances are the tightest.

**Motor power for coarse beards:** If your facial hair is thick and wiry, look for high-powered motors (6000+ RPM). Underpowered motors pull at coarse hair, especially at longer lengths.

**Self-sharpening blades save money:** Blade replacements for budget trimmers run $15–$25. Self-sharpening blades (Wahl) stay sharp for years without replacement.

**Oil your blades consistently:** One drop of clipper oil every 3–5 uses extends blade life and improves cut quality. This applies to every trimmer on this list.
