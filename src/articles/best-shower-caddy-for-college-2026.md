## Buying Guide: What Matters in a Dorm Shower Caddy

### Portable Tote vs Wall-Mounted
Dorms use communal showers — wall-mounted caddies (tension poles, suction cups) don't work in communal bathroom stalls. **A portable tote is the only practical option for dorm life.**

### Mesh vs Plastic
- **Mesh:** Drains immediately, lighter, more durable, can rust if not rust-resistant
- **Plastic:** Easy to wipe clean, cheaper, water pools slightly, can crack over time

Both work for 4 years if you buy quality. The choice comes down to preference.

### Size: What Fits in Your Routine?
Measure your bottles before you buy. Full-size shampoo bottles are typically 10–11" tall. Make sure the caddy's main compartment accommodates full-size bottles if you don't want to transfer to travel sizes.

### What to Pack in Your Caddy
- Shampoo and conditioner
- Body wash
- Face wash
- Razor + shaving cream
- Loofah or washcloth
- Toothbrush + toothpaste (or keep those in your room)
- Small towel or face towel

The best shower caddies for college hold all of this without becoming unwieldy. Stick to brands with rust-resistant construction and you'll use the same caddy for all 4 years.
