## Understanding Retinol Concentrations

Retinol is measured as a percentage — start low to let your skin adjust:

- **0.025% – 0.05%:** Beginner-safe. Gentle enough for nightly use from week one.
- **0.1% – 0.3%:** Intermediate. Use every 2–3 nights initially, increase slowly.
- **0.5% – 1%:** Advanced. For those who've used retinol for 3+ months.

**Retinol vs. retinaldehyde vs. retinyl palmitate:**
- Retinyl palmitate: Weakest, very gentle. Good for ultra-sensitive skin.
- Retinol: Standard OTC strength. Gold standard for anti-aging.
- Retinaldehyde: Stronger than retinol, closer to prescription but still OTC.

---

## How to Layer Retinol Correctly

1. Cleanse and dry skin completely (moisture increases irritation risk)
2. Apply a thin layer of retinol serum — less is more
3. Wait 20–30 minutes before applying moisturizer ("buffering" reduces irritation for beginners)
4. Always use SPF the next morning — retinol increases photosensitivity

**Start with 2 nights per week** for the first month, increase to nightly as tolerated.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take for retinol to show results?**
Expect 4–8 weeks of consistent use for texture improvements, 12+ weeks for wrinkle reduction.

**Q: Can I use retinol every night?**
Once your skin has adjusted (after 4–6 weeks of gradual introduction), nightly use is appropriate for most people.

**Q: Does retinol work for acne?**
Yes — retinol speeds cell turnover which helps unclog pores. However, it may cause initial "purging" in the first 2–6 weeks.

**Q: Can I use retinol with vitamin C?**
Not simultaneously — they can irritate together. Use vitamin C in the morning, retinol at night.

**Q: Should beginners use the CeraVe or L'Oreal retinol?**
Very sensitive skin: start with **CeraVe** (encapsulated, ceramide-buffered). Normal/combination skin: the **L'Oreal 0.3%** delivers faster results.
