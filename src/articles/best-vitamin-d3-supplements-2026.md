## How to Optimize Vitamin D Absorption

**Take with the largest meal of the day.** Vitamin D is fat-soluble — dietary fat in the same meal significantly increases absorption. A study in the Journal of Bone and Mineral Research showed 32% higher blood levels when D3 was taken with the highest-fat meal of the day versus the smallest meal.

**Split large doses.** If taking 5,000+ IU daily, splitting into two smaller doses (morning and evening with meals) may improve absorption efficiency over a single large dose.

**Check your levels first.** Before supplementing aggressively, request a serum 25(OH)D test. Optimal range: 40–80 ng/mL. Deficiency: below 20 ng/mL. Insufficiency: 20–30 ng/mL.

**Vitamin D status guide:**
| 25(OH)D Level | Interpretation | Action |
|--------------|----------------|--------|
| <20 ng/mL | Deficient | Higher doses under doctor supervision |
| 20–30 ng/mL | Insufficient | 2,000–5,000 IU daily supplementation |
| 30–40 ng/mL | Adequate | 1,000–2,000 IU daily maintenance |
| 40–80 ng/mL | Optimal | Maintain with sun + 1,000 IU |
| >100 ng/mL | Excess | Reduce supplementation |

Toxicity from vitamin D supplementation is rare but real at very high doses (10,000+ IU/day for extended periods). At 5,000 IU/day, most adults will reach optimal levels without approaching toxicity — but annual testing is good practice if supplementing consistently.
