## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Smart Door Lock

**ANSI security grade:** ANSI Grade 1 (Schlage) is the highest residential security certification — it requires more resistance to physical attacks. Grade 2 (Yale, others) is still secure for residential use. If security is your top concern, prioritize Grade 1.

**Hub vs. no hub:** Many earlier smart locks required a separate hub/bridge for WiFi control. Current top picks (Schlage, Yale WiFi, Kwikset, August Wi-Fi) have built-in WiFi and work without extra hardware. Check if a lock requires a hub before buying.

**Access codes:** If you manage rentals or have frequent visitors, look for locks that support 100+ codes. Yale's 250-code limit is the highest on this list.

**Smart home ecosystem:** If you use Apple HomeKit, prioritize locks with Apple Home Key (Schlage Encode Plus, Level Lock+, Yale with Home Key). For Google Home or Amazon Alexa, most locks on this list work via their respective apps through third-party integrations.

## FAQ

**Are smart locks secure?**
Modern smart locks from reputable brands (Schlage, Yale, Kwikset) are ANSI Grade 1 or 2 certified and as physically secure as traditional deadbolts. The security risk is digital — weak passwords, unsecured accounts, or outdated firmware. Use strong unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication on lock accounts.

**What happens if the battery dies?**
All smart locks on this list use standard AA or CR2 batteries with low battery warnings. In emergency, all models have an emergency power option: a 9V battery touched to external terminals provides enough power to enter a code and get in.

**Can smart locks be hacked remotely?**
Reputable brands use AES-128 or AES-256 encryption for communication. Remote hacking of properly configured smart locks is not a practical attack vector. The more common risk is account compromise from weak passwords or password reuse.

**Do smart locks work without WiFi?**
All keypad codes work without WiFi — the lock stores codes locally. Remote control via app requires WiFi or cellular connection. Auto-lock timers work locally.
