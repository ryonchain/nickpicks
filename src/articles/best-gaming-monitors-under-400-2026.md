## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Gaming Monitor

### Resolution vs. Refresh Rate
The core tradeoff in gaming monitors:

| Use Case | Best Choice |
|----------|-------------|
| Competitive FPS (CS2, Valorant) | 1080p, 240Hz+ |
| Balanced gaming | 1440p, 144–180Hz |
| Single-player / visual fidelity | 4K, 60–144Hz |
| Console gaming | 4K, HDMI 2.1 |

### Panel Types
- **IPS:** Best colors, widest viewing angles, 1ms response time. Best for most users.
- **VA:** Highest contrast ratio, best for dark environments. Slightly slower response.
- **TN:** Fastest response time, weakest colors. Only relevant for extreme competitive play.
- **OLED:** Best contrast (true black), fastest response, most expensive. Watch for burn-in.

### Adaptive Sync
- **FreeSync / FreeSync Premium:** Free, works on AMD and most NVIDIA cards
- **G-Sync Compatible:** NVIDIA-verified FreeSync
- **True G-Sync:** NVIDIA hardware module — premium price, minimal added benefit in 2026

### HDR Reality Check
- **HDR 400:** Minimal benefit over SDR — mostly a marketing label
- **HDR 600:** Noticeable improvement, worth seeking
- **HDR 1000+:** Excellent. Requires OLED or mini-LED backlight to achieve

## FAQ

**Q: Is 1440p worth it over 1080p for gaming?**
A: Yes for most gamers. At 27 inches, 1440p is noticeably sharper than 1080p. The GPU requirement increase is manageable with any mid-range card (RTX 4060 or above).

**Q: Do I need 240Hz if I'm not a competitive gamer?**
A: No. 144Hz is the diminishing-returns threshold for casual to serious gamers. The jump from 60Hz to 144Hz is dramatic; 144Hz to 240Hz is real but subtle for non-competitive play.

**Q: What's the difference between response time and refresh rate?**
A: Refresh rate (Hz) is how many frames the panel draws per second. Response time (ms) is how fast a pixel changes color. Both affect motion clarity — high refresh rate reduces stutter, low response time reduces ghosting.

**Q: Do I need G-Sync or FreeSync?**
A: Any adaptive sync is strongly recommended. It eliminates screen tearing without V-Sync input lag. G-Sync Compatible (FreeSync Premium certified by NVIDIA) works well on both AMD and NVIDIA cards.
