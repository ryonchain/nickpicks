## Commuter E-Bike Buying Guide

### Essential features for daily commuting

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|
| Integrated lights | Required for dawn/dusk commuting; aftermarket lights are inconvenient |
| Full fenders | Rain and puddles; without fenders, you arrive wet from road spray |
| Hydraulic disc brakes | Reliable stopping in wet conditions; cable disc brakes corrode faster |
| Puncture-resistant tires | Flat tires during commutes are the most common mechanical failure |
| Rear cargo rack | Carry laptop bag or groceries without a backpack |

### Class 1 vs. Class 2 vs. Class 3

- **Class 1** (pedal-assist to 20 mph): Most broadly permitted. Allowed in almost all bike lanes and multi-use paths. No throttle.
- **Class 2** (throttle + pedal-assist to 20 mph): Throttle is useful for traffic stops. Same speed as Class 1 but some paths restrict throttle bikes.
- **Class 3** (pedal-assist to 28 mph): Faster but restricted from many bike paths and trails. Check local laws before buying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How far can a commuter e-bike go on one charge?**
Most commuter e-bikes claim 40–60 miles of range, but real-world commuting range (hills, stops, heavier riders, higher assist levels) is typically 25–40 miles. The Rad Power RadCity 5 Plus averages 35–45 miles per charge in urban commuting conditions — enough for most round-trip commutes under 20 miles.

**Are e-bikes legal to ride in bike lanes?**
In the US, Class 1 (pedal-assist up to 20 mph) and Class 2 (throttle up to 20 mph) e-bikes are legal in most bike lanes. Class 3 (pedal-assist up to 28 mph) have restrictions in some states and cities. Always check local regulations — many cities restrict e-bikes from shared paths or require helmets above certain speeds.

**How long does an e-bike battery last before needing replacement?**
Most e-bike batteries last 500–1,000 charge cycles before capacity drops noticeably (typically around 80% of original). At 3 charges per week, that's 3–6 years of regular commuting. Battery replacements typically cost $300–600 depending on the brand.

**Do I need to pedal an e-bike or can I just use the throttle?**
Depends on the class. Class 2 e-bikes have a throttle that propels the bike without pedaling. Class 1 e-bikes require pedaling to activate assist — there's no throttle. Most commuter e-bikes are Class 2 or have both modes. Throttle-only is useful for stop-and-go city traffic; pedal assist is better for range.

**What weight can an e-bike carry for commuting?**
Most commuter e-bikes have 250–300 lb rider weight limits. The Rad Power RadCity 5 Plus supports 275 lbs rider weight plus cargo rack capacity. If you carry a laptop bag, groceries, or gear, factor in the total load — rear racks typically support 55–75 lbs of cargo.
