## What to Look for in a Travel Pillow

**Support style**: Traditional U-shaped pillows wrap around the front of your neck. Rear-clip designs like the Cabeau stabilize your head from behind. Side-sleeper pillows (like the Trtl) support one side of your head against the seat. Think about your natural sleeping position — window seat sleepers benefit most from side-support designs.

**Fill material**: Memory foam molds to your neck and holds shape. Microbeads are pliable but can shift. Inflatable pillows are the most packable but least comfortable. Shredded foam (like Coop) is adjustable and breathable.

**Packability**: If you're a carry-on-only traveler, size matters. The Cabeau Evolution S3 compresses to about 4 × 5 inches. The Trtl folds flat. Inflatable options shrink to almost nothing. Full-size memory foam pillows require a clip or luggage attachment.

**Washability**: Neck pillows sit against your face and neck for hours. Look for a removable, machine-washable cover. Not all options have this.

**Chin support**: Many travelers' biggest complaint is their chin dropping onto their chest during sleep. Pillows with higher front padding or chin cups (like the Cabeau S3) solve this.

## Travel Pillow FAQs

**Can I bring a travel pillow on a plane?**
Yes. Travel pillows are allowed in carry-on and personal item bags on all major airlines. If your pillow is bulky, consider a compression bag to reduce its size, or clip it to the outside of your bag using a carabiner.

**What's the best position to sleep on a plane?**
The recline-and-lean-against-window position works best for neck support — that's why the Trtl Pillow Plus does so well. Sleeping fully upright without support causes the chin-drop problem that even good pillows can only partially solve.

**How do I clean a travel pillow?**
Most covers are machine washable — check the label. Memory foam and TEMPUR inserts should not be machine washed. Spot clean with mild soap and warm water, then air dry completely before using (24–48 hours). The Coop Home Goods pillow is an exception — both cover and fill are machine washable.

**What size travel pillow should I buy?**
Most travel pillows are one-size-fits-most. If you have a very long or short neck, look for adjustable designs (Coop Home Goods) or pillows with size variants (Tempur-Pedic offers small, medium, and large neck heights).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best travel pillow for long flights?**
The Cabeau Evolution S3 is our top pick for long-haul flights — memory foam construction, a rear locking strap that keeps your head centered, and it compresses to about the size of a water bottle.

**What travel pillow actually keeps your head from falling forward?**
The Trtl Pillow Plus uses an internal scaffold that wraps around your neck and presses against the seat — it keeps your head upright on one side rather than letting it fall forward or to the other side.

**Are inflatable travel pillows any good?**
Inflatable pillows pack down to almost nothing, making them ideal for minimalist packers. They are less comfortable than memory foam but provide adequate support for shorter flights. Look for ones with a rounded shape rather than a flat horseshoe.

**How do I wash a travel pillow?**
Most travel pillow covers are machine washable — unzip and wash separately. Memory foam inserts should not be machine washed. Spot clean the insert with mild soap and water and air dry fully before reassembling.

**Is a travel pillow worth it for short flights?**
For flights under 2 hours, most people don't need a dedicated travel pillow. For flights 3+ hours, a good pillow significantly reduces neck stiffness and improves sleep quality — especially on overnight or red-eye flights.
