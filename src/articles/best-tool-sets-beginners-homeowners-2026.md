## What to Look For

- **Match the set to your actual tasks:** A homeowner who needs to hang shelves and fix a leaky faucet needs a general hand tool kit. A garage mechanic needs sockets and ratchets. Don't pay for 450 pieces if 65 covers your real use cases.
- **Chrome-vanadium vs. chrome-plated:** Chrome-vanadium (Cr-V) steel throughout the tool is stronger and more durable. Chrome-plated is a surface finish only — less important but still helpful for rust resistance.
- **Ratchet teeth count:** Higher tooth counts (72-tooth is typical in quality sets) require a smaller arc to advance, which is crucial when working in tight spaces like engine bays or behind appliances.
- **SAE vs. metric coverage:** If you work on vehicles, you need both. Most modern cars use metric fasteners; older American vehicles often mix both systems.
- **Warranty:** Look for lifetime warranties on hand tools — Stanley, Craftsman, Husky, and DeWalt all offer them. This matters because sockets and wrenches occasionally round off or crack under heavy use.
- **Case and organization:** Labeled cutouts tell you immediately when a tool is missing and prevent the chaos of a loose tool pile. Pay attention to case quality before buying.
