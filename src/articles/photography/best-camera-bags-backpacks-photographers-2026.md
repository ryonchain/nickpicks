## How to Choose the Right Camera Bag

**Match capacity to your kit.** Measure your camera + largest lens combination. Most bag specs list interior dimensions — verify your gear actually fits before buying.

**Consider your use case.** Street and travel photographers benefit from slings for quick access. Landscape shooters with tripods need backpacks with external attachment points. Commercial photographers moving between locations want rolling cases.

**Check weather resistance.** Nylon DWR (durable water repellent) coating handles light rain; a rain cover handles downpours. If you shoot outdoors often, get both.

**Evaluate access patterns.** Back-panel access is secure but slow; top-zip is fast but exposes gear; clamshell/butterfly is fastest. Choose based on how often you need to reach your camera mid-shoot.
