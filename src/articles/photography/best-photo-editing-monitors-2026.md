## Monitor Calibration — Essential Step

Even a factory-calibrated monitor drifts over time. Recalibrate every 3-6 months with a hardware colorimeter:

**X-Rite i1Display Studio** is the recommended entry-level calibrator. Works with all major monitor brands, creates precise ICC profiles, and costs about $120 — an excellent investment that makes any monitor more accurate.

**Datacolor SpyderX Pro** is the alternative at similar pricing. Slightly faster at measuring, good software, and more plug-and-play for beginners.

## Photo Editing Monitor Buying Guide

**For web photographers**: 99%+ sRGB coverage is all you need. The Dell S2722QC or ASUS ProArt PA278QV are excellent here.

**For print photographers**: 95%+ Adobe RGB coverage is important. The BenQ SW271C is the standout recommendation.

**For mixed photo+video**: DCI-P3 coverage matters alongside Adobe RGB. The ASUS ProArt PA32UCX or an OLED panel serves both.

**Panel size and resolution**: 27" 4K hits the sweet spot — pixel density is high enough to see fine detail without needing to zoom in. 32" 4K provides more canvas but needs more desk depth.
