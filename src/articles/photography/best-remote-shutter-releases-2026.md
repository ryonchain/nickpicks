## How to Choose a Camera Remote

**Check your camera's remote connection type first.** Older cameras use 2.5mm or 3.5mm jacks. Modern mirrorless cameras often use Bluetooth only (no physical remote port). Verify before buying.

**For Bulb mode exposures**, you need a remote with a locking feature — press once to open the shutter, lock, press again to close. Without a lock, you'd have to hold the button for 30+ seconds.

**For time-lapse**, you need intervalometer capability. Many cameras have this built in — check before buying a separate remote.

**Wireless vs wired for wildlife.** Wired remotes work from 60cm-1m away. If you need to trigger from farther — for bird photography or self-portraits — you need a wireless remote with 30-100m range.
