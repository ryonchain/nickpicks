## Filter Buying Advice

**Measure your filter thread first.** The diameter is stamped on your lens or printed on the lens cap (∅77mm, ∅82mm, etc.). Always match filter to lens thread diameter.

**Use step-up rings to standardize.** If you own lenses with 67mm, 72mm, and 77mm threads, buy your filters in 77mm and use 67-77 and 72-77 step-up rings. This saves hundreds of dollars buying the same filter in multiple sizes.

**Don't use cheap UV filters.** A $8 UV filter from an unknown brand is optically worse than no filter at all — lens flare, reduced contrast, color casting. If you want protection, spend at least $40 on a multi-coated option.

**Test your CPL in the field.** Polarizers work best at 90° to the sun. Rotate the outer ring while looking through the viewfinder until reflections disappear or sky contrast increases.
