## Ring Light Buying Guide

**Size determines application.** 10" rings are for phone/close-up content. 14-18" rings are for portrait photography with a camera. The larger the ring, the more flattering the light wrap.

**CRI matters for portraits.** CRI (Color Rendering Index) measures how accurately colors appear under the light. CRI 90+ is recommended for beauty and portrait work — skin tones look natural. Budget lights often rate CRI 80 or below.

**Check the color temperature range.** Most lights offer 3200K-5600K. Premium lights go down to 2500K (candlelight warm) or up to 6500K (cool studio). Wider range = more creative flexibility.

**Flicker matters for video.** Cheap LEDs can flicker at frequencies that cause banding in video shot above 1/100s. Look for "flicker-free" certification if you're recording video.
