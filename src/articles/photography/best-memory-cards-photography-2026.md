## Memory Card Buying Guide

**Check your camera's slot type first.** Buying the wrong card type is an easy mistake — a CFexpress Type B card does not fit a CFexpress Type A slot. Verify in your camera manual before purchasing.

**Buy two cards, not one big card.** A single 512GB card means a single point of failure. Two 256GB cards spread your risk and let you swap mid-session.

**Don't neglect the card reader.** USB-A card readers cap transfers at 100 MB/s regardless of card speed. Get a USB-C reader that supports UHS-II to unlock full speed from your cards.

**Fake cards exist.** Buy from Amazon directly or verified sellers. If a UHS-II card is priced like a budget UHS-I card, it's almost certainly counterfeit.
