## Tripod Buying Checklist

**Load capacity.** Take your heaviest camera + lens combination and double it. If your setup weighs 4 lbs, you want a tripod rated for 8 lbs minimum. Overloaded tripods vibrate, shift, and fail.

**Maximum height without center column.** Extend only the legs and check if the tripod reaches your eye level. Extending the center column to reach eye level degrades stability.

**Leg locks.** Twist locks are sleeker; flip locks are faster in the field. Both are secure when well-made. Avoid tripods with plastic leg lock mechanisms — they crack in cold weather.

**Weight.** If you're hiking, count every pound. If you're shooting in a studio or from a car, weight doesn't matter.
