## What to Look for in a Camera Strap

**Load capacity.** Know your camera + lens weight before buying. A mirrorless with a prime is 1-1.5 lbs; a full-frame DSLR with a 70-200mm is 4+ lbs. Choose straps rated above your maximum kit weight.

**Attachment system.** Standard split-ring lug attachments work with any strap. Quick-release systems (like Peak Design Anchors) are worth the premium if you swap straps frequently. Tripod-socket mounts offer single-point hang options.

**Material.** Neoprene is soft and grips clothing. Nylon is durable and easy to clean. Leather looks premium and lasts decades but requires care. Avoid thin synthetic straps on anything heavier than a compact camera.

**Adjustability.** You want one-handed length adjustment on a sling strap — being able to lengthen or shorten while the camera is hanging is essential for fast-moving shoots.
