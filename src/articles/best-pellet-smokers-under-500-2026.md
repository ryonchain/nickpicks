## What to Look For

- **PID controller:** A PID (proportional-integral-derivative) controller regulates temperature far more precisely than a simple digital controller. Look for this if you're doing long low-and-slow cooks where consistency matters.
- **Hopper size:** A 20+ lb hopper gets you through a 10–12 hour brisket smoke without a refill. Smaller hoppers require babysitting overnight cooks.
- **Cooking area:** 500–700 sq in covers most families. If you regularly cook for 10+ people, prioritize models with upper racks or bigger main grates.
- **Direct-flame searing:** Standard pellet grills can't sear like a gas or charcoal grill. If you want steak-worthy crust, look for a slide-and-sear or flame broiler design.
- **App connectivity:** Helpful for overnight smokes but not essential. Traeger's WiFIRE is the most polished; others are basic but functional.
- **Build quality:** Check lid-to-body seal, hopper lid fit, and leg stability. Better seals mean more consistent temperatures and longer grill life.
