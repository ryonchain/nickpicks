## How to Read Air Purifier Specs

**CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate):** The most important spec. Measured in CFM (cubic feet per minute) for dust, smoke, and pollen separately. Higher = faster air cleaning. Match CADR to your room size: CADR rating should be at least ⅔ of your room's square footage.

**Room coverage area:** Manufacturer-stated coverage assumes 8-foot ceilings and 4-5 air changes per hour (ACH). For allergy sufferers, aim for 5-6 ACH — which means sizing up or picking a model rated larger than your room.

**True HEPA vs HEPA-like:** True HEPA filters 99.97% of particles ≥0.3 microns. "HEPA-like" or "HEPA-type" filters are less efficient. Only buy True HEPA.

**CADR noise tradeoff:** Higher CADR at low fan speeds = better. Premium purifiers clean air efficiently at low speeds (quieter). Cheap purifiers require high fan speeds to achieve adequate CADR — which is loud.

**Filter cost:** Multiply the replacement filter cost by 2–4 (replacements per year) and add it to the device price for true cost of ownership. A $50 purifier with $60/year in filters is more expensive than a $170 purifier with $30/year in filters.

## How to Size Your Air Purifier

**Step 1:** Measure your room's square footage (length × width).

**Step 2:** Determine desired air changes per hour (ACH):
- Standard use: 4 ACH
- Allergy sufferers: 5-6 ACH
- Smoke or heavy pet odors: 6+ ACH

**Step 3:** Calculate minimum CADR needed:
- CADR = (Room sq ft × Ceiling height in feet × ACH) / 60

**Example:** 300 sq ft bedroom, 9ft ceiling, 5 ACH = (300 × 9 × 5) / 60 = 225 CFM minimum.

**Always size up:** A purifier running at 60% fan speed is quieter and filters more efficiently than one running at 100%.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I run my air purifier?**
Run it continuously on auto mode or low speed. Air purifiers are most effective when running constantly — particles resuspend when the unit is off.

**How often do HEPA filters need replacement?**
Every 12 months under continuous use. Pre-carbon filters every 3–6 months. Note the filter light indicator and don't exceed replacement intervals — old filters can become particle sources.

**Do air purifiers help with COVID and virus particles?**
HEPA filters capture virus-carrying aerosol droplets (0.1–1µm) effectively. This doesn't eliminate transmission risk but meaningfully reduces airborne particle concentration. For virus concern, prioritize high CADR and frequent air changes.

**Are ionizers safe?**
Most air purifiers with ionizers/PlasmaWave produce trace ozone levels well within EPA and CARB limits. If you're sensitive to ozone or have respiratory conditions, choose a purifier with ionizer disable option (Winix) or pure filtration (Blueair).
