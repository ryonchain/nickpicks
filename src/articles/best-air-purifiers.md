Poor indoor air quality contributes to allergy flare-ups, worse sleep, and lower concentration — and most people don't realize their indoor air is measurably worse than outdoor air. A HEPA air purifier addresses the problem directly: run one in the room where you spend the most time and you'll notice the difference within a few days. The challenge is sorting through dozens of models with confusing specs.

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) is the number that matters. It measures how much filtered air the purifier delivers per minute in cubic feet. A CADR of 100+ is sufficient for a 200 sq ft room; larger rooms need proportionally higher CADR. Here are the best air purifiers for small rooms in 2026.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose

**CADR is the key spec.** A CADR of 1 CFM per 1.5 sq ft of room area is a reliable baseline for five air changes per hour. Calculate your room size and match accordingly — an undersized unit will run on high constantly and wear out faster.

**HEPA certification.** Look for "True HEPA" or "H13 HEPA" certification. "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-style" filters are not held to the same standard and may miss a significant fraction of small particles.

**Noise level.** Noise matters most for bedrooms. Anything below 30dB is practically inaudible. Check the spec at the fan speed you'll actually run — many purifiers are quiet on low but loud on high. For bedroom use, the Levoit Core 300 at 24dB on low is the quietest option in this list.

**Filter cost.** A cheap purifier with expensive filters can cost more over time than a pricier unit with economical replacements. Calculate annual filter cost before buying — the Coway and Levoit both run under $25/year.
