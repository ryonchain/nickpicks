## How Active Noise Cancellation Works

ANC uses microphones to sample external noise and generates an inverse sound wave that cancels it before it reaches your ears. Quality depends on:

**Microphone count and placement** — more microphones picking up sound from more angles = better cancellation. Sony XM5 uses 8 microphones.

**Processor speed** — the cancellation signal must be generated and played faster than the sound reaches your ear. Faster chips = better cancellation at higher frequencies.

**Fit and seal** — passive isolation (the earcup seal against your head) is half of all noise blocking. Poor seal = poor ANC performance regardless of the chip.

**Adaptive vs fixed ANC** — adaptive systems detect your environment and adjust ANC depth automatically. Sony and Bose both offer this.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can ANC damage your ears?**
No. ANC generates a counterwave at very low amplitude — below threshold for hearing damage. Some people find it creates an "pressure" sensation, which is perception-based and varies by individual.

**How long do noise-canceling headphones last?**
3–5 years for premium models with normal daily use. Battery capacity degrades over cycles — after 2 years expect ~20% reduction in max battery life.

**Should I get IEMs instead of over-ear ANC headphones?**
In-ear monitors with foam tips provide excellent passive isolation that rivals ANC for stationary use. They're lighter and more portable. Over-ear ANC wins for long-wear comfort and consistent performance without perfect seal.

**Does ANC work for voices?**
Partially. Low-frequency sounds (rumble, hum, AC) are cancelled very effectively. Higher-frequency sounds like voices are reduced but not eliminated by any headphone.
