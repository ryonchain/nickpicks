## Reviews

### Sony WH-1000XM6 — Best Overall

Sony's flagship remains the ANC benchmark. The XM6 refines everything the XM5 did well: multipoint pairing (connect to two devices simultaneously), speak-to-chat that pauses music when you talk, and industry-leading ANC that absorbs low-frequency hum (planes, AC, traffic) completely.

**Pros:** Best ANC in class; excellent sound with LDAC Hi-Res support; 30h battery; comfortable even for 8-hour sessions.
**Cons:** Microphone call quality is mediocre in wind; plastic build feels less premium than competitors.

**Verdict:** The one to beat. Buy if you prioritize ANC and sound.

### Bose QuietComfort 45 — Best Comfort

Bose invented the ANC headphone category and the QC45 remains the comfort champion. The ear cushions are the softest in the business; many people wear them 10+ hours without fatigue. ANC is excellent — not quite Sony's level for deep bass rumble, but better for mid-frequency noise (voices, keyboards).

**Pros:** Most comfortable over-ears available; reliable ANC; balanced sound tuning.
**Cons:** No LDAC; no swiveling ear cups; premium-priced for a somewhat dated feature set.

**Verdict:** The first choice for anyone who wears headphones all day.

### Apple AirPods Max — Best for Apple Users

AirPods Max offer premium aluminum construction, exceptional spatial audio, and seamless switching across iPhone, iPad, and Mac. ANC is very good — trailing Sony slightly in raw blocking power but far ahead in transparency mode naturalness.

**Pros:** Premium build quality; exceptional spatial audio; instant Apple device switching; H2 chip integration.
**Cons:** Very expensive; Lightning/USB-C case is awkward; heavy at 385g.

**Verdict:** Worth every penny if you're all-in on Apple — but a tough sell otherwise.

### Anker Soundcore Q45 — Best Budget Pick

For $60, the Q45 delivers ANC that genuinely works. It won't vanish airplane engine noise like the Sony, but it tames office chatter, AC hum, and open-plan keyboards effectively. Critically, it sounds great for the price with punchy bass and clear mids.

**Pros:** Excellent value; 50h battery; decent ANC for under $100.
**Cons:** Plasticky build; ANC noticeably weaker than premium options; mediocre mic.

**Verdict:** The gateway ANC headphone — the Sony XM6 upgrade will feel like magic afterward.

### Jabra Evolve2 75 — Best for Remote Work

The Evolve2 75 is engineered specifically for calls. Eight microphones with advanced beamforming make your voice crystal clear even in loud environments. ANC performance rivals the Sony. Battery hits 36 hours.

**Pros:** Industry-leading microphones; excellent ANC; USB dongle for stable conference connections; long battery.
**Cons:** Office-focused design is less stylish; expensive; heavier than consumer options.

**Verdict:** The headphone to buy if your job runs on Zoom and Teams.

## Buying Guide

**ANC quality tiers:**
- **Excellent:** Sony XM6, Bose QC45, Jabra Evolve2 75 — silent offices, manageable airplane cabins
- **Good:** Anker Q45 — quieter environment, won't vanish jet engine noise

**Battery life:** 20h is the minimum for all-day use. 30h+ recommended for travel.

**Codec support:** LDAC (Sony) allows Hi-Res audio over Bluetooth. AAC is Apple's equivalent. Standard SBC is fine for most.

**Multipoint pairing** (connect to phone + laptop simultaneously) is now nearly standard — confirm before buying.

## Bottom Line

The **Sony WH-1000XM6** wins on specs. The **Bose QC45** wins on comfort. The **Anker Q45** wins on value. Pick based on what matters most to your daily use.
