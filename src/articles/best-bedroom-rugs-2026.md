# Best Bedroom Rugs in 2026

The bedroom is the best place for a plush, high-pile rug — you won't be spilling food here, and the traffic is light. Softness underfoot when stepping out of bed in the morning is the primary function. The secondary function: defining the sleeping area and adding warmth to a room that often has hardwood or tile floors.

---

## Sizing Guide for Bedrooms

Correct sizing is critical for bedroom rugs.

**Queen bed (60"x80"):**
- **8x10 rug:** 2 feet of rug visible on each side and at the foot of the bed. Feels balanced and complete.
- **5x8 rug:** Rug appears cramped; only bottom third of bed sits on it.
- **Rule:** Go 8x10 for queen beds in rooms that can fit it.

**King bed (76"x80"):**
- **9x12 rug:** 2 feet on each side, 2 feet at foot. Ideal.
- **8x10 rug:** 1 foot on each side — still works in smaller rooms.

**Partial rug under bed foot only:**
- Two runner rugs (2.5'x8') placed on each side of the bed, not under it. Common for low ceilings or when headboard sits against a wall where full rug won't fit.

## Rug Care for Bedroom Rugs

**Shag rugs:**
- Vacuum weekly with suction-only attachment (no beater bar)
- Shake outside monthly
- Spot clean with mild soap and water
- Dry completely before replacing (lay flat in sun)

**Low-pile rugs:**
- Standard vacuum, all settings
- Spot clean or professional clean for deep stains
- Annual professional cleaning optional

## Do You Need a Rug Pad Under a Bedroom Rug?

- Adds extra cushion underfoot
- Extends rug life by reducing friction against the floor
