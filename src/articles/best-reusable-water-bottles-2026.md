## How to Choose a Reusable Water Bottle

### Insulated vs. non-insulated

If you care about keeping water cold for more than 2–3 hours, get a vacuum-insulated stainless bottle (Hydro Flask, YETI, Owala). If you just want a durable everyday bottle and don't mind refilling with cold water throughout the day, a Nalgene saves money and weight.

### Lid type matters more than people expect

Lids determine daily usability. Screw-top lids (YETI Chug Cap) give fast flow but require two hands. Flip straw lids (Stanley IceFlow) are best for desks and cars. Dual-mode lids (Owala FreeSip) are the most versatile. Magnetic lids (CamelBak Chute Mag) are best for active use. Choose based on where and how you drink most often.

### Capacity vs. portability trade-off

21–24 oz: fits in any bag pocket and most car cup holders; needs refilling 2–3× per day.
30–32 oz: fewer refills; may not fit narrow bag pockets.
40+ oz (Stanley Quencher): full-day capacity but large — best suited for desk or car use.

### Mouth size and cleaning

Wide-mouth bottles (Nalgene, YETI, CamelBak) are easier to clean thoroughly and accept ice cubes. Narrow-mouth bottles are easier to drink from while walking or active. Most quality bottles work with long bottle brushes for deep cleaning.
