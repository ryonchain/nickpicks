## Priority 3 — Common Over-Packing Mistakes

These are things most packing lists include that you probably don't need:

**Extra towels (beyond 3):** Dorm closets don't hold 6 towels. Start with 2-3 and add later if needed.

**Full set of dishes/pots/pans:** Unless you have a full kitchen, a mug, bowl, and plate are enough. Wait to see if your building has a kitchen.

**Iron and ironing board:** Steam wrinkle-removal sprays ($8) eliminate most needs. Save the space.

**Printer:** Most campus libraries print for free or pennies per page. Wait until you confirm you actually need one.

**Desk chair:** Your dorm room already has one. If it's uncomfortable, a seat cushion ($20) fixes it without floor-space cost.

**Excessive decor:** One tapestry, string lights, and a few photos beat 40 decorative items you'll have nowhere to put.

## When to Pack and Ship

**Ship early:** Services like Campus Ship and Dorm Delivery allow packages to arrive before you do. Heavy items (books, appliances, extra bedding) ship cheaper than they fly.

**Pack last-minute items day-of:** Electronics, medications, toiletries, and phone chargers should go in your personal carry bag — not checked luggage or shipped boxes.

**What to buy when you arrive:** Wait to see your actual room before buying: floor mats, extra furniture, and specialty organization items that depend on layout.

The biggest mistake: bringing too much and spending the first weekend returning or storing things. Under-pack for the first week, then fill gaps with targeted Amazon orders.
