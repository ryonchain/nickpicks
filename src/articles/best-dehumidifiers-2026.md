## Understanding Dehumidifier Sizing

Capacity is measured in pints of water removed per day (at 80°F/60% RH). Key guidelines:

| Space Size | Humidity Level | Recommended Capacity |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Small room (500 sq ft) | Moderately damp | 20 pint |
| Medium space (1,000 sq ft) | Damp | 30 pint |
| Large space (2,500 sq ft) | Very damp | 50 pint |
| Whole home (4,500+ sq ft) | Extremely wet | 70 pint |

Note: DOE testing standards changed in 2019 — modern "50-pint" units were previously rated at 70-pint. A current 50-pint unit replaces an older 70-pint unit.

## Signs You Need a Dehumidifier

1. **Musty smell** in basement, closet, or bathroom
2. **Condensation on windows** regularly
3. **Visible mold** in corners or behind furniture
4. **Warping wood** furniture, doors, or floors
5. **Dust mite allergies** worsening (dust mites thrive above 50% humidity)
6. **Paint peeling** from walls or ceiling
7. **Rust forming** on tools, appliances, or furniture
8. **Humidity consistently above 60%** (check with a hygrometer, ~$10)

## Features to Prioritize

**Auto-humidistat:** Automatically cycles on/off to maintain your target humidity. Essential — without it you're manually managing a machine.

**Continuous drain:** Connects a hose to a floor drain. Eliminates bucket emptying — critical for basement units running continuously in summer.

**Auto-restart:** Resumes operation after power outages at previous settings. Important for always-on basement units.

**Auto-defrost:** Required if your space drops below 65°F — prevents coil icing that damages the compressor.

**Energy Star certification:** Confirms energy efficiency; saves ~$20-30/year in electricity.

## Operating Tips

- **Set target humidity:** 45-50% for living spaces; 50-55% for basements
- **Keep doors closed:** Dehumidifiers work best in enclosed spaces
- **Clean filter monthly:** Clogged filters reduce efficiency and can cause overheating
- **Position correctly:** 6-12 inches from walls for best airflow
- **Use continuous drain in summer:** Humidity load is heaviest June-August

## FAQ

**Q: Should I run my dehumidifier continuously?**
A: In humid climates (Southeast US, Pacific Northwest) during summer, yes. In winter, most basements don't need dehumidification — indoor air is already dry from heating.

**Q: Where should I put my dehumidifier?**
A: In the dampest area of the home — typically the basement or crawl space. For multiple problem areas, consider a unit per zone.

**Q: What humidity level is ideal?**
A: 45-55% for living areas; 50-60% for basements (too low and wood framing can dry out and crack).

**Q: How much does it cost to run a dehumidifier?**
A: A 50-pint Energy Star unit costs approximately $25-35/month running 12 hours/day at average US electricity rates.

**Q: Can a dehumidifier fix mold?**
A: A dehumidifier prevents new mold growth by eliminating the moisture mold needs. Existing mold requires physical remediation (scrubbing or professional removal).
