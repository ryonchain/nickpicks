## Why Chest Straps Beat Wrist Monitors for Exercise

During high-intensity exercise, wrist optical monitors face two major problems:

1. **Motion artifact** — the wrist moves substantially during running and cycling, creating vibration noise in the optical signal that algorithms can't fully correct.
2. **Peripheral vasoconstriction** — during intense effort, the body redirects blood away from extremities, reducing the optical signal strength at the wrist.

These effects cause wrist monitors to lag behind actual heart rate by 10–30 seconds during interval efforts and to report artificially low peak HR during hard efforts. Chest straps don't have either problem — ECG signal quality is unaffected by limb movement or vasoconstriction.

**Practical implication:** If you do zone 4 or 5 interval training, a chest strap is not optional — your wrist monitor is giving you unreliable data at the moments that matter most.

**Do chest strap heart rate monitors work with Apple Watch?**
