Under $300, the **Vizio M512a-H6** (~$280) delivers genuine Dolby Atmos performance that outperforms far more expensive soundbars from three years ago.

## The Best Soundbars in 2026

### 1. Sonos Arc Ultra — Best Premium Soundbar

**Price:** ~$900 | [Check price on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=sonos+arc+ultra+soundbar&tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"}

The Sonos Arc Ultra is the soundbar that people who've owned multiple premium soundbars end up recommending. It combines 14 drivers (including four upward-firing drivers for Atmos height), Sonos' proprietary Sound Motion technology for deep bass without a subwoofer, and the best multi-room audio ecosystem in the business.

**What makes it worth $900:**
- 14-driver array: 9 tweeters, 4 upward-firing mid-woofers, 1 center speech driver
- Sound Motion: patent-pending technology produces bass at frequencies below what the driver size suggests — no subwoofer needed for most content
- TruePlay acoustic tuning: uses your iPhone microphone to calibrate the soundbar to your room automatically
- Dolby Atmos and DTS:X support
- Apple AirPlay 2, Spotify Connect, Alexa built-in, Google Assistant (via phone)
- Arc Ultra + Sub 4 + Era 300 surrounds: upgrades to a full 9.1.4 system
- eARC HDMI connection for lossless Atmos audio from compatible TVs
- HDR10+ passthrough

**The one criticism:** No HDMI input passthrough (HDMI ARC out only). If your TV has only one HDMI ARC port and you want to connect a cable box or game console through the soundbar, you'll need a separate switch.

**Ideal for:** Anyone who wants the best-sounding single-bar soundbar available without adding a subwoofer or rear speakers, and plans to potentially expand into a Sonos multi-room system.

### 2. Samsung HW-Q990D — Best Full Home Theater Soundbar System

**Price:** ~$1,200 | [Check price on Amazon →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=samsung+hw-q990d+soundbar&tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"}

The Samsung HW-Q990D is an 11.1.4 channel system: 11 full-range speakers, 1 subwoofer, and 4 upward-firing Atmos drivers, including detachable wireless rear speakers with their own upward-firing units. It's the closest thing to a full home theater speaker system in a soundbar package.

**Why it's the most immersive option:**
- 11.1.4 channel configuration — the most channels of any soundbar on this list
- Wireless rear speakers with built-in upward-firing drivers (true surround + overhead)
- 656W total output — enough for large living rooms and open-plan spaces
- SpaceFit Sound Pro: analyzes your room and applies EQ correction automatically
- Q-Symphony 3.0: syncs the soundbar with compatible Samsung TV speakers for combined output
- Dolby Atmos + DTS:X + Samsung 360 Auto-surround
- Game Pro mode: optimized EQ for gaming with reduced audio latency
- HDMI eARC + optical input

**vs. Sonos Arc Ultra:** The Samsung wins on raw power, channel count, and surround immersion. The Sonos wins on sound quality per dollar, music performance, and multi-room ecosystem. If you watch lots of action movies and blockbusters in a larger room, the Samsung's physical rear speakers deliver a more convincing surround experience than any single soundbar.

**Ideal for:** Dedicated home theater rooms where immersion is the priority and budget allows for the full system.

- Built-in dual subwoofers (no external sub required)

The Vizio M512a-H6 is the clearest over-performer in this category. A 5.1.2 channel system — soundbar + wireless subwoofer + dual satellite speakers — for under $300 delivers an actual surround sound experience, not a simulated one. The separate sub handles low frequencies independently, and the satellite speakers create real left/right surround without virtual processing tricks.

**Why it beats soundbars twice its price from 3 years ago:**
- True 5.1.2 channel system: physical front, surround, and upward-firing speakers
- Wireless subwoofer: place it anywhere in the room

**Ideal for:** Apartments, smaller living rooms, or anyone upgrading from a TV's built-in speakers without the budget for a premium system.

### By TV brand

| TV Brand | Best Soundbar | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|

### Must I have a subwoofer?

Most soundbars above $500 include a wireless subwoofer or have strong built-in bass (Sonos Arc Ultra, Sony HT-A7000). Under $300, the Vizio M512a-H6 includes a wireless sub. The Amazon Echo Studio is the only option here without a dedicated subwoofer — it compensates with good low-frequency extension from its 5.25" woofer, but bass-heavy content (action movies, EDM) benefits from a dedicated sub.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best soundbar for most people?**
The Vizio M512a-H6 is the best value soundbar for most rooms — Dolby Atmos, 5.1.2 channels, and wireless subwoofer under $300. For premium home theater, the Sonos Arc Ultra delivers the best Atmos height effects of any single-bar system.

**How much should I spend on a soundbar?**
Spend $150–300 for a meaningful upgrade from your TV's built-in speakers. Under $150, quality drops significantly. The $300–600 range adds a wireless subwoofer and better Atmos. Above $600, you're paying for Sonos/Sony ecosystem features and premium build — worthwhile for dedicated home theaters.

**Is Dolby Atmos worth it on a soundbar?**
Yes, at $250+. Entry-level Atmos bars fire audio upward to simulate height — the effect varies by room ceiling height and materials. The Sonos Arc Ultra and Samsung Q990D deliver noticeably immersive height effects; budget Atmos bars are subtler but still better than non-Atmos for movies.

**Which soundbar works best with Samsung TVs?**
Samsung's own HW-Q990D integrates deepest with Samsung TVs via Q-Symphony, which syncs the TV speakers and soundbar for a wider soundstage. The Sonos Arc also pairs well via eARC. For budget Samsung pairings, any soundbar with HDMI eARC will work reliably.

**Are soundbars better than TV speakers?**
Almost always yes. Even a $100 soundbar delivers better dialogue clarity, more bass, and wider stereo separation than built-in TV speakers, which are limited by thin chassis and rear-facing drivers. A soundbar with a dedicated subwoofer completely transforms the home theater experience.
