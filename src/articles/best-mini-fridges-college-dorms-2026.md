## Size Guide: How Big a Mini Fridge Do You Need?

| Situation | Recommended Size |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Solo student, minimal food storage | 1.7–2.5 cu ft |
| Shared between 2 students | 3.0–4.5 cu ft |
| Heavy meal-prepper or longer periods without dining hall | 4.5+ cu ft |

Most students sharing a fridge are happy with 3.2–4.5 cu ft. Under 2.5 cu ft runs out of space fast if two people are both keeping food there.

**Energy note:** Many dorms require ENERGY STAR rated mini fridges. Check your dorm policy before ordering. All picks on this list are ENERGY STAR certified.

## Energy and Dorm Policy Notes

**ENERGY STAR:** Most college dorms that regulate mini fridges require ENERGY STAR certification. Every fridge on this list qualifies. When in doubt, check your dorm's specific policy — some specify a maximum wattage or size limit.

**Noise:** Compressor-based fridges create a low-frequency hum. In a quiet dorm room at night, this is noticeable. All models on this list have been selected for relatively quiet operation, but no mini fridge is silent.

**Defrosting:** Most mini fridges require manual defrosting once or twice per year. If ice builds up in the freezer compartment, unplug for 24 hours with a towel underneath. Don't use sharp objects to chip ice — they damage the refrigerant tubing.
