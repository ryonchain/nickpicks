## How to Choose a Wireless Home Security System

### Monthly Fee vs. No Monthly Fee

Every system on this list works without a subscription, but "no monthly fee" has degrees. Abode, Eufy, and Wyze give you full self-monitoring for free. Ring and SimpliSafe offer free self-monitoring but lock smart home integrations (like Alexa arming) behind a plan. Read the fine print before you buy — the "free" tier should cover at minimum: app alerts, remote arm/disarm, and local siren triggering.

### Cellular Backup Matters More Than You Think

A WiFi-only system goes dark the moment your router loses power or an intruder cuts your internet. Cellular backup keeps the hub communicating even through an outage. Abode, Ring, and SimpliSafe all offer cellular backup (built-in on Ring and SimpliSafe; module add-on on Abode). Eufy and Wyze are WiFi-only — pair them with a UPS battery backup for your router if this concerns you.

### Ecosystem Compatibility

If you own Alexa devices, lean toward Ring. If you're invested in Apple HomeKit, Abode is the only system here with native HomeKit support. If you prefer a closed loop with cameras and sensors all in one app, Eufy's self-contained approach is cleanest. Choose the system that fits the smart home platform you already use.

### Sensor Count and Coverage

A typical two-bedroom home needs at minimum: entry sensors on every exterior door (usually 2–3), one motion sensor covering the main living area, and a keypad near the front door. Most kits here cover that baseline. Budget an extra $15–$30 per additional door or window sensor if you want full perimeter coverage.
