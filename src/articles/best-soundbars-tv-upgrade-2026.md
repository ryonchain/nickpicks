Your TV's audio is holding back your viewing experience. Modern flat panels are too thin to house meaningful speakers — the drivers fire sideways or backward, dialogue gets muddied, and action sequences sound flat. A soundbar solves this with a single horizontal speaker bar that sits in front of your TV and delivers wider, fuller audio.

This guide focuses on **upgrade soundbars** — picks that deliver the biggest audio improvement for the simplest setup, without requiring an AV receiver or separate rear speakers.

**The short answer:** For most TV upgrades, the **Yamaha SR-B20A** (~$150) is the best single purchase you can make. It connects via one HDMI ARC cable, handles dialogue and movie audio better than soundbars twice its price, and works with any TV remote automatically.

The Yamaha SR-B20A consistently outperforms its price class on the metrics that matter most for TV audio: dialogue clarity, soundstage width, and bass extension from a single bar. Yamaha's Clear Voice technology enhances center-channel frequency response specifically to make speech intelligible — a problem most budget soundbars ignore.

**Key specs and features:**
- DTS Virtual:X and Dolby Atmos processing for virtual height effects
- Clear Voice technology for enhanced dialogue clarity
- HDMI ARC input for one-cable TV connection
- Optical digital input as backup
- Bluetooth 4.2 for music streaming
- Works with any TV remote via HDMI-CEC volume control
- 120W total output — fills rooms up to 400 sq ft effectively

**Why it's the best upgrade pick:**
The SR-B20A doesn't require any app setup or complex pairing. Connect one HDMI cable to your TV's ARC port, select the correct input, and you're done. Most users report their TV remote automatically controls the soundbar volume within seconds. Yamaha's tuning emphasizes movie and TV dialogue rather than boomy bass performance, which is exactly what most TV upgrades need.

**The trade-off:** No dedicated subwoofer included. For rooms under 300 sq ft and casual viewers, the built-in low-frequency extension is adequate. Action movie enthusiasts will eventually want to add a subwoofer — Yamaha sells the NS-SW050 ($130) as a compatible add-on.

**Ideal for:** Anyone upgrading from built-in TV speakers who wants the biggest improvement for the least setup effort.

### Do you need Atmos?

For TV viewing, virtual Dolby Atmos (as opposed to true multi-speaker Atmos) is a modest improvement over well-tuned stereo. It adds subtle height cues in compatible streaming content. All five soundbars on this list support it. **Don't make Atmos the primary purchase criterion** — dialogue clarity and soundstage width matter more for everyday TV watching.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the easiest soundbar to set up for a TV upgrade?**
The Yamaha SR-B20A is the easiest — one HDMI ARC cable connects to your TV, auto-syncs volume with your remote, and sounds dramatically better than built-in speakers within minutes. No app required, no pairing, just plug and play.

**Do I need a subwoofer with a soundbar?**
For movies and sports, a wireless subwoofer makes a noticeable difference in bass punch and explosions. The Polk Audio Signa S4 bundles a wireless subwoofer for under $300. If space is tight, the Yamaha SR-B20A has solid built-in bass and skips the extra box.

**Can any soundbar work with any TV?**
Yes — any soundbar with HDMI ARC or optical input works with any modern TV. HDMI ARC also lets you control the soundbar volume with your TV remote. For smart TVs made after 2020, HDMI eARC delivers better sound quality (including lossless Dolby Atmos).

**How much improvement does a soundbar make over built-in TV speakers?**
Significant. Even a $100 soundbar widens the soundstage, improves dialogue clarity, and adds bass that thin TV panels physically cannot produce. Most people report the upgrade as more impactful than going from 1080p to 4K.

**Are LG soundbars better with LG TVs?**
Yes — LG soundbars paired with LG TVs unlock LG Sound Sync (wireless audio), AI Sound Pro processing, and combined TV-plus-soundbar EQ. The LG S65Q offers the cleanest LG-to-LG integration. Non-LG TVs work fine via HDMI ARC but miss the ecosystem bonuses.
