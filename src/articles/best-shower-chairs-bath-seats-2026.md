## What to Look For

**Weight capacity:** Get at least 50 lbs more than the user's weight. Never use a chair at its maximum rated load routinely.

**Legs:** Rubber tips are non-negotiable. Check that all four tips are in contact with the floor before sitting.

**Seat:** Drain holes are important — water pooling on the seat gets cold fast and is slippery. A contoured seat is more comfortable for extended sitting.

**Transfer bench vs. chair:** If the user has a tub (not a walk-in shower), a transfer bench is safer than trying to step over the tub wall. For walk-in showers, a standard chair or fold-down seat works well.

## Accessories Worth Having

- **Handheld shower head:** Essential when showering seated — allows rinsing without standing. Target 60–72" hose length.
- **Shower caddy:** A small hanging caddy or suction-cup shelf keeps shampoo and soap within reach without bending.
- **Non-slip mat:** Place inside the tub/shower, not under the chair legs — the chair legs should grip the floor directly.
