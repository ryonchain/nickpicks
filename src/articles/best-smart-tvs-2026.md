## Smart TV Buying Guide

**Size recommendations by room:**
- Bedroom (8–12 ft viewing): 43–55"
- Living room (10–15 ft): 55–75"
- Large living room/media room (15+ ft): 75–85"+

**Panel technology explained:**
- **OLED:** Self-emitting pixels = perfect blacks, infinite contrast. Best picture. Risk of burn-in with static images.
- **QD-OLED:** OLED + quantum dots = brighter OLED with vivid colors (Samsung, Sony).
- **Mini-LED (QLED):** LED backlighting with hundreds to thousands of zones. Bright, no burn-in risk. Some light bloom.
- **Standard LED:** Entry-level backlighting. Good value, lower contrast.

**Refresh rate:**
- 60Hz: Fine for casual viewing and streaming.
- 120Hz+: Noticeably smoother for sports and essential for gaming.

**HDMI 2.1 for gaming:** Required for 4K/120Hz gaming with PS5 or Xbox Series X. Check that your TV has HDMI 2.1 inputs.

## FAQ

**Is OLED worth it over QLED?**
For dark-room home theater viewing — yes. For bright living rooms or those concerned about static-image burn-in, a bright QLED Mini-LED may be a better fit.

**How long do smart TVs last?**
Typically 7–10 years for the panel. OLED brightness can diminish slightly over thousands of hours; QLED panels are more stable long-term.

**Do I need a soundbar?**
For immersive audio, yes. Built-in TV speakers are fine for casual use, but a soundbar ($100–$300) makes a significant difference for movies.

**Which smart TV OS is best?**
Google TV (TCL, Hisense) has the most apps and best integration with Android. WebOS (LG) is intuitive and polished. Tizen (Samsung) is fast but more proprietary.
