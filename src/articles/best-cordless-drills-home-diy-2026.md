## What to Look for in a Cordless Drill

### Brushed vs. Brushless Motor
| | Brushed | Brushless |
|--|---------|-----------|
| Cost | Lower | Higher |
| Efficiency | Less efficient | 50% more runtime per charge |
| Maintenance | Carbon brushes wear and need replacement | No maintenance |
| Torque | Good | Better across RPM range |
| **Recommendation** | Budget only | Choose brushless if budget allows |

### Voltage: 18V/20V vs. 12V
- **12V compact:** For pure screwdriving and light drilling; significantly lighter
- **18V/20V:** Full power for drilling through hardwoods, concrete (with hammer mode), and driving large fasteners

Note: 20V MAX (DeWalt, BLACK+DECKER) and 18V (Milwaukee, RYOBI, Makita) refer to nominal vs. maximum battery voltage — they're comparable in output.

### Chuck Size
Most drills use a 1/2" chuck. Some compact models use 3/8". The 1/2" chuck accepts larger bits and provides better grip for demanding use. Keyless chucks (no key required to tighten) are standard on all modern drills.

### Battery Platform Investment
Once you own 2+ batteries from a platform, switching brands means replacing all your batteries. Think platform-first if you're planning to add tools. Milwaukee M18, DeWalt 20V MAX, and RYOBI ONE+ are the most versatile ecosystems for home use.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need both a drill and an impact driver?**
A: For most homeowners, a drill handles 80% of tasks. An impact driver adds value for driving large quantities of screws quickly (deck building, subfloor installation) but isn't essential for occasional home repair.

**Q: What's the difference between a drill and a hammer drill?**
A: A hammer drill adds a hammering action for drilling into concrete and masonry. Most homeowners working on occasional anchor installation can manage with a standard drill + masonry bit + patience. A hammer drill significantly speeds up concrete drilling.

**Q: How long do cordless drill batteries last?**
A: Lithium-ion batteries typically handle 500–1,000 charge cycles. For casual home use (10 charges per year), a quality battery lasts 50–100 years theoretically — but cell degradation means 5–8 years is more realistic before capacity drops noticeably.

**Q: What drill bit should I buy for a beginner?**
A: A 21-piece combination drill/driver bit set ($15–25 from DEWALT or Bosch) covers 90% of home use: standard twist bits, spade bits, Phillips/flat/Torx drivers. Buy quality bits — cheap bits strip and dull quickly.
