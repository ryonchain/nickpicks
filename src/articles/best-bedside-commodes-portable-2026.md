# Best Bedside Commodes and Portable Toilets in 2026

A bedside commode provides nighttime toilet access without the hazard of walking to the bathroom — critical for post-surgical recovery, late-stage illness, or anyone at fall risk. They can also be used directly over a standard toilet as a raised toilet seat with armrests. The key specs: height range, weight capacity, bucket type (removable for cleaning), and whether it folds for travel or storage.

## Cleaning and Maintenance

- Remove the bucket after each use when possible (or at least daily)
- Rinse bucket with warm water, then disinfect with a solution of 1 tablespoon bleach per quart of water
- Wipe the seat and armrests with disinfecting wipes
- Check leg tips regularly — replace if worn (can slide on hard floors)

---

## Raised Toilet Seats vs. Commodes

If the primary need is making the toilet easier to get on/off (not bedroom access), a **raised toilet seat with handles** is simpler:

- Raised toilet seats bolt onto the existing toilet rim
- Add 3"–6" of height
- Available with or without armrests
- No bucket to clean — user flushes normally

**Recommendation:** If the user can walk to the bathroom safely, a raised toilet seat is simpler. If nighttime bathroom trips are a fall risk or if getting to the bathroom is difficult, use a commode at the bedside.
