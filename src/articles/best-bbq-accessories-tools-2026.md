Great BBQ is 50% technique and 50% having the right tools at the right moment. The accessories that actually move the needle are the ones that give you control — temperature monitoring, fire management, surface preparation. The rest is nice to have.

This list covers the BBQ accessories worth buying in 2026, from the single upgrade that will most improve your grilling (a real thermometer) to the tools that make the experience more enjoyable.

---

## 1. ThermoWorks Thermapen ONE — Best Instant-Read Thermometer

The single most important BBQ upgrade: a fast, accurate meat thermometer. The Thermapen ONE reads in 1 second at ±0.5°F accuracy. Rotates to any angle, automatically turns on, IP67 waterproof, 3,000-hour battery. ThermoWorks makes the equipment used in commercial kitchens and food science labs. When you pull a steak at exactly 135°F instead of guessing, you understand why this costs $100.

**Price:** $100–110  
**Best for:** Anyone serious about food temperature, steaks, poultry, fish, pork  
**Why it's #1:** A digital thermometer is the single change that most improves grilling outcomes

---

## 2. Weber iGrill 3 Bluetooth Thermometer — Best App-Connected Probe Thermometer

Clips directly to Weber Spirit and Genesis grills. Monitors up to 4 meat probes simultaneously via Bluetooth to the Weber iGrill app on your phone. Set a target temperature, get a notification when your roast hits it. Stop lifting the lid to check. This is the thermometer for people who grill large cuts (brisket, roast, whole chicken) and don't want to babysit the grill.

**Price:** $70–90  
**Best for:** Weber grill owners, large cut cooking (brisket, whole chicken, roast), long cook times  
**Compatible with:** Weber Spirit II and Genesis series

---

## 3. MEATER Plus Long Range Wireless Probe — Best Wireless Probe

The MEATER Plus is fully wireless — no wires running from the probe to a transmitter on the grill. Insert the probe, close the lid, monitor from your phone from 165 feet away. Dual sensors (internal meat temp + ambient/grill temp). Guided cook system recommends resting time after cooking. The cleanest user experience in probe thermometers.

**Price:** $90–100  
**Best for:** Smokers, ovens, and any closed-lid cooking where wireless is critical, Kamado grill users  
**Limitation:** Battery must be charged (vs. AAA-powered alternatives)

## 4. Weber 6630 Grill Cleaning Brush — Best Grill Brush

Stainless steel bristle brush with a scraper, designed for porcelain and stainless grates. The 18" handle keeps hands away from heat. Weber quality means the bristles don't fall out — a legitimate safety concern with cheaper grill brushes. Clean the grates while hot, before and after cooking. This is the $15 item that should be on every grill.

**Price:** $12–18  
**Best for:** Gas and charcoal grills, all grate types  
**Safety note:** Replace any grill brush where bristles are loose — ingested bristles are a real medical risk

**Price:** $20–30  
**Best for:** All charcoal grillers, replacing lighter fluid method  
**Why it matters:** Lighter fluid leaves chemical residue on coals and transfers taste to food
