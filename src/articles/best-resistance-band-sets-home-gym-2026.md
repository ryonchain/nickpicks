## Resistance Band Buying Guide

### Loop vs Tube vs Flat: Which to Choose
- **Loop bands:** Best for lower body work, mobility, yoga, Pilates
- **Tube bands with handles:** Best for upper body cable exercises, full-body training
- **Flat bands:** Best for rehab, stretching, and shoulder/arm work
- **Fabric bands:** Best for hip hinge and glute work; don't roll or snap

### Stacking Resistance
With stackable tubes, you can combine them for heavier loads. But note: combined tension changes the elasticity curve. A 30 lb stack doesn't always feel the same as a single 30 lb band due to different stretch profiles.

### Resistance Level Guide
- **Extra Light (2–5 lbs):** Rehab, warm-up, face pulls
- **Light (5–15 lbs):** Shoulder work, biceps, lateral band walks
- **Medium (15–30 lbs):** Chest press, rows, banded squats
- **Heavy (30–50 lbs):** Lat pulldowns, Romanian deadlifts, hip thrusts
- **Extra Heavy (50+ lbs):** Loaded carries, power movements, heavy strength work

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do resistance bands actually build muscle?**
Yes, with caveats. Bands create mechanical tension (a primary muscle-building stimulus), but the resistance curve is different from free weights — highest tension at the end of range, lowest at the start. For beginners and intermediate lifters, bands build muscle effectively. Advanced lifters typically combine bands with free weights for comprehensive loading.

**How long do resistance bands last?**
Latex bands last 12–24 months with regular use before losing elasticity. Store out of direct sunlight (UV degrades latex). Don't stretch beyond 2.5× their resting length. Replace when you notice a loss of consistent resistance or cracking in the material.

**Are resistance bands safe for shoulders?**
Bands are often safer than free weights for shoulder work because they allow more natural wrist rotation through the movement. Physical therapists frequently use bands (especially flat TheraBands) for rotator cuff rehabilitation because of the controllable, low-impact loading pattern.
