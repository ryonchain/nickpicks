# Best Gun Safes in 2026

Gun safes serve two purposes: preventing unauthorized access (children, burglars) and preserving firearms during fire. The critical specs are steel gauge (lower number = thicker = harder to pry), lock type (biometric, key, combination), UL listing for fire rating (minutes/temperature), and California DOJ approval (the strictest state standard).

## Fire Rating Explained

- **No fire rating:** The safe is a security product, not fire protection. Plastic components inside will melt; electronics/documents will burn.
- **30 min / 1,200°F:** Average house fire burns at this temperature for about 20 minutes before fire department arrival. Sufficient for most areas.
- **60 min / 1,200°F:** For longer response times or higher risk.

**Documents and electronics:** Even fireproof gun safes are optimized for metal items. Printed documents and hard drives need a dedicated fireproof document bag or UL 125-rated data safe (interior temp stays under 125°F).
