## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Cordless Drill for Home Use

**Brushless vs. brushed motors.** Brushless motors are more efficient (20–30% more runtime per charge), generate less heat, and last significantly longer than brushed motors. They cost $30–$50 more. For any drill used more than occasionally, the brushless premium pays for itself in battery life and longevity.

**Voltage and torque.** 18V/20V MAX drills handle everything a homeowner needs. Higher voltage (40V, 60V) is for heavy construction use. For home use, focus on torque (in-lbs): 300–400 in-lbs covers furniture assembly, pilot holes, and decking. 400+ in-lbs handles lag screws and driving into hardwood.

**Chuck size.** 3/8" chucks accept most standard bits. 1/2" chucks accept larger bits and handle more torque — preferred for serious work. If you're hanging pictures, a 3/8" is fine. If you're building a deck, get 1/2".

**Battery platform.** Battery platforms matter more than the drill itself. Once you have two or three tools on the same platform, switching is expensive. DeWalt 20V MAX and Milwaukee M18 are the most premium. Ryobi ONE+ is the most affordable with the largest tool count. Pick a platform you'll stay with.

**Impact driver vs. drill.** A drill is for drilling holes and driving screws with finesse. An impact driver is for high-torque fastener driving (deck screws, furniture assembly). Many homeowners eventually own both — start with a drill, add an impact driver when you feel the need.

## FAQ

**What's the best cordless drill for a beginner homeowner?**
The DeWalt DCD777C2. It's lightweight, brushless, uses the industry's most common 20V MAX batteries, and handles every task a homeowner will face for years.

**Do I need a drill and an impact driver?**
Not immediately. A good drill handles most home tasks. If you regularly drive long deck screws, assemble lots of flat-pack furniture, or notice the drill stripping screws under load, add an impact driver. A combo kit (drill + impact driver) often costs only $30–$50 more than the drill alone.

**How long do cordless drill batteries last?**
With normal home use, lithium-ion batteries last 3–5 years. Store them at 50–80% charge in a cool, dry location. Avoid fully depleting batteries or leaving them on the charger indefinitely.

**What's the difference between 18V and 20V MAX?**
Marketing terminology for functionally equivalent systems. 20V MAX (DeWalt, Black+Decker) and 18V (Milwaukee, Makita, Ryobi) both deliver the same nominal 18V under load. The distinction reflects how each brand measures peak vs. nominal voltage — they're interchangeable in capability.

**What drill bits do I need for home projects?**
A 20-piece titanium twist bit set covers most drilling. Add a #2 Phillips, #2 square (Robertson), and T25 Torx for driving fasteners. A 5-piece spade bit set handles larger holes in wood. That covers 95% of home use cases.
