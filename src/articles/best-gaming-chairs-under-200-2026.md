## What to Look for in a Gaming Chair Under $200

**Frame Material:** Steel frames last longer than plastic. Look for "steel base" in specs.

**Cushion Type:** Cold-cure foam (Secretlab) > high-density foam > standard foam. Standard foam compresses flat within a year.

**Recline Range:** At minimum you want 90°–135°. Full 180° is a bonus.

**Weight Capacity:** If you're over 180 lbs, prioritize chairs rated 250 lbs or more.

**Armrest Adjustability:** 4D armrests (up/down/forward/back/pivot) are worth paying for. 1D armrests (height only) are barely better than fixed.

## Are Gaming Chairs Worth It vs. Office Chairs?

For sessions under 2 hours: probably not. A basic office chair with lumbar support does the same job.

For sessions over 3 hours daily: a gaming chair's contoured back and tiltable recline make a real difference. The neck/lumbar pillows also help if you're already dealing with neck strain.
