## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Dog Harness

**Front clip vs. back clip:**
- Front clip: Redirects pullers. Best for training. Leash tangles in legs less often than you'd expect.
- Back clip: Most comfortable for calm dogs. Good for running. Doesn't help with pulling.
- Dual clip: Maximum flexibility. Worth the extra cost if you want both.

**Fit matters more than price:** Measure your dog's chest girth (widest point behind the front legs) and neck. Most harnesses list both on the size chart.

**Escape-proof designs:** If your dog backs out of harnesses, look for H-style designs (Julius-K9, Ruffwear) that fit behind the front legs with a belly strap.
