# Best Laptop Stands in 2026: Top Picks for Desk Ergonomics and Portability

A laptop stand is one of the highest-return ergonomic purchases available. At their cheapest, they run $25–$40 and they immediately address the most common source of neck and upper back pain for laptop users: screen height. A laptop on a flat desk puts the display roughly 18 inches below comfortable eye level. Raising it to eye height with a stand, paired with an external keyboard and mouse, transforms a hunched laptop posture into a proper ergonomic workstation.

## Fixed vs. Adjustable vs. Portable: Matching Stand to Workflow

**Fixed-height stands** (like the Rain Design mStand) are designed for permanent desk placement. They're typically heavier, more stable, and better looking. The fixed height is optimized for the most common ergonomic position — approximately eye level when seated. If you work at the same desk every day, a fixed stand is the cleanest solution.

**Adjustable stands** offer multiple height positions, accommodating different desk heights, chair heights, and users. The best adjustable stands lock firmly at each position without wobble; the worst flex noticeably under typing pressure. Adjustability matters most if you share a desk, work at different heights, or sometimes prefer a reclined laptop position.

**Portable stands** prioritize packability. The best fold to a quarter their extended size. They sacrifice some stability for travel convenience but are still a dramatic improvement over bare desk or lap use. If you work from coffee shops, coworking spaces, or hotels as often as a dedicated desk, portable is worth the tradeoff.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Laptop Stand

### The External Keyboard Rule

A laptop stand only solves half the ergonomic equation. When you raise your screen to eye height, the laptop keyboard is now at face height — unusable for typing. An external keyboard and mouse are required to complete an ergonomic setup. Budget $40–$60 for a basic wireless keyboard and mouse if you don't already have them.

### Measuring the Right Height

Sit in your work chair at your normal position. Without a stand, note where your eyes rest relative to the screen. The goal is to raise the top of the display to approximately eye level, which means the screen face is tilted back slightly. Most users need 5–8 inches of lift. Measure from your desk surface to your natural eye level and subtract the height of your laptop's open display.

### Fixed vs. Adjustable for Shared Desks

If multiple people share a desk (different heights, different chairs), an adjustable stand is essential. If you're the only user at a fixed-height desk with a fixed-height chair, a well-positioned fixed stand is simpler and usually more stable.

### Cooling Considerations

Open-back or lattice designs allow more air underneath the laptop. This matters most for thin-and-light laptops with poor passive cooling. If your laptop fan runs loudly under normal load or you notice thermal throttling (processing slowing down during sustained work), prioritize an open-back stand design.
