## Buying Guide

### Switch Types: Choosing Your Feel

- **Linear switches (Red, Silver, Yellow):** Smooth keystrokes with no tactile bump. Fastest actuation, least noise. Preferred for gaming.
- **Tactile switches (Brown, Clear):** A noticeable bump at actuation. Popular for typing/gaming hybrid use. Not as fast as linear for gaming.
- **Clicky switches (Blue, Green):** Audible click + tactile bump. Satisfying for typing; loud for gaming environments and streaming.

For pure gaming: linear switches (Red or Speed Silver) are universally recommended.

### Layout: Form Factor Tradeoffs

- **Full-size (100%):** Complete keyboard with numpad. Best for work + gaming but uses most desk space.
- **Tenkeyless (TKL / 80%):** No numpad. More desk room for mouse movement. Most popular gaming layout.
- **75%:** Compact with F-row and arrow keys retained. Good balance of compactness and functionality.
- **65%:** No F-row. Very compact — popular for travel but requires Fn-layer for common keys.
- **60%:** Minimal. Arrow keys accessed via Fn layer. Preferred by minimalists and enthusiasts.

### Polling Rate: Does 8,000Hz Matter?

Standard keyboards poll at 1,000Hz (every 1ms). 8,000Hz keyboards poll every 0.125ms. In practice, 8,000Hz polling provides measurable input registration improvement only at very high mouse polling rates (8,000Hz mice). For most gamers, 1,000Hz keyboards and mice are sufficient.

### Hot-Swap PCBs: Worth It?

A hot-swap PCB lets you pull and replace switches without soldering. This is highly recommended: it lets you try different switches on the same board, replace a damaged switch easily, and upgrade switches as your preferences evolve.

## FAQ

### What switch is best for gaming?

Linear switches (Cherry MX Red, Gateron Red/Yellow, Kailh Speed Silver) are the standard recommendation for gaming: smooth actuation, low pre-travel, consistent keystroke feel. Speed switches (shorter pre-travel distance) offer marginally faster actuation.

### Are expensive mechanical keyboards worth it?

For gaming performance, a $50–100 board with a good linear switch and 1,000Hz polling is competitive with a $200 board. The price premium above $100 buys: build quality (aluminum vs. plastic), sound profile (gasket mount vs. top mount), switch consistency, and firmware features. For pure gaming input response, budget boards perform nearly identically to premium ones.

### Is wireless mechanical keyboard good for gaming?

Wired mode (via USB-C) on any wireless keyboard eliminates wireless latency entirely. Bluetooth polling rate (125Hz) is too slow for competitive gaming. For gaming, always use wired mode or a 2.4GHz dongle. Save Bluetooth for tablet/laptop use.

### How do I clean my mechanical keyboard?

Remove keycaps with a keycap puller, wipe the PCB with isopropyl alcohol on a soft cloth, clean keycaps in warm soapy water, and replace stabilizer lube annually. Do not use compressed air — it pushes debris into switch housings.
