## Buying Guide

### Platform Compatibility First

Before anything else, check what wireless protocol your console supports:
- **PS5:** USB wireless dongle or 3.5mm. No official Bluetooth gaming audio (Bluetooth is for Sony headsets only in latest firmware).
- **Xbox Series X/S:** Xbox Wireless protocol (no dongle), Bluetooth, or 3.5mm.
- **Nintendo Switch:** 3.5mm wired or Bluetooth (no 2.4GHz USB on handheld mode).
- **PC:** 2.4GHz USB dongle, Bluetooth, or wired USB/3.5mm.

### Virtual Surround Sound: What's Real vs. Marketing

True spatial audio requires both the headset and the platform/game to support the same standard:
- **Tempest 3D Audio** (PS5): Only meaningful with headsets processed through PS5's Tempest engine. PULSE 3D optimizes for this.
- **Dolby Atmos** (Xbox/PC): Works at the Windows/Xbox system level — many headsets support it.
- **Generic 7.1 virtual:** Software EQ simulation. Minimal benefit for most people. Can sometimes hurt soundstage accuracy.

For competitive gaming (hearing footsteps, positioning enemies), a quality stereo headset often performs better than cheap virtual surround implementations.

### Wired vs. Wireless for Gaming

Modern 2.4GHz wireless has essentially zero perceptible latency for gaming (5ms or less). The only meaningful advantage of wired is zero battery management. For console gaming, wireless is almost universally preferred. For competitive PC gaming, wired remains the professional standard — but more because of tradition than measurable latency difference.

### Microphone Quality: Why It Matters

If you play with a team or stream, your microphone quality affects others' experience. Look for:
- Noise-canceling (reduces keyboard, fan, and room noise)
- Frequency response specs (300 Hz–3.4 kHz covers clear voice intelligibility)
- Detachable or foldable boom arm (vs. built-in)

## FAQ

### Can I use a PS5 headset on Xbox or PC?

Most PS5 headsets use a USB dongle or 3.5mm connection — the USB dongle is typically PlayStation-licensed and won't pair with Xbox. The 3.5mm input works universally. For multi-platform use, choose a headset with broad platform support (SteelSeries, HyperX, Astro A50 X).

### Is surround sound worth it for gaming?

For immersive single-player games (horror, adventure, RPG), yes — spatial audio meaningfully improves atmosphere. For competitive multiplayer, the benefit of positional audio is real, but it requires high-quality implementation (not generic 7.1 software). A great stereo mix often beats poor virtual surround.

### What's the difference between gaming and audiophile headphones for gaming?

Gaming headsets prioritize: built-in mic, voice frequency tuning, boom arm placement, and wireless connectivity. Audiophile headphones have better music reproduction but require separate microphones. For pure competitive gaming, dedicated audiophile headphones + a separate mic is technically superior — but the convenience gap is significant.

### How important is headset weight for long sessions?

Very. A 350g+ headset causes noticeable head/neck fatigue after 3–4 hours. Target under 320g for extended sessions. Memory foam ear cushions and adjustable headbands distribute pressure — cheap plastic clamp pressure is the most common long-session discomfort culprit.
