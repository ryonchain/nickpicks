## Buying Guide

### 1080p vs. 4K for Streaming

Most streaming platforms compress video heavily:
- **Twitch:** Maximum 1080p60 for Partners; 720p60 for most accounts
- **YouTube Live:** 1080p60 and 4K60 both supported

For Twitch and most streaming scenarios, a 1080p60 webcam is technically sufficient — the platform compresses 4K to 1080p during broadcast. The reason to buy a 4K webcam:
1. **Recording quality:** OBS can record 4K locally while streaming at 1080p
2. **Source quality:** A 4K sensor crops to 1080p with more detail and color information
3. **Future-proofing:** 4K streaming will be more accessible as bitrate limits increase

For most new streamers: start with 1080p60, upgrade to 4K when you're ready to invest in production quality.

### Low-Light Performance: The Most Important Spec

Gaming rooms are often dark: a bright monitor, dim overhead lighting, and no dedicated camera lighting. This is where webcam sensors differ most dramatically. Key specs:
- **Aperture:** f/1.7 (Kiyo Pro Ultra) captures 2.3x more light than f/2.0. Larger aperture = better low light.
- **Sensor size:** Larger sensors collect more light. The Kiyo Pro Ultra's 1/1.2-inch sensor is significantly larger than the C922's 1/3-inch.
- **Software processing:** RightLight (Logitech), AI brightening (AVerMedia) — artificial brightening adds noise, but it's better than a dark image.

**Recommendation:** If you stream without a dedicated key light, prioritize aperture and sensor size over resolution. The Razer Kiyo Pro Ultra is the correct answer. If you have a ring light or softbox, any 4K webcam works well.

### FOV: Wide vs. Narrow

- **65°:** Tight, close-up face view. Good for professional streams where you want the face fill to be your channel art.
- **78°–82°:** Standard streaming FOV. Shows face and some background context.
- **90°–95°:** Wide view shows your full setup context. Better for just-chatting or desk tour content.

For face cam in a corner of the stream layout: 78° is standard. For full body or setup showcase: 90°+.

### Webcam vs. DSLR/Mirrorless Camera

A DSLR or mirrorless camera with a capture card (Elgato 4K60 Pro) produces dramatically better image quality than any webcam — depth of field blur, larger sensor, better lens. The tradeoff: $300–$800 for camera + $150–$200 for capture card vs. $200 for a premium webcam. For professional streaming at the affiliate or partner level, a camera + capture card setup is worth it. For streaming in early stages: a premium webcam is the right investment.

## FAQ

### Do I need a 4K webcam for streaming on Twitch?

Not strictly — Twitch caps most streams at 1080p60. A 4K webcam improves the source quality (you capture more detail that gets downsampled to 1080p) and enables local 4K recording. For absolute beginners, a 1080p60 webcam like the C922 is more than sufficient. Upgrade to 4K when you're growing an audience and production quality matters.

### How important is autofocus for gaming streaming?

Important for movement-heavy streams. If you lean back, forward, or turn your head during gaming, slow autofocus produces blurry footage. AI-powered autofocus (AVerMedia, Razer Kiyo Pro Ultra) tracks faces more accurately than older phase-detection systems. If you sit still at your desk, any webcam's autofocus is adequate.

### Should I use the webcam's built-in microphone?

No — for streaming, use a dedicated microphone. Built-in webcam mics pick up desk vibration, keyboard noise, and PC fan sound. A $50 dedicated USB microphone (Blue Snowball, HyperX SoloCast) dramatically outperforms any webcam mic. Use the built-in mic only as a backup or for video calls where a dedicated mic isn't needed.

### What's the difference between a streaming webcam and a regular webcam?

Streaming webcams typically offer higher frame rates (60fps vs. 30fps), manual control over exposure and white balance (to correct for monitor light), wider FOV options, and integration with streaming software. Regular webcams optimize for video calls — which prioritize compression and battery life over image quality. For streaming, use a dedicated streaming webcam.

### How do I mount a webcam for the best angle?

The ideal camera position is at eye level or slightly above — mounted on top of the monitor. Looking slightly down into a camera gives a natural conversational angle. Looking up into a camera from below is unflattering. Use the included monitor clip (standard on all five picks here) and adjust tilt so the lens is level with your eye line.
