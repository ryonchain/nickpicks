## Mechanical vs. Membrane: What Actually Matters for Gaming

Mechanical keyboards use individual spring-actuated switches under every key. Each keystroke registers at a precise actuation point — usually 1.5mm–2mm below the top — and resets cleanly without requiring a full press to the bottom. Membrane keyboards use a pressure pad under a rubber dome layer. The feel is mushier, actuation is less consistent, and membrane keys typically require bottoming out.

For gaming, mechanical keyboards win on:
- **Consistency** — same actuation point every keystroke, every time
- **N-key rollover** — register all keys simultaneously, not just 6
- **Longevity** — rated 50–100M actuations vs. ~5M for membrane
- **Switch choice** — linear (smooth), tactile (bump), or clicky (bump + sound)

Membrane keyboards win on:
- **Price** — $15–$40 for decent gaming membranes
- **Noise** — quieter in shared spaces
- **Availability** — every office supply store carries them

For competitive gaming, mechanical is the clear recommendation. For casual or budget-limited setups, a quality membrane is still a solid option.

## Switch Guide: Which Mechanical Switch for Gaming?

- **Linear (Red, Speed Silver):** Smooth, no tactile bump, lightest actuation. Best for competitive gaming and fast key presses.
- **Tactile (Brown, Clear):** Small bump before actuation. Good hybrid for gaming and typing.
- **Clicky (Blue, Green):** Bump + audible click. Great feedback, terrible for late-night gaming and shared offices.
- **Hall Effect (OmniPoint, Geon):** Magnetic, adjustable, no contact degradation. Premium option for competitive longevity.

For most gamers starting out, **linear switches** are the safest bet. They feel fast, require the least effort, and don't produce the clicky noise that becomes irritating over long sessions.
