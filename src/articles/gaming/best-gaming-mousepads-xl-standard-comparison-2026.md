## XL vs. Standard: Which Do You Need?

### Choose XL (900mm+) if:

- You use **low DPI / low sensitivity** (400–800 DPI, 1.0–2.0 in-game sensitivity): Low sensitivity requires large mouse movements — sweeping from one side of the desk to the other. Standard pads run out of space mid-flick.
- You have **a larger desk** (1400mm+ width): XL pads look proportionate on large desks and provide keyboard coverage.
- You want **keyboard and mouse coverage**: XL pads let you slide your keyboard onto the pad surface, improving wrist consistency and reducing desk noise.
- You use **multiple peripherals**: Streaming microphone, controller, phone dock — XL pads unify the desk surface.

### Choose Standard (400–500mm) if:

- You use **medium-to-high DPI** (1200+ DPI): Higher sensitivity means smaller mouse movements — a standard pad fits the entire range.
- You have **a smaller desk** (under 1200mm width): XL pads on small desks leave no room for anything else.
- You prefer **focused mouse zone**: Standard pads give you a defined space for the mouse — some players prefer this mental separation.
- You **travel or LAN**: Standard pads roll and transport more easily than XL mats.

### Surface Type: Speed vs. Control

- **Speed surfaces** (smooth weave, low resistance): Less friction — mouse moves fast with minimal effort. Best for: high DPI, fast repositioning, MOBA/RTS.
- **Control surfaces** (textured weave, more friction): More resistance gives micro-adjustment control. Best for: low DPI FPS, aim-dependent games.
- **Hybrid (micro-textured)**: Balance between speed and control. The QcK Heavy and Gigantus V2 sit here. Best for: most players.

### Hard Pad vs. Cloth: Worth Considering

Hard pads (plastic, aluminum, glass) offer the fastest and most consistent surface — no deformation, easy to clean. The tradeoff: harder on mouse feet, louder, less forgiving on wrist positioning. For most gamers, cloth is the right choice. Hard pads suit players who use very light pressure and don't rest their wrist on the surface.

## FAQ

### Does mousepad size actually affect aim?

Yes, specifically at low DPI settings. At 400 DPI with 2.0 in-game sensitivity, a 180° turn requires roughly 50–60cm of mouse movement on most setups. A standard 450mm pad limits your range mid-flick. An XL pad eliminates that constraint. At 1600+ DPI, a standard large pad is sufficient for any movement required.

### How do I clean a gaming mousepad?

Hand wash with mild soap and lukewarm water. Gently rub the surface, rinse thoroughly, and lay flat to air dry — do not put in a dryer or wring out. Do not machine wash XL pads (the stitching and rubber base can separate). Cleaning every 2–4 weeks prevents oil and dust buildup that degrades tracking.

### Are RGB mousepads worth it?

RGB mousepads (Corsair MM700, Razer Firefly V2) add visual appeal and sync with other RGB peripherals. The actual gaming surface quality is usually equivalent to non-RGB versions — you're paying $30–$60 extra for the lighting strip. If RGB matters to your setup aesthetic, it's a valid purchase. For performance-only buyers, save the money.

### How long does a gaming mousepad last?

With proper care, a quality cloth mousepad lasts 2–4 years. The surface degradation is gradual — tracking consistency decreases before it's visually obvious. If you notice your crosshair stuttering or drifting at low DPI, the surface may be the cause. Stitched edges prevent fraying significantly longer than non-stitched pads.

### What thickness should I choose?

4mm is the standard sweet spot — stable enough to isolate desk imperfections, thin enough for normal wrist positioning. 6mm (QcK Heavy) suits desks with surface irregularities or gaps. 2mm suits minimalist setups or users who prefer mouse-to-desk proximity. Thickness above 6mm adds no meaningful benefit.
