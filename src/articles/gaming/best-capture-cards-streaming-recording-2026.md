# Best Capture Cards for Streaming and Recording 2026

A capture card is the hardware bridge between your gameplay and your audience. Without one, you can stream from a PC using software alone (OBS, Streamlabs). With one, you can capture PS5 or Xbox Series X gameplay in 4K HDR, run a dual-PC streaming setup with zero performance impact on your gaming PC, or record uncompressed footage for professional video editing. This guide covers what you actually need at each tier.

---

## Do You Need a Capture Card?

**PC-only gaming:** You don't need a capture card. OBS or Streamlabs captures your screen directly. A capture card only adds value for quality (NVENC/AMF encoding offload is simpler) or dual-PC setups.

**Console gaming (PS5, Xbox Series X, Switch):** You need a capture card if you want to stream to Twitch or YouTube at quality above what the console's built-in share features offer. PS5 and Xbox have built-in streaming, but it caps at lower quality. A capture card unlocks 4K HDR recording and gives you OBS control.

**Dual-PC streaming:** One PC runs the game, one PC runs OBS and streams. The capture card sits between them, receiving HDMI output from the gaming PC. This is the setup used by most full-time streamers — zero streaming overhead on the gaming machine.

## Capture Card Setup Guide

### Single-PC Console Streaming
Console → HDMI → Capture Card → USB → PC (OBS) → Twitch/YouTube
Console → HDMI → TV (via capture card passthrough)

### Dual-PC Streaming
Gaming PC → HDMI → PCIe Capture Card (in Streaming PC) → OBS → Twitch
No performance impact on gaming PC; streaming PC handles all encoding.

### Required OBS Settings for Streaming
- **Source:** Video Capture Device → Select your capture card
- **Resolution:** 1920×1080 (1080p60 for most platforms)
- **Frame rate:** 60fps
- **Encoding:** NVENC (NVIDIA) or AMF (AMD) if hardware encoding; x264 if CPU
