## Buying Guide

### Do You Actually Need 4K?

Most streaming platforms cap video quality well below 4K: Twitch at 1080p60, YouTube at 1080p60 for most channels (4K available but rarely used for face cam). For face cam streaming, 1080p at 60fps is effectively the ceiling of useful quality. 4K webcams are most useful for:
- YouTube creators where 4K is available
- Zoom/Teams calls where you want the best image on a large display
- Future-proofing as platforms upgrade

For typical Twitch streaming: save the 4K premium and invest in better lighting instead.

### Lighting Matters More Than Camera Quality

The single biggest upgrade to face cam quality isn't a better camera — it's better lighting. A $60 ring light or $40 key light dramatically improves the image from even a budget 1080p webcam. If you're choosing between a $130 webcam and a $60 webcam + $60 key light, choose the latter. Camera sensor quality can't compensate for poor lighting.

### Microphone: Don't Rely on Built-In Webcam Mics

Webcam built-in microphones are backup audio at best. They pick up keyboard noise, fan noise, and echo off hard surfaces. For any serious streaming setup, a dedicated USB microphone or XLR mic with an interface will dramatically improve your audio quality — and audio quality matters more than video quality for viewer retention.

### Mounting: Where to Put Your Webcam

- **Top of monitor:** Most common. Eye contact is slightly above center frame.
- **Below monitor:** Not recommended — creates unflattering upward angle.
- **Separate arm mount:** Most flexible — allows fine position control. Requires tripod mount support.
- **On a boom arm:** Best for streamers who want to move the camera independently from the monitor.

## FAQ

### What's the best budget webcam for streaming?

The Logitech C920x at ~$60 is the best budget streaming webcam. It delivers reliable 1080p 30fps face cam quality with plug-and-play simplicity. The only meaningful upgrade path from the C920x is the Razer Kiyo Pro for low-light environments.

### Is 60fps important for streaming webcam?

For a face cam, 60fps provides noticeably smoother motion during animated reactions, head movement, and gesturing. It's not critical for static talking-head streams but is a meaningful upgrade for energetic streaming styles.

### Do I need a green screen for webcam background removal?

No. Software-based background removal in OBS (Virtual Background plugin) works without a green screen. Results are better with good lighting and a solid-color wall behind you. Green screens provide cleaner edges in complex hair/background situations but aren't required.

### How do I reduce webcam background noise?

Use OBS's built-in noise suppression filter on your webcam audio, or better — don't rely on webcam microphone audio for streaming at all. Invest in a dedicated USB or XLR microphone with cardioid pattern that rejects background sound.
