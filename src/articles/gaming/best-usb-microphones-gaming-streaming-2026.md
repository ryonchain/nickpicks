## Buying Guide

### Polar Patterns: What You Actually Need

- **Cardioid:** Captures sound from the front, rejects sides and rear. Best for solo gaming/streaming. What 90% of streamers need.
- **Omnidirectional:** Captures sound from all directions. Useful for group calls or recording a room.
- **Bidirectional (Figure-8):** Captures front and rear, rejects sides. Useful for two-person podcast format.
- **Stereo:** Captures left-right ambience. Useful for music or ASMR — not gaming.

Unless you specifically need stereo or bidirectional recording, a cardioid-only mic (Elgato Wave 3, Rode NT-USB Mini) is simpler and often better built.

### USB-C vs. USB-A: Does It Matter?

USB-C provides faster data transfer potential and reversible plugging. For audio purposes, 48kHz/24-bit audio requires negligible bandwidth — USB-A and USB-C perform identically for microphone use. Prefer USB-C for cable flexibility, not for audio quality reasons.

### Bit Depth and Sample Rate: Marketing vs. Reality

- **16-bit vs. 24-bit:** 24-bit provides 8 extra bits of dynamic range — audible only in very quiet recording environments or when doing post-processing. For streaming, 16-bit is sufficient.
- **48kHz vs. 96kHz:** Human hearing range tops at ~20kHz. 48kHz captures audio up to 24kHz — sufficient for streaming. 96kHz benefits post-production work but not live streaming.

For streaming, 48kHz/16-bit is perfectly adequate. 24-bit is a bonus, not a requirement.

### Boom Arm: Essential for Quality Streaming

Any USB microphone performs better on a boom arm than on its included desk stand:
- Positions the mic optimally (6–12 inches from your mouth, angled slightly down)
- Removes desk vibration transmission (keyboard/mouse clicks)
- Keeps the mic out of your webcam frame

The Blue Compass, Rode PSA1, and Elgato Wave Mic Arm are popular options. Any Amazon Basics boom arm at ~$20 is a significant quality improvement over a desk stand for streaming use.

## FAQ

### Do I need a USB audio interface for gaming/streaming?

No. USB microphones bypass the need for an audio interface — they have built-in analog-to-digital conversion in the mic body. An XLR microphone + interface setup can provide better audio quality at the same price tier, but USB mics are plug-and-play and perfectly capable for streaming.

### Will my USB mic pick up my mechanical keyboard?

Cardioid mics reject most sound from the sides and rear. Position your mic directly in front of your mouth and keep your keyboard outside the front cardioid capsule zone (to the side). This, combined with noise suppression in OBS or NVIDIA RTX Voice, reduces keyboard noise significantly. Quieter linear switches (or O-rings) help further.

### How do I reduce background noise on my USB mic?

Use OBS's Noise Suppression plugin (RNNoise or NVIDIA RTX Voice if you have an NVIDIA GPU). Set your microphone gain as low as possible while still capturing your voice clearly — lower gain = less background noise amplification. Acoustic treatment (foam panels, a reflection filter) helps significantly.

### Can I use a USB mic for Discord gaming?

Yes — USB mics work perfectly for Discord voice chat. The quality improvement over headset mics is dramatic and your teammates will notice immediately. Use push-to-talk in Discord to eliminate background noise transmission when you're not speaking.
