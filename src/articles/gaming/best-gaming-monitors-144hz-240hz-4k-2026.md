# Best Gaming Monitors 2026: 144Hz vs. 240Hz vs. 4K — What You Actually Need

The refresh rate war has stalled at a useful place. 144Hz is still the baseline that genuinely improves gameplay over 60Hz. 240Hz offers a real but diminishing return for competitive play. 4K at 144Hz+ is now achievable without a $1,500 GPU if you choose wisely. Here's what's actually worth your money in 2026.

---

## The Refresh Rate Debate: Settled

**60Hz → 144Hz:** The biggest single upgrade in gaming monitors. Motion clarity, input lag, and the overall sensation of responsiveness improve dramatically. Anyone gaming at 60Hz should upgrade before buying anything else.

**144Hz → 240Hz:** Real improvement, but smaller. Most useful in competitive FPS games (CS2, Valorant, Apex) where tracking fast-moving targets benefits from additional motion clarity. At 240fps, individual frames are 4.17ms apart vs. 6.94ms at 144fps — that's a measurable but not enormous difference.

**240Hz → 360Hz/480Hz:** Diminishing returns for most players. Measurable in controlled testing; imperceptible to most human reaction times. Only worth it if you're competing at a very high level in fast-paced FPS.

**4K at 60Hz vs. 4K at 144Hz:** Jumping to 4K at 60Hz is a significant visual upgrade for single-player games, but 60Hz feels sluggish after 144Hz. Target 4K at 144Hz minimum for a dual-purpose monitor.
