## Buying Guide

### Cable Management: Where to Start

The most impactful cable management projects in order of effort-to-impact ratio:

1. **Velcro tie existing loose cables** (~15 minutes, ~$7): immediately reduces visible clutter
2. **Cable management box for power strip** (~10 minutes, ~$20): eliminates the worst source of desk chaos
3. **J-channel under desk edge** (~30 minutes, ~$15): routes cables horizontally, off desk surface
4. **Grommet routing to floor** (~60 minutes, ~$25): runs cables vertically through desk to floor without hanging
5. **Monitor arm to raise monitor** (~45 minutes, ~$130): frees significant desk surface for accessories

Start with steps 1–3 before investing in a monitor arm or expensive cable management systems.

### Monitor Arms: What Weight Capacity Do You Need?

Measure your gaming monitor's weight before purchasing a monitor arm:
- 24"–27" monitors: typically 8–15 lbs (Ergotron LX handles all of these)
- 27"–32" monitors: typically 12–20 lbs
- 32"–34" ultrawide: typically 18–28 lbs
- 34"+ curved ultrawide: may exceed standard arm capacity — check specs carefully

Overloading a monitor arm causes drift (monitor slowly tilts down over time). Always buy an arm rated above your monitor's actual weight.

### Desk Pad Size: What Coverage Do You Need?

- **Small (30"×12"):** Mouse area only. For setups with little desk space.
- **Medium (36"×14"):** Mouse + keyboard together. Most common gaming size.
- **Large/XL (36"×16" to 48"×18"):** Full desk coverage including accessories. Best for streaming setups where desk visibility matters.
- **XXL (48"×24" to 60"×30"):** Full desk coverage for wide desks. Doubles as streaming background visible in webcam.

For a gaming desk, the minimum useful size is medium (covers keyboard and mouse). XL covers most streamable setups.

### USB Hub: Essential for Modern Gaming Desks

Modern gaming desks require more USB ports than any standard motherboard provides: gaming mouse, keyboard, headset dongle, webcam, microphone, capture card, controller charging — that's 7+ ports. A 10-port powered USB hub with individual switches prevents power fluctuation issues and lets you power down peripherals selectively.

## FAQ

### How do I hide cables on a gaming desk?

The most effective approach: (1) Use cable management trays under the desk to bundle and route cables horizontally, (2) use a J-channel along the desk back edge, (3) use a grommet in the desk surface to route cables cleanly through. Mount the power strip under the desk (Velcro or cable management box). The goal is to have zero cables running across the desk surface.

### Is a monitor arm worth buying?

Yes, if you have desk space constraints or ergonomic neck/eye issues. A monitor arm raises the monitor to exact eye-level height, frees 6–12 inches of desk depth (where the original stand was), and allows monitor adjustment for different activities (gaming vs. work vs. streaming). At the ~$130 Ergotron LX price, it's a high-value desk improvement for most setups.

### What size desk pad do I need for gaming?

For gaming: minimum 36"×16" to cover keyboard and mouse area. For streaming where your desk is visible on camera: 48"×24" or larger to cover the full visible desk surface and eliminate visible wood/surface in the webcam frame.

### Are desk cable grommets worth installing?

Yes — a cable grommet (a round hole in your desk surface with a rubber ring cover) allows routing cables cleanly from desk surface to underneath without using the desk edge. Most gaming desks include grommet holes; if yours doesn't, aftermarket rubber grommets are ~$15 and require a 1.5"–2" drill hole.
