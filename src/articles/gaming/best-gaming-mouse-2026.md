## Buying Guide

### Grip Style Determines Shape

Your grip style should dictate your mouse shape:
- **Palm grip:** Full hand rests on mouse. Choose larger, taller ergonomic shapes (DeathAdder, Basilisk).
- **Claw grip:** Palm on back, fingers curved over buttons. Ambidextrous or compact shapes work well.
- **Fingertip grip:** Only fingers contact the mouse. Lightweight, small mice preferred (Superlight 2, EC2-C).

### Sensor: What to Look For

Modern optical sensors at 100+ DPI are all excellent. Key specs that actually matter:
- **Tracking error / acceleration:** Zero-acceleration sensors maintain 1:1 cursor movement at all speeds. Avoid mice with "hardware acceleration" in the sensor.
- **Polling rate:** 1,000Hz is standard. 8,000Hz polling is now available but marginal benefit outside professional play.
- **Lift-off distance:** Lower is better — reduces accidental cursor movement when lifting and repositioning.

### Wireless vs. Wired in 2026

2.4GHz wireless gaming mice (Lightspeed, HyperSpeed) now match wired mice in click latency and tracking consistency. The only remaining wired advantages are:
- No battery management
- Slightly lower price floor
- Still standard in professional esports (partly tradition)

For casual-to-serious gaming, wireless is now the better user experience at most price points.

### DPI: What You Actually Need

Most players game at 400–1600 DPI. High-DPI specs are marketing. What matters more than maximum DPI is accuracy at your preferred DPI setting.

## FAQ

### Is wireless gaming mouse latency actually a problem?

No — modern 2.4GHz wireless gaming mice (Logitech Lightspeed, Razer HyperSpeed) achieve sub-1ms click registration. The latency difference between wired and 2.4GHz wireless is imperceptible in gameplay.

### What's the best mouse for FPS games?

For FPS, prioritize: accurate optical sensor, lightweight body (under 80g), and a shape that suits your grip. The Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 or Zowie EC2-C are consistently used by professional FPS players.

### Should I buy a heavy or lightweight mouse?

Lighter is generally better for precision aim and reduced wrist fatigue over long sessions. The trend in competitive gaming has moved decisively toward 60–80g mice. However, some players prefer the controlled resistance of heavier mice — try both if possible.

### How long do gaming mouse batteries last?

Budget wireless mice (AA battery): 150–300 hours. Premium rechargeable mice: 60–100+ hours per charge. Some (HyperX Cloud Alpha Wireless mouse variants, SteelSeries models) reach 200+ hours on a single charge.
