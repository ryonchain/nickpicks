## Buying Guide

### Placement: Where LED Strips Work Best

**Monitor backlighting (bias lighting):** Attach behind the monitor, facing the wall. Reduces eye strain by raising ambient room brightness near the display, reducing the contrast difference between the bright screen and dark room. Single warm white or matching screen-color works best here.

**Desk perimeter:** Run under the front and sides of your desk for a floating-desk glow effect. Most visible in streaming setups, less functional for gaming ergonomics.

**PC case interior:** ARGB strips connect to motherboard ARGB headers for full software control. Govee and other consumer strips require adapters for PC case use.

**Under-desk:** Runs under the desk surface for ambient floor glow. Requires longer strips and concealed power routing.

### RGBIC vs. RGB: The Key Difference

**Standard RGB:** Entire strip displays one color at a time. All zones change together.

**RGBIC (individually addressable):** Segments along the strip display independent colors simultaneously. Enables gradient effects and scene patterns across the strip length.

RGBIC is the minimum recommendation for gaming setups — RGB-only strips look significantly less dynamic.

### Music Sync: Mic vs. HDMI vs. Camera

- **Microphone-based sync:** Cheapest. Reacts to room audio. High latency (~200ms). Reactive to non-audio events (talking, background noise).
- **HDMI passthrough:** Precise video-based color sync. Low latency (~50ms). Works with any HDMI source.
- **Camera-based sync:** Compatible with any display (not just HDMI sources). Moderate latency (~100ms). Best for TV/console setups.

### Smart Home: Which Protocol Matters?

For gaming setups:
- **Alexa/Google Home:** Voice control and app automation. Useful for "Gaming mode on" voice commands.
- **Apple HomeKit:** iPhone users' preferred ecosystem with Shortcuts automation.
- **Matter:** The new cross-platform standard — increasingly supported across all brands.

If you're not invested in a specific smart home ecosystem, Govee and LIFX offer broad compatibility. If you're building a Hue ecosystem, buy Philips Hue.

## FAQ

### Are LED strips safe for monitors and desk surfaces?

Most consumer LED strips are low-voltage (12V DC) and run cool enough for close-surface use. However, extended periods of adhesive contact can damage monitor bezels or painted desk surfaces when removed. Use cable clips or 3M Command strips for removable mounting instead of relying solely on the included adhesive backing.

### Can I connect LED strips to a gaming PC for ARGB sync?

Govee and most consumer LED strips are standalone — they don't connect to motherboard ARGB headers (5V 3-pin ARGB). For PC-integrated ARGB sync with software like Corsair iCUE, ASUS AURA Sync, or MSI Mystic Light, purchase ARGB-specific strips designed for motherboard headers.

### How many feet of LED strip do I need for a gaming desk?

A typical gaming desk (60"×30") requires approximately 10–12 feet for full perimeter coverage. A single 16.4 ft Govee roll covers one desk with strip remaining for monitor back or PC case. For a dual-monitor setup with full desk coverage, purchase 16–20 feet.

### Do LED strip lights attract bugs or generate heat?

LED strips generate minimal heat compared to incandescent or fluorescent lighting. They don't attract insects (LEDs don't emit UV light). Running LED strips continuously is safe for 24/7 operation within the manufacturer's rated hours (typically 25,000–50,000 hours).
