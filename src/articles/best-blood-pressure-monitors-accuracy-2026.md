## What to Look For

**Clinical validation** is the single most important buying criterion — the FDA or AHA/ESH validation mark means the monitor was independently tested against a mercury sphygmomanometer and confirmed accurate. Skip any monitor without a stated validation protocol.

**Cuff size** matters as much as the monitor itself. A cuff that doesn't fit your arm properly will give systematically wrong readings regardless of monitor quality. Measure your arm before buying.

**Memory and connectivity** matter if you're tracking long-term trends or sharing data with a physician. Wi-Fi sync (Withings BPM Connect) is the most convenient — readings upload automatically without requiring you to pull out your phone.
