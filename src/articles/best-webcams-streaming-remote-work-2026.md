## Webcam Buying Guide

### Resolution vs Image Quality: What Really Matters

Resolution (4K vs 1080p) matters less than sensor size and glass quality. The Elgato Facecam Pro and Razer Kiyo Pro Ultra produce better images at 1080p than budget 4K cameras because their larger sensors capture more light and detail at the pixel level. Focus on sensor size and aperture when comparing within a price range.

### Streaming vs Video Calls: Different Priorities

| Feature | Streaming Priority | Video Call Priority |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| Resolution | 1080p/60fps minimum | 4K with AI downsampling |
| Autofocus | Manual/fixed preferred | Auto with face tracking |
| Exposure | Manual control | Automatic HDR |
| Software | OBS/Streamlabs compatible | Teams/Zoom certified |
| FOV | 60–80° (tight, face-focused) | 78–90° (wider frame) |

### Lighting First, Camera Second

A $50 ring light dramatically improves any webcam's image quality. Before spending $229 on a Razer Kiyo Pro Ultra, spend $60 on an 18" ring light — the lighting upgrade will have a larger visual impact than the camera upgrade if your current setup has poor lighting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use my mirrorless camera as a webcam for streaming?**
Yes — an HDMI capture card (Elgato Cam Link 4K, ~$130) converts your camera's HDMI output to a USB webcam signal. Image quality significantly exceeds any dedicated webcam, especially for background blur and low-light performance. Downsides: cameras can overheat during extended streams, require separate power management, and add setup complexity.

**What bitrate should I stream at for good webcam quality?**
For 1080p/60fps streaming, target 6,000–8,000 Kbps if your platform and upload speed allow. The camera's uncompressed output quality is the ceiling; your streaming encoder's bitrate determines how much of that quality reaches viewers.

**Does a 4K webcam require USB 3.0?**
Yes — 4K video requires the bandwidth of USB 3.0 (also called USB 3.1 Gen 1 or SuperSpeed USB). If connected to a USB 2.0 port, most cameras automatically drop to 1080p. Ensure your USB port is blue-coded (USB 3.0) or explicitly labeled SS (SuperSpeed).
