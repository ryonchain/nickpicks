## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### Brightness Is the Most Important Spec

ANSI lumens is the standardized brightness measurement — ignore manufacturer "lux" or non-ANSI claims, which are inflated and not comparable.

| Setting | Minimum ANSI Lumens |
|---|---|
| Fully dark room / post-dark backyard | 200–400 lm |
| Dusk / twilight outdoor | 700–1000 lm |
| After sunset with some ambient light | 1000–1500 lm |
| Late afternoon / pre-sunset | 2000+ lm |

For most backyard setups, **700–1000 ANSI lumens** lets you start watching comfortably around sunset rather than waiting 30+ minutes for full dark.

### Screen vs. Bed Sheet vs. Fence

A proper projector screen makes a dramatic difference to image quality:
- **White projector screen** (100"): best contrast ratio, most reflective, purpose-built
- **Gray projector screen**: better ambient light rejection, slightly reduced brightness
- **Bed sheet / white wall**: workable, much lower contrast and color accuracy
- **Wooden fence**: not recommended — grain pattern distracts, poor reflectivity

A 100-inch outdoor projector screen ($60–$150 on Amazon) is one of the highest-value upgrades for outdoor movie night quality.

### Built-in Battery vs. AC Power

Battery projectors are genuinely more convenient for backyards without outdoor outlets, camping, and travel. Trade-offs:
- Battery capacity is improving but still limits most units to 2–3 hours
- Brightness is typically lower in battery-powered units (heat management)
- AC-powered units (Cosmos Max, wall-mount models) can be brighter because heat is not a concern

For backyard-only use with outdoor power, AC-powered units offer the best brightness-per-dollar. For flexible or camping use, battery models are worth the trade-off.

### Android TV vs. Google TV vs. Phone Casting

- **Android TV** (XGIMI, BenQ): full app ecosystem, Chromecast built in, most apps available
- **Google TV** (Nebula): Google TV interface on top of Android TV — simpler UI, better content discovery
- **Phone casting only** (Vankyo budget): works but more friction, phone battery drains

Note: Netflix certification is complicated on projectors. Some Android TV projectors require sideloading the Netflix app. Check model-specific reviews for current Netflix compatibility before purchasing.

Aim for 700–900 ANSI lumens minimum for comfortable dusk viewing. Under 400 lm requires waiting for full dark — 30+ minutes post-sunset.

A purpose-built screen ($60–$150) dramatically improves contrast and color vs. a bed sheet. It's often a better upgrade than jumping to a more expensive projector.

**Q: How long does the battery last for a full movie?**
Most battery projectors offer 2–3 hours. The BenQ GP20 leads at 3 hours. A 100W+ power bank extends runtime for longer sessions.
