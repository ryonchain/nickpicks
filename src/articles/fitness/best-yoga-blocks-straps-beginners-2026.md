# Best Yoga Blocks & Straps for Beginners in 2026

Yoga props are not training wheels. Every studio keeps shelves stocked with blocks and straps because they make poses accessible, safer, and deeper — for beginners and advanced practitioners alike. A block under your hand in triangle pose removes the strain that forces collapse in your torso. A strap in seated forward fold lets you actually work the hamstring stretch instead of just rounding your back to reach your feet.

This guide covers the best blocks and straps for beginners, what materials actually matter, and why buying props is one of the smartest investments you'll make in your practice.

## Yoga Blocks: Foam vs. Cork vs. Wood

**Foam blocks** are the most common and affordable. Light, gentle on skin, and forgiving if you fall on them. The downside: foam compresses slightly under load, reducing stability for balancing poses.

**Cork blocks** are denser, more stable, and more eco-friendly. Slightly heavier than foam. Better for standing balance poses where you need firm ground. Naturally antimicrobial.

**Wood blocks** are the firmest and most stable but the least comfortable for resting body weight. Best for experienced practitioners. Not recommended for beginners.

## FAQ

### Do I really need yoga props as a beginner?

Yes. Props make poses accessible and safe. Without a block, many beginners round their spine in forward folds (defeating the purpose) or strain to reach the floor in standing poses. Props aren't a shortcut — they're the correct way to practice poses that your body isn't ready for yet.

### What size yoga block is best for beginners?

Standard 9" × 6" × 4" blocks fit most hands and body proportions. If you have larger hands or are a larger-framed person, look for the wider 9" × 6" × 4" option rather than miniature "travel" blocks which are too small for standing support.

### How long should a yoga strap be?

6 feet is sufficient for most practitioners under 5'10". If you're taller or have particularly tight hamstrings or shoulders, get an 8-foot strap. The extra length gives you room to properly set up poses without the strap running out of slack.

### Can I use a regular belt instead of a yoga strap?

In a pinch, yes — but regular belts have fixed lengths and buckles that don't adjust under tension. A proper yoga strap with a sliding buckle lets you shorten or lengthen mid-pose without interrupting your practice.
