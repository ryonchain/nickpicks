## EGCG Research Highlights

**Metabolism and fat oxidation:**
- Meta-analysis in Obesity Reviews (2009): Green tea catechins increase 24-hour fat oxidation by 10–16%
- Most effective when EGCG is combined with caffeine (synergistic thermogenic effect)
- Effect is more pronounced in people who are not habitual caffeine consumers

**Cardiovascular protection:**
- EGCG prevents LDL oxidation — oxidized LDL is the key step in atherosclerotic plaque formation
- Green tea consumption is associated with reduced cardiovascular mortality in Japanese cohort studies
- Blood pressure reductions of 2–3 mmHg in hypertensive subjects

**Brain health:**
- EGCG crosses the blood-brain barrier
- Inhibits amyloid-beta aggregation in vitro — relevant to Alzheimer's disease research
- L-theanine (in whole tea) + EGCG combination produces calm focus (the reason green tea is cognitively cleaner than coffee)

**Cancer research:**
- EGCG induces apoptosis in cancer cell lines across multiple cancer types
- Epidemiological data from Japan shows lower rates of certain cancers in high green tea consumers
- Not a cancer treatment — but evidence supports EGCG as a preventive compound

**Safe dosing summary:**
- 300–400mg EGCG/day is considered the optimal balance of efficacy and safety
- Always take with food
- Avoid combining with alcohol on the same day
- Discontinue if any signs of liver stress appear
