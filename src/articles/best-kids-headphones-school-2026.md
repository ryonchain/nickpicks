## Wired vs. Wireless for School

**Wired headphones** are simpler for young children — no charging, no pairing, and schools don't lose them to dead batteries in the middle of a lesson. The trade-off is the cord, which gets tangled in backpacks and catches on desk corners. Wired is typically the right choice for grades K–2.

**Wireless headphones** work better for grades 3+ where kids are managing their own gear more independently. The lack of a cord reduces classroom tangles and broken ports. The key discipline is charging the headphones every few days, which most third-graders and up can manage with a routine.

**Wired backup tip:** Even wireless headphones worth buying include a 3.5mm input for wired mode. In schools where charging isn't available, the wired option extends usability to any circumstance.

## What Schools Actually Need

Many schools specify headphone requirements. Before buying, check:
- Whether the school uses tablets (iPad: lightning or USB-C; Android/Chromebook: USB-C or 3.5mm)
- Whether Bluetooth is allowed in classrooms
- Whether headphones need to fold flat to fit in a communal storage bin
- Whether a microphone is required for video calls

Most schools that specify headphones for remote or hybrid learning accept any headphone with a 3.5mm jack and a working microphone — the wired [Mpow CH9](https://amazon.com/s?k=Mpow+CH9+Kids+Headphones&tag=nickpicks-20) covers this at $15 per pair.
