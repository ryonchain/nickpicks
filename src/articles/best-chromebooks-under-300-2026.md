## What to Look for in a Chromebook Under $300

### RAM: 8GB vs. 4GB

**The most important spec decision.** With 4GB RAM, you'll notice slowdowns when:
- Running more than 6–8 Chrome tabs
- Using Google Meet or Zoom while working in Docs
- Switching between apps quickly

8GB RAM is the minimum recommendation for a Chromebook you'll use seriously. If a model is available in both 4GB and 8GB configurations, the $20–30 price difference for 8GB is always worth it.

### Display Resolution

- **1366×768 (HD):** Functional but noticeably less sharp. Text looks fine; photos and video look compressed compared to Full HD.
- **1920×1080 (Full HD/FHD):** Standard for comfortable daily use. Sharp text, crisp video.
- **2560×1600 (QHD):** Rare in budget Chromebooks — the Acer 516 GE is an exception. Noticeably better for long work sessions.

### Storage: eMMC vs. SSD

Budget Chromebooks use eMMC storage (slower, more like a flash drive) rather than true SSDs. At 128GB+, eMMC is fine for ChromeOS. With Google Drive for cloud storage, even 64GB works acceptably.

**Note:** Chromebooks rely heavily on Google Drive rather than local storage. If you have good internet access, 64–128GB eMMC is workable. For offline-heavy use, prioritize 128GB+.

### Battery Life

Most Chromebooks in this range advertise 9–10 hours. Real-world is typically 7–8 hours with screen brightness at 50–60% and moderate use. For a school day of 6–8 hours, most will make it through with minimal charging needed.

## Should You Buy a Chromebook or a Windows Laptop at This Budget?

Under $300, a Chromebook wins most comparisons:

- **Speed:** ChromeOS boots in 8–10 seconds. Windows at $250–300 often uses slower hardware for the same price.
- **Battery:** Chromebooks consistently outlast comparably-priced Windows laptops.
- **Viruses and maintenance:** ChromeOS updates automatically in the background, doesn't get viruses, doesn't slow down over time.
- **Price:** You get more hardware for the same money because ChromeOS is free and lighter than Windows.

**The exception:** If you need Windows software, buy a Windows laptop. Period. No amount of Chromebook virtues matters if you can't run the program you need. For everything else, a Chromebook at $250–300 typically outperforms a Windows laptop at the same price.
