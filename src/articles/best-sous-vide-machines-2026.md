## What Is Sous Vide and Why Should You Care?

Sous vide (French: "under vacuum") means cooking food in a precisely temperature-controlled water bath. A 130°F water bath cooks a steak to exactly 130°F (medium-rare) — it's physically impossible to overcook it.

Results you can't achieve with traditional cooking:
- **Steak:** Edge-to-edge medium-rare with zero gray band
- **Chicken breast:** Perfectly juicy at 145°F (pasteurized but not dry)
- **Salmon:** Silky, moist texture impossible on a pan
- **Eggs:** Custom yolk runniness at 63°C–75°C

The learning curve is minimal: seal food in a bag, set temperature, start timer, finish with a quick sear.

## Complete Beginner's Guide

**What else do you need:**
1. Vacuum sealer ($25–$80) or heavy-duty zip-lock bags with the water displacement method (free)
2. Large pot or dedicated container (12–18 quart Cambro tub is ideal)
3. Lid or ping-pong balls to reduce evaporation on long cooks
4. Cast iron pan or torch for the finishing sear

**Temperature cheat sheet:**
- Rare steak: 125°F / 52°C (1–4 hours)
- Medium-rare steak: 130°F / 54°C (1–4 hours)
- Medium steak: 140°F / 60°C (1–4 hours)
- Chicken breast (juicy, safe): 145°F / 63°C (1–4 hours)
- Salmon (silky): 125°F / 52°C (30 min)
