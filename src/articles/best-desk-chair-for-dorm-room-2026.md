## What to Look For in a Dorm Desk Chair

### Adjustable Height (Non-Negotiable)
Dorm desks vary in height, and desk-chair height pairing matters for posture. You want your feet flat on the floor and your arms at 90° at the desk. Pneumatic (gas lift) height adjustment is standard on every chair on this list.

### Lumbar Support
Lower back pain from poor posture is the most common student complaint. A chair with at least fixed lumbar support is dramatically better than a chair without.

### Armrests
Fixed armrests are fine for most students. 4D adjustable armrests matter if you type long sessions and want precise positioning.

### Mesh vs Foam Back
Mesh breathes better — important for heated dorm rooms. Foam may feel cushier initially but compresses over time and retains heat.

### Floor Compatibility
Most dorm floors are tile or laminate. Standard casters work fine. Avoid chairs specifically designed for carpet if your dorm has hard floors.
