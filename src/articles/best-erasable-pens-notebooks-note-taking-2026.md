## How to Choose Pens and Notebooks for Class

**Matching pen to paper:**
- Heavy gel pens (FriXion, EnerGel) need at least 80g paper to avoid bleed-through
- Ballpoints work well on any paper, including 70g
- Felt-tip and fountain pens need 90g+ for clean results

**Color-coding system:** A 5-color system helps organize subjects: one color per subject or one color per note type (definitions, examples, formulas, questions, summaries). The FriXion 24-color pack gives you more than enough options.

**Ruled vs dot grid vs blank:** Dot grid (like the Leuchtturm bullet journal) is the most flexible — works for writing, drawing, diagrams, and structured layouts. Ruled is fastest for pure note-taking. Blank is best for art and visual notes.

## FAQ

**Do Pilot FriXion pens work in cold weather?**
The ink is heat-sensitive and reappears when cold. In normal conditions (room temperature), this isn't an issue. Avoid leaving notebooks in a cold car overnight — notes can become invisible. They reappear when warmed back up.

**Which notebook paper is best for the Pilot FriXion?**
Rhodia (90g) and Leuchtturm1917 (80g) both work well with FriXion pens. Moleskine (70g) shows slight ghosting but no bleed-through. For the cleanest FriXion results, choose Rhodia.

**Are Moleskine notebooks worth the price?**
They're worth it for the design, elastic closure, and brand reliability. For paper quality per dollar, Rhodia and Leuchtturm offer better paper at a similar price. The Moleskine premium comes from design and availability.
