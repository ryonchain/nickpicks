But the category has important distinctions: GPS trackers (cellular network, unlimited range, monthly subscription) vs Bluetooth trackers (Apple AirTag, no subscription, limited range). ## GPS vs Bluetooth: The Critical Distinction

**GPS Trackers (Cellular LTE):** Use cellular networks to transmit location. Range is unlimited — your dog can be 50 miles away and you'll see their location on your phone. Require a monthly subscription ($4–10/month). Real-time updates every 2–5 seconds. Best for dogs that escape frequently or live in rural/suburban areas.

**Bluetooth Trackers (Apple AirTag):** Use Bluetooth and crowdsourced Apple/Android networks to find location. Free — no subscription. But range depends on other Apple devices nearby. In a city, coverage is dense and AirTag is surprisingly effective. In a rural area or if your pet runs far, coverage gaps mean you may see nothing. Not a substitute for GPS in serious escape situations.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**GPS vs Bluetooth:** If your dog or cat escapes regularly or you're in a rural area, you need GPS (cellular). AirTag is a useful supplement but not a substitute if you have a serial escape artist.

**Subscription Cost Over Time:** Calculate 3-year total cost. A $99 Fi collar at $9.95/month costs significantly more than a $49 Tractive at $4.99/month over the same period. Factor the subscription into your decision.

**Battery Life:** 3-month Fi battery vs 2–5 day Tractive battery is a significant lifestyle difference. If you'll forget to charge weekly, Fi is worth the premium. If you can commit to weekly charging, Tractive's lower subscription cost wins.

**Cat-Specific Weight Limit:** For cats, the tracker must weigh under 40g (roughly 10% of a typical cat's body weight minimum). The Tractive Cat LTE at 35g is the right pick — most dog trackers are too heavy for cats.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do pet GPS trackers work internationally?**
Tractive has broad international coverage with separate plan options. Fi and Whistle are primarily US-focused. Check coverage maps for your specific country before purchasing.

**How accurate is pet GPS tracking?**
GPS accuracy is typically 10–30 feet under open sky. In urban canyons or dense tree cover, accuracy can drift to 50–100 feet. Fine for finding your general neighborhood, less precise for a specific backyard corner.

**Can cats wear GPS trackers safely?**
Yes, but only lightweight trackers (under 40g) on a breakaway safety collar. The Tractive Cat LTE at 35g is specifically designed for this. Never use a non-breakaway collar on a cat — entrapment risk is significant.

**Is the Apple AirTag good enough for pet tracking?**
In urban areas with dense iPhone coverage, the AirTag is surprisingly effective and costs nothing to subscribe to. But it has real limitations: no real-time updates, no range guarantee, and no rural coverage. Use it as a backup tracker, not your primary safety solution for a dog that escapes.
