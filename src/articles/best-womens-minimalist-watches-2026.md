## What Makes a Great Minimalist Watch

### Case Size for Women
The minimalist aesthetic requires restraint in case size. The standard for women's minimalist watches:
- **28–32mm:** Classic feminine scale, suits slimmer wrists
- **34–36mm:** Contemporary sweet spot, suits most wrist sizes
- **38mm+:** Trending oversized-minimal, but starts to read as statement piece

### Crystal Quality
- **Mineral crystal:** Standard. Scratch-resistant to a degree, replaceable cheaply.
- **Sapphire crystal:** Ranks 9 on Mohs hardness scale (diamond is 10). Nearly scratch-proof. Standard on watches above $200 and required for true luxury.

### Movement Types
- **Japanese quartz (Miyota, Seiko):** Extremely accurate, low maintenance, affordable. Industry standard for fashion watches.
- **Swiss quartz (ETA, Ronda):** Marginally more accurate, Swiss brand cachet. Common in $150–400 range.
- **Swiss mechanical/automatic:** Hallmark of fine watchmaking. Not necessary for a minimalist daily watch, but available at premium price.

### Strap Materials
- **Genuine leather:** Most elegant, requires rotation to avoid wear
- **NATO/nylon:** Casual, durable, machine washable
- **Stainless steel mesh:** Versatile, adjustable, dressier than leather for some users

## Watch Care for Daily Wearers

**Leather strap rotation:** Alternate between 2 straps. Leather needs 24 hours to dry between long wears or it deteriorates.

**Cleaning the case:** Soft microfiber cloth, no chemical cleaners. Clean around pushers and crown with a soft-bristled toothbrush.

**Water resistance:** Most fashion watches are 30m water resistant (splashes only). Remove before swimming unless rated 100m+.

**Battery life:** Japanese and Swiss quartz batteries last 2–3 years. Replace proactively at 2 years rather than waiting for failure mid-event.
