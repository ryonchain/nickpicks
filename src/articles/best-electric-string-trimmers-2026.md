## Buyer's Guide

### Battery vs. Gas vs. Corded

**Battery (Cordless):**
- Best for: Most homeowners with up to 1 acre
- Pros: Quiet, instant start, low maintenance, no fumes
- Cons: Runtime limited; need extra batteries for large properties

**Gas:**
- Best for: Large properties, professional use, extended sessions
- Pros: Unlimited runtime, maximum power
- Cons: Loud, maintenance required, startup issues, fumes

**Corded Electric:**
- Best for: Tiny yards, tight budgets
- Pros: Cheapest, infinite runtime
- Cons: 100ft cord limits range, must avoid wet conditions

### Key Specs
- **Cut width**: 13" for small yards; 15–16" standard; 17"+ for large properties
- **Battery voltage**: 20V handles edging; 40V for medium weeds; 56V/80V for heavy growth
- **Line diameter**: 0.065" (light), 0.080" (standard), 0.095–0.105" (heavy)
- **Feed system**: Bump feed (manual) vs. auto-feed (automatic)

### String/Line Guide
| Line Diameter | Best For |
|---------------|---------|
| 0.065"–0.080" | Thin grass, edging, light use |
| 0.080"–0.095" | Standard residential |
| 0.105"–0.130" | Thick weeds, tough growth |

### Edger Attachment
Many string trimmers convert to an edger by rotating the head 90°. Check for this feature if you want crisp sidewalk edges — saves buying a separate edger.

## FAQ

**How long does a battery string trimmer last?**
The tool itself lasts 5–10+ years with proper care. Batteries degrade over ~500 charge cycles — expect replacement after 3–5 years of regular use. EGO and Greenworks batteries are ~$50–$80 for a basic replacement.

**Can a battery trimmer handle thick weeds?**
Modern 56V–80V battery trimmers handle most thick weeds effectively. For truly overgrown areas or brambles, use a heavier 0.105" line and a gas trimmer or brush cutter.

**How often should I replace trimmer line?**
The line wears down during use and from hitting hard surfaces. Bump or auto-feed systems advance fresh line as needed. Keep a spare spool — most trimmers use 0.095" round line.

**Is it worth getting a brushless motor?**
Yes — brushless motors run cooler, last longer, and deliver better power efficiency. Most name-brand battery trimmers at $100+ use brushless motors. Budget corded trimmers use brushed motors.

**Can I use my trimmer as an edger?**
Many trimmers rotate 90° to become an edger. Check the product description. Dedicated edgers ($60–$120) create cleaner cuts, but a trimmer-edger combo is efficient for most homeowners.
