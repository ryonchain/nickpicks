## Types of Neck Massagers

**Shiatsu (kneading):** Rotating nodes that simulate finger-kneading massage. Best for chronic tension and muscle knots.

**Percussion (vibration):** Rapid, deep pressure pulses that break up fascia and muscle adhesions. Best for trigger point therapy.

**TENS (electric stimulation):** Low-frequency electrical pulses that interrupt pain signals. Best for nerve pain.

**Vibration:** Gentle vibration without deep penetration. Best for light relaxation, not serious knots.

## What to Look For

**Node count:** 8 nodes covers more surface area than 4. More nodes = less repositioning.

**Heat therapy:** Infrared or far-infrared heat penetrates deeper than surface warmth. Look for models with toggleable heat (not always-on).

**Bidirectional rotation:** Nodes that change direction mid-massage prevent "one-way chasing" and feel more like real massage.

**Portability:** Battery-powered models (TheraGun) are more versatile. Corded models are more powerful.

**Attachment type:** U-shape works on neck, shoulders, and calves. Flat-back works on lumbar. Get a U-shape if buying one device.
