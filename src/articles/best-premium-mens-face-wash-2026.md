### How Often Should You Wash Your Face?
Twice daily (morning and night) is the standard recommendation. If you have dry or sensitive skin, once daily (evening) with a water-only rinse in the morning is acceptable. Over-washing strips the skin's natural barrier and causes compensatory oil production.

### What to Avoid in Men's Face Wash
- **Sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS)**: harsh surfactant that causes irritation and stripping
- **Alcohol as a first ingredient**: drying; only beneficial in small amounts in specific formulations
- **Artificial fragrance in sensitive skin products**: a common irritant
- **Micro-plastic beads**: banned in many countries, still in some older formulations

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does price matter for face wash?**
Yes, but with diminishing returns. The jump from drugstore to mid-premium ($20–$30) face wash is meaningful — better surfactant systems, fewer harsh ingredients, more effective pH. Beyond $50, you're largely paying for brand and packaging. The picks above are all in the range where ingredient quality is genuinely better.

**Should I use a separate exfoliant or get an exfoliating cleanser?**
A dedicated chemical exfoliant (AHA/BHA toner) is more effective than a physical scrub cleanser. But a physical exfoliating cleanser like Baxter's is a practical option for men who don't want multiple steps. If you're prone to irritation, stick to chemical exfoliants used 1–2x weekly.

**Can I use women's face wash?**
Yes. Skin biology is more similar between men and women than marketing suggests. The main difference is that men's formulas often account for post-shave skin barrier disruption and are slightly more tolerant of rougher textured skin. Women's formulas with the same active ingredients are equally effective.

**How long does it take to see results from a better face wash?**
2–4 weeks of consistent twice-daily use. Skin turnover cycle is approximately 28 days, so you need a full cycle to evaluate a new cleanser fairly.
