## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Bedroom Air Purifier Under $100

### CADR Rating: The Most Important Number
CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) tells you how quickly the purifier cleans the air in cubic feet per minute. For a typical small bedroom (150–250 sq ft with 8 ft ceilings), look for a CADR of at least 100–150 CFM for dust and pollen. Higher is better — the Coway Mighty and Winix 5500-2 lead this price tier with CADR ratings above 240.

### True HEPA vs. "HEPA-Type"
This distinction matters enormously. A **True HEPA** filter (H11–H13) captures at least 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns or larger — including dust, pollen, pet dander, mold spores, and some bacteria. "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like" filters are less efficient and may only capture 85–95% of particles. For allergy and asthma sufferers, always choose True HEPA.

### Noise Level for Sleep
Bedroom purifiers need to run all night without waking you. Look for sleep mode noise levels under 30 dB (the average whisper is 30 dB). The Blueair Blue Pure 411 runs as quietly as 17 dB on the lowest setting — effectively inaudible. Most Levoit and Coway units hit 24–25 dB in sleep mode, which is acceptable for most sleepers.

### Filter Replacement Costs
The sticker price is just part of the story. Calculate your 2-year total cost of ownership by factoring in filter replacements. Most filters need replacing every 6–8 months at $15–$30 each. Some units use washable pre-filters to extend main filter life — look for these to reduce ongoing costs.

### Coverage Area
Match the purifier's rated coverage to your room size, and then size up slightly. If your bedroom is 200 sq ft, get a purifier rated for 250–300 sq ft. Manufacturers rate coverage at 2 air changes per hour (ACH) — for allergy relief, you ideally want 4–5 ACH, which effectively halves the listed coverage rating.
