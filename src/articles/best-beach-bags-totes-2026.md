## What to Look For

- **Volume:** For solo beach days, 20–30 liters is usually enough. For couples or families, look for 30–50+ liters to fit towels, toys, food, and gear for everyone.
- **Material and sand resistance:** Smooth nylon, laminated canvas, and RPET shed sand most easily. Natural canvas and straw weaves trap sand more aggressively but often look better.
- **Wet/dry separation:** A separate wet or waterproof pocket for damp swimwear and towels is a huge quality-of-life upgrade — it keeps dry items dry and contains swimsuit drips.
- **Closure type:** Open-top totes are easiest to access but risk spills and blowouts in wind. Zip-top bags offer better security and water resistance; magnetic snaps fall in between.
- **Weight when empty:** A heavy bag before you've loaded it makes a long beach walk miserable. Look for bags under 1.5 lbs empty if you're carrying significant gear.
- **Cleaning:** Sand and sunscreen accumulate fast. Machine-washable canvas and wipe-clean nylon are far easier to maintain than straw or fabric bags that require hand washing.
