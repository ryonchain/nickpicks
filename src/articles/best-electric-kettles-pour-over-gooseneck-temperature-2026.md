## Temperature Guide by Beverage

| Beverage | Temperature | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Green tea | 175°F (80°C) | Higher temp = bitter |
| White tea | 175°F (80°C) | Delicate — heat kills flavor |
| Oolong tea | 190°F (88°C) | Varies by type |
| Black tea | 208°F (98°C) | Near boiling for full extraction |
| French press / Chemex | 200°F (93°C) | Slightly off-boil |
| Pour-over / AeroPress | 195–205°F (90–96°C) | Adjust to taste |
| Herbal tisanes | 212°F (100°C) | Full boil fine |

Temperature control kettles make this precision automatic. A standard kettle that only boils gives you 212°F — fine for most teas, suboptimal for green tea and pour-over coffee.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Temperature Control vs Speed:** If you brew multiple beverage types (green tea, black tea, pour-over coffee), temperature control prevents over-extraction and bitterness. If you only boil water for instant coffee or black tea, a fast-boil basic kettle works fine.

**Gooseneck Spout:** Essential for pour-over coffee and AeroPress. The narrow curved spout allows slow, controlled pouring over coffee grounds — a wide-spout kettle makes this technique difficult. If you don't do pour-over, a gooseneck is less important.

**Capacity:** Solo or couple household: 0.6–1.0L. Family or multiple daily cups: 1.5–1.7L. Note that smaller capacity heats faster and cools faster — the Fellow's 0.6L is perfect for one cup but inconvenient for a teapot.

**Keep Warm:** Useful if you brew slowly or brew multiple cups. The Fellow's 60-minute hold is best for long pour-over sessions. 20–30 minutes covers most casual tea use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a gooseneck kettle worth it for regular coffee?**
Only if you do pour-over, AeroPress, or Chemex. For a French press or drip machine, a standard-spout kettle works equally well. For pour-over, a gooseneck is transformative — flow control is the difference between a great brew and an uneven extraction.

**What temperature kills the flavor in green tea?**
Boiling water (212°F) causes green tea to taste bitter and astringent. 175–185°F (80°C) extracts the delicate grassy, umami notes without the bitterness. Temperature control kettles make this automatic.

**Does the kettle material affect water taste?**
Stainless steel interior: neutral taste, easy to clean. Glass interior: completely neutral but fragile. Plastic interior: can impart taste, especially when new. Avoid plastic-interior kettles if taste sensitivity is a concern.

**How long does an electric kettle last?**
Quality electric kettles last 3–7 years with regular use. Budget brands often fail at the heating element or thermostat after 1–2 years. A mid-range Breville or Cuisinart represents better long-term value than replacing budget kettles every year.
