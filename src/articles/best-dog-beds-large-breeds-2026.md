## What to Look For

### Orthopedic vs Standard Foam

True orthopedic foam is 4–7 inches of high-density memory foam. Cheap beds labeled "orthopedic" use 1–2 inch egg-crate foam that compresses flat within weeks. If your dog is over 50 lbs or has any joint issues, only Big Barker or FurHaven's memory foam models qualify.

### Size Guide for Large Breeds

Measure your dog stretched out nose to tail, then add 6–12 inches to each dimension. The most common mistake is buying a bed that's technically "large" but too small for your specific dog to stretch out in.

| Dog Breed | Recommended Minimum Bed Size |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Labrador (70 lbs) | 40"×30" (Large) |
| Golden Retriever (75 lbs) | 42"×30" (Large/XL) |
| German Shepherd (80 lbs) | 42"×30" |
| Great Dane (150 lbs) | 52"×44" (Giant) |
| Mastiff (175 lbs) | 60"×48" (Giant+) |

### Machine Washable

Non-negotiable for large dogs. Dog beds accumulate dander, oils, and bacteria quickly. Beds should be washed every 2–4 weeks.
