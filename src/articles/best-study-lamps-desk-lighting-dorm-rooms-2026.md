## What Makes a Good Study Lamp for Dorms?

**Color temperature matters:** 2700–3000K (warm) is relaxing — use for reading before bed. 5000–6500K (cool/daylight) mimics daylight and improves alertness — use for focused studying. Look for a lamp with at least 2 color modes.

**Brightness control:** A single brightness level means the lamp is either too bright or not bright enough for different tasks. Minimum: 3 levels. Ideal: 5+ levels.

**Glare management:** If you study in front of a screen, a monitor light bar (like BenQ ScreenBar) eliminates the glare problem entirely. Standard desk lamps can create a bright spot that reflects off laptop screens.

**USB charging:** A lamp with a USB port eliminates one outlet from your required power strip. In dorm rooms with 2–4 wall outlets, every saved outlet matters.
