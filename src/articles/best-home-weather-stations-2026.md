## How to Choose a Home Weather Station

**Key measurements:**
- **Temperature and humidity:** Standard on all stations
- **Wind speed and direction:** Requires outdoor anemometer — most stations include this
- **Rainfall:** Tipping bucket rain gauge — included on mid-range stations and above
- **Barometric pressure:** Essential for weather forecasting — included on most stations
- **UV index / Solar radiation:** Useful for gardening, outdoor work planning
- **Air quality / CO2:** Specialty sensors on expandable systems

**Accuracy factors:**
- Temperature accuracy varies from ±1°F (Davis) to ±4°F (budget models)
- Radiation shielding on the outdoor sensor significantly improves temperature accuracy by preventing solar heating errors
- Sensor placement matters: mount outdoor sensor in a shaded, open location away from heat sources, reflective surfaces, and AC exhausts

**Connectivity options:**
- **Display only (no WiFi):** Simplest, most private, works during outages
- **WiFi to cloud:** Remote monitoring, historical data, smart home integration — requires internet
- **Weather Underground upload:** Share data publicly; access other nearby station data

**Smart home integration:**
- Ambient Weather and Ecowitt systems integrate with Amazon Alexa, Google Home, and Home Assistant
- IFTTT support allows trigger-based automations (e.g., "if wind > 30 mph, send alert")

## Placement Guide for Accurate Readings

| Sensor | Ideal Placement |
|--------|----------------|
| Temperature | Shaded, north-facing, 4–6 feet off ground, away from pavement and buildings |
| Wind | Open area, highest point possible — away from trees and structures |
| Rain gauge | Open area, away from trees — nothing blocking within 45° of vertical |
| Indoor display | Interior wall, away from windows and exterior walls |
