## Gaming Desk Cable Management: A Step-by-Step Approach

**Step 1 — Audit cables first.** Unplug everything and identify which cables you actually need. Most desks have 3–5 cables that can be removed (old USB hubs, unused chargers, HDMI cables for disconnected devices).

**Step 2 — Route before you bind.** Decide the path each cable takes before using any clips or ties. Short horizontal runs across the back of the desk. Vertical drops down one leg. All power cables grouped into the under-desk tray.

**Step 3 — Label bundles.** Before wrapping a bundle in a sleeve, label each cable with a bit of masking tape and a marker. Finding the right cable in a bundle three months later without labels wastes time.

**Step 4 — Under the desk first.** The biggest visual impact comes from getting everything off the desk surface and floor. Install the cable tray, route the surge protector and power bricks into it, and then work backward from there to the peripherals.

**Velcro over zip ties.** Velcro cable ties are reusable and don't require cutting when you need to add or swap a cable. Keep a pack on your desk for ongoing organization.
