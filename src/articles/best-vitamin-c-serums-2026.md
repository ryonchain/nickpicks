## What Makes a Vitamin C Serum Actually Work

### The Vitamin C Form Matters
- **L-Ascorbic Acid (LAA):** The gold standard with the most clinical data, but unstable at neutral pH. Requires pH below 3.5 to absorb effectively. Best results but most oxidation-prone.
- **Ascorbyl Glucoside:** Water-soluble derivative, more stable, but requires enzymatic conversion in skin. Slower onset, gentler on sensitive skin.
- **3-O-Ethyl Ascorbic Acid:** Newer stable derivative with direct activity. Converts efficiently without pH dependency. The best balance of stability and efficacy.
- **Ascorbyl Tetraisopalmitate:** Oil-soluble, excellent for dry skin and combination with niacinamide. Excellent stability.

### Concentration Sweet Spot
Below 8%, most people see minimal results. Above 20%, irritation increases without proportional benefit. The clinical sweet spot is **10–15%** for brightening and antioxidant protection.

### Packaging Is Non-Negotiable
Vitamin C oxidizes on contact with light and air. Opaque pump bottles or airless dispensers are required. Clear glass dropper bottles allow oxidation within 30–60 days of opening regardless of formulation quality.

## How to Use Vitamin C Serum

**Step 1:** Apply to clean, dry skin in the morning (vitamin C works synergistically with morning UV exposure protection).

**Step 2:** Wait 3–5 minutes for the low-pH formula to absorb before applying other products.

**Step 3:** Follow with a moisturizer, then SPF 30+. Vitamin C amplifies UV protection — this step is not optional.

**Step 4:** Don't layer with high-pH products immediately after (niacinamide, AHAs). Apply those in the evening.
