## Buying Guide: How to Choose an Electric Can Opener

**Smooth-edge vs. traditional cut.** The most meaningful choice. Smooth-edge openers cut along the outside of the lid seam, leaving no sharp edges on either the lid or the can. Traditional openers cut through the lid's top, leaving sharp edges on both pieces. If you've ever cut your finger on a can lid, a smooth-edge opener prevents this entirely. The Hamilton Beach Smooth Touch and Cuisinart SCO-60 both use smooth-edge technology.

**Corded vs. cordless.** Corded openers are more reliable for daily kitchen use — no battery management, stronger motors, and unlimited runtime. Cordless (battery-powered) openers like the Kitchen Mama are the right choice for portability: camping, RVs, and kitchens where the nearest outlet is across the room from the can storage.

**Hands-free operation.** If grip strength or hand mobility is a concern, look specifically for openers that require minimal hand involvement during the opening motion. The BLACK+DECKER Lids Off is the most hands-free option — you only need to press one button, and the device does the rest without requiring you to hold the can.

**Durability and construction.** Budget openers ($15) use mostly plastic construction and may wear out in 2-3 years with daily use. Mid-range models ($25-35) are similar. The Cuisinart SCO-60 at $50 uses stainless steel and is built for longer service life — worth the premium if you open cans daily.

**Cleaning ease.** All good electric can openers have a removable cutting assembly that's dishwasher-safe. The cutting wheel contacts food and should be cleaned after every few uses to prevent rust and residue buildup.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best electric can opener for arthritis?**
The Kitchen Mama Auto requires only one button press — no grip strength needed during operation. The BLACK+DECKER Lids Off is equally good; you place it on the can, press start, and it opens without requiring any hand involvement beyond that initial button.

**Are smooth-edge can openers safer?**
Yes. Smooth-edge openers cut the outside of the lid seam rather than through the top, leaving both the lid and the can rim smooth with no sharp edges. Traditional openers leave a sharp edge on both pieces that can cause cuts when handling.

**Can electric can openers open large #10 cans?**
Most handle standard sizes without issue. For large #10 restaurant cans, the Hamilton Beach Smooth Touch and Cuisinart SCO-60 are specifically designed to accommodate oversized cans. Check the "tall-can" capacity listed in the specs — anything rated for 7" cans will handle #10s.

**How do you clean an electric can opener?**
Remove the cutting lever after each use and rinse or dishwasher-clean it. Wipe the body with a damp cloth. Never submerge the motor unit. Regular cleaning prevents food residue and rust on the cutting wheel.

**Are cordless electric can openers worth it?**
For stationary kitchen use, corded models are more reliable. For camping, RV use, or kitchens far from outlets, a cordless model like the Kitchen Mama is genuinely worth the slight trade-off in motor power.
