## Espresso Machine Buying Guide

**Know your commitment level:**
- Want push-button coffee: super-automatic or capsule machine
- Want to learn the craft: semi-automatic
- Want serious barista skills: prosumer or lever machine

**Budget breakdown:**
- Under $200: capsule machines only at this range
- $200–$500: entry semi-autos and mid-range super-automatics
- $500–$1,000: quality semi-autos with integrated grinders, good super-automatics
- $1,000+: prosumer territory

**Don't forget the grinder:** A $50 blade grinder paired with a $700 espresso machine is a waste of money. Espresso requires consistency — invest in a proper burr grinder if you're buying a machine without one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a separate grinder?**
Yes, for semi-automatics. Grind consistency is the single most important variable in espresso extraction. Dedicated burr grinders from Baratza or DF64 are worth the investment.

**What's the difference between 15-bar and 9-bar?**
Marketing: machines are rated at 15 bars because that's the pump maximum, but espresso extracts optimally at 9 bars. A good 9-bar OPV setting matters more than the rated pump pressure.
