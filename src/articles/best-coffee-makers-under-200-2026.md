## The Right Coffee Maker Changes Your Morning

A great coffee maker does two things well: it brews at the correct temperature (195–205°F) and delivers consistent extraction every time. Most cheap drip machines miss on temperature, which is why "coffee maker coffee" has a bad reputation. The picks below don't cut that corner.

This guide covers six coffee makers under $200 that are actually worth owning — from budget-friendly programmable brewers to versatile single-serve options for households that can't agree on a pot.

---

## What to Look for in a Coffee Maker

**Brew temperature:** Optimal extraction happens at 195–205°F. Many cheap machines brew at 175°F and produce weak, under-extracted coffee. Look for SCAA certification or user testing that confirms proper temperature.

**Capacity:** A 12-cup carafe isn't always 12 actual mugs — a "cup" in coffee maker math is 5 oz. Plan accordingly if you need to serve a household.

**Programming:** Auto-brew timers mean coffee is ready when your alarm goes off. A must-have for weekday mornings.

**Carafe type:** Insulated carafes keep coffee hot for 2–3 hours without a hot plate. Glass carafes with hot plates can burn the coffee after 30–45 minutes.

**Cleanup:** Removable water reservoirs and dishwasher-safe carafes make daily cleanup much faster.
