# Ergotron LX Monitor Arm Review: Is It Worth $150 in 2026?

At roughly $150, the Ergotron LX costs two to three times more than the budget arms flooding Amazon. That gap is either completely justified or a brand tax — after living with this arm daily, the answer is clear.

---

## Quick Stats

- **Compatible monitor size:** Up to 34 inches
- **Weight capacity:** 7–25 lbs (3.2–11.3 kg)
- **VESA compatibility:** 75×75 mm and 100×100 mm
- **Arm reach:** Up to 25 inches from the post
- **Height adjustment:** Up to 13 inches of vertical range
- **Cable management:** Integrated channels run cables through the arm and post
- **Color options:** Matte black, white, polished aluminum
- **Mounting options:** Desk clamp or grommet mount (both included)
- **Tilt range:** -5° to +15° monitor tilt; 360° rotation at the monitor head

---

## What I Like

- **The tension is dialed in and stays there.** The Ergotron LX uses a Constant Force mechanism with a hex adjustment screw. Set it once for your monitor's weight and it holds position indefinitely — no sagging.
- **One-hand repositioning is genuinely one-handed.** Push up, pull toward you, or swing to the side using two fingers. Budget arms require both hands and usually a grunt.
- **The arm reaches far enough to actually matter.** 25 inches of reach versus the 15–17 inches most $50 arms provide. The difference in positioning range is immediately apparent.
- **Build quality is immediately obvious.** The aluminum construction has zero flex or wobble at any extension. No screen shake when typing — surprisingly fatiguing on cheaper steel arms.
- **Cable management is clean and functional.** Cables route internally from monitor to desk through the arm channels, not zip-tied to the outside.

---

## What Could Be Better

- **The hex wrench adjustment is fiddly the first time.** Dialing in tension requires the included hex key, and if your monitor is unusually light or heavy, you'll spend five to ten minutes finding the right setting.
- **It's overkill for light monitors.** The weight range starts at 7 lbs — lighter monitors can feel slightly over-tensioned even at the lowest setting.
- **The clamp footprint is wide.** The desk clamp spans about 3.5 inches and requires at least 2.5 inches of desk thickness. Thin surfaces or desks with cable trays can constrain mounting placement.

---

## Who It's For / Who Should Skip It

The Ergotron LX is the right buy if you spend 6+ hours a day at a monitor, have a 24–34 inch display in the 12–20 lb range, and value a desk setup that stays exactly where you put it. It's particularly worth it for dual-monitor users who've been burned by drift on a cheaper option — the contrast is immediate.

Skip it if you're mounting a small secondary monitor you rarely touch, working from a temporary desk, or comparing it against the Ergotron LX Dual specifically for a two-screen setup (a different model handles that better).

---

## Verdict

**Yes, buy it.** The Ergotron LX earns its price through build quality that outlasts multiple monitors, positioning that doesn't require daily re-adjustment, and reach that cheaper arms physically cannot match. This is the arm most desk setup regulars end up at after cycling through two or three cheaper options first.
