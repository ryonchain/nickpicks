## Pool Float Buying Tips

**Material matters:** Look for at least 0.4mm PVC for anything expected to last a season. Thin vinyl ($10 floats) cracks at seam points within weeks.

**Check the weight rating:** Budget floats often list capacity but use minimal reinforcement. If you're over 200 lbs, buy a float explicitly rated for your weight.

**Inflation time:** A full-body float requires a pump. Hand-pumping a 76" float is exhausting. An electric pump (Intex Electric Quick-Fill, ~$20) pays for itself on the first use.

**Storage:** Even quality floats degrade if stored inflated in direct sun. Deflate fully, dry before folding, and store in a bag or bin.

**Summer timing:** Pool float availability drops in late summer and prices spike. Buy in June, not August.
