  - question: "Is 7.1 surround sound worth it in a gaming headset?"
    answer: "Virtual 7.1 surround sound helps with directional audio cues — useful in competitive shooters where hearing footsteps and shots from specific directions matters. However, quality stereo often sounds better for music and cinematic games than low-quality virtual surround. At the under-$100 price point, look for headsets with decent virtual surround rather than expecting true multi-driver surround, which doesn't exist in a stereo headphone at any price."
  - question: "What should I look for in a gaming headset microphone?"
    answer: "For under $100, a solid microphone means: clear voice pickup (check for 'cardioid' or 'unidirectional' patterns that focus on your voice), noise reduction or a flexible boom that you can position close to your mouth, and a mute switch. Flip-up mutes are most convenient. USB headsets generally sound better on PC than 3.5mm. Avoid headsets with omnidirectional mics — they pick up keyboard and room noise equally."
  - question: "Can I use a gaming headset with multiple platforms?"
    answer: "Most wired gaming headsets with a 3.5mm plug work on PS5, Xbox Series X, Switch (handheld mode), and PC out of the box. USB headsets work on PC and sometimes PS5 (which has USB-A/C ports). Xbox requires either a 3.5mm plug into the controller or a dedicated Xbox wireless headset. Check platform compatibility before buying — specifically if you need wireless Xbox support."
  - question: "Are closed-back or open-back headsets better for gaming?"
    answer: "Closed-back headsets are better for most gaming scenarios because they block ambient noise (roommates, traffic) and prevent audio leakage. Open-back headsets have better soundstage and imaging — useful for competitive gaming in a quiet room — but leak sound and provide less noise isolation. At the under-$100 price point, almost all gaming headsets are closed-back, which is the right default choice."
