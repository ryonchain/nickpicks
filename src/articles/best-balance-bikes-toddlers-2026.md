## What to Look for in a Balance Bike

### Seat Height (Most Important)
The minimum seat height should be 1–2 inches below your child's inseam (measured from crotch to floor). Too high and they can't touch the ground; too low and they can't extend their legs to push. Measure first, then match.

### Weight
Lighter is better. A heavy bike is frustrating for small kids to lift, turn, and maneuver. Under 7 lbs is ideal; avoid anything over 9 lbs.

### Tire Type
- **Foam tires:** Maintenance-free, never go flat, smooth ride on pavement
- **Air tires:** Better traction on varied terrain, more realistic feel, can go flat (rare)

### Frame Material
- **Steel:** Durable, slightly heavier, typically $50–$110
- **Aluminum:** Lighter, slightly higher price range, $100–$200
- **Wood:** Looks cute, heavier than steel, harder to adjust, mostly novelty

### Age/Inseam Chart

| Child's Inseam | Recommended Seat Height |
|----------------|------------------------|
| 10"–12" | 9.5"–11" (very young) |
| 12"–15" | 11"–13" |
| 15"–18" | 13"–16" |
| 18"+ | 16"+ (larger kids) |

## FAQ

**Q: What age should a child start on a balance bike?**
A: Most kids are ready between 18–24 months. The key indicator is whether your child can walk steadily. Earlier is generally better — kids who start at 18 months often pedal by age 3.

**Q: Do balance bikes come with helmets?**
A: No. Always purchase a properly fitted helmet separately. For toddlers, look for MIPS certification and a snug fit.

**Q: How long will a toddler use a balance bike?**
A: Most kids use a balance bike for 1–3 years before transitioning to a pedal bike. The Strider and similar bikes fit roughly ages 18 months to 5 years with height adjustments.

**Q: Training wheels vs. balance bike — which is better?**
A: Balance bikes. Training wheels teach kids to lean INTO turns (wrong habit) and rely on the wheels for balance rather than developing it intrinsically. Kids on balance bikes consistently transition to pedal bikes faster than training-wheel kids.
