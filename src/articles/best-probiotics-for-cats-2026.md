## When to Use Cat Probiotics

**Post-antibiotic:** Any antibiotic course disrupts the feline microbiome. Start probiotics the same day (2+ hours after antibiotic dose) and continue for 2 weeks post-antibiotic.

**Stress events:** Boarding, vet visits, new pets in the home, moving — all trigger stress-related GI changes in cats. Start 2–3 days before the stressor.

**Diet transitions:** Changing food brands or protein sources. Add probiotics for 7–10 days during transition.

**Chronic IBD management:** As adjunct to veterinary treatment — not standalone.

**Kitten introduction to new home:** The stress of rehoming commonly causes diarrhea in kittens. Fortiflora is often sent home with kittens from breeders and shelters.

---

## Probiotic Dosing Tips for Cats

**Start low:** Begin with half the recommended dose for 3–5 days to minimize GI adjustment symptoms (mild gas or loose stool can occur as the microbiome shifts).

**Consistency:** Daily use is more effective than intermittent use. The microbiome requires consistent input to maintain shifts.

**Temperature:** Probiotics with live cultures should not be added to hot food — add to food at room temperature or cooler.

**Wet food is better:** Wet food is more palatable and the additional moisture helps the probiotic powder integrate without clumping.
