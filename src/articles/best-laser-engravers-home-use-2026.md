## Laser Engraver Basics

### Types of Home Laser Engravers

**Diode lasers (most common at home):**
- Wavelength: 450nm (blue light)
- Materials: wood, leather, acrylic (colored), rubber, paper, painted/coated metal, dark anodized aluminum
- **Cannot engrave:** clear acrylic, glass directly, uncoated metal, food-safe surfaces without coatings
- Typical power: 5W–40W (optical output)
- Price range: $150–$800

**CO2 lasers:**
- Wavelength: 10,600nm (infrared)
- Materials: wood, acrylic (including clear), glass, leather, fabric, most organics
- Can cut thick materials cleanly
- Price range: $400–$3,000+ for home-capable machines
- Require ventilation; more complex setup

### Key Specs Explained

**Optical watt output** is what matters for cutting/engraving speed and depth. Ignore "equivalent wattage" claims — look for the actual optical output.

**Spot size** (focus point size) determines engraving resolution. Smaller = finer detail. Look for spot sizes under 0.1mm for fine detail work.

**Work area** determines your project size. 400mm × 400mm is the most common (roughly 15.7" × 15.7").

**Air assist** (pump that blows air on the cutting point) significantly improves cut quality and reduces burn marks. Now standard on better machines.

## Safety Essentials

**Never engrave these without special coatings:**
- Clear acrylic (diode lasers pass through — use a spray coating first)
- PVC/vinyl (releases chlorine gas — genuinely dangerous)
- Mirrors (reflects laser back at the head)
- Polycarbonate

**Required safety equipment:**
- **Laser safety glasses** (~$20–40) rated for your laser's wavelength (450nm for blue diode lasers)
- **Ventilation** — open frame machines require a window fan or external air extraction
- **Fire extinguisher nearby** — especially when cutting
