# Best Archery Bows for Beginners in 2026

Archery is one of the few sports where an improperly sized setup makes progress nearly impossible. The two critical decisions are bow type (recurve vs. compound) and draw weight (how many pounds of force required to draw the string). Too much draw weight causes form breakdown and injury; too little reduces learning feedback. Most beginners start 15–25 lbs for youth and 25–35 lbs for adults.

---

## Recurve vs. Compound

**Recurve:** Traditional design, Olympic format. Requires more form development; the archer holds the full draw weight at peak draw. Simpler mechanically; no moving parts to service. Better for beginners learning form fundamentals.

**Compound:** Uses a cam-and-pulley system with "let-off" — at full draw, the archer only holds 65–80% of peak draw weight. Allows longer aim time and higher accuracy potential. More equipment to set up and maintain. Better for hunting applications.

## What You Need Besides the Bow

- **Arrows:** Matched to your draw length and bow weight. Carbon arrows are standard for beginners. Buy pre-cut to length from an archery shop.
- **Armguard:** Protects the forearm from string slap (painful without it, especially while learning form).
- **Finger tab or release aid:** For recurve — a finger tab. For compound — a mechanical release.
- **Target:** Foam block targets work for carbon arrows. Avoid hay bales (arrows are difficult to remove).
