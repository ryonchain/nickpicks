## Buying Guide

### RGBIC vs. Standard RGB Strips

**Standard RGB** strips change to a single color across the entire length. Changing to red makes the entire strip red.

**RGBIC** (independently controlled RGB) strips divide the strip into segments, each capable of displaying a different color simultaneously. Gradient effects, multiple-color chasing patterns, and section-specific control are only possible with RGBIC. For room ambiance or gaming aesthetics, RGBIC is worth the small price premium.

### Smart Control Options

| Protocol | Hub Required | Best For |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Wi-Fi (2.4GHz) | No | Most users — no extra hardware |
| Zigbee (Philips Hue) | Hue Bridge | Hue ecosystem users |
| Bluetooth | No (but short range) | Simple setups without Wi-Fi at desk |
| Matter | No (or smart hub) | Future-proofing for smart home |

### How Long a Strip Do You Need?

### Adhesive Quality: The Silent Killer

Many cheap LED strips fail within months because the adhesive backing peels off. Look for strips that include additional mounting clips or channels for sections where the adhesive might fail (corners, transitions from horizontal to vertical surfaces). Govee includes clips; budget alternatives often don't.

## FAQ

**Are LED strip lights safe?**
Yes when properly used. Ensure the power adapter is rated for the strip length and LED count. Don't run strips through areas where they can be pinched or stepped on. Keep away from flammable materials if using high-brightness strips that generate heat.

**Do LED strips work with Siri/Apple HomeKit?**
Philips Hue strips work natively with Apple HomeKit. Govee and Kasa strips require an Alexa or Google Home intermediary — direct Siri control isn't available. For native HomeKit integration, Philips Hue is the only mainstream option.

**How long do LED strips last?**
Quality LED strips (Govee, Philips Hue) are rated for 25,000–50,000 hours. At 8 hours per day, that's 8–17 years. The power supply and connectors typically fail before the LEDs.

**Can I cut LED strips to size?**
Most strips have marked cut points every few inches. Cut only at these points — cutting elsewhere permanently damages the circuit. After cutting, both sections are functional; the cut end of the second section is exposed and should be capped with end caps to prevent shorts.
