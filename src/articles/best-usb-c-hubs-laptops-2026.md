Modern laptops are thin and capable — but the port situation is a real problem. MacBook Air M3: two USB-C ports. Dell XPS 13: two Thunderbolt 4 ports and a headphone jack. Lenovo Yoga: three USB-C/Thunderbolt, no USB-A. Every year, manufacturers trim the port count, and every year, users need more ways to connect things.

A USB-C hub solves this immediately. One cable plugs into your laptop and gives you back HDMI, multiple USB-A ports, SD card slots, and pass-through charging — no dock, no desk clutter, no dongle cascade.

Here are the five best USB-C hubs for laptops in 2026, with a focus on the 7-in-1 form factor that works best for most users.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose a USB-C Hub for Your Laptop

**Check your laptop's USB-C spec first.** Not all USB-C ports are equal. Thunderbolt 4 ports support more bandwidth and can drive higher-resolution displays than standard USB-C ports. If your laptop has Thunderbolt 4, you can use any USB-C hub but get better results (especially for dual monitors) with a Thunderbolt dock. For standard USB-C, any hub in this list works.

**Power Delivery wattage.** The hub must pass enough power to charge your laptop. MacBook Air M2 requires 30W minimum to charge (67W to charge at full speed). MacBook Pro 14" needs up to 96W. Check your laptop's maximum charging wattage and match it against the hub's PD spec.

**4K vs. 1080p HDMI.** 4K monitors are standard for anyone doing design, video editing, or productivity work where screen real estate matters. Make sure the hub explicitly supports 4K@60Hz — some budget hubs support 4K only at 30Hz, which makes mouse movement noticeably choppy.

**When to step up to a docking station.** A hub connects via a short cable and moves with your laptop. A [USB-C docking station](/articles/best-usb-c-docking-stations-2026/) is larger, stays on your desk, charges via its own power adapter, and supports higher-bandwidth peripherals. If your desk setup is permanent and you want more ports and more power, a dock is the better investment.

**USB hub vs. USB-C hub.** A USB hub (USB-A input) just adds more USB-A ports. A USB-C hub takes a USB-C connection and adds multiple port types including HDMI. If your laptop only has USB-C ports, you need a USB-C hub. See our broader [USB hub guide](/articles/best-usb-hubs-remote-work-2026/) for USB-A expansion options.
