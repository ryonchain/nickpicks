## How to Choose the Right Smart Plug

### Do You Need Energy Monitoring?

Energy monitoring tracks how much electricity each device consumes. It's useful for:
- Identifying electricity hogs (space heaters, old refrigerators, gaming PCs)
- Verifying devices are truly off (phantom load)
- Calculating monthly costs per device

Kasa and Govee include energy monitoring at budget prices — this feature is worth having.

### Hub or No Hub?

All plugs in this roundup work without a hub — they connect directly to your 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi network. No Samsung SmartThings hub, no Philips Hue Bridge, no proprietary gateway required.

### Amp Rating

Most smart plugs are rated 10A or 15A. Standard household devices (lamps, fans, coffee makers, TVs) are well within 15A. Space heaters and window AC units may exceed limits — check the label before using a smart plug on high-draw appliances.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do smart plugs work with any outlet?**
Yes — smart plugs work with standard US 2-prong or 3-prong outlets (Type A/B). They require a 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi network (most don't support 5 GHz).

**Can smart plugs save money on electricity?**
Yes, in two ways: 1) Scheduling turns devices off when not needed (eliminate standby/phantom loads). 2) Energy monitoring identifies which devices cost the most, so you can make informed decisions.

**Are smart plugs safe for high-wattage appliances?**
Smart plugs rated 15A can handle most home appliances safely. Avoid using them with space heaters (>1,500W) or air conditioners without verifying the amperage. Always check the appliance label.

**What happens if the Wi-Fi goes out?**
Most smart plugs default to their last-known state (on or off) during a Wi-Fi outage. Manual physical control (pressing the button on the plug) still works. Schedules resume when Wi-Fi reconnects.

**Can I use a smart plug on a smart device that already has smart features?**
You can, but it's redundant — smart plugs cut power completely at the outlet level, which may interfere with device pairing or settings. Use smart plugs on "dumb" devices (lamps, fans, space heaters) for best results.
