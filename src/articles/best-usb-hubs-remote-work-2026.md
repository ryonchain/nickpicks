## Buying Guide: How to Choose a USB Hub

**Start with what your laptop actually outputs.** USB-C hubs connect through a USB-C port. If your laptop has Thunderbolt 4 ports, you can use either Thunderbolt docks or standard USB-C hubs — but Thunderbolt docks deliver more bandwidth and better display support. If you have USB-A ports, you need a USB-A hub (different product).

**How many monitors?** Single monitor needs only standard USB-C HDMI. Dual monitors require either Thunderbolt support or a hub with dual HDMI/DisplayPort outputs.

**Power Delivery wattage.** A hub or dock that charges your laptop should deliver at least 60W for thin-and-light laptops and 90W+ for 16-inch MacBook Pros or power-hungry Windows machines. Under 60W and your laptop will slowly drain while connected.

**Passive vs. powered hubs.** Most compact hubs are bus-powered (draw power from your laptop). If you're connecting many high-power devices (external hard drives, charging phones), look for a hub with its own power adapter.
