# Best Stud Finders & Wall Scanners in 2026

Drilling into a wall without knowing what's behind it is one of the easiest ways to ruin a weekend project — or worse, hit a live electrical wire or copper pipe. A good stud finder eliminates that guesswork, marking the exact edges of wood studs, metal framing, and deeper objects so you can anchor shelves, mount TVs, and hang cabinets with confidence. The difference between a $15 magnetic pin tool and a $70 digital scanner isn't just features — it's accuracy, and inaccuracy in this task has real consequences.

Modern stud finders break into three categories: edge-detection units that scan the wall in passes, multi-sensor units that light up all studs simultaneously, and deep-scanning detectors that can also locate AC wiring and pipes. The right choice depends on your wall type, how often you use the tool, and how much you trust the readings you're getting. This guide covers the best option at each level, from foolproof entry-level tools to professional-grade multi-material scanners.

## What to Look For

- **Detection modes:** A quality stud finder should detect wood, metal, and live AC wiring. Don't buy a tool that only finds one material type unless you have a specific use case.
- **Scan depth:** Standard 1/2-inch drywall requires only 1.5-inch detection depth, but plaster walls, tile backer, and exterior walls can require 3+ inches. Know your walls before buying.
- **Calibration sensitivity:** Some scanners require you to calibrate against an empty wall section before each use. Poor technique here causes false readings. Tools like the Franklin ProSensor eliminate calibration entirely.
- **Center vs. edge detection:** Edge-detection tools mark both edges of a stud and require you to find the middle manually. Center-finding modes do this automatically — worth the small extra cost.
- **AC live wire alert:** This is a safety feature, not a luxury. Never buy a stud finder that doesn't include AC wire detection if you're drilling into finished walls.
- **Wall type:** Plaster-and-lath, tile, concrete block, and steel stud framing each behave differently under a scanner. The Bosch GMS120 handles the widest range; magnetic finders work on none of these materials.
