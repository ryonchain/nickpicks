## Prime Day Strategy: How to Actually Save

**Set alerts the week before:** Use the Amazon price tracker feature or a third-party tool like Honey to track items you want. Prime Day deals sometimes match or beat pre-sale "Lightning Deal" prices.

**Check price history:** Use CamelCamelCamel.com to see if an item's "Prime Day price" is actually lower than its recent historical average. Some products see their "regular price" artificially inflated before Prime Day to make the discount look bigger.

**Prime Day Early Access:** Amazon now runs deals 24–48 hours before the official start for Prime members. The standing desk and monitor deals often surface here.

**Avoid impulse category:** Don't buy furniture or large items unless you researched them before Prime Day. Return shipping on a 50-lb standing desk is painful.
