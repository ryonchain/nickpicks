## What to Look for in an Infant Car Seat

### Safety Ratings
Check NHTSA ratings (1–5 stars) and IIHS "Best Bet" or "Good Bet" designations. All seats on this list meet federal safety minimums — top picks exceed them.

### Weight and Portability
Infant car seat carriers range from 5 to 13 lbs. If you'll be carrying baby in the seat frequently, lighter is significantly better on your back.

### Weight Limits
Most infant-only seats go up to 30–35 lbs. Some convertible-style infant seats go to 40+ lbs, extending the usable window.

### Ease of Installation
Look for level indicators, easy-to-thread LATCH connectors, and clear instruction manuals. Incorrect installation is the #1 car seat safety issue.

### Stroller Compatibility
If you want a travel system, confirm your car seat clicks into your stroller model before buying. Most brands publish compatibility lists.

## Infant Car Seat vs. Convertible Car Seat

**Infant car seat (this list):** Rear-facing only, smaller shell that fits newborns better, removable carrier. Best for 0–12 months.

**Convertible car seat:** Used from infant through toddler; cannot be removed from car. Less portable, but one purchase covers more years.

For newborns especially, an infant seat provides a better fit and allows you to move a sleeping baby without disturbing them.

## FAQ

**Q: When does a baby outgrow an infant car seat?**
A: When they exceed the seat's height or weight limit (check the label), whichever comes first. Most babies outgrow by 12–18 months.

**Q: Can I buy a used infant car seat?**
A: Only if you know the full history. Car seats involved in accidents should never be reused, and expired seats (6–10 years from manufacture) are unsafe. When in doubt, buy new.

**Q: Do I need to register my car seat?**
A: Yes. Register with the manufacturer to receive safety recall notices.

**Q: Is installation by a certified technician worth it?**
A: Absolutely. Many fire stations and hospitals offer free car seat inspections. A certified technician can verify your installation is correct.
