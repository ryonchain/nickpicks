## Head-to-Head: Key Decision Points

### Reliability

LG wins historically — the Linear Compressor design has fewer moving parts and a proven track record. LG's 10-year compressor warranty (vs Samsung's 5-year) reflects this confidence. Samsung has faced documented ice maker issues on certain French door models, though newer 2024–2026 models show significant improvement.

**Winner: LG** (track record), Samsung improving

### Smart Features

Samsung Family Hub is more comprehensive — touchscreen control, AI food tracking, SmartThings integration. LG ThinQ app is excellent for remote control and diagnostics but less flashy. If smart home ecosystem matters to you:
- **Amazon/Alexa/SmartThings users:** Samsung
- **Google Home users:** Both work; LG slightly better
- **Apple HomeKit users:** Neither supports HomeKit natively

**Winner: Samsung** (Family Hub), LG (app quality and diagnostics)

### Ice Maker

LG Craft Ice (spherical slow-melt ice) is a unique differentiator if you entertain or drink spirits. Samsung's dual ice maker is more standard but reliable in newer models. For functional ice, both work; for premium cocktail ice, LG wins.

**Winner: LG** (Craft Ice unique), Samsung (dual ice on most models)

### Storage and Layout

Samsung FlexZone drawer is a unique feature — convertible temperature zone is genuinely useful. LG Door-in-Door gives quick access to frequently used items. Both are practical innovations; which matters more depends on your household needs.

**Winner: Tie** (different strengths)

### Price

Samsung and LG are comparable at similar specification levels. Samsung Family Hub models cost more ($400–800 premium) for the touchscreen features. LG Craft Ice adds ~$200–300 over comparable standard models.

**Winner: Tie**
