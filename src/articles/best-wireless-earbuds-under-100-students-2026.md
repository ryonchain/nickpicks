## How to Choose Wireless Earbuds for Students

**Fit matters most for all-day wear:** In-ear (ear tip) earbuds with multiple tip sizes create a seal that improves both passive noise isolation and bass response. Test the fit first — earbuds that don't seal properly sound hollow and fall out.

**ANC for students:**
- Strong ANC (Samsung Buds2, EarFun Air Pro 3, Anker P3): Effective in libraries, dorms, and transit
- No ANC (JLab, SoundPEATS): Suitable for workouts or casual listening; won't block dorm noise

**For Zoom calls and online classes:** Prioritize call quality. EarFun Air Pro 3 has the best 6-mic call quality on this list. Samsung Galaxy Buds2 are a close second. SoundPEATS and JLab are adequate but not impressive.

**Battery reality:** "X hours" battery claims are at moderate volume without ANC. Real-world battery is typically 20–30% lower at higher volumes or with ANC on.

## FAQ

**Will under-$100 earbuds work as well as AirPods Pro for studying?**
For pure audio quality and passive isolation, many under-$100 earbuds match or exceed mid-tier AirPods. ANC strength in premium earbuds (AirPods Pro, Sony WF-1000XM5) is stronger than budget options — a meaningful difference in very loud environments. For most dorm and library use cases, the EarFun Air Pro 3 and Anker P3 deliver sufficient ANC.

**Do wireless earbuds work with both iPhone and Android?**
Yes — Bluetooth earbuds work with any device. However, Samsung Galaxy Buds2 have the deepest Android/Samsung integration. AirPods have the deepest iOS integration. For cross-platform use (laptop + iPhone + Android), the EarFun Air Pro 3 with Multipoint is the most flexible.

**How do I prevent earbuds from falling out during lectures?**
Use the correct ear tip size (included with most earbuds). The tip should create a gentle seal — not painful, but snug. If standard round tips don't fit, look for earbuds that include wing tips or sport fins for additional stability.
