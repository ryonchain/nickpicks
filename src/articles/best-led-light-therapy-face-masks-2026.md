## LED Light Therapy Buying Guide

### Understanding Wavelengths

**Red light (620–700nm):** Stimulates fibroblasts to produce collagen, reduces inflammation, improves skin texture. The workhorse for anti-aging.

**Near-infrared (800–880nm):** Penetrates deeper than red light, reaching subcutaneous tissue. Helps with deeper inflammation, wound healing, and cellular repair.

**Blue light (415–430nm):** Kills P. acnes bacteria responsible for breakouts. Use 2–3x per week for active acne.

**Green light (520–560nm):** Targets melanin, helps fade hyperpigmentation and even skin tone.

### What Actually Matters

**Irradiance (mW/cm²):** The power density delivered to skin. Higher is better — devices with low irradiance require much longer treatment times to achieve equivalent results.

**Wavelength accuracy:** Cheap devices claim 630nm but emit at 660nm — a meaningful clinical difference. Stick with established brands whose wavelength specs are verified.

**Flexible vs. rigid:** Flexible silicone masks conform to facial contours, delivering light to the nose, chin, and jaw that rigid masks miss. This matters more than LED count.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long until I see results?**
Most users see changes in skin texture within 4 weeks and more significant collagen-related improvements at 8–12 weeks. Consistency matters — missing weeks extends the timeline.

**Can LED masks cause eye damage?**
Never use an LED mask without the eye shields provided, and avoid staring at the LEDs. All masks in this guide are designed to be safe around closed eyes when used as directed.

**How often should I use an LED mask?**
Most devices recommend 3–5 sessions per week. More frequent use doesn't necessarily accelerate results — follow the manufacturer's protocol.

**Are LED masks safe for all skin types?**
Generally yes, but those with certain photosensitizing conditions or on medications that cause light sensitivity should consult a dermatologist before starting.

**Do I need to use skincare products with the mask?**
You can use the mask on clean, dry skin. Some practitioners apply a lightweight serum before treatment to enhance absorption; avoid heavy occlusive products that block light penetration.
