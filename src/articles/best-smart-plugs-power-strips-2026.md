## Smart Plug Buying Guide

### Hub-Free vs. Hub-Required: Do You Need a Hub?

Most modern smart plugs run on Wi-Fi and require no hub whatsoever. The exception is Zigbee-based plugs (like older SmartThings devices) which require a Zigbee hub. All five picks in this roundup are hub-free — just plug in and connect via their app.

### Energy Monitoring: Is It Worth It?

If you're trying to understand your electricity bill or identify energy-hungry devices, energy monitoring is invaluable. The Kasa EP25 typically shows which devices cost $8/month vs. $0.50/month. For devices that run continuously (space heaters, fans, refrigerators), the monitoring pays for the plug within weeks.

### Scheduling Best Practices

- **Coffee maker**: Schedule on 15 minutes before your wake time
- **TV**: Schedule off at a late time to prevent falling-asleep-with-TV-on power waste
- **Phone charger**: Schedule off after 2–3 hours to prevent overcharging
- **Outdoor lights**: Use sunrise/sunset scheduling (Kasa EP25 supports this natively)

## FAQs

**Can smart plugs control anything plugged into them?**
They control power — on or off. Devices that maintain their last state when power is restored (most lamps, fans, some coffee makers) work perfectly. Devices that require button presses to turn on after a power cut (many TVs, some coffee makers with programmable buttons) won't work as expected.

**Will a smart plug work during a Wi-Fi outage?**
Most plugs lose remote/voice control during an outage but retain any locally cached schedules. Physical on/off still works by pressing the button on the plug itself.

**Are smart plugs safe for space heaters?**
Check the plug's amperage rating against the heater's draw. Most 1500W space heaters draw 12.5A — within the 15A limit of plugs in this roundup. Never use a smart plug with heaters that exceed 15A (1800W). Always confirm the plug's max wattage rating in its specs.
