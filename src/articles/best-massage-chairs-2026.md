## Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Massage Chair

### S-Track vs L-Track vs SL-Track

**S-track** follows the S-shape of the cervical and lumbar spine — neck to lower back. It misses the glutes and hamstrings entirely. **L-track** extends the massage path from neck all the way down through the glutes and upper hamstrings — where a disproportionate amount of tension accumulates from sitting. **SL-track** combines both patterns in a single extended rail. For anyone with lower back tension, hip tightness, or who sits for work, L-track or SL-track is meaningfully better.

### Zero Gravity Explained

Zero gravity reclines the chair so your knees are slightly above your heart, distributing your weight across the chair rather than concentrating it at the spine and hips. This reduces spinal compression and allows the rollers to work with more consistent pressure. 3–4 position zero gravity provides more precise customization than 2-position.

### Body Scan Technology

Quality body scan technology uses sensors to map your shoulder height, spine length, and body weight, then auto-adjusts roller starting positions and pressure. This is especially important for users significantly shorter or taller than average — poorly calibrated rollers dig into shoulder blades rather than working along the muscle.

### Airbag Coverage

More airbags cover more body parts — but quality matters more than quantity. Shoulder, hip, calf, and foot airbag coverage addresses common sitting-related tension. Airbags work via compression cycles that improve circulation and reduce swelling, complementing roller pressure massage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should I use a massage chair per session?**
15–30 minutes per session, 1–2 times per day is generally recommended. Overuse can cause muscle soreness — the rollers create real pressure. Start with 15-minute sessions and adjust based on how your body responds.

**Are massage chairs good for back pain?**
Clinical studies show regular massage chair use reduces chronic lower back pain intensity and frequency. L-track chairs are more effective for lumbar pain than S-track models because they address the full tension chain from back through glutes.

**How much electricity does a massage chair use?**
Most consume 150–300 watts during operation — about the same as two incandescent light bulbs. A 30-minute daily session costs approximately $3–7 per month at average US electricity rates.

**Can I use a massage chair while pregnant?**
Consult your OB/GYN. Most manufacturers advise against use during the first trimester. After that, chairs with adjustable airbag pressure and the ability to disable specific zones (abdomen) are safer. Avoid heat settings during pregnancy.
