## How to Choose the Right Beginner Coffee Grinder

**Why not a blade grinder:** Blade grinders chop coffee unevenly, creating a mix of fine dust and large chunks. The fines over-extract (bitter), the chunks under-extract (weak/sour), and the combined result is coffee that tastes worse than a consistent medium grind.

**Burr grinder types:** Conical burrs (all picks above) are more common, quieter, and suitable for beginners. Flat burrs appear in professional espresso grinders — not the right starting point.

**Grind settings:** More settings = more precision. 15-40 settings covers most home brewing needs. If espresso is a future goal, prioritize 35+ settings (Fellow Opus, Baratza Encore ESP).

**Hopper vs. single-dose:** Hoppers are convenient for daily drip coffee routines. Single-dose grinding (weighing fresh each time) produces better results but requires a scale and more deliberate workflow.

**Build quality matters:** A $50 grinder that lasts 18 months costs more over 5 years than a $170 Baratza Encore that gets repaired and lasts a decade. Factor total cost of ownership into your decision.
