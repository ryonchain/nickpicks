## What Was Overhyped (Or a Bad Deal)

### Lightning Deals with Limited Inventory
Many "Lightning Deals" were posted with 50–200 units available. They sold out in minutes, leaving most people with a "Deal Ended" message. These generate traffic but most shoppers get nothing.

### Clothing and Fashion
Amazon's clothing discounts during Prime Day are modest (10–20%) compared to category-specific sales from fashion retailers. If you're shopping for clothes, wait for end-of-season clearance.

### Refurbished Tech at "Deal" Prices
Several third-party sellers listed refurbished phones and laptops at prices that weren't materially different from usual. Always compare against Amazon Renewed (certified) and check the regular price first.

### TVs Below 55 Inches
Small TV deals weren't impressive. 65"+ OLED and QLED sets had strong deals; 32"–50" sets saw marginal discounts.

## What to Wait For (Don't Buy Now)

**Mattresses**: Labor Day (August 29 – September 1, 2026) is the better time for mattresses. Brands like Nectar, Purple, and Casper run their deepest promotions of the year on Labor Day.

**Winter apparel**: Wait until September–October for fall/winter clothing deals.

**Black Friday tech deals**: If your headphone or laptop purchase isn't urgent, Black Friday 2026 (November 27) will revisit Prime Day prices and sometimes go lower on select items.

**Gaming consoles**: Console prices are rigid. No console had a meaningful Prime Day discount in 2026.
