## Cookware Material Guide

### Stainless Steel (with aluminum core)
- **Best for:** Searing, browning, making sauces
- **Pros:** Durable, oven safe, dishwasher safe, non-reactive
- **Cons:** Food sticks without proper technique (preheat and oil the pan)

### Nonstick (PTFE/Teflon)
- **Best for:** Eggs, fish, delicate foods
- **Pros:** Easy cleanup, forgiving for beginners
- **Cons:** Degrades over time (replace every 3-5 years), not for high-heat searing

### Ceramic Nonstick
- **Best for:** Health-conscious cooks, same uses as nonstick
- **Pros:** PTFE-free, more heat resistant than Teflon
- **Cons:** Less slippery than PTFE initially, degrades faster if overheated

### Cast Iron
- **Best for:** Searing steaks, cornbread, slow cooking
- **Pros:** Lasts generations, gets better with use, zero coatings
- **Cons:** Heavy, requires maintenance (seasoning and drying)

### Carbon Steel
- **Best for:** Woks, crepes, omelets
- **Pros:** Lighter than cast iron, heats fast, develops natural seasoning
- **Cons:** Reactive (avoid acidic foods), requires seasoning

## What to Look for When Buying

**Gauge/thickness:** Thicker = more even heating and durability. Look for 3mm+ for stainless.

**Core material:** Aluminum core for heat distribution; copper core for the fastest response.

**Number of plies:** 3-ply is sufficient; 5-ply is premium. 1-ply stainless has hot spots.

**Oven safety temp:** Look for 400°F+ minimum; 500°F+ is better for oven finishing.

**Induction compatibility:** If you have or might get an induction cooktop, check for magnetic stainless base.

---

## Care Instructions

- **Stainless:** Preheat before adding oil. Don't overcrowd. Barkeeper's Friend removes discoloration.
- **Nonstick/ceramic:** Never metal utensils. Medium heat maximum. Replace when coating damages.
- **Cast iron:** Dry completely after washing. Rub light oil while warm. Never soak in water.

## FAQ

**Q: How long should cookware last?**
A: Cast iron: generations. Stainless (quality): 20+ years. Nonstick: 3-5 years before coating degrades.

**Q: Do I need a full set or individual pieces?**
A: Start with a 10" skillet and 3 qt saucepan. Add a Dutch oven. These cover 90% of home cooking tasks.

**Q: Is expensive cookware worth it?**
A: For stainless, yes. The heat distribution difference between budget and premium stainless is significant. For nonstick, less so — even premium nonstick eventually degrades.

**Q: What cookware works on induction?**
A: Cast iron and stainless with a magnetic base. Most modern sets specify induction compatibility. Test with a magnet — if it sticks to the bottom, it works.
