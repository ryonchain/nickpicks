## What to Look for in a Fall Sweater

**Fiber type:**
- **Cashmere:** Warmest per weight, softest hand feel, most expensive. Real cashmere pills less than blends if cared for properly.
- **Wool/merino:** Natural warmth, moisture-wicking, odor-resistant. More structured than cashmere.
- **Cotton/waffle knit:** Breathable for mild fall days. Less warm than wool but easier care.
- **Acrylic blends:** Affordable, washable, holds color well. Less breathable than natural fibers but practical.

**Fit to consider:**
- **Fitted:** Goes under jackets and blazers. Classic look.
- **Relaxed/boyfriend:** Casual, layers over turtlenecks, versatile.
- **Oversized:** Statement-making. Works alone or over slim bottoms.

**Care:**
Most cashmere and wool knits require hand wash or delicate cycle. Acrylic blends are typically machine washable. Air dry flat to prevent stretching — never put a knit sweater in the dryer.
