## Buying Guide: Monitor Arms for Corner Desks

### Measure Your Corner Zone

Before buying, measure the distance from your planned mount point to where you want the monitor to sit. On a corner desk, you're often working diagonally — the monitor may need to be positioned 18–25 inches from the nearest straight edge.

### Clamp vs Grommet on Corner Desks

**Clamp:** Attaches to the desk edge. On corner desks, you need to find an edge section that's accessible and has the right thickness (most clamps: 0.75–3.5 inches). The corner joint itself is often not clamped — mount near the joint, not on it.

**Grommet:** Passes through a hole in the desk surface. More stable on corner desks because it doesn't depend on edge access. Requires a pre-drilled hole or willingness to drill one.

### VESA Compatibility

Verify your monitor's VESA pattern before ordering. Most monitors use 75×75mm or 100×100mm (both covered by all arms in this guide). Some ultrawide monitors use 100×200mm — check the arm's VESA specification carefully.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a monitor arm with an ultrawide monitor?**
Yes, but check weight and VESA specs carefully. Ultrawides often weigh 15–25 lbs and may use non-standard VESA patterns. Look for arms rated 20+ lbs with compatible VESA support.

**How do I manage cables with a corner desk monitor arm?**
Run cables along the arm through the built-in cable clips or channels, then route them down the pole to a cable box or tray under the desk. Magnetic cable clips along the desk edge keep the run clean.

**Will mounting a monitor arm void my desk warranty?**
Clamp mounts don't permanently alter the desk. Grommet mounts require drilling and may void furniture warranties — check with your desk manufacturer if this is a concern.
