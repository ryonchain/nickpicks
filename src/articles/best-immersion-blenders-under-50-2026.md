## The Most Underrated Kitchen Tool Under $50

Counter space is valuable. A good immersion blender can replace a full-size blender for soups, sauces, and even smoothies — and stores in a drawer. The key advantage is blending directly in the pot, which means no transferring hot liquids, no extra dishes, and no cleaning a full blender pitcher.

At under $50, you get a genuinely useful tool. These are the best options in 2026 across every budget within that range.

---

## When to Choose an Immersion Blender Over a Countertop Blender

**Get an immersion blender if you:**
- Make soups frequently and want to blend them directly in the pot
- Live in a small kitchen with limited counter or cabinet space
- Want an easier-to-clean option than a full-size blender
- Primarily make sauces, dressings, and simple smoothies

**Stick with a countertop blender if you:**
- Blend large volumes daily (1L+ per session)
- Crush ice or blend frozen fruit frequently
- Need absolutely silky-smooth results for nut butters or fine purees

For most home cooks, an immersion blender is the more practical tool.
