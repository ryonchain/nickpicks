## Clinical Evidence Summary

**BPH (Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia):**
- Older studies showed improvement in urinary flow and symptom scores
- NIH-funded JAMA 2006 trial found no benefit vs placebo at 320mg
- Most beneficial evidence is for mild BPH; moderate-severe requires urologist evaluation
- Consider saw palmetto as a first step for mild urinary symptoms before pharmaceutical intervention

**Hair Loss (Androgenetic Alopecia):**
- 2012 study: 200mg saw palmetto twice daily increased hair count +11.9% after 24 weeks
- 2020 topical and oral study: showed improved hair density and diameter
- Mechanism: partial inhibition of 5-alpha-reductase reduces DHT at the follicle level
- Effect size is smaller than finasteride but with significantly better tolerability

**Safety:**
- Well-tolerated at standard doses; occasional GI discomfort (take with food)
- Does not significantly affect PSA levels (important for prostate cancer screening — unlike finasteride which halves PSA)
- May have mild anticoagulant effects; discontinue 2 weeks before surgery
