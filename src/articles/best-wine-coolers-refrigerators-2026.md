## How to Choose a Wine Cooler

**Temperature zones:**
- **Single zone:** Best for storing one type of wine (all reds, or all whites ready to drink)
- **Dual zone:** Essential for mixed collections — store reds at 55°F–65°F and whites at 45°F–55°F simultaneously

**Cooling technology:**
- **Thermoelectric:** Quieter, no vibration, energy-efficient — but less effective in warm rooms (above 75–80°F ambient)
- **Compressor:** More consistent across all room temperatures, slight noise/vibration — necessary for larger units and warm environments

**Capacity guide:**
- **6–12 bottles:** Countertop/apartment
- **18–30 bottles:** Casual collector, kitchen bar
- **30–50 bottles:** Serious drinker or entertaining household
- **50–100+ bottles:** Dedicated collector, restaurant, wine room

**Built-in vs. freestanding:** Built-in units have front ventilation and can be installed in cabinetry. Freestanding units need 2–4 inches of clearance around the sides and back — do not install in enclosed cabinets.
