## How to Choose a Portable Charger for Students

**Capacity guide:**
- **5,000mAh:** ~1 phone charge. Good for a single backup charge.
- **10,000mAh:** ~2–3 phone charges. Best for daily campus carry.
- **20,000–30,000mAh:** Multi-device, multi-day capacity. Good for travel or laptop charging.

**What to look for:**
- **USB-C with Power Delivery:** For fast charging modern phones. USB-A alone is outdated.
- **Slim profile:** Look for under 10mm thickness for comfortable backpack pocket carry.
- **Pass-through charging:** Lets you charge the bank and a device simultaneously.

**TSA rules:** Power banks must go in carry-on luggage, not checked bags. Limits vary: most airlines allow up to 100Wh (roughly 26,000mAh). The ROMOSS 30,000mAh (111Wh) may exceed TSA limits — verify with your airline.

## FAQ

**How many times can a 10,000mAh power bank charge an iPhone 15?**
The iPhone 15 has a 3,349mAh battery. A 10,000mAh bank delivers approximately 2.5 full charges in ideal conditions (accounting for conversion losses of about 20%).

**Can a portable charger charge a laptop?**
Yes, if the charger has USB-C Power Delivery output of at least 45W for most laptops. Most 10,000mAh banks output 15–22.5W — enough to slow-charge or maintain a laptop battery but not fast-charge it. The ROMOSS 30,000mAh (22.5W USB-C) can charge a laptop slowly.

**How long does it take to recharge a portable charger?**
A 10,000mAh bank charging at 18W takes approximately 2.5–3 hours to fully recharge via USB-C. Charge overnight before a long campus day to ensure it's full.
