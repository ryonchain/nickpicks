Your bedroom air quality directly affects your sleep quality. Dust mites, pet dander, pollen drifting in from open windows, VOCs off-gassing from mattresses and furniture — you're breathing all of it for seven or eight hours every night. The right air purifier removes these irritants quietly enough that you never notice it running.

The key distinction: bedroom air purifiers are not the same as living room or whole-home units. Noise level on the lowest setting matters more than peak CADR. A purifier rated for 800 sq ft that runs at 45 dB helps no one who's trying to sleep. This list focuses on units optimized for bedroom use: quiet first, effective second.

## What to Look for in a Bedroom Air Purifier

### Prioritize noise level over coverage rating

In a bedroom, the dB rating on Sleep or low setting is the most important spec. An air purifier covering 600 sq ft but running at 42 dB will disrupt sleep. Always check measured noise levels — not just the labeled "quiet mode" claim.

**Bedroom target: 30 dB or lower on the lowest setting.**

### True HEPA is non-negotiable

Only True HEPA (H11–H13) filters capture 99.97% of airborne particles 0.3 microns and larger. "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like" filters are not certified and miss the smallest particles that cause the most respiratory irritation. Every pick on this list uses True or H13 HEPA.

### Match coverage to your room size — with a buffer

Air purifiers work best when they don't run on high continuously. Add a 20–25% buffer to your room size when choosing a model:

- 150 sq ft bedroom → choose a 200 sq ft rated purifier
- 250 sq ft bedroom → choose a 300+ sq ft rated purifier
- 400+ sq ft master bedroom → choose the Levoit Core 400S or Winix 5500-2

### Disable ionizers in bedrooms

Ionizers produce trace ozone — a respiratory irritant at high concentrations. Even low levels can be problematic in an enclosed bedroom over eight hours. If your purifier has an ionizer (Coway, Winix), keep it disabled for nighttime use.
