## Standing Desk Mat Buyer's Guide

### Flat vs. Contoured: Which is Right for You?
- **Flat mats** (Comfilife, Sky Solutions, WellnessMats): Simple surface, easy to clean, suits most users. Better for shorter standing sessions (1–3 hours).
- **Contoured mats** (Topo): Encourage micro-movements that activate muscles and improve circulation. Better for longer standing sessions (3+ hours) but require adaptation.

### Thickness: How Much Cushioning Do You Need?
- **1/2" (12mm):** Minimal cushioning — fine for light use on carpet
- **3/4" (19mm):** Standard — suitable for hard floors and 2–4 hour sessions
- **1" (25mm):** Maximum cushioning — ideal for 6+ hour sessions or users with plantar fasciitis, joint pain, or varicose veins

### Mat Size and Footprint
Most standing desk mats are 20" x 39" — enough for a natural standing position with room to shift front-to-back. Wider desks or users with longer stride preferences may want a 24" wide variant. The Topo's 26" x 29" footprint is compact — good for smaller desks or tight spaces.

### Flooring Compatibility
- **Hardwood/tile:** Any mat with a non-slip rubber or grip bottom works
- **Carpet:** Mats often require a hard-backed rug pad to prevent sinking
- **Polished/waxed floors:** Choose mats with micro-texture grip backing (WellnessMats and Topo both excel here)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do anti-fatigue mats actually work?
Yes — multiple peer-reviewed studies confirm that cushioned mats reduce subjective fatigue and discomfort during prolonged standing compared to hard floors. The effect is most pronounced after 90+ minute continuous standing sessions. Contoured mats show additional benefits by encouraging micro-movements that improve blood flow.

### How thick should an anti-fatigue mat be for a standing desk?
3/4" (19mm) is the sweet spot for most users on hard floors. Less than 1/2" provides minimal benefit. More than 1" can cause instability that strains ankles over long sessions. Users with specific foot conditions (plantar fasciitis, heel spurs) may benefit from 1" mats or mats with specialized cushioning patterns.

### Can I use an anti-fatigue mat on carpet?
Yes, but results are mixed. Thick carpet already provides some cushioning, reducing the marginal benefit of a mat. On thin or low-pile carpet, a mat still helps. On plush carpet, a mat can feel unstable. If using on carpet, choose a mat with a hard backing rather than a soft non-slip bottom.

### How long do standing desk mats last?
Budget foam mats (under $50) typically lose 20–30% of their cushioning after 12–18 months of daily use. Mid-range mats (Topo, Flexispot) hold their form for 3–5 years. Premium mats (WellnessMats) last 7–10+ years. If your mat looks visually uncompressed but you notice increased foot fatigue, it's likely time to replace it.

### Is the Topo mat worth the extra cost over flat mats?
For users who stand 2+ hours daily, yes. The micro-movement encouragement of the contoured surface meaningfully reduces fatigue accumulation over long sessions. For casual standers (under 2 hours/day), the price premium isn't justified — a quality flat mat does the job.
