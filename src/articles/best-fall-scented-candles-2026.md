## Fall Candle Buying Guide

### Wax Type Matters
- **Paraffin:** Most common, cheapest, burns well, not the cleanest option
- **Soy:** Burns cleaner and longer than paraffin, holds fragrance well
- **Coconut wax:** Premium choice — cleanest burn, best fragrance throw, longest lasting
- **Beeswax:** Natural, clean, subtle honey undertone, expensive

### Fragrance Load
A candle's fragrance load (the % of scent oil in the wax) determines throw. Quality candles use 8–12% fragrance load. Mass-market budget candles often use 3–5%, which is why some candles barely smell once lit.

### Burn Tips for Best Performance
1. **First burn:** Let the wax melt to the full edge of the container before extinguishing. This prevents tunneling (wax walls forming around a center hole).
2. **Trim the wick** to ¼ inch before each use. Long wicks create soot.
3. **Burn for 2–4 hours** at a time, not all day.
4. **Keep away from drafts** which cause uneven burning.

### When to Buy
Labor Day sales and Amazon Prime Day often include candle discounts (30–50% off). Yankee Candle runs its own seasonal sales heavily in September and October. Stock up for the season during early fall sales.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the best fall candle for a large room?**
The Yankee Candle Pumpkin Pie large jar has the throw to fill a living room. For premium quality in large spaces, Voluspa's large 2-wick versions have excellent throw.

**How do I pick a fall scent if I don't like pumpkin?**
Go woody and warm: Mahogany Teakwood (Bath & Body Works) or Baltic Amber (Voluspa) give fall vibes without any pumpkin sweetness.

**Are soy candles really better?**
Soy candles burn cleaner (less soot), last longer, and hold fragrance well. Whether that translates to significantly better throw depends more on fragrance load than wax type. For maximum performance, coconut wax wins.

**Can fall candles work year-round?**
Woody and amber scents work year-round — they don't read as strictly seasonal. Pumpkin and apple scents are season-specific and feel out of place in spring/summer.
