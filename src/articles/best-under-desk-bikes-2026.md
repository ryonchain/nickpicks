## Under-Desk Bike vs. Mini Pedal Exerciser

**Mini pedal exercisers** ($20–$50) are lightweight, fold flat, and can be used on floor or table. They slide during use unless on a mat, have minimal resistance, and provide less stable pedaling. Best for: light rehabilitation, occasional use, travel.

**True under-desk bikes** ($80–$160+) have magnetic or belt-drive resistance, built-in stability, and proper pedaling platforms. They don't slide during use and offer real workout intensity. Best for: daily office cycling, calorie burn goals, serious commitment.

## FAQ: Under-Desk Bike Questions

**How many calories do you burn with an under-desk bike?**
Light pedaling (~50–60 RPM) burns approximately 150–200 calories per hour. Moderate effort (~70–80 RPM with resistance) can reach 200–300 calories per hour. Results vary by weight and effort level.

**Can you focus while pedaling?**
Yes, for most knowledge work. Light, rhythmic pedaling actually improves focus for some people. Intense cycling impairs cognitive performance — keep resistance low during focused work.

**Will an under-desk bike damage my floor?**
Most under-desk bikes are safe on hardwood and tile. Use a mat if you're concerned. Avoid very high resistance on rough surfaces that could scuff floors.
