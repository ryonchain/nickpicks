## What to Avoid

- **Magnetic closures as the only closure on a clutch** — magnetic closures are convenient but unreliable if the bag gets bumped or jostled at a crowded event. Frame closures, snap closures, and turn-locks are more secure for actual evening use.
- **Chain straps with jump-ring connections** — the weakest point of any chain strap is where the links connect to the bag. Jump rings are small, single-bend wire rings that pull open under tension. Welded connections or split-ring hardware at attachment points are the reliable alternative.
- **Very shiny metallic fabrics** — at formal events, fabric that reads as costume-y rather than elegant undermines the look. The difference between "silver satin" and "silver mylar" is immediately visible in person.
- **Interior fabric that stains from cosmetics** — lipstick and foundation will inevitably transfer inside a clutch. Light-colored interior lining is a poor choice; dark or patterned interior linings hide cosmetic transfer much better.
