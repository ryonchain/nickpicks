## What to Look for in a Large Dog Bed

### Size
Most large dogs need at minimum a 44"x34" bed. Measure your dog nose-to-tail when stretched out, then add 12 inches. Giant breeds (over 100 lbs) should look at XXL options (55"x37" or larger).

### Foam Quality
- **Solid memory foam**: Best for joint support; doesn't flatten
- **Egg-crate foam**: Budget-friendly but less durable
- **Shredded foam**: Conforms well but can bunch up over time
- **Bolster fill**: Polyester fill for the sides; quality varies widely

### Washability
Look for removable, machine washable covers. Waterproof inner liners are worth the extra cost — large dogs with joint issues are more prone to accidents.

### Durability
Large dogs put enormous wear on beds. Look for reinforced seams, durable fabric covers, and orthopedic foam that won't compress to nothing after 6 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How thick should a dog bed be for a large dog?**
At minimum 4 inches of quality foam. Senior dogs or breeds prone to hip dysplasia benefit from 7-inch orthopedic beds like the Big Barker.

**Are orthopedic dog beds worth it?**
For large breeds over 60 lbs, yes — especially dogs over age 5. Joint problems are extremely common in big breeds, and a quality orthopedic bed can reduce pain and improve mobility. The Big Barker cites veterinarian studies showing measurable improvement in joint health.

**What's the best dog bed for an aggressive chewer?**
The K9 Ballistics TUFF is the clear winner for dogs who destroy beds. The ballistic nylon cover comes with a chew-proof guarantee and replacement covers are available.

**How often should I replace a large dog bed?**
Quality memory foam beds (Big Barker, PetFusion) last 5–10 years. Budget foam beds may need replacement every 1–2 years. Watch for flattening, lumping, or visible foam breakdown.
