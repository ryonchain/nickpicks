## Qi vs. Qi2 vs. MagSafe: What's the Difference?

### Qi (Original)
The universal wireless charging standard. Works with all wireless-capable phones. Maximum speed: 5–15W depending on the charger and phone. Does not include alignment magnets.

### Qi2
The updated standard introduced in 2023, co-developed by Apple and the Wireless Power Consortium. Includes built-in alignment magnets (like MagSafe) and delivers 15W to compatible devices. More affordable than Apple's proprietary MagSafe hardware while delivering identical speeds.

### MagSafe (Apple)
Apple's proprietary implementation. The original 15W standard that Qi2 is now based on. MagSafe and Qi2 are functionally equivalent for iPhone 12 and later — a Qi2-certified charger delivers the same 15W as Apple's official MagSafe charger.

**Summary:** For iPhone 12 and later, buy Qi2-certified chargers — identical speeds to Apple MagSafe at lower prices.

## What to Look for in a Wireless Charger

### Charging Speed
Match the charger's output to your phone's rated wireless charging speed:
- iPhone 12–16: 15W (MagSafe/Qi2 required)
- Samsung Galaxy S24: 15W
- Standard Qi devices: 5–10W

### Form Factor
- **Flat pad:** Simple, works lying down
- **Stand:** Holds phone upright — best for StandBy mode, video, notifications
- **3-in-1:** Charges phone + watch + earbuds simultaneously

### Multi-Device
If you have AirPods and an Apple Watch or Galaxy Watch, a 3-in-1 pad replaces three chargers and cables.

### Certifications
Look for MFi (Apple-certified) or Qi2 certification for guaranteed 15W iPhone speeds. Uncertified chargers may trigger "accessory not certified" warnings on iOS.
