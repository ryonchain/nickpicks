## Why Anti-Fatigue Mats Work

When you stand on a hard floor, your leg muscles lock to maintain balance — blood pools in your lower body and discomfort builds quickly. Anti-fatigue mats work through two mechanisms:

1. **Cushioning** — foam or gel layers absorb impact and reduce pressure on heels and balls of feet
2. **Terrain/texture** — raised ridges and contoured surfaces prompt involuntary micro-movements, keeping muscles active and circulation flowing

Studies from the Cornell Ergonomics group show that anti-fatigue mats reduce discomfort by 50% after 2 hours of standing compared to standing on bare floor.

---

## What to Look For

**Thickness:** 0.75" to 1.5" is the sweet spot. Too thin (under 0.5") and you lose cushioning. Too thick and the mat becomes unstable.

**Surface texture:** Flat mats are fine for casual standing. Terrain-surface mats (raised domes, hills, ridges) actively encourage movement and are superior for all-day standing work.

**Material:** PU foam holds its shape longer than EVA foam. Look for closed-cell foam that resists moisture and doesn't compress permanently.

**Size:** Match to your standing footprint. A 20" x 32" mat suits most single-monitor setups. Wider desks or dual-monitor arrays benefit from 24" x 36" or larger.

**Edge bevels:** Beveled edges prevent tripping hazards and allow rolling chairs to glide on and off without catching.

## Flat vs. Terrain Mats: Which Should You Buy?

**Choose flat if:** You stand 1–2 hours/day and want basic cushioning without fuss. Budget-friendly flat mats like the Sky Mat are ideal.

**Choose terrain if:** You stand 3+ hours/day, you want active movement throughout the day, or you've tried flat mats and found them insufficient. The Topo is worth the extra spend.

## Care and Maintenance

- **Wipe down weekly** with a damp cloth — anti-fatigue mats collect skin cells and dust
- **Rotate the mat** 180° monthly to even out compression wear
- **Replace when compressed** — when the mat no longer springs back after pressing with your thumb, it's lost its effectiveness (typically 2–5 years for foam, 8–10 for PU)
