## The Science: Does This Actually Work?

### Microcurrent (NuFACE, Ziip)
**Evidence level: Strong.** Multiple peer-reviewed studies confirm microcurrent stimulates ATP production in facial muscles, improving muscle tone and contour. FDA-cleared devices meet clinical standards. Results require consistent maintenance (like exercise — stop and muscles relax).

### LED Light Therapy (CurrentBody, Trophy Skin)
**Evidence level: Strong for red/NIR.** Red (630–660nm) and near-infrared (810–850nm) wavelengths have the strongest clinical evidence for collagen stimulation and wound healing. Blue light (415nm) for acne has solid evidence. Other wavelengths (amber, yellow) have less rigorous support.

### Sonic Cleansing (Foreo, PMD)
**Evidence level: Moderate.** Improved cleanser penetration and makeup removal vs. manual cleansing is documented. Direct skin improvements from cleansing alone are less well-studied.

### Radio Frequency (not in our list)
**Evidence level: Moderate.** RF devices heat dermal tissue to stimulate collagen. At-home devices operate at lower energy than clinical devices — results are more modest but real.
