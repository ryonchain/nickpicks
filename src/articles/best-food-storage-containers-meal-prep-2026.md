## Why Good Containers Make Meal Prep Actually Work

Meal prep fails for predictable reasons: food goes soggy because containers aren't airtight, lunches leak into bags, you can't see what's inside, and mismatched lids turn the cabinet into chaos. Good containers eliminate all of these problems.

**What to look for:**
- **Airtight lids:** True airtight sealing (not just snap-close) extends food freshness by 1–2 days and prevents spills.
- **Stackability:** Containers that stack uniformly (same footprint, same height) take up a fraction of the fridge space of mixed-size sets.
- **Microwave and dishwasher safety:** Non-negotiable for anyone who uses containers daily. Plastic that can't be microwaved gets replaced by your lunch break.
- **Transparency:** You should be able to see what's inside without opening.
- **Material:** Glass for fridge/oven, plastic for portability, silicone for freezing.

## Meal Prep Container Organization Tips

**Label everything.** Write the contents and date on masking tape or use a dry-erase marker directly on glass. Unlabeled containers become a guessing game by day 4.

**Prep for uniform container sizes.** If all your containers are the same height, they stack 3-4 high without toppling — critical for fridge efficiency.

**Use the right material for the job.** Glass for fridge and oven. Plastic for packed lunches and portability. Silicone bags for freezing and marinating.

**Portion proteins separately from grains.** Cooked proteins mixed with rice or grains get soggy faster. Store components separately and assemble at mealtime when possible.
