## Men's Fall Sweater Buying Guide

**Fiber choices:**
- **Cashmere:** Softest, warmest per weight, most expensive. Pilling is normal and reduces over time.
- **Merino wool:** Warm, breathable, odor-resistant. Great for active wear and travel.
- **Lambswool:** Warmer and bulkier than merino. More traditional look.
- **Cotton:** Breathable but not as warm. Better for transitional weather.
- **Acrylic/polyester blend:** Most affordable. Machine washable. Less breathable than natural fibers.

**Silhouette choices:**
- **Crewneck:** Classic, versatile. Works under or over everything.
- **Turtleneck:** Warm, sophisticated. Best for colder weather or fashion-forward looks.
- **Quarter-zip:** Casual, athletic-adjacent. Iconic American prep look.
- **Cardigan:** Maximum versatility. Can substitute for a light jacket indoors.
- **V-neck:** Classic layering piece over collared shirts.

**Care tips:**
- Cashmere and fine wool: hand wash or machine delicate in cold water, lay flat to dry
- Never put a wool or cashmere sweater in the dryer — shrinkage and felting are permanent
- Store folded, not hung — hangers stretch knit shoulders over time
- A fabric shaver ($15 on Amazon) removes pilling on otherwise good sweaters
