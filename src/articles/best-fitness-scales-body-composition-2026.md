# Best Smart Scales & Body Composition Scales in 2026

A bathroom scale that only shows weight is a limited tool for tracking fitness progress. Body composition scales use bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) — a small, imperceptible electrical current passed through the body via the scale's electrodes — to estimate body fat percentage, muscle mass, bone density, water weight, and other metrics. Combined with a smartphone app, these scales let you track trends over weeks and months and see whether the changes you're making are actually shifting the metrics that matter for health.

The important caveat: BIA measurements are estimates with a margin of error of ±3–5% for body fat percentage under ideal conditions (standing on the scale consistently, at the same time of day, same hydration level). They're best used for tracking trends rather than absolute values. What matters is whether your body fat is trending down and muscle mass is trending up over a training cycle — the actual percentage numbers should be treated as approximate. This guide covers the best smart scales from the most established brands in the category.

## What to Look For

- **Wi-Fi vs. Bluetooth:** Wi-Fi scales sync automatically every time you step on, regardless of phone proximity. Bluetooth scales require your phone to be nearby with the app open (or background sync enabled) to upload readings.
- **BIA accuracy:** All consumer body composition scales have a ±3–5% margin of error for fat percentage. Use readings to track trends over time, not as absolute diagnostic values.
- **Consistency of use:** To get meaningful trend data from BIA, weigh at the same time of day (morning, after using the bathroom, before eating) under the same hydration conditions each time.
- **User profiles:** Families need automatic user recognition by weight (common in premium models) or manual profile selection (common in budget models). Check whether unlimited profiles are supported if you have more than 4 people using the scale.
- **App longevity:** Consider how long the manufacturer has maintained their app. Withings and Garmin have the strongest track records for long-term app support; newer brands are less certain.
- **Who shouldn't use BIA:** People with pacemakers, implanted electronic devices, or who are pregnant (unless using a pregnancy-specific mode) should avoid BIA-based body composition measurements.
