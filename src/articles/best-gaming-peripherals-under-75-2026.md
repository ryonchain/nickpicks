## Buying Guide: Building a Gaming Peripheral Setup Under $75

### Where to Spend vs. Save

**Spend on the mouse first.** Your mouse is the highest-impact peripheral for most game genres. The G305 wireless or DeathAdder Essential are strong picks that won't need replacing for years.

**Save on the keyboard if you're new to mechanical.** Start with the Redragon K552 at $29 to confirm you prefer mechanical switches before investing in a premium board.

**The headset is the easiest upgrade.** Any of the picks above outperform built-in mic solutions dramatically for online gaming and Discord calls.

### Switch Types at a Glance

| Switch Type | Feel | Sound | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Linear (Red) | Smooth, no bump | Quiet | Fast FPS, racing games |
| Tactile (Brown) | Bump, no click | Medium | Typing + gaming mix |
| Clicky (Blue) | Bump + click | Loud | Typing, casual gaming |

### Mouse Sensor: Optical vs. Laser

At this price tier, all recommended mice use optical sensors. Optical sensors are faster, more consistent on gaming mouse pads, and avoid the acceleration issues that plagued older laser sensors.
