## Buying Guide

### Thickness Guide

| Practice Style | Recommended Thickness |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Hot yoga / Vinyasa | 3–4mm (stability over cushion) |
| Hatha / Slow flow | 5–6mm (balanced) |
| Restorative / Yin | 8–10mm (maximum cushion) |
| Travel / portability | 1–2mm |

### Material Comparison

**PVC (Manduka PRO):** Most durable, excellent grip after break-in, not eco-friendly, easy to clean.

**Natural rubber (Liforme, Manduka eKO):** Excellent grip, eco-friendly, heavier. Avoid if you have latex allergies.

**NBR foam (Gaiam, Amazon Basics):** Lightweight, inexpensive, less durable. Good for beginners.

**TPE foam:** Eco-friendly PVC alternative, lighter than rubber, less durable than PVC.

### Non-Slip Performance

Grip degrades with sweat for most materials. Solutions:
- **Polyurethane top layer (Liforme):** Gets grippier when wet
- **Yoga towel:** Lay over any mat for hot yoga grip
- **Natural rubber (Manduka eKO):** Good wet grip without towel

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What thickness yoga mat is best for home practice?**
5–6mm is the sweet spot for most practitioners — enough cushioning for floor work without compromising balance in standing poses. Go thicker (8–10mm) only if you have knee or hip issues.

**How do I clean my yoga mat?**
Wipe with a diluted tea tree oil solution (a few drops in water) or a yoga mat spray after each session. Deep clean monthly by wiping with soapy water, rinsing, and air-drying flat. Never machine wash or put in the dryer.

**How long does a yoga mat last?**
Budget foam mats: 6–18 months. Mid-range mats: 2–5 years. Manduka PRO: lifetime (with their guarantee). Natural rubber mats: 3–7 years depending on use and care.
