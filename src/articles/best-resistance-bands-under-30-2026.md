## Which Type of Resistance Band Do You Need?

**Loop bands (mini bands):** Flat latex circles, typically sold in sets of 5 with progressive resistance. Best for lower body work, glute activation, lateral movements, and warm-up protocols. Most versatile for home training.

**Tube bands with handles:** Cylindrical rubber tubes with plastic or foam handles. Best for upper body exercises — bicep curls, tricep pushdowns, rows, shoulder presses.

**Fabric/hip bands:** Wider cloth bands (usually with a latex inner lining). Don't roll during squats, don't pinch skin, and hold position on your thighs better than latex. Best for glute-focused lower body work.

**Pull-up/powerlifting bands:** Thick, wide flat bands designed to assist pull-up progressions or add resistance to barbell movements.
