## Cold Water vs. Warm Water — Do You Need It?

Most bidet attachments under $100 use cold water only. Here's the honest breakdown:

- **In warm climates or summer:** Cold water is completely comfortable for most users
- **In cold bathrooms in winter:** Cold water can feel jarring at first — the body adapts within 1–2 weeks for most people
- **Workaround:** Let the line run for 3–5 seconds before use. Water sitting in a room-temperature supply line will be warmer than fresh cold water.

For genuine warm water, you have two paths:
1. **TUSHY Spa** or equivalent — connects to sink hot water line (~$100)
2. **Bidet seat** (TOTO Washlet, Brondell Swash) — onboard heater, full heated seat, ~$300+

## How to Install a Bidet Attachment (10–20 Minutes)

No plumber needed. You'll use one tool at most: an adjustable wrench.

1. Turn off the toilet water supply valve (behind the toilet)
2. Flush to empty the tank
3. Disconnect the water supply line from the fill valve
4. Attach the included T-connector to the fill valve
5. Reconnect the supply line to the bottom of the T-connector
6. Connect the bidet hose to the side port of the T-connector
7. Slide the bidet attachment under your toilet seat and tighten the mounting brackets
8. Turn water back on and check all connections for drips
9. Test the bidet — adjust pressure and angle to preference

Total time: 10–20 minutes for a first install.
