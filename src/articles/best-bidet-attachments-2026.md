## Cold Water vs. Warm Water: Do You Need It?

Most bidet attachments (under $100) use cold water only. In summer, this is fine. In winter bathrooms, cold water wash can be shocking.

**Workaround:** Run the attachment for 3–5 seconds first to clear cold water from the line — the water that hits you will be closer to room temperature.

For true warm water, you need either:
- A bidet seat (like TOTO) with an onboard heater, or
- A warm water bidet attachment with a T-valve connected to your sink's hot water line

## Installation: What You Actually Need

Tools: Usually just an adjustable wrench (or none at all).

Steps:
1. Turn off toilet water supply valve
2. Flush to empty tank
3. Disconnect supply line from fill valve
4. Attach T-connector to fill valve
5. Reconnect supply line to T-connector
6. Connect bidet hose to T-connector
7. Slide attachment under toilet seat
8. Turn water back on, test

Total time: 10–20 minutes.
