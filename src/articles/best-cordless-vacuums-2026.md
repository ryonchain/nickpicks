## Cordless vs. Corded: When Does Cordless Win?

**Cordless wins when:**
- You vacuum multiple rooms and hate unplugging/re-plugging
- You have hard floors (LVP, tile, hardwood) where cordless handles daily maintenance well
- You need a secondary handheld for cars, stairs, or furniture

**Corded still wins when:**
- You have deep-pile carpet throughout and vacuum for 45+ minutes at a stretch
- You want maximum sustained suction without battery management
- Budget is the primary constraint (corded uprights are cheaper for equivalent power)

## What to Look for in a Cordless Vacuum

**AW (air watts) suction:** The most honest measure of suction power. 100 AW is adequate for hard floors; 200+ AW handles carpet. The Dyson V15 at 240 AW leads the category.

**Battery runtime:** Listed runtimes are usually MAX mode, which drains faster. Standard mode runtimes are what you'll actually experience — look for 40–60 min on standard.

**Filtration:** HEPA filtration matters for allergy sufferers. Check if the system is fully sealed — a leaky HEPA filter is little better than no filter.

**Dust bin size:** Small bins require more frequent emptying. 0.5–0.76 liters is standard; check if this feels adequate for your household size.
