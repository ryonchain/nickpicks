## How to Actually Pick a Robot Vacuum

The marketing arms race around suction numbers (some now claim 10,000+ Pa) has disconnected specs from real-world performance. What matters:

**Navigation type** separates the categories. **LiDAR** (laser distance sensor) creates a precise map, cleans in organized rows, and remembers room layouts between sessions. **Camera SLAM** (simultaneous localization and mapping) uses a camera rather than laser — works well in good lighting, struggles in dark rooms. **Gyroscope/bump navigation** is random-walk with obstacle avoidance — works eventually but is inefficient and misses areas repeatedly.

**Suction at threshold**: on hard floors, 1,500 Pa is sufficient for daily maintenance. On medium-pile carpet, 2,500 Pa is needed for embedded dust. Heavy pile carpet requires 4,000+ Pa. Any spec above your carpet threshold doesn't add cleaning benefit — it adds noise and battery drain.

**App and smart home integration**: the best apps allow no-go zones (keep the vacuum away from pet bowls, cables, charging pads), room-specific scheduling, and cleaning history. Google Home and Alexa integration enables voice control and routines (e.g., "run vacuum after I leave home" via location trigger).

## Buying Guide: Robot Vacuum Essentials

### Navigation Quality Is the Biggest Decision
LiDAR navigation pays for itself in cleaning completeness. Random-walk vacuums miss 20–30% of the floor on any given run. LiDAR models clean in organized rows and learn your home over time. If your budget allows one upgrade, spend it on LiDAR navigation.

### How Much Suction Do You Actually Need?
- **Hard floors (hardwood, tile, vinyl)**: 1,500 Pa is sufficient daily maintenance
- **Low-pile rugs and area rugs**: 2,000–2,500 Pa
- **Medium-pile carpet**: 3,000–4,000 Pa
- **Thick/plush carpet**: 4,000+ Pa; some robot vacuums aren't suitable regardless of Pa

### Auto-Empty: Worth It?
If you have pets or high-debris floors, auto-empty saves 1–2 minutes every run and prevents forgetting to empty the bin (which causes suction loss). At mid-range prices ($300–$400), self-empty bundles like the Dreame D10 Plus offer the best value. At budget prices, skip it and empty manually.

### Robot Vacuum Limitations
Robot vacuums don't replace upright vacuums for quarterly deep cleans — corners, stair edges, and thick carpet accumulate debris that robots can't reach. They dramatically reduce the frequency of manual vacuuming but are a complement, not a replacement.
