## How to Use a Posture Corrector Effectively

**Start short.** Begin with 20–30 minute sessions, once or twice a day. This is long enough to build awareness without fatiguing the muscles you're trying to strengthen.

**Don't use it as a crutch.** If you wear a passive brace for 8 hours a day, your postural muscles learn to disengage. The goal is to develop muscle memory, not permanent mechanical support.

**Pair with exercise.** Face pulls, band pull-aparts, and chest stretches address the muscular root causes of poor posture. Use your posture corrector to reinforce alignment during these exercises for double the benefit.

**Consistency beats duration.** 20 minutes daily for 4 weeks creates lasting change. 4 hours once a week does not.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between a posture corrector and a back brace?**
A posture corrector focuses on shoulder and upper spine alignment — pulling shoulders back and reinforcing thoracic extension. A back brace provides lumbar support for lower back pain and injury recovery. They address different areas and are not interchangeable.

**Should I see a physical therapist before using a posture corrector?**
If you have chronic pain, a history of spinal injury, or scoliosis, consult a PT or physician before using any posture device. For general desk-posture improvement, a posture corrector is safe to try without a prescription.
