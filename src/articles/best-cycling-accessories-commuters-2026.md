## Safety First: Lights and Visibility

Visibility is the non-negotiable. Most bike commuting accidents happen at dawn, dusk, and night — when ambient light is low and drivers underestimate closing speeds. A quality front and rear light combination is the highest-ROI safety investment a commuter can make.

**Front light minimum:** 400 lumens for urban roads, 800+ for roads with minimal street lighting.
**Rear light minimum:** 50 lumens flashing, positioned at eye level for following drivers.

## Locking Technique (Most People Do This Wrong)

A lock is only as good as how you use it. The right technique:

1. Lock through the **rear triangle** (chain stay or seat stay) AND the rear wheel — never just the wheel
2. If possible, lock through the **front wheel** too with a secondary cable or second U-lock
3. Use **a fixed object** (bike rack, parking meter) — not other bikes or objects a thief can easily remove
4. Keep the lock **tight** with minimal slack — slack gives leverage for bolt cutter attacks
