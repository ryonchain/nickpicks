## Why White Noise Works

White noise masks disruptive sounds (traffic, snoring, neighbors) by raising the ambient sound floor. When sudden sounds occur, the brain detects contrast — white noise reduces that contrast, letting you stay asleep or focused. It's not a trick; it's physics.

## Buying Guide

### White Noise vs. Brown Noise vs. Pink Noise

| Type | Sound Character | Best For |
|------|----------------|----------|
| White noise | High-pitched, hissy | Masking high-frequency sounds |
| Pink noise | Balanced, natural | General sleep — most popular |
| Brown noise | Low, deep rumble | Heavy sleepers, ADHD focus |

Most people prefer pink or brown noise to white noise. Try all three — individual preference varies significantly.

### Looping: Why It Matters

Budget machines play a 30–60 second audio clip on loop. At the loop point, there's a slight glitch or rhythm that wakes light sleepers repeatedly. LectroFan and Marpac Dohm both produce continuous non-looping sound. Avoid machines that don't specify "non-looping."

### Volume: How Loud Should It Be?

Sleep specialists recommend 50–65 dB — roughly the volume of a running shower. Loud enough to mask disruptions, quiet enough to be comfortable. Most quality machines reach 70+ dB at max, allowing you to find your ideal level.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is white noise safe to sleep with every night?**
Yes, for adults. Use at moderate volume (under 65 dB). Long-term use at high volume (over 85 dB) can affect hearing.

**Is white noise safe for babies?**
Yes at appropriate volume. American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping sound machines below 50 dB and at least 7 feet from the baby's crib.

**Does white noise help with tinnitus?**
Many tinnitus sufferers find white or pink noise helpful for masking ringing. Brown noise is often more effective — the low frequencies better cover the high-pitched tinnitus tone.
