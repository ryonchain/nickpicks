## Hard Board vs. Inflatable: The Decision Framework

### Choose Inflatable If:
- You live in an apartment or have limited storage (packs to carry-on size)
- You car-top without a roof rack (fits in a duffel bag in your trunk)
- You want something forgiving if you fall on the board
- You travel with your board (airlines allow inflatables as checked luggage)
- You're a beginner building skills

### Choose Hard Board If:
- You have dedicated storage or transport solution (roof rack, truck)
- You're surfing or paddling in consistent wind/chop conditions
- You want maximum glide efficiency for fitness paddling
- You've outgrown your inflatable and want to improve pace

### What Inflatable Boards Have Improved (2026)
Dual-layer fusion construction (vs. glued double-layer) is now standard on quality inflatables, reducing delamination and improving rigidity. At 15 PSI, high-quality inflatables flex <5mm under 200 lbs—approaching hard board rigidity for recreational paddling. Cheap inflatables (single-layer, under $200) flex dramatically and feel unstable.

## SUP Beginner Tips

**Start wide:** 32"–34" is appropriate for beginners. Don't be tempted by a narrow board because it's faster—you'll spend more time falling than paddling.

**Knees first:** Paddle from your knees until you feel comfortable, then stand. Most beginners skip this step and make their first session miserable.

**Paddle height:** Blade length above your head in a vertical reach equals correct paddle length. Too short strains your back; too long is inefficient.

**Leash always:** Wear the leash whenever paddling on open water. A board without a paddler becomes dangerous in wind and can travel hundreds of meters in minutes.
