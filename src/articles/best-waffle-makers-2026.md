### Fixed vs. Removable Plates
- **Removable plates:** Dramatically easier to clean. Go dishwasher. Worth the extra cost.
- **Fixed plates:** Wipe with a damp cloth when cool. Budget-friendly.

### Wattage and Heat Recovery
Higher wattage (1,800W) heats faster and recovers between batches more quickly. For high-volume waffle making (brunch for 6+), 1,800W vs. 1,000W means a 30-second vs. 90-second wait between batches.

## FAQ

**What makes a waffle crispy?**
Two things: steam escape and temperature. The waffle needs to be hot enough (350°F+) to crisp the exterior while the interior steams fully. A lid that traps steam results in soggy waffles; good irons vent steam effectively.

**Can I use pancake batter for waffles?**
Yes, but the result is denser. Waffle batter typically has more fat and less liquid, producing a crispier exterior. Many waffle recipes separate and beat egg whites for extra lift.

**How do I keep waffles warm for a crowd?**
Place finished waffles in a single layer on a wire rack in a 200°F oven. Never stack them — they'll steam each other and lose crispness.

**Can waffles be made ahead and frozen?**
Yes. Cool completely, freeze in a single layer, then transfer to a bag. Toast from frozen on the highest toaster setting for best results.
