## Posture Corrector Buying Guide

### Types of Posture Support

**Smart trainers** (Upright GO 2): stick to your back, vibrate when you slouch. Train habits over time; don't provide physical support.

**Clavicle braces** (BackEmbrace, Truweo): loop around shoulders, pull them back. Passive support; require you to consciously not slouch against them.

**Rigid braces**: hard frames that limit movement. Typically medical-grade for post-injury rehab — overkill for postural improvement.

**Lumbar belts** (Mueller): target lower back, provide compression. Different product for a different problem.

### How Long Should You Wear a Posture Brace?

**Start gradually:** 20–30 minutes daily for the first week. Your postural muscles need to adapt.

**Build up:** Increase by 15–30 minutes per week, up to a maximum of 2–3 hours daily for passive braces.

**Don't over-rely:** Wearing a brace all day weakens the muscles it's supporting. The goal is reminding, not replacing.

**Smart trainers** can be used for longer sessions since they don't provide structural support.

### Posture Correctors vs. Physical Therapy

A posture corrector is a habit-building tool, not a medical treatment. If you have:
- Acute or severe back pain
- Pain that radiates to arms or legs (possible nerve involvement)
- Pain that hasn't improved in 4–6 weeks

See a doctor or physical therapist. A corrector helps with postural habits; it doesn't treat structural issues.

### What Actually Causes Poor Posture?

For most desk workers, the culprit is muscle imbalance: tight chest/anterior shoulder muscles pull shoulders forward while weak mid-back muscles can't pull them back. Stretching the chest and strengthening the mid-back (rows, face pulls) addresses the root cause; posture correctors address the symptom.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do posture correctors actually work?**
They work for what they do: remind you to sit up straight. Research suggests consistent use reduces slouching during the wear period. They don't fix muscle imbalances or structural issues. For lasting improvement, combine with targeted strengthening exercises.

**How long until I see results?**
With consistent use (30 min/day minimum), most people notice improved posture awareness within 2–4 weeks. Habitual improvement typically takes 8–12 weeks of consistent use.

**Can I sleep in a posture corrector?**
No — sleeping in most posture braces is uncomfortable and can disrupt sleep. They're designed for waking use. The Upright GO 2 sensor can technically stay on but isn't designed for sleep mode.

**Are posture correctors covered by insurance?**
Standard posture correctors are not typically covered. Medical-grade spinal supports prescribed for specific conditions sometimes are. Check your specific plan.

**Can kids use posture correctors?**
Children should see a pediatrician or physical therapist before using posture correctors — their postural issues often have specific causes that need evaluation before bracing.
