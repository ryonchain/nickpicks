## Cable Management Planning Guide

### The Right Order of Operations

Cable management works best when approached systematically:

1. **Plan all cable routes first** — before buying anything, trace where each cable needs to go from device to outlet/hub
2. **Install the under-desk tray** — this hides the power infrastructure first
3. **Mount raceways** — for desk-edge runs or wall-adjacent cable paths
4. **Bundle loose cables** — velcro ties group cables running the same path
5. **Cover the power strip** — if floor-standing, a cable box finishes the look
6. **Use cable clips** — for the final few inches of cable at desk surface level

### Cable Management by Desk Type

**Minimalist laptop desk:**
- Cable clip channel along desk rear edge
- 1 cable box for power strip
- Velcro ties for USB bundle

**Full workstation (desktop + monitors + peripherals):**
- UPLIFT under-desk tray (essential)
- J channel or D-Line raceway for monitor cables
- Spiral wrap for desktop tower to outlet run
- Cable box if power strip is floor-standing

**Standing/sit-stand desk:**
- Under-desk tray that moves with the desk surface
- Cable spine or spiral wrap for the floor-to-desk cable run (must extend and compress with desk height changes)
- Extra slack in all cable routes to accommodate full height range

## FAQs

**How do I hide cables on a standing desk?**
Standing desks require cables that can flex with height adjustment. Use a cable management spine (a flexible spiral sleeve) between the desk underside and the floor — it compresses when the desk is lowered and extends when raised. Pair with an under-desk tray to hide the power strip on the desk itself.

**Can cable raceways be painted?**
Yes — Alex Tech J channel is explicitly paintable. D-Line raceways accept spray paint but have a smoother finish that requires light sanding first for paint adhesion. Use latex spray paint and apply 2 thin coats for best results. Painting to match wall color makes raceways effectively invisible.

**What's the difference between a cable tray and a cable box?**
A cable tray mounts under the desk surface and hides cables above the floor. A cable box sits on the floor (or desk) and encloses a power strip. Both serve complementary roles — the tray for under-desk infrastructure, the box for floor-level power strips. Most complete setups benefit from both.
