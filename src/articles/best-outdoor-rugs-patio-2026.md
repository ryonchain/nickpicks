# Best Outdoor Rugs for Patio and Deck in 2026

An outdoor rug transforms a concrete patio or bare deck into a defined living space. The right outdoor rug needs to handle sun (UV resistance prevents color fade), rain (water must drain through — don't use indoor rugs outside), and humidity (mold/mildew resistance). Most outdoor rugs are polypropylene — it's the only practical material for exterior use.

---

## What to Look For

**Must have:**
- Polypropylene or other synthetic fiber (not wool, cotton, or natural fibers)
- UV-treated or solution-dyed yarns (not surface-dyed, which fades quickly)
- Open weave or drainage holes (water must pass through, not pool)
- Mold/mildew resistant label

**Sizing:** Same rules as indoor rugs — aim for front legs of furniture on the rug, or all legs for smaller settings. Common patio sizes: 5x7, 5x8, 8x10, 9x12.

## How to Keep an Outdoor Rug from Blowing Away

Without furniture weight on it, outdoor rugs blow in wind. Solutions:

1. **Furniture weight:** Place all patio furniture legs on the rug. This is the best solution.
2. **Rug stakes:** Tent-stake style anchors that press through rug grommets into grass or mulch. Not for hard surfaces.
3. **Rug anchors (hard surfaces):** Non-slip pads with adhesive grip work for patios. Velcro strips along edges prevent flip in wind.
4. **Edge weights:** Heavy potted plants at corners.

## Maintenance

- **Weekly:** Shake out or sweep with a broom
- **Monthly or after heavy use:** Hose down on the rug surface, flip over, hose the backing, prop up to dry fully before replacing
- **End of season:** Roll and store indoors or in a covered area. Never store wet.
- **Mildew:** If mildew develops, mix 1 part white vinegar with 4 parts water, spray, let sit 10 minutes, then hose off.
