## Men's Winter Boot Buying Guide

**Temperature ratings — what they actually mean:**
- Most winter boot temperature ratings assume low activity (standing). If you're walking actively, add 20-30 degrees of warmth.
- For -20°F rated boots: comfortable standing to about 10°F; comfortable walking to about -5°F.
- For serious outdoor cold, prioritize a removable liner system (like Sorel's felt liner) — you can dry the liner separately.

**Waterproofing levels:**
- **Rain/slush resistant:** Light waterproofing, duck boot level. Fine for city winters.
- **Seam-sealed waterproof:** Handles submersion and deep slush.
- **Full neoprene:** Complete waterproofing for standing in water.

**Fit considerations:**
- Winter boots should be fitted wearing thick wool socks (at least 1/4" of extra room)
- Too tight = cold feet (reduces blood circulation)
- Removable liners allow fine-tuning fit and warmth

**Traction:**
- Lug soles handle snow and uneven terrain
- Ice-specific soles (Sorel, Baffin) have specialized tread patterns
- For serious ice, consider adding Yaktrax slip-on traction devices
