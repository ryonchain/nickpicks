## The Qi vs Qi2 vs MagSafe Difference

**Qi:** Original standard. 5–7.5W for iPhones, up to 10W for some Androids. Alignment-sensitive — off-center placement means slower charging.

**Qi2:** Updated standard with magnetic alignment ring. Delivers 15W on compatible devices regardless of which brand makes the charger. Works on iPhone 13+ and Qi2-certified Android devices. The current best standard for most buyers.

**MagSafe:** Apple-exclusive magnetic system. Delivers 15W on iPhone 12+ but requires Apple-certified chargers. Third-party MagSafe-compatible chargers deliver 7.5W (not 15W).

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Standard:** Buy Qi2 if your phone supports it. For iPhone 13+, Qi2 gets you 15W without paying Apple's MagSafe premium. For Android, check if your device is Qi2 certified — Samsung Galaxy S24+ and newer support it.

**Multi-Device vs Single Pad:** Multi-device stations cost more but eliminate cable clutter. If you charge iPhone + Apple Watch nightly, a 3-in-1 pays for itself in nightstand organization.

**Speed Claims:** Actual charging speed depends on the adapter wattage. A 15W Qi2 charger paired with a 5W adapter will not charge at 15W. Always check what adapter is included — or budget for a 30W+ USB-C adapter separately.

**Travel:** Foldable chargers are worth the premium if you travel frequently. A dedicated travel wireless charger replaces multiple cables and adapters.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Qi2 better than MagSafe?**
For non-Apple chargers, yes. Qi2 delivers 15W on compatible iPhones without requiring Apple-certified chargers — so third-party Qi2 pads match MagSafe speed. On Apple's own MagSafe chargers, performance is equivalent.

**Can I use an iPhone charger on Android?**
Qi is universal — any Qi/Qi2 pad works with any Qi-enabled Android phone. The speed depends on what your phone supports (typically 5–10W for most Androids, 15W for Qi2-certified devices).

**Does wireless charging work through a phone case?**
Yes, through most cases up to 3mm thick. Very thick cases (folio wallets, multi-card holders) can block charging. Magnetic attachment cases (PopSocket Magsafe, etc.) work fine with Qi2 pads.

**How much slower is wireless vs wired charging?**
At 15W Qi2, wireless is roughly half as fast as a 30W wired charger. For overnight charging, the difference doesn't matter. For a quick top-up in 30 minutes, wired wins.
