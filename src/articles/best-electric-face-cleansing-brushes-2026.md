## Why Electric Cleansing Beats Washing by Hand

Manual cleansing removes approximately 98% of surface debris. Electric cleansing devices remove up to 99.5% — a small percentage difference with a meaningful impact on pore congestion over months of use. The sonic or T-Sonic vibrations physically dislodge makeup residue, pollution particles, and sebum that manual cleansing misses.

More importantly, consistent use of an electric cleansing brush over 4–8 weeks produces measurable improvements in pore appearance, skin texture, and the efficacy of serums applied afterward (clean skin absorbs active ingredients more effectively).

**Silicone vs. nylon bristles:** Silicone heads are non-porous — bacteria cannot grow in them, and they never need replacing. Nylon bristle heads clean with more mechanical friction, which some users prefer, but must be replaced every 3 months to prevent bacterial buildup.

## Buying Guide

### Silicone vs. Bristle: The Real Decision
If you want the most hygienic, lowest-maintenance experience with the best long-term value — go silicone (Foreo, PMD). If you prefer the sensation of physical bristle exfoliation and don't mind replacing heads — go bristle with antimicrobial protection (Michael Todd, Clinique).

### Vibration Speed and Intensity
Most users do best with medium intensity. High speeds on sensitive skin can cause irritation. Start at the lowest setting and increase over 1–2 weeks as skin acclimates.

### Usage Protocol
1 minute of cleansing is sufficient — 30 seconds on each half of the face. Longer sessions don't add cleansing benefit and can over-exfoliate. Use morning and evening, or evening only if skin is sensitive.

## FAQ

### How long should I use an electric cleansing brush per session?
60 seconds total is the standard protocol: 30 seconds on each half of the face. Most devices have a built-in 1-minute timer with a 30-second interval indicator. Using longer than 2 minutes per session risks over-exfoliating and disrupting the skin barrier.

### Can I use an electric cleansing brush with any cleanser?
Yes — gel, foam, cream, and oil cleansers all work. Avoid using the brush with physical scrubs or exfoliants, as combining mechanical brush action with abrasive particles can over-exfoliate. The brush itself provides the exfoliation.

### Are electric cleansing brushes safe for sensitive skin or rosacea?
Silicone devices (Foreo, PMD) are generally safe for sensitive skin — they don't physically abrade. Nylon bristle devices may aggravate sensitivity or rosacea. Always start at the lowest intensity setting and increase only if skin tolerates it well. Consult a dermatologist if you have rosacea before using any cleansing device.
