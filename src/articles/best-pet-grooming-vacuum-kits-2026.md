### Desensitizing Dogs to At-Home Grooming

Dogs that resist grooming often experienced aversive grooming early in life. Desensitization approach:
1. **Tool introduction**: let the dog sniff each tool with high-value treats present
2. **Proximity**: touch tool to dog without running it, reward heavily
3. **Sound**: run clippers/vacuum in the room without touching the dog, reward calm behavior
4. **Contact**: briefly touch running tool to dog (away from sensitive areas), reward
5. **Short sessions**: 2–3 minutes initially, build up gradually

Most dogs adapt within 5–10 sessions. Force and restraint make grooming harder long-term — patience and positive reinforcement pay off.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use human clippers on my dog?

Human clippers are not designed for pet coats — they overheat faster, have the wrong blade gaps for animal fur, and aren't calibrated for the different coat textures dogs have. Pet-specific clippers have cooler motors, appropriate blade gaps, and often quieter operation. Use pet-specific tools.

### How often should I groom my dog at home?

Depends entirely on breed and coat type. Short-coated shedding breeds: brush weekly. Double-coated breeds: brush 2–3x weekly during shedding season. Clip-required breeds: clip every 6–8 weeks. All breeds: bathe when dirty or smelly (over-bathing strips natural oils).

### Is the Neabot vacuum loud enough to scare dogs?

At 64dB, it's quieter than most household vacuums. Most dogs habituate within 2–5 sessions using the desensitization approach. Dogs with severe noise phobias (storm anxiety, firework anxiety) may never fully adapt — try during a calm, low-stimulus time and go slowly.

### Can I groom my cat at home?

Yes, though cats are generally less tolerant of grooming tools than dogs. For cats: a grooming glove (feels like petting) is the lowest-stress option. The FURminator has cat-specific sizes. Most cats do not tolerate clippers — professional grooming or sedated grooming at a vet is recommended for cats needing haircuts.

### What's the single most impactful at-home grooming purchase?

For shedding breeds: the FURminator dramatically reduces loose fur in the home and is the highest-impact single purchase. For clip-required breeds: quality clippers (Wahl Deluxe). For owners who groom completely at home: the Neabot P1 Pro eliminates the floor-fur problem that makes grooming sessions feel chaotic.
