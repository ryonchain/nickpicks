## What to Look For

- **UV400 protection is non-negotiable:** Any sunglasses labeled UV400 block 100% of UV-A and UV-B rays. Avoid any pair that only says "UV protection" without specifying 400nm coverage.
- **Polarized vs. non-polarized:** Polarized lenses eliminate horizontal glare from water, roads, and snow. They're worth the upgrade for driving, beach, and outdoor sports — but can interfere with LCD screens.
- **Lens material matters:** Glass lenses offer the best optical clarity but are heavier and can shatter. Polycarbonate and TAC (Tri-Acetate Cellulose) lenses are lighter, more impact-resistant, and suitable for most everyday use.
- **Frame fit for your face shape:** Oversized frames suit oval and heart-shaped faces; cat-eye frames flatter round and square faces; round frames work well on square and diamond face shapes.
- **Coverage area:** Larger lenses and wrap-around styles block more peripheral UV light and wind, which is especially important for outdoor activities and driving.
- **Lens tint:** Gray tints preserve natural color balance; brown/amber tints enhance contrast and are great for fishing and golf; rose/copper tints boost contrast in low light.
