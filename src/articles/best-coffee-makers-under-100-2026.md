## Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Coffee Maker

**Match the machine to your routine.**
- **Morning ritual drinker:** Programmable drip (Cuisinart or Ninja) — wake up to fresh coffee.
- **Speed above all:** Keurig K-Slim — 60 seconds from button to cup.
- **Coffee quality first:** OXO Brew or French press — best extraction at this price tier.
- **Multiple people, multiple preferences:** Hamilton Beach 2-Way.
- **Cold coffee drinker:** Toddy cold brew system.

**Brew temperature matters more than most buyers realize.**
Most cheap coffee makers brew below 190°F — under-extracting coffee and producing flat, bitter cups. The OXO Brew's SCA certification (195–205°F) and Cuisinart's adjustable temperature are reasons they taste better than similarly-priced competitors.

**Pod machines vs. ground coffee.**
Keurig pods cost $0.50–0.80 per cup. Ground coffee costs $0.10–0.20 per cup. Over a year of daily coffee, the difference is $150–250. Ground coffee wins on cost and quality; pods win on convenience and variety.

**Keep-warm plates damage coffee over time.**
Coffee left on a keep-warm plate starts to taste bitter and over-extracted after 20–30 minutes. If you brew a pot but drink it slowly, look for machines with auto-off or an insulated carafe.
