## Why Vitamin C Serum Belongs in Your Routine

Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) is one of the most evidence-backed topical skincare actives. Decades of peer-reviewed research show it:

- **Brightens hyperpigmentation** by inhibiting melanin synthesis (dark spots, sun damage, uneven tone)
- **Stimulates collagen synthesis** — reducing fine lines and improving skin elasticity over time
- **Neutralizes free radicals** from UV exposure and pollution (antioxidant protection)
- **Enhances sunscreen efficacy** — applied under SPF, vitamin C provides additive photoprotection

The caveat: formulation quality varies enormously. L-ascorbic acid (pure vitamin C) is the most researched form but also the least stable — it oxidizes and loses effectiveness quickly when exposed to air and light. Effective serums use dark-glass packaging, airtight applicators, or stabilized derivative forms to preserve activity.

## How to Use Vitamin C Serum Correctly

1. **Apply on clean, dry skin** — ideally immediately after washing
2. **Wait 30–60 seconds** before layering other products (allows absorption)
3. **Follow with moisturizer**, then SPF (always SPF in the morning)
4. **Start every other day** if you're new to vitamin C — build tolerance before daily use
5. **Store properly** — cool, dark cabinet; don't leave in a hot bathroom

**Do not mix with:** Benzoyl peroxide (deactivates vitamin C), niacinamide at very high doses (can reduce efficacy — fine at moderate levels as in TruSkin).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does it take to see results from vitamin C serum?**
Brightening effects on hyperpigmentation typically appear in 4–6 weeks of consistent daily use. Anti-aging collagen benefits develop over months of sustained use. Antioxidant protection begins immediately.

**Can I use vitamin C serum every day?**
Yes, once skin is acclimated. Start every other day for 1–2 weeks, then daily. Morning use is preferred as the antioxidant properties complement daytime UV protection.

**Is vitamin C serum safe during pregnancy?**
Topical vitamin C is generally considered safe during pregnancy. Consult your OB — most recommend avoiding retinoids and high-dose exfoliants but consider vitamin C acceptable.

**Does vitamin C serum expire?**
L-ascorbic acid serums oxidize over time. Discard when the serum turns noticeably yellow or orange — oxidized vitamin C is less effective and may even be pro-oxidant. Typically 3–6 months after opening.
