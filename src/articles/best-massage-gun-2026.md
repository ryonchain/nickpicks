## What Makes a Massage Gun Worth Buying

Four specs determine whether a massage gun actually delivers therapeutic value:

- **Amplitude (Stroke Depth):** How deep the head travels per stroke — 10–12mm handles neck and shoulders; 14–16mm reaches dense muscle groups like glutes and hamstrings.
- **Stall Force:** How much pressure the motor handles before stopping. Under 30 lbs limits serious recovery work; 40+ lbs lets you lean in on large muscle groups.
- **Noise Level (dB):** Budget models run 70–80 dB (noisy). Premium models run 45–60 dB — quiet enough to use while watching TV.
- **Battery Life:** Budget for 2–3 sessions per day at 10–20 minutes. Models with 3-hour batteries need daily charging; 6–8 hour models last most of the week.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### Amplitude vs. Stall Force
Amplitude determines how deep the percussive stroke travels. Stall force determines whether the motor keeps running when you press hard. For neck and shoulders, amplitude matters most (16mm is better). For glutes, hamstrings, and quads, stall force is what you feel when it's too low.

### Noise Level
If you work in an office or live with a partner who sleeps while you recover, noise level matters more than any other spec. Look for models rated under 60 dB. Don't trust unverified marketing claims — look for independent dB measurements.

### Handle Shape
Ergonomic handles (Therabody's triangle, Ekrin's angled grip) let you reach your own back without help. T-bar handles require a training partner for anything between shoulder blades.

### App Integration
Therabody and Hyperice both offer app-guided recovery programs built by physical therapists. If you're new to percussion therapy, the guided protocols are worth the brand premium.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should I use a massage gun on one area?**
2 minutes per muscle group is standard. Move the device continuously — don't hold in one spot longer than 15–30 seconds. For warm-up purposes, 30–60 seconds per area is enough.

**Can I use a massage gun every day?**
Yes, daily use is safe for most people. Treat it like foam rolling — more beneficial when done consistently than sporadically.

**Is a more expensive massage gun always better?**
No. The Ekrin B37S matches or beats Theragun and Hypervolt on key specs (stall force, battery life) at lower cost. Brand premium buys app ecosystems and design polish, not necessarily better muscle recovery.

**Can I use a massage gun on my neck?**
Yes, on the trapezius and neck muscles — but avoid direct contact with the cervical spine, carotid artery, and any bony prominences. Use the dampener attachment on the lowest speed setting.

**What attachment should I use for each area?**
Ball head: general use on large muscle groups. Fork: spine-adjacent muscles and Achilles. Flat: dense muscles like glutes and quads. Thumb: trigger points. Dampener: bony areas and sensitive spots.
