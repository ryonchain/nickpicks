## Tips for Better Home Haircuts

**Start with more length than you want.** Guards only remove material going down — you can always take more off, never put it back.

**Cut on dry hair.** Wet hair clings together and appears longer, leading to shorter cuts than intended. Cut dry, then style wet.

**Use two guards to blend.** For a natural look, use one guard for the bulk of the cut and the next size up at the transition zone. The blend hides the line.

**Invest in an outliner trimmer.** Clippers cut volume; outliners cut edges. Clean sideburns and a sharp neckline make an average clipper cut look sharp.

**Clean and oil blades after every use.** A single drop of clipper oil on the blade before and after each use extends blade life significantly and prevents pulling.

## Home Clipper vs. Professional Barber

Home clippers are ideal for maintaining a consistent length between professional cuts, and for men whose style is simple (guard 3 all over, for example). Fades, tapered styles, and complex cuts are significantly harder to replicate at home and are better handled by a professional every 4–6 weeks.
