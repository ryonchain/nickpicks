# Best Safety Razors & Wet Shaving Kits (2026)

Wet shaving with a safety razor is one of those upgrades that sounds old-fashioned but delivers noticeably better results. A single double-edge blade costs under $0.50 and lasts a week of daily shaves. Compare that to a multi-blade cartridge at $4–6 per refill. The shave is closer, the ritual is more satisfying, and the long-term cost savings are real.

The category divides into razor-only buys for shavers who already have a kit, and complete starter sets for beginners building their first setup.

## Blades: The Variable You Control

The razor is a fixed cost; blades are where you tune your shave. Popular double-edge blades to try:

- **Astra Superior Platinum** — Sharp, smooth, affordable. Most recommended starter blade.
- **Feather Hi-Stainless** — Sharpest available. For experienced shavers with good technique.
- **Gillette 7 O'Clock SharpEdge** — Smooth, forgiving, great for sensitive skin.
- **Derby Extra** — Mildest of the major brands. Good for learning technique.

Buy a sampler pack of 5–10 blade varieties and spend two weeks finding your preferred combination of razor + blade.

## Building Your Kit

Beyond the razor and blades, a basic wet shave kit includes:

1. **Shaving brush** — Synthetic is fine for beginners; badger hair holds heat better
2. **Shaving soap or cream** — Far superior lather to aerosol foam
3. **Pre-shave oil** (optional) — Softens hair and reduces friction on sensitive skin
4. **Aftershave balm** — Soothe and moisturize post-shave; avoid high-alcohol splashes on sensitive skin
