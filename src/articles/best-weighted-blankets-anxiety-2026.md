## Do Weighted Blankets Actually Work?

Short answer: yes, for many people. Weighted blankets use **deep pressure stimulation** — the same mechanism as a firm hug — which triggers the parasympathetic nervous system and increases serotonin production.

Research findings:
- A 2020 study in the Journal of Sleep Research found weighted blankets reduced insomnia severity in 59% of participants
- Multiple studies show reduced cortisol (stress hormone) levels during weighted blanket use
- Particularly effective for anxiety disorders, autism spectrum, and ADHD

The ideal weight is 10% of your body weight. A 150-lb person would use a 15 lb blanket.

## What to Look For

**Weight:** 10% of body weight is the standard starting point. Most adults find 15–20 lbs ideal. Start lighter if you've never used one.

**Filling:** Glass micro-beads are standard — they're hypoallergenic, quiet, and distribute evenly. Avoid poly-pellets; they're noisy and can shift.

**Breathability:** Cotton and bamboo sleep cooler than polyester. Chunky-knit blankets (Bearaby) have no filling and sleep coolest.

**Pocket construction:** More pockets with smaller beads = better distribution. 100+ pockets minimum; 400 pockets is excellent.

**Washability:** Full-size weighted blankets need a commercial washing machine at a laundromat (home machines often can't handle 15–25 lbs safely).
