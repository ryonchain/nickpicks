## FAQ

**What is Qi wireless charging?**

Qi (pronounced "chee") is the universal wireless charging standard developed by the Wireless Power Consortium. It uses electromagnetic induction to transfer power between a charging pad and a compatible device without any cables. Nearly all modern smartphones — including iPhones (iPhone 8 and later) and Android devices — support Qi charging, which means any pad on this list will work with your phone regardless of brand.

**Do you need a specific cable or adapter to use a wireless charging pad?**

The charging pad itself connects to power via a USB or USB-C cable (usually included in the box). However, the pad draws more power than a standard 5W USB port can supply, so you'll typically need at least a 10W–18W USB wall adapter to unlock faster charging speeds. Many pads in this price range ship without an adapter, so check what's included before you buy — otherwise factor in an extra $8–$15 for a quality adapter.

**Is wireless charging slower than wired charging?**

Generally, yes. Wireless charging is slower than a wired fast-charge connection at equivalent wattages, partly due to energy lost as heat during inductive transfer. A wired 20W charger can top up a phone significantly faster than a 15W Qi pad. That said, for overnight charging or desk use where you set your phone down repeatedly throughout the day, the speed difference is rarely a practical issue — your phone will be full when you need it.
