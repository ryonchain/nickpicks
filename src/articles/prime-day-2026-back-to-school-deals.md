## What to Skip on Prime Day for Back to School

**Printers** — Campus printers are free or nearly free. Ink refills make home printers expensive over time. Skip unless your program specifically requires one at home.

**Desktop computers** — Too hard to move, too inflexible. Laptops with external monitors beat desktops for student life.

**"Gaming chairs"** — Most are uncomfortable for actual work. A used office chair from Facebook Marketplace beats a $200 gaming chair.

**Smart fridge/mini fridge combos** — Check your dorm's wattage rules first. Many universities restrict mini fridges above a certain wattage.

## FAQ

**Should I buy now or wait for back-to-school sales in August?**  
Buy during Prime Day. Retailers match Prime Day pricing in August but rarely beat it. August "back to school" sales are typically 10–15% off; Prime Day hits 25–40% off.

**Do I need Prime membership to get Prime Day deals?**  
Yes. A 30-day free trial covers Prime Day. Cancel before your first renewal if you don't plan to keep it.

**Is it better to buy a refurbished laptop for college?**  
Amazon's certified refurbished MacBooks are worth considering at significant discounts. For Windows laptops, buy new — refurbished Windows laptops have too much variability in condition.

**What's the best Prime Day deal for teachers?**  
Laminator kits (~$30), wireless presentation remotes (~$25), document cameras (~$99), and Amazon Echo Pop for classroom music are the teacher Prime Day staples. Classroom organization bins and label makers also drop.
