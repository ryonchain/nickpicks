## Buying Guide: What Matters in Bathroom Storage

**Measure first:** The space between a standard toilet and the wall behind it ranges from 4–8 inches. Over-toilet units need to straddle the toilet tank and extend to the wall. Before ordering, measure your toilet's width (typically 14–18 inches) and the clearance behind the tank.

**Weight capacity:** Towels are heavy. Six rolled bath towels can weigh 8–12 lbs. Ensure your chosen organizer has sufficient weight rating — most freestanding units support 25–50 lbs total.

**Material matters in bathrooms:** Bathrooms are humid. Avoid untreated wood, unsealed MDF, and cheap metals that rust. Look for moisture-resistant materials: coated metal, powder-coated steel, sealed bamboo, or quality plastics.

**Enclosed vs. open:** Enclosed cabinets with doors look cleaner but limit accessibility. Open shelves are faster to access but require visual discipline. Most bathrooms benefit from a mix: enclosed storage for utilitarian items, open shelves for towels and decor.

## FAQs

**Can over-toilet storage damage my toilet?**
Freestanding units don't touch the toilet — they straddle it. The only contact is with the floor. As long as the unit is level and not overloaded, it won't affect your toilet.

**What's the weight limit for over-toilet shelving?**
Most freestanding over-toilet units support 20–50 lbs total. For heavy items like full towel sets or multiple cleaning product bottles, verify the weight rating before purchasing.

**How do I keep bathroom storage from getting moldy?**
Regular cleaning and maintaining ventilation are key. Choose moisture-resistant materials, leave space between items for air circulation, and wipe shelves monthly.
