## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Standing Desk Converter

**Z-lift vs. monitor arm converters:** Z-lift converters (all on this list) raise a platform above your desk. Monitor arm-based converters mount to the desk edge and raise your screen separately. Z-lift is simpler; arm-based can achieve better ergonomics for monitors but requires separate keyboard management.

**Height range for your height:** For optimal ergonomics, your keyboard should be at elbow height when standing. Calculate: your standing elbow height minus your desk height = minimum converter height needed. Most desks are 29–30 inches. A 6-foot person needs converter height around 14–16 inches at standing position.

**Stability at max height:** This is the most under-tested variable in converter reviews. Any converter will be stable at mid-height. Test maximum-height stability specifically — wobble during typing at 19+ inches is common in cheaper models.

**Keyboard tray positioning:** Ideally, your keyboard and mouse should be on a surface 2–4 inches below your monitor surface. Most converters with separate keyboard trays achieve this. Single-surface converters require external solution (monitor stand or laptop stand).

## FAQ

**Are standing desk converters better than full standing desks?**
It depends on your situation. Converters cost $150–$400 vs. $400–$1,500 for full standing desks, preserve your existing desk, and are easily moved. Full desks have more surface area, better stability at height, and more professional setups. For most WFH users, a quality converter is an excellent solution.

**How long should you stand at a standing desk?**
Current ergonomics guidance recommends alternating between sitting and standing approximately every 30–60 minutes. Extended standing (4+ hours) without movement creates its own fatigue. The goal is movement variety, not replacing sitting with standing.

**Can a standing desk converter support two monitors?**
Yes — all converters on this list support dual 24" monitors. For larger monitors (27"+), verify the weight capacity. Two 27" monitors typically weigh 15–20 lbs combined; the converters here support 30–35 lbs.

**Do standing desk converters wobble?**
All converters wobble to some degree. The Flexispot M2B and Varidesk Pro Plus 36 have the least wobble at maximum height. Budget options wobble more noticeably during typing — factor this into your decision.
