## How to Choose a Men's Fall Jacket

**Temperature guide:**
- 50–65°F: Fleece jacket, quilted vest, or light denim jacket
- 40–55°F: Sherpa trucker, heavy fleece, waxed canvas field jacket
- Below 40°F: Heavy wool coat, lined canvas jacket, or down/puffer

**Layering guide:**
1. **Base layer:** T-shirt or lightweight long-sleeve
2. **Mid layer:** Fleece, sweater, or cardigan for warmth
3. **Outer layer:** Wind/water-resistant shell that fits over the mid layer

Most fall jackets serve as the outer layer in a 3-layer system. Size accordingly — if you plan to layer a thick sweater underneath, size up.

**Material quick guide:**
- **Fleece:** Best warmth-to-weight ratio. Not windproof, not waterproof.
- **Waxed cotton/canvas:** Water-resistant, durable, develops patina. Heavy.
- **Denim:** Classic look, moderate warmth. Not waterproof.
- **Quilted/puffer:** Best warmth per ounce. Compressible for packing.
- **Wool:** Naturally temperature-regulating, heavy, excellent for city wear.
