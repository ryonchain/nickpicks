## Stability — The Most Important Factor

At standing height, desks wobble. How much they wobble determines whether you can actually work comfortably standing. Wobble is measured in lateral and front-to-back sway when you push the desk edge.

**Uplift V2 Commercial:** Lowest wobble in the class. Uplift's commercial frame uses thicker steel and a more rigid leg crossbeam design. At 45"+ standing height with a 60"+ desktop and dual monitors, sway is minimal. This is the gold standard.

**FlexiSpot E7 Pro:** Noticeably better than the standard E7 thanks to the upgraded frame. For most single-monitor or dual-monitor setups with normal use, it's stable enough. You'll notice more sway than Uplift at maximum heights.

**Fully Jarvis:** At full extension with a heavy setup, wobble is the most noticeable of the three. Still acceptable for most users, but if you're sensitive to desk movement or have a triple-monitor + ultrawide setup, you'll feel it.

---

## Height Range

**Uplift V2:** 25.5"–51.1". This wide range accommodates both shorter and taller users without accessory legs. Very tall users (6'5"+) can reach proper standing height. Very short users (5'2" and under) can get proper sitting height.

**FlexiSpot E7 Pro:** 24.4"–49.2". Excellent range, including low enough for users with shorter sitting heights.

**Fully Jarvis:** 27.75"–47.25". Narrower range. Shorter users may find the minimum sitting height too high without riser legs; taller users may not reach full standing height ergonomically.

---

## Motor and Noise

All three use brushless DC motors and operate between 48–50 dB (roughly conversational speech level). The differences in motor quality are minimal at comparable price points. Uplift and FlexiSpot both use dual-motor systems at their standard configurations; Fully Jarvis uses a single motor at lower tiers (dual motor upgrade available for ~$100 more).

For consistent daily use, the motor differences won't be noticeable. All three should last 5,000+ lift cycles.

---

## Warranty

**This is where Uplift separates itself:** a 15-year warranty on the frame vs 5 years for FlexiSpot and Fully. If you're spending $1,000 on a desk, you want to know it'll last. Uplift's warranty is the most comprehensive in the consumer market.

FlexiSpot's 5-year warranty is standard for the category. Fully Jarvis's 2-year desktop warranty is notably shorter — worth noting if you're buying an expensive bamboo or solid wood desktop from them.

---

## Customization Options

**Uplift:** Extensive. Desktop materials (bamboo, solid wood, MDF, specialty), shape options (standard, curved, L-desk, C-leg), color choices for legs, and a huge accessories catalog (monitor arms, keyboard trays, cable management systems).

**FlexiSpot:** Moderate. Desktop materials (MDF, bamboo), multiple frame colors, some accessories (cable management kit, monitor arms sold separately).

**Fully Jarvis:** Limited desktop materials (laminate and bamboo). Frame colors are limited. Good bolt-on accessories from Fully and compatible third-party brands.
