## Robot Vacuum Buyer's Guide: Under $200

### Navigation: What You Get at This Price Point

At under $200, expect one of three navigation types:

- **Random bounce:** The robot moves in straight lines until it hits something, then turns. Covers most of the floor eventually, but misses corners and repeats areas. Found on sub-$150 models.
- **Gyroscope systematic:** Cleans in structured straight lines using gyroscope sensors. More efficient, slightly repeatable. Can't create a saved room map. $150–200 range.
- **Camera-based:** Builds a room layout using visual landmarks. More accurate than gyroscope, less precise than LiDAR. Starting around $200.

True LiDAR mapping — which creates a laser-accurate floor plan with room segmentation — starts around $250. If you need zone cleaning and reliable repeat paths, budget $250+.

### Suction Power (Pa) Guide

- **Hardwood and tile:** 1,500–2,000 Pa is sufficient
- **Low-pile carpet:** 2,000 Pa minimum
- **Medium-pile carpet:** Look for 2,500+ Pa — and consider spending more

### What to Skip at This Price

- **Auto-empty bases:** Only available as add-ons starting around $80, and most sub-$200 robots don't support them
- **Sonic mopping:** Real scrubbing mop functionality starts at $300+
- **LiDAR room mapping:** Sub-$200 LiDAR models don't exist yet (2026)

### Dustbin Size Matters

Most budget robots use 0.45–0.6 L dustbins. For homes over 1,000 sq ft or with pets, expect to empty after every run. This is the biggest quality-of-life compromise at this price point.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a robot vacuum under $200 replace a regular vacuum?

For hardwood and low-pile carpet in small to medium homes: partially yes, as a daily maintenance tool. For deep carpet cleaning or larger homes, you'll still need a traditional vacuum periodically. The key benefit is daily convenience, not replacing quarterly deep cleans.

### Do robot vacuums work on thick carpet?

No sub-$200 robot vacuum handles thick carpet well. The suction Pa ratings aren't high enough, and the brush rolls can get stuck. If you have plush carpet, budget $300+ for models with 4,000+ Pa and LiDAR.

### How long do robot vacuums under $200 last?

Expect 2–4 years with regular maintenance: clean the brushes weekly, replace filters every 2–3 months, and clean the sensors monthly. Eufy and iLife have good parts availability for budget models.

### Is LiDAR worth paying $50 more?

For single-room apartments or simple floor plans: probably not. For multi-room homes where you want zone cleaning and reliable scheduling: yes. LiDAR also finds its dock reliably, while gyroscope models sometimes wander.
