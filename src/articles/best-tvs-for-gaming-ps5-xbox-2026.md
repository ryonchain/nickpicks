## Gaming TV Buying Guide: Key Terms Explained

**HDMI 2.1:** The connection standard required for 4K at 120Hz or higher. Make sure the specific ports on your TV support the full 48Gbps bandwidth — some manufacturers label ports as "HDMI 2.1" while limiting bandwidth to 40Gbps, which cuts off 4K/144Hz.

**VRR (Variable Refresh Rate):** Eliminates screen tearing by matching the TV's refresh rate to the GPU's output. PS5 uses HDMI Forum VRR; Xbox uses both HDMI Forum VRR and FreeSync; PC with Nvidia uses G-Sync Compatible; PC with AMD uses FreeSync. The LG C4 supports all three.

**ALLM (Auto Low Latency Mode):** Automatically switches the TV into game mode when it detects a gaming console signal. Convenient — no need to manually enable game mode every time.

**Input lag vs. response time:** Input lag (measured in milliseconds) is how long it takes the TV to display a signal from your console. Response time is how fast individual pixels change color. Both matter for gaming; input lag more so for competitive play.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does a gaming TV need HDMI 2.1?**
Yes, if you want 4K at 120Hz or higher frame rates. HDMI 2.1 provides the 48Gbps bandwidth needed for 4K/120Hz, VRR, and ALLM. HDMI 2.0 maxes out at 4K/60Hz. The PS5, Xbox Series X, and modern gaming PCs all benefit from HDMI 2.1 connections.

**What is VRR and do I need it for gaming?**
VRR (Variable Refresh Rate) syncs the TV's refresh rate to the GPU's output frame by frame, eliminating screen tearing and reducing stuttering when frame rates fluctuate. PS5, Xbox Series X, and PC all support VRR. It's a meaningful improvement in games with variable frame rates — worth having on any gaming TV.

**Is OLED better than QLED for gaming?**
OLED wins on input lag (typically under 2ms vs 10–15ms for QLED), contrast ratio (infinite vs finite), and viewing angles. QLED wins on peak brightness, which matters in bright gaming rooms. For most gaming setups — dim to moderate room lighting — OLED is the superior choice.

**What TV size is best for console gaming?**
For a dedicated gaming setup at 6–8 feet viewing distance, 55–65 inches is ideal. Closer setups (4–6 feet) work well with 48–55 inches. Larger than 75 inches at typical console viewing distances can cause eye fatigue from excessive head movement.
