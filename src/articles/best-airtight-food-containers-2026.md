## Buying Guide: What to Look for in Airtight Food Containers

- **Material:** Glass is flavor-neutral, stain-resistant, and longer-lasting; BPA-free plastic is lighter and cheaper. Choose glass for leftovers and sauces, plastic for dry pantry goods.
- **Seal quality:** Look for silicone gaskets and multi-tab locking lids — they outperform simple push-fit lids for true airtight storage.
- **Microwave and freezer safety:** If you meal-prep and reheat in containers, prioritize sets rated for both. Check whether lids are also safe or must be removed.
- **Stackability:** Flat-topped lids and uniform base sizes are essential for cabinet efficiency. Measure your shelf depth before buying sets with tall containers.

## FAQ

**Q: Are plastic food containers safe for long-term food storage?**
A: BPA-free plastic containers are safe for most uses, but glass is preferable for acidic foods (tomato sauce, citrus) and high-heat applications. Plastic can absorb odors and stains over time; glass does not.

**Q: How long do airtight containers keep food fresh?**
A: Dry goods like flour, cereal, and pasta last 2–4× longer in airtight containers versus open bags. Refrigerated leftovers in airtight glass containers typically last 3–5 days versus 1–2 days in loosely covered dishes.

**Q: Can airtight containers go in the dishwasher?**
A: Lids with silicone gaskets should be hand-washed or placed on the top rack only — high dishwasher heat can warp silicone over time. Glass containers and most BPA-free plastic containers are dishwasher safe. Always check the manufacturer's guidance.
