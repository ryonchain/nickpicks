## How to Choose the Right Portable Projector

**Brightness (ANSI lumens):** 300 ANSI = dark room only. 500-800 ANSI = dim rooms, evening outdoors. 1,500+ ANSI = moderate ambient light. 3,000+ ANSI = fully lit rooms. Most portable projectors work best in darkness.

**Resolution:** 1080p is fine for screens up to 100 inches at normal viewing distances. 4K adds visible sharpness on 120+ inch screens or at close viewing distances.

**Light source:** Laser (Anker, LG, BenQ) lasts 20,000+ hours and needs no maintenance. LED lasts 10,000-30,000 hours. Traditional lamp-based projectors are rare now — avoid if possible.

**Smart OS:** Android TV (Anker, BenQ) has the broadest app support including Netflix. Tizen (Samsung) and webOS (LG) cover all major apps. Make sure your required streaming service is available.

**Throw ratio:** A standard projector needs 1.5-2x the screen width in distance. Short throw: 0.5-1x. Ultra-short throw (LG CineBeam): 0.1-0.3x. Measure your room before buying.

**Battery vs. power:** Battery portability comes with brightness tradeoffs — battery-powered models are dimmer than plugged-in equivalents.
