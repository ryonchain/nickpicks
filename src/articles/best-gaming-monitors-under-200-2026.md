## What to Expect Under $200

Budget gaming monitors have improved significantly. Here's what's realistic at this price:

**What you get:** 1440p resolution, 144–165Hz refresh rate, IPS panel (good color/viewing angles), FreeSync/G-Sync Compatible, 1ms response time (GTG)

**What you give up vs. $300–400 monitors:**
- OLED or Mini-LED panels (not available at this price)
- 240Hz+ refresh rates (rare under $200, and requires a powerful GPU to feed them)
- Built-in speakers and USB hubs (usually missing or weak)
- Wider color gamut (sRGB vs. DCI-P3)

For most gamers, the step from 144Hz to 240Hz is less noticeable than the step from 60Hz to 144Hz. Under $200 at 165Hz is an excellent position for 2026 gaming.

## 1440p vs. 1080p Under $200: Which Should You Get?

**Get 1440p (1440p monitors at ~$180–200) if:**
- You have a mid-to-high-end GPU (RTX 4070, RX 7700 XT, or better)
- You care about image sharpness and detail
- You use the same monitor for work and gaming
- You don't want to upgrade monitors in 2–3 years

**Get 1080p (at ~$130–150) if:**
- You're on a budget and need to save money elsewhere
- Your GPU is entry-level (RTX 4060 or below, RX 7600 or below)
- You primarily play esports titles (Valorant, CS2, Apex) that prioritize FPS over resolution

## What GPU Do You Need?

| Setup | Recommended GPU |
|-------|----------------|
| 1080p 144Hz budget gaming | RX 7600 / RTX 4060 |
| 1440p 144Hz medium settings | RX 7700 XT / RTX 4070 |
| 1440p 165Hz high settings | RX 7800 XT / RTX 4070 Super |
| 1440p 165Hz max settings | RX 7900 XT / RTX 4080 |

Running a 1440p 165Hz monitor on an entry-level GPU means you won't hit the refresh rate in demanding games. That's fine for some players, but 1080p 144Hz might deliver smoother gameplay on a budget GPU.
