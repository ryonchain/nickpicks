## When Black Friday 2026 Starts (It's Earlier Than You Think)

Retailers have been starting Black Friday earlier each year:

- **Amazon:** Typically runs "Early Access" Black Friday deals for Prime members starting the week of November 16–20. The actual November 27 date becomes less important each year as deals roll in waves.
- **Best Buy:** "Black Friday Starts Now" previews usually begin mid-November with new deals releasing daily.
- **Target + Walmart:** Both have moved to weekly deal releases throughout November, culminating in the biggest discounts on BF day itself.
- **Cyber Monday (Nov 30):** Still strong for tech, especially laptops, monitors, and software.

**Strategy:** Don't wait for November 27. Set price alerts now and buy when your target price is hit — even in October, some retailers drop prices to match BF levels.

## Category-by-Category: What to Expect

### TVs — Best BF Category Every Year

4K TVs consistently see the deepest, most reliable discounts on Black Friday. This is the single best category to wait for if you're TV shopping.

**Expected discounts (based on historical patterns):**
- 55-inch 4K smart TVs: $200–350 range (from $350–500 MSRP) — 30–40% off
- 65-inch 4K TVs: $300–500 range (from $500–800 MSRP) — 30–50% off
- 75-inch TVs: $500–700 range — historically the best $/inch value at BF

**Expected standout deals:**
- **Amazon Fire TV 4-Series:** Amazon's own TVs routinely hit $150 (55-inch) and $280 (65-inch) on BF — 40–50% off normal pricing
- **TCL QM8 QLED:** TCL delivers reliable deep discounts — expect the 55-inch to hit $280–320
- **Hisense U8:** Strong performer that usually drops $150–200 off on BF
- **Samsung 4K QLED:** Samsung tends to offer gift card bundles rather than direct price cuts

**Skip:** OLED TVs rarely see deep BF discounts — manufacturers hold prices. If you want OLED, buy during LG's sales or at Costco.

### Laptops — Good Deals But Act Fast

Laptop Black Friday deals are strong but sell out in hours. Unlike TVs (which restock), specific laptop configurations at deep discounts often sell out same-day.

**Expected deal patterns:**
- **Chromebooks:** $80–120 for 11-inch models (good for school/basic use) — some of the deepest relative discounts
- **Budget Windows laptops ($400–600 range):** Expect $50–150 off on Acer Aspire, Lenovo IdeaPad, HP 15
- **MacBook Air:** Apple rarely discounts directly; Best Buy gift card bundles ($100–150 gift card with purchase) are the typical BF offer
- **Gaming laptops:** $100–200 off on mid-range configurations is common

**Strategy for laptops:** Know your specific model target before BF. When you see it at your price target, buy immediately — don't wait to "see if it goes lower." Laptop deals often sell out within 2–4 hours of posting.

## Black Friday 2026: Strategy Guide

### Step 1 — Build Your List Now (July–October)

List every item you want. Prioritize by: "Would I regret not buying at BF pricing?" If yes, it belongs on your BF list.

Add every item to your Amazon wish list and use **CamelCamelCamel** to see the historical price. This reveals whether the "BF deal" is actually the lowest price or just the lowest since last month.

### Step 2 — Identify True vs. Fake Deals

**Signs a BF deal is real:**
- Price is 20%+ below the CamelCamelCamel 90-day low
- It's a retailer's own brand (Amazon devices, Best Buy house brands)
- Stock quantity is limited and listed

**Signs a BF deal is inflated:**
- The MSRP was artificially raised 2–4 weeks before BF
- It matches the price the item sold at during Prime Day
- It's a brand you've never heard of with inflated "was" pricing

### Step 3 — Set Price Alerts (September–October)

- **CamelCamelCamel.com:** Set an email alert for any specific Amazon ASIN at your target price
- **Amazon wish list:** Add items to track price history in-app
- **Honey browser extension:** Tracks prices across retailers
- **Slickdeals.net:** Community-sourced deal alerts with real user verification

### Step 4 — Know Your Budget Per Category

Set category budgets before BF week. Without one, the deal-browsing environment is optimized to make you spend more than planned. Know what you're buying and what you're ignoring.

## What Not to Buy on Black Friday

Some categories reliably disappoint:
- **LEGO:** LEGO does not discount on BF; any "deals" are minor bundles
- **Apple products (directly from Apple):** Apple rarely discounts; third-party retailers offer gift cards instead
- **Luxury items:** Watches, designer goods, high-end jewelry don't meaningfully discount at major retailers
- **Furniture:** BF furniture "deals" are often equivalent to any weekend sale; buy during the Presidents' Day or Memorial Day sales

The best Black Friday is a focused one. Know your list. Have your prices. Buy what you planned.
