## How Home Brewing Works (Quick Overview)

Beer brewing involves four steps:

1. **Mashing/Steeping** (1–2 hours): Heating malted grain or malt extract in water to extract fermentable sugars
2. **Boiling with hops** (1 hour): Adding hops for bitterness, flavor, and aroma; sanitizing the liquid (wort)
3. **Fermentation** (1–2 weeks): Yeast converts sugar to alcohol and CO2; the beer develops its flavor
4. **Conditioning/Bottling** (1–2 weeks): Beer carbonates in sealed bottles or a keg

Total time from brew day to first drink: 2–4 weeks. Active time on brew day: 3–5 hours. Waiting time: 10–25 days.

## What to Brew First

**First-time brewers:** American Wheat, Blonde Ale, or American Amber — forgiving fermentation, crowd-pleasing flavors, drinks well at 2–4 weeks (no extended conditioning needed)

**IPA lovers:** Wait until your second or third batch — IPAs are sensitive to fermentation temperature and hop freshness, making them harder to nail as a first attempt

**Beer styles to avoid early:** Lagers (require cold fermentation — 45–55°F for weeks), Belgians (sensitive yeast strains), and barrel-aged (requires months of patience)
