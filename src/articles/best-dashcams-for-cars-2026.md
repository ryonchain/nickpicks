### Key Features to Prioritize

**1. Parking mode** — Protects against hit-and-runs while parked. Requires hardwire kit or built-in battery.

**2. GPS logging** — Records your location and speed alongside video. Critical for insurance disputes.

**3. Night vision sensor** — Look for Sony STARVIS or similar CMOS sensors for low-light performance.

**4. Storage capacity** — Most dashcams support 64GB–256GB microSD. Budget for a quality card (SanDisk Endurance series recommended).

**5. Build quality** — Avoid no-name brands. Look for recognizable manufacturers (Vantrue, Garmin, Thinkware, Nextbase).

## FAQs

**Do dashcams record when the car is off?**
Yes, if you have parking mode enabled and the camera is either hardwired or has a built-in battery. Most standard dashcams require hardwiring for 24/7 parking surveillance.

**Can dashcam footage be used in court?**
Yes. Dashcam footage is admissible in court in most US states and has been used successfully in insurance claims and legal disputes.

**Do I need to tell passengers I'm recording?**
Laws vary by state. In two-party consent states (California, Illinois, etc.), you should inform passengers. Exterior recording of roads is generally unrestricted.

**What memory card should I use?**
Use a card rated for dashcam use — specifically endurance-class cards like SanDisk High Endurance or Samsung Pro Endurance. Regular cards wear out faster under constant recording.
