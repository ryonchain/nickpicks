## Buying Guide

### Jump Rope Types

**Speed ropes (thin cable):** Fastest rotation, used for double-unders and cardio intervals. Best for intermediate/advanced users.

**Weighted ropes (CrossRope system):** Extra weight increases calorie burn and challenges shoulders/arms. Best for conditioning.

**Beaded ropes:** Heavy plastic beads on a nylon cord. Slowest rotation — good for learning rhythm. Common in boxing training.

**Smart ropes:** LED-lit cables with counter displays or app tracking. Gimmicky for most users, but jump rope counters can be motivating.

### Sizing Your Jump Rope

**Height → Rope Length:**
- Under 5'0": 7-foot rope
- 5'0"–5'5": 8-foot rope
- 5'5"–6'0": 9-foot rope
- Over 6'0": 10-foot rope

Test: Stand on the middle of the rope. Handles should reach your armpits.

### HIIT Jump Rope Protocol

**Beginner:** 30 seconds on / 30 seconds off × 10 rounds (~10 minutes)
**Intermediate:** 40 seconds on / 20 seconds off × 12 rounds (~12 minutes)
**Advanced:** 45 seconds on / 15 seconds off × 15 rounds (~15 minutes)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is jump rope good for HIIT?**
Exceptionally good. Jump rope naturally lends itself to interval training — the intensity spikes quickly and drops to near-zero during rest. Burpees + jump rope alternated for 20 minutes is one of the most efficient fat-burning protocols available.

**How long does it take to learn jump rope?**
Most beginners can basic skip (single-unders) within 1–2 weeks of short daily practice (5–10 minutes). Double-unders take 4–12 weeks to learn consistently. Start with a slower, thicker rope before moving to a speed rope.

**What surface is best for jump rope?**
Rubber flooring, hardwood, or interlocking foam tiles. Avoid carpet (drags the rope), concrete without shoes (hard on joints), and asphalt (shreds cables). A 3'×4' foam puzzle mat under your feet protects both you and the floor.
