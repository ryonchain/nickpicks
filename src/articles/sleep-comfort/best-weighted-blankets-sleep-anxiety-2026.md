## Weighted Blanket Buying Guide

### How to Choose the Right Weight

The 10% body-weight guideline is a starting point, not a law. Go lighter if you:
- Sleep warm
- Have claustrophobia or sensitivity to being "pinned"
- Are new to weighted blankets

Go heavier if you:
- Have significant anxiety or sensory needs
- Already sleep well under heavy blankets
- Have tried lighter blankets without effect

### Glass Beads vs. Cotton Fill

**Glass beads** are the most common fill. They're small, heavy, and smooth. Modern designs use fine-grid stitching to prevent them from shifting. Slightly warmer than bead-free alternatives.

**Cotton chain/knit fill** (Bearaby, Baloo) distributes weight through the fabric itself. More breathable, fully machine washable, and has a more natural feel. Less precision in weight distribution.

### Who Benefits Most

- **Generalized anxiety**: Blankets most consistently help with anxiety-driven sleep problems.
- **ADHD**: Some research supports improved sleep in adults with ADHD.
- **Sensory processing**: Strong evidence for autism and SPD populations.
- **General insomnia**: Moderate benefit; more effective when anxiety is a contributing factor.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What weight weighted blanket should I get?**
The standard guideline is 10% of your body weight. A 150 lb person should use a 15 lb blanket. Go slightly lighter (8%) if you sleep warm or are sensitive to weight. Heavier than 12% can feel restrictive and disrupt sleep.

**Do weighted blankets actually help with anxiety?**
Research supports their use for reducing anxiety symptoms. The deep pressure stimulation mimics the feeling of being held, which activates the parasympathetic nervous system and increases serotonin. Multiple studies show reduced cortisol levels and improved sleep onset times.

**Can you wash a weighted blanket at home?**
Most 15 lb and under weighted blankets fit in a standard home washer. Blankets 20 lb and above typically require a commercial washer to avoid damaging the drum. Always check the manufacturer's specific care label.

**Are weighted blankets safe for children?**
Weighted blankets are generally safe for children over 2 years old when sized appropriately (no more than 10% body weight). Consult a pediatrician before use with young children or those with respiratory conditions. Never use on infants.

**Do weighted blankets help with insomnia?**
Studies show weighted blankets reduce sleep onset time (how long it takes to fall asleep) and nighttime awakening frequency. They're most effective for anxiety-driven insomnia and sensory processing issues, less so for insomnia caused by pain or apnea.
