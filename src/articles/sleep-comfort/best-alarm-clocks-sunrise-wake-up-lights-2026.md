## Buying Guide

### Understanding Sunrise Simulation

A true sunrise simulation requires:
- **Light output of 200+ lux** — below this, the light effect on sleep stages is minimal
- **Gradual progression** — sudden full brightness is as jarring as an auditory alarm
- **Color temperature shift** — from warm red to neutral white mimics natural progression

### When Sunrise Lights Are Most Effective

- **Winter months**: When natural sunrise is after your wake time
- **Shift workers**: Realigning sleep-wake cycles on rotating schedules
- **Sleep inertia sufferers**: Anyone who needs 30+ minutes to feel alert after waking
- **Children and teenagers**: Gentler alternative to startling auditory alarms

### Auditory Alarm Backup

Always use an auditory backup alarm. Set it to fire 5–10 minutes after your sunrise reaches full brightness. Most people who adapt to sunrise alarms find they wake naturally before the audio triggers within 2–3 weeks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do sunrise alarm clocks actually work?**
Yes, and they're backed by research. Gradual light in the morning suppresses melatonin more gently than abrupt loud alarms, raising cortisol incrementally. Studies show sunrise simulators improve morning alertness, reduce sleep inertia, and improve mood at waking compared to standard auditory alarms — particularly in winter months when natural sunrise is late.

**What is sleep inertia and how do sunrise lights help?**
Sleep inertia is the grogginess, disorientation, and reduced cognitive function that follows abrupt waking — it can last 15–60 minutes. A gradual light alarm mimics a natural sunrise, allowing the body to shift naturally through lighter sleep stages before the final auditory alarm triggers, significantly reducing sleep inertia duration.

**How bright should a sunrise alarm clock be?**
Effective sunrise simulation requires at least 200 lux at eye level. The best units reach 300–500 lux at full brightness. Light therapy for seasonal affective disorder (SAD) requires 10,000 lux from a separate therapy lamp — sunrise alarms are not light therapy devices.

**Should I still use an auditory alarm with a sunrise light?**
Most sunrise lights include a backup auditory alarm in case you sleep through the light. Set it for 5–10 minutes after the light reaches full brightness. Many people find they wake naturally before the audio alarm triggers once they've used a sunrise light for a few weeks.

**Can sunrise alarm clocks help with seasonal affective disorder?**
Sunrise simulators can reduce the impact of winter mornings on mood and energy, but they are not replacements for 10,000-lux light therapy used for SAD. If you have clinically significant SAD, consult a healthcare provider about a proper light therapy box in addition to a sunrise alarm.
