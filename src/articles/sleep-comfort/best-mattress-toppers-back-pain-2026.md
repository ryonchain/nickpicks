## Mattress Topper Buying Guide

### What Thickness Do You Need?

- **1–1.5 inches**: Minimal feel change — suited to mattresses that need a small softness boost.
- **2 inches**: Good pressure relief without dramatically changing mattress feel.
- **3 inches**: Significant feel change — can transform a medium-firm mattress to medium-soft.
- **4 inches**: Dramatic softness — can cause alignment issues for back sleepers if the base mattress is already soft.

### How Topper Material Affects Back Pain

**Memory foam** cradles the spine but can let hips sink too deep on already-soft mattresses. Best on medium-firm to firm bases.

**Latex** is more responsive — it pushes back proportionally to body weight, keeping the pelvis higher and spine more neutral. Better for those who wake with lower-back stiffness from sinkage.

**Fiber/down alternative** adds surface softness without pressure relief. Better for comfort than therapeutic use.

### When a Topper Won't Help

A topper cannot fix a mattress with a failed support core. Signs your mattress is beyond topper help: visible sagging of 1.5+ inches, springs you can feel through the mattress, or back pain that's worse in the center of the mattress than the edges.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a mattress topper help with lower back pain?**
Yes — if your mattress is too firm or has lost support, a 2–3 inch memory foam or latex topper can restore pressure relief at the hips and shoulders, allowing the spine to maintain neutral alignment. However, a topper cannot fix a mattress with a broken-down support core.

**What thickness mattress topper is best for back pain?**
2–3 inch toppers are ideal for back pain. Thinner toppers don't provide enough pressure relief; 4-inch toppers can make the sleep surface too soft, causing the hips to sink and misaligning the spine.

**Is memory foam or latex better for back pain?**
Both work well. Memory foam provides deeper contouring for pressure points. Latex is more responsive and keeps the body from sinking too deep — often preferred by back and stomach sleepers with back pain. Try latex if memory foam makes you feel "stuck."

**How long do mattress toppers last?**
Quality latex toppers last 4–6 years. Memory foam toppers typically last 2–4 years before compression becomes noticeable. Budget foam toppers may need replacing within 12–18 months.

**Will a soft topper help or hurt back pain?**
It depends on your mattress. If your mattress is too firm, a medium or soft topper can relieve pressure at hips and shoulders. If your mattress is already soft, adding more softness will worsen alignment and increase pain.
