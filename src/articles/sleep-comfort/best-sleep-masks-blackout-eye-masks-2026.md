## Sleep Mask Buying Guide

### Blackout Performance

Most cheap masks allow light at the nose bridge — this is the primary failure point. Look for:
- Contoured nose bridge or adjustable nose wire
- Thick side padding or gusseted edges
- Modular eye cups (Manta) for perfect facial fit

### Eye Space vs. Flat

**Eye space (contoured/dome)**: Eyes can open inside the mask. Better for lash extensions, REM sleep, people with dry eyes. May let light in at the edges.

**Flat**: Lighter, more packable, less light leakage overall. Eye contact during sleep can disrupt some people; fine for most.

### Travel vs. Home

For travel: flat silk masks (Alaska Bear) pack to nearly nothing. For home: modular or memory foam designs that don't need to travel light.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do sleep masks actually improve sleep quality?**
Yes. Light suppresses melatonin production, and even dim ambient light (from streetlights, electronics, or early sunrise) reduces REM sleep duration. Sleep masks that achieve true blackout can meaningfully increase melatonin levels and improve REM sleep quality, particularly for shift workers and light-sensitive sleepers.

**What is the best sleep mask for side sleepers?**
Side sleepers need a mask that doesn't shift or press into the eye socket when lying on their side. Contoured masks (like the Nidra) with 3D cups that protect eye space, or flat silk masks with secure straps, work better than rigid designs that dig in when pressed against a pillow.

**Can sleep masks damage eyelashes or eyes?**
Flat masks that press directly on the eyelids can damage eyelash extensions and feel uncomfortable for those with dry eyes. Contoured masks with eye cups that float above the lid eliminate this issue and allow blinking. If you wear lash extensions, choose a 3D contoured design.

**What material is best for a sleep mask?**
Silk and satin feel smooth against skin and eyelids. Memory foam conforms to face shape for a better seal. Cotton breathes well but may not block light as effectively. For those with sensitive skin, look for OEKO-TEX certified materials free of dyes and treatments.

**How do I clean a sleep mask?**
Silk masks should be hand-washed in cool water with a gentle detergent. Cotton and polyester masks are usually machine washable on delicate cycle. Memory foam masks typically have removable covers that are washable. Replace any mask if the elastic loses tension and it no longer stays in place during sleep.
