## Buying Guide

### Incline Height and Angle

- **4–5 inch (20–25 degrees)**: Gentle elevation — reading, minor positional adjustment. Not sufficient for acid reflux.
- **7 inches (30–45 degrees)**: Standard therapeutic range — adequate for acid reflux, snoring, post-surgery.
- **10–12 inches (45–60 degrees)**: Significant elevation — used in specific medical contexts. May be uncomfortable for sleep.

### Full Torso vs. Head Only

Most wedge pillows on the market are too short (12–18 inches). For acid reflux, the entire torso from buttocks to head should be elevated — this requires a 24–32 inch wedge. Short wedges can bend the body at the waist and actually worsen reflux symptoms.

### Foam Density

Higher density foam (4–5 lb/cubic ft) maintains shape and angle under body weight. Lower density foam (<3 lb/cubic ft) compresses significantly, reducing the effective incline within months. For nightly therapeutic use, prioritize foam density.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What angle wedge pillow is best for acid reflux?**
A 30–45 degree incline (corresponding to 7–8 inch height at head end) is the clinically recommended elevation for GERD and acid reflux. This angle prevents stomach acid from reaching the esophagus by keeping the upper body elevated above the stomach level. Lower angles (under 6 inches) provide insufficient protection.

**Can a wedge pillow help with snoring?**
Yes — elevating the upper body by 30–45 degrees opens the airway by preventing the tongue and soft palate from collapsing backward during sleep. This can reduce positional snoring significantly. For sleep apnea, a wedge alone is not a treatment — consult a sleep specialist for proper diagnosis and CPAP evaluation.

**Are wedge pillows good for back pain?**
It depends on the type of back pain. Elevating the upper body (upper wedge) can relieve lumbar pressure for back sleepers. Elevating the legs (lower wedge) reduces tension on the lower back by flattening the lumbar curve. Many people use two separate wedges to achieve both simultaneously.

**How long does it take to adjust to sleeping on a wedge pillow?**
Most people need 1–3 weeks to adjust to wedge sleeping. The elevated position feels unnatural at first. Using a regular pillow on top of the wedge for the first week helps ease the transition. Persistence matters — the benefits for reflux and positioning typically kick in after full adaptation.

**Can I use a wedge pillow on the couch?**
Yes — most wedge pillows are equally useful for back support while seated. An upper-body wedge used while reading or watching TV reduces lumbar strain compared to flat couch cushions. Look for removable, washable covers if you plan dual-use.
