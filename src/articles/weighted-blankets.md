## Weighted Blanket Buying Guide

### What Weight Should I Choose?

The general recommendation is **10% of your body weight**, but this is a guideline, not a rule. If you're between sizes, round up for more calming pressure or round down if you're uncertain. Most adults find 15 lbs sufficient. Couples sharing a blanket should size to the heavier person. Children should use 5–8% of their body weight and never be left unattended under a weighted blanket.

### Weight options guide:
- Under 120 lbs: 10–12 lb blanket
- 120–150 lbs: 12–15 lb blanket
- 150–180 lbs: 15–20 lb blanket
- 180–220 lbs: 20–25 lb blanket
- 220+ lbs: 25–35 lb blanket

### Materials: Cotton vs. Bamboo vs. Polyester

**Cotton** is breathable and durable but holds slightly more heat than bamboo. **Bamboo viscose** is the coolest option and moisture-wicking — best for hot sleepers or warmer climates. **Polyester/micro-fleece** is warm and soft but traps heat significantly — better for cold climates or those who sleep cold. Avoid polyester if you tend to overheat.

### Washability: Cover Type Matters

Weighted blankets are heavy and large — washing them is genuinely inconvenient. A **removable duvet cover** (like the Gravity Blanket) means you wash the light cover weekly and the heavy insert monthly. An **all-in-one washable blanket** (Luna, YnM) is simpler but requires a front-load or large-capacity washer for 15 lb+ options. Top-load agitator washers can damage weighted blankets — always use front-load or no-agitator machines.

### Glass Beads vs. Organic Fill

**Glass beads** are the standard — small, dense, and widely used. They provide smooth weight distribution. Minor bead noise is inherent. **Organic cotton fill** (Bearaby) uses no beads — weight comes from layered fiber. It's quieter and cooler but has a distinct chunky texture. There's no clear winner: choose based on whether you want a traditional blanket feel or breathable chunky-knit aesthetic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are weighted blankets scientifically proven to help with sleep?**
The research is promising but not conclusive. Multiple peer-reviewed studies show reductions in anxiety, cortisol, and insomnia severity with weighted blanket use. The mechanism is deep pressure stimulation — similar to a firm hug. The effect is most pronounced for anxiety-related sleep issues. For pure insomnia without anxiety, the evidence is more mixed.

**Can weighted blankets help with anxiety?**
Yes — this is the strongest application supported by research. Deep pressure stimulation activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing heart rate and cortisol. Several studies specifically on anxiety disorders (including PTSD) show measurable improvements with consistent weighted blanket use.

**Are weighted blankets safe for children?**
They're generally safe for children over 2 years old who can remove the blanket themselves, using 5–8% of their body weight. Never use a weighted blanket on infants or very young children — the risk of suffocation is real. Consult a pediatrician or occupational therapist before using with children who have developmental conditions.

**Can I use a weighted blanket if I sleep hot?**
Yes, but choose carefully. The Baloo Living (100% cotton, micro-glass beads) and YnM bamboo variant are the best options for hot sleepers. The Bearaby chunky knit is more breathable than it looks. Avoid polyester-shell blankets like the Gravity Blanket if you tend to overheat.
