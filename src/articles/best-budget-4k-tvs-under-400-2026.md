## Budget 4K TV Buying Guide

**Don't compromise on smart OS.** The smart TV platform you use daily matters more than marginal picture quality differences at this price tier. Google TV (TCL 5-Series, Hisense A6) and Fire TV (Amazon Omni QLED, Insignia F50) are both capable and well-supported. Avoid off-brand TVs running proprietary platforms with poor app support.

**Dolby Vision is worth paying for.** On Netflix alone, Dolby Vision content is visibly better than HDR10 at the same bitrate. The TCL 5-Series is the only 55" TV in this price range with Dolby Vision — if you watch Netflix or Apple TV+ regularly, it's worth the slight premium.

**Size vs. quality at this price.** You can either get a smaller TV with better picture quality (Amazon Omni QLED 43") or a larger TV with acceptable picture quality (TCL 5-Series 55"). There isn't enough room in a $350 budget for both in 2026.

**Gaming expectations.** All budget TVs are limited to HDMI 2.0 (4K/60Hz). PS5 and Xbox Series X will work but are limited to 60fps in 4K. For serious gaming, step up to the TCL QM8 or LG C4.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a budget 4K TV worth buying in 2026?**
Absolutely. The gap between budget and premium 4K TVs has narrowed significantly. You won't get OLED blacks, high peak brightness, or low input lag at sub-$400 prices, but you will get sharp 4K resolution, HDR support, and smart TV capabilities that were premium features just four years ago. For bedrooms, kitchens, guest rooms, or households replacing an older 1080p TV, a budget 4K TV is excellent value.

**What HDR formats do budget 4K TVs support?**
Most budget 4K TVs in 2026 support HDR10 and HLG, which cover the majority of streaming content. Dolby Vision is available on TCL's 5-Series and some Hisense models — a meaningful advantage for Netflix and Apple TV+ content. HDR10+ is found on Amazon Fire TV models. None support both Dolby Vision and HDR10+.

**Can a budget 4K TV handle gaming?**
For casual gaming — yes, comfortably. Most budget 4K TVs support HDMI 2.0, which enables 4K at 60Hz. That's sufficient for PS5 and Xbox Series X at 60fps, which is how most games on those consoles run in quality mode. For 4K/120Hz gaming, you need HDMI 2.1, which isn't available at these price points. The TCL 5-Series 55" is the best budget pick for gaming due to its lower input lag.

**Which budget 4K TV has the best smart TV experience?**
The Amazon Fire TV Omni QLED and TCL 5-Series (Google TV) are tied for the best smart TV experience under $400. Fire TV has excellent Alexa integration and a broad app library. Google TV has the cleanest interface and best content aggregation. Hisense's VIDAA and Insignia's Fire TV both work well but feel less polished than TCL's Google TV implementation.
