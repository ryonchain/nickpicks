## Cashmere Buying Guide

### What Is Grade A Cashmere?

Cashmere fiber quality is graded by micron (fineness) and length. Grade A cashmere uses fibers 14–15.5 microns in diameter and at least 36mm in length — the finest, softest category. Grade B (15.5–18 microns) and Grade C (18–19+ microns) are coarser and pill more quickly. Always look for "Grade A Mongolian cashmere" in product descriptions.

### 1-Ply vs. 2-Ply

Single-ply cashmere is lighter and cheaper but pills faster and wears thinner over time. 2-ply (two strands twisted together) is more durable, holds its shape better, and maintains its feel after washing. For investment pieces, always choose 2-ply.

### Washing Cashmere

Most high-quality cashmere can be hand-washed in cold water with a delicate detergent (Woolite or similar). Lay flat to dry — never hang, which stretches the knit. A cashmere comb removes pills and keeps the surface looking new. With proper care, a quality cashmere sweater lasts 10–20 years.

### Pilling: What to Expect

All cashmere pills — even the finest grades. Pilling occurs where friction is greatest (underarms, sides). Good Grade A cashmere pills less and the pills are smaller. A fabric shaver or cashmere comb removes them easily. Pilling does not indicate poor quality; it indicates real cashmere (synthetics don't pill the same way).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is $80–$100 cashmere actually real cashmere?**
It can be. Brands like Quince and Amazon Essentials have cut retail margins by going direct-to-consumer, making genuine Grade A Mongolian cashmere accessible at lower price points. Avoid cashmere labeled simply "cashmere blend" without specifying the percentage — true 100% cashmere should be stated clearly.

**How do I know if a cashmere sweater is high quality?**
Look for: 100% cashmere (no blends), Grade A or Mongolian sourcing claim, 2-ply construction, and soft handfeel from the start. Stiff or scratchy cashmere is lower grade. High-quality cashmere softens with wear; low-quality cashmere stays rough.

**How long should a cashmere sweater last?**
With proper care (hand wash cold, lay flat to dry, store folded with cedar blocks to prevent moths), a quality 2-ply cashmere sweater can last 15–20 years. Many vintage cashmere pieces from 30+ years ago are still wearable. The investment is measured in decades, not seasons.
