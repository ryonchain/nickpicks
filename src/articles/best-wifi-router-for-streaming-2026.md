## What Makes a Router Good for Streaming?

**Consistent signal strength:** A streaming stick that loses 2 bars of Wi-Fi is going to buffer. Mesh systems — where nodes are placed throughout the home — deliver consistent signal to every TV and streaming device, regardless of where they are in the house.

**MU-MIMO and OFDMA:** These technologies allow a Wi-Fi 6 router to talk to multiple devices simultaneously (MU-MIMO) and divide each transmission into smaller sub-channels for efficiency (OFDMA). The result: when 4 people are streaming simultaneously, each device gets its own dedicated slice of bandwidth rather than taking turns.

**QoS (Quality of Service):** Smart QoS categorizes traffic and prioritizes video streams over background downloads. Your 4K Netflix stream takes precedence over an OS update happening in the background.

**Strong 5 GHz signal to the streaming device:** Most streaming sticks and smart TVs connect on 5 GHz for its higher bandwidth capacity. A router with strong, consistent 5 GHz coverage throughout the home is the key requirement.

## Wired vs. Wireless Streaming: Does It Matter?

**Ethernet is always best** for a streaming device that's stationary (TV, set-top box, Blu-ray player). A wired connection provides:
- Zero wireless interference
- Consistent 1 Gbps throughput
- No band congestion
- Eliminates buffering from neighbor Wi-Fi interference

If your TV is close to your router or you can run an Ethernet cable, do it. Your router's LAN ports deliver 1 Gbps dedicated to that device.

**When Wi-Fi is necessary:** Streaming sticks (Roku, Fire TV, Chromecast) and portable TVs need Wi-Fi. For these, a mesh system that places a node near the streaming device is the next-best solution.

## FAQ

**Why does my Wi-Fi streaming buffer even though my internet is fast?**
Most buffering happens due to Wi-Fi signal strength issues (distance, walls, interference) rather than internet bandwidth. If a speed test on your phone shows 200 Mbps but your streaming stick buffers at 4K, the streaming stick likely has a weaker signal or is congested. Move a mesh node closer, or connect the streaming device via Ethernet.

**Does 4K streaming require Wi-Fi 6?**
No. 4K streaming needs 25 Mbps — any Wi-Fi 5 router within range handles this easily. Wi-Fi 6 improves concurrent device handling and reduces congestion. If you have 5+ streaming devices running simultaneously, Wi-Fi 6's improvements become noticeable.

**Should I buy a mesh system or a single powerful router for streaming?**
Single-story homes and apartments under 1,200 sq ft: a single powerful router is usually fine. Multi-story homes, large homes over 1,500 sq ft, or homes with thick concrete/brick walls: mesh is the right answer. For streaming, the goal is signal strength at the streaming device's location — distance is the enemy.
