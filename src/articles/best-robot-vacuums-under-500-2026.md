## Robot Vacuum Buying Guide

### Navigation: Random vs. Mapped

**Random/bounce navigation** (Roomba 694 and older models): The robot wanders in a pattern designed to cover the floor, bouncing off walls and furniture. It usually cleans most of the floor but isn't efficient and can miss spots or repeat others.

**LiDAR laser mapping** (Roborock, Dreame): A spinning laser measures the room precisely and creates an accurate map. The robot cleans methodically in rows, covers everything, and you can set room-specific cleaning schedules in the app.

**Camera mapping** (Roomba j7+): Uses a camera to build the map and identify obstacles. Slightly less precise than LiDAR in the dark but better at recognizing objects.

**Recommendation:** For homes with multiple rooms, LiDAR or camera mapping is worth the premium. For studio apartments or simple floor plans, random navigation is fine.

### Suction: How Much Do You Need?

| Floor Type | Recommended Suction |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Hardwood / tile only | 1,500–2,500 Pa |
| Low-pile carpet | 2,000–3,000 Pa |
| Medium carpet | 3,000–4,000 Pa |
| High-pile carpet / pet hair | 4,000+ Pa |

### Auto-Empty Base: Is It Worth It?

For most users: yes. Auto-empty bases hold 30–60 days of debris per bag, meaning you genuinely don't touch the robot for months. The bag system is also more hygienic — you never directly touch the dustbin contents. If you have allergies, an auto-empty base is almost essential.

Downside: the bags cost $15–$25 for a pack of 3, and you need to buy replacements. Factor this into the ongoing cost.

### Robot Vacuums and Pet Hair: What to Know

Pet hair is the enemy of cheap robot vacuums — it tangles in brush rolls, clogs filters, and can burn out motors. Look for:
- **Rubber brush rolls** instead of bristle brushes (hair slides off instead of tangling)
- **4,000+ Pa suction** for high-traffic pet areas
- **Easy-clean brush roll access** — weekly cleaning is necessary with pets
- **High-efficiency filters** (HEPA-level) to capture dander, not just redistribute it

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a robot vacuum replace a regular vacuum?**
For maintenance cleaning on hardwood and low-pile carpet, yes — running daily or every other day keeps floors consistently clean. For deep cleaning, moving furniture, doing stairs, or cleaning upholstery, you'll still need a traditional vacuum.

**Do robot vacuums work on thick rugs?**
Most modern robot vacuums handle rugs up to about 0.8 inches thick. Deep-pile rugs (shag, thick wool) can obstruct the robot's movement and suction. Check the specific model's maximum pile height specification.

**How long does a robot vacuum battery last?**
Most run 90–180 minutes on a charge — enough to clean 1,500–2,500 sq ft. They automatically return to base to recharge and then resume cleaning where they left off (called "recharge and resume"). Homes larger than ~2,000 sq ft may require multiple cleaning sessions.

**Can robot vacuums handle multiple rooms?**
Yes, with mapping. LiDAR-mapped robots navigate room to room through open doorways, build a floor plan of your home, and let you schedule specific rooms for specific days. Non-mapping robots wander randomly through open doors.

**Do I need Wi-Fi for a robot vacuum?**
You need Wi-Fi for app scheduling, remote start, and room-specific cleaning. Most robots also have a physical button for a basic cleaning cycle without Wi-Fi. Some features (like obstacle avoidance maps) require Wi-Fi.
