## What to Look For in a Baby Monitor

**Resolution:** 720p is sufficient for a nursery. 1080p is nice if you want to see finer detail.

**Wi-Fi vs. dedicated signal:** Wi-Fi monitors allow remote viewing but carry privacy risks. Dedicated FHSS signals are more secure for home use.

**Battery life:** Parent units should last at least 6–8 hours — enough for a full night without charging.

**Range:** 500–1,000 feet is standard. Consider whether you need outdoor range or just within-home coverage.

**Night vision:** Infrared (black and white) is standard. Color night vision, like Owlet Cam 2, costs more but provides more detail.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a subscription for a baby monitor?**
Most dedicated (non-Wi-Fi) monitors need no subscription. Cloud-connected monitors like Nanit may charge for advanced features.

**Is Wi-Fi baby monitor safe?**
Look for AES encryption. Avoid monitors with no password protection or outdated firmware.

**At what age can I stop using a baby monitor?**
Most parents phase out around 2–3 years old, but this is entirely personal preference and home layout.
