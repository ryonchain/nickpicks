## What to Look for in a Backyard Swing Set

### Material: Wood vs. Metal vs. Plastic

| Material | Pros | Cons | Lifespan |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| Cedar wood | Natural look, no heat, customizable | Annual maintenance, higher cost | 10–20 years |
| Powder-coated steel | Low maintenance, strong | Gets hot in sun, can rust if scratched | 10–15 years |
| Plastic | No maintenance, toddler-safe | Fades/cracks in UV, limited age range | 5–8 years |

### Age Range & Weight Limits
Always verify that all planned users fall within the weight limit. Most residential sets support 200–250 lbs per swing seat and 600–1,000 lbs structural capacity. Growing families should buy for the age your youngest will be in 3 years, not their current age.

### Safety Surface
No swing set is safe on bare dirt or concrete. Plan for:
- Rubber mulch (10–12" depth): $150–$400 for a standard footprint
- Wood chips: Less expensive; replenish annually
- Rubber tiles: Premium cost but long-lasting

The fall zone extends 6 feet beyond the set in every direction.

### Assembly
Be honest about your DIY skills. Wooden sets with 100+ pieces require a full weekend and ideally two people. Metal sets are typically faster (2–4 hours). Many local handymen and assembly services (TaskRabbit) will do it for $200–$500.

## FAQ

**Q: What's the minimum yard size for a swing set?**
A: For a standard 3-swing set with slide, plan for at least 20x20 feet including the required 6-foot safety clearance zone on all sides.

**Q: Do I need to anchor a swing set to the ground?**
A: Yes. Most sets include ground anchors; all should be anchored. Unanchored sets can tip if a child swings high or multiple children climb one side.

**Q: How long do wooden swing sets last?**
A: Cedar sets properly treated and maintained can last 15–20+ years. Untreated sets in wet climates may start rotting in 5–8 years. Apply a UV wood sealer annually.

**Q: Can I move a swing set if we move houses?**
A: Metal sets disassemble and reassemble relatively easily. Large wooden sets are difficult to move without losing pieces or compromising structural integrity — most buyers leave them.
