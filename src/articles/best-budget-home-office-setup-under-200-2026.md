**What we like:**
- J-channel design holds multiple cables in a single path
- Adhesive or screw mounting options (adhesive for most desks)

**Q: What ergonomic upgrades have the biggest impact?**
Monitor riser (screen at eye level) and lumbar pillow (lower back support). Together they address the root cause of most desk worker neck, shoulder, and back pain.

**Q: What height should my monitor be?**
Top of screen at or slightly below eye level, tilted back 10–20 degrees. A 4–6 inch riser typically achieves this from a standard desk + chair combo.

**Q: Best way to hide cables on a budget?**
J-channel cable raceway ($15–$25) under the desk edge. Pair with velcro cable ties to bundle before routing. A cable management box hides the power strip cluster.

**Q: Is a USB-C hub enough for home office use?**
For single-monitor, standard peripherals: yes. For dual monitors, 4K at 60Hz, or high-bandwidth devices: upgrade to a Thunderbolt dock.

**Q: Does a desk mat actually help?**
Yes — consistent tracking surface for mice, prevents desk surface wear, unifies the desk aesthetic, and provides mild wrist cushioning.
