## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Face Serum

**Match the ingredient to your concern.** The most important decision. Vitamin C for brightening and dark spots. Hyaluronic acid for hydration and plumping. Retinol for wrinkles and anti-aging. Niacinamide for pores and oil control. Peptides for collagen support. AHAs (glycolic, lactic acid) for exfoliation and texture. Don't use a retinol serum if your only concern is dehydration — use hyaluronic acid.

**Skin type compatibility.** Sensitive skin should start with hyaluronic acid or a ceramide-paired vitamin C derivative. Oily and acne-prone skin benefits from niacinamide and zinc. Dry skin responds well to hyaluronic acid and peptide serums. Normal/combination skin can tolerate most ingredients including straight vitamin C and retinol.

**Ingredient stability.** Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid) oxidizes quickly — buy small bottles and replace every 2–3 months. A good vitamin C serum should be pale yellow; if it's orange or brown, it's oxidized. Retinol is photosensitive — always use PM only. Hyaluronic acid and niacinamide are the most stable serums and don't require special handling.

**Layering order.** Thinnest first. Water-based serums before oil-based serums. Serums before moisturizer. If you use multiple serums, apply the most active ingredient first. Don't mix vitamin C and retinol in the same application — use vitamin C in the morning and retinol at night.

**Introduce one at a time.** The single most common skincare mistake is starting multiple new products simultaneously. If you have a reaction, you won't know which product caused it. Introduce one new serum, use it for 4 weeks, then add the next.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What face serum should I use first?**
Start with hyaluronic acid — it's appropriate for all skin types, provides immediate visible hydration, and has zero irritation risk. Once you have a baseline routine established, add vitamin C (morning) or retinol (evening) based on your specific concerns.

**Can I layer multiple face serums?**
Yes. Apply thinnest first — water-based before oil-based. Common effective pairings: hyaluronic acid + vitamin C (morning); or niacinamide + moisturizer. Keep vitamin C and retinol in separate routines (morning and evening respectively).

**When should I apply face serum?**
After cleansing, before moisturizer. The sequence: cleanser → toner (optional) → serum → moisturizer → SPF (morning only). Apply to clean skin so active ingredients can penetrate effectively.

**How long does it take for a face serum to work?**
Hyaluronic acid: visible plumping within hours. Vitamin C: brightening visible in 4–8 weeks. Retinol: significant anti-aging results visible at 12–16 weeks. Niacinamide: pore and oil improvements visible in 4–8 weeks with consistent daily use.

**Do face serums expire?**
Yes — typically 6–12 months after opening (check the open-jar symbol on packaging). Vitamin C serums oxidize fastest; discard if they turn dark orange or brown. Store away from light and heat to maximize shelf life.
