## Buying Guide

### Stabilization: The Most Important Feature

Bad stabilization ruins footage. Good footage from shaky video is unusable. Priority order:
1. GoPro HyperSmooth 6.0 — best
2. DJI RockSteady 3.0 — excellent
3. Insta360 FlowState — excellent for 360°
4. Budget EIS — acceptable for light activity

### Video Resolution: What You Actually Need

- **4K/60fps** is the sweet spot for most content
- **5.3K** allows cropping for reframing in post
- **4K/120fps** for buttery slow-motion (4x speed)
- **1080p/400fps** for extreme slow-motion

### Battery Life Planning

| Activity | Minutes Needed |
|---------|---------------|
| Surf session | 60–90 min |
| Mountain bike trail | 30–60 min |
| Day hike | 120–240 min |
| Vlog/event | 60–180 min |

DJI's 160-minute battery is the best in class. GoPro's 90 minutes often requires a spare battery.

### Accessories Worth Buying

- **Chest mount:** Best POV for cycling, skiing, running
- **Head mount:** Better for POV when hands are in use
- **Floating grip:** Essential for water sports
- **ND filter kit:** Reduces overexposure in bright sun

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is GoPro worth the premium over DJI?**
For most users, no. DJI's stabilization is 90% as good, battery life is better, and it costs $100 less. GoPro wins on ecosystem and raw stabilization for the most extreme activities.

**Can I use an action camera as a dashcam?**
Technically yes, but dedicated dashcams are better — they loop record, have wider dynamic range for license plates, and are designed for always-on use.

**Do I need to buy extra batteries?**
Yes, always. Even with DJI's 160-minute battery, pack a spare for full-day adventures.
