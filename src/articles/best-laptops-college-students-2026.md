## Buyer's Guide

### What Specs Actually Matter in 2026?
- **RAM**: 16GB minimum for comfortable multitasking. 8GB is tight with modern apps.
- **SSD**: 256GB is tight; 512GB is comfortable. External drives are cheap backup.
- **Battery**: 10+ hours real-world is the target. Under 8 hours means you carry your charger everywhere.
- **Display**: 1080p minimum; 1440p+ for creative work. OLED if budget allows.
- **CPU**: Any modern Intel Core Ultra, AMD Ryzen 7000+, or Apple M-series is more than enough.

### Mac vs. Windows for College
**Choose Mac if:** You're already in the Apple ecosystem, you value battery life above all, you want the best build quality, or your major uses Final Cut Pro, Logic Pro.
**Choose Windows if:** Your program requires Windows-specific software, you game, you prefer customization, or budget is tight (MacBooks don't come in budget configurations).

## FAQ

**Should I get a Mac or PC for college?**
Both work for most majors. Mac advantages: better battery, better build, macOS integration. Windows advantages: more game compatibility, Windows-specific software, more price options. If your school doesn't require Windows apps, MacBook Air M3 is our top pick.

**Is 8GB RAM enough for college?**
Barely. 8GB is workable but will start to feel tight with many browser tabs and apps open. 16GB is the current recommended baseline for a laptop you'll use 4 years.

**What's the best laptop for college under $500?**
Acer Aspire 5 (AMD Ryzen configuration) or HP 15 with Ryzen 5. Both handle coursework. Don't expect premium build quality or long battery life.

**Do I need a touchscreen laptop?**
Not for most students. Useful for 2-in-1s (HP Spectre x360, Microsoft Surface) if you take handwritten notes. Otherwise skip it — it adds cost and reduces battery.

**Should I buy a laptop protection plan?**
Yes. Accidental damage coverage (not just manufacturer warranty) is worth it for a $1,000+ laptop. AppleCare+ for Mac, Accidental Damage coverage for PCs.
