## How to Activate Your Own Modem (Step by Step)

**Before you start:** Note your new modem's MAC address and serial number (on the label on the bottom of the device).

**Activation process:**

1. **Do not return your rental modem yet.** Wait until your new modem is activated and working.

2. **Connect your new modem:** Plug coaxial cable from the wall into the modem. Connect the modem to your router via Ethernet. Power on.

3. **Activate online:** Visit your ISP's activation portal:
   - Xfinity: activate.xfinity.com
   - Cox: cox.com/activate
   - Spectrum: spectrum.com/selfinstall

4. **Or call:** If online activation fails, call your ISP's customer service with your modem's MAC address and serial number. Activation takes 5–15 minutes by phone.

5. **Confirm internet works:** Run a speed test at fast.com or speedtest.net.

6. **Return rental equipment:** Take the ISP's modem/router to their nearest store and get a receipt confirming return. Rental fees stop the day you return it.

## ISP Compatibility Check

**Always verify before buying.** Most ISPs publish an approved device list:
- **Xfinity:** xfinity.com — search "compatible modems"
- **Cox:** cox.com/residential/internet/modem-purchase.html
- **Spectrum:** spectrum.com — search "approved modems"
- **Suddenlink/Optimum:** suddenlink.com — search "bring your own modem"

Do NOT buy a modem until you've confirmed ISP approval. Unapproved modems cannot be activated.

**Note on Spectrum:** Spectrum accepts purchased modems but has restrictions on which models. Verify on their site before purchase.

## FAQ

**Will a better modem improve my internet speed?**
If your ISP provides 1 Gbps and you're using an old DOCSIS 3.0 modem, yes — switching to DOCSIS 3.1 can unlock your full plan speed. For plans under 400 Mbps, most DOCSIS 3.0 modems aren't the bottleneck.

**How long do cable modems last?**
4–6 years is typical before DOCSIS standard upgrades or component wear makes replacement worthwhile. The SB8200 and CM1000 will remain fully functional through DOCSIS 3.1's lifecycle.

**Can I use the same modem I bought 3 years ago?**
Check your ISP's current approved device list — some older models get de-listed as ISPs upgrade infrastructure. If it's still on the approved list, it should work fine.

**What's the difference between a modem and a router?**
A modem connects your home to your ISP's network via the coaxial cable. A router creates your Wi-Fi network and manages traffic between devices. Most rental equipment combines both. Buying separately gives you more flexibility and usually better performance.
