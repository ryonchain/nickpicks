## STEM Kit Buyer's Guide

### Matching the Kit to Your Child's Age

| Age | Best STEM Focus | Recommended Kit |
|-----|----------------|-----------------|
| 5–7 | Sensory science, simple experiments | 4M Kidz Labs |
| 8–11 | Electronics, multi-subject | Snap Circuits, National Geographic |
| 12+ | Advanced chemistry, engineering | Thames & Kosmos C3000, KiwiCo |

### One-Time Purchase vs. Subscription

**One-time kits** (Snap Circuits, Thames & Kosmos, National Geographic) offer more controlled spending and greater depth on a single topic. **Subscription boxes** (KiwiCo) keep kids engaged over months but cost more long-term. If your child has a defined interest, buy a focused kit. If they're still exploring, a subscription is a better discovery tool.

### What Makes a STEM Kit Worth the Money

The best STEM kits share three characteristics: clear instructions that kids can follow independently, supplies that last through all the included experiments, and a design that teaches the *why* behind the activity — not just the *how*. Kits that only produce a single result and can't be repeated don't deliver lasting value.
