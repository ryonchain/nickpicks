## How to Foam Roll Effectively

### The Basics

Roll slowly — this isn't a race. Move 1-2 inches per second over the muscle, pause on tight spots for 20-30 seconds, and let the pressure do the work. Rushing through it defeats the purpose.

### When to Foam Roll

Rolling before a workout as part of warm-up can increase range of motion. Rolling after a workout, when muscles are warm, is when most people find the most relief. Avoid rolling directly over bones or joints.

### Foam Rolling for Back Pain

For the lower back, roll the glutes and piriformis instead of directly on the lumbar spine. For upper back tightness, the TriggerPoint GRID or any textured roller works well — cross your arms in front to open the shoulder blades.

### How Often

Daily rolling sessions of 5-10 minutes are ideal. Even 3x per week will show meaningful improvement in flexibility and soreness over time.
