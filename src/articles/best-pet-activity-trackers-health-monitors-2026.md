## Pet Tracker Buying Guide

### GPS vs. Behavior Monitor: What Do You Need?

**Choose GPS tracking if:**
- Your dog or cat escapes or roams frequently
- You have a large property and need to locate your pet
- You want real-time location during off-leash activities
- Peace of mind knowing where your pet is at all times

**Choose behavior monitoring if:**
- You have an indoor-only pet or confined outdoor space
- You want early health indicators and trend data
- You have a senior pet or pet with a chronic condition
- You want data to discuss with your vet

Many pet owners with outdoor dogs benefit from both: GPS for location and behavior monitoring for health trends.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do pet GPS trackers work everywhere?

GPS accuracy is good in open areas. Urban areas with tall buildings can reduce accuracy. Inside buildings, GPS loses signal entirely (Wi-Fi-based location is less precise). Coverage for the cellular network varies — most trackers use a combination of LTE networks similar to your cell phone.

### Can I use a dog GPS tracker on a cat?

Only the Tractive CAT 4 is designed for cats. Most dog trackers weigh 40–80g — too heavy for most cats (cat collars should weigh under 40g, and ideally have a breakaway safety mechanism). Never put a dog GPS collar on a cat.

### How accurate is the GPS location?

GPS accuracy is typically within 6–30 feet in open areas. Wi-Fi-based location is accurate to 30–150 feet. In practice, you'll know your pet is in a specific yard or building, not exactly which corner they're in.

### Do these trackers work as a substitute for microchipping?

No — never replace microchipping with a GPS tracker. Microchips are permanent, battery-free, and scanned by shelters and vets worldwide. GPS collars can fall off, run out of battery, or be removed. Both together provide the best protection.

### What should I do if my pet's GPS tracker shows them escaped?

1. Open the app and check the live location
2. Activate Lost Dog/Cat Mode for faster updates
3. Walk toward the last known location
4. Call your pet by name and check with neighbors
5. Post on Next Door and local Facebook groups
6. If location stops updating, the collar may have been removed or run out of battery — contact local shelters
