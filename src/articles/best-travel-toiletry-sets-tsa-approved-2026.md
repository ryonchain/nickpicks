## Travel Toiletry Buying Guide

### TSA liquids rule: what you need to know

The rule is 3-1-1: containers must be **3.4 oz (100ml) or smaller**, all containers must fit in **1 quart-sized clear zip-top bag**, and each traveler gets **1 bag**. This covers liquids, gels, aerosols, creams, and pastes — basically anything that isn't solid or powder.

The practical takeaway: a quart-size bag is about 7 × 8 inches and holds roughly 6–8 standard 3 oz bottles when packed efficiently. If you're bringing more than 6 products, prioritize and leave some behind or use solid alternatives.

### Refillable vs. pre-filled: which is right for you?

| Scenario | Better choice |
|----------|---------------|
| Specific brands you can't replace | Refillable bottles |
| Occasional traveler, less than 5 trips/year | Pre-filled set |
| Sustainable/reducing single-use plastic | Refillable bottles or solid bars |
| Budget under $20 for the whole kit | Pre-filled drugstore set |
| Want a set that lasts 3+ years | Premium refillable kit |

### Silicone vs. hard plastic bottles

**Silicone** squeeze bottles compress as they empty and are more durable than hard plastic. They don't crack when squeezed and seal better against leaks. The downside: they're harder to clean thoroughly, so don't mix strongly-pigmented products (like purple shampoo) with light formulas.

**Hard plastic** bottles are easier to clean and see the fill level through the translucent walls. They're lighter and cheaper. The tradeoff: they can crack under extreme cold or pressure in checked luggage.

### Solid toiletry alternatives: skip the liquids rule entirely

Solid toiletry alternatives bypass the 3-1-1 rule completely:
- **Shampoo bars** (Ethique, Lush, Function of Beauty)
- **Conditioner bars**
- **Solid moisturizer sticks** (Avène, First Aid Beauty)
- **Powder sunscreen** (Colorescience, CoverFX)
- **Sheet masks** instead of liquid toners/essences

A fully solid toiletry kit takes up minimal space and passes any checkpoint with zero liquid concerns.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the TSA liquids rule for toiletries?**
Each liquid, gel, or cream must be in a container of 3.4 oz (100ml) or less. All containers must fit in a single quart-sized clear zip-top bag. You are allowed one such bag per person.

**How long does a travel-size toiletry set last?**
A 3 oz bottle of shampoo lasts approximately 6–10 uses depending on hair length. For a 5–7 day trip, one 3 oz shampoo bottle is typically sufficient.

**Are pre-filled travel toiletry sets worth it vs. buying individual products?**
Pre-filled sets with your preferred brands are more convenient but cost more. A middle ground: buy a quality set of empty refillable bottles and fill them with your own products.

**What toiletry items don't count as liquids for TSA?**
Solid toiletries — bar shampoo, solid conditioner bars, solid deodorant, and bar soap — are not subject to the 3-1-1 rule. Powder-form products are also generally exempt, though powders over 12 oz may get additional screening.
