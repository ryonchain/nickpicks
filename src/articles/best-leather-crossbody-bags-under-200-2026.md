## Leather Crossbody Buying Guide

### Types of Leather: What to Look For

**Full-grain leather** is the top layer of the hide — the strongest, most durable, and most beautiful grade. It develops a patina over time. Only the best brands use full-grain leather under $200.

**Top-grain leather** is sanded to remove natural imperfections — slightly weaker than full-grain but more uniform in appearance. Most $75–$150 quality bags use top-grain leather.

**Genuine leather / bonded leather** are the lowest grades — thin, processed, and much less durable. Avoid for any bag you want to last more than 2–3 years.

### Size: What Fits in a Crossbody?

A standard crossbody bag comfortably holds:
- Smartphone (even large-screen models up to 6.7")
- Wallet (standard or slim card holder)
- Keys
- Lip gloss or small compact
- AirPods case

For phones larger than 6.7", earbuds, a full cosmetics kit, or a tablet, look for larger hobo-style crossbodies (like the Coach Hadley).

### Hardware Care

Polish metal hardware with a dry cloth to prevent oxidation. Keep leather conditioned with a leather balm (Leather Honey or Bickmore Bick 4) every 3–6 months to prevent drying and cracking.

**What leather crossbody bag is worth the money?**
Full-grain leather bags from Fossil, Coach, and Frye in the $120–$180 range are genuine investments — with proper care, they outlast 5–10 fast-fashion bags. A well-made leather crossbody from a heritage brand costing $150 today will still look excellent in 10 years.

**How do I clean and maintain a leather crossbody bag?**
Wipe with a slightly damp cloth to remove surface dirt. Apply a leather conditioner (Leather Honey or similar) every 3–6 months — this prevents cracking and maintains suppleness. Store stuffed with tissue paper to maintain shape. Avoid prolonged exposure to rain (if caught in rain, wipe dry immediately and let air dry away from direct heat).
