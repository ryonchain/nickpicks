## The Case for Tummy Time (And How Play Mats Help)

Tummy time is non-negotiable for baby development. It:
- Prevents flat spots (positional plagiocephaly) from time spent on their back
- Builds the neck, shoulder, and arm muscles needed for rolling and crawling
- Develops core strength foundational for sitting and walking

**The AAP recommends** starting tummy time from Day 1, beginning with just 1–2 minutes several times daily, building to 30+ minutes total per day by 3 months.

**How a play mat helps:** A cushioned, stimulating surface makes tummy time more engaging for baby and more comfortable to do on hard floors. High-contrast visuals at baby eye-level (many activity gyms include these) give babies a visual goal to reach for during tummy time.

## What to Look for in a Baby Play Mat

### Materials Safety
Look for products free from:
- BPA (bisphenol-A)
- Phthalates
- Formaldehyde
- Lead

GREENGUARD Gold certification or ASTM toy safety standards are reliable signals.

### Cleaning
Babies spit up. Products with machine-washable covers or waterproof surfaces are significantly more practical than hand/spot-clean only options.

### Developmental Alignment
Good activity gyms include elements aligned with the first year's developmental stages: high-contrast patterns (newborn), auditory stimulation (0–3 months), reaching objects at the right distance (3–6 months), mirrors for self-recognition (4–6+ months).

### Longevity
The best activity gyms work from 0–12 months across multiple configurations. Cheaper models may only be engaging for 2–3 months.

## FAQ

**Q: When can I start using a play mat?**
A: Day 1. Place baby on their back to look at high-contrast patterns. Start supervised tummy time within the first week (just 1–2 minutes initially).

**Q: How often should I do tummy time?**
A: AAP recommends several short sessions daily, working toward 30 minutes total per day by 3 months old.

**Q: Are foam play mats safe?**
A: Choose XPE or EVA foam that is GREENGUARD Gold certified and explicitly states it is free from formamide. Interlocking puzzle mats (common in gyms) sometimes contain formamide — verify before purchasing.

**Q: Can I wash the hanging toys from an activity gym?**
A: Check the label — most are surface-washable only. The mat cover is often removable and machine-washable. Toys with electronic components cannot be submerged.
