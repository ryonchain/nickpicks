## Noise-Cancelling vs. Regular: Quick Answer

**Active noise cancelling (ANC)** uses microphones and signal processing to actively cancel out ambient sound — particularly effective against constant low-frequency noise like HVAC, traffic, and bass-heavy music. It costs more ($80–$350).

**Passive isolation** (regular headphones) physically blocks sound by sealing over or in your ear. Effective against moderate ambient noise. Costs less ($20–$100).

**For dorm use specifically:** ANC is worth it if your roommate watches loud TV, plays music, or has frequent guests. If your main complaint is just wanting to focus without distractions, good passive isolation headphones in the $40–$70 range are often sufficient.

## When Is Active Noise Cancelling Worth It for College?

**ANC is worth it when:**
- Your dorm roommate has late-night social habits
- You study in coffee shops or public spaces
- Your building has loud HVAC systems
- You can't study without silence but can't always get a quiet space

**Passive headphones are sufficient when:**
- You have a quiet room or study space available
- You just want music/podcast accompaniment, not isolation
- Budget is $30–$50
