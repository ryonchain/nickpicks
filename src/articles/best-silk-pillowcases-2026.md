## What Momme Weight Means

Momme (mm) is the weight measure for silk — higher momme means denser weave, more silk, and longer durability. Think of it like thread count in cotton, but more meaningful:

- **16–19mm:** Entry-level. Genuine silk but thinner — feels luxurious initially, may show wear after 1–2 years.
- **22mm:** Sweet spot. Substantial drape, good durability, used by most premium brands.
- **25mm+:** Heavyweight. Most durable, richest texture, higher price. Worth it for long-term investment.

All listed options use mulberry silk (the highest quality variety, from silkworms fed exclusively on mulberry leaves). Avoid "charmeuse satin" marketing — charmeuse is a weave pattern, not a silk quality indicator.

## Buying Guide

### Silk vs. Satin: The Key Difference

Satin is a weave pattern, not a material. Polyester satin pillowcases are marketed as "silk alternatives" but don't provide the same benefits: polyester creates static, doesn't regulate temperature, and doesn't have the same low-friction surface as real silk. If the pillowcase is under $20 for a pair and doesn't specify mulberry silk and momme weight, it's almost certainly polyester satin.

### How to Care for Silk Pillowcases

- Machine wash on delicate/gentle cycle in a mesh laundry bag
- Use a gentle silk detergent (Woolite or dedicated silk wash)
- Cold water only — heat degrades silk proteins
- Air dry flat or hang — never tumble dry
- Iron inside-out on low setting if needed; most won't require ironing

### Real Benefits vs. Marketing Claims

**Proven:** Reduced hair friction → less overnight frizz and mechanical breakage. Particularly significant for textured, curly, or fine hair.

**Likely:** Reduced facial pressure creasing from sleeping on a softer surface. Won't eliminate sleep lines but may reduce their depth and duration.

**Overstated:** "Anti-aging" and "skin hydration" claims. Silk doesn't hydrate skin or reverse aging — it just doesn't absorb moisture from your skin the way cotton does (keeping your moisturizer on your face rather than on the pillow).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do silk pillowcases really help with hair?**
Yes — the friction reduction is real and measurable. Less friction = less mechanical breakage, split ends, and frizz. Most significant for textured or fine hair that tangles easily overnight.

**How often should I wash a silk pillowcase?**
Every 1–2 weeks is typical. More frequent washing if you use heavy leave-in products or face oils overnight.

**Will a silk pillowcase help with acne?**
Indirectly — silk doesn't absorb skincare products or sebum the way cotton does, so it's more hygienic for acne-prone skin. But it doesn't treat acne.

**What's the difference between 19mm and 22mm silk?**
22mm is heavier, more durable, and has a richer texture. 19mm delivers the same benefits but is more delicate and won't last as long with frequent washing. Start with 19mm if you're uncertain; upgrade to 22mm if you decide to commit.
