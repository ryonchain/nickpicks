High school shopping has changed. The graphing calculator is still required. But now you also need a laptop that handles Google Classroom, a sturdy backpack for six classes, and earbuds that don't die at lunch. Here's what to actually buy for high school in 2026 — organized by what matters and what doesn't.

---

## What High Schoolers Actually Need (And What Parents Think They Need)

**They definitely need:**
- Laptop or Chromebook for schoolwork
- Quality backpack (will carry 15–20 lbs daily)
- Graphing calculator (required for Algebra 2, Pre-Calc, AP Calculus)
- Pens, notebooks, and folders
- Earbuds or headphones

**Nice to have:**
- Noise-canceling headphones for study sessions
- Good desk lamp
- USB-C charger brick (fast charging)

**Usually skip:**
- Tablet (redundant with laptop for most students)
- Fancy pen sets (Pilot G2 is all you need)
- Wireless printer (school printers are free)

## Graphing Calculator: Non-Negotiable

If your school requires a graphing calculator (check your math syllabus), buy it — phone calculator apps aren't allowed on most tests.

### Texas Instruments TI-84 Plus CE (~$95)

The TI-84 Plus CE is the standard. Most teachers teach on this calculator. It's allowed on the SAT, ACT, AP exams, and most standardized tests. Buy the color edition — the rechargeable battery is a significant upgrade over the older AAA battery version.

**Avoid:** TI-83 (too old), TI-89 (not allowed on many exams), Casio alternatives (less teacher support)

**Tip:** Buy used or refurbished TI-84 on Amazon or eBay for $40–60. The calculator software hasn't changed. Used is fine.

## School Supplies: Buy Less, Buy Better

The urge to buy a beautiful new supply setup every fall is real. Resist over-buying — most of it won't get used.

**Essential list:**
- 3-ring binder (1.5–2 inch, with pockets) x2
- Loose-leaf college-ruled paper (1 pack)
- Mead 5-subject notebook (for classes with lots of notes)
- Ballpoint pens — Pilot G2 (black, 0.7mm) is the gold standard
- Yellow highlighters (Sharpie brand — they don't bleed through as much)
- Tab dividers for binders

**Skip:**
- Multicolor pen sets (you'll use black 95% of the time)
- Fancy planner systems (use Google Calendar — it's on every device)
- Mechanical pencil sets (get two Pentel P205 pencils and call it done)

## Study Tech That Actually Helps

**Desk lamp:** Natural daylight LED temperature (5000K–6500K) for evening study, warm (2700K–3000K) for late night. BenQ ScreenBar or a simple Govee adjustable desk lamp both work.

**White noise app or speaker:** Spotify has "study noise" playlists. A $15 white noise machine in your room is worth it if you have siblings or thin walls.

**Blue light glasses:** Evidence on benefits is mixed, but many students swear by them for long screen days. If you find eye strain during evening studying, $20 computer glasses are worth trying.

## Back to School Sales Calendar

**Tax-free weekends:** Many states offer sales-tax-free shopping on school supplies and clothing in late July and early August. Search your state's schedule — in Texas and Florida, for example, that's $0 in tax on the calculator, backpack, and clothing purchases.

**Amazon back-to-school:** Runs through late August with rotating deals on electronics and supplies. Price-check on CamelCamelCamel — not every "sale" is actually a price drop.

**Target Circle:** Extra 5–15% off school supplies for Circle members in August.

**Apple Education Store:** Students (and parents buying for students) can get $100–150 off MacBooks and iPads through the education store at apple.com/education.
