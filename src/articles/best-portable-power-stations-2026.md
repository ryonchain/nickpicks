## How to Calculate What You Need

**Step 1 — List your devices:**

| Device | Typical Watts |
|--------|--------------|
| Laptop | 45–90W |
| Phone charger | 18–65W |
| CPAP (no heat) | 30–60W |
| Mini fridge | 40–100W |
| Fan (small) | 20–50W |
| LED lamp | 5–15W |
| Electric blanket | 50–100W |
| Coffee maker | 900–1,500W |
| Toaster | 800–1,800W |

**Step 2 — Estimate daily Wh:** Watts × Hours of use = Wh. A mini fridge (60W) running 8 hours = 480Wh. A CPAP at 45W for 8 hours = 360Wh.

**Step 3 — Add 20% buffer:** Real-world efficiency loss means you get roughly 80% of rated capacity.

**Step 4 — Size your station:** Need 500Wh daily? A 1,000Wh station gives you ~2 days per charge.

## Solar Charging Guide

| Panel Watts | Time to Charge 1,000Wh Station |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| 100W (1 panel) | 10–14 hours (full sun) |
| 200W (2 panels) | 5–7 hours |
| 400W (4 panels) | 2.5–3.5 hours |

Solar charging time varies with cloud cover, panel angle, and latitude. Size your panel array for 3–4× the station's max solar input rate for reliable daily recharging.
