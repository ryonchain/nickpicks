## Dishwasher Buying Guide

### How Quiet is Quiet Enough?

| dBA Level | Comparison | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| 38–42 dBA | Library whisper | Open-plan living areas, conversation during cycle |
| 44–47 dBA | Refrigerator hum | Standard kitchens adjacent to living areas |
| 48–52 dBA | Moderate background noise | Separate kitchens with door closure |
| 53+ dBA | Noticeable appliance noise | Utility rooms, kitchens away from living spaces |

### Drying Methods Compared

**Condensation drying (Bosch standard):** No heating element — moisture evaporates by condensing on the cool stainless steel interior. Energy-efficient but slow on plastics.

**AutoAir / auto-open:** Door automatically cracks at cycle end, allowing steam to escape and outside air to circulate. Significantly improves plastic drying over condensation-only.

**CrystalDry (Bosch 800+):** Zeolite minerals absorb moisture and release heat. Best results on plastics of any passive system.

**Fan drying (KitchenAid ProDry, LG):** Active fan circulates air post-wash. Fast on plastics, higher energy use.

**Heated dry:** Electric heating element dries fast but uses the most energy and can damage some plastics over time.

### Installation Checklist

Before ordering, confirm:
- Standard opening is 24" wide — measure height clearance (standard 34")
- Hot water line and drain connection available under sink
- Electrical: 120V, 15A dedicated circuit (most installations)
- Flooring: dishwasher height adjustment handles minor floor height variation
- Removing old unit: most retailers remove old appliance for $25–$50

### Maintenance Basics

- **Monthly:** Clean filter (bottom of tub) — clogged filters are the leading cause of poor wash results
- **Quarterly:** Run empty with dishwasher cleaner (Affresh is the most recommended brand)
- **Annually:** Check door seal for cracks or debris
- **Use rinse aid:** Dramatically improves drying results regardless of drying method
