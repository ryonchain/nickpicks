### 5. Roli Seaboard Block — Best MIDI Controller with Polyphonic Aftertouch

**Price: ~$350**

The Roli Seaboard uses a silicone surface instead of traditional keys. Each "key" responds to five simultaneous types of touch: strike (velocity), slide (pitch bend per note), glide (vertical position), press (aftertouch), and lift. This is called MPE (MIDI Polyphonic Expression) — each note carries independent expression data, like a string instrument.

**Why it's different:**
- **MPE-enabled** — each note has individual expression control (pressure, slide, vibrato)
- Silicone "keywaves" — no mechanical noise, ultra-expressive per-note control
- 25-note playing range, 5-octave pitch range via slide
- Compact, portable — connects via USB or Bluetooth
- Works with MPE-capable instruments in Logic, Ableton, Bitwig, and standalone apps

**Honest trade-off:** Requires a learning curve. Traditional piano technique doesn't translate directly. Best for producers who want expressivity beyond velocity.

**Best for:** Producers working with cinematic soundscapes, expressive electronic music, and MPE-capable software instruments.

## Key Count Guide

- **25 keys (2 octaves):** Portable. Use octave up/down buttons constantly. Fine for drums, samples, and simple melodic lines.
- **37 keys (3 octaves):** Better range without size penalty. The minimum for comfortable melodic playing.
- **49 keys (4 octaves):** The sweet spot. Enough range for most pop and electronic production without being desk-filling.
- **61 keys (5 octaves):** For piano players who think in full range. Covers most piano literature without octave-shifting.
- **88 keys (full piano):** Full piano range, usually weighted keys. For serious piano players or film composers.
