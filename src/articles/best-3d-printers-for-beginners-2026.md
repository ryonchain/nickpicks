## What to Look for in a Beginner 3D Printer

**Auto-Leveling:** Non-negotiable in 2026. Manual bed leveling was the #1 frustration for beginners. Every printer on this list has it.

**Build Volume:** 220x220x250mm is the sweet spot for beginners — large enough for most projects, small enough for affordable filament use.

**Filament Compatibility:** Start with PLA — it's the easiest material. PETG is the next step up. ABS requires an enclosure.

**Open-Source vs. Proprietary:** Bambu's ecosystem is excellent but proprietary. Creality's Klipper-based machines are open-source and endlessly hackable.

**Print Speed:** Faster isn't always better for beginners. Slower speeds (60–100mm/s) produce cleaner prints while you're learning slicer settings.

## Best Filament for Beginners

| Filament | Difficulty | Temp | Best For |
|----------|-----------|------|---------|
| PLA | Easy | 190–220°C | Everything — start here |
| PETG | Medium | 230–250°C | Stronger parts, functional prints |
| TPU | Medium | 220–240°C | Flexible parts |
| ABS | Hard | 230–260°C | Heat-resistant parts — needs enclosure |
