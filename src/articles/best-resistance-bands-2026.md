## Buying Guide: What to Look for in Resistance Bands

- **Resistance range:** Beginners need 5–30 lb; intermediate lifters benefit from stackable sets reaching 75–150 lb.
- **Material:** Latex loop bands are versatile; fabric bands are better for lower-body work (no rolling); tube bands with handles replicate cable machine movements best.
- **Included accessories:** Door anchors and ankle straps dramatically expand the exercise library — prioritize sets that include them.
- **Portability:** All bands pack small, but tube sets with accessories are larger; loop bands are the lightest travel option.

## FAQ

**Q: Can resistance bands replace weights?**
A: For many exercises — rows, presses, curls, squats — yes. Bands create constant tension throughout the range of motion, which can actually be more effective than free weights for muscle activation. For heavy compound lifts like deadlifts, bars and plates remain superior.

**Q: How do I know which resistance level to use?**
A: Choose a band that challenges you by the 12th rep of any exercise. If you can do 20+ reps easily, go heavier; if you can't complete 8 reps with good form, go lighter.

**Q: Do resistance bands snap?**
A: Quality latex bands rarely snap under normal use. Inspect bands before each use for nicks or cracks, avoid exposing them to direct sunlight for long periods, and replace any band that shows visible wear.
