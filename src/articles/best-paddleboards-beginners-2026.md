## Paddleboard Buying Guide

### Inflatable vs. Hard Board: Which Should Beginners Choose?

**Inflatable (99% of beginners):**
- Packs into a backpack: transport in any car, store in a closet
- Virtually indestructible (military PVC bounces off rocks)
- Performance matches hard boards for recreational paddling under 10 mph
- Pump included; inflates to paddling pressure in 8–12 minutes

**Hard board (advanced paddlers, racers):**
- Faster and more responsive for performance paddling and racing
- No pump — launch-ready immediately
- Requires roof rack for transport, dedicated storage space
- More fragile (can crack or delaminate if dropped)

For 95% of beginners, inflatable is the right choice.

### Sizing for Beginners

| Paddler Weight | Recommended Board |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Under 150 lbs | 10'–10'6", 30"–32" wide |
| 150–200 lbs | 10'6"–11', 31"–33" wide |
| 200–250 lbs | 11'–11'6", 32"–34" wide |
| 250+ lbs | 11'6"+, 33"+ wide |

Stability tip: Wider boards are more stable but slower. Choose width over length for your first board.

### Essential Safety Gear

- **Leash:** Keeps the board attached to you if you fall. Always wear it.
- **Personal Flotation Device (PFD):** Required by law in most US waters. A lightweight waist-belt PFD is least intrusive.
- **Sun protection:** Water reflects UV — reapply sunscreen every 60–90 minutes.

## FAQ

**How difficult is paddleboarding to learn?**
Most beginners are standing and paddling within 15–30 minutes of first trying. Starting on your knees and transitioning to standing as you get comfortable eliminates most of the early frustration.

**Where can I paddleboard?**
Any calm-to-moderate water body: lakes, bays, rivers, protected ocean coves. Avoid strong currents, heavy boat traffic, and breaking surf until you're experienced.

**Can paddleboards hold two people?**
Some tandem boards are designed for two riders. Standard 10'–11' boards have weight capacities of 250–350 lbs — technically enough for two light adults, but stability suffers significantly. A tandem-specific board is better if paddling together regularly.
