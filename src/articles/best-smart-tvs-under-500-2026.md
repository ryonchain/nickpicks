## Smart TV Buying Guide

### QLED vs. OLED: Which Wins Under $500?

True OLED is rarely available under $500 in 2026 (you'll pay $700+). In this price range, QLED Mini-LED is the winner for most rooms — it gets much brighter than OLED (critical in lit rooms) at competitive cost.

| Technology | Best For | Brightness | Contrast |
|------------|----------|-----------|---------|
| QLED Mini-LED | Bright rooms, sports, gaming | Excellent | Very Good |
| OLED | Dark rooms, movie watching | Good | Perfect |
| IPS LED | Budget, well-lit spaces | Moderate | Average |

### What Size to Buy?

| Room & Viewing Distance | Recommended Size |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| Bedroom (8–10 ft) | 43"–55" |
| Living room (10–12 ft) | 55"–65" |
| Living room (12–15 ft) | 65"–75" |
| Home theater (15ft+) | 75"–85" |

### Key Features to Check Before Buying

**1. HDMI 2.1** — Required for 4K/120Hz gaming (PS5, Xbox Series X). Not all TVs under $500 include it.

**2. Dolby Vision vs. HDR10+** — Samsung uses HDR10+ exclusively. All others support Dolby Vision. Both are fine; Dolby Vision has wider streaming support.

**3. Refresh rate** — 60Hz is fine for movies and TV. 120Hz is important for sports and gaming.

**4. VESA mount pattern** — Confirm your wall mount matches the TV's VESA pattern before buying.

**5. Smart TV OS** — Google TV and webOS receive more frequent app updates than proprietary systems.

## FAQs

**Is a $500 smart TV actually worth buying in 2026?**
Absolutely. The TCL QM8 and Hisense U8N at this price point outperform $1,000+ TVs from just a few years ago. Mini-LED backlighting and Dolby Vision are now affordable enough to be competitive with mid-range OLED.

**Do I still need a streaming stick if I buy a smart TV?**
In most cases, no. Google TV (TCL, Hisense), Fire TV (Amazon), webOS (LG), and Tizen (Samsung) are all fast and well-stocked with apps in 2026. A streaming stick may marginally improve performance on older or budget models with sluggish processors.

Yes — all five picks are VESA-mount compatible. Check the TV's specific VESA pattern (printed in the manual or Amazon listing specs) to match it with your wall mount bracket.
