## Men's Dress Shirt Buying Guide

### Finding Your Size

Dress shirts are sized by **neck** and **sleeve** measurements — not S/M/L. Measure:

1. **Neck:** Measure around the base of your neck, finger under the measuring tape. Add 0.5" for comfort.
2. **Sleeve:** Measure from the center back of your neck, over the shoulder, to the wrist bone with arm slightly bent.

Common example: 15.5 neck / 34 sleeve = "15.5/34" or "M/34."

S/M/L shirts exist but vary significantly by brand; neck/sleeve measurement eliminates guesswork.

### Fit Guide

| Fit | Who It's For | Chest Allowance |
|-----|-------------|-----------------|
| Slim/Tailored | Athletic or slim builds | 2–3" |
| Regular/Classic | Average builds | 3–4" |
| Relaxed/Comfort | Larger builds or comfort preference | 4–6" |

### Fabric Guide

- **100% Cotton (Poplin, Pinpoint, Oxford):** Most breathable, most comfortable, requires ironing unless treated
- **Cotton-Polyester blend:** Wrinkle resistant, less breathable, best for travel or iron-free wearers
- **Cotton-Spandex:** Adds stretch, good for movement; slight sheen, slightly less formal
- **Non-Iron/Wrinkle-Free treatments:** Applied to cotton to resist wrinkling; quality varies significantly

### Care Tips

- Wash in cold water on a gentle cycle
- Remove promptly from dryer — hang immediately to prevent wrinkles setting in
- For Non-Iron shirts: hang directly from dryer; do not fold and leave
- Collar and cuffs: pre-treat with stain spray before washing

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many dress shirts should I own?**
At minimum, 5–7 allows Monday–Friday wear without rushing laundry. If you wear them daily, 10–12 is practical with weekly washing.

**Should I choose regular or slim fit?**
Try both if possible. Slim fit looks more modern; regular fit is more comfortable. Athletic builds often find regular fit boxy — start with slim. Larger builds may find slim tight through the torso — regular or comfort fit is better.

**Are non-iron treatments permanent?**
No. The treatment fades after many washes (typically 50–80 washing cycles). Re-treating at home is possible with fabric starch sprays, though not as effective as factory treatment.

**Can dress shirts be machine-washed?**
Yes, all listed options are machine-washable. Use cold water, gentle cycle. Hot water and aggressive cycles degrade both fabric and any non-iron treatment.
