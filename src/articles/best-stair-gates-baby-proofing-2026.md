# Best Stair Gates for Baby Proofing in 2026

A baby gate at the top of stairs is one of the most critical safety measures in a home with young children — a fall down stairs is a leading cause of serious injury in children under 5. The critical distinction: **hardware-mounted gates** (which screw into the wall or stair post) are required for the top of stairs; **pressure-mounted gates** (which push outward against the door frame) are only safe for doorways, not stairs.

---

## Hardware-Mounted (Top of Stairs) vs. Pressure-Mounted (Doorways)

| Type | Where to Use | Installation |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Hardware-mounted | TOP OF STAIRS (required) | Screws into wall studs or newel post |
| Pressure-mounted | Doorways, bottom of stairs | Pressure against door frame only |

**Never use a pressure-mounted gate at the top of stairs.** A determined toddler will dislodge it and fall.

## Installation Safety for Stair Gates

1. **Find the studs:** Use a stud finder. Gates at top of stairs must be screwed into studs, not drywall.
2. **Use the hardware provided:** Don't substitute shorter screws. The included screws are sized for load-bearing into studs.
3. **Test after installation:** Put weight against the gate before relying on it. Try to push it toward the stairwell with significant force.
4. **Monthly check:** Tighten all screws monthly — vibration loosens hardware over time.

---

## When Does a Child No Longer Need Gates?

Most children can reliably navigate stairs by age 2–3. Gates become unnecessary when:
- The child can consistently climb and descend stairs safely
- The child understands not to run near stairs
- You've had conversations about stair safety and the child demonstrates it

Remove gates when they're no longer needed — a child who knows stairs are safe and shouldn't need a gate but always sees a gate may push on it with more force than the hardware supports.
