## How to Choose MagSafe Accessories

**Check the wattage claim.** Only Apple MagSafe and certified Qi2 chargers deliver 15W to iPhone 17. Generic magnetic chargers are often 7.5W Qi or 10W Qi — they work, but not at full speed.

**Look for MFi certification.** "Made for iPhone" accessories are Apple-tested and won't mess with Face ID or the camera.

**Avoid knockoffs.** Cheap magnetic wallets and mounts often use weak magnets that drop your phone at 30mph or a speed bump.

**Match your case.** Many MagSafe accessories require a MagSafe-compatible case (with the built-in magnet ring). A non-MagSafe case will reduce magnet strength significantly.

## Do You Need MagSafe Accessories?

Not everyone does. If you charge with a cable overnight and don't carry extra cards, you can skip MagSafe entirely. But if you:
- Want wireless charging that actually aligns properly
- Carry 1–3 cards without a wallet
- Mount your phone in the car for navigation
- Use iPhone 17 as a FaceTime or StandBy display

...then MagSafe accessories replace several single-purpose gadgets with one clean magnet system. The ecosystem has reached the point where it's genuinely useful for everyday iPhone users.
