## How to Choose: Machine Types Explained

### Pod/Capsule Machines (Nespresso, Keurig)
- **Consistency:** 10/10. Same shot every time.
- **Control:** 0/10. No adjustability.
- **Coffee quality ceiling:** Medium. Good for milk drinks; flat for pure espresso.
- **Best for:** Speed-first households; non-coffee-enthusiasts; low-mess setups

### Super-Automatic (bean-to-cup)
- **Consistency:** 9/10. Automated grind, tamp, extract, steam.
- **Control:** Low to medium (basic adjustments possible).
- **Coffee quality ceiling:** High (2024 machines produce genuinely excellent shots).
- **Best for:** Coffee lovers who want quality without daily manual process

### Semi-Automatic (manual espresso)
- **Consistency:** Depends entirely on operator skill.
- **Control:** Maximum.
- **Coffee quality ceiling:** Highest possible (limited only by beans and operator skill).
- **Best for:** Enthusiasts who want to learn and master espresso craft

### Key Specs That Actually Matter
- **Boiler type:** Single (one temp setting), dual (independent brew and steam temps), HX (heat exchanger—compromise between both)
- **Brew pressure:** 9 bars is ideal; avoid machines claiming "15 bars" as the marketing includes pump pressure, not extraction pressure
- **Portafilter size:** 58mm is commercial-standard; smaller sizes (51–54mm) limit tamper and basket options
- **Steam wand:** Panarello (automatic frothing, less control) vs. commercial wand (manual microfoam, latte art capable)

- **Tamper:** 58mm calibrated tamper if your machine uses standard portafilter
