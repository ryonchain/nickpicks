## When to Use Probiotics

**Antibiotic recovery:** Start probiotics the day antibiotics begin (dose them 2+ hours apart) and continue for 2 weeks after the antibiotic course ends. Antibiotics disrupt the entire gut microbiome, not just pathogenic bacteria.

**Travel and stress:** Begin 3–5 days before boarding, travel, or any stressful event. The microbiome stress response can cause diarrhea before any symptoms appear.

**Diet transitions:** When switching dog food brands or formulas, add probiotics during the transition period (typically 7–10 days) to minimize digestive upset.

**Post-illness:** After any illness involving diarrhea or vomiting, probiotics help re-establish healthy populations. Use for at least 2 weeks after symptoms resolve.

## Prebiotic vs Probiotic — What's the Difference?

- **Probiotics:** Live bacteria you add to the gut
- **Prebiotics:** Dietary fiber (FOS, inulin, chicory root) that feeds the existing beneficial bacteria
- **Synbiotics:** Products combining both — the gold standard for microbiome support

Look for products that include prebiotic fiber alongside probiotic strains. Fiber feeds the bacteria you're adding, dramatically increasing their colonization success.
