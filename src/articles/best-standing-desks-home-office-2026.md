## Standing Desk Buying Guide

### Single Motor vs. Dual Motor

| | Single Motor | Dual Motor |
|--|-------------|------------|
| Stability | Less stable at full height | More stable |
| Speed | Slower | Faster |
| Weight capacity | 100–200 lbs typically | 200–400 lbs |
| Price | Lower | Higher |

**Recommendation:** Always get a dual-motor desk. The stability and lifting capacity are worth the extra $50–100.

### Height Range: Will It Fit You?

Most desks range from ~23"–49". This covers users from 5'2" to 6'3" in standard sitting and standing positions. If you're under 5'2" or over 6'4", verify the specific height range before buying.

**Standing desk height formula:**
- Elbows at 90° when standing = correct monitor arm height
- Most ergonomics guides suggest standing desk height = floor-to-elbow measurement (standing)

### Frame Width and Desktop Size

Standard home office desks: 48" or 60" wide. Larger setups (dual monitors + laptop stand): 70"–80". Wider desks add stability and workspace; they also require more floor space.

### Anti-Collision Technology

Anti-collision stops the desk when it detects resistance — preventing it from crushing items (or hands) left under the desk. Standard on most quality desks above $300. Worth confirming before buying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much weight can a standing desk hold?**
Entry-level desks: 150–200 lbs. Quality dual-motor desks: 300–355 lbs. Most home office setups (dual monitors, PC, accessories) weigh under 100 lbs — the weight limit is rarely a practical constraint.

**Are standing desks worth it?**
Research consistently shows that alternating between sitting and standing reduces back pain and increases energy levels. The benefit comes from *movement*, not standing itself — use the sit-stand cycle: sit 20 minutes, stand 8, move 2.

**How long do electric standing desks last?**
Quality motors (FlexiSpot, Uplift) are rated for 50,000+ cycles — one cycle is one height change. At 10 cycles per day, that's over 13 years of daily use. Frame warranties of 5–15 years reflect this durability.

**Can I convert my existing desk to a standing desk?**
Yes — standing desk converters (like FlexiSpot M7) sit on top of your existing desk and raise your monitor and keyboard. They're cheaper ($200–400) but less ergonomic than a full standing desk.

**How noisy are electric standing desks?**
Most dual-motor desks operate at 45–50 dB during height changes — comparable to a quiet conversation. Adjustments take 15–30 seconds. Noise is not a practical issue in a home office.
