## How to Choose Window and Door Alarm Sensors

### Start With Your Ecosystem

The single most important question is what smart home platform you're already using. Ring sensors only work with Ring Alarm. SimpliSafe sensors only work with SimpliSafe. If you have Ring cameras and Echo devices, the Ring Contact Sensor is the logical choice. If you're starting fresh with no existing system, evaluate what you want: Eufy offers the most ecosystem flexibility via Matter, Wyze offers the lowest cost, and SABRE offers the simplest standalone solution.

### Hub vs. Standalone

Hub-based sensors (Ring, SimpliSafe, Wyze, Eufy) enable smartphone alerts, remote arm/disarm, and integration with other smart devices. They require a base station or hub to function, which adds upfront cost but unlocks significantly more capability. Standalone sensors (SABRE) sound a local alarm immediately with no internet, no account, and no monthly fee — but you won't receive an alert if you're away from home. For primary residences where remote awareness matters, hub-based systems are superior. For cabins, rental units, or backup coverage, standalone sensors are often the better choice.

### Coverage Planning: How Many Sensors Do You Need?

Count every entry point on the ground floor: front door, back door, garage entry door, and every ground-floor window. A typical home has 6–12 entry points. Add sensors on any basement windows and second-floor windows accessible from a deck or porch. Most security professionals recommend securing every ground-floor window — not just doors — since windows are the second most common entry point after doors. Systems like SABRE (45 sensors) and Ring (no practical limit) scale to full coverage without degraded performance.

### Professional Monitoring vs. Self-Monitoring

Professional monitoring sends a human dispatcher to verify alarms and call emergency services if you're unreachable. Ring Protect Pro and SimpliSafe's Fast Protect plan both offer this for around $20/month. Self-monitoring relies on you to respond to smartphone alerts. Self-monitoring is sufficient for attentive homeowners who are typically available; professional monitoring adds a critical backup for travel, late nights, or phone-unreachable situations. Both Ring and SimpliSafe offer professional monitoring without long-term contracts, making it easy to activate during periods when you need it and cancel when you don't.
