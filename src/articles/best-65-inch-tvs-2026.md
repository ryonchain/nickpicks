## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is 65 inches too big for a living room?**
At 8–10 feet of viewing distance, which is typical for most living rooms, 65 inches is the sweet spot — large enough to be immersive without requiring uncomfortable head movement. The general rule is to sit at a distance of 1.5 to 2.5 times the screen's diagonal measurement. For 65 inches, that's 8–14 feet.

**What is the difference between OLED and Mini-LED for a 65-inch TV?**
OLED produces true black (pixel-level off), infinite contrast, and wider viewing angles. Mini-LED uses thousands of LED zones behind an LCD panel to approximate OLED blacks — it achieves higher peak brightness (better for bright rooms) but with some light bloom around bright objects in dark scenes. OLED wins in dark rooms; Mini-LED (especially TCL QM8 and Hisense U8N) wins in bright rooms.

**Do I need a soundbar with a 65-inch TV?**
Most 65-inch TVs sound noticeably better than smaller sets due to larger speaker enclosures, but built-in audio still sounds thin compared to even a budget soundbar. For a dedicated living room setup, a soundbar adds meaningful impact to movies, sports, and gaming. Sony's A95L with Acoustic Surface Audio is the exception — its built-in audio is genuinely impressive.

**Which 65-inch TV is best for a bright living room?**
The Samsung S95D and Hisense U8N are the top choices for bright rooms. The S95D's QD-OLED panel hits ~2,000 nits with an excellent anti-reflective coating. The Hisense U8N's Mini-LED can exceed 3,000 nits and offers strong anti-glare performance at a much lower price.
