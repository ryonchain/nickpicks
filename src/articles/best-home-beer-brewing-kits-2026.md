## Homebrewing Beginner Guide

**Extract vs. all-grain brewing:** Extract brewing uses pre-made malt extract — you add it to water, boil with hops, add yeast, and ferment. All-grain brewing requires converting grain starches to sugar yourself (mashing). Beginners start with extract; it's faster, easier, and produces excellent beer.

**The 4-week timeline:** Most ales ferment in 1–2 weeks and condition in bottles for another 1–2 weeks before they're ready to drink. Total time from brew day to first sip: typically 3–4 weeks.

**Essential additions:** A wort chiller ($30–$50) rapidly cools your beer before pitching yeast, reducing the risk of infection and improving flavor. It's the single best equipment upgrade after your first batch.

**Legal note:** Home beer brewing is legal for personal consumption in all 50 US states (up to 100 gallons/year for a single adult, 200 gallons for a two-adult household). Selling homebrew is not legal without a license.
