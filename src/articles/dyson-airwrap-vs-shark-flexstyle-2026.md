The Dyson Airwrap launched at $550 in 2018 and created an entirely new product category: the air-powered multi-styler. The Shark FlexStyle launched in 2022 at $250 and directly challenged it. Four years later, both have been refined — and the question isn't "is the FlexStyle just a cheap copy?" (it's not) but "is the Airwrap worth $200–300 more?"

Short answer: for most people, no. Here's why, and when the Airwrap is worth it.

---

## How Both Work

Both tools use the **Coanda effect** — high-velocity air flowing over a curved surface creates negative pressure that pulls hair toward the barrel and wraps it, without the hair ever touching a hot surface. The result: curls, waves, and volume with significantly less heat damage than traditional hot tools.

This is the key innovation. Traditional curling irons grip hair directly at 350–400°F. Airwrap and FlexStyle curl hair using airflow at 150–200°F. Lower heat, similar results.

## Performance Comparison

### Curling and Waving

Both tools produce comparable curl results on fine-to-medium hair. The auto-wrap barrel on both works similarly — you hold the tool near your hair and air draws it in. The technique takes 10–15 minutes to learn on either tool.

**Airwrap advantage:** The barrel diameter options cover a wider range of curl sizes in the Complete Long set. The styling airflow feels more refined — slightly more consistent wrapping, slightly more predictable results on a first pass.

**FlexStyle advantage:** The attachments click on faster. The auto-wrap barrel wraps consistently and delivers results within 10% of Airwrap performance in most testing scenarios.

**Verdict:** On fine hair, results are nearly identical. On medium to thick hair, the Airwrap handles the load more smoothly. On coarse or very thick hair, neither is as fast as a direct-heat curling iron.

### Drying

The FlexStyle has a legitimate practical advantage here: it folds flat and functions as a standalone hair dryer with a conventional concentrator nozzle. You can use it as your daily dryer, then add attachments when styling. This means one tool replaces two.

The Airwrap also includes a drying attachment, but it's an attachment — you switch to it. The FlexStyle is functionally a dryer-first that adds styling. Different philosophy.

**Verdict:** FlexStyle wins on drying versatility.

### Build Quality

Dyson's build quality is meaningfully better. Magnesium alloy housing, magnetic attachment system that feels premium, refined styling elements. The FlexStyle feels like quality plastic — not cheap, but plastic.

**Verdict:** Airwrap wins on build quality.

## What the Airwrap Does Better

1. **Build quality** — magnetic attachments, premium materials, feels worth $550
2. **Curl refinement on medium-thick hair** — more consistent wrapping, more predictable results
3. **Color range** — Dyson releases seasonal colorways, the Complete Long set covers more styling options
4. **Warranty and support** — 2-year warranty vs. 1-year, Dyson's service experience is more established

## What the FlexStyle Does Better

1. **Drying versatility** — folds into a functional everyday dryer, true 2-in-1
2. **Price** — $200–300 cheaper, real money
3. **Attachment speed** — click-on mechanism is faster than magnetic barrel rotation
4. **Value per result** — delivers 85–90% of Airwrap results for most hair types at significantly lower cost

## Who Should Buy Each

**Buy the Dyson Airwrap if:**
- Budget is not a constraint and you want the best-in-class tool
- You have medium-thick hair and want the most refined curling performance
- You already own a separate hair dryer and don't need 2-in-1 functionality
- You value build quality and magnet attachment system
- You're a collector of premium versions of things you use daily

**Buy the Shark FlexStyle if:**
- You want to replace both your dryer and styler with one tool
- Budget matters — $200–300 savings is real to you
- You have fine-to-medium hair (performance gap is minimal)
- You're buying for a teenager, college student, or travel
- You've never used either tool and want to try the technology before committing to $550+

---

## Bottom Line

The Shark FlexStyle is not a cheap copy of the Dyson Airwrap — it's a genuine competitor that delivers comparable results on fine-to-medium hair at significantly lower cost. The price difference ($200–300) is the primary decision factor.

If budget allows: the Airwrap is the more refined, premium tool and worth the investment for medium-thick hair where the performance gap is real. If budget matters or you have fine hair: the FlexStyle is the better value by a clear margin.
