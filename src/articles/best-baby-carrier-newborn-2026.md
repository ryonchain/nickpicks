## Carrier Types Explained

**Stretchy Wraps:** Long piece of fabric wound around both baby and caregiver. Most gentle and close for newborns. Learning curve for the tie. Best for 0–4 months.

**Woven Wraps:** Same concept as stretchy but with woven fabric — more supportive, better for toddlers. Steeper learning curve.

**Ring Slings:** Fabric through two rings on one shoulder. Quick to put on, good for hip carry with mobile toddlers. One-sided weight distribution.

**Soft Structured Carriers (SSC):** Padded waist belt + padded shoulder straps with buckles. Most accessible for new users. The dominant choice for most parents.

**Meh Dai / Onbuhimo:** Asian-inspired designs. Meh Dai uses tie straps; Onbuhimo is a buckle carrier without a waist belt (popular for toddler back carry).

## Safety: T.I.C.K.S. Rule

All babywearing follows the TICKS safety guideline:
- **T**ight: carrier snug against your body
- **I**n view at all times: always see baby's face
- **C**lose enough to kiss: chin at your chest
- **K**eep chin off chest: baby's chin tilted up
- **S**upported back: back in natural curved position
