## Solar Outdoor Light Buying Guide

### How Do Solar Lights Work?

Solar outdoor lights contain a small photovoltaic (PV) panel that converts sunlight into electricity during the day, stored in a rechargeable battery (typically NiMH or lithium-ion). At dusk, a light sensor triggers the LEDs to illuminate using the stored power.

### Key Specifications to Understand

**Lumens:** The actual measure of brightness. Path lights are typically 10–50 lumens (decorative). Security and spotlight use should be 400–1,500+ lumens. String lights are measured differently — look for warm ambient glow descriptions.

**IP Rating:** Ingress Protection rating — how well the fixture resists dust and water. IP44 handles splashing; IP65 handles direct rain jets. For outdoor use, look for IP65 or higher.

**Battery Capacity (mAh):** Larger battery = longer runtime on cloudy days or during winter nights. 1,000–2,000 mAh is standard for path lights; 3,000+ mAh for security lights.

**Solar Panel Size:** Larger panels charge faster and perform better in partial shade. Adjustable panels that can be angled toward the sun maximize efficiency.

### Best Locations for Solar Lights

- **Full sun (6+ hours):** Any solar light will perform optimally.
- **Partial sun (3–6 hours):** Choose models with larger panels and higher-capacity batteries.
- **Deep shade or north-facing walls:** Solar lights will underperform. Consider plug-in or wired options instead.

### Season Considerations

Solar lights perform best in summer. In winter, shorter days and lower sun angles reduce panel efficiency. In northern climates, expect reduced brightness and runtime from December through February.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do solar lights work in winter?

Yes, but with reduced performance. Solar lights charge from daylight (not heat), so they work in cold climates. However, shorter winter days and lower sun angles reduce charging time and stored capacity. Lithium-ion battery models perform better in cold temperatures than NiMH alternatives.

### How long do solar outdoor lights last?

Quality solar lights last 2–5 years before the battery degrades significantly. LED elements themselves can last 25,000–50,000 hours — usually longer than the battery life. Some manufacturers sell replacement batteries, extending the overall lifespan.

### Can solar lights replace hardwired security lights?

In sun-rich locations, high-lumen solar security lights like the Aootek genuinely replace hardwired fixtures. For northern climates, heavily shaded yards, or where 100% reliability in winter is required, hardwired remains more dependable. A hybrid approach — solar primary, plug-in backup — is often the best solution.

### Do solar string lights work in rain?

Solar string lights rated IP44 or higher can handle rain. Check the IP rating before purchasing. Brightech's outdoor-rated lights are designed for all-weather outdoor use.

### How do I improve solar light performance?

Keep panels clean (dust and grime reduce efficiency by 15–25%), position panels at an angle facing south for maximum sun exposure, and periodically wipe LEDs for maximum brightness output.
