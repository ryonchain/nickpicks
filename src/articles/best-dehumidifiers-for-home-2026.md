## Buyer's Guide: What Size Dehumidifier Do You Need?

### By Pint Capacity

| Condition | Room Size | Capacity Needed |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Mildly damp (slight musty odor) | Up to 500 sq ft | 25–30 pint |
| Moderately damp | 500–1,000 sq ft | 30–40 pint |
| Very damp (visible moisture on walls) | 1,000–1,500 sq ft | 40–50 pint |
| Wet (standing water on floor) | Any size | 50+ pint + consult remediation |

**Note:** DOE updated test standards in 2020, so older "70-pint" models now sell as "50-pint." If you see an old-spec unit claiming 70+ pints, verify against new DOE test results.

### Built-In Pump vs. Gravity Drain

- **Gravity drain:** Water flows downhill through a hose. Works only if your drain point is lower than the unit.
- **Built-in pump:** Motor pushes water up and out — essential for basements below sewer lines or anywhere without a nearby floor drain.
- **Manual bucket:** Filled bucket must be emptied. Inconvenient for 24/7 operation.

### Key Features Worth Paying For

- Auto-restart after power outage (prevents forgetting to turn it back on)
- Washable filter (saves money vs. replaceable filters)
- Humidity readout + automatic shutoff at target humidity (true "set it and forget it")

## FAQ

**Where should I place a dehumidifier?**
Center of the affected room when possible, or near the moisture source (basement sump area, crawl space entry). Keep 12 inches of clearance around all sides for airflow.

**What humidity level should I set?**
EPA recommends 30–50% year-round. Most people set to 45–50% for comfort. Below 30% causes static electricity and dry skin.

**Do dehumidifiers raise room temperature?**
Slightly — by 2–4°F. The heat comes from the compressor. Running AC alongside a dehumidifier is more effective than a dehumidifier alone in summer.

**How often should I clean the filter?**
Every 2–4 weeks during heavy use. Rinse with warm water, let dry completely, then reinstall.
