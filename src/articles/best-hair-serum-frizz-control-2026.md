## Why Hair Serums Beat Standard Conditioners for Frizz

Conditioners hydrate and detangle. Hair serums go further — they coat the hair shaft with silicones, oils, or proteins that create a physical barrier against humidity, reduce friction between strands, and add concentrated shine. The best serums do multiple jobs: protect from heat, seal the cuticle, and make styling dramatically easier.

What to evaluate when choosing a serum:

- **Hair type:** Fine hair needs lightweight, non-greasy formulas (water-based serums, thin oils). Thick, coarse, or curly hair can handle heavier oils and richer creams.
- **Primary concern:** Anti-frizz, shine, heat protection, or bond repair each require different active ingredients. Most serums prioritize one or two of these.
- **Application timing:** Pre-styling serums are applied to wet hair before blow-drying. Finishing serums are applied to dry hair for gloss and flyaway control.
- **Ingredients:** Argan oil, silicones (cyclopentasiloxane, dimethicone), and keratin are the primary frizz-fighting ingredients. Olaplex's bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate is unique for bond repair.

## Application Tips for Maximum Frizz Control

**Work in sections:** For very frizzy hair, applying serum section by section ensures even distribution and prevents some areas being under-treated.

**Mid-shaft to ends only:** Roots that get serum will go flat and oily. Start at least 2–3 inches from the scalp.

**Use heat strategically:** Serum creates a thermal shield, but heat styling still does its best work in the direction of the cuticle (downward with the nozzle attachment on the dryer).

**Humidity-proof tip:** After blow-drying, seal with a light mist of flexible-hold hairspray over the serum. The combination of serum and hairspray creates a better humidity barrier than either alone.
