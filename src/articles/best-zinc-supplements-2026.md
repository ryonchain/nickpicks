## Zinc Deficiency: Are You at Risk?

**High-risk groups:**
- Vegetarians and vegans (plant phytates block zinc absorption)
- People with Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis
- Heavy alcohol users (alcohol increases zinc excretion)
- Older adults (absorption declines with age)
- Pregnant women (need is increased)

**Signs of deficiency:**
- Frequent infections / slow immune response
- Hair loss
- Poor wound healing
- Loss of taste or smell
- Skin problems including acne

**Testing:** Serum zinc is a rough indicator (normal: 70–120 mcg/dL), though it doesn't fully reflect tissue-level zinc status. Alkaline phosphatase enzyme activity is a more sensitive functional marker.

**Zinc-rich foods to pair with supplementation:**
- Oysters (highest dietary source)
- Red meat, poultry
- Pumpkin seeds, cashews
- Fortified cereals

Take zinc with food (not on an empty stomach — causes nausea) and separate from calcium supplements, which compete for absorption.
