## Fire Pit Buyer's Guide

### Choosing the Right Type

| Fire Pit Type | Best For | Avoid If |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| **Wood-burning** | Authentic ambiance, roasting marshmallows, wood-fire cooking | You hate smoke, or HOA restricts wood fires |
| **Propane** | Convenience, decks, young children, no wood storage | You want the crackling wood experience |
| **Smokeless (Solo Stove)** | Best of both worlds — wood fire with minimal smoke | Budget under $200 |
| **Tabletop** | Small spaces, ambiance only, apartment balconies | You want heat or to roast food |

### What Size Do You Need?

- **19-22 inch diameter:** Solo patio use, 2-4 people, small spaces
- **24-26 inch diameter:** The standard — works for 4-6 people
- **30-36 inch diameter:** Larger gatherings, bigger fires, more heat output
- **Tabletop (12-18 inch):** Ambiance only, not a heat source

## Fire Pit Safety Basics

- **Keep a bucket of water or sand nearby** — always
- **10-foot clearance from structures** — minimum safe distance from home, fence, or overhanging branches
- **Never leave unattended** — especially with children or pets nearby
- **Let the fire burn down completely** before adding a cover or leaving
- **Check local burn bans** before use — fire bans apply to fire pits
- **Wind matters** — light the fire with the wind at your back, not your face

A fire pit bought in summer is ready for the first cold fall evening. Order early — quality options like Solo Stove sell out when fall arrives.
