## How to Choose a Dorm Mini Fridge

**Size guide:**
- **1–2 cu ft:** Just drinks and snacks — minimal food storage
- **2.4–2.5 cu ft:** Snacks, drinks, condiments, and a few meals — ideal for one student
- **3–3.5 cu ft:** Full meal prep for one student or shared use between two

**Check your dorm's rules:** Most universities allow mini fridges but have wattage limits (typically 750W max) and specific size restrictions. Verify before ordering. Most mini fridges draw well under 200W — no issues with standard dorm policies.

**Noise matters in a dorm:** Thermoelectric fridges (Frigidaire EFMIS129) are the quietest but less efficient in warm rooms. Modern compressor fridges are quieter than older models — the Galanz and Midea are notably quiet.

**Energy costs:** Mini fridges run 24/7. At $0.13/kWh, an Energy Star 2.4 cu ft fridge costs about $25–35/year. A non-Energy Star older model can cost 2× more. Over a 4-year college career, the savings add up.

## FAQ

**Do I need a mini fridge if my dorm has a shared kitchen?**
A shared kitchen is useful but inconvenient for late-night snacking or storing daily items like milk and drinks. Most students with shared kitchen access still benefit from a personal mini fridge for daily convenience items and drinks.

**Can I share a mini fridge with my roommate?**
Yes — a 3.1–3.3 cu ft fridge can reasonably serve two students sharing a room. Designate a shelf for each person. The hOmeLabs 3.3 cu ft and Galanz 3.1 cu ft are the best options for shared use.

**How do I clean a mini fridge in a dorm?**
Unplug the fridge, remove all contents, take out shelves and door bins, and wipe the interior with a mild soap and water solution. Let it dry completely before replacing shelves. Clean at the start of each semester to prevent odors.
