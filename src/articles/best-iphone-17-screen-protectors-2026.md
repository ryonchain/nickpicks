## Anti-Glare vs. Clear: Which Should You Get?

**Clear screen protectors** maintain your display's full color accuracy and brightness — they look like nothing is there. Best for indoor and office use.

**Anti-glare (matte) screen protectors** reduce reflections and are far easier to use outdoors in sunlight, but they slightly soften the image quality and reduce brightness perception. Best for outdoor workers, students, or anyone using their phone in bright environments often.

Both Spigen and Zagg make anti-glare versions of their top picks at similar price points.

---

## What About Privacy Screen Protectors?

Privacy screen protectors limit your viewing angle so people beside you can't see your screen. Useful for commuters, business travelers, or anyone in a public environment with sensitive data. Spigen and Belkin both make privacy screen protectors for iPhone 17 at about $5–10 more than the standard versions.

The trade-off: privacy protectors are noticeably dimmer, even viewed straight-on.

---

## Do I Need a Screen Protector on iPhone 17?

The iPhone 17 uses Ceramic Shield on the front — the toughest front glass Apple makes. But "toughest" is relative. Keys in a pocket, gravel on a table, or a drop onto concrete will scratch or crack it. A screen protector adds a sacrificial layer you can replace for $10–40 instead of paying $279–$399 for an Apple screen repair.

If you have AppleCare+, your risk calculus changes — one cracked screen is $29 with AppleCare+. But screen scratches aren't covered, and a screen protector also guards against those.

**Verdict:** Get a screen protector. At $10–20, it's the cheapest insurance on a $1,000 phone.
