## Air Purifier Buying Guide

**Understanding CADR:**
CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) measures how fast a unit cleans a room. The AHAM recommendation: CADR ≥ 2/3 of room square footage.
- 500 sq ft room → need CADR ≥ 333
- 800 sq ft room → need CADR ≥ 533

**True HEPA vs. HEPA-type:**
"True HEPA" captures 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger (captures viruses, bacteria, PM2.5, allergens, dust). "HEPA-type" is a marketing term with no standardized minimum — avoid it.

**Activated carbon filters:**
Standard HEPA only captures particles — not gases, odors, or VOCs (volatile organic compounds). Activated carbon adds chemical filtration for smoke, cooking odors, and off-gassing materials.

**Noise levels:**
- Under 40 dB: near-silent (whisper)
- 40–50 dB: quiet room background noise
- 50–60 dB: white noise / fan noise
- 60+ dB: audible and intrusive

**Filter replacement costs:**
Budget $30–$80/year for filter replacements. Factor this into total cost of ownership.

## FAQ

**How long should I run an air purifier?**
For best results, run continuously on auto mode or low-medium. Running 24/7 on low uses very little power and maintains clean air. One-hour-a-day use is insufficient.

**Can an air purifier help with allergies?**
Yes — True HEPA captures pollen, pet dander, dust mites, and mold spores — the primary seasonal allergens. Significant allergy relief is one of the most well-documented benefits.

**Do air purifiers help with COVID-19 or viruses?**
True HEPA filters capture virus-sized particles. ASHRAE and EPA recommend them as a supplemental measure alongside ventilation and other precautions.

**How often do I change the filter?**
Every 6–12 months for most models, depending on air quality and run time. Follow the manufacturer's indicator; running a clogged filter defeats the purpose.
