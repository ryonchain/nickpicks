## Candy Hosting Setup Tips

**Volume calculation:** In a typical suburban neighborhood, expect 50-100 trick-or-treaters. Each needs 2-3 pieces. Buy 200-300 pieces plus buffer.

**Battery prep:** Motion-activated bowls use 3-4 AA batteries. Fresh batteries handle 4-6 hours of regular activation. Buy extras — the last thing you want is a dead candy bowl at peak trick-or-treat hours (7:00-8:30 PM).

**Weather contingency:** Keep a backup supply of candy inside. If rain keeps trick-or-treaters away, the candy stores for next year if unopened.

**Giveaway positioning:** Motion-activated bowls work best on a low table (2-3 feet high) where kids can see into the bowl and the sensor can detect their reach. Too high and kids struggle; too low and adults are constantly triggering it.
