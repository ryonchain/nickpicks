## Should You Buy on Big Deal Days or Wait for Black Friday?

**Laptop deals on Big Deal Days rival Black Friday.** Here's the honest comparison:

| Timing | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Big Deal Days (Oct) | Less competition, more stock, fast shipping | Slightly fewer competing deals |
| Black Friday (Nov) | Most deals on record, retailer competition | Stock issues, doorbuster games, shipping delays |

**Buy on Big Deal Days if:**
- A specific model you want drops 15%+ from its regular price
- You need the laptop before the holidays (gifts, school year mid-semester)
- You don't want to deal with Black Friday chaos

**Wait for Black Friday if:**
- The Big Deal Days price doesn't move significantly from regular price
- You're buying a mainstream model that stocks well

## Tips for Getting the Best Laptop Deal

1. **Know the regular price** — Use CamelCamelCamel to track Amazon price history. A "50% off" laptop is meaningless without context.
2. **Check RAM upgradability** — Budget laptops often let you add RAM. 8GB is fine now; make sure 16GB is possible later.
3. **Prioritize SSD** — Avoid HDD laptops at any price. 256GB minimum; 512GB preferred.
4. **Verify HDMI/Thunderbolt** — Know what ports you need before buying

## MacBook vs. Windows in 2026

**Buy a MacBook if:**
- You're in the Apple ecosystem (iPhone, iPad, AirDrop workflow)
- Battery life is critical (MacBook Air gets 18+ hours; most Windows laptops get 8–12)
- You want a machine that lasts 5–7 years without slowdown

**Buy Windows if:**
- You need specific Windows-only software
- You game
- You want more port variety or upgradability
- Budget under $700 (Windows has more options)
