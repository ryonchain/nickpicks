## Switch Types and What They Mean for Gaming

**Linear switches** (Red, Yellow, Silver) actuate smoothly without a tactile bump or click. They're fast, consistent, and quiet. Most competitive gamers favor linear switches for rapid key presses without accidental force modulation from the bump. Cherry MX Red, Gateron Yellow, and Razer Yellow are popular linear choices.

**Tactile switches** (Brown, Blue-tactile) provide a physical bump at the actuation point. Good for gaming because you feel when the key registers without bottoming out. Less fatiguing over long sessions for some users. Cherry MX Brown and Gateron Brown are the standards.

**Clicky switches** (Blue, Green) add an audible click at actuation. Popular for typing feel but polarizing in gaming due to noise and the slight reset behavior of clicky mechanisms. Not recommended for dormitory or shared-space gaming.

**Actuation force and travel:** Most linear gaming switches actuate at 45g at 2.0mm pre-travel. Speed/Silver switches actuate earlier (1.2mm) for faster response. High-actuation switches (65g+) reduce accidental keypresses but require more deliberate force — preferred by some strategy game players.

## Buying Guide: Choosing a Mechanical Gaming Keyboard

### Layout Prioritizes Mouse Space

For FPS games played at low sensitivity (2–4 inches per 360°), choose TKL or smaller. For strategy games, MOBAs, and MMOs where you use the numpad for abilities, full-size is worth the extra width. 60% boards suit dedicated FPS players with large mouse pads.

### Wireless Is Viable for Competitive Play

If you were avoiding wireless because of latency concerns, 2026 is the year to reconsider. SteelSeries Quantum 2.0, Logitech LIGHTSPEED, and Razer HyperSpeed all operate at latencies indistinguishable from wired in real game scenarios. The only remaining argument for wired is eliminating battery management entirely.

### Switch Choice Is Personal — Sample Before You Commit

If you've never used mechanical switches, the linear vs. tactile decision matters. Some retailers and keyboard-focused communities offer switch sample kits ($10–$20) that let you feel 20+ switches before buying. Buying a hot-swappable keyboard gives you the option to change your mind later without buying a new board.

### Polling Rate and Real Gaming Impact

1,000 Hz polling (standard) reports mouse position every 1ms. 8,000 Hz reports every 0.125ms. The real-world competitive impact is measurable in professional tournament conditions. For most recreational gamers, 1,000 Hz is more than sufficient.
