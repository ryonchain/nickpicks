## Running Shoe Buying Guide

### Gait Analysis: The Most Important Factor

Before choosing a shoe, understand your gait:

**Neutral/Supination:** Weight rolls to the outside of the foot. Often associated with high arches. Choose a cushioned neutral shoe (New Balance 1080, HOKA Clifton).

**Neutral:** No significant inward or outward roll. Most runners fall here. Most shoes work — prioritize comfort and cushioning.

**Overpronation:** Foot rolls inward excessively on landing. Often associated with flat feet. Choose a stability shoe (ASICS GT-4000, Brooks Adrenaline GTS).

### Drop: Understanding Heel-to-Toe Offset

Drop = the height difference between the heel and the forefoot in the midsole.

- **High drop (10–12mm):** Encourages heel strike. Gentler on calves and Achilles. ASICS GT-4000.
- **Mid drop (6–8mm):** Works for most gaits. New Balance 1080, HOKA Clifton.
- **Low drop (0–4mm):** Promotes forefoot/midfoot strike. More calf-demanding. Saucony Kinvara.

Never jump from 10mm to 4mm drop without a gradual transition — it significantly increases Achilles and calf injury risk.

### Sizing: Women's Running Shoe Specific Notes

- Go up 0.5 size from your street shoe size — feet swell during runs
- Make sure there's a thumb's width between your longest toe and the front of the shoe
- Width matters: New Balance offers the widest range (2A through 4E)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are the best women's running shoes for beginners?**
For beginners, the New Balance Fresh Foam X 1080v13 or HOKA Clifton 9 are the safest choices. Both offer maximum cushioning to protect joints that haven't yet adapted to running impact, with forgiving fits that reduce blistering and hotspots during the break-in period. If you know you pronate (flat feet), the ASICS GT-4000 3 is the most appropriate beginner stability shoe.

**How many miles do women's running shoes last?**
Most quality running shoes last 300–500 miles. Heavier runners, those who heel-strike heavily, and trail runners will see the lower end of this range. HOKA's firmer foam and ASICS' AHAR rubber outsole tend to last closer to 500 miles. Track mileage with a running app and inspect the midsole compression regularly — when the foam no longer bounces back, it's time to replace.

**Should women's running shoes be a size bigger?**
Yes — most running experts recommend sizing up 0.5 size from your everyday shoe size. Running feet swell during activity, and a full thumb's width of space between the longest toe and the end of the shoe prevents black toenails on long runs. Some runners with wide feet benefit from sizing up a full size.
