## 4K Projector Buying Guide

### True 4K vs. 4K Enhancement
**Native 4K:** 3840x2160 actual pixels (like Sony SXRD chips). Sharper on-screen.
**4K Enhancement/Pixel-Shift:** HD chip that shifts pixels rapidly to create 4K appearance. Epson's 5050UB uses this — genuinely excellent results at significant cost savings.

### Lumens: How Bright Do You Need?
| Room Condition | Lumens Needed |
|---------------|--------------|
| Completely dark (dedicated theater) | 1,500–2,000 |
| Dim room (blackout curtains) | 2,000–2,500 |
| Semi-dark (evening, shaded) | 2,500–3,500 |
| Living room (ambient light) | 3,500+ |

### Lamp vs. Laser Light Source
**Lamp:** 3,000–5,000 hours before replacement ($200–$400/bulb). Cheaper upfront.
**Laser:** 20,000–25,000 hours. No maintenance; higher upfront cost. Almost always the better long-term value.

### Throw Distance
Check the throw ratio before buying. Standard projectors need 10–15 feet for a 100" image. Short-throw models work at 4–6 feet. Ultra-short-throw models work under 2 feet.

## FAQ

**Q: Is a 4K projector better than a 4K TV?**
A: For screen size over 85", projectors deliver larger images at lower cost per inch. For image quality in average room lighting, OLED TVs often outperform projectors. Best scenario: TV for everyday use, projector for movies/events.

**Q: Do I need a special screen for a 4K projector?**
A: A white wall works but a proper projection screen improves brightness, contrast, and uniformity. Budget: $100–$500 for a good fixed or motorized screen.

**Q: How long do projector bulbs last?**
A: 3,000–5,000 hours for standard lamps. Run a projector 3 hours/day and a lamp lasts 3–5 years. Replacement bulbs cost $100–$400 depending on brand.

**Q: Can I use a projector in daylight?**
A: Only ultra-bright models (4,000+ lumens) or specialized ALR (Ambient Light Rejecting) screens make daytime viewing possible. Most projectors require a dark room.
