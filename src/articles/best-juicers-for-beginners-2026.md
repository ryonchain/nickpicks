## Buying Guide

### Produce Type Determines Your Machine

**Centrifugal handles well:**
- Apples, oranges, pears
- Carrots, beets, ginger
- Celery, cucumber
- Pineapple, watermelon

**Centrifugal handles poorly:**
- Kale, spinach, lettuce
- Wheatgrass
- Herbs (mint, parsley)
- Soft berries

**Masticating handles everything listed above.**

### Feed Chute Size: The #1 Time-Saver

3-inch feed chutes accept whole apples and oranges — no cutting required. 1.5-inch chutes (masticating) require pre-cutting everything. For beginners, the wide chute makes the difference between consistent daily juicing and giving up.

### Juice Quality: What Matters

**Nutrient preservation:** Cold press is better — lower RPM reduces heat and oxidation. Difference is real but modest for hard fruits/vegetables; significant for leafy greens.

**Yield:** Masticating produces 15–25% more juice from the same produce — reduces grocery cost over time.

**Taste:** Masticating juice tastes smoother; centrifugal has more foam and separation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is homemade juice healthier than store-bought?**
Yes — fresh juice preserves vitamins (especially C) that degrade in commercial processing. No added sugars or preservatives.

**How long does fresh juice last?**
Centrifugal: 24–48 hours refrigerated in an airtight container. Cold press: 48–72 hours.

**Is juicing worth it vs. eating whole fruit?**
Different benefits. Juice extracts nutrients quickly without fiber; eating whole fruit is better for satiety and gut health. Many nutritionists recommend juicing as a supplement, not a replacement.
