## How to Choose the Right Electric Scooter

**Range:** Calculate your round-trip commute distance and add 20% buffer for hills and battery degradation. A 10-mile one-way commute needs at least a 25-mile rated range.

**Weight:** Every pound matters if you carry the scooter up stairs or on transit. Under 30 lbs (Unagi, Razor) for frequent carrying. 35-46 lbs acceptable if you rarely lift it.

**Speed:** Most cities cap personal e-scooters at 15-20 mph on bike lanes and sidewalks. 22 mph is the practical maximum for most legal use cases.

**Tires:** Pneumatic (air-filled) tires absorb road vibration significantly better than solid rubber. For urban use with cracked pavement, prioritize pneumatic.

**Braking:** More powerful brakes matter more at higher speeds. Look for hydraulic or drum brakes if you'll be riding faster than 20 mph or in hilly terrain.

**Local laws:** Check your city's e-scooter regulations before buying — speed limits, registration requirements, and helmet laws vary significantly.
