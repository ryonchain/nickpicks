Variable speed pumps, now required by federal law for new inground pool installations, can cut energy costs by 80% over single-speed equivalents. Whether you're replacing a failing pump, upgrading for efficiency, or setting up an above-ground pool for the first time, here are the five best pool pumps for 2026.

## Buying Guide

**Variable Speed vs. Single Speed:** Variable speed pumps cost 2–3x more upfront but save $500–$800/year in electricity for the average pool. They typically pay for themselves in 2–3 years and are now federally required for new inground pool installations.

**Sizing Your Pump:** A pump sized for your pool's volume is critical — oversized pumps waste energy; undersized pumps can't maintain proper water quality. Match the pump's flow rate (GPM) to your filter's capacity and your pool's volume.

**Self-Priming:** Most inground pumps are self-priming. Above-ground pumps rely on gravity — the pump must sit below the water line.

**Electrical Requirements:** Most pool pumps require a dedicated 240V circuit. Confirm your electrical panel can handle the load before purchasing. Variable speed pumps often reduce required amperage compared to single-speed equivalents.

**Noise:** Variable speed pumps at low speed are dramatically quieter than single-speed pumps. If your equipment pad is near a patio or neighbor's fence, noise matters.

**Utility Rebates:** Many electric utilities offer $100–$300 rebates for installing ENERGY STAR certified variable speed pumps. Check your utility's website before purchasing.
