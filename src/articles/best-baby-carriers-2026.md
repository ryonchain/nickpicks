## Baby Carrier Buyer's Guide

### Types of Baby Carriers

| Type | Best For | Learning Curve | Age Range |
|------|----------|----------------|-----------|
| Stretchy wrap | Newborns | Medium (practice needed) | 0–6 months |
| Woven wrap | Extended use | High (technique-intensive) | All ages |
| Structured carrier | All ages | Low | 0–toddler |
| Ring sling | Quick up/down | Low-medium | 0–2 years |
| Meh dai | Middle ground | Medium | 0–toddler |

### Ergonomics: The "M" Position
The "M" position (baby's knees higher than their bottom, thighs supported by the carrier seat) is the gold standard for infant hip development. Avoid carriers where baby's legs dangle — this position can contribute to hip dysplasia.

Look for carriers with IHDI (International Hip Dysplasia Institute) certification.

### What to Consider
1. **Baby's age:** Stretchy wrap for newborns; structured carrier once neck control develops
2. **Parent's height/build:** Check waistband max and shoulder strap width
3. **Carry positions needed:** Front in is essential; front out is nice-to-have; back carry extends useful life
4. **Climate:** Mesh/cotton for hot climates; structured panel for cooler weather
5. **Learning curve willingness:** Ring slings and wraps require practice; structured carriers are immediate

## FAQ

**When can I face baby outward in a carrier?**
Most experts recommend waiting until 4–5 months when baby can hold their head steadily independently. The Ergobaby Omni 360 and Lillebaby Complete support face-out from that stage.

**How long can I carry baby at once?**
There's no strict limit — listen to your body and baby's cues. Most parents comfortably carry for 1–3 hours. Extended carries work best with proper lumbar support (waistband-style carriers).

**What's the TICKS rule for safe babywearing?**
- **T**ight — carrier should be tight enough that baby can't slump down
- **I**n view — you should see baby's face at all times
- **C**lose enough to kiss — baby's head near your chin
- **K**ept chin off chest — at least a 2-finger gap under baby's chin
- **S**upported back — baby's back supported in a natural curve

**Can I use a baby carrier for hiking?**
Yes — structured carriers work well for hiking. For longer hikes with babies over 15 lbs, consider a frame backpack-style carrier (Osprey Poco, Deuter Kid Comfort) designed specifically for hiking.
