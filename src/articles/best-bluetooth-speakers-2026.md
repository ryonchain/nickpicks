## What Makes a Great Bluetooth Speaker

Bluetooth speakers have exploded in quality over the past few years. The key metrics that separate good from great:

- **Frequency response:** A wider Hz range means the speaker can reproduce more of the audio spectrum. Speakers with bass drivers extending below 80Hz sound noticeably fuller. Most portable speakers roll off below 100Hz.
- **IPX rating:** IPX4 means splash-resistant. IPX7 means submersible to 1 meter for 30 minutes. For pool use or hiking, don't settle for anything below IPX7.
- **Battery life:** 10–12 hours is enough for most outings. 20+ hours suits all-day events or travel. Some speakers can charge other devices via USB — worth checking if you camp or travel light.
- **Portability:** Weight matters. A 1-pound speaker lives in a jacket pocket. A 3-pound speaker needs a bag. Match size to use case.
- **Codec support:** AAC and aptX improve audio quality over Bluetooth. SBC is the standard fallback. The difference is audible mostly in detailed music like acoustic or classical.

## FAQ

**Does Bluetooth audio quality matter?**
At normal listening volumes outdoors, the difference between codecs (SBC vs AAC vs aptX) is minor. Speaker driver quality, enclosure design, and tuning have far more impact on perceived sound quality than codec alone.

**Can I pair two speakers for stereo?**
Most brands support stereo pairing within their ecosystem (two JBLs, two UEs, two Ankers). Cross-brand stereo pairing isn't supported. True stereo separation only works if you position the speakers several feet apart.

**What IPX rating do I actually need?**
IPX4 (splash-proof) is fine for rain and sweat. IPX7 (submersible) is the right choice if you use it near water where submersion is possible. IP67 adds dust resistance — worth it for beach and construction environments.
