## How Much B12 Do You Need?

**RDA:** 2.4 mcg/day for adults (a very small amount — most foods and supplements provide far more)

**Typical supplement doses (1,000 mcg):** This high "megadose" compared to the RDA is because only a small percentage of orally consumed B12 is absorbed through passive diffusion — taking 1,000 mcg ensures that the absorbed fraction (~1–3%) meets daily needs.

**For confirmed deficiency:** Doctors often prescribe 1,000–2,000 mcg daily, sometimes via injection for severe cases with absorption problems.

**For vegans:** 250–500 mcg daily or 2,500 mcg weekly is commonly recommended to maintain normal serum levels.

**Testing:** If you suspect deficiency, a simple blood test for serum B12 and methylmalonic acid (MMA) confirms status. Methylmalonic acid is the more sensitive marker — elevated MMA indicates functional B12 deficiency even when serum B12 appears normal.

You cannot "overdose" on B12 — it's water-soluble, and excess is excreted. Higher doses than strictly necessary carry no known harm.
