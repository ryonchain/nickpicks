## Buying Guide: Which Band Type Is Right for You?

### Loop Bands vs. Tube Bands

**Loop bands (WODFitters, Serious Steel, Fit Simplify):**
- Better for compound movements (squats, deadlifts, pull-ups)
- Attach to barbells, power racks, poles
- More durable under heavy tension
- No handles — less comfortable for isolation work

**Tube bands with handles (Bodylastics, HPYGN):**
- Better for isolation exercises (curls, extensions, rows)
- Mimic cable machine exercises
- More comfortable to grip
- Multiple bands stack for resistance variety

### How to Build Muscle With Bands

**Progressive overload still applies.** Increase resistance (move to heavier band), increase reps, or decrease rest time over weeks. Bands that feel easy won't build muscle — you need the 8–15 rep range to feel challenging.

**Best muscle-building exercises with bands:**
- Banded squats and deadlifts (add resistance to barbell or bodyweight)
- Resistance band rows and lat pulldowns
- Banded push-ups and chest press
- Bicep curls and tricep pushdowns (tube bands)
- Glute bridges and hip thrusts (loop bands)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you really build muscle with resistance bands?**
Yes — research shows bands produce comparable muscle growth to free weights when matched for intensity. The key is progressive overload: consistently increase tension or reps over time.

**What resistance level should I start with?**
Beginners: light to medium loop bands (10–40 lbs) or the #1–#2 WODFitters band. If you can do 20+ reps easily, go heavier.

**How long do resistance bands last?**
Quality latex bands from WODFitters or Serious Steel last 1–3 years with regular use. Avoid leaving them in direct sunlight or extreme heat, which degrades latex. Always inspect for nicks before use.
