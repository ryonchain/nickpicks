## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Thickness.** 4–5mm is standard for vinyasa and active practice where you need stability for balance poses. 6mm provides more joint cushioning — good for slower practices and practitioners with knee or hip sensitivity. For restorative yoga or yin practice, go up to 8mm.

**Material.** PVC mats (Gaiam, Manduka, IUGA) are durable, easy to clean, and consistent. Natural rubber mats (Jade Harmony) are eco-friendly and grip excellently. Polyurethane top layers (Liforme) excel at sweat-wicking grip but require more careful cleaning.

**Length.** Standard yoga mats are 173cm (68"). If you're over 5'9", a 183cm (72") mat prevents you from stepping off the back edge in forward-facing poses. The Liforme Original at 185cm accommodates most heights.

**Break-in period.** Some mats require break-in before reaching full grip — the Manduka PRO most notably. If you need grip from session one, choose the Jade Harmony or Liforme Original.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How thick should a yoga mat be for home practice?**
For most home yoga, a 5–6mm mat is ideal — enough cushioning for joints without being so thick that balance poses feel unstable. The Gaiam Premium at 6mm is a good home practice thickness. If you practice mainly restorative or yin yoga, go up to 8mm.

**What is the best yoga mat for sweaty hands?**
The Liforme Original and Jade Harmony are the best options for sweaty hands. Liforme uses a polymer top layer that gets grippier as it gets wet. Jade Harmony is natural rubber, which provides reliable grip without a special coating.

**Is the Manduka PRO worth the price?**
For regular practitioners, yes. The Manduka PRO is virtually indestructible — Manduka offers a lifetime guarantee. If you practice 4–5 times per week, the per-session cost over 5–10 years is lower than replacing cheaper mats repeatedly.

**Can you use a yoga mat for other exercises?**
Yes — yoga mats work well for Pilates, stretching, bodyweight exercises, and meditation. For high-impact exercises like jumping jacks or burpees, a thicker exercise mat (10mm+) provides better shock absorption than a standard yoga mat.

**How do you clean a yoga mat?**
Wipe down with a diluted tea tree oil spray (a few drops in water) or a gentle mat cleaner after each use. Avoid fully submerging rubber mats in water. The Liforme can be spot-cleaned; the Manduka PRO can be cleaned with mild soap and water and air-dried flat.
