# Best Bathroom Faucets in 2026

Bathroom faucets get used 15–20 times daily per person. The specs that determine longevity: valve type (ceramic disc valves outlast rubber washer valves significantly), body material (solid brass doesn't corrode; zinc alloy will pit over time), and finish quality (PVD finishes outlast electroplated finishes). The hole configuration must match your existing sink.

---

## Hole Configuration Guide

**Single hole:** One hole in the sink deck. Faucet, handle(s), and supply lines come through one opening. Simplest.

**Center set (4-inch):** Three holes, 4 inches center-to-center. Faucet body bridges all three; handles on each side of spout.

**Widespread (8-inch):** Three holes, 8+ inches center-to-center. Separate faucet body and two separate handles. More spread out; more formal look.

**Wall-mount:** Faucet mounts to wall above sink. Requires in-wall plumbing roughed in.

## What to Look For (and Avoid)

**Look for:**
- Solid brass body (not zinc alloy)
- Ceramic disc valve (not rubber washer or ball)
- WaterSense certification (1.2 GPM — saves water without reducing feel)
- Lifetime warranty (indicates manufacturer confidence)

**Avoid:**
- Zinc alloy or "pot metal" construction — shows up as "zinc alloy" in specs or very low prices
- Decorative-only ceramic handles on an otherwise low-quality faucet
- Unknown brands with no stated valve type
