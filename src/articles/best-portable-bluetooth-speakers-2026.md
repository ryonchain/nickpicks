## Speaker Buying Guide

### IP Rating Explained for Speakers

| Rating | Dust | Water Protection | Recommended Use |
|--------|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| IPX4 | No | Splash from any direction | Indoor use, light rain |
| IPX5 | No | Low-pressure jet water | Poolside, light shower |
| IPX7 | No | 1m submersion for 30 min | Kayaking, pool, rain |
| IP65 | Yes | Jet-proof | Beach, dusty trail |
| IP67 | Yes | 1m submersion for 30 min | All outdoor environments |

**Recommendation:** Target IP67 for serious outdoor use. IPX7 gives you submersion protection but not dust protection — fine for water-only environments.

### Battery Life: What the Numbers Mean

Most manufacturers rate battery at 50–60% volume. Outdoors at 75–80% volume (which is typical to overcome ambient noise), expect 30–40% less runtime than stated. A 20-hour rated speaker becomes ~14 hours at outdoor volumes.

### Bluetooth Range

30ft is the spec you'll see everywhere. In real-world outdoor use with obstacles (people, trees, walls), 20–25ft is practical. Bluetooth 5.0+ improves connection stability significantly over 4.2.
