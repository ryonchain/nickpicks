## How to Choose a Portable Speaker

### Water Resistance Rating
- **IPX4**: Splash-resistant. Handles rain and accidental splashing, not submersion.
- **IPX7**: Submersible up to 1 meter for 30 minutes. Good for pool/beach.
- **IP67**: Dustproof + submersible (1m, 30 min). Best for dusty outdoor environments.
- **IP68**: Deeper submersion rating — limited options, usually overkill.

### Battery Life Reality
Marketing battery life is measured at 50–60% volume with Bluetooth only. Real-world use (higher volume, lights, powerbank use) typically gives 60–75% of the claimed figure.

### Size and Weight Trade-offs
- **Compact (Flip 7)**: 1–1.5 lbs, fits in bag pocket. Bass limited by physics.
- **Medium (Charge 6, XB33)**: 1.5–2.5 lbs, pack-able. Best all-around balance.
- **Large (Xtreme, Hyperboom)**: 4–5 lbs. Loud enough for large spaces, not backpack portable.

### Sound Profile
- **JBL**: Forward, energetic V-shape EQ. Great for pop, EDM, hip hop.
- **Sony XB**: Bass-heavy. Best for electronic and bass-heavy genres.
- **Bose**: Balanced, detailed, spacious. Best for all genres at medium volumes.
- **UE**: Punchy, loud. Best for outdoor parties.
