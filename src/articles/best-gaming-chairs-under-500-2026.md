## Gaming Chair vs. Office Chair: Which Should You Buy?

This is the honest question most gaming chair reviews avoid.

**Buy a gaming chair if:**
- You want built-in ergonomics at a price below premium office chairs ($400–500 range)
- You care about aesthetics and want your setup to look like a gaming setup
- You prioritize reclining and footrests for casual gaming sessions

**Buy an ergonomic office chair if:**
- You work 8+ hours and game 2+ hours — the cumulative sitting time justifies a true ergonomic investment
- You're experiencing back pain that a gaming chair hasn't fixed
- Budget extends to $700–1,200 (Herman Miller Aeron, Steelcase Leap)

The Herman Miller x Logitech G Embody (~$1,495) is the gold standard gaming-meets-ergonomics chair — and it's genuinely worth it if you're spending 10+ hours daily in a chair. But for most gamers under $500, the Secretlab TITAN Evo 2022 is the honest answer.

## Key Features Explained

### 4D Armrests
Adjust in four directions: height, depth, width, and pivot angle. This matters because your arms should rest naturally with your elbows at roughly 90° and your shoulders relaxed — not hunched upward or reaching.

### Lumbar Support Types
- **Pillow:** Removable, repositionable, but tends to slide and requires readjustment
- **Built-in adjustable:** Built into the chair frame. Better for consistent support, can't be lost

### Recline Range
Most gaming chairs recline 135–155°. Under 135° feels restrictive for lounging; over 155° approaches "sleeping" territory. 145° is the sweet spot for reading or casual play.

### Weight and Height Limits
Ignore weight limits as just a number — they affect how quickly foam compresses. A 280 lb person in a chair rated to 300 lbs will experience compression faster than a 180 lb person in the same chair.
