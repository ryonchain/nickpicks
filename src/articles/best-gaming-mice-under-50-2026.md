## What Makes a Gaming Mouse Different?

**High polling rate (1,000 Hz).** Regular office mice poll at 125 Hz — they report position 125 times per second. Gaming mice at 1,000 Hz report 1,000 times per second. This is the biggest functional difference, reducing input lag to ~1ms.

**Optical vs. laser.** Modern optical sensors are more accurate for gaming than laser (laser over-samples and can cause jitter on glass). Nearly all competitive mice use optical sensors.

**Switch longevity.** Rated in click cycles. 10M clicks is 3–5 years of heavy gaming; 60M clicks is effectively lifetime. Lower-quality mice use switches rated at 1–3M.

**Weight.** Lighter mice (under 80g) let you aim faster and reduce fatigue. Heavier mice (95g+) feel more stable to some users. Neither is objectively better — preference and playstyle matter.

## FAQ: Gaming Mouse Questions

**Do gaming mice improve your aim?**
The sensor and weight can measurably improve aim consistency at high-skill levels. For most casual players, the difference is minimal — fundamentals matter more than hardware.

**What DPI should I use?**
Most competitive FPS players use 400–1,600 DPI. Higher DPI doesn't mean better — it's a sensitivity setting. Use whatever feels controlled for your physical sensitivity and screen resolution.

**Is wireless gaming as responsive as wired?**
At 1,000 Hz polling rate (Logitech G305), wireless and wired are functionally identical in input lag for most users. Wireless flagship mice at 4,000 Hz are technically faster but not perceivably so.
