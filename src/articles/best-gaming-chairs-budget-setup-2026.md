## Gaming Chair Buying Guide

**Foam density is everything.** High-density cold-cure foam maintains its shape for years. Shredded or loose-fill foam compresses flat within months. If a chair doesn't specify foam type, that's a red flag.

**Match seat depth to your proportions.** Sit all the way back — there should be 2–3 finger-widths between the seat edge and the back of your knees. A seat that's too deep forces you to perch forward, eliminating any lumbar benefit.

**Armrest quality matters more than most reviews note.** 1D armrests (height only) work, but 3D or 4D armrests that adjust depth and angle dramatically reduce wrist and shoulder strain during long mouse sessions.

**Recline range:** 130° handles most gaming positions. 160°+ is worth it if you frequently lean back for cutscenes, streaming, or between sessions.
