## What to Look for in a Home Blood Pressure Monitor

**Upper arm vs wrist:** Upper arm is always more accurate. Wrist monitors work only if held at heart level — hard to do consistently. Unless you have a medical reason (obese upper arm, cast), choose upper arm.

**Clinical validation:** Look for AHA (American Heart Association) validation or inclusion in the BIHS/dabl validated device list. Most Omron and Withings models pass; many cheap no-name brands do not.

**Cuff size:** Measure your upper arm circumference. Most standard cuffs fit 9–17 inches. If your arm is larger, get a monitor with a ComFit cuff (Omron) or order a large cuff separately.

**Memory and app connectivity:** If you're monitoring for a doctor, pick a model with Bluetooth and an app that exports PDF reports. Omron Connect and Withings Health Mate both do this cleanly.

## How to Take an Accurate Reading

1. Sit quietly for 5 minutes before measuring
2. Rest your arm on a flat surface at heart height
3. Don't talk, move, or cross your legs during the reading
4. Take readings at the same time each day (morning before coffee is ideal)
5. If a reading is unusually high, wait 5 minutes and retake
