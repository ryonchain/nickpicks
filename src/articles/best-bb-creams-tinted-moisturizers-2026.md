## Buyer's Guide: BB Cream vs. CC Cream vs. Tinted Moisturizer

### What's the Difference?
- **BB Cream** (Beauty Balm): Originally Korean, typically includes SPF, skincare ingredients, and light-to-medium coverage
- **CC Cream** (Color Correcting): Color-correcting formula designed to neutralize redness and uneven tone, often with higher coverage
- **Tinted Moisturizer**: The most sheer option — primarily moisturizer with added pigment for the lightest coverage

### Which Should You Choose?
- **Want sheer, natural look:** Tinted moisturizer (NARS, Laura Mercier)
- **Need coverage for redness/spots:** BB or CC cream (IT Cosmetics)
- **Sensitive skin:** Mineral SPF tinted moisturizer (Neutrogena)
- **Budget:** Drugstore BB cream (Maybelline)

### SPF Notes
- SPF in BB creams and tinted moisturizers often doesn't provide full protection because we don't apply enough product
- Supplement with a dedicated SPF moisturizer underneath, especially on high-UV days
- Reapply SPF via setting spray or SPF powder during the day

### Shade Matching Tips
- Order 2 shades if unsure — return the mismatched one
- Photograph in natural light to see true undertone
- In doubt: go with the lighter shade and blend down your neck

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a BB cream enough coverage to wear alone?**
Depends on the formula. IT Cosmetics CC+ delivers near-foundation coverage. Laura Mercier and NARS are genuinely sheer. If you want to skip foundation, choose a higher-coverage BB/CC cream.

**Can I mix BB cream with moisturizer?**
Yes — mixing thins coverage and boosts hydration. Great for a truly skin-like finish in lower-stakes settings.

**Do tinted moisturizers replace sunscreen?**
No — unless it's an SPF 50+ mineral formula applied very generously. Use as an SPF supplement, not a replacement, especially for outdoor days.

**Can I use tinted moisturizer on acne-prone skin?**
Yes — choose non-comedogenic, fragrance-free formulas. Neutrogena and Maybelline are good starting points for acne-prone types.
