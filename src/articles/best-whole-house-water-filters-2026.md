## Buying Guide: Whole House Water Filters

### What Type of Filtration Do You Need?

**City water issues:** Chlorine, chloramines, VOCs, PFAS, disinfection byproducts → Use carbon-based filtration (iSpring WGB32B or Aquasana)

**Well water issues:**
- Sediment → Sediment pre-filter
- Iron/sulfur smell → KDF filter
- Bacteria → UV system
- Hardness → Softener or TAC conditioner

### Flow Rate (GPM)
- Small home (1–2 bathrooms): 7–10 GPM is adequate
- Medium home (2–3 bathrooms): 10–15 GPM recommended
- Large home (3+ bathrooms): 15+ GPM to prevent pressure drops

### Filter Lifespan and Ongoing Costs
A cheap filter with expensive cartridges costs more long-term than a premium system with efficient filtration. Calculate cost per 1,000 gallons when comparing systems.

### Professional vs. DIY Installation
Most systems can be DIY-installed if you're comfortable with basic plumbing. Expect 2–4 hours and basic plumbing tools (pipe cutter, teflon tape, adjustable wrenches). Complex multi-stage systems benefit from professional installation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a whole house water filter worth it?**
For homes on city water, it extends appliance lifespan (water heaters, dishwashers, washing machines), improves shower water quality, and eliminates the need for under-sink or countertop filters. For well water users, it's essential for safety and quality.

**Does a whole house filter replace a water softener?**
Standard filtration doesn't soften water (remove hardness). You need a water softener or salt-free conditioner specifically for hardness control.

**How often do I need to replace whole house filters?**
Most sediment pre-filters: every 3–6 months. Carbon filters: every 6–12 months. UV lamps: annually. High-capacity systems (like Aquasana EQ-1000): every 10 years.
