## Buying Guide: What to Look for in Bamboo Sheets and Cooling Pillowcases

### Bamboo Fabric Types: Not All Bamboo Is the Same
The marketing term "bamboo" covers several distinct manufacturing processes with different results:

- **Bamboo viscose/rayon**: Most common and most affordable. Soft and breathable, but the chemical process uses solvents (though it's still more sustainable than polyester). Most bamboo sheets on Amazon fall here.
- **Bamboo lyocell**: Closed-loop process that recaptures and reuses chemicals. More eco-friendly and often produces a lighter, more breathable fabric. More expensive.
- **Bamboo linen**: Rare and durable, slightly rougher feel — similar to traditional linen.
- **Bamboo blends**: Paired with cotton, microfiber, or TENCEL. Budget-friendlier but dilutes the cooling and softness benefits.

### Thread Count: A Misleading Metric for Bamboo
Unlike cotton where thread count correlates with quality, bamboo fibers are naturally longer and finer. A 300 TC bamboo sheet can feel smoother than a 600 TC cotton sheet. Focus on fabric type and weave (sateen for silky softness; percale for crisp breathability) rather than thread count numbers.

### Weave: Sateen vs. Percale
- **Sateen**: 4-over-1-under weave, silky and lustrous, slightly warmer than percale
- **Percale**: 1-over-1-under weave, matte and crisp, lightest and most breathable

For maximum cooling, percale is the better weave choice.

### Certifications to Trust
Look for **OEKO-TEX Standard 100** certification — this independently verifies the finished fabric is free from harmful chemicals. GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) applies to organic cotton blends. **B Corp** certification (Ettitude) indicates broader environmental commitments.

### Care Instructions
Bamboo sheets are more delicate than cotton. Most need cold or warm water (not hot), a gentle cycle, and low-heat tumble drying. Avoid fabric softeners — they coat bamboo fibers and reduce their natural moisture-wicking properties over time.
