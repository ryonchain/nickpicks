## Green Screen Setup Guide

### Lighting Your Green Screen Properly

Even, consistent lighting is more important than which green screen you buy. Uneven lighting creates shadows that produce different shades of green across the backdrop — and chroma key software removes a specific shade, leaving patches of darker or lighter green visible in the key.

**Two-light setup for green screen:**
1. One light positioned to the left of the green screen at 45°, approximately 4–5 feet from the screen
2. One light positioned to the right of the green screen at 45°, approximately 4–5 feet from the screen

Both lights should be the same color temperature (daylight 5600K recommended) and equal brightness. The goal is an evenly lit green surface with no shadows visible from your camera angle.

**Key light for subject:** A separate key light aimed at the subject (you) at a higher power level than the screen lights. Your key light and screen lights should be the same color temperature to avoid mixed-color spill onto your shoulders and hair.

### OBS Chroma Key Settings

In OBS, add the green screen as a video source, then right-click → Filters → Add → Chroma Key. Starting settings:

| Setting | Starting Value |
|---------|--------------|
| Key Color Type | Green |
| Similarity | 400 |
| Smoothness | 80 |
| Key Color Spill Reduction | 100 |
| Opacity | 100 |

Adjust Similarity up if background isn't fully removed; adjust down if subject edges show transparency. Smoothness controls edge feathering — higher values create softer, more natural edges.

### Distance from Green Screen

Stand at least 3 feet (1 meter) from the green screen. Closer positioning creates green color spill — the green light reflects from the screen onto your clothing, skin, and hair, making those areas partially transparent in the key. More distance reduces spill dramatically.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does green screen work with webcams or do I need a better camera?**
Green screens work with webcams, but camera quality affects key quality. A better camera with a larger sensor, lower ISO noise, and manual exposure control produces cleaner chroma keys because there's less digital noise in the image that can interfere with color detection. A basic ring light ($60) improves green screen keying more than a camera upgrade in most cases.

**Can I use a virtual background in Zoom without a green screen?**
Yes — Zoom's AI background removal works without a green screen on most modern computers. The quality is noticeably lower than a physical green screen: hair edges are rough, movement creates ghosting, and complex backgrounds cause the key to flicker. For occasional calls, virtual background AI is acceptable. For regular streaming or professional video calls, a physical green screen is worth the investment.

**What software beyond OBS works with green screens?**
Streamlabs OBS, XSplit, vMix, Wirecast, and DaVinci Resolve all support chroma key natively. Zoom, Teams, and Meet support physical green screens via their virtual background + green screen detection feature (enable "I have a green screen" in settings for much better key quality than AI-only removal).
