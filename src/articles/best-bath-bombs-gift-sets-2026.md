## Bath Bomb Buying Guide

### Key Quality Indicators
1. **Weight per bomb:** Quality bombs weigh 4.5–7 oz. Anything under 3 oz is underwhelming
2. **Fizz duration:** 90 seconds to 3 minutes is ideal. Under 60 seconds = low citric acid ratio
3. **Skin feel after:** Good bath bombs leave skin noticeably softer. If your skin feels dry, the formula is cheap
4. **Fragrance type:** Essential oil fragrances cost more but are more nuanced and skin-friendly than synthetic

### What to Avoid
- Sets with no ingredient list (potential allergens)
- "200-pack" bulk sets under $20 — these are tiny and low quality
- Bombs with colorants that stain the tub (test new brands in small quantities first)

### How to Store Bath Bombs
Keep bath bombs in a cool, dry place away from humidity. The citric acid and baking soda react when they contact moisture — a bathroom cabinet can prematurely activate them. Airtight jars or sealed bags extend shelf life to 12–18 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do bath bombs actually soften skin?**
Quality bath bombs with added oils (coconut, almond, argan, shea) do measurably soften skin. The fizz reaction disperses these oils throughout the water and they deposit onto skin. Cheap bombs without oils are mostly just fragrance and color — a more luxurious experience than plain water, but minimal skin benefit.

**Are bath bombs safe for sensitive skin?**
Look for fragrance-free or essential oil-scented options. Avoid bombs with artificial colorants if you have color allergies. Urb Apothecary and other natural brands formulate specifically for sensitive skin with minimal synthetic ingredients.

**How often can I use bath bombs?**
Daily use is fine for most people. If you have sensitive skin or are prone to yeast infections, limit to 2–3 times per week — the fragrances and oils can occasionally disrupt vaginal pH balance.
