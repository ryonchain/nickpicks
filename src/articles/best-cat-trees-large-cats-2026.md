## What Makes a Cat Tree Safe for Large Cats

### Base Width

Wider bases prevent tipping. For large cats, look for bases at least 16"×16". Standard trees start at 12"×12" — insufficient for cats that jump to upper levels with force.

### Post Diameter

3-inch posts are standard. 4-inch posts significantly reduce wobble for large cats. Always check the post diameter spec, not just the height.

### Platform Size

Large cats need platforms at least 12" in diameter to rest comfortably. 14–16" platforms allow full-body stretching.

### Weight Rating

Check weight capacity per platform, not total tree weight. A tree rated "50 lbs total" across 5 platforms means 10 lbs per platform — insufficient for a 20-lb Maine Coon.

**Do large cats use cat trees?**
Yes — large cats especially benefit from vertical space. Maine Coons are known to be highly active climbers.

**How do I stop a cat tree from wobbling?**
Wider base + thicker post diameter. If your tree still wobbles, anchor it to the wall with a furniture strap (most modern cat trees include mounting hardware).
