## Oscillating vs. Sonic: Which Technology Is Better?

**Oscillating (Oral-B):** Small round brush head spins and counter-rotates, "sweeping" plaque off each tooth individually. Dentists often prefer the guided per-tooth technique it encourages.

**Sonic (Sonicare):** Brush vibrates at 31,000+ strokes/minute, creating hydrodynamic fluid turbulence that reaches between teeth. Better for gum line cleaning according to some studies.

**Bottom line:** Both clean significantly better than manual brushing. Oral-B tends to win for plaque removal per tooth; Sonicare tends to win for gum and interdental cleaning.

## What Features Actually Matter

**Pressure sensor (essential):** Prevents gum recession from over-brushing. Over 40% of adults brush too hard. Look for this in any electric toothbrush purchase.

**2-minute timer + quadrant pacer (essential):** Research shows most people brush 45-70 seconds without a timer. The pacer ensures equal time per quadrant.

**Multiple modes (nice, not essential):** Studies show little difference in cleaning outcome between modes. Useful for sensitive users (gentler modes) or whitening focus.

**App connectivity (optional):** Genuinely useful for tracking brushing coverage and pressure. Makes the most difference for people who've never trained good technique.

**Round vs. elongated head:** Round (Oral-B) guides per-tooth technique. Elongated (Sonicare) covers more surface per stroke. Personal preference after the learning curve.

## FAQ

**Q: Is electric better than manual?**
A: Consistently yes — studies show 21% more plaque removal. But technique matters. A well-used manual beats a poorly used electric.

**Q: How long do electric toothbrushes last?**
A: 3-5 years for the body; replace heads every 3 months.

**Q: Should kids use electric toothbrushes?**
A: Yes, from age 3+ with supervision. Oral-B and Sonicare both make children's versions with smaller heads and gentler pressure.

**Q: What's the right brushing technique with an electric?**
A: Guide the brush slowly from tooth to tooth. Let the vibration/oscillation do the work. Don't scrub — the machine handles that.

**Q: Is whitening mode worth it?**
A: Whitening modes are slightly higher frequency — they help with surface stains (coffee, tea) but don't change tooth color. For whitening, use whitening toothpaste + consistent brushing. Professional whitening is more effective for significant color change.
