## Home Theater Projector Buying Guide

### Choosing Your Screen Size

A projector's throw ratio determines how far back it needs to sit for a given screen size. Use this guide:

| Screen Size | Standard Throw Distance | UST Distance |
|------------|------------------------|--------------|
| 80 inches | 8–10 feet | 8–12 inches |
| 100 inches | 10–13 feet | 10–16 inches |
| 120 inches | 12–16 feet | 12–20 inches |

### How Many Lumens Do You Actually Need?

| Room Condition | Lumens Needed |
|---------------|---------------|
| Completely dark (basement/dedicated theater) | 1,500–2,000 |
| Mostly dark (room-darkening shades) | 2,000–3,000 |
| Mixed lighting (partial shade) | 3,000–4,000 |
| Daylight/living room ambient | 4,000+ |

A UST projector in a living room needs 4,000+ lumens. A dedicated home theater needs 1,500–2,000 and benefits more from deep contrast than raw brightness.

### Lamp vs. Laser Light Source

**Lamp projectors** (Epson 5050UB, BenQ HT3550i):
- Lower purchase price
- Lamp replacement every 4,000–6,000 hours (~$200–$300)
- Color accuracy can shift as lamp ages
- Often produce deeper blacks (better contrast for cinephiles)

**Laser projectors** (LG HU810P, Samsung LSP9T):
- Higher purchase price
- 20,000+ hour lifespan (no replacement)
- Instant-on/instant-off (no warm-up time)
- Consistent color over lifespan

### Throw Type Comparison

**Long-throw (standard):** Ceiling mount or rear shelf placement, 10–15 feet back. Best image quality per dollar. Requires room planning.

**Short-throw:** 4–8 feet from wall. Good compromise between placement flexibility and cost.

**Ultra-short throw (UST):** 4–12 inches from wall. Sits below the screen like a soundbar. Most placement-flexible but highest cost and most sensitive to wall imperfections.

## FAQs

**Is a 4K projector better than a 4K TV for home theater?**
For screen sizes above 100 inches, a projector is both cheaper and produces a more cinematic experience. Below 85 inches, a 4K TV typically has better brightness, contrast, and HDR performance than a projector at the same price. The crossover point is roughly 85–100 inches.

**Do I need a special projector screen?**
For the best results, yes. A dedicated ALR (Ambient Light Rejecting) screen dramatically improves contrast and color when there's ambient light. In a dark room, a white-painted wall or basic white screen works fine for casual viewing.

**Can projectors be used during the day?**
Standard projectors (under 3,000 lumens) require a darkened room. UST projectors at 4,000+ lumens can compete with moderate ambient light. Dedicated outdoor/daylight projectors at 6,000+ lumens exist but are expensive and less common for home use.

**How long do projector lamps last?**
Lamp-based projectors typically rate lamps at 4,000–6,000 hours in normal mode, 10,000–15,000 hours in eco mode. At 3 hours of viewing per night, that's 3–6 years before lamp replacement. Replacement lamps cost $100–$300.
