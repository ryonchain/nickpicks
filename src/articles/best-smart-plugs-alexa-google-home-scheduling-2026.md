# Best Smart Plugs for Alexa, Google Home & Energy Monitoring (2026)

Smart plugs are one of the highest-value smart home investments you can make. They transform any dumb outlet into a remotely controlled, schedulable, voice-activated power switch — for lamps, fans, space heaters, coffee makers, or anything else with a plug. And unlike replacing light switches (which requires an electrician), you just plug them in.

The best smart plugs cost under $20 each and pay for themselves in convenience within weeks. Here's what separates a reliable smart plug from one that disconnects at 2am, and three picks worth installing.

## What to Look for in a Smart Plug

- **Voice assistant compatibility:** Confirm compatibility with your specific assistant (Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit, or Samsung SmartThings). Some plugs support all four; others are locked to one ecosystem. Matter-certified plugs work across all major platforms through the Matter standard.
- **Scheduling and automation features:** A basic on/off schedule is the minimum. Better plugs support sunrise/sunset-relative scheduling, random vacation mode (to simulate occupancy), and integration with automation routines in their respective apps.
- **Energy monitoring:** Not all smart plugs track power draw. Plugs with energy monitoring (measured in kWh) let you see which devices cost the most to run, identify devices left on accidentally, and verify that "standby" appliances aren't drawing significant phantom load.
- **Form factor and outlet blocking:** Many smart plugs block the second outlet on a duplex receptacle. Slimmer plugs that offset downward avoid this. Check dimensions if you're using multiple plugs in the same outlet strip or panel.

### Energy Monitoring: Who Needs It?

Energy monitoring smart plugs are worth the slight premium if you have: space heaters (often the biggest energy hogs), window AC units, dehumidifiers, wine fridges, or any device you're curious about. They're also useful for confirming that a "smart" appliance isn't drawing significant phantom load in standby mode — many older TVs and desktop computers do.

For basic lamps, phone chargers, and coffee makers, non-monitoring plugs are sufficient.

### Scheduling Best Practices

- **Coffee maker:** Set to turn on 5 minutes before you wake up — hot coffee without touching a button.
- **Space heaters:** Schedule off at bedtime and on 30 minutes before you wake up. A space heater left on overnight is a safety and cost risk; smart scheduling mitigates both.
- **Lamps:** Sunset-relative scheduling keeps your home lit automatically as seasons change.
- **Vacation mode:** Random on/off scheduling on a few lamps creates occupancy cues that deter opportunistic break-ins — free security upgrade included.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do smart plugs work without Wi-Fi?**
Most cloud-dependent smart plugs (Amazon Smart Plug, basic Tapo models) require internet connectivity for remote control and voice commands. Matter-certified plugs (Kasa EP25 with Matter) support local control through a Thread Border Router or Apple HomePod/Google Nest Hub — they'll respond to local automations even if your internet is down.

**Can smart plugs control high-wattage appliances like heaters?**
Yes, if the plug is rated for the wattage. Most smart plugs are rated 15A/1800W, which handles most space heaters (up to 1500W). Confirm the wattage of your appliance and the plug's amp rating before using with high-draw devices.

**Is it safe to leave a smart plug on 24/7?**
Yes, reputable smart plugs from TP-Link, Amazon, and similar brands are designed for continuous use with appropriate safety certifications (UL listed). Avoid no-brand imports with no certification markings.
