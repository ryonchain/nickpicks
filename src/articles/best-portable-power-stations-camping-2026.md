## What Makes a Portable Power Station Worth Buying

Four specs determine whether a power station meets your actual power needs:

- **Capacity (Watt-Hours):** The total energy stored. A 500Wh station runs a 60W laptop for ~7 hours, or keeps a CPAP machine running all night. For refrigerator backup during outages, look for 1,000Wh+.
- **Continuous AC Output (Watts):** The maximum wattage the AC outlets can deliver continuously. Most home appliances list power draw in watts — your power station's AC output must exceed whatever you plan to run.
- **Recharge Speed:** A power station that takes 14 hours to recharge from wall power is impractical. Look for under 2 hours for emergency use; newer LFP (LiFePO4) models recharge in 1–2 hours.
- **Battery Chemistry:** LFP (LiFePO4) batteries last 3,000–10,000 charge cycles vs. 500–800 for lithium-ion NCM. If you're buying for long-term use, LFP is worth the premium.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### LFP vs. NCM Battery Chemistry
LiFePO4 (LFP) batteries last 3,000–10,000 cycles and are safer (no thermal runaway). NCM batteries are more energy-dense (smaller/lighter for same Wh) but degrade faster. If you're buying for 5+ years of regular use, pay the LFP premium — the Anker 535 and EcoFlow DELTA 2 both use LFP at accessible prices.

### Solar Charging Compatibility
Most power stations accept solar input, but the MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking) controller efficiency varies. Higher MPPT wattage acceptance (500W on DELTA 2, 700W on AC200P) means faster solar charging. Pair with panels rated within the voltage and wattage specs — exceeding these voids warranties.

### AC Output vs. Surge Capacity
Appliances with motors (refrigerators, air compressors, power tools) draw 2–3× their rated wattage at startup — this is surge current. A 2,000W rated power station with a 4,800W surge handles refrigerator startup safely; a 500W unit with 1,000W surge does not.

### Weight and Portability
For backpacking: under 10 lbs. Car camping: 10–30 lbs is manageable. Overlanding/van life: 30–60 lbs is acceptable with wheels. The EcoFlow DELTA 2 Pro has a telescoping handle and wheels — worth it above 40 lbs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I run a refrigerator on a portable power station?**
A full-size refrigerator draws 100–400W continuously and surges to 800–1,200W at compressor startup. You need at least 2,000W AC output and 2,000Wh+ capacity. The Bluetti AC200P handles this; smaller units cannot.

**How long will a power station last during an outage?**
Divide capacity (Wh) by your device's power draw (W). A 1,000Wh station runs a 100W fan for 10 hours, a 60W laptop for ~16 hours, or a 150W mini-fridge for ~6 hours. Use the EcoFlow app's "Power Usage Calculator" for multi-device scenarios.

**Can I leave a power station plugged in all the time for home backup?**
Most modern units (EcoFlow, Bluetti, Jackery) support "UPS mode" or always-on AC passthrough where the unit stays charged while providing backup power during outages. Check the specific model's manual — not all support this.

**Are portable power stations allowed on airplanes?**
FAA limits lithium batteries to 100Wh per unit without airline approval, and 160Wh with approval. Most of the power stations on this list (300Wh+) are not TSA-approved for carry-on. Treat them as checked baggage or ship separately.
