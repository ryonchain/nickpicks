## How to Interpret Sleep Data

### Sleep Stages Explained
- **Light Sleep (N1/N2):** Your body temperature drops and heart rate slows. Memory consolidation begins. Healthy adults spend ~55% of sleep here.
- **Deep Sleep (N3/Slow Wave):** The most restorative phase — tissue repair, immune function, growth hormone release. Adults need 1–2 hours nightly. Decreases with age.
- **REM Sleep:** When most dreaming occurs. Critical for emotional regulation, creativity, and learning. Increases in the second half of the night.

### What Makes a Good Night's Sleep?
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Total sleep time | 7–9 hours |
| Sleep efficiency | >85% (time asleep / time in bed) |
| Deep sleep | 13–23% of total sleep |
| REM sleep | 20–25% of total sleep |
| Sleep onset | <20 minutes |
| HRV | Higher = better recovery (individual baseline matters more than absolute number) |

### What Can Lower Your Sleep Score?
- Alcohol (reduces REM sleep by up to 24%)
- Late caffeine (adenosine blockade delays sleep onset)
- Blue light exposure in the last 2 hours before bed
- Elevated resting heart rate (may indicate incoming illness)
- High overnight skin temperature (room too warm, or infection brewing)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are sleep trackers medically accurate?**
Consumer sleep trackers are not medical devices and cannot diagnose sleep disorders. However, the Oura Ring 4 and WHOOP 4.0 have been validated in peer-reviewed studies to be within 85–95% agreement with PSG (polysomnography) for sleep stage detection — which is impressive for a consumer product.

**Can a sleep tracker help with insomnia?**
Sleep trackers can make patterns visible — spotting that you consistently get less deep sleep on nights with wine, for example. But "orthosomnia" (anxiety about perfect sleep scores) can worsen insomnia. If you have clinical insomnia, a therapist specializing in CBT-I is more effective than any tracker.

**Which tracker works best for shift workers?**
The Garmin Venu 3 with nap detection and the Amazon Halo Rise (no wearable discomfort) are most accommodating for irregular schedules. WHOOP's 24/7 continuous monitoring also handles irregular sleep patterns well.

**Is the Oura Ring subscription worth it?**
At $5.99/month, the membership is almost mandatory — without it, you're paying $349 for a device that shows minimal data. Over a year, total cost is ~$421. Vs. WHOOP at ~$360/year including hardware. Oura makes more sense if you prioritize ring form factor and sleep accuracy over athletic tracking.
