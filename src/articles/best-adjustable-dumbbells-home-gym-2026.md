## Buying Guide

### Weight Range: Match to Your Fitness Level

| Fitness Level | Recommended Range |
|--------------|------------------|
| Beginner | 5–30 lbs |
| Intermediate | 5–52.5 lbs |
| Advanced | 5–70+ lbs |

### Mechanism Types Compared

**Dial (Bowflex):** Fastest adjustment, most beginner-friendly. Plastic parts are the weak point.

**Pin selector (PowerBlock):** Very fast, more durable. Compact square shape.

**Quick-lock screw (Ironmaster):** Slowest to adjust, most durable. Best for heavy use.

**Motorized (JAXJOX):** Coolest technology, requires app and battery charge.

### Common Mistake: Buying Too Light

Most beginners underestimate how quickly they'll progress. A 30-lb max dumbbell feels adequate for 3 months — then you've outgrown it. Buy at least 52.5 lbs per dumbbell to have 2–3 years of progression room.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are adjustable dumbbells as good as fixed?**
For home use, yes. The slight inconvenience of changing weight (3–5 seconds) is trivial vs. the space savings.

**Can I use adjustable dumbbells for all exercises?**
Most exercises work perfectly. Some awkwardness with exercises like hammer curls (especially PowerBlocks) and floor presses (Bowflex is long at max weight).

**Do they wear out?**
Quality sets last 10+ years with normal home use. Avoid dropping them — most adjustable systems are not drop-proof.
