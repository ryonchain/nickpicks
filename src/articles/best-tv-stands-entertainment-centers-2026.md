## TV Stand Sizing Guide

Before buying, measure your TV width and match to the stand:

| TV Size | TV Width | Minimum Stand Width |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| 55" | ~49" | 52" |
| 65" | ~57" | 60" |
| 75" | ~66" | 70" |
| 85" | ~75" | 78" |

*Rule of thumb: Stand should be at least 3 inches wider than the TV on each side.*

## TV Stand Features Checklist

- **Width:** Stand should extend 3"+ beyond TV edges on each side
- **Weight capacity:** Most TVs weigh 40–100 lbs. Ensure the top surface is rated adequately
- **Component shelves:** Each major component (console, cable box, Blu-ray, soundbar) needs a shelf with at least 4" of clearance
- **Cable management:** Look for rear cable channels or pass-through holes — exposed cables look messy
- **Ventilation:** Electronics need airflow; open-back designs are better than enclosed
- **TV mounting:** Some stands include a wall-mount bracket for added stability

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I wall-mount my TV or use a stand?**
Wall mounting saves space and looks cleaner, but requires stud anchoring and hides cable management. A stand is easier to set up and reposition. For large TVs in permanent setups, wall mounting is preferred. For renters or flexible setups, a stand is more practical.

**What height should my TV stand be?**
Center of your TV screen should be at eye level when seated — typically 42–48 inches from the floor. Most TV stands are 20–24" tall. With a 40" TV, the center would be at 40" (24" stand + 8" center of 40" TV).

**How much weight can TV stands hold?**
Budget stands: 100–150 lbs on the top surface. Mid-range stands: 150–200 lbs. Premium stands: 200+ lbs. Modern flat-panel TVs are much lighter than older models — a 75" TV typically weighs 70–90 lbs.

**What's the best TV stand for a gaming setup?**
Look for stands with multiple shelves (PS5, Xbox, controllers, games), cable management, and enough width for 65"+ TVs. The Walker Edison open-shelf design works well because the open structure makes it easy to access and swap components.
