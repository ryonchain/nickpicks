# Best Smart Thermostats for Renters (2026)

**Bottom line:** The **Google Nest Thermostat** (~$130) is the best smart thermostat for most renters — no C-wire required on most systems, reversible installation, and full remote control via the app. For electric baseboard heat (common in apartments), the **Mysa** is the only viable smart option.

Renters face a specific problem with smart thermostats: you need to install one, keep your landlord happy, and be able to uninstall it completely when you move out. That eliminates options requiring complex rewiring, permanent wall damage, or systems-level changes your landlord won't approve.

The good news: most smart thermostats install in 30 minutes with a screwdriver, leave no damage behind, and restore the original thermostat identically when you leave. The key variables: whether your system has a C-wire (common wire), and what type of HVAC system you're heating and cooling with.

---

## The C-Wire Problem Explained

Smart thermostats need continuous power to run Wi-Fi radios and displays. Older thermostats used battery power or drew from heating/cooling signals. Smart thermostats need a dedicated "C-wire" (common wire) for continuous power — or a workaround.

**Do you have a C-wire?** Check your current thermostat's wiring terminal. A wire connected to the "C" terminal means you're C-wire compatible with any smart thermostat. No "C" terminal wired? You need a thermostat with a built-in power adapter or compatibility kit.

**Quick check:** If your current thermostat has a digital display and works without batteries, you likely have a C-wire.

## Renter Installation Checklist

Before buying, confirm:
1. **HVAC type:** Central forced-air (heat/AC)? Electric baseboard? Heat pump? This determines compatibility.
2. **Current thermostat wire count:** 2-wire systems (heat only) may need additional compatibility steps.
3. **C-wire status:** Check your existing thermostat terminal for a "C" wire.
4. **Landlord notice:** In most cases not legally required for reversible installations, but worth an email for goodwill.

When you move out: save the original thermostat and wiring photo (take it before you disconnect anything). Reinstallation takes 10 minutes.
