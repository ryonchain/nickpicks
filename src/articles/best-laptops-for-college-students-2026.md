## How to Choose the Right College Laptop

**Step 1: Determine your major's software requirements.**
Some fields have hard requirements:
- **Engineering/CS:** Many simulation and development tools require Windows. Check department requirements before choosing Mac.
- **Architecture:** AutoCAD, Revit, and Rhino are Windows-only. MacBook Pro with Parallels is possible but expensive.
- **Film/Media/Design:** Final Cut Pro and Logic Pro are Mac-only. Adobe tools run on both.
- **Business/Humanities:** Full platform flexibility — choose based on preference.

**Step 2: Pick your battery priority.**
A laptop that dies in a 3-hour lecture is a liability. If you live in libraries and lecture halls, prioritize battery over specs.

**Step 3: Budget honestly.**
A $600 laptop that lasts 4 years beats a $400 laptop that needs replacing in 2. Buy the best you can afford — the cost-per-month calculation usually justifies spending more upfront.

**Step 4: RAM and storage minimums.**
- **RAM:** 16GB is the minimum for comfortable multitasking in 2026. 8GB will slow you down by Year 3.
- **Storage:** 512GB is the practical minimum. Photographers and video editors need 1TB.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**MacBook or Windows for college?**
For most students with no software constraints: MacBook Air M3. The battery, reliability, and build quality justify the price premium over 4 years. For students with required Windows software: Dell XPS 13 or Lenovo IdeaPad depending on budget.

**Is 8GB RAM enough for college?**
In 2026, 8GB is marginal. It handles basic coursework, but running multiple browser tabs, a PDF reader, a video call, and a research app simultaneously will show lag. Spend the upgrade cost if you can.

**Do I need a gaming laptop for college?**
No. Gaming laptops are heavy, loud, hot, and have short battery life — all bad traits for academic use. Buy a gaming PC for the dorm room and a proper ultrabook for class.

**Should I buy AppleCare+ for a MacBook?**
Yes for college students. Accidental damage coverage matters when a laptop might get water spilled on it, dropped, or left in a hot car.
