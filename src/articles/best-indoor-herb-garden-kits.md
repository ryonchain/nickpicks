## Indoor Herb Garden Buyer's Guide

### Hydroponics vs. Soil-Based
Hydroponic kits (AeroGarden, iDOO) grow plants faster — typically 2–5× faster than soil — because roots access nutrients directly in water. Soil-based systems (Click & Grow) are simpler to maintain but grow at a more natural pace.

### What Herbs Grow Best Indoors?
- **Easy:** Basil, mint, chives, parsley, cilantro
- **Medium:** Thyme, oregano, rosemary, dill
- **Harder:** Lavender (needs more light), large fruiting plants

### Light Requirements
Most indoor herb systems include LEDs that run on an 8–16 hour automated schedule. If you're near a bright south-facing window (6+ hours direct sun), you can supplement with natural light.

### Ongoing Costs
Factor in pod/seed costs: AeroGarden pods are ~$5–7 each; Click & Grow pods are ~$6–8 each. You'll replant every 3–6 months per pod.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which indoor herb garden is easiest to maintain?**
The **Click & Grow Smart Garden 9** is easiest — just add water every 1–2 weeks, no nutrient solution needed.

**How long does it take to grow herbs indoors?**
With hydroponic kits like AeroGarden, basil sprouts in 7–14 days and is ready to harvest in 4–6 weeks. Soil-based systems take 6–10 weeks.

**Can you use your own seeds with these kits?**
AeroGarden sells "Grow Anything" pods for your own seeds. Click & Grow offers grow-anything pods too. Some users find custom seeding more cost-effective long-term.

**Do indoor herb gardens need sunlight?**
The kits on this list include built-in LED lights, so no sunlight is required. They work in any room — cabinet, closet corner, or north-facing windowsill.

**How often do I need to add nutrients?**
AeroGarden-style systems require adding liquid nutrients every 1–2 weeks. Click & Grow's Smart Soil doesn't require separate nutrients — everything is in the pod.
