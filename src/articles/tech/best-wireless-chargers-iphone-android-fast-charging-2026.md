## Buying Guide

### Qi vs. Qi2 vs. MagSafe: What's the Difference?

- **Qi:** Universal wireless standard. Max 7.5W for iPhone, 10W for Samsung, 5W for everything else. No magnets.
- **Qi2:** Updated standard (2024+) with magnetic alignment ring (same magnets as MagSafe). 15W output for compatible phones. Works on iPhone 12+ and certified Android phones (Pixel 9 series, Samsung Galaxy S24+).
- **MagSafe:** Apple's proprietary magnetic charging. 15W for iPhone 12+. Qi2 chargers now match MagSafe speeds on iPhone.

**Bottom line:** For iPhones, any Qi2 charger now delivers full 15W charging — you no longer need to pay for Apple-branded MagSafe pads.

### Does Wireless Charging Work Through Cases?

Yes, through most cases up to 4mm thick. MagSafe/Qi2 magnetic cases align with the charging coil automatically. Thick battery cases, metal cases, and wallets with cards block wireless charging entirely.

### What Power Adapter Do I Need?

- Single-device pads (10W): 18W USB-C adapter is sufficient.
- Fast wireless (15W): 30W USB-C adapter recommended.
- 3-in-1 stations: 30W–65W adapter depending on brand. Check what's included or specified.

Never use the old 5W Apple brick — it limits output and charges slowly.

### Does Wireless Charging Damage Phone Batteries?

No more than wired charging at equivalent speeds. Heat is the main concern — some pads get warm during extended overnight sessions. Pads with active cooling (ESR CryoBoost) or certified temperature management (Belkin, Anker) run cooler. Avoid cheap unbranded pads, which may lack temperature protection.

## FAQ

### What's the fastest wireless charging speed available?

15W is the practical maximum for most flagship phones in 2026 via Qi2 or MagSafe. Some Android phones support proprietary fast wireless at 45W+ (Samsung, OnePlus) — these require brand-specific chargers, not generic Qi2 pads.

### Can I use a MagSafe charger on a non-MagSafe case?

Yes — the iPhone will charge at 5–7.5W without the magnetic alignment. To get full 15W, you need a MagSafe-compatible case (Apple or MFi-certified).

### Do wireless chargers work for Samsung Galaxy phones?

Yes. Samsung Galaxy S and A series phones support Qi wireless charging at 10–15W. Qi2-certified Galaxy phones (S24+) also support magnetic alignment on Qi2 pads.

### Is a multi-device pad worth it over separate chargers?

If you have 2+ devices to charge simultaneously (phone + earbuds, or phone + watch), a 3-in-1 station is absolutely worth it. One power cable, one footprint, three devices charged — it simplifies every nightstand and office desk.
