## How to Choose a Portable Bluetooth Speaker

### Battery Life vs. Portability

These trade off directly. Larger battery means heavier, larger speaker. The JBL Charge 5 (20h) weighs nearly twice the Bose SoundLink Flex (12h). For day trips, 10–12h is enough. For multi-day camping without a power source, 20h+ is worth the size penalty.

### Waterproof Ratings Explained

- **IPX4:** Splash-resistant — handles rain and splashing but not submersion. Fine for outdoor use, risky near pools.
- **IP67:** Waterproof to 1m for 30 minutes — can be submerged briefly, floated in water, used at the pool.
- **IP68:** Waterproof beyond 1m — diving and underwater use (rare in consumer speakers).

For pool and beach use, IP67 is the minimum worth buying. The JBL Charge 5, Bose SoundLink Flex, and Sony SRS-XB43 all hit IP67.

### Sound Signature

- **Bose:** Flat, balanced, accurate — best for diverse music genres
- **JBL:** Bass-forward with good mid presence — works for most casual listening
- **Sony EXTRA BASS:** Heavily bass-forward — built for dance/hip-hop in outdoor settings
- **Ultimate Ears:** Balanced 360-degree — best for filling space evenly

### 360-Degree vs. Directional Sound

Most portable speakers are directional — they project sound toward you and away from surfaces behind them. 360-degree speakers (Ultimate Ears, some JBL models) project in all directions, which matters in outdoor group settings where people surround the speaker.
