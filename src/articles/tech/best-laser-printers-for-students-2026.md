## What Students Should Know About Toner

**Always buy high-yield:** Every laser printer comes with a "starter cartridge" that prints 700–1,200 pages. The high-yield cartridge replacement ($30–$45) prints 3,000 pages. Cost per page with the starter cartridge is 3–5× higher than with the high-yield. Replace the starter cartridge with a high-yield immediately when it runs out — don't buy the standard cartridge.

**Brother TN-760 vs. TN-730:**
- TN-730: 1,200 pages, ~$30 (standard)
- TN-760: 3,000 pages, ~$35 (high-yield)
- **Always buy TN-760.** $5 more, 2.5× more pages.

**Generic vs. OEM toner:** Third-party Brother-compatible toner ($15–$20) works adequately for most uses. Print quality is slightly lower and failure rate is slightly higher than genuine Brother toner, but for student essay printing, generics are acceptable. Use OEM toner for anything where print quality matters.

## Dorm Setup Tips

**Place on your desk or a small shelf** — a 250-sheet paper tray means you don't need to reach under a desk to reload.

**Use WiFi not USB** — wireless printing from your laptop, phone, and tablet without cable gymnastics is worth the 5-minute setup.

**Load a full ream of paper immediately** — running out of paper at deadline time is almost as frustrating as a dead cartridge. Keep a 500-sheet ream on your desk.

**Share with roommate** — split the upfront cost, share a WiFi printer, pay for toner together when it runs out. Two students sharing a Brother HL-L2350DW have a printing cost near zero.
