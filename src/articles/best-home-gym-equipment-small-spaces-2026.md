## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I build real muscle with a small home gym?**
Yes. Progressive overload — gradually increasing resistance over time — drives muscle growth regardless of equipment type. Adjustable dumbbells covering 5–52.5 lbs and a bench enable every major compound movement (press, row, deadlift variation, squat variation).

**What's the minimum space for a home gym?**
With adjustable dumbbells and a TRX, you can train in any 6x6 ft clear area. Add a foldable bench and you need 8x8 ft with the bench unfolded. A full functional trainer needs a 10x10 ft minimum.

**Should I get a pull-up bar?**
If you can mount one, yes. A door-frame pull-up bar costs under $30 and enables pull-ups, chin-ups, hanging leg raises, and inverted rows — all high-value exercises that dumbbells don't cover.

**Is rubber flooring necessary?**
For protecting floors and reducing noise, yes — especially in apartments. 3/4" thick rubber tiles (often sold as stall mats at farm supply stores) are the most cost-effective option.
