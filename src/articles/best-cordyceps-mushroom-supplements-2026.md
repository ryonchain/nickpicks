# Best Cordyceps Mushroom Supplements of 2026: Energy and Athletic Performance Picks

Cordyceps occupies a unique space among functional mushrooms — it's the one with the strongest evidence for athletic performance enhancement. Athletes have used it to improve VO2 max, reduce fatigue, and accelerate recovery. The challenge is that wild Cordyceps sinensis costs thousands per kilogram — commercial products use either the CS-4 fermentation strain or the more accessible (and arguably more potent) Cordyceps militaris.

## Cordyceps and Athletic Performance: The Research

**VO2 max improvements:**
- A randomized controlled trial in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine found CS-4 supplementation (3g/day for 12 weeks) increased VO2 max by 7% in healthy older adults vs 1% in placebo group
- A cycling-specific study showed reduced fatigue and improved power output after 3 weeks of militaris extract

**Mechanism — ATP synthesis:**
- Cordycepin activates AMPK (AMP-activated protein kinase), a cellular energy sensor that upregulates mitochondrial biogenesis
- Improved oxygen utilization at the cellular level reduces perceived exertion at the same workload

**Best for:**
- Endurance athletes (cycling, running, swimming)
- High-altitude training or acclimatization
- People with chronic fatigue seeking non-stimulant energy support
- Older adults experiencing age-related energy decline

**Stacking:**
Cordyceps pairs well with lion's mane in the morning (energy + focus) and reishi in the evening (recovery + sleep). The "mushroom stack" approach is increasingly popular among biohackers and is supported by each mushroom's complementary mechanisms.
