## Body Lotion Buying Guide

### Understanding Moisturizer Types

**Lotions:** Highest water content, lightest texture, absorbs fastest. Best for normal skin and warm weather.

**Creams:** Balanced water and oil content. More moisturizing than lotion, suitable for most skin types year-round.

**Body Butters:** Highest oil content, richest texture, most intensive moisture. Best for very dry skin or winter use.

### Key Ingredients to Look For

**Shea Butter:** Rich in fatty acids that mimic skin lipids. Higher concentration = more effective.

**Glycerin:** Humectant that pulls water from the air into skin. Found in nearly every effective moisturizer.

**Plant Oils (Argan, Jojoba, Rosehip):** Deliver fatty acids that repair the skin barrier and add natural radiance.

**Ceramides:** Skin-identical lipids that restore barrier integrity. Particularly helpful for eczema-prone or mature skin.

### Application Tips

Apply body lotion immediately after showering, while skin is still slightly damp — the moisture barrier is open and absorption is significantly better. For extremely dry areas (heels, elbows, knees), apply a thicker cream and cover with socks or light fabric for 20 minutes to intensify absorption.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How is a luxury lotion different from a drugstore brand?**
Ingredient quality, concentration, and formulation sophistication. Luxury formulas typically use higher concentrations of active ingredients (e.g., 25% shea butter vs. 3%) and more sophisticated delivery systems. The sensory experience is also meaningfully better.

**Can I use body lotion on my face?**
Not recommended. Body lotions are formulated for thicker skin and may clog facial pores. Use dedicated facial moisturizers for the face.

**How often should I apply body lotion?**
Once daily is sufficient for most people, ideally post-shower. Those with very dry skin benefit from twice-daily application.

**Do luxury body lotions expire?**
Yes — most have a shelf life of 12–24 months after opening. Look for the "period after opening" symbol (an open jar icon) on the packaging.
