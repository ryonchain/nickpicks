## What to Look For in an Indoor Electric Grill

Indoor grilling differs from outdoor grilling in two key ways: heat ceiling and smoke management. Most electric grills top out around 450–500°F, while charcoal and gas can reach 600°F+. That gap matters for sear lines and crust formation. The best indoor grills compensate with contact heat (pressing the food from both sides) or infrared elements.

Key factors:

- **Max temperature:** Look for 450°F or higher. Anything below 400°F struggles to sear.
- **Heating element type:** Open coil elements heat unevenly. Ceramic or infrared elements distribute heat more consistently and produce less smoke.
- **Smoke management:** Water trays, infrared elements, and sloped drip channels all reduce smoke. "Smokeless" is a spectrum, not a binary.
- **Cooking surface area:** Measured in square inches. Solo use: 80–120 sq in. Family of four: 200+ sq in.
- **Cleanup:** Dishwasher-safe plates make the biggest difference in daily use.
