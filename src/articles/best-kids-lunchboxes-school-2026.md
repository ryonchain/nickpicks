## What to Look for in a Kids' Lunchbox

**Insulation quality:** For food safety, a lunchbox should keep cold foods below 40°F for at least 5–6 hours. Look for thick walls, a full-perimeter seal, and good reviews on temperature retention — not just marketing language.

**Age-appropriate closures:** A kindergartner can't open a stiff zipper or complex latch. Test that your child can open and close independently before the first day of school.

**Leakproof vs. leak-resistant:** True leakproof designs (Yumbox, Bentgo) seal the inner tray against the lid. "Leak-resistant" bags just have a coated interior that slows the spread of spills.

**Washability:** You will need to clean this container daily. Machine-washable bags save significant time over the school year. Dishwasher-safe trays are a meaningful convenience upgrade.

**Size for age:** Toddlers (PreK–K) need smaller boxes with simple openings. Elementary kids (grades 1–5) can handle most standard sizes. Middle schoolers may want larger-capacity bags.
