## How Heavy Should You Go?

**Beginner:** Start with 10% of bodyweight. A 150 lb person starts at 15 lbs.

**Intermediate:** 15–20% of bodyweight is the sweet spot for most loaded carries and rucking.

**Advanced/Military prep:** 30–45 lb for GoRuck challenge events or heavy-carry training.

**General rule:** If your form breaks down, stride changes, or you feel joint stress, the load is too heavy. Weighted vests should challenge your cardiovascular system, not your joints.

---

## What to Look for in a Weighted Vest

**Fit:** The vest should sit snug against your chest with no gap at the shoulders. A loose vest bounces with every step — painful and inefficient.

**Weight distribution:** Front-back balance matters. Vests that load only the front (some budget models) create neck and lower back strain.

**Plate vs. shot:** Shot-filled vests (Hyperwear) conform to your body but are fixed weight. Plate-carrier vests (GORUCK, Rogue) let you swap weight — better for progressive overload.

**Ventilation:** For HIIT and rucking, mesh panels or open-weave construction prevents overheating. Closed shell vests (like some beginners models) can get hot fast.
