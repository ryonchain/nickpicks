# Best Portable Solar Generators for Camping (2026) — Jackery, EcoFlow, Bluetti

A portable solar generator is the difference between a camping trip with dead devices and one where you can charge phones, run a CPAP machine, or keep a small cooler running. Unlike gas generators, solar power stations are silent, fume-free, and rechargeable from the sun.

In 2026, three brands dominate the market: **Jackery**, **EcoFlow**, and **Bluetti**. Here's how to choose and which model to buy.

---

## Understanding Wattage and Capacity

**Capacity (Wh — Watt-hours):** How much total energy the battery stores. A 500Wh unit can run a 100W device for ~5 hours.

**Output (W — Watts):** Peak power the unit can deliver at once. A 1000W output means you can run appliances up to 1000W simultaneously.

**Recharge options:** Solar (via included or compatible panels), AC wall outlet, car 12V/cigarette lighter.

**Rough use-case guide:**
- Phone charging weekend: 200–300Wh
- Car camping with lights + devices: 500–700Wh
- Off-grid cabin or CPAP support: 1000Wh+
- Van life / extended off-grid: 2000Wh+

## FAQ

**Q: Can I charge a solar generator while using it?**
Most units support pass-through charging. Check the manual — some require simultaneous solar + wall input to enable it.

**Q: How many solar panels do I need?**
For a 500Wh unit, one 100W panel will recharge it in roughly 5–7 hours of direct sun.

**Q: Are solar generators safe to use inside a tent?**
Yes — they produce no fumes. Unlike gas generators, solar power stations are fully safe indoors. Keep them out of direct rain.

**Q: What's the lifespan of a solar generator?**
LFP battery units (Bluetti AC200P) last 3,500+ charge cycles. Standard NMC units last 500–800 cycles.
