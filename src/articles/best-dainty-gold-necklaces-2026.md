## Gold Necklace Buying Guide

### Understanding Gold Types

**Solid gold** (10K, 14K, 18K) is the real thing. 14K is 58.3% gold; 18K is 75%; 10K is 41.7%. Higher karat = softer and more yellow but also purer.

**Gold filled** = 14K gold layer mechanically bonded to a base metal, comprising at least 5% of the item's weight. 100x more gold than plated. Looks and wears like solid gold for years.

**Gold vermeil** = sterling silver base plated with at least 2.5 microns of 14K+ gold. More durable than standard gold plating but less so than gold filled.

**Gold plated** = thin gold layer over any base metal. Can look like solid gold initially but tarnishes in 6–18 months with regular wear, especially with water or sweat exposure.

### How to Make Gold Jewelry Last

1. Remove before swimming, showering, and exercise
2. Apply perfume, hairspray, and lotion before putting on jewelry
3. Store separately in a soft pouch or anti-tarnish bag
4. Clean gently with a microfiber cloth
5. For solid gold: an occasional soak in warm water + mild dish soap removes oils and restores shine

### Popular Dainty Necklace Styles for Layering

- **Cable chain** (plain round links) — the versatile base layer

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best material for a dainty gold necklace for everyday wear?**
14K solid gold is the best material for true everyday wear that will last decades without tarnishing or chipping. For a lower-cost alternative, 14K gold filled is the next best option — it contains substantially more gold than plated pieces and can last 5–30 years with regular care. Avoid gold-plated pieces for daily wear unless you're prepared to replace them every 1–2 years.

**What length dainty necklace should I get?**
16 inches is the most popular length — it sits just below the collarbone on most women and works both alone and as a base layering piece. 18 inches is the classic standard length — slightly lower and more versatile for different necklines. 14 inches is a choker length — works best with crew necks and boat necks, or as the shortest layer in a multi-necklace stack.

**How do you keep dainty gold necklaces from tangling?**
Store each necklace separately in its own small pouch or hang individual hooks. When traveling, thread both ends of a chain through a plastic straw and clasp it — this prevents tangling in a jewelry bag. For layering, use a necklace layering clasp that holds multiple chains together at the back of the neck.
