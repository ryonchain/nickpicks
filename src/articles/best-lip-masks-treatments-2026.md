## Lip Skin Anatomy: Why Lip Products Are Different

### The Lip Barrier Problem
Regular skin has 15–20 layers of stratum corneum cells providing a robust barrier. Lip skin has only 3–4 layers — which is why lips dehydrate, chap, and peel far faster than facial skin under the same environmental conditions.

### Ingredients That Actually Work on Lips

**Humectants (attract water):**
- Hyaluronic acid: draws moisture from the environment into lip tissue
- Glycerin: high-concentration glycerin creates significant moisture attraction
- Honey/propolis: natural humectant with antimicrobial properties

**Occlusives (seal moisture in):**
- Mineral oil: most effective occlusive, forms a complete seal
- Petrolatum (Vaseline/petroleum jelly): the reference occlusive, used in every dermatology comparison
- Shea butter + wax: balances occlusion with cosmetic elegance

**Emollients (soften and smooth):**
- Castor oil: unique viscosity that smooths lip surface
- Sweet almond oil: lightweight, softening
- Vitamin E (tocopherol): antioxidant + skin conditioning

**Exfoliation (for product penetration):**
- AHA (lactic acid, citric acid): dissolves dead lip cells without scrubbing
- Enzymatic: papaya/pineapple enzymes dissolve surface debris gently
