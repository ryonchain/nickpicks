## LED Desk Lamp Buying Guide

### What Is Color Temperature?

Color temperature is measured in Kelvin and affects how the light appears and how it affects you:

- **2,700K–3,000K (warm white):** Like incandescent bulbs. Warm, cozy, better for evening use. Not ideal for focus tasks.
- **4,000K (neutral white):** The sweet spot for most home office tasks — bright enough to work, not as harsh as daylight.
- **5,000K–6,500K (cool daylight):** Crisp, stimulating. Best for detail tasks and morning work sessions. Can be harsh in the evening and disrupts sleep if used late.

For home office use, a lamp that covers 3,000K–5,500K gives you flexibility to adapt to the time of day and task.

### What Causes Eye Strain from Lamps?

Four main factors:
1. **PWM flicker:** LEDs dimmed via PWM flicker rapidly. Not consciously visible but causes visual fatigue. DC dimming (BenQ) eliminates this.
2. **Uneven illumination:** A hot spot in the center and dark edges cause your eyes to constantly readjust. Wider, more even spread reduces this.
3. **Glare on screen:** Lamp light reflecting off your monitor. Solved by monitor-mount lamps or positioning the lamp to the side.
4. **Wrong color temperature for time of day:** Bright cool light in the evening suppresses melatonin. Warm light in the morning can make you feel sleepy.

### Brightness: How Much Do You Need?

- **500 lux:** Comfortable for reading documents, general computer work.
- **1,000 lux:** Better for detailed work, drawing, tasks requiring fine focus.
- **2,000+ lux:** Professional level — excellent for detail work, rarely needed for typical office tasks.

Most home office workers are well-served by 800–1,500 lux. The BenQ's 2,500 lux max is overkill for screen work but useful if you also do paper-based work or crafts at the same desk.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What color temperature is best for home office work?**
4,000K–5,000K for focused daytime work. Drop to 3,000K–3,500K for evenings to avoid disrupting sleep.

**Is LED lighting better than halogen for a desk lamp?**
Yes. LEDs use 75–80% less energy, last 25x longer, produce less heat, and modern LEDs have excellent color rendering. Halogen is no longer a reasonable choice for new desk lamp purchases.

**How do I reduce glare on my monitor from a desk lamp?**
Position the lamp to the side or above, not directly in front of or behind you. Monitor-mount lamps (Humanscale) solve the problem by design. If using a traditional lamp, tilt the head down toward the desk rather than angling toward the screen.

**Do I need a desk lamp if I have overhead lighting?**
Overhead lighting typically creates shadows on your workspace from your own body. A desk lamp on your non-dominant side illuminates your work area without creating shadows. Even with adequate overhead light, a desk lamp generally improves work quality.

**Can desk lamps damage eyes?**
Modern LED desk lamps don't damage eyes. The concern is eye strain from suboptimal lighting — too dim (eyes work harder to see), too bright (glare), flicker, or color temperature mismatch. None of these cause permanent damage; they cause temporary fatigue and discomfort.
