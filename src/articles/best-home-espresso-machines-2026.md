## Espresso Basics: What You Need to Know

**Extraction:** Espresso is brewed by forcing 9 bars of pressure through 18–20g of finely ground coffee in 25–30 seconds. Variables to control: grind size, dose, tamp pressure, water temperature, and time.

**The "shot dial":**
- Under-extracted (too fast, too light, too sour) → grind finer
- Over-extracted (too slow, too dark, too bitter) → grind coarser
- Perfect (golden crema, sweet-bitter balance, 25–30 second pull) → don't change anything

**Beans:** Use espresso roast (dark) or medium-dark roast. Light roast requires significant grinder adjustment. Avoid pre-ground for serious espresso — grind immediately before brewing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a $700 espresso machine worth it vs $5 café lattes?**
At 2 lattes/day from a café: ~$3,650/year. The Barista Express pays for itself in under 3 months. Yes.

**Do I need a separate grinder with the Barista Express?**
No — the built-in grinder is good enough for excellent home espresso. Upgrading to a dedicated burr grinder improves results by 10–20% but isn't necessary to start.

**What's the difference between 15-bar and 9-bar machines?**
Marketing. Espresso is optimally extracted at 9 bars. Machines that advertise 15 bars usually regulate down to 9 bars at the group head. The 15-bar label is meaningless — look for machines with an OPV or PID instead.
