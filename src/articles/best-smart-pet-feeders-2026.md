## What to Look for in a Smart Pet Feeder

**Hopper size:**
- Cats / small dogs: 3–5L is enough for 1–2 weeks
- Medium dogs: 5L for 3–5 days depending on portion size
- Large dogs: 6L+ for daily refilling convenience

**Dispensing reliability:**
Jamming is the #1 failure mode for automatic feeders. Look for:
- Anti-jam sensors or rotor design
- Desiccant layer to prevent moisture clumping
- Kibble size compatibility (check manufacturer specs)

**Camera:**
Cameras add $30–$50 to price but are worth it for anxious pet owners. 1080p is noticeably better than 720p for identifying what your pet is doing.

**Battery backup:**
Power outages happen. A feeder with battery backup ensures your pet doesn't miss meals during a storm.

**App quality:**
Read recent reviews specifically mentioning the app. Several feeders have solid hardware but flaky apps — a bad app negates the "smart" value.

## FAQ

**Can smart pet feeders work without Wi-Fi?**
The on-device scheduling works without Wi-Fi — meals dispense on the programmed schedule. You just lose remote app control and camera access until connectivity is restored.

**Are automatic pet feeders safe for cats?**
Yes, with caveats. Use only dry kibble (wet food spoils). Avoid feeders with large hopper openings a determined cat can access. Most quality feeders have cat-resistant designs.

**How often should I clean the feeder?**
Clean the food hopper and dispenser weekly (or more often in humid climates). Rinse with mild dish soap and air dry completely before refilling.

**Can I use a smart feeder for multiple pets?**
Yes — but you can't guarantee one pet doesn't eat another's portion. For medical diet separation, use individual feeders with microchip or RFID tag recognition (specialty products).
