## Understanding CADR and Room Coverage

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) measures how quickly a purifier filters a specific volume of air. It's expressed in cubic feet per minute (CFM) for dust, pollen, and smoke. The general rule: your purifier's CADR should be at least two-thirds of your room's square footage.

For a 200 sq ft room: you need at least 133 CFM CADR.  
For a 300 sq ft room: you need at least 200 CFM CADR.

Anything labeled "covers 300 sq ft" without a CADR rating is a marketing claim, not a technical spec.

## What Actually Matters When Buying a Small Room Air Purifier

**Skip "HEPA-type" and "HEPA-like" filters.** Only True HEPA (H13 standard) captures 99.97% of 0.3-micron particles. Lesser designations are unregulated marketing terms.

**Filter costs matter more than purchase price.** A $60 purifier with $80/year filters costs more than a $100 purifier with $20/year filters within 18 months.

**CADR beats claimed coverage.** When a manufacturer claims "500 sq ft coverage" without listing CADR, treat it skeptically. Legitimate purifiers publish their AHAM-certified CADR numbers.
