## Types of Bed Frames

**Metal platform:** Minimal, affordable, fits any mattress. Best for minimalists or rental situations.

**Upholstered:** Padded headboard in fabric or faux leather. Adds warmth to the bedroom.

**Wood platform:** Solid, timeless look. Natural wood grain adds texture. Heavier and harder to move.

**Storage bed:** Drawers or lift-top storage underneath. Adds significant storage capacity — important in smaller bedrooms.

**Adjustable base:** Electric head and foot elevation. Great for reading, reflux, or snoring relief.

## What to Check Before Buying

1. **Box spring required?** Platform beds don't need one. Some frames (especially adjustable ones) do.
2. **Center support:** Essential for queen and king sizes. Verify the frame includes a center support bar or leg.
3. **Clearance height:** 7" minimum for under-bed storage. 14" for bins and larger items.
4. **Weight capacity:** Check total weight — mattress (150–200 lbs for king), plus sleepers.
5. **Slat spacing:** Maximum 2.75" between slats for foam and hybrid mattresses.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a box spring with a platform bed?**
No. Platform beds have a solid or slatted base that supports any mattress directly. Adding a box spring raises your bed height by 9 inches, which may be too high.

**What is the best bed frame for heavy people?**
Metal platform frames with multiple center support legs have the highest weight capacities (750–1,000 lbs). Look for frames rated for at least 500 lbs total and with legs spaced no more than 18 inches apart.

**Do upholstered beds squeak?**
The headboard doesn't squeak, but the frame can if it uses metal brackets without rubber damping. Top-rated upholstered frames (Zinus, Wayfair house brands) typically include anti-creak rubber grips.

**How high should my bed be?**
Sitting height for most adults is 24–26 inches from floor to mattress top. For a 10-inch mattress: frame height of 14–16 inches. For a 12-inch mattress: frame height of 12–14 inches.
