## Photo Printer Technology Explained

**Inkjet:** Uses liquid ink sprayed through microscopic nozzles. Best for color accuracy and large formats. Ink can be expensive. Heads can clog if you don't print regularly.

**Dye Sublimation (dye-sub):** Heat transfers dye directly into specially coated paper. Produces continuous-tone prints (no visible dots). Consistent quality, sealed against fingerprints. Best for 4"×6" volume printing.

**ZINK (Zero Ink):** Dye crystals embedded in the paper are activated by heat in the printer. No ink or toner needed. Convenient but lower quality than dye-sub or inkjet.

**Laser:** Not recommended for photo printing — laser toner struggles to reproduce the color depth and gradients that inkjet and dye-sub deliver naturally.
