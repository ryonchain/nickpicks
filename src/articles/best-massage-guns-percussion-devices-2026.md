## Massage Gun Buying Guide

### Understanding the Key Specs

**Stall Force (Most Important)**
Stall force measures how much resistance a massage gun's motor can handle before it stalls. Higher stall force = deeper tissue penetration.
- 25–30 lbs: Light relaxation, surface muscle tension
- 40–50 lbs: Moderate — good for most fitness users
- 55–60 lbs: Professional/deep tissue level

**Amplitude (Depth of Stroke)**
Amplitude is how far the head moves per stroke in millimeters. Deeper amplitude reaches further into muscle tissue.
- 10–12mm: Light to moderate penetration
- 14mm: Mid-range (Hyperice standard)
- 16mm: Deep tissue (Theragun standard)

**Speed (Percussions Per Minute)**
PPM ranges typically from 1,750 to 3,200. Higher speeds deliver more percussions but aren't always better — lower speeds with high stall force are better for deep tissue work.

### Which Attachment Should I Use?

| Attachment | Best For |
|-----------|---------|
| Large ball | Large muscle groups (quads, glutes) |
| Dampener | Sensitive areas, bony regions |
| Thumb | Spine, trigger point release |
| Cone | Deep pinpoint tissue, feet |
| Wedge | Shoulder blades, IT band |
| Flat | Large flat muscles (chest, back) |

### How to Use a Massage Gun Safely

- **Don't use directly on joints, bones, or the spine** — stay on muscle bellies
- **Limit sessions to 1–2 minutes per muscle group**
- **Start at the lowest speed** and increase as needed
- **Move slowly** — 1 inch per second is a good pace
- **Avoid bruised, inflamed, or injured areas**
- **Consult a doctor** if you have circulatory conditions or recent injuries

## Frequently Asked Questions

**When should I use a massage gun — before or after workouts?**
Both are beneficial but serve different purposes. Pre-workout: use for 30–60 seconds per muscle group to increase blood flow and activation. Post-workout: use for 1–2 minutes per muscle group to reduce soreness and speed recovery.

**Can massage guns replace a professional massage?**
They complement professional massage but don't fully replace it. Massage therapists work with the whole body, address compensatory patterns, and provide benefits massage guns can't. Think of a massage gun as maintenance between professional sessions.

**Are massage guns safe for people with high blood pressure?**
Generally yes, but consult your doctor first. Percussion therapy increases local blood flow and shouldn't significantly affect systemic blood pressure, but individual variation exists.

**Why do some massage guns go quiet after a few months?**
Carbon brush wear in lower-quality motors causes this. Brushless motors (used in Theragun, Hypervolt, and Ekrin) don't have this problem. Budget massage guns with brushed motors can degrade in performance over 6–12 months.

**Can I use a massage gun on my neck?**
Only the sides and back of the neck — avoid the front where arteries and nerves run. Use the lowest speed setting. Many physical therapists recommend professional guidance for neck use.
