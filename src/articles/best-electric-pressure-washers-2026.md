## Electric Pressure Washers in 2026: Gas vs. Electric Has a Clear Answer Now

For five years, the conventional wisdom was that gas pressure washers were for serious work and electric models were for car washing. That distinction has collapsed. Modern electric pressure washers from Sun Joe, Greenworks, and Ryobi now deliver 2,000–3,000+ PSI — sufficient for driveway cleaning, deck stripping, siding washing, and patio restoration. At the same time, electric models eliminate the maintenance (oil changes, carburetor clogs, pull-start failures) that made gas ownership frustrating.

The key specs: **PSI** (pressure per square inch) measures cleaning force, and **GPM** (gallons per minute) measures flow rate. **Cleaning Units (CU) = PSI × GPM** is the most useful single number for comparing real-world cleaning performance. A machine with 2,300 PSI at 1.2 GPM (2,760 CU) cleans faster and more effectively than a 2,000 PSI / 1.0 GPM machine (2,000 CU).

**Quick verdict:** The **Sun Joe SPX3001** (~$150) delivers the best cleaning performance at a price point most homeowners can justify — 2,030 PSI, 1.76 GPM, and a 20-foot extension cord that handles most property sizes. For larger properties and heavier cleaning tasks, the **Greenworks 2300 PSI** (~$200) adds power and a longer cord.

## Pressure Washer Buying Guide

**PSI vs. GPM — which matters more?** For removing stubborn stains from concrete, PSI matters more (you need force to break the bond). For rinsing large surfaces quickly, GPM matters more (more water flow = faster cleaning). Cleaning Units (PSI × GPM) is the most useful single comparison number.

**Electric vs. gas:** Electric washers require no maintenance, start instantly, run quieter, and have lower total cost of ownership. Gas washers deliver more PSI (3,000–4,000+ PSI for commercial units) and don't require an extension cord. For homeowner use up to 2,500 PSI, electric is the right choice.

**Nozzle selection:** The 0° (red) tip is for stripping paint and stubborn deposits — never use it on wood, siding, or skin. The 15° (yellow) tip handles concrete. The 25° (green) covers most general cleaning. The 40° (white) safely cleans vehicles, vinyl, and wood decks. Always start with a wider nozzle and step down if needed.

**Surface damage prevention:** Electric pressure washers can damage soft wood, old paint, and vehicle finishes if used too close or with too narrow a nozzle. Test on a small inconspicuous area first. Maintain 12–18 inches of distance for most surfaces.
