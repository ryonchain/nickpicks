## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**PSI (Pounds per Square Inch):** Pressure. Higher PSI removes stubborn stains. For cars, stay under 1,900 PSI to protect paint.

**GPM (Gallons per Minute):** Flow rate. Higher GPM rinses dirt away faster. Don't obsess over PSI alone — GPM matters equally.

**Cleaning Units (CU):** PSI × GPM. This is the real measure of total cleaning power. A 2,000 PSI / 1.76 GPM machine (3,520 CU) beats a 3,000 PSI / 1.1 GPM machine (3,300 CU) on total cleaning performance.

**Hose length:** 25 ft minimum for driveways. 20 ft forces awkward repositioning on anything larger than a patio.

**GFCI protection:** Required for safe outdoor use. Don't buy an electric pressure washer without it.

**Nozzle variety:** The turbo/rotary nozzle dramatically increases concrete cleaning speed. Look for kits that include it.

## FAQ

**What PSI do I need for a driveway?**
1,500–2,000 PSI is adequate for normal concrete driveways. For oil stains or heavy grime, 2,000+ PSI with a turbo nozzle works best. More PSI can etch softer concrete — stay at 2,000 PSI or lower for standard residential concrete.

**Can electric pressure washers clean a driveway?**
Yes. A 2,000 PSI / 1.76 GPM electric washer (3,500+ cleaning units) handles 95% of residential driveway cleaning. Gas machines are faster on very large areas but not necessary for a standard two-car driveway.

**Is Sun Joe or Kärcher better?**
Sun Joe wins on value — more cleaning units per dollar. Kärcher wins on durability and longevity — the induction motor lasts significantly longer. If you clean 3× a year, buy Sun Joe. If you clean 3× a week, buy Kärcher.

**What is TSS on a pressure washer?**
Total Stop System. When you release the trigger, the pump automatically shuts off to prevent overheating and extend motor life. This is a significant feature that Sun Joe includes — cheaper models run the pump continuously whether you're spraying or not.

**Can I use a pressure washer to wash my car?**
Yes, with the right nozzle. Use the 25° or 40° nozzle (never the 0° or 15° on paint) and stay at least 12 inches from the surface. Pressure washing is safe on cars with factory paint in good condition — avoid on chipped paint or older vehicles with rust.
