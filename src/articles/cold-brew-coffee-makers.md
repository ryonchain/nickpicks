## Cold Brew Coffee Buying Guide

### Concentrate vs. Ready-to-Drink

Most cold brew makers produce **concentrate** — coffee steeped at a higher ground-to-water ratio. Dilute 1:1 with water, milk, or milk alternative before drinking. Ready-to-drink cold brew (pre-diluted) can also be made in these same makers using a lower coffee-to-water ratio. Concentrate is more efficient for fridge storage — same volume stores twice the caffeine.

### Filter Types

**Stainless mesh:** Most common. Easy to clean, reusable, produces slightly more sediment than paper or felt. The Hario Mizudashi has the finest stainless mesh available.

**Felt/cloth:** Produces the clearest cold brew (Toddy system). Must be rinsed thoroughly after each use or will develop mold. Replace every 2–3 months.

**Paper filters (optional):** Double-filter by adding a paper filter inside any mesh filter for near-espresso clarity. Adds cost but produces the cleanest cup.

### Steep Time and Temperature

**Room temperature:** 12–16 hours produces a slightly brighter, more acidic cold brew. Slightly higher caffeine extraction.

**Refrigerator:** 18–24 hours produces smoother, lower-acid cold brew. The OXO and KitchenAid are designed for fridge steeping. Longer is better for clarity and smoothness.

**Cold brew vs. cold drip:** Cold drip uses a slow drip of cold water through grounds over 3–8 hours. Cold brew is immersion steeping. Immersion (all makers on this list) is easier and more accessible.

### Glass vs. Plastic

**Glass:** Better flavor clarity (no plastic taste), more attractive, dishwasher safe. **Plastic:** Lighter, less expensive, more durable. For a liquid-storage application where flavor matters, glass is worth the premium.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does cold brew last in the fridge?**
Cold brew concentrate keeps for 2 weeks in an airtight glass carafe. Ready-to-drink (diluted) cold brew is best consumed within 3–5 days. The lower acidity of cold brew (compared to hot coffee) means it doesn't go stale as quickly as hot-brewed coffee stored cold.

**What coffee grind size should I use for cold brew?**
Coarse grind — similar to kosher salt granule size. Coarse grind extracts slowly and cleanly, producing a smooth brew. Fine or medium grind over-extracts and produces bitter, murky cold brew. Use a burr grinder set to coarse, or ask your coffee shop to grind coarsely for cold brew.

**What's the coffee-to-water ratio for cold brew concentrate?**
The standard ratio is 1:4 to 1:5 by weight (1 gram coffee per 4–5 grams water). For a 32 oz batch, use 4–6 oz of coarsely ground coffee. For stronger concentrate (for milk drinks), use 1:4. For lighter concentrate (for black coffee), use 1:5 or 1:6.

**Is cold brew less acidic than regular coffee?**
Yes — cold brew is typically 65–70% less acidic than hot-brewed coffee. The lack of heat prevents the extraction of certain acidic compounds. This makes cold brew a good option for people with acid reflux or who find hot coffee hard on their stomach.
