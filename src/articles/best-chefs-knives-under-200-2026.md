## Chef's Knife Buying Guide

### German vs Japanese: Which is Right for You?

| Factor | German (Wüsthof, Victorinox) | Japanese (MAC, Shun, Global) |
|--------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Steel hardness | 56–58 HRC | 59–64 HRC |
| Edge angle | 15–20° per side | 12–16° per side |
| Initial sharpness | Good | Exceptional |
| Edge retention | Moderate | Better |
| Durability (chipping) | Very durable | More fragile |
| Weight | Heavier | Lighter |
| Maintenance | Steel hone + whetstone | Whetstone only |
| Best for | Versatile, rough prep | Precision work |

### The Most Important Number: Steel Hardness (HRC)
- **54–56 HRC**: Soft — dulls quickly, very easy to sharpen
- **56–58 HRC**: German standard — good balance of durability and sharpness
- **59–62 HRC**: Japanese standard — sharper, better retention, chips more easily
- **63+ HRC**: Very hard — maximum sharpness and retention, brittle, professional use

### How to Maintain a Chef's Knife
1. **Hone before every use** (German/hybrid knives): a honing steel realigns the edge without removing metal
2. **Sharpen every 3–6 months** on a whetstone or via a professional
3. **Hand-wash only**: dishwashers destroy knives through heat, vibration, and harsh detergent
4. **Store on a magnetic strip or in a knife block**: never in a drawer where edges contact other metal
5. **Use a proper cutting board**: wood or plastic — never glass, stone, or ceramic (they destroy edges)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size chef's knife should I buy?**
8-inch is the standard recommendation for most home cooks. It handles 95% of kitchen tasks — vegetable prep, meat portioning, herb chopping. 10-inch is for high-volume prep and large proteins. 6-inch is for smaller hands or people who find 8-inch unwieldy.

**German or Japanese — which knife is better?**
Neither is objectively better; they serve different styles. German knives (Wüsthof, Victorinox) are more forgiving, handle rough use, and are easier to maintain. Japanese knives (MAC, Shun) achieve superior sharpness but require more careful use and whetstone sharpening. If you're not sure, start with German.

**Is the Victorinox Fibrox really as good as Wüsthof?**
For most cooking tasks, yes. Professionals choose it in working kitchens because it outperforms its price. The Wüsthof is better-balanced, holds an edge slightly longer, and has better fit-and-finish — but those differences matter less than most buyers expect. Both are excellent knives.

**Can I put my chef's knife in the dishwasher?**
No. Dishwashers damage knife edges through heat expansion/contraction, vibration against other utensils, and harsh detergents. Even "dishwasher-safe" knives degrade faster in the dishwasher. Hand-wash and dry immediately.
