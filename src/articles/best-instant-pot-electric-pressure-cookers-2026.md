## Electric Pressure Cooker Buying Guide

### What Size Do I Need?

| Household Size | Recommended Capacity |
|---------------|---------------------|
| 1–2 people | 3 quart |
| 3–4 people | 6 quart (most popular) |
| 5+ people | 8 quart |
| Batch cooking | 8–10 quart |

**Pro tip:** When in doubt, go larger. A 6-quart pot can cook for 2 people just as easily as for 6. You can't, however, cook 6 servings in a 3-quart pot.

### Pressure Cooker vs. Instant Pot: What's the Difference?

"Instant Pot" is actually a brand name — like Kleenex for tissues. Instant Pot is a Canadian company that makes electric pressure cookers under the Instant Pot brand. "Electric pressure cooker" is the generic category that includes Instant Pot, Ninja Foodi, Breville Fast Slow Pro, and others.

### Key Safety Features to Look For

Modern electric pressure cookers are extremely safe thanks to multiple redundant safety systems:

- **Pressure release valve** — manually vent steam or use auto-release
- **Anti-blockage vent** — prevents the steam release from clogging
- **Safety lid lock** — lid can't be opened under pressure
- **Excess pressure protection** — pressure regulator releases excess steam
- **High-temperature warning** — alerts if temperature is too high

### How Much Time Does Pressure Cooking Actually Save?

Typical time comparisons (pressure cook vs. stovetop/oven):
- Beef stew: 35 min vs. 2.5 hours
- Whole chicken: 25 min vs. 1.5 hours
- Dried beans: 30 min vs. overnight soak + 1.5 hours
- Rice: 12 min vs. 20 min (modest gain)
- Hard-boiled eggs: 5 min vs. 12 min (modest gain)

Pressure cooking shines most for tough cuts of meat, dried legumes, and long-braised dishes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is an Instant Pot the same as a pressure cooker?**
Instant Pot is a brand of electric pressure cooker. The Instant Pot brand makes several models, all of which include pressure cooking as a primary function. Other brands like Ninja and Breville make competing electric pressure cookers.

**Can I make yogurt in an electric pressure cooker?**
Yes — most Instant Pot models include a dedicated Yogurt function. It's one of their most popular non-pressure features.

**Is it safe to leave an Instant Pot unattended?**
Yes, this is one of the major advantages over stovetop pressure cookers. Electric models are designed to be set and left — they automatically maintain pressure, switch to Keep Warm when done, and have multiple safety systems to prevent accidents.

**Can an electric pressure cooker replace my slow cooker?**
Most electric pressure cookers include a Slow Cook mode, so yes — you can sell or donate your old slow cooker. However, dedicated slow cookers are generally cheaper if that's all you need.

**Why is my Instant Pot not coming to pressure?**
The most common cause is an insufficient liquid level (most require at least 1 cup of liquid) or a sealing ring that isn't seated properly. Check that the steam release valve is set to "Sealing" rather than "Venting."
