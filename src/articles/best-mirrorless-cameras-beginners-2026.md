## Kit Lens vs Body Only

Most beginners should buy the kit lens bundle (typically a 16–50mm or 18–55mm zoom) unless they already have a specific lens in mind. Kit lenses offer:
- Good sharpness for learning composition
- Useful zoom range for portraits, landscapes, and travel
- Lower total cost vs buying body + prime lens separately

If you shoot mostly video or portraits, consider adding a 35mm f/1.8 prime — it's faster in low light and produces the "cinematic look."

## FAQ

**What is the best beginner camera under $1,000?**
The Sony ZV-E10 II for video-first creators, the Canon EOS R50 for photography-first beginners.

**Should a beginner buy a mirrorless or DSLR?**
Mirrorless in 2026 — DSLRs are no longer being developed. Mirrorless autofocus, video, and future lens support are all superior.

**What lens should a beginner buy first?**
Start with the kit zoom (16–50mm or 18–55mm). Next buy: a 35mm or 50mm prime for low-light portraits.

Canon is more beginner-friendly (guided interface, simpler menus). Sony is better for video and autofocus performance.
