## What to Look for in a Portable Solar Charger

### Real-World Wattage (Not Rated Wattage)
Manufacturers rate panels under ideal lab conditions (perpendicular to direct noon sun, 77°F). Real-world output is typically 70–80% of rated wattage in optimal conditions. A 28W panel delivers about 20–23W in real use. Use this rule when comparing specs.

### Panel Type
- **Monocrystalline:** More efficient (15–22%), better in low light, standard for quality portable panels
- **Polycrystalline:** Less efficient (13–16%), cheaper, fine for casual use
- **Amorphous:** Most flexible, least efficient (7–10%), mostly found in specialized products

### Output Ports
- **USB-A + Smart IC:** Sufficient for phones and small devices
- **USB-C Power Delivery:** Required for laptops and modern iPads
- **DC output:** Required for power stations and 12V devices

### Weight and Fold Size
The trade-off is always wattage vs. portability:
- Under 15W: Ultra-compact, fits in a jacket pocket, charges phones slowly
- 20–28W: Standard backpacking size, charges phones at roughly wall-speed in full sun
- 40–60W: Camp/overlanding scale, handles laptops and power stations

### Durability
Look for IPX4 or higher water resistance. Panel stitching and kickstand hinges are the typical failure points — read user reviews for long-term durability reports.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a solar charger charge my laptop directly?**
A: Only panels with USB-C PD (Power Delivery) output at 45W+ can charge most laptops directly. The Goal Zero Nomad 50 with USB-C PD works for many ultrabooks. For most laptops, a solar panel → power station → laptop is more practical.

**Q: How much solar do I need to charge a phone per day?**
A: A 10,000mAh phone needs about 18Wh to fully charge. A 20W panel in 2 hours of direct sun delivers ~28Wh — enough for a full charge plus some power bank top-off. Plan for 2–3 hours of good sun exposure per phone charge.

**Q: Do solar panels work on cloudy days?**
A: Yes, but at 10–25% of rated output. Diffuse cloud cover is not zero — it's just much slower. Plan for reduced charging in overcast conditions and supplement with a power bank.

**Q: How do I clean solar panels in the field?**
A: Wipe with a damp cloth when the panel is cool. Dust and smudges reduce output measurably. No soap or chemical cleaners needed.
