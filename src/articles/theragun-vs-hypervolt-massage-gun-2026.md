## Amplitude and Percussion Depth

**Theragun** uses a 16mm amplitude — the distance the head travels with each stroke. This deeper penetration reaches fascia and muscle tissue that shallower devices can't access. For tight glutes, IT bands, and dense back muscles, Theragun's depth makes a real difference.

**Hypervolt 2** uses a 12mm amplitude. This is still effective for general muscle tension and post-workout recovery. For average users who experience typical soreness and tightness, 12mm is sufficient. For serious athletes or chronic knot sufferers, the shallower stroke may not fully release deeper tissue.

**Winner:** Theragun for deep tissue work. Hypervolt adequate for most everyday users.

## Noise Level

**Hypervolt** is significantly quieter. The "Quiet Glide" technology dampens percussive noise to ~45–55 dB, roughly equivalent to a normal conversation. You can use it in a bedroom without waking others.

**Theragun** runs louder (65–75 dB range). The motor power required for 16mm amplitude generates more sound. It's not unbearably loud, but it's noticeable in quiet rooms.

**Winner:** Hypervolt — by a wide margin.

## Battery Life

**Hypervolt 2** provides roughly 3 hours of continuous use. **Theragun Prime** offers around 2 hours. For a typical 10–20 minute recovery session, both are adequate between charges.

**Winner:** Hypervolt for longer battery life.
