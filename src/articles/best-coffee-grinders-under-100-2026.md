## Your Morning Coffee Deserves Better Than a Blade Grinder

Pre-ground coffee starts going stale within 15 minutes of grinding. Blade grinders produce inconsistent particle sizes that brew unevenly. If you're serious about your morning cup — or even just serious about not drinking mediocre coffee — a burr grinder is the single best investment you can make.

The good news: you don't need to spend $200+ to get exceptional results. These five grinders under $100 represent the best value available in 2026 for home brewers across every method.

---

## Burr vs. Blade: Why It Matters

**Blade grinders** work like a blender — a blade spins and randomly chops beans. The result is a mix of powder-fine dust and large chunks. When you brew, the dust over-extracts (bitter) while the chunks under-extract (sour). You get both in every cup.

**Burr grinders** crush beans between two abrasive surfaces set a precise distance apart. Every particle comes out the same size, which means every particle extracts at the same rate. The result is cleaner, sweeter, more complex coffee.

Even a budget conical burr grinder at $50–$60 will outperform a $30 blade grinder in cup quality every time.
