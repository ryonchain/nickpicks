## What to Look For in a Tire Inflator

**Auto-shutoff:** The single most important feature. An auto-shutoff inflator stops automatically when target pressure is reached, preventing over-inflation (which can damage tires or cause blowouts). All picks above include this feature.

**PSI capacity:** Standard car tires require 30–35 PSI. Truck tires may need 40–45 PSI. 100 PSI capacity is sufficient for cars; look for 150 PSI if you drive a truck or want to handle construction equipment.

**Corded vs. cordless:** Corded (12V or AC) units inflate faster due to higher-powered motors. Cordless units are more convenient and don't require the car engine running. For emergency kits, a self-contained cordless unit provides more flexibility.

**Gauge accuracy:** Look for stated accuracy of ±1.5 PSI or better. Cheap inflators with ±3 PSI accuracy can leave tires significantly under or over-inflated.
