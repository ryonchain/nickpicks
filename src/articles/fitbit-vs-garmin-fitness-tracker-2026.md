Fitbit and Garmin both make excellent fitness trackers. They're not competing for the same person.

Garmin builds tools for athletes who want training data. Fitbit builds health monitors for everyday users who want insights without overwhelming detail.

Figure out which describes you, and the choice makes itself.

## Quick Comparison

| | Fitbit (Charge 6) | Garmin (Forerunner 255) |
|---|---|---|
| Target user | Health-conscious everyday user | Runners, triathletes, athletes |
| GPS | Built-in | Built-in + multi-band |
| Battery | 7 days | 14 days |
| Heart rate zones | 6 zones | 5 customizable zones |

## Where Fitbit Wins

### Everyday Health Monitoring
Fitbit's core strength is accessibility. Sleep scores, daily readiness, Active Zone Minutes, and stress tracking through EDA (electrodermal activity) sensors are presented in a clean, actionable format.

If you want to know "should I work out today or rest?" Fitbit's Daily Readiness Score answers that simply. Garmin answers the same question with more data, which requires more interpretation.

### EDA Stress Sensor
No Garmin has an EDA sensor. Fitbit's electrodermal activity sensor measures your body's stress response through electrical skin conductance. The Stress Management Score is a genuine differentiator for people who care about stress physiology.

### Google Ecosystem Integration
Since Google acquired Fitbit, the integration with Google Maps, Google Pay, and Spotify has improved substantially. If you use Android, Google's ecosystem ties together in ways that Garmin's app doesn't.

### Price
The Fitbit Charge 6 at $150 vs. the Garmin Forerunner 255 at $250–300 is a real $100+ difference. For casual users who want health tracking without training analytics, paying more for Garmin's running features makes no sense.

---

## Where Garmin Wins

### Running and Athletic Training
Garmin's training load analysis, VO2 max tracking, race predictions, suggested workouts, and recovery advisor are unmatched at any price point. The Forerunner 255 generates training plans based on your fitness level and goals.

Fitbit tracks runs. Garmin coaches them.

### Battery Life
Garmin Forerunner 255: 14 days in smartwatch mode.  
Fitbit Charge 6: 7 days.

For athletes who sleep with their trackers to capture HRV and go straight into training, 14 days means fewer charging disruptions. For everyday users, 7 days is plenty.

### GPS Accuracy
Garmin uses multi-band GPS on higher-end models. In real-world tests in cities (tree cover, tall buildings), Garmin's GPS routing is measurably more accurate than Fitbit's. For running routes that matter, this is significant.

### Durability
Garmin devices are built for outdoor punishment. Military-grade durability standards, deeper water resistance, and better thermal tolerance. The Forerunner 255 withstands conditions the Charge 6 doesn't.

### Long-Term Data
Garmin Connect stores lifetime training history with detailed charts. Serious athletes reviewing 2 years of training data get more from Garmin's platform.

## Head-to-Head: Specific Use Cases

**Couch to 5K training:** Fitbit. The guided breathing, Active Zone Minutes, and sleep readiness scores are motivating without overwhelming.

**Half marathon training plan:** Garmin. Adaptive training load, pace alerts, race prediction tools.

**Sleep optimization:** Tie. Both provide sleep stages and HRV. Fitbit's EDA sensor adds stress context Garmin lacks.

**Business meetings (smartwatch style):** Garmin Venu 3 > Fitbit Charge 6 on aesthetics. The Charge 6 looks like a fitness band.

**Weight loss tracking:** Fitbit. The food logging integration, Active Zone calorie tracking, and Daily Readiness scores are calibrated for this use case.

**Triathlon / multi-sport:** Garmin (no contest). Fitbit doesn't have triathlon mode.

## Budget Picks

**Fitbit Inspire 3 (~$100):** Entry-level Fitbit with sleep tracking, SpO2, stress monitoring. No built-in GPS. Best for light tracking.

**Garmin Forerunner 55 (~$200):** Entry-level running GPS watch. No music storage, fewer metrics, but full GPS running analytics in Garmin's ecosystem.

---

## The Subscription Question

**Fitbit Premium:** $10/month or $80/year. Required for Daily Readiness Score, sleep analysis details, and mindfulness programs. Without Premium, Fitbit is significantly less useful. Factor this into the total cost.

**Garmin Connect:** Free. All Garmin's training analytics, body battery, sleep data, and coaching features are free. This is a meaningful advantage.

---

## Who Should Buy Each

**Buy Fitbit (Charge 6) if:**
- You want health tracking without training complexity
- You're focused on sleep, stress, and daily readiness
- You use Android and want Google ecosystem integration
- Budget is under $200 and you don't need advanced running metrics

**Buy Garmin (Forerunner 255) if:**
- You run, cycle, swim, or train regularly
- You want coaching, not just tracking
- Battery life matters (2x longer than Fitbit)
- You want a subscription-free platform with complete data access

**Buy Garmin Venu 3 if:**
- You want Garmin's training features with a smartwatch aesthetic
- You want more smartwatch apps alongside fitness tracking

---

## Bottom Line

Fitbit is a health device. Garmin is a training device. The best one for you is the one that matches how you actually use it.

If the gym is your main activity and you don't analyze your training, Fitbit. If you run races, track sports, or want adaptive training guidance, Garmin. Buying the wrong one means paying for features you'll never use.
