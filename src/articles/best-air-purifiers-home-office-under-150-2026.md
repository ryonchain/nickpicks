## What to Look for in a Home Office Air Purifier

### True HEPA vs. "HEPA-type" Filters
True HEPA filters capture 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger — including dust mite allergens, pet dander, and most mold spores. "HEPA-type" filters are cheaper knockoffs that capture far less. Only buy True HEPA.

### CADR Rating
Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR) measures how fast the purifier cleans air. For a 150–200 sq ft home office, target a CADR of at least 100–150 CFM. Bigger rooms need proportionally higher CADR.

### Noise Level
You'll be on Zoom calls. Look for units with a low fan speed under 35 dB — whisper-quiet mode that runs continuously without interrupting video calls.

### Smart Features vs. Basic
Smart purifiers connect to your phone, show real-time AQI readings, and auto-adjust fan speed. Nice-to-have but not required. Basic units with manual controls work just as well for most home offices.

## Do You Actually Need an Air Purifier?

Signs you'd benefit from one:
- You notice dust accumulating on your monitor or desk frequently
- You work in a basement or room with limited ventilation
- You have allergies, asthma, or chemical sensitivities
- You live in an area with poor outdoor air quality (wildfire smoke, urban pollution)
- You have pets that shed or produce dander

If none of these apply, simply opening a window during good air quality days might be enough.

---

## Maintenance: Don't Skip Filter Changes

Even the best air purifier becomes useless with a clogged filter. Most True HEPA filters need replacement every 6–12 months depending on usage. Pre-filters should be cleaned monthly. Set a calendar reminder — it takes 3 minutes and makes a real difference in performance.
