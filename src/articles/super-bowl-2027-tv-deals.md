## Why Super Bowl Week Is Good for TV Buyers

- **Model year changeover:** January–February is when 2027 models start arriving. Retailers need to clear 2026 inventory.
- **Competing promotions:** Best Buy, Amazon, Walmart, Costco, and Target all run simultaneous sales. Price competition is real.
- **Bundle incentives:** TV + soundbar bundles are common. The soundbar is often worth the bundle price alone.
- **Big-screen demand:** Consumers actively shopping for 65"+ screens drives retailer motivation to discount.

## Super Bowl TV Sizing Guide

| Room Width | Viewing Distance | Recommended Screen Size |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Small living room | 6–8 feet | 55" |
| Standard living room | 8–10 feet | 65"–70" |
| Large living room | 10–13 feet | 75"–77" |
| Open plan / party setup | 13+ feet | 85"–86" |

The most common upgrade is from 55" to 65". If you're hosting a Super Bowl party, prioritize size over panel quality at equivalent prices.

## Super Bowl TV Buying Strategy

1. **Set your budget first** — Dealers use Super Bowl excitement to upsell. Know your ceiling.
2. **65" is the standard** — Going 75"+ has real diminishing returns in average-sized rooms.
3. **OLED vs QLED** — OLED wins on contrast and color accuracy. QLED wins on peak brightness for bright rooms. For a typical evening Super Bowl viewing room, either works.
4. **Check bundle deals** — TV + soundbar bundles frequently save $150–$300 on the soundbar.
5. **Don't buy a TV on game day** — You're not getting it in time. Buy January 26–February 3.
6. **Confirm viewing angle** — QN90D handles off-axis viewing; LG C4 doesn't (OLED) — know where guests will sit.
