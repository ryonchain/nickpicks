## How to Buy Luxury Shoes Wisely

### Buy for longevity, not trend
Classic silhouettes (pumps, loafers, pointed-toes) outlast seasonal trends by decades. Avoid novelty designs unless you can see yourself wearing them in 10 years.

### Fit is everything
Many luxury shoes run narrow or require a half-size up. Read brand-specific sizing guides and factor in break-in time (most luxury leather shoes require 10–15 wears to conform to your foot).

### Resale value considerations
Iconic styles from Louboutin, Manolo Blahnik, and Gucci hold 40–70% of retail value in good condition. Buy with the knowledge that if they don't work for you, they're resalable.

### Authentication when buying pre-owned
For pre-owned luxury shoes, buy through platforms with authentication services (The RealReal, Vestiaire Collective, Fashionphile) rather than unverified sellers.

## FAQ

**Q: Are luxury shoes worth the investment?**
A: For specific categories (classic heels, quality leather loafers), yes — they outlast fast fashion alternatives by years and hold resale value. For trendy designs, less so.

**Q: How do I care for luxury leather shoes?**
A: Rotate wear (rest shoes 24+ hours between wears), use cedar shoe trees, apply leather conditioner every few months, and have them resoled by a cobbler when the sole wears thin.

**Q: What size should I order in Louboutins?**
A: Generally, size up half a size to a full size. The pointed toe is narrow. If between sizes, go up.
