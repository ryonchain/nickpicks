## Face Oil Science: Why They Work

### Oils vs. Moisturizers: Different Mechanisms
Moisturizers (humectants + emollients + occlusives) add and trap water in skin. Oils work differently:

- **Barrier repair:** Certain oils mimic the skin's own lipids (ceramides, fatty acids), repairing microscopic barrier gaps that cause dehydration and sensitivity
- **Antioxidant delivery:** Oil-soluble antioxidants (vitamin E, beta-carotene, polyphenols) penetrate more efficiently in lipid carrier than water-based serums
- **Sebum regulation:** Counterintuitively, non-comedogenic oils can reduce excess oil production by signaling to skin that its lipid barrier is intact
- **Luminosity:** Oils optically smooth the skin surface, reflecting light more uniformly

### The Comedogenic Scale
Not all oils clog pores equally. Key guidance:
- **Non-comedogenic (0–1):** Squalane, rosehip seed, argan, camellia — safe for oily and acne-prone skin
- **Moderate (2):** Jojoba, marula — fine for most skin, test for acne-prone
- **Higher risk (3–5):** Coconut, palm, wheat germ — typically inappropriate for face use

## How to Layer Face Oils

**Rule:** Apply oils after water-based serums, before or instead of moisturizer (depending on skin type).

**Oily skin:** Apply 3–4 drops after serum, skip moisturizer. Squalane or rosehip only.

**Normal/combination:** Apply 3–4 drops after serum, mix with moisturizer or apply before.

**Dry skin:** Apply liberally as the final skincare step, sealing all previous layers.

**Under makeup:** Squalane only — other oils may affect makeup wear time.
