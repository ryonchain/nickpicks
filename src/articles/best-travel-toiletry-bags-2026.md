## Minimalist Toiletry Packing System

**Carry-on travel (TSA 3-1-1):**
- All liquids in 3.4 oz or smaller containers
- All liquid containers in one clear quart-size bag
- Eliminate: full-size shampoo/conditioner (hotel or solid bars)
- Keep: medications, contact lens solution, essentials only

**Checked bag travel:**
- Full-size products allowed
- Use hanging organizer for hotel use convenience
- Pack toiletry bag last (on top) for quick access on arrival

**Minimalist product selection:** Solid shampoo bars, solid conditioner bars, and 2-in-1 products reduce liquid count significantly — useful for strict carry-on travel.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size toiletry bag do I need for travel?**
For carry-on only travel, choose a bag that fits the TSA 3-1-1 liquid rule: all liquids in 3.4 oz (100ml) or smaller containers, all containers fitting in a single 1-quart clear zip-top bag. A dedicated clear TSA bag fits inside most toiletry organizers. For checked baggage travel, full-size hanging organizers (11–13 in tall when hanging) accommodate full-size products.

**What is the benefit of a hanging toiletry bag?**
Hanging toiletry bags have a hook that lets you hang them on a towel bar or bathroom door hook, giving you full access to all compartments without setting the bag on a potentially wet counter. This is particularly useful in small hotel bathrooms where counter space is limited, and it keeps the bag contents dry and accessible.

**Are clear toiletry bags required for airport security?**
Only the inner bag containing liquids needs to be clear (the TSA 3-1-1 quart-size bag). Your overall toiletry bag does not need to be clear. At the security checkpoint, remove the clear liquid bag from your toiletry bag and place it in the bin separately. Many toiletry bags include a TSA-compliant clear liquid pouch.

**Can a toiletry bag go in the washing machine?**
Many nylon and polyester toiletry bags are machine-washable on a gentle cycle with cold water. Open all compartments and zippers before washing to allow thorough cleaning and faster drying. Canvas and fabric-lined bags are generally machine-safe; leather and faux-leather bags should be hand-wiped only. Check the manufacturer's care instructions before washing.
