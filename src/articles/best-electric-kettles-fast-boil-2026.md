## Buying Guide: How to Choose an Electric Kettle

**Do you need temperature control?** If you drink green or white tea (needs 160–175°F), pour-over coffee (195–205°F), or oolong (185°F), yes. Boiling water (212°F) scorches delicate teas and slightly over-extracts pour-over. If you drink black tea or French press coffee, a standard boil-only kettle works fine.

**Gooseneck vs. standard spout:** Gooseneck spouts are for pour-over and V60 coffee where you need precise, controlled pouring in slow circles. For everything else, a standard spout is faster and more practical.

**Capacity:** 1L–1.7L covers most household needs. 0.9L (Fellow Stagg) is enough for 1–2 cups. 1.7L fills a standard teapot.

**Keep warm duration:** 20–30 minutes is standard. If you frequently need hot water over extended periods (tea ceremonies, slow mornings), prioritize the Fellow's 60-minute hold.

## FAQ

**What temperature should tea be brewed at?**
White tea: 160–170°F. Green tea: 170–180°F. Oolong: 185–195°F. Black tea: 200–212°F. Herbal tea: 212°F (full boil). Using too-hot water on green and white teas creates bitterness — temperature control matters.

**Are electric kettles faster than stovetop?**
Yes, significantly. A 1500W electric kettle brings 1L to boil in approximately 4–5 minutes. A stovetop burner at equivalent wattage takes 8–10 minutes. Electric kettles are more energy-efficient for small volumes as well.

**Is stainless steel or plastic kettle better?**
Stainless steel interiors are preferred — they don't absorb odors, don't leach plasticizers, and last longer. All five kettles on this list have stainless steel interior water contact surfaces.

**How do I descale an electric kettle?**
Fill the kettle halfway with a 50/50 white vinegar and water mixture. Boil the mixture, let it sit for 30 minutes, then empty and rinse several times. For heavy mineral buildup, repeat. Descale every 1–3 months depending on water hardness.
