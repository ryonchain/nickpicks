## What to Look For

### Range: Marketing vs. Reality

Always cut claimed range by 20–30% for real-world estimate:
- 220 lb rider
- Mixed terrain (not just flat)
- Some hills
- 50–70°F weather

A scooter claiming "40-mile range" realistically delivers 25–32 miles for most riders.

### Motor Power
- **250W:** Light rider, flat terrain only
- **350–500W:** Most commuters, moderate hills
- **500–700W:** Heavier riders, regular hills, or performance preference

### Tire Type
- **Pneumatic (air-filled):** Best ride quality, handles bumps well. Risk of puncture (self-sealing versions mitigate this).
- **Solid/foam-filled:** Flat-proof, but transmits more vibration.

### Braking Systems
- **Electronic regenerative:** Standard on most scooters. Adequate for gradual stops.
- **Mechanical disc brakes:** Significantly stronger, essential for speeds above 20 mph or steep hills.
- **Dual braking (electronic + disc):** Best safety redundancy.

### Folding & Weight
Under 30 lbs: easy to carry upstairs, on transit
30–40 lbs: manageable but noticeable
40+ lbs: effectively stationary for most users

## Legal Considerations (US)

Electric scooter regulations vary by state and city. In general:
- Most states limit street-legal e-scooters to 20–25 mph
- Helmet laws vary — check local regulations
- Some cities restrict sidewalk use; bike lane rules vary
- Always check your local ordinances before purchase
