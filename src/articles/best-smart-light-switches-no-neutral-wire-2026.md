## The Neutral Wire Problem (And How to Solve It)

Most smart switches require a neutral wire (white wire in a standard US electrical box) to maintain a trickle of power to the switch electronics when the circuit is "off." Older homes — especially those built before the 1980s — frequently lack a neutral wire at the switch box. Before buying, check your switch box for a white wire connected to the switch (not just passing through). If there's no neutral wire, you have three options:

1. **No-neutral switches:** Lutron Caseta, GE Cync No-Neutral, and some Leviton models are engineered to work without a neutral wire.
2. **Add a neutral wire:** An electrician can run a neutral wire to the switch box, typically for $100–$200 per switch location.
3. **Smart bulbs instead of smart switches:** Eliminates the switch wiring issue entirely but requires leaving physical switches on always.

The smart switch category has matured enough that no-neutral options no longer require significant performance trade-offs.

## Installation Tips and Common Mistakes

**Turn off the circuit breaker first.** Not the wall switch — the breaker. Smart switch installation requires touching live wires if you don't cut power at the breaker. Verify the circuit is off with a non-contact voltage tester ($15) before touching any wires.

**Identify your wiring before ordering.** Take a photo of your existing switch wiring before disconnecting anything. Common configurations: single-pole (one switch, one location), 3-way (two switches for one light), and 4-way (three or more switches). Some smart switches don't support 3-way without additional hardware.

**LED compatibility.** Smart dimmers and LED bulbs don't always get along. Low-cost LED bulbs sometimes flicker or buzz at dimmed settings. Lutron maintains a compatibility list of tested LED bulbs at their website. When in doubt, use bulbs from the compatibility list.

**Wi-Fi vs. Z-Wave vs. Clear Connect:** Wi-Fi switches are the easiest to set up but add load to your Wi-Fi network (each switch is a connected device) and can have reliability issues in crowded environments. Z-Wave and Zigbee require a hub but are purpose-built for smart home reliability. Lutron Clear Connect uses a dedicated 434 MHz channel that avoids all Wi-Fi interference entirely.

**Professional installation.** If you're unsure about any wiring, hire an electrician. A professional installation of a smart switch costs $50–$100 and eliminates the risk of incorrect wiring that can damage the switch, the fixture, or create a fire hazard.
