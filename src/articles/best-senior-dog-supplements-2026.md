## Observational Checklist for Senior Dogs

Use this to track improvement after starting supplements (score 1–5, re-evaluate after 6 weeks):

**Mobility:**
- Getting up from lying position
- Climbing stairs
- Walking distance before tiring
- Reluctance to jump

**Cognitive:**
- Recognizes family members
- Responds to name
- Sleeps normal hours (not day/night reversal)
- Doesn't get "lost" in familiar spaces

**General vitality:**
- Appetite and food enthusiasm
- Coat quality
- Eye clarity
- Interaction and play interest

## What to Discuss with Your Vet

Before starting a senior supplement regimen, ask about:
- **Baseline bloodwork:** Kidney, liver, and thyroid panels help identify specific needs
- **Medication interactions:** NSAIDs, phenobarbital, and heart medications can interact with supplements
- **Weight management:** Many senior dogs are overweight, which accelerates joint disease
- **Dental health:** Affects nutrient absorption and overall health
