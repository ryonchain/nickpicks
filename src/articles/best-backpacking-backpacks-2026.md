## Backpacking Packs in 2026: Fit Is Everything

A backpacking pack is the most personal gear purchase you'll make — more than boots, more than sleeping bags. A pack with poor fit causes pain, slows you down, and ruins the trip. A pack with excellent fit disappears on your back. Getting this decision right matters.

The good news: the three dominant brands — Osprey, Gregory, and Deuter — all make excellent packs in the $200–$350 range. The differences are in fit philosophy, load transfer systems, and organizational design. Getting the right fit requires trying packs in-store or committing to a brand's fitting methodology.

**Quick verdict:** The **Osprey Atmos AG 65** (~$270) is the best all-around backpacking pack for most people — the Anti-Gravity suspension system distributes load better than anything at this price, and Osprey's "All Mighty Guarantee" replaces any pack they've ever made, for any reason, for life. For heavier loads (35+ lbs), the **Gregory Baltoro 65** (~$330) transfers weight to your hips more efficiently. For ultralight backpacking, the **Hyperlite Mountain Gear 3400** (~$325) at 26 oz is in a different category.

## Backpack Buying Guide

**Volume:** Match volume to trip length. 45–55L suits weekend trips (2–3 nights). 65L handles 4–7 nights. 70L+ is for extended expeditions or winter camping with large gear. Avoid oversizing — extra volume leads to overpacking.

**Fit:** Measure your torso length (base of neck to top of hip bone), not your height. Most brands offer Small (15–17"), Medium (18–20"), and Large (21"+) torso sizes. Width and hipbelt size are separate measurements.

**Suspension:** Anti-gravity/mesh systems (Osprey AG, Deuter Aircontact) = best ventilation but less rigid load transfer. Framesheet + straight foam pad = better heavy-load transfer but hotter back. Frameless = ultralight but requires low total pack weight.

**Volume vs. total weight:** Focus on total pack weight, not pack weight alone. A 3-lb ultralight pack loaded with heavy conventional gear doesn't save you anything.
