## What to Look for in a Walking Shoe

**Cushioning:** More is generally better for all-day walking — look for brands that use proprietary foam (Fresh Foam, HOKA's EVA, CloudTec). Cheaper EVA compresses and loses cushion within 300–500 miles.

**Toe box width:** Your foot spreads during walking. A toe box that's too narrow creates blisters and bunion pressure. Many women do well in "wide" (D) or even 2E widths.

**Heel drop:** This is the height difference between heel and toe. High drop (8–12mm) suits heel strikers (most walkers). Low drop (0–4mm) suits midfoot strikers and people building toward barefoot-style walking.

**Arch support:** Unless you have flat feet or a specific condition, most people are fine with the standard insole. Add aftermarket insoles (Superfeet, Powerstep) if you need extra arch support.

## Breaking In New Shoes

Even good walking shoes need 1–2 weeks of gradual wear. Start with 30–60 minute sessions before committing to all-day wear. Blisters in the first week are normal; blisters after two weeks signal a fit issue.
