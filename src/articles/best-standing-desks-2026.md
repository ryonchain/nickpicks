## Standing Desk Buying Guide

### Stability: The Most Important Spec
Wobble at standing height (highest extension) is the #1 complaint among standing desk owners. Key factors:
- **Dual motors** are more stable than single
- **Oval-profile legs** reduce wobble vs square-profile
- **BIFMA certification** signals commercially-tested stability
- **Weight capacity** — stay below 60% capacity for stable operation

### Height Range Calculation
To find your ideal standing height: measure your elbow height while standing (arms at 90°). That's your desktop target. For sitting: same measurement seated. Verify the desk's range covers both before purchasing.

- Average sitting height: 26"–29"
- Average standing height: 38"–44"
- Tall users (6'2"+): need 48"+ max
- Short users (5'2"–): need 24"–26" min

### Weight Capacity Reality Check
Most desks list max weight capacity, but real-world stability degrades above 60% of rated capacity. For dual monitor setups:
- 27" monitor: ~15–20 lbs each
- Monitor arm: 15–25 lbs
- Laptop: 4–7 lbs
- Accessories: 5–15 lbs
- Total typical setup: 50–80 lbs — well within 176+ lb desks

The weight limit matters more for security than everyday stability, unless you have a very heavy setup.
