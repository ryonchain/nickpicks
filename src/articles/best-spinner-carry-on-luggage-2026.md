## Spinner Carry-On Buying Guide

### What makes a spinner wheel good?

The critical component is the bearing. Cheap spinners use plain bushings — metal-on-metal contact — that develop friction and resistance within months. Quality spinners use precision ball bearings, sometimes called Japanese Hinomoto bearings after the dominant manufacturer. Ball bearings reduce friction dramatically and maintain smooth rotation for years.

Wheel housing design also matters. Recessed wheel housings (where the wheels sit slightly inside the bag profile) protect the wheels during rough handling. Protruding wheels are more vulnerable to being snapped off.

**Signs of quality spinner wheels:**
- Double-wheel design (two small wheels per corner vs. one large wheel)
- Recessed housing
- Precision bearing mechanism
- Quiet roll (no rattling or grinding sound on hard floors)

### Hardside vs. softside spinner: the real tradeoffs

| Factor | Hardside | Softside |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Contents protection | Better | Adequate |
| Weight | Usually heavier (by 0.5–2 lbs) | Lighter |
| Flexibility | None | Can squeeze into tight overhead bins |
| Weather resistance | Excellent | Good (if water-resistant fabric) |
| Overpacking | Not possible | Can expand 10–15% |
| External pockets | Rare | Common |

### Airline size restrictions

Most US domestic carriers accept 22 x 14 x 9 inches as the standard carry-on maximum. Spirit Airlines and Frontier Airlines enforce this most strictly. For international travel:

- **Air France/KLM:** 21.5 x 13.5 x 8 inches max
- **Ryanair:** 21.5 x 15.5 x 7.5 inches max  
- **EasyJet:** 22 x 17.5 x 9.5 inches max

If you frequently fly European budget carriers, verify the dimensions of any bag against that carrier's current policy before purchasing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between a 2-wheel and 4-wheel carry-on?**
Two-wheel luggage must be tilted and pulled behind you. Four-wheel spinner luggage rolls in any direction upright, letting you push it alongside you. Spinners are easier on crowded airports but can roll away on inclined surfaces.

**Do spinner wheels hold up in checked luggage?**
Spinner wheels are more vulnerable than 2-wheel designs because they stick out from the bag and can be broken off by rough baggage handling. Quality spinners use double-wheel axle systems and recessed housings to protect against this.

**What carry-on size fits in most airline overhead bins?**
The most universally accepted size is 22 x 14 x 9 inches. This fits in most domestic US carriers including Spirit and Frontier. International airlines may have tighter restrictions.

**Are hardside spinners better than softside spinners?**
Hardside spinners protect contents better from crushing and are more water-resistant. Softside spinners flex to fit in overhead bins when full and are typically lighter. For carry-ons, the choice comes down to whether you prefer rigid protection or overpacking flexibility.
