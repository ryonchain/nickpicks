## Eye Area Anatomy: Why It Requires Special Products

### Structural Differences from Facial Skin
- **Thickness:** Under-eye skin is 0.5mm thick (compared to 2mm on the cheeks). Every fine line and structural change is more visible here.
- **Fat pad movement:** Under-eye puffiness is partly caused by orbital fat that shifts forward during sleep due to fluid redistribution. Morning puffiness is normal; persistent puffiness indicates chronic inflammation or genetic fat prolapse.
- **Vascular visibility:** Dark circles are often shadows from vascular structures visible through thin, translucent skin rather than pigmentation deposits.
- **Lymphatic drainage:** Under-eye puffiness responds to lymphatic drainage techniques (jade rollers, gua sha, gentle tapping) precisely because the lymphatic system drains poorly during horizontal sleep.

### Dark Circle Causes: Different Problems, Different Solutions
**Vascular dark circles (most common):** Blue/purple tint from veins visible through thin skin. Treated with: caffeine (vasoconstriction), vitamin K, retinol (thickens skin over time).

**Pigmentation dark circles:** Brown tint from hyperpigmentation. Common in deeper skin tones. Treated with: vitamin C, niacinamide, kojic acid.

**Shadow dark circles:** Hollowness/sunken eye creating a shadow. Structure problem, not pigmentation. Treated with: hyaluronic acid (temporary plump), or filler (clinical).

## Eye Product Application Protocol

**Application technique (critical):** Always use the ring finger (least pressure of any finger). Tap, never rub. Apply to orbital bone, not directly on lashes.

**Amount:** Less than you think. A rice-grain sized amount per eye is correct for most formulas.

**Layering order:** Eye cream goes after serum, before moisturizer, and before eye patches (use patches on bare skin for maximum adhesion and penetration).

**Eye patch timing:** Apply after cleansing, before any other products. Leave on 10–20 minutes, pat in remaining serum rather than wiping off.

**Morning vs. night:**
- AM: Caffeine-containing creams for puffiness reduction
- PM: Retinol-containing creams (never use retinol under daytime UV exposure)
- Patches: Can be used AM or PM depending on formula

## Complement Your Eye Routine

**Jade roller or gua sha:** Lymphatic drainage in 2 minutes. Roll outward from nose to temples, gently, 5 passes each side. Reduces morning puffiness physically.

**Cold spoon or ice roller:** Vasoconstriction reduces puffiness rapidly. 30 seconds per eye, press gently.

**Sleep elevation:** Elevating the head 10–15 degrees during sleep reduces overnight fluid accumulation under the eyes significantly.
