## What to Look For

**Frame:** Powder-coated aluminum is the gold standard — lightweight, rust-proof, doesn't corrode. Steel costs less but needs more care. Avoid wrought iron for sectionals; it's too heavy to rearrange.

**Cushions:** Sunbrella is the fabric benchmark for outdoor cushions. Olefin and Nuvella are strong alternatives. Avoid polyester-only cushions in wet climates — they become mildew factories. Removable, washable covers are non-negotiable.

**Weave:** Synthetic PE rattan (all-weather wicker) won't deteriorate in sun and rain. Natural rattan belongs indoors. Check that the weave is tight and consistent — gaps or loose sections indicate lower quality.

**Modular vs. fixed:** Modular sets let you reconfigure. Fixed sets are typically more structurally robust. If your patio layout might change, go modular.

## Size Planning: How Much Furniture Do You Actually Need?

| Patio Size | Recommended Set Size |
|---|---|
| Under 150 sq ft (apartment balcony) | 4-piece conversation set |
| 150–250 sq ft (small patio) | 5–6 piece set |
| 250–400 sq ft (medium patio/deck) | 7–8 piece sectional |
| 400+ sq ft (large patio/backyard) | 9–12 piece set or multiple zones |

Leave at least 18–24 inches of walkway clearance around seated furniture. Measure before buying — sectionals look smaller in product photos than in real life.
