## What to Look For in a Patio Furniture Set

**Frame material matters most.** Aluminum > steel for longevity outdoors. HDPE lumber is the best for pure weather resistance. Teak and other hardwoods are excellent but expensive. Avoid low-grade softwoods like pine unless you're ready to seal them every year.

**Cushion quality is underrated.** Cheap polyester cushions turn into mildew sponges. Look for Sunbrella, Olefin, or Nuvella fabrics — they're worth the premium. Ensure the cushion covers are removable and machine washable.

**Size your set to your actual space.** Measure your patio and leave at least 2 feet of walkway clearance around chairs. An oversized set will make your patio feel cramped.

**Consider how you'll store it.** Even the best outdoor furniture benefits from winter storage. If you have no garage or shed, prioritize materials (aluminum, HDPE, teak) that can stay out year-round with covers.
