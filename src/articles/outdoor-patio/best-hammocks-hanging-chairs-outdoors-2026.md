## Hammock Types: What's the Difference?

**Rope hammocks:** Traditional woven cotton or polyester net design. Classic look, breathable in heat, but the weave can leave impressions on skin and requires a supportive spreader bar for comfort.

**Brazilian/Mayan hammocks:** Tightly woven fabric with no spreader bar. Cocoons around you — extremely comfortable, but require more hanging height and take practice to get in and out of.

**Quilted/Fabric hammocks:** Solid fabric with pillow-style cushioning. The most comfortable option for extended lounging, but heavier and slower to dry when wet.

**Camping hammocks:** Lightweight nylon, packs small, sets up fast with straps. Designed for portability but works perfectly in a backyard.

**Hanging chairs:** Single-point suspension, upright seating. Ideal when you want the relaxation of swinging without committing to lying down.

## Installation Safety Notes

**Hammock straps:** Always use tree straps rated for more than your body weight — ideally 500+ lbs each. Never hang from ropes tied around trees without protective padding; it can damage trees and creates a failure point.

**Ceiling hooks:** Hanging chairs from a ceiling require the hook to be anchored into a ceiling joist, not just drywall. Use a stud finder and a hook rated for at least 500 lbs. For porches, find the structural beam.

**Angle matters:** The ideal hammock hang angle is 30° from horizontal for the straps/rope. Too steep and the hammock pulls tight and uncomfortable; too shallow and you're too close to the ground.
