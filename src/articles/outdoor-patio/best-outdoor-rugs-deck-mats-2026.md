## Outdoor Rug Materials: What to Know

**Polypropylene (olefin):** The gold standard for outdoor rugs. UV-resistant, water-resistant (dries quickly), mold- and mildew-resistant, easy to clean with a hose. Most quality outdoor rugs are polypropylene.

**Recycled plastic (PET):** Made from recycled plastic bottles. Extremely durable and fully waterproof. Texture can feel slightly stiffer than polypropylene but improves over time. Very eco-conscious choice.

**Natural fibers (jute, sisal, seagrass):** Beautiful natural look but can only work on covered porches where they'll never get truly wet. Moisture causes mold, mildew, and deterioration in natural fibers.

**Avoid indoors rugs outside:** Regular cotton or wool rugs will deteriorate rapidly and create mold/mildew issues on outdoor surfaces.

## Sizing Guide for Common Patio Setups

| Setup | Recommended Rug Size |
|---|---|
| Two chairs + small table (balcony/small patio) | 4x6 ft |
| 4-chair dining set | 6x9 ft |
| Large 6–8 chair dining set | 8x10 or 9x12 ft |
| L-shaped sectional sofa | 8x10 or 9x12 ft |
| Large multi-zone patio | Multiple rugs or 10x14 ft |

**Rule of thumb:** All four legs of your seating furniture should sit on the rug, or at minimum the front two legs. A rug that only covers the center of a furniture grouping looks disconnected and too small.

## Maintenance Tips

**Regular care:** Shake or sweep debris off weekly. Hose down monthly and allow to dry fully before placing back.

**Mold prevention:** Even polypropylene rugs can develop mold on their underside from deck moisture. Lift the rug every few weeks to air out the surface below.

**Winter storage:** Roll (don't fold) and store rolled horizontally in a dry location. Folding causes permanent creases in polypropylene weaves.
