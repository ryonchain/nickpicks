## Wood-Burning vs. Propane: The Real Trade-Off

**Wood-burning fire pits** produce genuine crackling fire with real heat output. They require wood sourcing, ash cleanup, and attention to fire management. They're also banned in some HOAs and during fire weather advisories.

**Propane fire pits and fire tables** ignite with the push of a button, have zero cleanup, produce zero ash, and can be turned off instantly. They produce less heat than wood fires and the flame looks slightly artificial but are vastly more convenient.

**Smokeless fire pits** use secondary combustion to burn off most of the smoke from wood fires — dramatically improving the experience of sitting around a wood fire.

## Fire Pit Safety Essentials

**Clearance:** 10 feet minimum from structures, overhanging branches, and fence lines. More is better.

**Never burn:** Treated wood, plywood, particle board, or garbage — they release toxic fumes. Dry hardwood (oak, hickory, cherry) burns cleanest.

**Keep nearby:** A bucket of water, garden hose, or fire extinguisher rated for Class A fires.

**Extinguish completely:** Let the fire burn down to embers, then douse with water and stir until the hissing stops. Never leave a fire unattended or assume embers are out.
