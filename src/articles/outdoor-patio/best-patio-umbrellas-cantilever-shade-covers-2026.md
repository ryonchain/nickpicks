## Market Umbrella vs. Cantilever: Which Do You Need?

**Market (center post) umbrellas** mount through a hole in your patio table or stand in a freestanding base. They're simpler, cheaper, and more wind-stable. The trade-off: the center pole takes up table space and limits where you can position the shade.

**Cantilever (offset) umbrellas** have a side post with an arm extending over the shaded area. You can shade a sofa, lounger, or dining set without a pole in the middle. They're more expensive, need a heavier base, and require more wind management, but the flexibility is genuinely useful.

## Umbrella Maintenance Tips

**Wind management:** Close your umbrella when wind picks up above 20 mph. Even "wind-resistant" umbrellas can fail, and an open umbrella in a storm becomes a sail that can tip or destroy the furniture underneath.

**End of season:** Store the canopy indoors. UV and moisture degrade fabric even when closed. Most quality umbrellas have removable canopies — unlatch the ribs and store the fabric dry.

**Mold on canopy:** Mix 1 cup bleach + ¼ cup mild soap in 1 gallon water. Scrub with a soft brush, rinse thoroughly, and air dry completely before storing. Sunbrella fabric handles this treatment well.
