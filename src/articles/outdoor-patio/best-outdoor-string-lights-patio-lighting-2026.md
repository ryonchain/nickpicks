## What Makes Outdoor String Lights Last

**IP Rating:** Look for IP44 (splash-resistant) at minimum; IP65 (water jet resistant) for climates with heavy rain. Most quality outdoor string lights sit at IP44 or IP65.

**Bulb type:**
- **Incandescent/Edison:** Warm, golden glow (2200–2700K). The "café light" look most people want. Bulbs burn out individually (replaceable on quality models).
- **LED:** Longer lifespan, lower power consumption. Can feel cooler/harsher unless specifically marked as "warm white" (2700K or lower).
- **Smart LED:** Color-changing, app-controlled. Govee and similar brands have made this accessible and reliable.

**Wire gauge:** Thinner wire (20-gauge) is fine for short runs. Commercial-grade options use 14–16 gauge for longer installations.

**Shatterproof vs. glass:** Commercial-grade string lights use shatterproof acrylic or plastic Edison bulbs. Home use can use glass, but plastic bulbs hold up better on windy patios.

## Installation Tips

**Suspension methods:**
- Overhead: run wire or metal cable between poles, hooks, or house/fence, then hang lights underneath
- Perimeter: wrap around fence posts or railings
- Catenary: true café style hangs in gentle curves between anchor points

**Hooks and poles:** Use heavy-duty outdoor screw hooks in wood surfaces. For brick or stucco, use concrete anchor screws. Freestanding posts let you create overhead coverage anywhere — look for weighted patio string light poles on Amazon.

**Extension cords:** Use only outdoor-rated extension cords (orange or yellow). Don't daisy-chain standard extension cords.
