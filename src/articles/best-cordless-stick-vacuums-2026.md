## Cordless Stick Vacuum Buying Guide

### Suction Power: What Numbers Mean
Manufacturers measure suction in AW (Air Watts), Kpa (kilopascals), or just watts. Higher is generally better, but the brush head design matters equally. A 100 AW Dyson with a great brush will outperform a 150 AW no-name with a poor head design.

### Runtime vs. Max Power Trade-Off
Nearly every cordless vacuum has multiple power modes. "Eco" or "standard" mode gives you maximum runtime — often 40–60 minutes. "Max" or "boost" mode reduces runtime to 5–15 minutes. For real-world use, eco mode handles normal messes; max mode is for spot-treating embedded dirt.

### Removable Batteries: A Hidden Premium Feature
High-end models like the Shark Vertex Pro have **removable, replaceable batteries**. This means you can extend cleaning sessions by swapping batteries, and when the battery degrades after 2–3 years, you can replace just the battery (not the whole vacuum). It's a significant long-term value feature.

### Hard Floors vs. Carpets
Different brush heads matter for different surfaces:
- **Hard floors**: Soft roller heads (like Dyson's) prevent scattering and scratching
- **Carpets**: Motorized brush rolls agitate fibers to dislodge embedded dirt
- **Both**: Look for multi-head kits or automatically-adjusting brush rolls

### HEPA Filtration for Allergy Sufferers
If anyone in your home has allergies or asthma, look for **HEPA filtration**. HEPA filters capture 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns or larger — including dust mites, pollen, and pet dander. Without HEPA, vacuuming can actually spread fine allergens back into the air.
