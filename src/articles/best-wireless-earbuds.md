## What to Look For in Wireless Earbuds

**ANC quality** in-ear is different from over-ear: good fit is required for effective noise cancellation. In-ear ANC blocks midrange and high-frequency noise better than over-ear; low-frequency (rumble) is slightly less effective.

**Fit and comfort**: Most earbuds include multiple ear tip sizes. Foam tips provide more passive isolation and better ANC; silicone tips are easier to put in and out. Stability fins prevent movement during exercise.

**Battery life**: Stated earbuds-only life is typically 6–9 hours; total with case is 24–36 hours. ANC reduces battery by 20–30%.

**Codec**: aptX Lossless and LDAC provide Hi-Res Audio quality; AAC is sufficient for streaming; SBC is lowest quality. Apple uses its own AAC implementation which is excellent on Apple devices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do wireless earbuds work for phone calls?**
Yes, but quality varies. Jabra earbuds are the best for calls. AirPods Pro 2 is excellent for Apple-to-Apple calls. Sony earbuds are good but not the primary strength.

**How long do wireless earbuds last?**
Battery capacity degrades over charge cycles. After 500 cycles (1.5–2 years of daily use), expect 20–30% reduction in battery life. Cases can be replaced cheaper than earbuds in most cases.

**Can wireless earbuds cause ear infections?**
Prolonged wear in warm, moist environments can create conditions for bacteria. Clean earbuds weekly with isopropyl alcohol, let ears breathe between sessions, and avoid wearing while sleeping.

**Are $50 earbuds worth it?**
At $50, ANC is either absent or ineffective. Audio quality is passable. If budget is the constraint, budget earbuds are fine for casual use. Below $50, skip ANC claims — they're marketing.
