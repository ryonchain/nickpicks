## What Makes a Great Portable Monitor

### Resolution and Panel Type
FHD (1920×1080) is the minimum for text-heavy work. 2K/QHD (2560×1440) or higher significantly improves text sharpness on a 15–16" panel. Always buy IPS for wide viewing angles — TN panels wash out when viewed from slight angles, making collaboration impossible.

### Connectivity
The best portable monitors power and transmit video over a single USB-C cable. This works natively with modern MacBooks and USB-C laptops. Some include mini HDMI for compatibility with older machines or HDMI dongles. Check what ports your laptop has before buying.

### Weight and Thickness
For travel, aim for under 2 lbs (907g) and under 10mm thin. The best portable monitors are thinner than an iPad and slide into a laptop sleeve.

### Built-in Stand
Integrated kickstands or fold-out covers that double as stands are essential — you shouldn't need a separate stand bracket for desk use.

### Brightness
Travel environments are brighter than home offices. Target 300+ nits for comfortable use near windows or in bright hotel rooms.

## Setup Tips for Remote Work

**Single USB-C cable workflow:** Connect the USB-C cable from the portable monitor to your laptop. Modern MacBooks, ThinkPads, and Dell XPS laptops will recognize it immediately and extend the display. If it doesn't work, check that your laptop supports DisplayPort Alt Mode over USB-C (most do).

**Hotel room pro setup:** Pack your portable monitor, a compact USB-C hub, and a travel keyboard. Total bag weight under 5 lbs for a dual-screen anywhere setup.

**Privacy screens:** If you frequently work in public spaces, consider a privacy screen protector (sold separately) for your portable monitor — prevents screen peeking on planes and in coffee shops.
