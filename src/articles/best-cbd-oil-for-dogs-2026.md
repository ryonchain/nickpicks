## What to Look For in Dog CBD Oil

**Third-party testing:** This is non-negotiable. Every batch should have a Certificate of Analysis (COA) from an independent lab showing cannabinoid content and confirming it's free of pesticides, heavy metals, and solvents.

**Hemp source:** US-grown hemp under USDA oversight is the gold standard. Colorado and Oregon are particularly well-regulated states for hemp cultivation.

**THC content:** Always verify less than 0.3% THC, and for dogs sensitive to THC, look for non-detectable (ND) results on the COA.

**Carrier oil:** MCT oil (from coconut) is preferable to hemp seed oil — it improves CBD absorption and most dogs tolerate it well.

**Concentration options:** A quality brand offers multiple concentrations so you can dose accurately for your dog's weight.

## Is CBD Oil Legal for Dogs?

Yes — hemp-derived CBD products (containing less than 0.3% THC) are federally legal in the US under the 2018 Farm Bill. However, your vet's involvement is important: CBD can interact with liver enzymes that metabolize many common medications, including phenobarbital (anti-seizure), NSAIDs, and heart medications.
