## Refrigerator Buying Guide

### Configuration Comparison

| Configuration | Best For | Fresh Food Access | Freezer Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| French Door | Most households | Excellent (eye level) | Good (pull-out drawer) |
| Side-by-Side | Frequent freezer use | Good (half width) | Excellent (full height) |
| Bottom Freezer | Fresh food priority | Excellent (full width) | Adequate (pull-out) |
| Top Freezer | Budget, small spaces | Good | Excellent (eye level) |

### Capacity Guide by Household Size

- **1–2 people:** 18–22 cu ft
- **3–4 people:** 22–26 cu ft (most common household)
- **5+ people:** 26–30+ cu ft

**Rule of thumb:** Allow 4–6 cubic feet per adult household member.

### Key Specs to Verify Before Purchasing

1. **Opening width:** Standard is 36". Many kitchens were built for 33" or 30" models — measure before ordering.
2. **Door swing clearance:** French Door and Bottom Freezer models require clearance for doors to open 90°.
3. **Delivery and installation:** Most retailers charge separately for removal of old unit.
4. **Water line access:** Ice makers and water dispensers require a water line — verify connection exists or budget for installation.

### Energy Efficiency

Look for ENERGY STAR certification. A certified 28 cu ft model uses approximately 450–550 kWh/year — roughly $55–$70 annually at national average electricity rates. Non-certified models can run 20–30% higher.
