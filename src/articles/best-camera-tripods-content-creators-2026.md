## What to Look for in a Creator Tripod

### Load Capacity
Match the tripod to your heaviest likely setup. A mirrorless camera with a 24-70mm lens might weigh 2.5 lbs. Add a cage, monitor, and microphone and you could be at 4–5 lbs. Choose a tripod rated at least 50% above your heaviest kit.

### Head Type
- **Ball heads** are fast to reposition; common on photography tripods
- **Pan-tilt heads** offer independent horizontal/vertical control; better for video
- **Fluid heads** provide resistance that makes video panning smooth — worth paying for

### Height Range
Most consumer tripods max out at 55–67 inches, which covers eye-level shooting for a standing average-height creator. For overhead shots or tall creators, look for 70"+.

### Leg Locks
- **Twist locks** are compact and reliable; take practice to master quickly
- **Flip locks** (lever) are faster for frequent setup/teardown; slightly bulkier

### Portability
Under 3 lbs for travel; 3–5 lbs is fine for car-to-location shooting. Beyond 5 lbs, you'll want wheels or to only set up the tripod once per shoot.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a tripod for a phone or mirrorless camera?**
A: Stabilization (OIS/IBIS) in modern cameras helps with handheld shots, but a tripod is still essential for: talking-head videos, long exposures, time-lapses, and any shot where you're also in the frame.

**Q: What's the difference between a video head and a photo head?**
A: Photo heads (usually ball heads) move freely and lock in position. Video heads add resistance or fluid so panning motions look smooth instead of jerky. For content creation, a fluid pan-tilt or video ball head is worth the upgrade.

**Q: Can I use a camera tripod for my phone?**
A: Yes, with a phone clamp adapter (most come with cheaper tripods; add-on for premium ones). Most have a 1/4"-20 thread that accepts standard phone mounts.

**Q: How much should I spend on my first tripod?**
A: $30–50 is reasonable to start. Once you're shooting consistently and know your setup weight, upgrade to a $100–150 tripod with a better head. Beyond $200, you're buying durability and professional-grade stability.
