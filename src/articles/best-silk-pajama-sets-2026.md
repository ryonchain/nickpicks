## Silk Pajama Buying Guide

### Understanding Momme Weight

Momme (mm) is the silk quality standard — equivalent to thread count in cotton, but more reliable. Higher momme = heavier, more durable silk.

- **12–16 momme:** Lightweight, sheer. Good for scarves and lingerie. Less durable for daily pajama use.
- **19–22 momme:** The ideal range for pajamas. Substantial feel, good temperature regulation, durable with proper care.
- **25+ momme:** Heavy, opulent. More common in bedding than sleepwear.

For pajamas: look for **19–22 momme** as the minimum for quality you'll feel.

### Real Silk vs. Satin

**Real (mulberry) silk:** Natural protein fiber that regulates temperature, is hypoallergenic, and benefits hair and skin by reducing friction. Requires hand washing or delicate machine wash.

**Satin:** A weave pattern (not a fiber). Polyester satin mimics silk's sheen at a fraction of the cost but lacks temperature regulation and skin benefits. Often labeled "silk-satin" which can be misleading.

To identify real silk: pinch the fabric and hold it — real silk warms quickly from body heat. Polyester satin stays cool.

### Caring for Silk

- Hand wash in cold water with a silk-specific detergent (Woolite Delicates or The Laundress Delicate Wash)
- Never wring or twist — gently press water out
- Lay flat or hang dry away from direct sunlight
- If ironing, use the lowest steam setting and iron inside-out
- Store folded in a cool, dark place — not on a hanger, which stretches the fabric

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What momme silk is best for pajamas?**
19–22 momme is the sweet spot for pajamas. Lower momme (under 16) feels too thin and sheers out quickly. Above 22 momme is durable but heavier than necessary for sleepwear. The OLESILK and LILYSILK sets at 22 momme represent the ideal quality for regular use.

**Are silk pajamas worth it?**
Yes, for two reasons. First, real silk has genuine functional benefits: temperature regulation (unlike polyester satin), hypoallergenic surface, and friction-reducing properties that benefit both skin and hair overnight. Second, a quality 22-momme mulberry silk set lasts 5–10 years with proper care — the cost-per-wear over time competes favorably with replacing cheaper pajamas every year.

**How do you wash silk pajamas without ruining them?**
Hand wash in cold water with a delicate detergent. Never use hot water, bleach, or fabric softener. Gently squeeze (never wring) excess water and lay flat to dry or hang in the shade. Avoid direct sunlight, which yellows and weakens silk fibers. Machine washing in a mesh bag on the coldest, most delicate setting is acceptable for higher momme weights (19+) but hand washing extends the garment life.
