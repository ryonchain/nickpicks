## Ergonomic Office Chair Buying Guide

### Lumbar Support Is Non-Negotiable
The lumbar region (lower back) is where most desk workers feel pain first. Look for chairs with **adjustable lumbar** — both height and depth matter. A fixed lumbar at the wrong position is worse than no lumbar at all.

### Seat Depth Adjustment Changes Everything
Most people overlook seat depth. If the seat is too deep, you'll slump to reach the back; too shallow, and your legs won't be supported. The ideal seat depth leaves 2–3 fingers between the edge of the seat and the back of your knees.

### Armrest Types Matter
- **2D armrests** (up/down only): basic, fine for most
- **3D armrests** (add pivot): better for mouse-heavy users
- **4D armrests** (add forward/back): best for people who switch between keyboard and mouse frequently

### Breathable Mesh vs. Foam Padding
Mesh backs keep you cooler but offer less cushioning. Foam padding is more comfortable initially but can compress over time. For hot climates or sweaty workers: mesh wins. For cold offices: foam.

### Weight and Height Limits
Always check the manufacturer's weight capacity. Most budget chairs are rated for 250–275 lbs. If you need higher capacity, look for chairs rated at 300+ lbs.
