## What Makes a Standing Desk Converter Worth Buying

A standing desk converter sits on top of your existing desk and raises your monitor and keyboard to standing height. Not all are worth the investment — here's what actually matters:

- **Lift Mechanism:** Gas spring (smooth, one-hand lift) beats X-lift (jerky, two-hand adjustment) for daily use. Avoid purely manual friction models.
- **Platform Size:** Your monitor, keyboard, and mouse all need to fit. Look for a minimum 30" wide keyboard tray. Some models offer 35–47" wide surfaces for dual-monitor setups.
- **Weight Capacity:** Most gas spring converters handle 15–35 lbs. Dual monitors plus a mechanical keyboard can hit 20–25 lbs — verify capacity before buying.
- **Height Range:** You need the keyboard at elbow height when standing. Most converters raise 10–20 inches. Taller users (6'2"+) may need converters with the full 20" range.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### Gas Spring vs. X-Lift Mechanism
Gas spring lets you adjust height with one hand, smoothly and quickly. X-lift requires both hands and typically a lever or button press while lifting the platform. If you genuinely plan to alternate between sitting and standing multiple times per day, gas spring pays off. If you set your height once and leave it, X-lift saves $40–70.

### Platform Width and Dual Monitor Support
Dual monitors typically need 32–35+ inches of platform width. Measure your monitors including bezels before purchasing. Many converters list a platform that barely fits two 24-inch monitors — verify your specific setup.

### Keyboard Tray Placement
A raised keyboard tray that positions your wrists at 90° when standing is critical for ergonomics. Some converters leave the keyboard at the same height as the monitors — this forces you to raise your shoulders uncomfortably.

### Wobble and Stability
All standing desk converters wobble slightly at maximum height. Gas spring models with wider bases wobble less. If you type aggressively or use a mechanical keyboard, test for wobble tolerance before buying — reviews mentioning wobble are usually the most honest signal.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does a standing desk converter fit any desk?**
Most converters require a desk at least 24 inches deep and wide enough for the converter platform. Most standard desks (60" × 30" or larger) accommodate any converter in this roundup.

**How long should I stand at a standing desk?**
The research-backed guideline is to alternate sitting and standing every 30–60 minutes, not to stand for full workdays. Starting with 20–30 minutes of standing per hour is a reasonable target.

**Will a standing desk converter damage my existing desk?**
No — converters rest on top of your desk without mounting or drilling. They can be removed completely.

**Can I use a standing desk converter with an ultrawide monitor?**
Yes, if the platform is wide enough (35"+) and the weight capacity covers your monitor. A 34-inch ultrawide typically weighs 14–18 lbs — verify weight, not just monitor size.

**Are standing desk converters better than full standing desks?**
Converters are better when you want to preserve your existing desk or have a limited budget. Full electric standing desks offer more stability and a larger surface, but cost $400–900+. For most people starting a sit-stand habit, a converter is the right first step.
