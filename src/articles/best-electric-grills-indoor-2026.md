## Buyer's Guide: Indoor Electric Grills

### What Makes an Indoor Grill Actually Smokeless?

True smokeless performance requires two things:
1. **High enough temperature** to cook fast (shorter cook time = less smoke generated)
2. **Effective smoke extraction** — either cyclonic air redirection (Ninja) or fan + filter extraction (Cuisinart)

Budget grills claiming "smokeless" typically only have a grease trap. They reduce smoke from fat dripping on a heat element but don't handle smoke from the cooking surface itself.

### Temperature Matters for Flavor

| Temp | What You Get |
|------|-------------|
| 375–400°F | Cooked, no crust — food tastes "pan-cooked" |
| 425–450°F | Light grill marks, some Maillard browning |
| 475–500°F | True sear — steaks, burgers develop a crust |

**Rule of thumb:** If flavor matters, buy a model that reaches 475°F+.

### Open vs. Lidded Grills

**Open grills:** More like an open-air grill. Better for steaks (direct heat on one side), asparagus, and anything you want to watch.

**Lidded grills (contact grills):** Cook both sides simultaneously. Faster for thick chicken breasts but can't sear a steak properly — it steams as much as it grills.

## FAQ

**Do indoor grills actually not smoke?**
Premium models (Ninja Foodi, Cuisinart CGG-900) achieve near-smokeless performance with lean meats at moderate temperatures. Fatty meats (bacon, 80/20 burgers) at 500°F will produce some smoke regardless of extraction technology.

**Can I cook a steak on an indoor electric grill?**
Yes, on models that reach 475°F+. Preheat the grill for 10 minutes, pat the steak dry, and sear 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare on a 1-inch ribeye. Don't use a lidded contact grill for steaks — open grill only.

**What's the best indoor grill for burgers?**
The Ninja Foodi XL Pro at 500°F — maximum sear and the large surface handles multiple patties.
