## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Handheld Garment Steamer

- **Heat-up time:** 30–45 seconds is the sweet spot; anything over 60 seconds gets inconvenient for daily use.
- **Water tank size:** Under 150 mL requires frequent refills; 200+ mL handles a full outfit comfortably in one fill.
- **Steam output (g/min):** 20–25 g/min handles shirts and blouses; 30+ g/min is needed for thick fabrics like denim, suits, and curtains.
- **Cord length:** Short cords (under 5 ft) are frustrating in large rooms. Look for 6 ft+ for easy maneuvering.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a handheld steamer replace an iron?**
A: For most fabrics — yes. Steamers are faster and gentler on delicate fabrics like silk, rayon, and wool. For crisp shirt collars and precise trouser creases, a traditional iron still does better work.

**Q: Is steaming clothes safe for all fabrics?**
A: Most fabrics handle steam well. Avoid steaming suede, waxed cotton, and some pleather materials. Always check the garment care label first.

**Q: How do I prevent water spots when steaming?**
A: Use distilled water to prevent mineral deposits, hold the steamer head at least 1–2 inches from fabric, and don't tilt the steamer too far horizontally (which causes water to drip).
