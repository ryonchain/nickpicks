## Bedroom Air Purifier Buyer's Guide

### CADR: What the Numbers Mean

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) measures how fast a purifier cleans a room. Three numbers are given: smoke, dust, and pollen. For bedrooms:

- **Small rooms (under 200 sq ft):** 100–150 CFM is sufficient
- **Medium rooms (200–350 sq ft):** 200–300 CFM
- **Large rooms (350+ sq ft):** 300+ CFM

The AHAM formula: room sq ft × ceiling height ÷ 60 = minimum CADR needed. Most bedrooms have 8-foot ceilings, so a 200 sq ft room needs ~27 CFM minimum — most purifiers in this guide far exceed that requirement.

### Filter Types Compared

- **True HEPA:** Captures 99.97% of particles ≥0.3 microns. Essential for dust, pollen, pet dander, mold spores. The standard for allergy/asthma relief.
- **Activated carbon:** Absorbs VOCs, odors, and gases. Thicker carbon layers (like Blueair) outperform thin pre-carbon coatings.
- **PlasmaWave/ionization:** Breaks down biological contaminants and VOCs. Effective but produces trace ozone — disable if you're ozone-sensitive.

### Noise Levels for Bedrooms

- **Under 25 dB:** Near-silent (library ambient is ~30 dB). Levoit Core 300S and Coway AP-1512HH hit this.
- **25–35 dB:** Noticeable but not disruptive for most sleepers.
- **35+ dB:** Audible white noise — may help some sleepers, disrupt others.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Where should I place a bedroom air purifier?

Place it within 6–10 feet of your bed, away from walls and corners when possible. Air purifiers work by drawing in room air — they need clearance on all sides. Floor placement near the door is also effective since it captures air as you enter.

### How long should I run a bedroom air purifier?

Run it continuously on auto or low speed for best results. Running intermittently (only when sleeping) is less effective because particles accumulate throughout the day. Most models draw under 50 watts — the electricity cost is negligible.

### Will an air purifier help with snoring?

Not directly — snoring is a physical airway issue. However, if nasal congestion from allergens is contributing to snoring, a True HEPA purifier can reduce allergen exposure and may help. Consult a doctor for persistent snoring.

### How often should I replace HEPA filters?

Manufacturers recommend 6–12 months depending on usage and air quality. In high-pollution areas or homes with pets, replace every 6 months. Pre-filters (washable) should be rinsed monthly to maintain airflow.
