# Best Under-Desk Treadmills 2026

The average office worker sits 9–12 hours per day. Under-desk treadmills let you log 5,000–8,000 steps during calls, emails, and meetings — without disrupting productivity.

Here are the best options in 2026, tested for noise, stability, and actual usability while working.

---

## What Makes a Good Under-Desk Treadmill?

**Noise level** — critical for video calls and open offices. Look for models rated under 60 dB at 2 mph.

**Speed range** — walking desks cap at 3–4 mph. You don't need running speeds; you need smooth, quiet low-speed operation.

**Deck stability** — a shaky belt at 2 mph makes typing impossible. Wider belts (18"+) and heavier frames are more stable.

**Height clearance** — under-desk treadmills need to fit under your desk legs and still leave 6–8 inches below desk surface. Most treadmill decks are 4–6 inches high.

**Storage** — fold-flat designs matter if you have limited floor space.

## Desk Compatibility: What You Need to Know

Before buying an under-desk treadmill, measure your standing desk height:

1. Your standing desk must raise to at least **42 inches** for comfortable typing while walking (more for taller users)
2. The treadmill deck adds **4–8 inches** to your effective standing height
3. Width: most under-desk treadmills are 24–28 inches wide — ensure your desk legs are wider than this
