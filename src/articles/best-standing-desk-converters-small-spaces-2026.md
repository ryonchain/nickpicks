## Buying Guide: Standing Desk Converters for Small Spaces

### Measure Before You Buy

The single biggest mistake is buying a converter without checking its footprint dimensions against your desk. Most converters need:
- **Width:** 28–36 inches minimum desk width
- **Depth:** 16–22 inches of desk depth (z-lift designs need less)
- **Weight support:** Most desks handle 20–40 lbs, but verify for thin IKEA tops

### X-Lift vs Z-Lift vs Fixed

**X-lift (scissors):** Smoothest adjustment, widest range, but takes more desk depth. Best for mid-sized desks.

**Z-lift:** More compact footprint when lowered, better for desks against a wall. Lower weight capacity typically.

**Fixed riser:** Most stable, easiest setup, no moving parts. Best if you never need to sit at the same desk.

### Keyboard Tray: Don't Skip It

Without a keyboard tray, your hands rest on the elevated work surface — meaning your elbows are bent upward at an angle that causes shoulder fatigue. A keyboard tray that hangs below the converter surface keeps your arms at the proper 90–100 degree angle. It's not optional if you plan to type standing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use two monitors on a standing desk converter?**
Yes, but check the weight capacity carefully. Two 27-inch monitors typically weigh 14–22 lbs combined. Most converters in this guide support that, but thinner displays help.

**How long should I stand each day?**
Research suggests alternating 30–50 minutes sitting with 10–20 minutes standing. Standing all day isn't the goal — movement variety is.

**Will a standing desk converter damage my desk surface?**
Most converters sit on top of the desk and don't contact the surface aggressively. Consider a desk pad under heavy units to prevent scratches.
