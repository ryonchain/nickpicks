## What Enzymes Do and Don't Do

**Enzymes help:**
- Break down proteins, fats, and carbohydrates more completely
- Reduce fermentation in the gut (decreasing gas/bloating)
- Improve availability of nutrients for absorption
- Reduce the digestive burden on the pancreas

**Enzymes don't help with:**
- Bacterial overgrowth (requires probiotics or antibiotics)
- Food allergies or sensitivities (requires allergen elimination)
- Inflammatory bowel disease (requires dietary and medical management)
- GI motility disorders (requires veterinary evaluation)
- Intestinal parasites (requires deworming)

---

## Important: EPI Requires Prescription Treatment

Over-the-counter enzyme supplements are NOT appropriate for treating Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency. EPI requires:

- **Prescription pancrelipase** (Viokase-V, Pancre-Plus, or generic pancrelipase)
- Much higher enzyme concentrations than OTC products provide
- Veterinary monitoring of body weight and stool quality
- Cobalamin (B12) supplementation (EPI typically causes severe B12 deficiency)
- Potentially vitamin B12 injections

If you suspect EPI, your vet should perform the cTLI blood test first. German Shepherds in particular should be tested if they show characteristic symptoms.

## Signs Digestive Enzymes Are Helping

After 2–4 weeks of consistent supplementation, look for:
- Firmer, better-formed stools
- Less gas and bloating
- Reduced volume of feces (better nutrient extraction = less waste)
- Improved coat quality over 6–8 weeks
- Weight stabilization or gain in previously thin dogs
- Decreased coprophagia (eating feces)
