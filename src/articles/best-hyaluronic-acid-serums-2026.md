Hyaluronic acid (HA) is a molecule your skin naturally produces to retain moisture — a single gram can hold up to six liters of water. As we age, HA production declines, which is why targeted HA serums are among the most evidence-backed skincare products available. The catch: molecular weight determines where HA works. High-molecular-weight HA sits on the surface and plumps temporarily. Low-molecular-weight HA penetrates deeper for longer-lasting hydration. The best serums use both. Here are the six formulations worth using.

## What to Know Before You Buy

**Molecular weight matters:** High-MW HA plumps the skin surface immediately. Low-MW HA penetrates deeper for sustained hydration. The best serums use multiple molecular weights — check that any serum you buy specifies at least two.

**Apply to damp skin:** HA draws moisture from its environment. On dry skin in low humidity, HA can pull moisture out of the deeper dermis, causing temporary tightness. Always apply to slightly damp skin and follow immediately with a moisturizer to seal it in.

**Layering order:** HA serum goes on after cleansing and toning, before heavier moisturizers and oils. It should always sit underneath SPF, not on top of it.

**Concentration doesn't tell the whole story:** 2% in The Ordinary is not the same as 5 proprietary forms in SkinMedica — molecular weight blend and delivery system matter as much as the number on the label.
