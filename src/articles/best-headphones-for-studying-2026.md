## Buying Guide: Choosing Study Headphones

### ANC vs. Passive Isolation

**Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)** uses microphones to capture ambient sound and produces an inverse wave to cancel it. Best for consistent low-frequency noise: HVAC, train rumble, crowd ambience.

**Passive Isolation** uses physical ear cup padding to block sound mechanically. Better for high-frequency noise and doesn't require battery power.

For studying, ANC headphones are superior because campus environments feature unpredictable noise profiles that passive cups handle inconsistently.

### Over-Ear vs. On-Ear vs. In-Ear

| Type | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Over-ear | Best ANC, most comfortable long-term | Bulkier, hotter in warm rooms |
| On-ear | More compact | Less comfortable over 2–3 hours |
| In-ear (AirPods Pro) | Portable, lightweight | Less ANC depth, ear fatigue |

For 4–6 hour study sessions, over-ear wins.

### Key Specs for Studying

- **ANC Rating:** Look for specs claiming 30+ dB noise reduction
- **Battery:** 24+ hours to last multiple study days between charges
- **Multipoint Bluetooth:** Connect to laptop + phone simultaneously
- **Carrying Case:** Protects during commute between library, dorm, and class
