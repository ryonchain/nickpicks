## What to Look for in a Standing Desk Converter

### Adjustable vs. Fixed Height
Adjustable converters cost more but allow you to alternate between sitting and standing throughout the day — which is where most ergonomic benefits come from. Fixed converters are best if you have a dedicated standing workstation or already know your exact height.

### Lift Mechanism
- **X-lift:** Most common, lowest cost, can wobble at maximum height under heavy loads
- **Z-lift:** Less desk depth required, more stable, slightly higher price
- **Counterbalanced spring:** Smoothest adjustment, highest stability, premium price

### Keyboard Tray
A separate keyboard tray that stays lower than the monitor surface is essential for ergonomic standing posture. Without it, your monitors end up too low (to keep your keyboard reachable) or your arms are raised awkwardly. Don't skip this feature.

### Weight Capacity
A dual 27-inch monitor setup weighs 25–35 lbs including stands. Add keyboard, mouse, and accessories and you're at 30–40 lbs. Check that your converter's rated capacity exceeds your actual load.

### Surface Size
Minimum 32 inches wide for a comfortable dual-monitor setup. Measure your existing desk to ensure the converter won't overhang the edges.

### Height Range
Most converters raise 6–19 inches above the desk surface. Your ideal standing height depends on your height — taller users need more maximum height. If you're 6'2" or above, check the upper limit explicitly.

## Standing Desk Converter vs. Full Standing Desk

**Choose a converter if:**
- You have a desk you love and don't want to replace
- Budget is under $300
- You rent and can't install a full desk
- You only want occasional standing (not all-day sit-stand cycling)

**Choose a full standing desk if:**
- You plan to stand for 3+ hours per day
- You want the maximum stability (full desks don't wobble)
- You're building a new workspace from scratch
- You want the cleanest aesthetic
