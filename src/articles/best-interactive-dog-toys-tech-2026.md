## Interactive Dog Toy Buying Guide

### Matching Toys to Your Dog's Drive Type

Different dogs need different engagement:
- **High-energy/fetch dogs** (Labs, Golden Retrievers, Border Collies): automatic launchers, ball toys, active movement toys
- **Prey-drive dogs** (Terriers, Huskies, Beagles): moving toys like Wickedbone, tug toys, lure-style toys
- **Food-motivated dogs**: puzzle feeders, stuffed KONGs, treat-dispensing cameras
- **Anxious dogs**: KONG, Furbo camera (owner presence), calming chew toys

### The Enrichment Equation

Mental exercise is as important as physical exercise. A dog that receives adequate mental stimulation through puzzle feeders, interactive toys, and training:
- Shows less destructive behavior
- Is calmer and easier to manage
- Sleeps better
- Has better cognitive function as they age

### Preventing "Toy Boredom"

Dogs habituate to toys — rotate them. Put 2–3 toys away for a week, then swap. The "new" toy gets the same enthusiasm as day one. For puzzle feeders, increase difficulty as your dog solves the current level — Nina Ottosson's progression system is designed for exactly this.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I teach my dog to use the iFetch?

Start by hand-loading the ball multiple times so the dog learns that balls come out the front. Then place the ball in the top opening with the dog watching and let it launch. Feed high-value treats each time the dog retrieves the ball and approaches the launcher. Most dogs self-load within 5–15 sessions.

### Are puzzle feeders good for puppies?

Yes, with size-appropriate selections. Start with Level 1 puzzles — easier compartments that build confidence. Puppies engage more readily with food-motivated puzzles. Avoid small pieces that could be swallowed. Supervised play recommended for puppies with any interactive toy.

### How long should a dog play with interactive toys unsupervised?

Automatic launchers: 20–30 minutes at a time to avoid obsession. Stuffed KONGs: as long as they last (usually 15–60 minutes) — fine unsupervised. Puzzle feeders: supervised, especially initially. App-controlled toys: best with occasional human engagement.

### Can interactive toys help with separation anxiety?

Yes — particularly for mild to moderate anxiety. A frozen KONG given at departure, a Furbo allowing owner voice check-ins, and leaving puzzle feeders that engage the dog in the first 10–20 minutes after departure can significantly reduce anxiety behaviors. Severe separation anxiety typically requires behavioral training in addition to enrichment tools.
