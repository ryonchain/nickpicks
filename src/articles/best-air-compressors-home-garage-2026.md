## Buying Guide

**Tank Size:** Larger tanks (6+ gallons) allow longer use between motor cycles. For nailers, 6 gallons is sufficient. For spray painting or sustained impact wrench use, consider 10+ gallons.

**CFM:** Cubic feet per minute determines whether your compressor can keep up with your tools. Check the CFM requirement of your most demanding tool and buy a compressor that exceeds it.

**PSI:** Most air tools operate at 90 PSI. Anything above 150 PSI max is ample for home use.

**Oil vs. Oil-Free:** Oil-free pumps need no maintenance and can operate at any angle. Oil-lubricated pumps run cooler and last longer under heavy professional use.

**Noise:** Standard compressors run 78–85 dB (similar to a vacuum). If you have an attached garage or do late-night work, an ultra-quiet model is worth the extra cost.
