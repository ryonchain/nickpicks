## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Jump Starter

**Lithium vs. lead-acid:** Lithium jump starters (NOCO, Antigravity, Tacklife) are compact, lightweight, and retain charge for months. Lead-acid (Clore JNC660) is heavier but delivers more raw current for difficult cases. For modern vehicles with healthy-if-dead batteries, lithium is the right choice. For very old or deeply damaged batteries, lead-acid has an edge.

**Peak amps for your vehicle:** 4-cylinder: 400–600A minimum. 6-cylinder gas: 600–800A. V8 gas: 1000–1500A. Diesel: Match or exceed gas ratings. Bigger is generally better — a 1000A unit will always work where 600A will sometimes fall short.

**Charge retention:** NOCO claims 1 year; Tacklife claims 6 months. In practice, lithium cells self-discharge at approximately 3–5% per month. Check and top up your jump starter every 3–6 months to ensure it's ready.

**Safety features:** Spark-proof clamp technology (NOCO) prevents sparking when connecting to a battery — important safety feature, especially for users who don't regularly jump-start cars.

## FAQ

**Can a portable jump starter start a completely dead battery?**
Yes — most lithium jump starters can start a battery that shows 0V if the battery isn't physically damaged. For severely sulfated or old batteries that a lithium unit struggles with, a lead-acid booster (Clore JNC660) has more success.

**How do you use a portable jump starter safely?**
Connect red (+) clamp to battery positive terminal. Connect black (−) clamp to an unpainted metal grounding point on the vehicle (not the negative battery terminal) to minimize spark risk near the battery. Turn on the jump starter, attempt to start the vehicle, then disconnect in reverse order (black first, then red).

**How long does it take to charge a portable jump starter?**
Most lithium units take 2–3 hours to fully recharge via the included micro-USB or USB-C cable. Some units also charge from your vehicle's 12V port while driving.

**How long do portable jump starters last?**
Lithium jump starters typically maintain 80%+ capacity for 300–500 charge cycles — approximately 3–5 years of regular use. NOCO and Antigravity units often last longer with proper care (periodic charging, storage at room temperature).
