## Standing Desk Converter Buyer's Guide

### Height Range: Calculating What You Need

The ergonomic standing position has your elbows at 90° when hands are on the keyboard, and the top of your monitor at or just below eye level. Use this formula:

**Converter height needed = your standing elbow height − your desk height**

For a 30-inch desk and 44-inch elbow height (typical for a 5'10" person), you need a converter that reaches 14 inches. Most converters in this guide reach 17–20 inches, providing adequate headroom.

### Surface Area for Your Monitor Setup

- **Single monitor (24–27 in):** 28×20 inches minimum
- **Dual monitors (2× 24 in):** 35×20 inches minimum
- **Dual monitors (2× 27–32 in):** 40×22 inches — consider an electric converter with large surface

### Keyboard Tray Depth

A keyboard tray should be deep enough for your keyboard plus mouse side-by-side. Standard keyboards are ~17.5 inches wide; with mouse, you need at least 24 inches of width and 10 inches of depth. Check tray dimensions, not just whether a tray is included.

### Weight Capacity

Most converters are rated 33–35 lbs. A typical dual-monitor setup (two 27-inch monitors + keyboard + accessories) weighs 20–25 lbs, well within limits. If you have multiple 32-inch monitors or a heavy laptop dock, verify the combined weight.

### Stability at Height

All standing desk converters wobble slightly at maximum height — that's physics. Monitors on monitor arms (attached to the desk, not the converter) remain more stable. For keyboard use, minor sway is unnoticeable. Typing at maximum height will feel slightly different from a fixed desk.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a standing desk converter damage my desk?

No — converters sit on top of existing desks without drilling or permanent modification. They're fully removable. Some converters use rubber grip pads to protect desk surfaces; check that your converter includes them.

### How long should I stand each day?

Research suggests alternating sitting and standing every 30–60 minutes rather than standing continuously. A good rule: sit 20 minutes, stand 8 minutes, move for 2. Standing all day is not the goal and can increase lower-back fatigue.

### Do I need an anti-fatigue mat?

Yes, if you plan to stand for more than 20 minutes at a time. Anti-fatigue mats with beveled edges reduce foot and leg fatigue significantly. The Topo Mat (Ergodriven) and Comfort Craft (Standingdesktopper) are popular options.

### Is a converter better than a full standing desk?

Converters are better if you want to preserve an existing desk (especially a large wooden desk), need portability, or have a limited budget. Full standing desks offer wider surface area and more stability but cost $400+ and require a full desk replacement.
