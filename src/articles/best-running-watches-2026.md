## What to Look for in a Running Watch

**GPS accuracy:** Multi-band GPS (L1/L5) significantly improves accuracy in urban canyons, forests, and hilly terrain. For most recreational runners, standard GPS is fine. For road racing, trail running, or any situation where pace accuracy matters, multi-band is worth the upgrade.

**Optical heart rate vs chest strap:** Optical wrist HR is convenient and accurate enough for zone-2 training and general monitoring. For HIIT, tempo runs, and intervals where zone accuracy is critical, a paired chest strap still outperforms wrist optical. Most serious runners train by pace and use HR as secondary data.

**Battery life:** Smartwatch battery (1–2 days) vs dedicated GPS battery (7–15 days typical, up to 30+ days for Garmin Fenix). If you're training for a marathon, week-long battery means one less thing to manage.

**Training metrics:** V02 max estimate, training load, training effect (aerobic/anaerobic), recovery advisor, and race predictor are now standard on Garmin and available on COROS. These features meaningfully improve training consistency for runners doing structured programs.

**Platform ecosystem:** Garmin Connect and Strava integration are the default running data platforms. Apple Watch integrates deeply with Apple Fitness+ but exports to Strava. COROS and Polar export to Strava and TrainingPeaks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need multi-band GPS?**
For most recreational runners on open roads and trails, standard GPS is accurate enough. Multi-band GPS is noticeably better in dense urban environments, heavy tree cover, and canyons — and during races where precise pace matters. If you're training for PRs or racing frequently, it's worth the upgrade.

**Can I swim with a running watch?**
Most GPS running watches are water-resistant to 5 ATM (50 meters), suitable for showering and shallow swimming. The Garmin Forerunner 965, COROS PACE 3, and Apple Watch Ultra 2 add open-water swim tracking. For lap swimming, consider a dedicated swim tracker — see our [Best Swim Fitness Trackers 2026](/articles/best-swim-fitness-trackers-2026/) guide.

**How accurate is optical heart rate for running?**
During steady-state runs, optical HR is typically within 2-4 BPM of a chest strap. During intervals, sprint finishes, or high-cadence efforts, accuracy drops. For zone-2 training, optical HR is more than adequate. For precise lactate threshold work, pair your watch with a Garmin HRM-Pro or Polar H10 chest strap.
