# Best Portable Grills for Camping & Tailgating in 2026

## What to Look For

- **Fuel type:** Propane is faster and more convenient; charcoal and wood add authentic smoke flavor. For camping trips without resupply, propane canisters are easier to pack and plan.
- **BTU output:** 5,000–8,500 BTU handles basic grilling fine; 20,000 BTU is worth it for larger groups or faster cook times. More BTUs means more fuel consumption.
- **Cooking area:** 150–200 sq in works for 1–2 people; 250+ sq in handles groups of 4–6. Always size up if you plan to cook multiple foods at once.
- **Setup time:** The best portable grills set up in under 2 minutes. If setup is a hassle, you'll use it less — prioritize simple leg deployment and propane connections.
- **Weight vs. cook quality:** Under 15 lb = backpacking-viable; 15–30 lb = car camping; 30–50 lb = tailgate. Match weight to how you'll transport it.
- **Grate material:** Cast iron grates hold more heat and sear better; porcelain-enameled steel is lower maintenance. Both outperform thin chrome wire grates.
