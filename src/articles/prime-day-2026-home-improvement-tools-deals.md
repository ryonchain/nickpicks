## Buying Strategy: How to Get the Best Tool Deals

### 1. Pick a Battery Ecosystem First

DeWalt 20V MAX, Milwaukee M18, or Ryobi ONE+ — pick one and stick with it. Batteries are interchangeable within systems and cost $50–$80 each. Buying into one ecosystem means every new tool benefits from batteries you already own.

### 2. Buy Tool-Only Versions When You Have Batteries

"Bare tool" or "tool only" versions (sold without battery/charger) are often 30–40% cheaper than kits — and if you're already in the ecosystem, you don't need redundant batteries.

### 3. Use Prime Day to Start Your Home Project Stockpile

Buy the power tools on Prime Day, then pick up materials (lumber, concrete, drywall) at local hardware stores year-round. The tools are where Amazon shines; materials aren't.
