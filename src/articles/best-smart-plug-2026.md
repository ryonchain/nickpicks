## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Smart Plug

- **Ecosystem compatibility:** Match your plug to your voice assistant — Alexa, Google, or HomeKit. Multi-platform plugs (Wemo) offer flexibility but often cost more.
- **Energy monitoring:** Useful for identifying energy hogs and estimating appliance costs. Worth the small price premium for long-term insight.
- **Form factor:** Compact plugs that don't block adjacent outlets are worth the slight premium in multi-outlet strips and dual-outlet wall plates.
- **App quality:** A reliable app with intuitive scheduling makes daily use frictionless. Check recent app reviews on the App Store before buying.

## FAQ

**Q: Do smart plugs work without Wi-Fi?**
A: Most smart plugs require a 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi connection for remote control and scheduling. Some (like Eve Energy with Thread) work locally and faster, but still need a Thread border router (HomePod mini, Apple TV 4K).

**Q: Can smart plugs reduce my electricity bill?**
A: Smart plugs with energy monitoring help you identify standby power drains and schedule devices off when not needed. Turning off vampire load devices (TVs, game consoles, chargers) can save $50–100/year.

**Q: Are smart plugs safe to leave on 24/7?**
A: Yes — quality smart plugs are UL-listed and designed for continuous use. Always check that the plug's amperage rating exceeds your device's draw (most handle 10–15A, sufficient for standard appliances).
