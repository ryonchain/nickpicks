## Wireless Gaming Mouse Buying Guide

### Wireless Technology: What Matters

Not all wireless gaming mice are equal. The three technologies to know:

- **LIGHTSPEED (Logitech):** Sub-1ms polling, most reliable connection, proprietary USB dongle
- **HyperSpeed (Razer):** Comparable to LIGHTSPEED, supports multiple devices on one dongle
- **Quantum 2.4 (SteelSeries):** Reliable 2.4GHz connection with Bluetooth fallback option

All three are competitive for gaming. Bluetooth-only mice (often advertised as gaming) are NOT recommended for competitive play — they add 8–15ms latency.

### Sensor Specifications

**DPI (Dots Per Inch):** More DPI is not better. Most players use 400–1600 DPI. Look for sensors with consistent 1:1 tracking and no angle snapping, not maximum DPI numbers.

**IPS (Inches Per Second):** The maximum speed the sensor tracks without losing accuracy. 300+ IPS handles all normal gaming movements.

**Polling Rate:** 1000Hz (1ms response) is standard. Some mice now offer 4000Hz or 8000Hz polling for enthusiasts, though the difference in normal gameplay is negligible.

### Weight: The Gram War

Under 60g: Ultralight tier — Razer Viper V2 Pro (58g), G Pro X Superlight 2 (60g). Best for fast-paced FPS and long sessions.

60–80g: Performance tier — most competitive mice fall here. Excellent for all game types.

80–100g: Standard tier — generally ergonomic or budget mice. Fine for casual gaming.

Over 100g: Avoid for competitive gaming.

### Battery Life Reality Check

Most premium wireless mice last 70–100 hours. Budget mice using AA batteries (Logitech G305) last 200–250 hours. For reference, charging a mouse weekly at 2 hours/day gaming takes less than 5 minutes per day on average.
