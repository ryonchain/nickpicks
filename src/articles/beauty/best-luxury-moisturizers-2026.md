## How We Evaluated These Moisturizers

Luxury moisturizers are a minefield of hype. Our evaluation focuses on three things:

1. **Active ingredient quality and concentration** — Does the formula contain meaningful amounts of clinically validated actives?
2. **Texture and sensorial experience** — Does it feel luxurious enough to justify the price?
3. **Real-world results** — Consumer reviews, clinical study data where available, and ingredient transparency.

We skipped moisturizers that are pure marketing with no substantive formula behind them.

## Are Luxury Moisturizers Worth It?

The honest answer: sometimes. Here's the breakdown:

**Worth it when:**
- The formula contains meaningful concentrations of clinically validated actives (retinoids, peptides, HA)
- You have specific skin concerns (barrier damage, significant aging, reactive skin) that respond to premium ingredients
- The ritual and sensorial experience genuinely enhances your skincare consistency
- You can afford it without financial stress

**Not worth it when:**
- The brand relies on one "miracle ingredient" with thin clinical backing
- The ingredient list shows actives buried below preservatives and fragrance
- You'd be skipping SPF or other basics to afford it (SPF beats any moisturizer for anti-aging)

For most people, the Tatcha Dewy Skin Cream delivers 80% of the experience at 20% of the cost of the top-tier options. If you have the budget, Augustinus Bader is the one with the strongest science behind it.
