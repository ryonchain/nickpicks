## Why Electric Outperforms Manual (And By How Much)

The ADA and multiple independent clinical reviews consistently find that electric toothbrushes — specifically oscillating-rotating types — remove significantly more plaque than manual brushing and reduce gingivitis more effectively over time.

The two mechanisms in consumer electric brushes:
- **Oscillating-rotating (Oral-B):** Round brush head rotates and counter-rotates at 7,600–8,800 strokes/minute. Most effective at the gumline. This is what the evidence most consistently supports.
- **Sonic (Sonicare):** Bristles vibrate at 31,000 strokes/minute, creating fluid dynamics that flush between teeth beyond where bristles reach. Gentler feel, excellent for sensitivity.

The difference between mid-range and premium electric brushes in the same brand family is almost entirely feature-based (apps, modes, sensors) — not cleaning performance. The motor and brush head are what clean your teeth.
