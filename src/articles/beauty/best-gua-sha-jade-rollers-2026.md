## What These Tools Actually Do (and Don't Do)

Let's be honest about the mechanism: gua sha and facial rolling are massage tools. They don't restructure bone, permanently lift tissue, or "sculpt" the face in any structural sense. What they do:

- **Stimulate lymphatic flow** → temporary reduction in facial puffiness
- **Increase local circulation** → temporary glow and warmth
- **Release myofascial tension** → genuine relief in jaw, temple, and brow tension (particularly useful for people who clench or grind)
- **Improve product penetration** → rolling after serum application increases absorption
- **Provide a ritual** → the 5-minute morning practice has mental health value that isn't nothing

For anything beyond the above — visible lifting, volume restoration, skin tightening — you need heat (HIFU), electricity (microcurrent or RF), or a needle (microneedling, filler).

## Gua Sha Technique Basics

Getting gua sha right requires three things:
1. **Oil or serum on the skin.** The tool must glide — never drag on dry skin.
2. **Light pressure.** You're stimulating lymph, not scraping bone. The pressure should be firm but not painful.
3. **Upward and outward strokes.** Always move lymph toward drainage points: toward the ear, down the neck, away from the center of the face.

The goal is to hold each stroke for 3–5 seconds and repeat 3–5 times per zone before moving to the next.
