## What LED Light Therapy Actually Does

LED masks emit specific wavelengths of light that penetrate skin tissue at different depths. Unlike lasers, they don't ablate or heat tissue — the mechanism is photobiomodulation: cells absorb photons and convert them into metabolic energy, triggering downstream effects like collagen synthesis, reduced inflammation, and accelerated cell repair.

The three wavelengths that matter in a home mask:

- **Red (630–660nm):** Stimulates fibroblasts → more collagen and elastin. The anti-aging workhorse.
- **Near-infrared (810–850nm):** Penetrates to muscle and bone layers. Reduces inflammation, supports healing.
- **Blue (415–430nm):** Destroys *C. acnes* bacteria on the skin surface. The acne-targeting wavelength.

Cheap masks often use superficial LEDs that barely penetrate the epidermis. The devices below are worth paying for because they use clinically validated wavelengths at therapeutic irradiance levels.

## How to Get the Best Results from Your LED Mask

1. **Start on clean, dry skin.** Serums and moisturizers can block light penetration. Apply LED therapy first, then layer your actives afterward.
2. **Be consistent, not intense.** 3–5x per week for 8–12 weeks. Daily use won't speed results dramatically; skipping weeks will stall them.
3. **Pair with retinol at night.** LED stimulates collagen; retinol accelerates cell turnover. Together they're synergistic — just use retinol after your LED session, not before.
4. **Don't expect miracles in week 2.** LED therapy works on biological cycles. Expect first results around weeks 6–8.
