## Why Steaming Works (and What It Can't Do)

Facial steaming is one of the oldest skincare rituals, and it persists because it genuinely helps with one specific thing: softening congestion before extraction or cleansing. Steam heats the skin surface, increasing circulation and temporarily softening the waxy sebum that clogs pores. That's it — steaming doesn't detox skin, doesn't shrink pores, and doesn't treat acne. It's a prep step.

Used correctly before a cleansing routine, steamer use can:
- Make blackhead extraction easier and less traumatic
- Improve absorption of serums applied immediately after
- Increase product penetration by temporarily disrupting the stratum corneum
- Provide a moment of hydration for dry, tight skin

## Pro Tips for Better Steaming Results

- **Always cleanse before steaming.** Steam drives whatever is on your skin deeper — start with a clean canvas.
- **Stay 8–10 inches from the device.** Closer isn't better; too close can cause burns or excessive moisture loss.
- **Steam for 5–10 minutes maximum.** The softening effect is achieved in the first 5 minutes; longer sessions can over-hydrate and irritate.
- **Extract or apply actives immediately after.** The window of enhanced penetration closes quickly as skin cools.
- **Follow with a hydrating toner.** Steam removes moisture as well as softening pores; replace it.
