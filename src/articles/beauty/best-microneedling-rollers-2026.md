## What You Need to Know Before You Roll

Microneedling creates controlled micro-injuries in the skin that trigger the body's wound-healing cascade: collagen and elastin production, increased cell turnover, and significantly enhanced absorption of topical products. It works. But at-home versions are fundamentally different from clinical treatments:

**At-home (0.25–0.5mm):**
- Penetrates epidermis to upper dermis
- Primarily enhances serum absorption
- Modest collagen stimulation at 0.5mm
- Low risk when technique is correct

**Clinical (1.0–2.5mm):**
- Deep dermal penetration
- Strong collagen induction, significant texture improvement
- Visible results for acne scars and stretch marks
- Requires professional training and sterile environment

If your goal is treating deep acne scars or significant textural issues, a monthly professional treatment delivers more impact than any home device. At-home microneedling is best positioned as a weekly absorption-enhancing practice and light stimulation tool.

## Post-Treatment Protocol (Critical for Safety and Results)

What you apply immediately after microneedling matters as much as the treatment itself. The micro-channels created by needles allow topical ingredients to penetrate 4–20x deeper than normal. Use this window wisely:

**Apply:**
- Hyaluronic acid serum
- Peptide serums (copper peptides are particularly effective post-needling)
- Growth factors / EGF serums
- Centella asiatica (cica) for calming

**Never apply immediately after:**
- Vitamin C (ascorbic acid — causes stinging and can irritate micro-channels)
- Retinol (too aggressive on freshly wounded skin)
- Chemical exfoliants (AHAs, BHAs)
- Niacinamide at high concentrations (some sensitivity risk)

Wait 24–48 hours before reintroducing actives.
