## Why Your Peripherals Matter More Than Your GPU

Your graphics card determines frame rate. Your peripherals determine whether you can act on it. A responsive mouse with a consistent optical sensor translates directly to aim accuracy. A full-surface mousepad eliminates micro-stutters from inconsistent surfaces. These are high-ROI upgrades — often more impactful than a $150 GPU bump.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What gaming peripherals should I buy first on a budget?**
Prioritize in this order: (1) gaming mouse — it has the biggest impact on gameplay, (2) large mousepad — cheap upgrade, huge ergonomic benefit, (3) wrist rest — prevents fatigue during long sessions.

**Is a $25 gaming mouse good enough for PC gaming?**
Yes. The Logitech G203 and Redragon M711 at $25–$35 offer optical sensors and polling rates competitive with $80+ mice. For casual and mid-level gaming, you won't notice a difference.

**Do I need a mechanical keyboard under $50?**
You don't need one, but great options exist under $50. The Redragon K552 and Tecware Phantom offer true mechanical switches (Outemu Blue/Red) under $45. They're louder but more tactile than membrane keyboards.

**What size mousepad should I get?**
Get an XL desk mat (80x40cm or larger) if you can — it covers your entire desk surface, protects the desk, and gives your mouse full range of motion without edges. Most quality XL pads are under $20.

**Are cheap gaming chairs worth it under $100?**
Budget gaming chairs under $100 often sacrifice lumbar support and durability. For under $50, a lumbar pillow or seat cushion for your existing chair provides better ergonomics than a cheap racing-style chair.
