## Buying Guide

### The Features That Actually Matter for Running

**GPS accuracy:** All watches in this list are accurate to within 1-3% on road running. Dual-frequency GPS (COROS PACE 3) adds accuracy on trails and under tree cover.

**Battery life:** For most runners, 20 hours is plenty (covers a 20-mile training run at 9min/mile pace with hours to spare). Ultramarathon runners should look at COROS (38 hours) or Polar (35 hours).

**Heart rate training zones:** Every watch here tracks HR zones. The difference is in how they're calculated — Garmin's and Polar's zone customization are the most advanced at this price.

**VO2 max:** Garmin and COROS both estimate VO2 max. These are estimates based on pace vs. heart rate data — useful for tracking trends, not as accurate as lab testing.

**Training Plans:** Garmin's Daily Suggested Workouts and COROS's training plans are both free and genuinely useful for structured training.

### What You Can Skip Under $200

**Multi-band GPS:** Nice for trails, not necessary for road running. Adds to cost.
**Music storage:** Useful but not essential if you run with your phone.
**Cellular/LTE:** Costs extra monthly. Not worth it for most runners.
