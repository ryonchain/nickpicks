## Solar Charger Buying Guide

### Understanding Watt Ratings

The wattage rating indicates maximum power output in ideal conditions (full sun, optimal angle, cool temperature):
- **10W**: slow phone charging — best for supplemental or all-day trickle charging
- **21–28W**: matches a USB wall charger in speed — charges a phone in 1.5–2.5 hours in good sun
- **60–100W**: suitable for charging power stations and running small appliances

Real-world output is typically 60–80% of rated output in typical outdoor conditions.

### Efficiency Matters More Than Wattage Alone

Panel efficiency determines how much power you get per square inch of panel:
- **Monocrystalline cells** (SunPower, PERC): 22–25% efficiency, highest performance, best in low light
- **Polycrystalline cells**: 15–18% efficiency, cheaper but less power per area
- **Amorphous/thin-film**: 10–12% efficiency, flexible but lowest power

For hiking and camping where weight and packability matter, monocrystalline panels give you more power without more surface area.

### Emergency Preparedness Use Case

A portable solar charger is an excellent emergency kit component:
- Charges phones during extended power outages
- Powers portable radios and emergency communication devices
- Charges power banks for lighting and appliance backup
- No fuel storage or maintenance required

A 21–28W panel and a 20,000mAh power bank gives a household 6–10 phone charges worth of emergency power per day of sun.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much power do I actually get from a portable solar panel?

In full sun (10am–2pm in summer), plan for 60–75% of rated output. A "28W" panel delivers 17–21W in practice. On overcast days, output drops to 10–20% of rated. Direct full sun positioning and a clean panel surface significantly impact real-world performance.

### Can I charge a laptop with a portable solar panel?

Larger panels (60–100W) with USB-C PD output (65W+) can charge some laptops. Most laptops require 45–100W — the Jackery SolarSaga 100W can charge laptops via USB-C on compatible models. Smaller 10–28W panels cannot charge laptops at a useful rate.

### Do solar chargers work in winter or cloudy weather?

Yes, but at significantly reduced output. Overcast skies reduce output by 70–80%. Cold temperatures slightly improve panel efficiency but short days reduce available charging time. For winter emergency preparedness, pair a solar panel with a large power bank charged during good weather days.

### Are these panels waterproof?

Most portable solar panels are water-resistant, not waterproof — they handle light rain and splashing but should not be submerged. The Jackery SolarSaga 100W is rated IP68 (fully waterproof). For others, bring panels inside during heavy rain and dry thoroughly before folding.

### How do I get the most out of a portable solar panel?

1. **Direct sun angle**: position the panel perpendicular to the sun
2. **Cool temperature**: panels lose 0.4–0.5% efficiency per degree C above 25°C
3. **Clean surface**: wipe panels with a damp cloth — dust and debris reduce output
4. **Minimize shade**: even partial shading of one cell can reduce the whole panel's output
