## Chemical vs. Mineral Sunscreen Explained

**Chemical sunscreen (avobenzone, octinoxate, octisalate):**
- Absorbs UV rays and converts to heat
- Lighter, more spreadable, better for daily wear under makeup
- More finishes available (matte, serum, invisible)
- Some people react to specific filters (octinoxate)

**Mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide, titanium dioxide):**
- Sits on skin surface and reflects UV rays
- Less likely to cause reactions for sensitive skin
- Can leave a white cast (less with newer formulations)
- Generally considered more environmentally responsible
- Biodegrades differently than chemical filters (relevant for coral reef concerns)

**Broad spectrum:** Both types must say "broad spectrum" to protect against UVA (aging/cancer) AND UVB (burning).

- **SPF 30:** Blocks ~97% of UVB
- **SPF 50:** Blocks ~98% of UVB
- **SPF 100:** Blocks ~99% of UVB
