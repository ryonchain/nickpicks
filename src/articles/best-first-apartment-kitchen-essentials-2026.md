Your first apartment kitchen can feel overwhelming to set up. You go from a dining hall or dorm microwave to a full stove, oven, and empty cabinets. This guide gives you the exact items to buy — in priority order — so you can cook real meals from day one without overspending or under-equipping.

## Phase 1: Buy These Before You Move In

### A Complete Cookware Set

Don't buy pans one at a time. A 10–15 piece cookware set gives you everything you need for less than buying pieces individually.

**Best options at each price point:**

**Budget ($60–80):** Amazon Basics 15-Piece Non-Stick Cookware Set — covers all basics. Non-stick makes cleanup easy, which you'll appreciate on late-night cooking sessions.

**Best value ($90–120):** T-fal Initiatives 18-Piece Nonstick Cookware Set — thicker construction than the budget options, better heat distribution, handles don't wiggle after months of use. This is the right buy for a 1–4 year apartment.

**Upgrade ($150–200):** Cuisinart 11-Piece Stainless Steel Set — handles oven transfer safely (no plastic), works on induction, lasts 10+ years. Worth it if you actually cook or your parents are helping fund the setup.

**What you'll actually use in a set:**
- 8-inch skillet: eggs, smaller sautés, grilled cheese
- 10-inch skillet: everything else — stir fry, chicken breast, sautéed vegetables
- 1.5-quart saucepan: rice, oatmeal, heating soup
- 3-quart saucepan: mac and cheese, pasta sauce, ramen with stuff added
- 8-quart stockpot: pasta, soups, anything bulk
- Lids for each

### One Good Chef's Knife

You don't need a 15-piece knife block. You need one chef's knife and one paring knife.

**The best first knife:** Victorinox Fibrox Pro 8-Inch Chef's Knife (~$45) — the knife professional kitchens use as a workhorse. It's dishwasher safe, holds an edge well, and the handle doesn't get slippery when wet. Used in culinary schools. Genuinely better than knives that cost 3× as much.

**Budget alternative:** Cuisinart 15-Piece Knife Set (~$40) — gets the job done if you want a full block cheap. Not as sharp as Victorinox but functional.

**Cutting boards:** Get two. OXO Good Grips 3-Piece Set (~$30) comes color-coded for meat/poultry/produce — keeps your kitchen sanitary without having to think about it.

**Also needed:** A knife sharpener. The KitchenIQ Edge Grip 2-Stage Sharpener (~$10) keeps your chef's knife sharp with 10-second touch-ups.

**Mixing bowls:** Pyrex Smart Essentials 8-Piece Mixing Bowl Set with Lids (~$30) — glass so they don't stain or absorb odors, microwave and dishwasher safe, lids turn them into storage containers.

### Sheet Pan + Baking Basics

**Half-sheet baking pan** (18×13): This is the single most versatile cooking surface in your kitchen. Sheet pan dinners — chicken thighs + vegetables roasted together — are the easiest complete meal you can make. Nordic Ware Natural Aluminum Half Sheet Pan (~$16) is the standard.

**Baking dish:** Pyrex 9×13 Glass Baking Dish (~$12) — for casseroles, lasagna, roasted chicken, anything oven.

**Cast iron skillet (optional, but worth it):** Lodge 10.25-Inch Cast Iron Skillet (~$30) lasts forever, works on any heat source including induction and open flame, and gives better sear than non-stick. Use it for steak, cornbread, and anything that benefits from high heat.

## Phase 2: Small Appliances

Buy these in your first week based on your actual habits.

### Coffee

**Drip maker:** Mr. Coffee 5-Cup Coffee Maker (~$22) — the standard. Makes 5 cups, takes up minimal counter space, auto-off safety feature.

**Single-serve:** Keurig K-Mini (~$65) — convenient if you drink one cup and don't want leftovers. Pods cost more per cup (~$0.65) vs. ground coffee (~$0.15/cup).

**French press:** Bodum Chambord 34-oz French Press (~$35) — best coffee quality of these three options, no electricity required, easy to clean, no pods or filters. Makes 4 cups at once.

**Espresso/lattes:** DeLonghi Stilosa Espresso Machine EC260BK (~$120–140) — lowest-cost entry into real espresso. Makes actual espresso and steam-froth milk for lattes. Significantly better coffee than any pod machine.

### Toaster

Hamilton Beach 2-Slice Toaster (~$20) — simple, reliable, fits any counter. Does exactly one thing perfectly. Don't overthink this.

**Toaster oven alternative:** If you're tight on counter space, the Breville Compact Smart Oven (~$130) toasts, bakes, and broils — replaces both toaster and oven for small batches. More expensive but consolidates two appliances.

### Microwave

Most apartments either include one or don't. If yours doesn't:

**Counter microwave:** Toshiba EM131A5C-BS 1.2 Cu. Ft. Microwave (~$100–120) — the most popular apartment microwave. Good power, fits most spaces, simple controls.

**Compact option:** Toshiba MM2-EM25PEY 0.9 Cu. Ft. (~$80) — for tight counter situations.

### Air Fryer (Optional But Popular)

If you cook regularly and want to level up, an air fryer changes the meal game. Ninja AF101 Air Fryer (~$100) does crispy chicken, frozen foods, and roasted vegetables in 10–15 minutes vs. 30–40 in the oven.

**Honest take:** If you already have an oven, an air fryer is a convenience upgrade, not a necessity. Add it after you're settled and cooking regularly.

## Phase 3: Pantry Setup

**Stock these on move-in week:**

**Spice basics:**
- Kosher salt + black pepper grinder
- Garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, paprika, Italian seasoning, red pepper flakes
- A decent olive oil (don't buy the cheapest — flavor matters)
- Neutral oil (vegetable or canola for high-heat cooking)

**Pantry staples:**
- Pasta (various shapes)
- Rice (white and brown)
- Canned tomatoes (whole and diced)
- Canned beans (black, chickpea, kidney)
- Chicken or vegetable broth (carton, not can)
- Soy sauce, hot sauce, Worcestershire
- Flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda (if you bake)

## What to Skip (for Now)

- **Stand mixer:** Not needed until you bake regularly
- **Instant Pot/Ninja Foodi:** Add after you've established your cooking habits
- **Full knife block set:** One good chef's knife outperforms 15 mediocre ones
- **Fancy appliances:** Coffee grinder, milk frother, egg cooker — all can wait
- **Full dinner sets:** Paper plates work fine for the first month. Buy an 8-piece dish set (~$30) on Amazon after you know how many people typically eat at your place

The best kitchen is one you actually cook in. Start simple, cook regularly, and add equipment as you identify actual gaps.
