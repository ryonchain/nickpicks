## Tips for Buying Toys on Prime Day

### 1. Compare Against Holiday Season Prices

Many toys see equivalent discounts on Black Friday and Cyber Monday. Use Keepa (price tracker) to see the 6-month price history before assuming Prime Day is the all-time low.

### 2. Buy Ahead for Birthdays and Holidays

Prime Day is in July — the perfect time to buy Christmas and birthday gifts months in advance. Toys don't expire. Stock up on the wishlist while prices are at their lowest.

### 3. Watch Lightning Deals on Popular Sets

LEGO and board games frequently appear as Lightning Deals (4–8 hours only). Popular sets sell out within minutes. Add items to your watchlist and set deal alerts in the Amazon app.

### 4. Check the "Deals" Tab Filtered by Toys

Go to Amazon's Deals page → filter "Department: Toys & Games" — this shows all active Prime Day toys discounts in one place.

### 5. Look for Multi-Pack and Bundle Value

Toy sellers often bundle related items (e.g., LEGO set + extra brick pack, board game + expansion) that represent better per-unit value than buying separately.

## What to Skip in Prime Day Toy Deals

**Hot 2026 new releases:** The hottest new toys rarely see Prime Day discounts in their first release year. If it's sold out elsewhere, Amazon won't be discounting it.

**Cheap generic toys from unknown brands:** $5–$10 toy price drops from unrated sellers. These are impulse buys that often disappoint on quality.

**Electronics-adjacent toys at minimal discounts:** Nintendo Amiibo, gaming accessories — these see 10–20% off at most. Wait for holiday season if the discount doesn't feel substantial.
