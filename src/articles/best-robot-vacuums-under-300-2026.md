## What to Look for in a Budget Robot Vacuum

**Mapping technology:** LiDAR (laser) mapping is far superior to camera-based or random-bump navigation. Under $300, LiDAR is now achievable — don't settle for bump-and-go.

**Suction power (Pa):** 2000 Pa handles hard floors and low-pile carpet. 3000+ Pa tackles medium-pile carpet and pet hair effectively.

**Battery life:** Look for 90+ minutes. Larger homes need 120+ minutes or auto-resume after charging.

**Auto-empty base:** The best convenience upgrade available. Expect to pay $30-50 extra, but cleaning the bin goes from daily to monthly.

**App quality:** Check reviews for the companion app. Some brands ship a great robot with a broken app. Roborock and Ecovacs have the strongest software ecosystems under $300.

## FAQ

**Do budget robot vacuums work on thick carpet?**
LiDAR models with 3000+ Pa suction handle medium-pile carpet well. Thick shag carpet or high-pile rugs are still challenging under $300.

**How often should I empty the robot vacuum?**
Without an auto-empty base: every 1-3 uses depending on floor size and pet hair. With an auto-empty base: every 30-60 days.

**Can robot vacuums handle pet hair?**
Yes — look for anti-tangle brushrolls (Shark, iRobot rubber brushes) if pet hair wrapping is a concern.
