## How to Choose

### Plate Material

- **Ceramic:** Most gentle on hair. Even heat distribution. Best for regular use on fine or normal hair.
- **Titanium:** Heats fastest, holds highest temperatures. Best for thick, coarse, or resistant hair. Use lower settings to minimize damage.
- **Tourmaline:** Ceramic or titanium infused with tourmaline mineral. Emits negative ions that reduce frizz and static. Best for color-treated hair.

### Temperature Guide by Hair Type

| Hair Type | Recommended Temp |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Fine/damaged | 300–360°F |
| Normal | 360–400°F |
| Thick/coarse | 400–450°F |
| Color-treated | 300–380°F max |

### Plate Width

- **1 inch:** Most versatile — straight styles, loose curls, bob lengths to long hair.
- **1.5–2 inch:** Best for thick or very long hair (faster in fewer passes).
- **0.5–0.75 inch:** Short hair, precision work, bangs.

## FAQ

**What is the best flat iron that won't damage hair?**
The GHD Platinum+ with its fixed 365°F is the safest for regular use — it never overheats. The Dyson Airstrait is the best option for truly damaged hair.

**How often should I replace my flat iron?**
Every 3–5 years. Ceramic plates chip and lose heat distribution; titanium plates dull over time. Replace when you notice pulling or uneven heat.

GHD Platinum+ for most users — superior straightening results and more reliable. Dyson Airstrait for wet-to-dry convenience or severely damaged hair.
