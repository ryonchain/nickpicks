## What to Look For

- **Brushless vs. brushed motor:** Brushless motors run cooler, last longer, and deliver more runtime per charge. Worth the extra cost for drills you'll use regularly.
- **Voltage and torque:** 20V/18V is sufficient for homeowners. Focus on torque (in-lbs or UWO) rather than voltage — higher torque drills through hardwood and masonry with less effort.
- **Battery capacity (Ah):** Higher amp-hours (2.0Ah, 3.0Ah) mean longer runtime. If you're doing extended projects, don't settle for a single 1.5Ah pack.
- **Chuck size:** A 1/2-inch chuck accepts larger bits for drilling big holes. A 3/8-inch chuck is fine for standard household fasteners and small bits.
- **Clutch settings:** More clutch positions give finer torque control. At least 15 settings is recommended to avoid stripping delicate hardware.
- **Ecosystem compatibility:** If you already own or plan to buy other cordless tools, choosing a drill in that brand's battery family saves money over time.
