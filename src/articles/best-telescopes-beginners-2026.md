## Telescope Buying Guide

**Aperture is everything:** The primary specification to optimize. More aperture = more light = more detail and fainter objects. A 130mm reflector will always show more than an 80mm refractor at the same price.

**Avoid high magnification claims:** Cheap telescopes advertise "400× magnification!" — but useful magnification is limited by aperture. A 70mm telescope tops out around 140× before the image becomes fuzzy. Any advertisement emphasizing magnification over aperture is selling a bad scope.

**Mount quality matters:** A shaky mount makes every observation frustrating. The Celestron and Sky-Watcher options above all have stable, smooth mounts. Avoid telescopes sold with spindly tripods.

**Great first targets:** The Moon is spectacular even in modest telescopes. Jupiter and its four largest moons are visible in any quality telescope above. Saturn's rings are visible at 50×+ magnification.
