## Garage Door Opener Buying Guide

### Belt Drive vs. Chain Drive vs. Screw Drive

| Type | Noise | Durability | Best For | Cost |
|------|-------|-----------|---------|------|
| Belt drive | Quietest | Good | Attached garages below living space | $$ |
| Chain drive | Loud | Excellent | Detached garages, heavy doors | $ |
| Screw drive | Moderate | Good in mild climates | Standard doors, mild weather | $$ |

For attached garages where noise transmits into the house, belt drive is almost always the right choice.

### HP Rating: How Much Do You Need?

- **1/2 HP:** Single-car garage doors, lightweight doors
- **3/4 HP:** Standard single or double garage doors (most common)
- **1 HP+:** Heavy insulated doors, oversized doors, or climates with extreme temperatures

Most residential doors work fine with 3/4 HP or 1 HP. Higher HP motors run quieter and last longer on heavy doors.

### Smart Features Worth Paying For

1. **Smartphone control:** Monitor and operate from anywhere — worth it for almost everyone
2. **Battery backup:** Critical if your garage is your only entry point during power outages
3. **Camera:** Useful if your garage receives deliveries or you have family members with varying schedules
4. **Amazon Key compatibility:** Allows Amazon to deliver packages inside your garage while you're away (you receive real-time notifications and video)

## FAQ

**Can I install a garage door opener myself?**
Yes — most homeowners with basic DIY skills can install a new opener in 2–4 hours. The hardest part is typically running the power outlet. Full instructions are included; YouTube has brand-specific installation walkthroughs. If replacing an existing opener with the same rail type, installation is simpler.

**How long do garage door openers last?**
Quality openers: 10–15+ years with regular maintenance (annual lubrication of the chain/belt and springs). Basic openers: 5–10 years. The motor typically outlasts the trolley hardware, which can be replaced separately.

**What's the difference between Chamberlain and LiftMaster?**
LiftMaster is Chamberlain's professional/commercial brand — built to higher standards, specified by installers, often available at lower street prices than Chamberlain consumer products. Both use the myQ platform and are functionally interchangeable.
