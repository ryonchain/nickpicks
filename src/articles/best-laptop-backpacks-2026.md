## Laptop Backpack Buying Guide

### Size Guide

**Laptop fit:** Always match the backpack's labeled laptop sleeve size to your exact laptop. A "15" sleeve" may be too tight for some 15.6" laptops — read the measured dimensions, not just the marketing label.

**Capacity:**
- 15–20L: Minimalist daily carry
- 20–25L: Standard commute with laptop + accessories
- 30–35L: Travel + overnight trips + heavier loads
- 35L+: Multi-day travel replacing checked luggage

### Features Worth Paying For

**TSA-friendly layout:** If you fly more than once a month, this feature saves 2–3 minutes per security checkpoint and eliminates the frustration of partially unpacking at the belt.

**USB charging port:** Useful but requires you to carry a power bank inside the bag. Verify the pass-through cable is included, as many bags require you to supply your own.

**Luggage sleeve:** The horizontal sleeve that slides over rolling luggage handle is essential for business travelers pulling carry-on luggage through airports.

**Water resistance:** Not the same as waterproof. Water-resistant bags resist splashes and light rain. For heavy rain, use a rain cover (many bags include one).

### Organizational Priorities by User Type

**Professionals:** Dedicated laptop + tablet + document sleeve, plus a front organizer for pens, business cards, charger
**Students:** Large main compartment + laptop sleeve + water bottle pocket on the side
**Travelers:** Clamshell opening, TSA-friendly laptop, luggage sleeve, packing cube compatibility
