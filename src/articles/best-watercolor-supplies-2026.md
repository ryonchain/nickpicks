Watercolor rewards the right supplies more than almost any other art medium. The wrong paper will fight every wash; the wrong paint will produce muddy, unpigmented strokes. These picks cover every level — from first tube to professional studio.

## Paints

### 1. Winsor & Newton Cotman 45-Pan Set — Best Beginner Set

Student-grade but the highest quality at the price point. 45 half-pans, genuine color names (not "Bright Blue"), and genuine watercolor behavior. Cotman is the entry-level set recommended by almost every watercolor instructor because it teaches proper technique without burning $80+ on paint you might not continue using.

**Price:** ~$45  
**Best for:** Absolute beginners, anyone testing watercolor before committing

---

### 2. Daniel Smith Watercolor 15-Tube Set — Best Professional Paint Set

Daniel Smith uses single-pigment formulations (where possible) with no fillers — transparent, highly pigmented, and designed to layer and granulate. The 15-tube set covers a full primary+secondary+earth palette. This is the paint most professional watercolorists use.

**Price:** ~$80–$100  
**Best for:** Serious hobbyists, artists with 6+ months of practice

---

### 3. Arteza Premium Watercolor 60-Pan Set — Best Value for Hobbyists

60 colors in a tin, higher pigment density than most hobbyist sets, and good color mixing behavior. Not professional grade but meaningfully better than cheap alternatives. A great set for anyone past beginner who wants more color variety without spending on professional tubes.

**Price:** ~$40  
**Best for:** Hobbyists, anyone who wants a wide palette at low cost

---

### 4. Mijello Mission Gold 34-Color Set — Best Korean Professional Pan Set

Mijello Mission Gold is Korean watercolor at professional quality — single pigments, high lightfastness, and creamy pans that rewet instantly. Beloved by many professional watercolorists for the pan format convenience. 34 colors cover a complete palette.

**Price:** ~$80  
**Best for:** Intermediate to advanced artists, pan-format preference

## Brushes

### 5. Princeton Neptune Synthetic Squirrel Set — Best Beginner Brush Set

Synthetic hair that mimics the water-holding capacity of natural squirrel at a fraction of the price. The Neptune set typically includes rounds in sizes 4, 8, 12, and a flat wash brush — the exact set needed to start watercolor. Long-lasting, cruelty-free, and well-balanced.

**Price:** ~$35  
**Best for:** All beginners, anyone who wants a complete starter brush set

---

### 6. Da Vinci Casaneo Series 498 Round Brush — Best Premium Single Brush

Synthetic Casaneo hair holds a significant amount of water and snaps back to a fine point. Available in size 6 through 20. A size 12 Da Vinci Casaneo handles large washes, wet-on-wet backgrounds, and still comes to a detailed tip. The best single-brush upgrade from beginner sets.

**Price:** ~$25–$45  
**Best for:** Intermediate painters upgrading from beginner brushes

---

### 7. Escoda Reserva Round Kolinsky — Best Professional Single Brush

Natural Kolinsky sable — the traditional watercolor brush hair that holds more water, snaps to a finer point, and releases paint more responsively than any synthetic. Expensive, but a single Escoda Reserva size 10 outperforms any 6-brush synthetic set for serious watercolor work.

**Price:** ~$40–$70  
**Best for:** Advanced watercolorists, professional use

## Paper

### 8. Strathmore 400 Series Watercolor Pad 140 lb — Best Budget Pad

Cold-press texture, 140 lb weight (no buckling with wet washes), and 15 sheets. Strathmore 400 is the best paper available under $20 per pad — significantly better than multi-media pads or basic watercolor pages in cheaper sets. Use this for studies and practice.

**Price:** ~$16–$22  
**Best for:** Beginners, practice work, daily studies

---

### 9. Fabriano Artistico Cold Press Block 140 lb — Best Paper Block

Fabriano Artistico is Italian 100% cotton watercolor paper — acid-free, buffered, and archival. The block format is glued on all four sides so sheets don't warp when wet. Paint lifts and corrects more cleanly on cotton paper than on wood-pulp alternatives.

**Price:** ~$35–$50  
**Best for:** Intermediate painters, finished artwork, framing-quality work

---

### 10. Arches Cold Press Block 140 lb — Best Premium Paper

Arches is the watercolor paper standard most professionals use — 100% cotton, acid-free, and with a pronounced cold-press texture that holds pigment beautifully. Washes bloom naturally, wet-on-wet is controllable, and the paper doesn't fight you. Worth the price on finished pieces.

**Price:** ~$50–$75  
**Best for:** Advanced painters, professional artwork, gallery-quality output

## Additional Supplies Worth Adding

**Masking fluid** (Winsor & Newton): preserves white areas under paint layers — essential for tight highlights.

**Mixing palette**: a white ceramic or porcelain palette with wells and a large mixing area — MIJELLO leak-proof palettes are popular.

**Water containers**: two jars (clean rinse + dirty rinse) keeps colors clean longer.

**Spray bottle**: a fine-mist spray keeps pans moist and paint workable.

**Artist tape / masking tape**: 1-inch tape around paper edges creates a clean white border and prevents warping.
