# Best Robot Vacuums for Hardwood Floors 2026

Hardwood floors and robot vacuums are a great match — but not all robots are created equal. Stiff bristle brushrolls scatter dust. Hard-edged side brushes can scratch. And some robots simply can't handle the transition from wood to area rugs without losing suction.

Here are the best robot vacuums specifically tested for hardwood floor performance.

---

## What Makes a Good Hardwood Robot Vacuum?

**Rubber brushroll** — not bristle. Rubber rolls pull debris inward rather than flinging it sideways. Key for hardwood where debris scatters easily.

**Side brush design** — soft rubber or bristle tips only. Avoid models with hard plastic side brush arms that can drag on finished hardwood.

**Navigation quality** — floor mapping and obstacle detection prevent the robot from bumping furniture repeatedly, which can ding hardwood edges over time.

**Suction power** — moderate suction (1,500–3,000 Pa) is enough for hardwood. Over-powered suction on carpet mats is wasted; on hardwood, all suction levels perform similarly.

**Mopping (optional)** — hardwood benefits from light damp mopping to remove fine dust that vacuuming leaves behind.

## Hardwood + Rug Homes: What to Know

If you have a mix of hardwood and area rugs, here's what matters:

**Suction boost for rugs:** Most robots auto-detect carpet and boost suction. Verify this is a feature on any model you buy.

**Mop lift:** If you add mopping, ensure the robot lifts or retracts the mop pad automatically on carpet contact. Wet mopping carpets causes mildew and damage. Roborock and Roomba Combo j7+ both do this.

**Edge cleaning:** Hardwood floors collect debris along baseboards. Look for models with stronger edge brushes and side-cleaning modes (Roomba's Edge-Detect or Shark's EdgeDetect mode).
