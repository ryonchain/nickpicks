## Types of Dressers

**Wide dresser (horizontal):** 5–6 drawers, wide and low. Works as a TV stand and dresser combo. Typically 60–72" wide, 30–32" tall.

**Tall chest (vertical):** 5–7 drawers stacked vertically. Fits in narrow wall spaces. Typically 18–24" wide, 50–58" tall.

**Double dresser:** Wide, 6-drawer design. The most common bedroom dresser.

**Lingerie chest:** Very tall, narrow, with small drawers. Maximizes vertical space in tight rooms.

## Dresser Buying Guide

**Drawer construction matters most:**

1. **Dovetail joinery** — Corner joints cut at interlocking angles. The strongest and most expensive drawer construction. Found in solid wood and high-end furniture.

2. **Metal drawer slides** — Metal runners provide smooth, consistent drawer action. Standard on quality furniture.

3. **Wood-on-wood guides** — Cheapest construction. Drawers slide on wood channels that can warp, stick, or swell in humidity.

**Anti-tip anchoring is mandatory:**

All freestanding dressers must be wall-anchored. Unanchored dressers can tip when heavy drawers are fully extended — a well-documented hazard with children. Every dresser should ship with anti-tip hardware; verify this before purchasing.

**Drawer depth:**

- **Shallow drawers (3–4"):** For socks, underwear, small items
- **Medium drawers (5–7"):** For folded t-shirts, workout clothes
- **Deep drawers (8–10"):** For jeans, sweaters, bulky items

A 6-drawer dresser typically has 2 shallow, 2 medium, and 2 deep drawers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between a dresser and a chest of drawers?**
A dresser is wider than it is tall (horizontal). A chest of drawers is taller than it is wide (vertical). Both store clothes; the difference is footprint. Dressers accommodate more folded items per "layer," chests maximize vertical space in narrow rooms.

**How do I prevent dresser drawers from sticking?**
Sticky drawers are usually caused by: (1) humidity warping wood guides, (2) overloading the drawer, (3) debris on the track. Rub a wax candle on wooden slides to reduce friction. Replace damaged metal runners with new hardware from the manufacturer.

**Can a dresser double as a TV stand?**
Yes — a 6-drawer horizontal dresser is often the same height (28–32") as a TV stand and provides a stable, level surface. Ensure the TV weight is within the dresser's rated top-surface capacity (usually 50–100 lbs) and secure the TV to prevent tipping.

**What's the best wood for a dresser?**
Solid hardwoods (oak, maple, cherry, walnut) last longest but cost more. Birch plywood is a quality middle option — strong, stable, and handles humidity better than particleboard. MDF (medium-density fiberboard) takes paint well and is stable, but heavier and not as strong. Particleboard is cheapest and least durable.
