## How to Use a Luggage Scale

1. Hook the scale strap around your bag handle
2. Lift the bag off the floor using the scale handle — hold steady for 2–3 seconds
3. The lock function holds the reading after you lower the bag
4. Read the weight on the display
5. Add 0.5 lb margin to account for small scale variation vs. airline equipment

**Target weight:** For a 50-lb limit, aim for 49 lbs or less at home. The 1-lb buffer accounts for scale variance and any items you might add at the airport (duty-free purchases, etc.).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How accurate are digital luggage scales?**
High-quality digital luggage scales are accurate to within ±0.1 lbs (±50 grams). Most airline scales are accurate to ±0.2 lbs. As long as your home scale reads more than 0.5 lbs under the airline limit, you'll be within safe margin. Cheap luggage scales can be off by 0.5–1 lb, which is not enough margin to rely on.

**What is the standard checked baggage weight limit?**
Most major US airlines (Delta, United, American) allow checked bags up to 50 lbs at standard rates. Bags between 51–70 lbs incur overweight fees of $100–$200 per bag. Bags over 70 lbs are usually refused entirely. International business class typically allows 70 lbs. Always check your specific airline and fare class before travel.

**Can I use a bathroom scale to weigh luggage?**
Yes — weigh yourself, then weigh yourself holding the luggage, and subtract. This works but is awkward for heavy bags and introduces measurement error from multiple readings. A portable luggage scale is more accurate, easier to use, and costs under $15.

**Do luggage scales work in kilograms for international travel?**
Most digital luggage scales support both lbs and kg, switchable with a button. This is important for international travel where airlines set weight limits in kilograms (typically 23 kg for economy class, 32 kg for business class).
