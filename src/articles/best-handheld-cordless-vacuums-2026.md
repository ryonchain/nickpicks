## Handheld vs. Stick Vacuum: Which Do You Need?

**Handheld vacuums** (this article) are typically 2–4 lbs, run 10–25 minutes, and prioritize compact one-handed use. They excel at spot cleaning, upholstery, stairs, and car interiors.

**Stick vacuums** are taller, run 30–60 minutes, and convert between floor vacuuming and handheld modes. They replace your regular vacuum for floor cleaning while also doing handheld tasks — but cost more.

**Choose a handheld if:**
- You already have a regular or robot vacuum for floors
- You primarily need quick spot-cleaning capability
- Car vacuuming is a primary use case
- Counter space or storage is limited

## Buying Guide: Choosing a Handheld Cordless Vacuum

### Runtime: How Much Do You Need?

**10–15 minutes:** Sufficient for spot cleaning (one spill, one couch cushion, one car seat row).  
**20–25 minutes:** Comfortable for cleaning a full car interior or multiple pieces of upholstery.  
**30+ minutes:** Needed only if you plan to vacuum a whole room with a handheld — at this point, consider a stick vacuum instead.

### Suction Measurement

Air watts (AW) is the clearest metric — it combines airflow and suction pressure. Dedicated handhelds typically deliver 15–25 AW; the Dyson V8 delivers 40 AW. Higher AW matters most for fine debris on hard floors and embedded dirt in carpet. For crumbs and pet hair on upholstery, 15–20 AW is sufficient.

### Filtration for Allergy Sufferers

If you have dust allergies, look for HEPA filtration. HEPA captures 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger — including pet dander and dust mite waste. Most budget handhelds use foam pre-filters only; Dyson V8 includes post-motor HEPA filtration.

### The Case for a Car-Specific Unit

Car vacuuming is a fundamentally different task: you need longer runtime (a thorough job takes 15+ minutes), sustained suction, and a cord that reaches every corner. A dedicated 12V corded car vacuum like the ThisWorx often outperforms cordless handhelds for automotive use. If car cleaning is your primary use case, consider a corded 12V unit for the car and a separate cordless unit for home.

## FAQ

**How do I clean the filter on my handheld vacuum?**
Most handheld vacuums use washable foam or felt filters. Rinse under cold water, squeeze until the water runs clear, and allow to dry completely (24 hours) before reinstalling. Never vacuum with a wet filter — it reduces suction and can cause motor damage.

**Can a handheld vacuum replace a regular vacuum?**
Not for most households. Handhelds lack the reach, capacity, and runtime for thorough floor cleaning. They work best as a complement to a regular or robot vacuum — fast spot cleaning vs. thorough floor maintenance.

**How long does a handheld vacuum battery last over time?**
Li-ion batteries in handheld vacuums typically last 2–4 years before experiencing significant capacity degradation. To maximize battery life: don't store fully depleted, avoid extreme heat or cold, and charge after each use rather than storing discharged.

**Why does my handheld vacuum lose suction?**
Three common causes: full dustbin, clogged filter, or blocked intake. Empty the bin and clean or replace the filter first. If suction doesn't return, check the intake nozzle and hose for blockages.
