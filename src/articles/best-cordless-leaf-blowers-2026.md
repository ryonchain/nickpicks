## Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Cordless Leaf Blower

### CFM vs MPH: Which Matters More?

**CFM (cubic feet per minute)** measures volume of air moved — how much debris you can displace at once. **MPH** measures air velocity — the force to break loose stuck debris. The best blowers optimize both: high CFM moves volume, high MPH breaks loose packed leaves and wet debris. For most yards, CFM matters more: a 600+ CFM blower clears faster than a 400 CFM / 200 mph unit.

### Battery Voltage and Capacity

Higher voltage (56V, 80V) generally means more power for demanding tasks. Battery capacity (Ah — amp hours) determines runtime: a 5.0Ah battery lasts longer than a 2.5Ah battery at equivalent discharge rates. The best systems let you mix large-capacity batteries for runtime and smaller batteries for lighter tasks.

### Ecosystem Investment

Battery cordless tools are a platform investment. If you own 3 tools from the same brand's battery system, adding a 4th tool at "tool only" price eliminates the cost of a new battery and charger. Before buying a blower from a new brand, check whether you already own compatible batteries.

### Noise Levels

Gas leaf blowers typically operate at 95–105 dB — loud enough for ear protection and potential HOA violations. Battery cordless blowers range from 62–75 dB — noticeably quieter and compatible with most residential noise ordinances for daytime weekend use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much CFM do I need for my yard size?**
- Under ¼ acre: 300–450 CFM is sufficient for light leaf cleanup
- ¼–½ acre: 500–600 CFM handles moderate leaf volume
- ½–1 acre: 600–700 CFM recommended
- 1+ acre: 700+ CFM or backpack blower

**Can battery blowers match gas performance?**
Top-tier 80V cordless blowers (Greenworks 760 CFM) match mid-range gas blowers (500–650 CFM range). Very high-output gas blowers (800+ CFM commercial) still exceed residential cordless options, but for residential use, the gap is minimal.

**How do I extend battery runtime?**
Use eco/low mode for light debris and save turbo for heavy piles. For large properties, consider a backpack blower with a 7.5Ah+ battery or purchase a second battery to swap midway through. EGO's Rapid Charge charger (sold separately) recharges in 30 minutes.

**Are cordless leaf blowers good for mulching leaves?**
Blowers move leaves but don't mulch them. A leaf mulcher or mulching mower shreds leaves into fine particles for composting or lawn topdressing. Some vacuums (like the Black+Decker BV6600) both blow and vacuum with a bag for collection.
