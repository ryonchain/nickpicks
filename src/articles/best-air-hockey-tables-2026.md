## Air Hockey Table Buying Guide

### Table Size
- **40–48 inches:** Tabletop or budget floor models. Good for young children.
- **54–60 inches:** Mid-size. Good for family play and rooms with space constraints.
- **7 feet:** Regulation size. Best for serious play and dedicated game rooms.
- **8 feet:** Commercial/arcade standard. Rare in homes.

### Motor Power
The blower motor determines how smoothly the puck glides. Budget models use lower-voltage motors that create uneven airflow near the edges. Better tables use 110V+ motors with uniform air distribution.

### Surface Material
Look for a high-gloss laminate surface. Cheaper surfaces scratch and lose smoothness over time, degrading gameplay.

### Electronic vs. Manual Scoring
Electronic scoring with lights and sounds is more engaging, especially for kids. Manual slide scores are simpler but require players to keep track honestly.

### Assembly
Most tables require 2–3 hours of assembly and benefit from two people. Read reviews for assembly difficulty before buying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do home air hockey tables last?**
Quality tables with metal legs and thick playfields last 10–20+ years. Budget tables with plastic components may show wear in 3–5 years with heavy use.

**What size room do I need for a 7-foot table?**
Allow at least 12 feet in length and 8 feet in width for comfortable play. The table itself is 7'x3.5', but players need clearance behind each goal.

**Is air hockey a good game for kids?**
Absolutely — it develops hand-eye coordination, is easy to learn (no instruction needed), and is equally fun for adults. Most kids ages 4+ can enjoy a table immediately.
