## How Indoor Hydroponics Works

Hydroponic gardens grow plants in water rather than soil. Roots access nutrients dissolved in the water directly, which is why plants grow faster — no energy wasted searching for nutrients through soil.

**Key components:**
- **Water reservoir**: holds nutrient solution
- **Grow pods**: hold seeds or seedlings above the water
- **Pump/aerator**: circulates and oxygenates water
- **Grow lights**: LED lights simulate sunlight spectrum (replaces window light)
- **Nutrient solution**: dissolved fertilizer optimized for leafy greens, herbs, or fruiting plants

**What grows well indoors:** Herbs (basil, mint, cilantro, parsley), leafy greens (lettuce, kale, spinach, arugula), cherry tomatoes, peppers, strawberries

**What needs more space/light:** Full-size tomatoes, cucumbers, zucchini — possible but challenging indoors

## What to Grow First

**Easiest (guaranteed success):**
- Basil — thrives in hydroponic conditions, fast growth, high demand in cooking
- Lettuce — continuous harvest every 3–4 weeks
- Mint — nearly impossible to kill, grow separately (invasive root system)

**Medium difficulty:**
- Cherry tomatoes — need 8+ weeks, support staking, and pollination help (shake flowers gently)
- Cilantro — bolts quickly in warm environments; grow in cooler room
- Peppers — slow grower, but productive once established

**More challenging:**
- Cucumbers — need significant space and hand pollination
- Strawberries — best in larger systems, slow to fruit
