Prime Day officially ends July 14 at midnight. But every year, Amazon keeps a significant number of deals running for 48–72 hours post-event.

## Why Deals Extend Beyond Prime Day

Amazon has three reasons to extend deals after Prime Day:

1. **Inventory to clear** — items that didn't sell as fast as projected get extended to avoid restocking costs
2. **Competitor response** — Walmart, Target, and Best Buy run their own competing sales after Prime Day, so Amazon matches with extended pricing
3. **Algorithm testing** — Amazon A/B tests which deals drive more Subscribe & Save conversions when extended past the event date

The extended period is usually quieter but the discounts are nearly as good. Less traffic means no Lightning Deal countdowns, which can actually work in your favor.

## Comparing Extended Deals vs. Black Friday

The main question post-Prime Day: should I wait until Black Friday?

**Buy now if:**
- You need it this summer (air purifier, air conditioner, fitness equipment before fall)
- The extended price is within 5–10% of the Prime Day low
- The product is in your daily rotation (coffee maker, headphones)

**Wait until Black Friday if:**
- It's a gift you're buying for November or December
- The Prime Day discount was less than 20% (Black Friday often goes deeper on big-ticket items like TVs and laptops)
- You can genuinely wait 4+ months

Rule of thumb: electronics and home goods rarely hit lower prices at Black Friday than Prime Day. The exception is TVs and laptops, which sometimes see deeper November cuts.

## Extended Deal Tracking Schedule

Amazon typically runs post-Prime Day deals in waves:

**July 15 (Day 1 post-Prime Day):** Best extended inventory. Similar to Day 2 Prime Day pricing. High-demand items from Day 1 that restocked may appear here.

**July 16–17:** Deals begin narrowing. Clearance on items that didn't sell fast enough.

**July 18–20:** Extended period closes. Subscribe & Save discount stacks disappear. Prices begin returning to pre-Prime Day normal.

**Beyond July 20:** A handful of extended "Summer Sale" deals may persist, but they're no longer Prime Day pricing.
