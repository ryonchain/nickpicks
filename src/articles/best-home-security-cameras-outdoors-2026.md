## How to Choose an Outdoor Security Camera

### Weatherproofing: what IP ratings actually mean

IP (Ingress Protection) ratings use a two-digit system. The first digit rates dust protection (6 = fully sealed). The second rates water resistance: 5 = protected against low-pressure water jets (rain, sprinklers), 6 = high-pressure jets, 7 = submersion up to 1 meter for 30 minutes. For most outdoor residential use, IP65 is the right minimum. Cameras installed under wide eaves or in covered porches can get away with IP54, but anything exposed to direct rain should be rated IP65 or higher.

### Resolution and detection distance

Resolution matters more outdoors than indoors because subjects are farther from the camera. At 15 feet, both 1080p and 2K cameras can identify a face. Beyond 20 feet, 2K provides meaningfully better detail for identification in footage review. If your primary concern is a long driveway, parking area, or large yard, 2K (Arlo Pro 5S) is worth the price premium. For porch coverage, close-range driveway monitoring, or supplemental coverage, 1080p is sufficient.

### Color night vision vs. infrared

Traditional infrared night vision produces black-and-white footage after dark. Color night vision — either via a built-in spotlight or starlight sensor — captures full-color footage that reveals clothing color, vehicle color, and facial features. For identifying suspects in footage, the difference is significant. The Arlo Pro 5S, Ring Spotlight Cam Plus, and Wyze Cam Outdoor v2 all offer color night vision. The Blink Outdoor 4 uses IR only.
