## Standing Desk Converter Buying Guide

### Mechanism Type: Gas Spring vs. Scissor X-Lift

**Gas spring** converters (FLEXISPOT, Ergotron) use a pressurized cylinder to counterbalance the weight. Adjustments are smooth and require minimal effort — you simply lift or push down. **Scissor X-lift** converters use a folding X-frame with a lever or knob lock. They're cheaper but require more effort to adjust and are generally less stable at maximum height. For daily multi-transition use, gas spring is worth the extra cost.

### Height Range

For most users (5'4" – 6'2"), a minimum height range of 12–15 inches provides comfortable standing desk ergonomics. The correct standing height is elbow-level: your forearms should be parallel to the floor when typing. Measure your sitting-to-standing difference — that's the range you need.

### Surface Width: Single vs. Dual Monitor

For a single 27" monitor: a 24-inch converter surface is sufficient. For dual 24" monitors: you need at least 32 inches. For dual 27" monitors: 36 inches is the minimum. Don't try to squeeze dual monitors onto a 24-inch platform — the resulting screen angle will cause neck strain.

### Weight Capacity

Most converters are rated for 22–35 lbs. A 27" monitor typically weighs 10–15 lbs. Two monitors can push 25–30 lbs. Add the weight of any laptop or accessories. Budget at least 10 lbs of margin below the rated capacity for stability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do standing desk converters wobble?**
Most do wobble slightly at maximum height — this is inherent to the converter design vs. a full standing desk. Gas spring mechanisms (FLEXISPOT, Ergotron) wobble less than scissor lifts at full extension. If you're a heavy typist at maximum height, expect minor wobble regardless of converter type.

**Is a standing desk converter worth it vs. a full standing desk?**
For users who want to test standing work habits without committing $500+, yes. Converters are also better for rented spaces where desk replacement isn't an option. If you're standing more than 4 hours per day, a full sit-stand desk with a wider surface and cleaner cable management is worth the investment.

**How much height adjustment do I need?**
Measure the height of your current desk surface. At standing height, your elbows should be at 90° while typing. Most people need 12–18 inches of clearance above their desk surface. The FLEXISPOT M7B's 15-inch range covers most users between 5'4" and 6'2".

**Can I put a standing desk converter on any desk?**
Yes — converters sit on top of any flat desk surface and don't require drilling or mounting. The only constraint is desk width and depth: ensure your desk is at least as wide as the converter and at least 20 inches deep so the converter base has full support.
