## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best type of travel pillow for neck support?**
Memory foam U-shaped pillows provide the best neck support for most travelers, as they conform to neck shape and hold position during sleep. However, ergonomically designed pillows like the Trtl (a scarf-style design with internal spine support) can be more effective for side-sleepers and those with chronic neck issues.

**Are inflatable travel pillows worth buying?**
Inflatable pillows are the best choice for travelers prioritizing pack size — they deflate to under 3 inches. The tradeoff is that they don't conform to neck shape as well as memory foam, can feel firm at full inflation, and require inflation before use. For short flights, inflatable is ideal; for long haul, memory foam is more comfortable.

**How do I sleep on a plane without neck pain?**
Use a travel pillow rated for side or front support (not just behind the head), recline your seat at least slightly, keep your neck neutral rather than bent forward, and consider a window seat so you can lean against the cabin wall. Pillows that wrap to the front of the neck (like the Trtl) significantly reduce the head-dropping-forward problem.

**Can I bring a travel pillow through airport security?**
Yes — travel pillows are not restricted by TSA. You can carry them in your bag or attach them to the outside of your carry-on. Inflatable pillows go through the X-ray machine deflated. Note that very large pillows may need to fit within your personal item allowance on some airlines.
