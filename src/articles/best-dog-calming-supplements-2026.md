## Behavioral vs. Supplement Approach

**Supplements work best when combined with:**
- Desensitization training (gradual exposure to triggers)
- Counter-conditioning (pairing triggers with positive experiences)
- Management (preventing overexposure to triggers)
- Routine and consistency

For separation anxiety specifically, supplements are most effective as a bridge while implementing behavior modification — not as a standalone solution.

---

## Safety Note: What to Avoid

**Diphenhydramine (Benadryl):** Often recommended as a home remedy, but dogs develop tolerance quickly and the sedation often impairs their ability to positively associate with the stressor.

**Human melatonin gummies:** Most contain xylitol, which causes life-threatening hypoglycemia in dogs. Check ingredients carefully or use pet-specific formulations.

**Acepromazine (from older vets):** A sedative that blocks motor function but does NOT reduce anxiety — the dog is physically incapacitated but still terrified. No longer recommended by veterinary behaviorists.
