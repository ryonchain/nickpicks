## Bidet Seat Buying Guide

**Electric vs. non-electric:** Electric bidet seats offer heated water, heated seat, warm air dryer, and automatic nozzle cleaning. Non-electric bidet attachments use cold water only (or warm via sink line) and offer basic spray functionality. If you live in a climate with cold winters, heated water is a meaningful quality-of-life improvement.

**Compatibility:** Most bidet seats fit elongated or round toilet bowls — verify your bowl shape before purchasing. TOTO and Brondell have online fit check tools. TOTO seats are also specific to TOTO toilet models on some variants; check compatibility explicitly.

**Installation requirements:** Electric bidet seats require a GFCI-protected outlet within 4 feet of the toilet. Most new bathrooms have one; older bathrooms may require an electrician to add one ($100–$200). Non-electric seats require only a shutoff valve at the toilet water supply.
