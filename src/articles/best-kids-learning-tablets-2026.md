## Kids Tablet Buying Guide

### Age-Appropriate Selection

**Ages 2–4:** Dedicated learning devices (LeapFrog) or Amazon Fire 7 Kids with parental controls fully locked down. Focus on phonics, numbers, and creative apps. Durability is paramount.

**Ages 5–7:** Fire HD 8 Kids or Fire 7 Kids. Amazon Kids+ content grows with them. Introduce reading apps (Epic!), math practice (Khan Academy Kids), and simple creativity tools.

**Ages 8–11:** Amazon Fire HD 8 or Samsung Tab A9 for more advanced content. Begin transitioning to educational productivity (Google Docs, Slides) alongside games and media.

**Ages 12+:** iPad or full Android tablet. They're students who need the same tools as adults for school.

### What Are Parental Controls Actually Good For?

Good parental controls enforce:
- **Screen time limits** — automatic shutoff at the day's usage limit
- **Bedtime curfews** — device locks at bedtime regardless of activity
- **Content filtering** — blocks age-inappropriate apps, videos, and web browsing
- **Educational goals** — requires reading/math practice before entertainment apps unlock
- **Activity reports** — shows what your child is doing on the device

Amazon Parent Dashboard (Fire Kids) is the most seamless. Apple Screen Time is the most granular. Samsung Kids is adequate but less polished.

### Durability: Cases and Guarantees

Kids will drop tablets. Buy accordingly:
- **Amazon Fire Kids** comes with a case and a 2-year replace-it-free guarantee
- **LeapFrog** has a rubberized bumper integrated
- **iPad and Samsung** require third-party cases (OtterBox Defender recommended) and AppleCare+ for accident coverage

### Screen Time and Blue Light

Most pediatric associations recommend limiting screen time, not eliminating it. Tools like Amazon's educational goals function — earning entertainment screen time through reading or math practice — are more effective than hard bans. Look for tablets with blue light reduction modes for evening use.

Most child development experts suggest waiting until 2–3 years old, with strict time limits. Learning-focused devices like LeapFrog are designed for this age range. General-purpose tablets are better suited for 5 and up.
