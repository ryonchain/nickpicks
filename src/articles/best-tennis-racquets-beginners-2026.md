## Key Specs Explained for Beginners

**Head size:** Measured in square inches. Larger head = more sweet spot = more forgiveness on off-center hits. Beginners should look for 100–110 sq in. Advanced players use 95–100 sq in. Oversize (115+ sq in) racquets are the most forgiving but can be difficult to control as skills improve.

**Weight:** Lighter racquets (9–10.5 oz strung) are easier to swing and cause less arm fatigue. Heavier racquets (11+ oz) provide more stability but require more strength. Beginners: aim for 9.5–10.5 oz strung weight.

**Balance:** Head-light (more weight in handle) racquets are easier to maneuver and faster through the swing. Head-heavy racquets provide more power but feel sluggish. Most beginner racquets are slightly head-heavy to offset their lighter weight.

**String pattern:** Open string pattern (16×18, 16×19) creates more spin potential. Denser patterns (18×20) offer more control but less spin. Beginners don't need to obsess over this — most beginner racquets come with appropriate open patterns.

**Grip size:** The most overlooked spec. Wrong grip size causes arm fatigue and missed shots. Measure your grip: distance from your ring finger's base crease to the tip of your ring finger. Adults typically range from grip size 1 (4 1/8") to grip size 4 (4 1/2").

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I buy a racquet pre-strung or unstrung?**
Pre-strung racquets are convenient and fine for beginners. The included strings are typically basic nylon — they work, but won't last more than 20–30 hours of play before losing tension. Once you're playing 2–3x per week, a restring at a pro shop ($30–50 with better strings) is worth it.

**What grip size should I start with?**
The quick test: hold the racquet with your dominant hand in a forehand grip. You should be able to fit one finger from your other hand between your fingertips and palm. If you can't fit a finger, size up; if there's too much space, size down. When in doubt, go smaller — you can always add overgrip to build up the handle.

**Do I need to buy strings separately?**
Not for beginners. Most racquets in this guide come pre-strung with serviceable strings. Buy separate strings only when you're restringing — ask your local pro shop what they recommend for your level.
