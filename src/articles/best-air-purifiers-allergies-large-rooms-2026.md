## Air Purifier Buyer's Guide

### How Much CADR Do You Need?

A rough rule: target a CADR of at least 2/3 your room's square footage for 5 air changes per hour. For a 300 sq ft bedroom, you need ~200 CFM. For a 600 sq ft living area, aim for 400 CFM.

### True HEPA vs. H13 HEPA

True HEPA captures 99.97% of particles at 0.3 microns. H13 HEPA captures 99.95% at 0.1 microns — higher standard, used in hospitals. For allergy and asthma sufferers, H13 HEPA (Levoit Core 400S) is worth the premium. For general air quality, True HEPA is sufficient.

### Should You Use Auto Mode?

If you have seasonal allergies or live near wildfire zones, auto mode is essential. A particle sensor detects pollen spikes and smoke events and ramps up before the air quality noticeably degrades. Without auto mode, you're reacting to symptoms rather than preventing them.

### Annual Filter Costs

Don't judge purifiers by upfront price alone. Budget the full ownership cost:

- Coway AP-1512HH: ~$120 upfront + $30/year filters
- Blueair 211+ Auto: ~$230 upfront + $70/year filters

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best air purifier for dust mite allergies?

Any True HEPA purifier works for dust mites, but the key is running it in your bedroom continuously. The Levoit Core 400S (H13 HEPA) and Coway AP-1512HH are the top picks. Run on low or auto mode 24/7 for maximum dust mite reduction.

### Can air purifiers help with pollen allergies?

Yes. True HEPA filters capture 99.97% of pollen particles. Keep windows closed during high pollen season and run your purifier continuously. An auto mode model (Coway, Winix, Levoit Core 400S) will spike fan speed during outdoor pollen peaks.

### Do air purifiers help with pet dander?

Yes — particularly models with pre-filters and HEPA stages. The Levoit Core 300's pet allergy filter variant and the Winix 5500-2's four-stage filtration are both excellent for pet households. Brush your pets regularly and vacuum before running the purifier for best results.

### Do I need one purifier per room?

Ideally yes, starting with your bedroom (where you spend the most time). A purifier rated for 400 sq ft will not meaningfully clean a 400 sq ft living room AND an adjacent 200 sq ft bedroom — air doesn't flow freely between rooms.
