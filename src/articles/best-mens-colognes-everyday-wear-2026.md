## What Makes a Great Everyday Cologne

An everyday cologne needs to earn its spot on your dresser every morning. That means it should be:

- **Versatile:** Works for office, errands, and casual evening plans — not just special occasions.
- **Non-offensive:** Fresh and clean rather than heavy and polarizing. You'll be around coworkers and strangers throughout the day.
- **Long-lasting without needing reapplication:** At least 6–8 hours of reasonable sillage. For an EDT, 2–3 sprays should get you through a full workday.
- **Seasonally flexible:** Ideally wearable in multiple seasons, or at least three of the four.

The picks below represent the strongest everyday options across different character profiles: fresh, sophisticated, aquatic, bold, and clean classic.

## How to Apply Cologne for Maximum Effect

**Apply to pulse points:** Wrists, neck (behind the ears), inner elbows, and chest. Body heat at these points continuously diffuses the fragrance throughout the day.

**Don't rub your wrists together:** This breaks fragrance molecules and kills the top notes before they finish developing. Spray and let it dry naturally.

**Layer with an unscented moisturizer:** Fragrance lasts longer on hydrated skin. Apply unscented lotion to pulse points before spraying cologne.

**Time the application:** For work, apply after your shower, before getting dressed. For evening events, apply 10–15 minutes before leaving to let the top notes settle.

**Match intensity to setting:** One spray of an EDP like Bleu de Chanel is enough for a board meeting. Two or three sprays of a lighter EDT works for casual outdoor settings.
