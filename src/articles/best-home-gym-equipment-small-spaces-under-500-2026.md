## The Small-Space Home Gym Philosophy

The mistake most people make is buying equipment that mirrors a commercial gym floor — a full rack, separate dumbbells for every weight, a barbell set. For most home trainers, this is wasteful and space-killing.

Instead, prioritize:
1. **Adjustable** over fixed (adjustable dumbbells replace 20 pairs)
2. **Foldable** or **wall-mounted** over permanent
3. **Bodyweight-first** foundation, weights as accessories
4. **Cardio efficiency** — rowers and bikes fold; treadmills don't

## What You Don't Need (Yet)

- **Barbell + plates:** Space-intensive, requires a rack or floor space you may not have. Wait until you've outgrown dumbbells.
- **Treadmill:** Unless you have a dedicated room. Foldable ones still require significant floor space when in use.
- **Cable machine:** Great equipment, impractical at home without a 7'+ anchor point and significant floor area.
- **Bench:** A 15" step riser or stability ball works for most bench movements at far lower cost and space.
