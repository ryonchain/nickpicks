## What to Avoid

- **"Satin" scarves marketed as silk** — satin is a weave pattern, not a fiber. A "satin scarf" is almost certainly polyester unless "silk satin" is explicitly stated with fiber content disclosed.
- **Printed scarves with vague wash instructions** — silk that can't be hand-washed will fade and degrade quickly.
- **Scarves without rolled edges** — machine-stitched edges cut corners on quality; hand-rolled edges are the hallmark of genuine silk scarf finishing.
- **Very cheap "100% silk" claims under $12** — mulberry silk at that price is nearly impossible to source. At under $12, the material is almost certainly synthetic regardless of labeling.
