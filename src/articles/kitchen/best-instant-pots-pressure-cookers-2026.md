## What Makes a Great Pressure Cooker?

Before we get to picks: understand what you're actually buying. Pressure cookers cut cooking time by 60–70% by trapping steam and raising the boiling point of water. A chicken that takes 90 minutes in the oven cooks in 20 minutes under pressure. Beans that need overnight soaking cook from dry in 45 minutes.

The best electric pressure cookers add:

- **Multiple cooking functions** — slow cook, sauté, steam, keep warm
- **Intuitive controls** — clear presets, not cryptic button combos
- **Quality sealing ring** — the rubber gasket is critical for safety and function
- **Easy cleanup** — removable, dishwasher-safe pot
- **Reliable pressure release** — both quick-release and natural-release options

## Common Beginner Mistakes

**Not enough liquid:** Pressure cookers need at least 1 cup of liquid to generate steam. Too little liquid and you'll get a burn warning.

**Overfilling:** Never fill past the max fill line (usually 2/3 full for most foods, 1/2 for foods that expand like grains or beans).

**Opening too fast:** Some recipes require natural pressure release — the pressure drops slowly over 10–20 minutes. Quick-releasing stews can spray hot liquid; quick-releasing legumes can cause foaming.

**Forgetting to check the sealing ring:** If the ring isn't seated properly, the pot won't pressurize. Always check before cooking.
