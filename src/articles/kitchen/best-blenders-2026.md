## How to Choose a Blender

### Daily Smoothies

For daily smoothie-making with frozen fruit, spinach, and protein powder, the question is how smooth you need your results. Budget blenders leave small chunks. Mid-range blenders (~$130–200) handle most smoothies. Vitamix turns everything invisible. If you drink green smoothies with raw kale or beet stems, spend at least $350.

### Hot Soups

Only high-performance blenders (Vitamix, Blendtec) can heat soup via friction — blending on high for 5–6 minutes raises the temperature enough to serve hot soup from cold ingredients. Mid-range blenders work for pureeing pre-cooked soups but can't friction-heat from cold.

### Nut Butters

Nut butter requires sustained high-torque blending for 2–4 minutes without motor heat buildup. Only Vitamix and Blendtec handle this reliably. Ninja and budget blenders overheat.

### Occasional Use

If you blend once a week for margaritas or salsa, a $100 Ninja handles the job without requiring a $500 investment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Vitamix worth $500?**
For daily blending, yes. Over 10 years of use, that's $50/year. The smoothing results, minimal cleanup, and near-zero failure rate over that period justify the cost versus replacing a cheap blender 3–4 times.

**What's the difference between Vitamix and Ninja?**
Vitamix motors maintain consistent RPMs under resistance; Ninja motors slow down. Vitamix blades sweep the full container diameter; Ninja blades stack vertically. Vitamix handles thick, stiff blends better. Ninja is more than adequate for everyday smoothies.

**Can I use a blender to make hot soup?**
Yes, if you have a Vitamix or Blendtec. Running on high for 5–6 minutes generates enough friction heat to serve warm soup. With any other blender, purée pre-cooked (warm) soups — the blender smooths but doesn't heat.
