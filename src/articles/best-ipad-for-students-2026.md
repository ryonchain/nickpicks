## Best Budget iPad for Students: iPad 10th Gen — ~$329

If your budget is under $400 and you primarily use the iPad for note-taking, reading, video calls, and web browsing — the base iPad is 90% of what you need at 55% of the price.

**What you lose vs. the Air:**

## iPad Pro M4: Who Actually Needs It?

The iPad Pro M4 is genuinely impressive — OLED display, M4 chip, 120Hz ProMotion, nano-texture glass option. It's also $999 for the 11" model.

**You actually need the iPad Pro if:**
- You're a music production student (GarageBand / Logic projects at 30+ tracks)
- You're an illustration or design major who does full-resolution work in Procreate
- You need the 13" display for architectural drawing or film storyboarding
- You're buying it as a laptop replacement (with Magic Keyboard)

**You don't need the iPad Pro if:**
- You take notes and read PDFs (iPad Air handles this perfectly)
- You watch videos and attend Zoom classes
- You're buying your first iPad

## iPad vs. MacBook: Should You Get Both?

Some students genuinely need both. Others can get away with just one. Here's how to decide:

| Scenario | What to Buy |
|---------|------------|
| Writing-heavy major (literature, history) | MacBook — keyboard workflow is faster |
| Diagram/sketch-heavy major (nursing, art, architecture) | iPad Air + Apple Pencil |
| Engineering/CS | MacBook — iPad won't run MATLAB/Xcode |
| General liberal arts | Either; iPad Air if you prefer handwriting |
| Both (ideal setup) | iPad Air for class + budget MacBook for writing |
