## Electric Bike Buyer's Guide

### Understanding E-Bike Classes

- **Class 1:** Pedal assist only, up to 20 mph. Allowed on most bike paths.
- **Class 2:** Pedal assist + throttle, up to 20 mph. Allowed most places Class 1 is.
- **Class 3:** Pedal assist up to 28 mph. May require helmet; check local laws.

### Key Specs to Compare

**Motor wattage:** 250W is good for flat terrain; 500W–750W handles hills and heavier riders. Peak wattage (e.g., 1,000W peak) differs from continuous rated wattage.

**Battery capacity (Wh = Volts × Amp-hours):** More Wh = more range. A 500Wh battery gives roughly 30–50 miles depending on assist level, terrain, and rider weight.

**Weight:** E-bikes range from 45–75 lbs. Lighter is better for carrying upstairs or loading into cars.

### What's Not Included (Usually)
Most e-bikes require additional purchases: a quality helmet, a U-lock or chain lock, a pump for tubeless or fat tires, and lights if not integrated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How far can an e-bike go on one charge?**
Most e-bikes in this price range go 25–60 miles per charge, depending on assist level, terrain, rider weight, and weather. Using lower assist levels significantly extends range.

**Do I need a license for an electric bike?**
In the US, Class 1, 2, and 3 e-bikes don't require a license, registration, or insurance in most states. Class 3 may require a helmet. Always check your local laws.

**Can I ride an e-bike in the rain?**
Yes — most e-bikes are rated for light rain (IP rating). Avoid submerging components. Wipe down the drivetrain and battery connections after wet rides.

**What's the best e-bike under $1,500 for commuting?**
The **Rad Power RadCity 5 Plus** is our top commuter pick for its combination of power, range, integrated accessories, and Rad Power's customer support.

**How long does an e-bike battery last before replacement?**
Most e-bike batteries last 500–1,000 charge cycles, or roughly 3–5 years of regular use. A replacement battery typically costs $300–$600.
