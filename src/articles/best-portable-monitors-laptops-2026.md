## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Portable Monitor

**Check your laptop's USB-C compatibility first.** Not all USB-C ports carry video signal. Your laptop needs a USB-C port that supports DisplayPort Alt Mode (or Thunderbolt 3/4). Check your laptop's spec sheet or look for a lightning bolt symbol next to the USB-C port. If you have USB-C but no video output, you'll need a USB-C to mini HDMI cable and a monitor with that input.

**Size and portability trade-off.** 15.6 inches is the sweet spot — large enough to be genuinely useful, small enough to fit in a standard laptop bag. 13-inch portable monitors are more packable but harder to use for productivity at a distance. 17-inch models are more comfortable to use but too large for most laptop bags.

**Resolution at size.** At 15.6 inches, Full HD (1920x1080) is acceptable; 2560x1600 is noticeably sharper; 4K is the premium. At 16 inches with a MacBook, the 16:10 2560x1600 LG gram +view renders at the same pixel density as a MacBook Pro display.

**Panel type.** IPS: consistent colors, adequate contrast, lower cost. OLED: true blacks, exceptional contrast, better color, higher cost. For productivity work without color requirements, IPS at $150-250 is the practical choice. For design or media work, OLED is worth the premium.

**Weight.** Most 15.6-inch portable monitors weigh 1.6–2.0 lb — light enough for a daypack but noticeable in a tote bag over a long commute. If you carry a monitor daily, prioritize the lighter options in this range.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best portable monitor for laptop use?**
For most remote workers: the LG gram +view 16MR70 (MacBook users) or ViewSonic VA1655 (Windows/budget). For premium display quality: the ASUS ZenScreen OLED. For budget buyers: the ViewSonic VA1655 at $150.

**Do portable monitors work with any laptop?**
Most work with laptops that have USB-C ports supporting DisplayPort Alt Mode. Check whether your USB-C port carries video — not all do. Thunderbolt 3/4 ports always support DisplayPort Alt Mode. Standard USB-C data ports may not.

**Can a portable monitor be powered by USB-C alone?**
Yes, most draw 5–10W and run off a laptop's USB-C port with no external power. Some high-brightness models need a powered USB-C hub or separate adapter for full-brightness operation.

**What resolution should a portable monitor be?**
1920x1080 minimum for a 15.6-inch display. 2560x1600 is sharper and worth the price difference for daily use. 4K is a premium that makes sense for design/editing work but draws more GPU power.

**Are portable monitors good for gaming?**
For casual gaming yes; most cap at 60Hz which is insufficient for competitive FPS. A few gaming-specific portable monitors reach 120-144Hz but are heavier and more expensive.
