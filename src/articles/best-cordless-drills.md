## How to Choose the Right Cordless Drill

**Voltage:** 12V for light tasks (picture hanging, furniture assembly). 18-20V for general DIY and home improvement. Professional users should prioritize the brand ecosystem they already own.

**Brushless vs. brushed:** Brushless motors (DeWalt, Milwaukee, Makita, Bosch) last longer and run cooler. Brushed motors are cheaper but wear faster.

**Torque:** More in-lbs means more driving power. For lag screws, deck building, or steel: 450+ in-lbs. For general home use: 300–450 in-lbs. For light tasks: 100–300 in-lbs is sufficient.

**Ecosystem:** If you already own batteries from one brand, stay in that ecosystem. Batteries are not cross-compatible between brands and account for a significant portion of system cost.

**Clutch settings:** More clutch settings give more control over screw driving depth. Valuable for precise cabinetry, drywall, and furniture work where screw depth matters.
