## Espresso Machine Buying Guide

**The grinder matters more than the machine.** Espresso requires a consistent, fine grind — blade grinders produce uneven particles that lead to channeling and sour or bitter shots. A burr grinder (entry: $50–$150 for a Baratza Encore or Timemore C3) is the single most important upgrade for home espresso.

**9 bar vs. 15 bar:** Real espresso is extracted at 9 bar. Machines marketed as "15 bar" use a low-cost vibration pump that generates 15 bar; a pressure regulator reduces it to 9 bar at the group head. Both approaches produce 9 bar extraction. The number is a marketing metric, not a quality signal.

**Thermoblock vs. boiler:** Thermoblock systems heat water on-demand — fast heat-up, compact design, but less thermal stability for back-to-back shots. Single-boiler machines offer more stability and longevity for high-volume use. For 1–2 drinks per day, thermoblock is fine.
