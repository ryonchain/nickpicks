## Buying Guide: What to Know Before You Buy

### Pressure: 9 Bars is What Matters

Most machines advertise 15-bar pumps. The extra pressure is used during pump stroke — the actual brewing pressure should be 9 bars. Focus on machines that regulate pressure properly, not just raw bar count.

### Semi-Auto vs. Pod: Key Tradeoff

| Feature | Semi-Auto | Pod Machine |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Shot quality | Excellent (with practice) | Good, consistent |
| Cost per shot | $0.20–0.50 | $0.80–1.50 |
| Effort | Moderate | Zero |
| Flexibility | Use any coffee | Proprietary pods |

### Don't Skip the Grinder

For semi-automatic machines, a burr grinder is essential. Fine, consistent grind = better extraction. Budget ~$80–150 for a decent burr grinder (Baratza Encore, Bodum Bistro).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a grinder with an espresso machine?**
Yes, for semi-automatic machines. Pre-ground coffee won't extract properly. The grinder is as important as the machine.

**How long do espresso machines last?**
Quality machines last 7–15 years with proper maintenance. Descale every 2–3 months with machine-specific solution.

**Can I make lattes with these machines?**
Yes, any machine with a steam wand can froth milk for lattes and cappuccinos. The Bambino Plus has the easiest automated steam.
