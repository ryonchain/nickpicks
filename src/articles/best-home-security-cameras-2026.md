## Security Camera Buying Guide

**Wired vs. wireless: the core tradeoff.**
- **Wired:** Continuous recording possible, never need to recharge, more reliable. Requires power source and cable routing.
- **Wireless (battery):** Install anywhere without cables. Recharging required every 3–12 months. Most do event-based recording only (not continuous).

**Resolution: 1080p vs. 2K vs. 4K.**
1080p captures general scene context. 2K improves facial recognition and license plate legibility at distance. 4K is overkill for most residential use and demands more storage.

**Subscription costs: the real ongoing expense.**
- Ring Protect: $10/month per camera or $100/year unlimited
- Arlo Secure: $10–13/month
- Nest Aware: $8–15/month
- Eufy/Wyze: Free with local storage; optional subscriptions for AI

Over 3 years, subscription costs often exceed the hardware cost. Factor this into your comparison.

**Local storage vs. cloud storage.**
Local microSD or NAS storage means no subscription fees and your footage stays private. Cloud storage means access from anywhere but adds monthly cost and puts your footage on a company's servers.

**Indoor vs. outdoor: weather rating matters.**
Look for IP66 or IP67 for outdoor cameras. IP65 works in most climates. Avoid unrated cameras outdoors.
