## Ankle Weight Buying Guide

### Weight Selection by Goal
- **Walking and toning:** 1–3 lbs per ankle (heavier affects gait)
- **Pilates, barre, yoga:** 1–5 lbs per ankle
- **Rehabilitation/PT:** Start with 1 lb, progress slowly
- **Strength training:** 5–20 lbs per ankle for serious hypertrophy

### Common Exercises for Ankle Weights
- **Donkey kicks:** Gluteus maximus activation
- **Side leg raises:** Hip abductors (gluteus medius)
- **Leg curls (lying):** Hamstrings
- **Straight leg raises:** Hip flexors and lower quad
- **Clam shells:** Hip external rotators

### Features to Look For
1. **Velcro width:** Wider velcro (2.5 in+) slips less during dynamic movements
2. **Neoprene thickness:** Thicker = more comfort for long sessions
3. **Fill material:** Iron shot (compact, dense) is better than sand (can shift, lumpy)
4. **Ankle circumference fit:** Most fit 9–18 inches; measure before buying large sizes

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will ankle weights help tone my legs?**
Yes, within limits. Ankle weights increase time-under-tension in leg isolation exercises (donkey kicks, leg raises), which directly stimulates the target muscles. For overall leg mass/shape, add compound movements (lunges, squats) alongside ankle weight work.

**Is it safe to walk with ankle weights?**
Light weights (1–2 lbs) during walks are safe and increase caloric burn. Heavier weights change your gait pattern and can increase injury risk at the hip and knee over time. Keep walking weights at or under 2 lbs per ankle.

**How do I know if my ankle weights are too heavy?**
If you can't complete the exercise through the full range of motion with control, the weight is too heavy. Reduce to a weight where you can do 15–20 controlled reps before fatigue sets in.
