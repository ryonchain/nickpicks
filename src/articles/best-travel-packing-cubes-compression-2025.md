## Why Packing Cubes Actually Work

Most people pack wrong: everything loose in one compartment, digging for items at every stop. Packing cubes solve this with category-based organization:
- **Large cubes**: tops, pants, dresses
- **Medium cubes**: accessories, tech, workout gear
- **Small cubes**: socks, underwear, cables

When you arrive, you pull out cubes into the hotel drawer — no unpacking individual items. When you leave, everything goes back in the cubes without refold. Three minutes, done.

Compression cubes add a second zip pass that forces air out, physically reducing bulk — ideal for bulky items like fleeces, jeans, or swimwear.

## How to Pack with Cubes (3-Minute System)

1. **Sort by category**: tops, bottoms, socks/underwear, accessories, tech
2. **Roll, don't fold**: rolling reduces wrinkles and fits more in each cube
3. **Large cube**: pants and shirts in two layers, rolled tight
4. **Medium cube**: cables, chargers, adapters, sunglasses
5. **Small cube**: socks rolled into balls, underwear folded flat
6. **Leave one empty**: you'll pick up things at your destination

At your hotel, pull all cubes into the drawer. Repacking takes 3 minutes because nothing unfolded.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do packing cubes actually save space?**
Regular packing cubes organize your suitcase but don't compress. Compression packing cubes (with a secondary zip) squeeze air out of clothes and can reduce clothing volume by 25–30%. Both types make unpacking and repacking dramatically faster.

**What size packing cubes do I need?**
For a carry-on, a set with small (2L), medium (4L), and large (8L) cubes handles most trips. Small for socks/underwear, medium for shirts, large for pants and bulkier items. Most 6-piece sets include a mix of all sizes.

**Are expensive packing cubes worth it?**
Eagle Creek and Away cubes are genuinely better built — YKK zippers, reinforced seams, and decade-long warranties. But $20 sets from Gonex or BAGAIL perform nearly identically for most travelers. Price matters less than using them consistently.

**Can you use packing cubes in a backpack?**
Yes — packing cubes were originally designed for backpacking. The key is choosing flexible cubes rather than rigid-sided ones, so they conform to the backpack's shape. Eagle Creek Specter and Gonex sets work well in backpacks.

**How many packing cubes do I need for a week-long trip?**
A 6-piece set is typically enough for a 7-day trip in a medium suitcase: 2 large cubes for clothes, 1 medium for accessories, 1 medium for electronics, 1 small for toiletries layout, 1 small for socks and underwear.
