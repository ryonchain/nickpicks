## FAQ

**What is the difference between KSM-66 and Sensoril ashwagandha?**
KSM-66 is a full-spectrum root extract standardized to 5% withanolides, widely used in research on physical performance and stress. Sensoril is a root and leaf extract standardized to 10% withanolides at a lower dose (125–250mg vs KSM-66's 300–600mg). KSM-66 has more published human clinical trials; Sensoril is often used in lower-dose products and nootropic stacks.

**How long does ashwagandha take to work?**
Customers widely report that ashwagandha effects build over 4–8 weeks of consistent use. Research trials typically run 8–12 weeks to measure stress, recovery, and hormonal outcomes. Unlike stimulants, ashwagandha is an adaptogen with cumulative effects — don't expect immediate results.

**What dose of ashwagandha should I take?**
The most commonly studied dose is 300–600mg of KSM-66 or a full-spectrum root extract per day. Some studies use up to 600mg twice daily (1,200mg total). For Sensoril, the studied range is 125–250mg daily. Always follow the product label and start at the lower end of the range.

**Can I take ashwagandha every day?**
Yes — ashwagandha is widely used as a daily supplement. Some practitioners recommend cycling (8–12 weeks on, 2–4 weeks off) to assess ongoing need and prevent adaptation, though this is not universally recommended. Consult a healthcare provider if you are pregnant, on thyroid medication, or have autoimmune conditions.
