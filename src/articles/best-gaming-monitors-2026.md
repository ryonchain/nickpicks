## Buying Guide: Resolution, Refresh Rate, and Panel Type

### Resolution Guide

**1080p (1920×1080):** Standard HD. Fine at 24 inches, soft at 27 inches. Your GPU needs to hit ~144+ fps to justify a high-refresh monitor. Best for: budget builds, competitive gamers on older hardware.

**1440p (2560×1440):** The sweet spot. Sharp at 27 inches, excellent text clarity, demanding but achievable for modern mid-range GPUs (RTX 4070, RX 7800 XT). Best for: most PC gamers in 2026.

**4K (3840×2160):** Beautiful. Requires a powerful GPU (RTX 4080+) to maintain high frame rates. Diminishing returns for fast-paced games where resolution matters less than frame rate. Best for: single-player cinematic gaming, console gaming, productivity.

### Refresh Rate Guide

- **60 Hz:** Minimum. If you're gaming at 60 Hz, upgrading to 144+ Hz is the single biggest perceived improvement in gaming.
- **144 Hz:** The baseline for PC gaming. Smooth, responsive, accessible.
- **165 Hz:** The sweet spot for most builds. Perceptibly smoother than 144 Hz.
- **240 Hz:** Competitive advantage in FPS games. Requires a powerful GPU to fully utilize.
- **360 Hz+:** Esports professionals. Marginal gains over 240 Hz for casual gamers.

### Panel Type Summary

| Panel | Strengths | Weaknesses | Best For |
|-------|-----------|------------|----------|
| IPS | Color accuracy, fast response | Average contrast | Competitive + creative |
| VA | High contrast, vivid dark scenes | Ghosting in fast motion | Single-player, cinematic |
| OLED | Perfect blacks, fastest response | Burn-in risk, expensive | Premium cinematic + competitive |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I buy a 1440p or 4K gaming monitor?**
For most gamers: 1440p. At 4K, you need an RTX 4080 or better to maintain 60+ fps in demanding games. 1440p on a modern mid-range GPU gives you higher frame rates and better motion clarity than 4K at lower frame rates.

**Is 27 inches too big for a gaming desk?**
27 inches is the sweet spot for 1440p at normal desk depth (24–30 inches from screen to eyes). At 24 inches distance, 27 inches fills ~55° of your horizontal field of view — comfortable and immersive without being overwhelming.

**What's the difference between G-Sync and FreeSync?**
Both eliminate screen tearing by syncing monitor refresh rate to GPU frame rate. G-Sync works natively with Nvidia GPUs; FreeSync with AMD. G-Sync Compatible monitors (Nvidia certified) work with Nvidia cards via FreeSync — effectively making most modern high-refresh monitors cross-compatible.

**Do I need HDR for gaming?**
HDR adds visible impact in dark, high-contrast scenes (space, horror, night environments). For meaningful HDR, you need a panel with either Mini-LED backlighting or OLED (for true local dimming). Most "HDR400" monitors are marketing claims — the entry bar for perceptible HDR is HDR600+ with local dimming.
