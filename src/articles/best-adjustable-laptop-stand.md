# Best Adjustable Laptop Stand: Top 5 Ergonomic Picks for 2024

## Why Your Neck Needs a Laptop Stand

If you're using a laptop on a desk without a stand, your neck is bent down at an angle that strains muscles and compresses the cervical spine over hours of work. A laptop stand raises the screen to eye level, which eliminates neck pain, reduces shoulder tension, and — if paired with an external keyboard and mouse — gives you a genuine ergonomic workstation setup from a laptop.

## FAQ

**Do I need an external keyboard if I use a laptop stand?**
Yes — raising the screen to eye level makes the built-in keyboard too high to use comfortably. A separate keyboard and mouse are necessary to complete the ergonomic setup.

**What laptop size should I check for compatibility?**
Most stands accommodate up to 15" or 17" laptops. The Lamicall and Roost both support up to 17–18", making them better choices for larger MacBook Pros or Windows gaming laptops.

**Is aluminum better than plastic for a laptop stand?**
Aluminum is more durable, dissipates heat better (helping laptop cooling), and generally looks more premium. For a permanent desk setup, aluminum is worth the premium. For travel, high-quality plastic like the Nexstand is lighter.

**Will a laptop stand help with overheating?**
Yes — elevating the laptop improves airflow beneath it, which can reduce operating temperatures by 5–10°C during sustained workloads.
