## What to Look For

**Warm vs. cool light:** Cool (blue-white) light is harder on eyes and disrupts melatonin production. Warm or neutral white light is better for bedtime reading — look for 2700–3500K color temperature.

**Clip strength:** A weak clip tilts and loses position as you read. Test reviews for complaints about clipping strength.

**Brightness range:** 5–10 lux is usually enough for comfortable reading. Very bright settings can disturb partners — having multiple brightness levels gives you control.

**Rechargeable vs. battery:** Rechargeable is more convenient long-term. Battery-powered is better for travel or emergencies.
