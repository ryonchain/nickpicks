## Why Microplane Specifically

Microplane started as a woodworking tool company. In the mid-1990s, a woman in Canada used her husband's woodworking file to zest a lemon and realized the stainless steel etching technique produced a dramatically sharper kitchen grater than anything sold at the time. The company pivoted, and Microplane graters are now in professional kitchens worldwide.

The difference from a standard box grater or drugstore zester: Microplane's photo-etching process creates razor-sharp holes that cut food cells cleanly. Competing box graters tear food cells, which releases more moisture and creates a different texture. With citrus specifically, a Microplane produces dry, fluffy zest; a conventional grater produces wet, compressed zest. This matters for baking, cocktails, and any dish where you want the bright aromatic oils from the peel without the bitter pith.

**What Microplane graters excel at:**
- Citrus zest (lemon, lime, orange, grapefruit)
- Hard cheeses (parmesan, pecorino, aged cheddar)
- Fresh ginger, garlic, and horseradish
- Dark chocolate and cocoa over desserts

## Using Your Microplane: Pro Techniques

**Zesting citrus.** Wash the fruit. Hold the zester over a bowl or cutting board. Move the fruit across the blade (not the blade across the fruit) in one direction, rotating as you go. Stop when you see white — that's bitter pith. Zest: the flavorful outer layer only.

**Parmesan over pasta.** Hold the zester perpendicular over the bowl and move the cheese block in one direction. You want snow-like flakes, not shreds. A cold, hard parmesan block works better than room-temperature cheese.

**Fresh ginger.** Peel with a spoon (edge of a spoon removes ginger skin more efficiently than a peeler). Run the peeled ginger across a fine Microplane over your dish or a small bowl. The resulting paste is more intense than chopped ginger.

**Frozen butter into pastry.** Freeze a stick of butter for 20 minutes. Grate on the coarse Microplane directly into flour before making biscuits, pie crust, or scones. The tiny frozen butter pieces distribute more evenly than hand-cutting and produce flakier results.

**Cleaning.** Rinse under hot water immediately after use — dried food bonds to the etched metal. A pastry brush or vegetable brush removes stuck food. Dishwasher is fine but degrades sharpness faster.

## FAQ

**How long does a Microplane stay sharp?**
With proper care (hand washing, not dishwashing), a Microplane blade stays sharp for 5–8 years of regular home use. Dishwashers accelerate dulling significantly.

**Can I use a Microplane on soft cheese?**
No — soft cheese (brie, fresh mozzarella, cream cheese) clogs the holes and tears rather than grating cleanly. Microplanes work for firm, dry cheeses only.

**What's the difference between a zester and a grater?**
In the Microplane lineup: fine = zester (citrus, chocolate, hard cheese). Coarse = grater (large shreds of ginger, vegetables, cheddar). The shape is the same — a flat blade — unlike a box grater which is a four-sided tool.

**Is Microplane overrated?**
No. The sharpness improvement over conventional graters is measurable and produces visibly better results for zesting specifically. It's not marketing; the cooking difference is real.
