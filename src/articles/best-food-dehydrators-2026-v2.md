## Horizontal vs Vertical Airflow: What Actually Matters

**Horizontal rear-fan airflow** (Cosori, Excalibur, BioChef) pushes air across each tray evenly. Results: consistent dehydration on every tray simultaneously, no rotation required. This is the right design for serious use.

**Vertical top or bottom fan** (Nesco, cheaper round dehydrators) pushes air up or down through stacked trays. The tray closest to the fan dries faster; bottom trays get less airflow. Rotation (moving trays every few hours) is required for consistent results. Acceptable at the budget tier; annoying if you want to set and forget.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Tray count for your use case:**
- 4–5 trays: casual use, herbs, small jerky batches
- 6 trays: standard home use, 3–5 lbs jerky, a few pounds of fruit
- 9 trays: bulk processing, hunting season, homesteading

**Temperature range for food safety:**
- Beef jerky: USDA recommends 160°F internal meat temperature. Use a meat thermometer to verify, regardless of dehydrator setting.
- Fruit and vegetables: 125°–135°F works well
- Herbs: 95°–115°F preserves essential oils
- Raw food: 77°–95°F required

**Timer and shutoff:** An automatic shutoff timer is almost mandatory for overnight drying sessions. Budget dehydrators without timers require separate outlet timers or manual monitoring.
