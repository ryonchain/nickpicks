## Hair Dryer Buying Guide

**Wattage and drying speed:** More wattage = faster drying. Consumer dryers run 1000–1600W. Professional dryers run 1800–2000W. For thin or short hair, 1200–1400W is sufficient. For long or thick hair, target 1600W minimum.

**Ionic technology:** Negative ions close the hair cuticle during drying, reducing frizz and increasing shine. All premium dryers include it; budget dryers vary. Look for "tourmaline ionic" or "nano titanium ionic" labels.

**Heat settings:** More settings = more control. 3-heat, 3-speed is the professional standard. For fine or damaged hair, you need a genuine low-heat option — not just a cool shot. Dyson's Intelligent Heat Control is the most sophisticated implementation.

**Weight and balance:** A lighter dryer (1.3–1.8 lbs) reduces arm fatigue on longer styling sessions. Handle-mounted motors (Dyson, Shark) distribute weight more evenly than barrel-mounted motors.

**Attachments:** A diffuser is essential for curly hair. A concentrator nozzle narrows airflow for smooth, straight styles. Magnetic attachments (Dyson, Shark) are faster to change than press-fit options.
