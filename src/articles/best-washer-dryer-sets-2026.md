## Washer and Dryer Buying Guide

### Front-Load vs. Top-Load

| Factor | Front-Load | Top-Load |
|---|---|---|
| Water use | ~12–14 gallons/cycle | ~18–25 gallons/cycle |
| Cleaning performance | Excellent (tumble action) | Very good (agitator/impeller) |
| Cycle time | Longer (45–75 min) | Shorter (35–55 min) |
| Upfront cost | Higher | Lower |
| Maintenance | Door seal and gasket care | Less maintenance overall |
| Bulky items | Less accommodating | Better for large items |
| Stacking option | Yes | No |

### Capacity Guide

| Household Size | Recommended Washer Capacity |
|---|---|
| 1–2 people | 3.5–4.5 cu ft |
| 3–4 people | 4.5–5.0 cu ft |
| 5+ people | 5.0+ cu ft |
| King comforter test | Need 4.5+ cu ft to wash without damage |

### Gas vs. Electric Dryer

- **Gas dryers** typically cost $50–$100 more upfront but run at approximately half the operating cost of electric. If your home has a gas hookup, gas is nearly always the better long-term choice.
- **Electric dryers** are simpler to install and work on any standard 240V outlet. The right choice when gas isn't available.

### Installation Checklist

1. Measure opening dimensions (width, depth, height with door clearance)
2. Confirm gas vs. electric dryer compatibility with existing hookup
3. Check drain standpipe height (washer drain hose must reach it)
4. Budget for stacking kit if stacked configuration is planned ($30–$100 extra)
5. Confirm delivery includes both units and removal of existing appliances
