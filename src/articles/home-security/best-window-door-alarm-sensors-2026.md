## Sensor Technology: What to Know

**Magnetic contact sensors** are the standard for doors and windows. Two pieces — one on the frame, one on the door — trigger when separated. Reliable, long battery life, inexpensive.

**Z-Wave and Zigbee sensors** (Ring, Samsung SmartThings) use mesh networking protocols, which are more reliable than Wi-Fi in large homes. They require a compatible hub but handle interference better.

**Wi-Fi sensors** (Wyze, Govee) connect directly to your router. Easy setup but dependent on Wi-Fi strength and can drain batteries faster.

**Frequency:** Most alarm systems operate on 319–434 MHz or Z-Wave 908 MHz. Interference with consumer Wi-Fi (2.4/5GHz) is rare.

## How Many Sensors Do You Need?

Cover every first-floor entry point as a minimum:
- All exterior doors (front, back, side, garage entry)
- All ground-floor windows, especially those not easily visible from the street
- Basement entry points and windows

Upper-floor windows add meaningful coverage for multi-story homes with accessible rooflines. A 3-bedroom single-story home typically needs 8–14 sensors for full coverage.
