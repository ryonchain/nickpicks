## Key Buying Considerations

**Fire rating:** Look for UL or ETL verified ratings. "30-minute at 1200°F" is basic; "60-minute at 1700°F" (SentrySafe SFW123) covers house fires more reliably. Paper chars at 451°F; USB drives and hard drives fail at 125°F — look for "media-rated" safes for digital storage.

**Burglary resistance:** Consumer safes rated UL RSC (Residential Security Container) have been tested against tool attacks for 5 minutes. Premium ratings (TL-15, TL-30) mean 15–30 minute tool attack resistance — used by businesses, not typically needed for homes.

**Anchor it:** Any safe under 300 lbs can be carried off by two people. Bolt-down hardware (included with most SentrySafe models) prevents this. Anchor to the floor or a structural wall stud.

**Lock type:** Electronic keypads are convenient but require batteries. Biometric is fast but can fail. Key locks never run out of battery. Best practice: choose a safe with both electronic and backup key.
