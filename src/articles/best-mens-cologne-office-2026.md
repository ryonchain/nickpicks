## What Makes a Good Office Cologne

Not every great cologne is appropriate for work. Office environments require restraint:

- **Projection:** Should be detectable up close, not across the conference room. Light-to-medium projection is ideal.
- **Scent family:** Fresh, clean, and woody scents are office-appropriate. Heavy orientals (oud, vanilla, musk at high concentrations) can be overwhelming in close quarters.
- **Longevity:** Needs to last a full workday (6–8 hours) without reapplication.
- **Inoffensiveness:** You're not dressing to impress yourself — you're sharing air with colleagues. Universal appeal matters.

Under $80 budget also changes the calculus: niche and artisan fragrances worth the price ($150+) are excluded. These are accessible, high-quality options at each price tier.

## Tips for Office Cologne Success

**Apply to skin, not clothes:** Cologne on skin reacts with your body heat to project and evolve. On fabric, it just sits static and can stain.

**Spray, don't dab:** The spray mechanism atomizes fragrance into micro-droplets that distribute evenly. Dabbing concentrates fragrance in one spot and can be too intense.

**Apply before dressing:** Gives fragrance time to absorb and the initial alcohol carrier to evaporate before you put on a shirt.

**Rotate seasonally:** Light aquatics in warm months; woody ambers in cool months. The same cologne that's perfect in February may be too heavy in August.

**Less is always more in close-quarters environments:** You can always add another spray tomorrow. You can't remove a cologne that everyone in the meeting noticed.
