## Inflatable Hot Tub Buying Guide

### Electricity Costs: What to Expect

Running an inflatable hot tub costs $30–$75/month depending on:
- Climate (cold climates = more heating energy)
- Usage frequency
- Insulation quality of the tub

Practical math: Heating 170 gallons from 65°F to 104°F uses approximately 7–9 kWh. Maintaining temperature daily: 2–5 kWh/day. At $0.13/kWh average national rate: $8–$20/month for maintenance heating, plus $1–$1.50 per heat-up from cold.

### Chemical Maintenance Requirements

Inflatable hot tubs require water chemistry maintenance to prevent bacteria, algae, and scale buildup:
- **Chlorine or bromine:** Weekly testing and adjustment (tablets + test strips)
- **pH balancer:** Maintain pH 7.2–7.6
- **Shock treatment:** Weekly oxidizing shock keeps water clear
- **Algaecide:** Monthly preventive treatment

Total chemical cost: $15–$30/month. Filter cartridges: replace every 2–4 weeks ($5–$10 each).

**Alternative:** Saltwater systems ($100–$200 add-on) generate chlorine automatically and reduce manual maintenance significantly.

### Year-Round Use: What Temperature Limits Apply?

Most inflatable hot tubs are rated for use when ambient air temperatures stay above 40°F. Below that, the pump heater struggles to maintain 104°F. Some premium models (MSPA, higher-end Intex) include insulation that allows use in lower temperatures — check specifications before purchasing for cold-climate use.

### Placement Requirements

- **Level surface:** Concrete, pavers, or well-leveled ground. Avoid soft grass — it can sag under the weight of 170 gallons (1,400 lbs of water) plus occupants.
- **Electrical access:** Most units require a standard 120V outlet within 10 feet.
- **Drainage:** Plan for where you'll drain the water (garden hose to street or lawn). Draining takes 1–2 hours with the included drain valve.

## FAQ

**How long does an inflatable hot tub last?**
With proper care (proper chemical balance, off-season storage, avoiding sharp objects), quality inflatable hot tubs last 3–7 years. Hard-shell spas last 15–25 years. The 3–5× price difference means inflatables often win on cost-per-year calculations.

**Can inflatable hot tubs be used in winter?**
Yes, with caveats. Most models maintain 104°F to ambient temperatures of 40°F. In colder climates, use an insulating cover (most models include one) and expect higher electricity costs. Some owners use inflatable tubs year-round in climates as cold as 20°F with a premium insulated model.

**How many gallons does an inflatable hot tub hold?**
Small models (2–3 person): 60–80 gallons. Medium (4 person): 100–130 gallons. Large (6 person): 170–240 gallons. Fill time with a standard garden hose: 1–2 hours depending on water pressure.
