Video quality in low light matters more than megapixel count — most accidents happen in dawn, dusk, or nighttime conditions.

---

## Dash Camera Buying Criteria

**Sensor quality over resolution.** A 4K camera with a poor sensor can produce worse nighttime footage than a 1440p camera with a Sony Starvis 2 sensor. Check sensor spec and look for actual low-light sample footage, not marketing claims.

**Loop recording and storage.** Dash cameras continuously overwrite the oldest footage when storage is full (loop recording). Most use microSD cards — verify the camera supports the capacity you need. High-endurance microSD cards (Samsung Pro Endurance, SanDisk High Endurance) last significantly longer in dash cameras due to continuous write cycles.

**Parking mode.** When the car is off, parking mode records motion or impact events. Hardwire kits draw power from the car's fuse box. Battery-only parking mode is limited by the internal battery duration (typically 30–60 minutes of standby).

**GPS logging.** GPS records location, speed, and g-force data alongside video — useful for insurance disputes and mapping your route.

## Dash Camera Setup Guide

**Install position:** Mount behind the rearview mirror, centered, as high as possible without blocking your view. This minimizes glare from the sun while maximizing road coverage.

**Use high-endurance microSD cards.** Standard cards fail within months under constant dash cam write cycles. Use Samsung Pro Endurance, SanDisk High Endurance, or Transcend High Endurance. Minimum 64GB, 128GB preferred.

**Format regularly.** Format the card in the camera (not your computer) monthly to prevent file system corruption. Never remove the card while the camera is recording.

**Parking mode needs power.** Without a hardwire kit, parking mode relies on the internal battery — typically 30–60 minutes of monitoring. For long-term parking coverage, run a hardwire kit to a fused slot in your car's fuse box.

**Check local recording laws.** Some jurisdictions restrict audio recording of vehicle occupants without consent. Most dash cameras allow disabling audio recording in settings.
