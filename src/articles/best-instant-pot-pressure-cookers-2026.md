## The Case for Pressure Cooking in 2026

A good electric pressure cooker cuts cook times by 70% without sacrificing flavor. Dried beans in 30 minutes. Chicken thighs from frozen in 25 minutes. Braised short ribs in 45 minutes instead of 3 hours. And unlike a stovetop setup, you set it and walk away — no monitoring, no spills, no steam burns.

Instant Pot pioneered the category and still dominates it. But Ninja, Breville, and a few others have closed the gap — and for specific use cases, they're genuinely better.

This guide covers six of the best electric pressure cookers in 2026, from the $80 entry-level pick to the $200 premium unit worth every dollar.

---

## Pressure Cooker vs. Multi-Cooker: What You're Actually Buying

**Electric pressure cookers** do one thing well: pressure cook. They're faster, simpler, and cheaper.

**Multi-cookers** (the Instant Pot category) add sauté, slow cook, steam, yogurt, air fry, and more. The tradeoff: more functions = more opportunities for mediocre results in each mode. The pressure cooking function on any Instant Pot is excellent. The air fry function on a combo unit is almost always worse than a dedicated air fryer.

**Our recommendation:** If you want one appliance that does it all, the Instant Pot Duo or Pro is the pick. If you already own a good air fryer (see [our air fryer guide](/articles/best-air-fryers-2026/)), skip the combo unit.

## Instant Pot Size Guide

**3 Qt (Mini):** Couples, singles, or tight counter space. Too small for whole chickens or large batch cooking.

**6 Qt:** The sweet spot for 2–5 people. Fits whole chickens (up to 5 lbs), large batches of soup (6+ servings), and a full pound of dried beans.

**8 Qt:** Families of 5+, meal preppers, or anyone who regularly cooks for crowds. Takes longer to pressurize and takes up more counter space.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best Instant Pot to buy?**
The Instant Pot Duo 7-in-1 (6 Qt) is the best for most households — it pressure cooks, slow cooks, sautés, steams, and keeps warm at a price that's frequently under $80 on sale.

**How much faster is pressure cooking than regular cooking?**
Pressure cooking cuts most cooking times by 60–70%. Dried beans that take 90 minutes on the stovetop cook in 25–30 minutes. A whole chicken that roasts for 90 minutes is done in 25 minutes. Rice finishes in 3 minutes of pressure cooking.

**Is the Ninja Foodi worth buying over an Instant Pot?**
The Ninja Foodi 9-in-1 is worth the extra cost if you want air frying capability in the same unit — it pressure cooks identically to the Instant Pot Pro but adds a crisping lid for roasting and air frying without a second appliance.

**What size Instant Pot is best for a family?**
A 6 qt model is the best size for 3–5 people — it handles a 3-pound roast, a whole chicken, or a large batch of soup without being oversized for smaller meals. The 8 qt is better for meal prepping or families of 6+.

**Are electric pressure cookers safe?**
Yes. Modern electric pressure cookers like the Instant Pot have multiple safety mechanisms including pressure release valves, lid-lock sensors, and automatic temperature controls. They're significantly safer than stovetop pressure cookers.
