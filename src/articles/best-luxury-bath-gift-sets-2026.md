## What Makes a Luxury Bath Set Actually Luxury

### Ingredient Concentration
Cheap bath products use large quantities of water, cheap surfactants (SLS/SLES), and synthetic fragrance at minimum percentages. Luxury formulas use higher active-ingredient concentrations—essential oils at 2–5% versus the 0.5% that most mass-market products use.

### Fragrance Architecture
Mass-market fragrance is applied on top of a formula. Luxury fragrance is integrated into the formula—the bath oil smells the same at the bottom of the bottle as the top, and the fragrance remains on the skin after rinsing rather than washing off with the product.

### Packaging Integrity
True luxury sets use glass bottles, aluminum tubes, or dense cardboard with UV coating. Fragrance degrades in clear plastic bottles; products oxidize in standard cardboard. The packaging is functional, not just decorative.

### Skin Feel
The best bath products leave skin with a perceptible difference—softness from occlusives (shea, cocoa butter, oils), not just temporary moisture from humectants (glycerin) that evaporates post-bath.
