## Multi-Cooker Buying Guide

### What Size Do You Need?

| Household Size | Recommended Capacity |
|----------------|---------------------|
| 1–2 people | 3 qt |
| 3–5 people | 6 qt |
| 6+ people / batch cooking | 8 qt |

Note: Pressure cookers should be filled no more than 2/3 full (1/2 for liquids). An 8 qt pot effectively holds ~5 qt of food — plan accordingly.

### Functions: Which Ones Will You Actually Use?

The most-used functions in order:
1. **Pressure cook** — soups, stews, beans, rice, meats
2. **Slow cook** — chili, pulled pork, overnight oats
3. **Sauté** — browning meat before pressure cooking
4. **Rice/grain** — set-and-forget rice cooking
5. **Keep warm** — holding food temperature before serving

Functions like yogurt, sous vide, and sterilize are valuable for specific users. Air fry is genuinely useful for finishing dishes — but only if you'll actually use it. Don't pay for functions you won't use within the first month.

### Cleaning: The Real Differentiator

Inner pot cleaning:
- **Stainless steel:** Dishwasher safe, more durable, nothing sticks to metal (but higher sticking risk)
- **Ceramic/non-stick:** Requires handwashing, more delicate, easier food release

The sealing ring collects odors over time. Replace every 12–18 months, or have two rings (one for savory dishes, one for sweet).

## FAQ

**How much time does pressure cooking actually save?**
Significant for specific foods: beef stew in 35 minutes vs 2.5 hours on the stovetop. Dried beans in 25 minutes vs 2+ hours of soaking and simmering. Hard-boiled eggs (easy-peel) in 5 minutes. The savings are real for long-cook items; less meaningful for fast-cooking proteins.

**Is Instant Pot safe?**
Yes. Modern multi-cookers have 10+ safety mechanisms including pressure sensors, lid-lock while pressurized, automatic pressure release, and overheat protection. The lid physically cannot open while the unit is pressurized. The safety record of Instant Pot over millions of units is excellent.

**Can I use my Instant Pot for canning?**
Pressure canning (the USDA-approved method for low-acid foods) requires more specific pressure control than most electric multi-cookers provide. The Breville Fast Slow Pro and some Instant Pot Pro models are compatible with certain canning recipes. Water-bath canning (high-acid foods like jams, pickles) works fine in any multi-cooker.

**Instant Pot vs Ninja Foodi: which is better?**
Different strengths. Instant Pot is better for pure pressure cooking performance, stainless steel durability, and price. Ninja Foodi is better when you want to air fry in the same unit. For cooking flexibility in one appliance: Ninja Foodi. For the best pressure cooker value: Instant Pot.
