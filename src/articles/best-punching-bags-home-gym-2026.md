## Hanging vs. Free-Standing: Which Should You Choose?

| | Hanging Bag | Free-Standing Bag |
|--|-------------|-------------------|
| Realism | More realistic movement | Restricted by spring/base |
| Installation | Requires ceiling mount | No installation |
| Space | More swing clearance needed | Compact footprint |
| Cost | Bag + mount hardware | Bag only |
| Best for | Serious training | Beginners, renters, limited ceilings |

**For beginners:** Start with a free-standing bag (Powercore or BOB) to learn technique without installation commitment.
**For serious training:** A hung heavy bag at 80–100 lbs with a heavy bag stand is the best training environment achievable at home.

## Essential Accessories

**Hand wraps (required):** Protect knuckles and wrist joints. Buy cotton wraps (180" length) and learn the simple wrapping technique. Without wraps, you risk wrist injury within a few sessions.

**Boxing gloves:** 12–14 oz for bag work; 14–16 oz for partner/sparring work. Everlast, Ringside, and Venum all offer quality beginner gloves.

**Heavy bag stand (if not mounting to ceiling):** Required for hung bags without beam access. Freestanding stands hold 60–100 lb bags; look for all-steel construction.

**Floor mat:** Rubber gym mat under the bag absorbs sound and prevents floor scratching from the stand.

## FAQ

**Q: What weight punching bag should I buy?**
A: A general rule: bag weight should be approximately half your body weight. A 150 lb person does well with a 70–80 lb bag. A 200 lb person should use a 100 lb bag for resistance that doesn't swing excessively but isn't completely rigid.

**Q: Can I mount a heavy bag in an apartment?**
A: A ceiling-mounted bag requires a structural joist rated for 3–4x the bag weight (for dynamic load). Most apartment ceilings are not rated for this. Free-standing bags are the practical solution for renters.

**Q: What's inside a heavy bag?**
A: Typically a combination of sand, fabric scraps, sawdust, or foam. Water-filled and foam-core bags maintain consistency; sand-fill bags can develop hard spots over time. The shell material (vinyl, leather, canvas) affects durability and feel.

**Q: Do I need boxing training to use a heavy bag?**
A: No. Basic punching technique (jab, cross, hook, uppercut) takes 10 minutes to learn from YouTube. A heavy bag delivers fitness benefits regardless of technique sophistication — but proper technique prevents wrist and shoulder injury.
