## Buying Guide: Choosing a Lighting Kit

### Continuous vs. Strobe
**Continuous lights** (LED, CFL) stay on constantly — what you see is what you get. Great for video, YouTube, and product photography where you want to see light placement before shooting.

**Strobe/flash** lights fire only when the shutter triggers — much more powerful burst of light. Better for freezing motion, overpowering sunlight, and high-volume portrait sessions where heat from continuous LED becomes a factor.

### Power output
For a home studio or bedroom setup: 60W LED is sufficient. For a living room or studio with ambient light: 100–150W. For outdoor work or overcoming direct sunlight: portable strobe (200Ws+).

### Modifiers change everything
A bare LED bulb produces harsh, directional light with hard shadows. A softbox diffuses the same light into soft, wrapping illumination. A beauty dish adds specular punch for beauty/fashion work. The right modifier for your subject matters as much as the light itself.

### Three-point lighting basics
1. **Key light:** Main light. Position at 45 degrees from subject, slightly above eye level.
2. **Fill light:** Reduces shadow intensity. Position on opposite side at lower power (1:2 or 1:4 ratio to key).
3. **Back/rim light:** Separates subject from background. Position behind and above subject pointing toward camera.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a studio to use photography lighting kits?**
No. Most of the kits listed work in bedrooms, living rooms, and garage studios. The key requirements are a space roughly 3×3 meters minimum (for a single subject) and a wall or ceiling to bounce or block unwanted light.

**Q: Are LED lights better than softboxes with CFL bulbs?**
Yes, in almost every metric: LED runs cooler, uses less electricity, lasts longer, and offers more consistent color temperature. The Neewer CFL kit earns its place only on budget grounds — given a few more dollars, buy LED.

**Q: What's the minimum lighting kit for professional-looking portraits?**
One Godox SL60W with a 60×90cm softbox as a key light, and a reflector (silver or white foam board, $10) as a fill. This two-element setup produces quality better than a three-point CFL kit for less money.

**Q: Do I need TTL (automatic flash) or can I use manual?**
Manual lighting control is fine for studio work where the subject-to-light distance doesn't change between frames. TTL is useful for event, wedding, and run-and-gun photography where camera-to-subject distance varies constantly.

**Q: Is it safe to leave LED photography lights on for long periods?**
Yes — LED lights run cool and are designed for continuous operation. Fan-cooled lights like the Godox SL60W handle extended video recording sessions without overheating. CFL lights run warmer and shouldn't be left on unattended.
