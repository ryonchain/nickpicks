August and September are the best time to buy summer gear. Retailers need to clear inventory before fall merchandise arrives, so prices on grills, patio furniture, AC units, outdoor furniture, and pool equipment drop 30–60% below their spring peaks. Here's a calendar-driven guide to what's worth buying and when.

---

## Why End-of-Summer Is a Buyer's Market

Summer merchandise has a shelf life. Retailers order spring inventory in February and need it gone by Labor Day to make room for fall and holiday products. This creates a predictable clearance cycle:

- **Late July:** First markdowns on slow-moving summer inventory
- **August 1–15:** Broad 20–30% discounts on outdoor furniture and grills
- **August 15 – Labor Day:** Deepest discounts of the season — 40–60% off on clearance items
- **Post-Labor Day:** Final clearance, but limited selection

The rule: if you can buy it now and store it until next spring, the wait pays off.

## Best End-of-Summer Deal Categories

### 1. Grills & BBQ Equipment

**Best time to buy: Mid-August through Labor Day**

Grills are the canonical end-of-summer deal. Weber, Traeger, and Char-Broil all discount their lineup by 20–40% in August to move units before fall. What you'll find:

**Propane grills:** Weber Genesis and Spirit models often drop $100–200 from their spring list price. A $699 Weber Spirit regularly hits $499 in late August.

**Pellet grills:** Traeger Pro 575 and Pit Boss models hit their annual lows in August — typically $50–150 off regular price.

**Charcoal grills:** Smaller charcoal grills (Weber Original Kettle, $99 spring price) often drop to $65–75 in late August.

**What to buy:**
- Buy if you plan to grill into fall or want to store for next season
- Don't buy if you only grill in June–July — you'll overpay in spring

---

### 2. Patio Furniture

**Best time to buy: August 1 through mid-September**

This is the single biggest summer clearance category. Patio furniture is bulky, seasonal, and retailers hate carrying it into October. Expect:

- **Conversation sets** (4-6 piece): 30–50% off in August (a $1,200 set dropping to $600–800 is common)
- **Lounge chairs and chaises:** 40–60% off
- **Dining sets:** 25–40% off
- **Umbrellas and shade:** 50–70% off

**Retailers to check:**
- Target's Threshold and Studio McGee outdoor lines clear fast in August
- Wayfair runs major outdoor clearance events the last two weeks of August
- Home Depot and Lowe's start significant discounts around August 10
- Costco's outdoor section marks down quickly

**Key tip:** Cushion sets are often sold separately and discount even more aggressively than frames.

---

### 3. Window Air Conditioners

**Best time to buy: Late August through September**

Window AC units are one of the most predictable clearance items. Nobody buys them in October. Retailers slash prices aggressively — and that timing lines up with the first cool days when buyers lose interest.

**Typical August/September discounts:**
- Frigidaire 8,000 BTU: Spring price ~$329 → August price ~$199
- LG 10,000 BTU: Spring price ~$449 → August price ~$289
- GE 6,000 BTU: Spring price ~$229 → August price ~$149

**Should you buy one now?** If your current AC underperformed this summer, or you want to add AC to a room before next year — yes. Store it in the box for next spring.

---

### 4. Pool Equipment

**Best time to buy: Late July through mid-August**

Above-ground pools, pool toys, and pool maintenance equipment clear fast because retailers need floor space. By September, selection is decimated.

**What goes on sale:**
- Above-ground pools (Intex, Summer Waves): 30–50% off
- Pool pumps and filters: 25–40% off
- Floats, toys, and loungers: 50–70% off

**Best deals:** Floats and loungers are the no-brainer buy — they store easily, and the markdowns are significant. Giant inflatable pools and pool frames should be bought early in the clearance window before stock runs out.

---

### 5. Camping Gear

**Best time to buy: Late August**

End-of-summer camping gear clearance is excellent but brief. The good stuff sells out before September. Target these categories:

- **Tents:** Coleman 4-person tents regularly drop from $120 to $70–80 in late August
- **Sleeping bags:** 30–40% off as temperatures drop
- **Camp chairs:** Often 40–50% off (Helinox chairs being the exception)
- **Camp stoves and cookware:** 25–35% off
- **Coolers:** Rotomolded coolers (Yeti, RTIC) rarely discount, but soft coolers do

**Camping gear I'd buy:** A quality tent in your target size (2-person for couples, 4-person for families), and a set of camp chairs. These store well and will outlast their clearance price by years.

---

### 6. Outdoor Lighting

**Best time to buy: August through September**

String lights, solar pathway lights, and outdoor lanterns drop 40–60% in late summer. Home Depot and Amazon both run clearance on outdoor lighting.

**Best buys:**
- LED string lights (50-foot sets go from $30 to $10–15)
- Solar pathway lighting sets
- Outdoor lanterns and candleholders

These are small and easy to store — load up on them in August and you'll have a full outdoor lighting setup for next summer at clearance prices.

## Where to Find End-of-Summer Deals

**Amazon:** Check the Outdoor Living clearance section. Amazon often matches Target and Home Depot clearance prices, and Prime shipping is fast.

**Target:** Circle app members get early access to clearance. The patio section gets marked down aggressively starting early August — visit stores mid-week when restocking happens.

**Home Depot & Lowe's:** Both start significant outdoor clearance in early August. Visit in-store for the best selection — online inventory doesn't always reflect what's available.

**Costco:** Their outdoor furniture often sells out before it can be heavily discounted, but what remains in August is excellent value. Also check Costco.com for website-only clearance.

**Wayfair:** Runs "Summer Blowout" style sales in late August. Use their price history tools (like Camelcamelcamel for Amazon, or similar tools) to verify you're getting a real discount.

---

## What NOT to Buy at End of Summer

A few categories don't discount meaningfully at end of summer:

- **Electronics:** Back-to-school sales are the discount event, and that ends in September. Post-school season isn't a deal period.
- **Yeti & premium coolers:** They hold their price year-round
- **Power tools:** No seasonal clearance pattern
- **Bikes and outdoor recreation equipment:** Some discount, but REI's Yeti sale and clearance doesn't typically start until later in fall

| Aug 16–31 | Peak clearance: best prices on everything outdoor; camping gear |

**The rule:** If you see something in your size and it's 30%+ off, don't wait. End-of-summer clearance sells out — the $600 patio set that was $1,200 in May doesn't restock when it sells out in August.
