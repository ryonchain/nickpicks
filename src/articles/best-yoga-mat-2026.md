## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Yoga Mat

- **Thickness:** 3–4mm for travel and balance; 5–6mm for standard practice; 10mm+ for restorative/joint support.
- **Material:** Natural rubber offers the best grip but can degrade in UV; TPE is eco-friendly and odor-free; PVC is durable but not eco-friendly.
- **Grip texture:** Smooth mats slip when dry; textured or open-cell surfaces grip better when wet. Match grip type to your sweat level.
- **Dimensions:** Standard mats are 68"×24" — if you're over 6 feet tall or practice wide-arm flows, look for 72"+ length.

## FAQ

**Q: How thick should a yoga mat be for beginners?**
A: For beginners, a 5–6mm mat balances cushioning and stability well. Thicker mats (10mm+) are softer but can make balance poses harder.

**Q: Can you use a yoga mat for other workouts?**
A: Yes — yoga mats work well for pilates, stretching, and bodyweight exercises. They're not ideal for heavy weights (which can damage the surface) or jumping (too thin for impact absorption).

**Q: How do I clean a yoga mat?**
A: Wipe with a damp cloth and mild soap after every session. For deeper cleaning, mix water and white vinegar (1:1), spray, wipe, and air-dry flat. Avoid machine washing most mats.
