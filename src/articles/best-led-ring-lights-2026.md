## Ring Light Buying Guide

### Size Selection

**6–10 inch:** Desktop and video call use. Compact, easy to position, suitable for most home offices. Best for: video calls, streaming close-ups, TikTok/Instagram.

**12–14 inch:** Mid-range — balances size and light output. Best for: streaming, YouTube, small portrait sessions.

**18–19 inch:** Professional photography and video. Requires floor space. Best for: portrait photography, professional streaming, multi-person video.

### Color Temperature Explained

**2700–3200K (Warm White):** Candle/incandescent warmth. Flattering and cozy for evening content.

**4000–4500K (Neutral White):** Close to office lighting. Natural-looking for professional video calls.

**5500–6500K (Daylight):** Mimics natural daylight. Best for accurate color representation in photography.

For most video calls: **4000–5000K** produces the most natural-looking result regardless of time of day.

### Brightness and Distance

Ring light brightness is measured in lux. Guidelines:
- 500–1000 lux: Basic fill for close-range video calls (1–2 feet away)
- 1500–2500 lux: Professional video call and streaming (2–3 feet)
- 3000+ lux: Photography, large streaming setups, multi-person video

**Note:** Lux decreases with distance. A 2500-lux light at 1 foot may only deliver 800 lux at 3 feet. For accurate brightness, position consistently.

### Power Source Considerations

**USB-A/USB-C:** Convenient for desk setups, can run from laptop ports. Limited maximum brightness.

**AC-powered:** Higher maximum brightness. Required for professional studio use. Best for 18"+ lights.

**Battery-powered:** Most flexible for location use. Not recommended for stationary desk setups — AC is more reliable.
