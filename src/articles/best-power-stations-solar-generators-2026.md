### Understanding Watt-Hours (Wh)

Watt-hours = how long you can run a device.
- A 100W device running for 10 hours = 1,000Wh consumed
- Formula: Device wattage × hours needed = Wh required
- Always add 20% overhead for inverter efficiency loss

### Battery Chemistry: LFP vs. Standard Li-ion

| Feature | LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) | Standard Li-ion (NMC) |
|---------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| Cycle life | 2,000–3,500 cycles | 500–1,000 cycles |
| Weight | Heavier | Lighter |
| Thermal safety | More stable, less fire risk | Standard |
| Price | Higher | Lower |
| Best for | Long-term home backup | Portability |

## FAQ

**Can a power station replace a gas generator?**
For short outages (4–24 hours) and essential devices: yes. For multi-day outages or running a whole house: no — unless you pair 2,000Wh+ stations with 400W+ solar. Gas generators produce 3,000–8,000W continuously; portable power stations max out at 3,600W.

**Are these safe to use indoors?**
Yes — unlike gas generators, portable power stations produce zero emissions and are safe to use indoors. No carbon monoxide risk.

**How long do the batteries last?**
Standard Li-ion: 500–1,000 charge cycles to 80% capacity (1–3 years with daily use). LFP chemistry: 2,000–3,500 cycles (5–10 years). For home backup use (occasional outages), a station could last 15–20+ years.
