Black Friday 2026 is the best time of year to buy large appliances. Washers, dryers, refrigerators, dishwashers, and ranges all see significant discounts — often the deepest cuts of the entire year. If you're replacing an aging appliance, have a renovation planned, or want to upgrade before the holidays, this is your window.

## When Do Black Friday Appliance Deals Start?

Unlike TVs that often drop mid-November, large appliance deals follow a different pattern:

| Timing | What Happens |
|--------|--------------|
| Early November | First "pre-Black Friday" bundles from Home Depot, Lowe's |
| Week of Black Friday (Nov 25–28) | Peak appliance deals — buy now for holiday-week delivery |
| Cyber Monday (Dec 1) | Some deals extend; fewer new ones for appliances |
| Post-Christmas (Dec 26–Jan) | Second major appliance sale window |

**Best strategy:** Shop the week of Black Friday for appliances. Unlike electronics, appliance deals don't go live as early.

## Should You Buy on Black Friday or Wait?

**Buy on Black Friday if:**
- Your current appliance is failing or failed (don't wait)
- You're renovating and have a timeline
- A specific model you've researched drops 25%+ from MSRP

**Skip Black Friday and shop post-Christmas (Dec 26–Jan) if:**
- No urgency — post-holiday deals are the year's second-best appliance window
- You want more time to research (new models often launch in January)

## Pro Tips for Appliance Shopping

1. **Measure your space before buying** — Width, depth, height, and door clearance. Always.
2. **Check delivery lead times** — Black Friday appliances ship in 1–3 weeks, not same-day.
3. **Factor in installation costs** — Most retailers charge $79–$149 for delivery + installation + haul-away of old appliance.
4. **Match your brands** — Washer/dryer from the same brand often share the same app and optimize cycles together.
5. **Energy Star saves money long-term** — An Energy Star washer saves ~$35/year vs. standard; refrigerators save ~$50/year.
6. **Extended warranty consideration** — Appliances (unlike electronics) often make warranty sense; refrigerators and washers can have $500–$800 repair bills.
