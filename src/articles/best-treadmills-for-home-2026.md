## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Home Treadmill

**Motor rating: what to look for.**
- **Below 2.5 CHP:** Walkers and light joggers only. Will wear out under regular running use.
- **2.5–3.5 CHP:** Standard home use. Good for jogging and moderate running 3–5x/week.
- **3.5 CHP+:** Heavy use, daily running, multiple users. Commercial-grade durability.

**Folding vs. non-folding.**
Folding is convenient for most homes but adds weight and mechanical complexity. Non-folding machines are sturdier — if you have dedicated space, go non-folding.

**Belt size matters for tall runners.**
Standard belts are 20" x 55". Look for 22" x 60" or longer if you're over 6'0" or have a long stride.

**Subscription platforms: worth it?**
iFit, Peloton All-Access, and JRNY all add $30–45/month. The research on adherence is clear — coached workouts significantly improve consistency. If you'll use the platform, it's worth the ongoing cost. If you prefer running to your own playlist, save the money.

**Warranty: the hidden indicator of quality.**
Good treadmill warranties: lifetime frame, 5+ year parts, 2+ year labor. Short warranties (1 year parts) signal cheap components. Sole's 5-year parts warranty is the industry benchmark.
