## Electric vs Manual Crank: Which Should You Buy?

**Electric:** Push a button, desk moves automatically. You can save height presets (sitting and standing heights). Recommended for anyone who wants to actually switch positions throughout the day — manual cranking adds enough friction that most people just stop using it.

**Manual Crank:** No motor = lower cost and no electronics to fail. Works for people who only need to adjust height occasionally. If you'll set it once and leave it, a manual crank is fine.

**Corner/L-Shaped:** Ideal for dual-monitor setups or people who need a dedicated area for peripheral tasks. The L-shape creates a natural workflow division. Cost more and require more floor space.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Height Range:** Your standing desk needs to hit both your sitting and standing height. Use the rule: seated height = elbow height when arms are at 90 degrees; standing height = same elbow position while standing. Most desks cover 24"–48" — enough for 5'0" to 6'4" users. Taller than 6'4"? Check max height specifically.

**Weight Capacity:** Add up your monitors, laptop, monitor arms, and peripherals. A dual 27" monitor setup plus accessories typically runs 60–80 lbs. Budget 150+ lbs of rated capacity to maintain structural margin.

**Motor (Electric):** Single-motor desks wobble more at full height. Dual-motor desks cost more but are meaningfully more stable. If you plan to use your desk at standing height for hours per day, dual-motor is worth the premium.

**Stability:** The most important variable and the hardest to judge from specs. Uplift V2 has the best documented stability; FlexiSpot E7 Pro is the best stability per dollar.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should I stand per day?**
The evidence-based recommendation is 30 minutes of standing for every 30–60 minutes of sitting. Standing all day is not the goal — alternating position is. Most standing desks with presets make this easy.

**Do I need an anti-fatigue mat?**
Yes, for extended standing. An anti-fatigue mat reduces leg fatigue significantly — see our [best anti-fatigue mats guide](/articles/best-anti-fatigue-standing-desk-mats-2026/) for picks.

**Can I assemble a standing desk alone?**
Most electric desks technically allow solo assembly, but the frame is heavy and long. Having a second person for the frame and desktop placement step saves time and reduces injury risk.

**How noisy are electric standing desks?**
Modern dual-motor desks run at 45–50 dB during adjustment — comparable to a quiet conversation. Single-motor older models can reach 55–60 dB. Motors are audible but not disruptive in shared spaces.
