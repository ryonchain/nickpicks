## Sheet Mask Buying Guide

### Sheet Materials Compared
- **Cotton/fabric:** Most common, good adherence, lower serum capacity
- **Hydrogel:** Clear, gel-based, cools skin, better adherence to contours
- **Biocellulose:** Premium coconut-derived fiber, best adherence, most concentrated serum delivery
- **Foil:** Rare, creates occlusive environment for maximum absorption

### How to Maximize Sheet Mask Results
1. **Cleanse first:** Apply to clean skin free of sunscreen and makeup
2. **Apply toner:** A hydrating toner before the mask primes skin to absorb more serum
3. **15–25 minutes maximum:** Longer doesn't increase benefit — once the sheet dries, it starts drawing moisture back out of skin
4. **Pat in excess serum:** Don't rinse — pat remaining serum into skin and follow with moisturizer

### Frequency
- **Normal to dry skin:** 2–3x per week
- **Oily/acne-prone skin:** 1–2x per week (hydrating only; avoid pore-clogging formulas)
- **Pre-event:** Night before + morning of for maximum plumping effect

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do sheet masks actually work or is it mostly marketing?**
The mechanism is real: occlusion under the sheet prevents evaporation and increases absorption of active ingredients. Clinical studies on biocellulose masks show measurable increases in skin hydration for 24–48 hours. Results depend heavily on active ingredient quality and concentration.

**Can I reuse a sheet mask?**
No. Once the sheet dries, it can't rehydrate effectively. The remaining serum in the packet can be applied to hands, neck, or décolleté, but the sheet itself is single-use.

**Is it safe to sleep in a sheet mask?**
Not recommended. Once the sheet begins to dry (after 20–25 minutes), it can draw moisture from skin rather than depositing it. Extended wear also increases risk of irritation from prolonged contact with actives.
