## Smart Sprinkler Controller Buying Guide

**Do I need to replace my existing system?**
No. Smart controllers replace only the timer/controller box — the valves, pipes, and heads stay in place. Most existing multi-zone systems are compatible.

**How many zones do I need?**
Count your valve wires. Each wire = one zone. Most residential systems have 6–8 zones; larger properties may have 12–16.

**Key features to prioritize:**
1. **Weather Intelligence / skip logic** — the core value proposition. Look for ET-based watering, not just simple rain skip.
2. **FlexDaily or equivalent** — controllers that learn your yard vs. following a fixed calendar save significantly more water.
3. **Smart home integration** — Alexa, Google Home, and HomeKit support if you have a smart home ecosystem.
4. **Flow meter support** — high-end add-on that detects leaks automatically.

**Installation:**
- Turn off power to old controller
- Label each wire (usually numbered zones + common wire)
- Follow the app's zone-by-zone pairing
- Takes 20–45 minutes for most homeowners

## FAQ

**Can a smart controller work without Wi-Fi?**
Basic pre-programmed schedules will still run without Wi-Fi. You lose remote access and weather adjustment until connection is restored.

**Will a smart controller work with well water systems?**
Yes — it controls the valves regardless of water source. Just configure zone schedules according to your well's pump capacity.

**What's the difference between weather skip and seasonal adjustment?**
Weather skip pauses watering when it has recently rained or will rain soon. Seasonal adjustment changes the watering duration based on the season (50% in winter, 100% in peak summer). The best controllers do both.

**Can I monitor water usage with a smart controller?**
Yes — all top models track estimated water usage in the app. For actual flow measurement, add a compatible flow meter (Rachio and RainBird both support this).
