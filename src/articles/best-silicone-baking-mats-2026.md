## What Makes One Silicone Baking Mat Better Than Another

All silicone mats look similar, but material quality, fiberglass mesh construction, and edge sealing vary significantly between brands:

- **FDA food-grade silicone:** Look for explicit "FDA food-grade" or "LFGB-certified" claims. These standards verify the silicone is free of harmful fillers and won't leach at cooking temperatures. Avoid mats with no safety certification listed.
- **Size (half sheet vs. full sheet):** Half-sheet (11.6" × 16.5") fits standard half-sheet baking pans (18" × 13"). Full-sheet mats fit professional 26" × 18" pans. For most home kitchens, half-sheet is correct — measure your pan before buying.
- **Temperature range:** Quality silicone mats are rated from approximately -40°F to 480°F. This covers everything from freezer-to-oven prep to high-heat roasting and most baking applications. Avoid mats with temperature ratings below 425°F, which rules out pizza, high-heat roasting, and some cookie applications.
- **Non-stick performance:** The fiberglass mesh embedded in the silicone provides structure while the silicone surface releases food cleanly. Macarons, caramel, and sugar work (the most demanding tests) require the highest-quality mats. Cheaper mats fail this test.
- **Edge durability:** Exposed fiberglass edges fray over time with washing and bending. Better mats have sealed edges (silicone folded completely over the mesh perimeter). Frayed edges are both a durability and food-safety concern.
- **Ease of cleaning:** Grease and sugar residue rinse off quality silicone mats with warm soapy water. Heavily-used mats can develop a grease haze over time — soaking in warm water with baking soda resolves this. Machine washing is technically possible but degrades mats faster.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Verify the size fits your pan before buying.** Half-sheet mats fit half-sheet pans (18" × 13") — they're slightly smaller than the pan so they don't curl up at the edges. Full-sheet mats fit 26" × 18" professional pans. If you're not sure of your pan size, measure it.

**Check for FDA or LFGB certification.** These certifications are the minimum bar for food safety. Cheap mats without listed certifications may contain fillers or plasticizers not safe for high-heat cooking. The price difference between certified and uncertified mats is negligible.

**Sealed edges are non-negotiable.** Exposed fiberglass mesh at the edges frays with washing and bending. Fiberglass fibers in food are a health risk. Only buy mats with visibly sealed edges where silicone completely covers the mesh perimeter.

**Hand washing extends mat life significantly.** Dishwashers gradually degrade silicone and can cause mats to stiffen over time. Warm soapy water and a soft sponge removes almost everything. For stubborn grease residue, soak in warm water with a tablespoon of baking soda for 30 minutes.
