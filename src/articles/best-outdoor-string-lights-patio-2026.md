## What to Look For

- **IP waterproof rating:** IP44 handles rain and splashing and is fine for most covered or semi-covered patios. IP65 is fully water-jet resistant and better for exposed outdoor areas or year-round permanent installation.
- **Bulb style:** G40 globe lights are the classic patio look; S14 Edison bulbs evoke a more sophisticated bistro aesthetic; lantern lights are the most decorative and festive.
- **Bulb replaceability:** Look for strings where individual bulbs can be unscrewed and replaced. Strings with permanently installed non-replaceable bulbs become useless when bulbs fail — which they inevitably do.
- **Color temperature:** 2200K–2700K produces warm, amber-toned light that's flattering and atmospheric outdoors. Avoid anything labeled "cool white" or above 3000K for evening ambiance — it looks harsh.
- **Wire length and coverage:** Measure your space before buying. A 48ft string zigzagged creates roughly 10ft x 10ft of coverage at maximum density. Many strings are connectable end-to-end for larger spaces.
- **Dimmability:** Standard string lights run at full brightness only. Dimmable versions (with a compatible dimmer switch) let you adjust the atmosphere without changing the display.
