## How to Spot Real Leather at This Price Point

**Look for:** "Genuine leather," "full-grain leather," or "top-grain leather" in product descriptions. These are real leather — just different grades.

**Avoid:** "PU leather," "bonded leather," "vegan leather" (unless intentional) — these are synthetic and won't age as well.

**Price reality:** True full-grain leather under $100 is rare. $120–200 is the sweet spot where brands like Fossil, Frye, and Madewell can offer real leather construction.

**Hardware test:** Quality bags have metal hardware that feels weighty. Plastic snaps and lightweight zippers signal corners cut elsewhere.
