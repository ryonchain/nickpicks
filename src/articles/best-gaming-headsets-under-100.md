The gaming headset market has never been more competitive — and the sub-$100 category has never been better. ## What Makes a Great Gaming Headset Under $100?

- **Sound stage and positional audio** — hearing footsteps and direction in competitive games is critical.
- **Microphone clarity** — teammates should hear you clearly, not through static.
- **Comfort over long sessions** — look for memory foam ear cups, adjustable headbands, and lightweight builds.
- **Connectivity** — wired USB or 3.5mm is reliable; wireless models under $100 are increasingly available.
- **Cross-platform compatibility** — especially important if you game on multiple platforms.

## Top 5 Gaming Headsets Under $100

SteelSeries took everything that made the Arctis line famous and distilled it into an affordable, wired package. The Nova 1 features neodymium drivers tuned for gaming, a retractable ClearCast microphone with excellent noise cancellation, and the signature ski-goggle headband that distributes weight evenly across your head — making marathon sessions comfortable.

**Pros:**

### 2. HyperX Cloud II Wireless — Best Wireless Under $100

The Cloud II Wireless pulls off the rare trick of being wireless under $100 while delivering genuinely good audio. Up to 30 hours of battery, a detachable cardioid mic, and 7.1 virtual surround sound via its USB dongle make it a complete package. It's heavier than the Arctis Nova, but the freedom from wires is worth it for many gamers.

**Pros:**
- 2.4GHz wireless with USB-C dongle — zero noticeable lag
- 30-hour battery life
- 7.1 virtual surround sound via software
- Comfortable memory foam leatherette ear cushions
- Detachable boom microphone

**Cons:**
- Heavier than wired alternatives
- Leatherette ear cups can cause heat buildup
- Virtual surround must be enabled via HyperX software
- PS5/Nintendo Switch wireless mode requires adapter

**Price:** ~$80–$100

### 3. Razer BlackShark V2 X — Best for Competitive Gaming

Razer's TriForce 50mm drivers in the BlackShark V2 X are tuned specifically for competitive play — boosted highs help you hear footsteps and audio cues that cheaper headsets bury in the mix. The Hyperclear cardioid mic delivers crisp, clear voice and is tunable via Razer Synapse. If you play shooters or battle royale games, this is the pick.

**Pros:**
- TriForce 50mm drivers — excellent clarity for competitive gaming
- Hyperclear cardioid mic — clear voice with adjustable EQ in software
- 7.1 THX Spatial Audio support on PC
- Lightweight at 240g
- Memory foam FlowKnit ear cushions for breathability

**Cons:**
- No wireless version
- Razer Synapse software required for full features
- THX Spatial Audio is a paid subscription on PC
- Build feels plasticky compared to metal-framed competitors

**Price:** ~$50–$65
