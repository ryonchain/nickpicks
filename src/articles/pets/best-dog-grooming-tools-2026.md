## FAQ

### How often should I groom my dog at home?

- Brushing: Daily for long coats; weekly for short coats
- Deshedding: Weekly during shedding season (spring/fall); every 2 weeks otherwise
- Nail trimming: Every 3–4 weeks (nails should not click on hardwood floors)
- Full bath: Every 4–8 weeks depending on activity level
- Professional grooming (for cut breeds): Every 6–10 weeks

### Is it safe to bathe a dog at home?

Yes — use a dog-specific shampoo (human shampoo pH strips the coat's natural oils), rinse thoroughly, and dry completely. Long-coated breeds need blow-drying to prevent hot spots from trapped moisture. A handheld dog dryer speeds drying and reduces heat risk vs. human hair dryers.

### How do I stop my dog from hating nail trims?

Desensitization over weeks: Day 1, just touch the paw. Day 5, add clipper sounds. Day 10, touch nail with clipper. Day 15, one nail. Go at your dog's pace — forcing creates lasting negative associations. Pair every step with high-value treats. Most dogs can be trained to accept nail trims with patience.
