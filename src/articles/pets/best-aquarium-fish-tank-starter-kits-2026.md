## Aquarium Beginner Buying Guide

### The Nitrogen Cycle: The Most Important Concept

Before adding fish, an aquarium must cycle — beneficial bacteria must colonize the filter media to convert toxic ammonia (from fish waste) to nitrite, then to relatively harmless nitrate. This process takes 2–6 weeks. Skipping it kills fish within days. Three ways to speed the cycle:

1. **Seed with established filter media** from a cycled tank
2. **Add bottled bacteria** (Tetra SafeStart, Dr. Tim's One and Only)
3. **Fish-in cycle with hardy fish** (danios, feeder guppies) and daily water testing + changes

Test ammonia, nitrite, and nitrate with the API Freshwater Master Test Kit ($25) — not strip tests, which are inaccurate.

### Choosing Fish: Start with Easy Beginners

Not all fish are equal difficulty. Beginner-friendly species:
- **Guppies** (livebearers, hardy, colorful)
- **Platies and mollies** (livebearers, adaptable)
- **Neon and cardinal tetras** (schooling, peaceful — need 6+ in school)
- **Corydoras catfish** (bottom cleaners, peaceful)
- **Betta fish** (solo tank — highly aggressive with other bettas)

Avoid as a beginner: goldfish (require 30+ gallons and cooler temps), discus (require pristine water chemistry), wild-caught fish (disease-prone).

### Equipment You'll Need Beyond the Kit

Even the best starter kits are missing:
- **Adjustable heater** ($20–30): The preset heaters in most kits can't be adjusted for different tropical species
- **API Master Test Kit** ($25): Accurate water testing is non-negotiable
- **Gravel vacuum** ($15): Weekly partial water changes are essential
- **Water dechlorinator** ($8): Tetra AquaSafe or Seachem Prime

Budget an additional $50–80 beyond the kit cost for these essentials.

## FAQ

### How often do I need to change aquarium water?

For a properly cycled aquarium: 25–30% water change weekly for most freshwater setups. This exports nitrates (the final product of the nitrogen cycle). Larger and less densely stocked tanks can go 2 weeks between changes. Never do 100% water changes — it removes beneficial bacteria from the substrate.

### Can I add fish the same day I set up the tank?

No. You need at least a cycled aquarium (2–6 weeks minimum without bacterial supplements, or 7–14 days with Dr. Tim's One and Only and a careful fish-in cycle). Adding fish to an uncycled tank kills them within days from ammonia poisoning.

### What's the easiest fish for beginners?

Betta fish in a 5+ gallon tank, or a community of guppies and corydoras in a 20-gallon. Both are hardy, forgiving of beginner water chemistry mistakes (within limits), and visually rewarding.
