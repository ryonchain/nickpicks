## Automatic Pet Feeder Buying Guide

### Gravity vs. Scheduled Dispensers

**Gravity feeders:** The kibble slides down as the pet eats from the bowl. No scheduling, no portioning. Good for cats who self-regulate (rare); bad for dogs or food-motivated cats that will eat until sick. Never use a gravity feeder for a pet on a portioned diet.

**Scheduled dispensers:** Release a measured portion at set times. The correct choice for any pet on a diet, multiple pets with different schedules, or fast-eating dogs.

### Wet Food Feeders

Most automatic feeders are designed for dry kibble. Wet food requires a specialized feeder with a sealed, refrigerated compartment — standard feeders with wet food grow bacteria and spoil quickly. Look for feeders specifically marketed as "wet food compatible" with ice-pack compartments. Options include the PETLIBRO Seal series.

### Dual-Pet Households

Two cats with different dietary needs (one on a diet, one not) need separate feeders or a feeder with individual access control. Microchip-activated feeders open only for the registered pet's chip. The SureFeed Microchip Pet Feeder is the best option for this use case.

### Hopper Capacity: How Often Will You Fill It?

- Average cat (¼ to ½ cup/day): A 5L hopper lasts ~30–60 days
- Medium dog (2 cups/day): A 15-cup hopper lasts ~7 days
- Large dog (4+ cups/day): A 20-cup hopper lasts ~5 days

Size up the hopper if you want weekly (not daily) refills.

## FAQ

### Will an automatic feeder work during a power outage?

Only if it has battery backup. Always choose a feeder with D-cell battery backup if power reliability is a concern. Note that Wi-Fi-connected features (app control, camera) typically don't work on batteries — only the scheduled dispensing continues.

### Can two pets eat from the same feeder?

Not reliably. Dominant pets will eat the subordinate's portion. Use separate feeders positioned apart, or a microchip-activated feeder that only opens for the assigned pet's chip.

### Do automatic feeders work for wet food?

Standard dry kibble feeders do not work for wet food — the moist food clogs the dispenser and spoils quickly without refrigeration. Use a wet food feeder specifically designed for canned or pouch food, typically with an ice pack compartment.

### How do I transition my pet to an automatic feeder?

Run the feeder at your normal feeding time for the first week (not replacing your routine — running it alongside so the sound becomes familiar). On day 7, transition fully to feeder-only. Most cats adapt within 2–3 days; dogs even faster.
