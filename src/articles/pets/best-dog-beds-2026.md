## Dog Bed Buying Guide

### Memory Foam vs. Standard Foam

**Memory foam** (orthopedic grade, 3–5 lb/ft³): Conforms to the body, distributes pressure evenly, ideal for joint pain, arthritis, and post-surgery recovery. Higher cost.

**Standard polyester fill**: Light, inexpensive, washable, not supportive enough for dogs with joint issues. Fine for young, healthy dogs without orthopedic needs.

**Egg crate foam**: The wavy foam used in cheap "orthopedic" beds provides more air circulation but less actual support than solid memory foam. Often marketed as orthopedic but performs closer to standard foam.

### Size: Measure Your Dog First

Always measure your dog nose-to-tail when stretched out and add 6–12 inches. Most "large" beds (36"x27") are sized for 50–65 lb dogs, not the 80–100 lb dogs they're sometimes sold for. Giant breeds need XL or XXL sizing — verify the listed dimensions match your dog's measurements, not just the weight recommendation.

### Washability: A Non-Negotiable for Most Owners

Dogs track in dirt, shed, drool, and have accidents. Any dog bed you buy should have:
1. A removable, zipper-off cover
2. Machine-wash compatibility for the cover
3. (Ideally) a waterproof liner between foam and cover

Beds without removable covers will smell within months regardless of spot-cleaning.

### Raised Bolsters vs. Flat Pads

**Bolster/nest style:** Dogs that rest their chin on the edge of the bed, anxious dogs, and breeds that curl up prefer bolstered edges. Provides psychological comfort for anxious or nervous dogs.

**Flat pad style:** Dogs that stretch out flat (labs, greyhounds), dogs recovering from surgery, and large dogs with limited mobility prefer flat beds. Easier to get on and off without stepping over a raised edge.

## FAQ

### How long should a dog bed last?

A quality orthopedic dog bed (Big Barker, PetFusion) should last 3–5 years before foam compression becomes noticeable. Budget polyester beds typically compress and need replacement within 12–18 months of daily use.

### Can I wash the foam?

No — foam should not be machine washed. It can be spot-cleaned with a damp cloth and mild detergent. Only the removable cover should go in the washing machine. This is why a waterproof liner (between cover and foam) is important for dogs with accidents.

### My dog won't use the bed. What do I do?

Try placing it where your dog already chooses to sleep, spraying it with a calming spray (Adaptil), or placing a worn t-shirt of yours on it. Dogs often refuse new beds because they lack familiar scent. Give it 1–2 weeks before concluding it won't work.

### Is an orthopedic bed worth it for a puppy?

For puppies, orthopedic foam isn't necessary — but chew resistance is. A K9 Ballistics bed is a better puppy investment than an expensive orthopedic bed that will be destroyed in days. Upgrade to orthopedic when they reach adult size and chewing behavior stabilizes.
