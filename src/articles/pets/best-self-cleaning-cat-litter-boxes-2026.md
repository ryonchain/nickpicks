## Buying Guide

### True Self-Cleaning vs. Rake Systems

**Globe rotation (Litter-Robot, Petree):** The entire chamber rotates, sifting all litter through a screen while waste falls into a sealed drawer. Most effective cleaning mechanism — misses almost no clumps. Higher price.

**Rake systems (PetSafe ScoopFree, cheaper alternatives):** A motorized rake sweeps horizontally across the litter surface after use. Can miss clumps that aren't directly in the rake path, and rakes can jam if litter sticks together before drying.

For thoroughness, globe rotation > rake systems. For simplicity and price, rake systems can work fine for single-cat households.

### Litter Compatibility

- **Litter-Robot 4**: Requires clumping clay litter, 3/8" or smaller particle size
- **PetSafe ScoopFree**: Requires proprietary crystal trays
- **Petree**: Standard clumping clay
- **OmegaPaw**: Any clumping litter

Crystal litter (silica gel) provides better odor control than clay. Clumping clay is cheaper and more widely available. Avoid non-clumping litter with any automatic box — it doesn't form separable clumps and defeats the automation entirely.

### Health Monitoring Value

The Litter-Robot 4's health monitoring feature tracks:
- Visit frequency (deviations can indicate UTI, constipation, or stress)
- Time spent per visit (extended visits may indicate straining)
- Weight trends over time (gradual weight loss is often the first detectable sign of illness in cats)

For multi-cat households where vet bills from undetected illness are a real concern, this feature alone can justify the premium over a rake-type system.

## FAQ

### Will my cat use a self-cleaning litter box?

Most cats adapt within 1–2 weeks. The largest barrier is the globe shape (Litter-Robot) — some cats prefer open litter boxes. To transition: start the LR4 in non-automatic mode (no rotation) so the cat gets used to it, then enable automation after a week. Placing their old litter box nearby during transition helps.

### How often do I still need to empty the waste drawer?

- Single cat: Every 7–14 days (Litter-Robot 4)
- Two cats: Every 5–7 days
- Three to four cats: Every 3–5 days
The app notifies you when the drawer is full.

### Are self-cleaning litter boxes worth it?

Break-even calculation: Litter-Robot 4 at $500 vs. manual scooping at 2 minutes/day = ~12 hours of scooping per year. If your time is worth $20/hour, payback is 2.5 years. The actual value is odor control and health monitoring — those benefits are harder to price but real.
