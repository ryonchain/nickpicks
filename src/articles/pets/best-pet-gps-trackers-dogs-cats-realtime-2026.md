## GPS vs. Bluetooth Trackers: Know the Difference

**GPS Trackers (Whistle, Fi, Tractive):** Use cellular networks to provide real-time location anywhere with cell coverage. Require monthly subscriptions. Track your pet anywhere in the country. This is what you need for escape-prone dogs and outdoor cats.

**Bluetooth Trackers (Apple AirTag, Tile):** Use crowdsourced Bluetooth detection. Free after hardware purchase. Only work if another Apple/Tile device user is within ~30 feet of your pet. Useful for lost collars and indoor cats but NOT reliable for real-time outdoor tracking.

For outdoor dogs and cats that go missing, GPS trackers with cellular subscriptions are the only option that actually works.

### Coverage: Does It Work in Your Area?

GPS trackers use cellular networks (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon depending on brand). Check coverage in your specific area — rural properties or areas between cell towers can have coverage gaps. Whistle's dual-carrier approach (AT&T + T-Mobile) provides the best US coverage. Tractive uses T-Mobile primarily in the US.

### Battery Life: What the Numbers Mean

GPS updates drain batteries quickly. A "2-week battery" assumes a mix of low-power GPS polling at home and periodic LTE cellular updates. If your dog is always away from home (using full LTE), battery life is 3–5 days. Plan your charging routine accordingly — the Fi tracker's base station charging model (charges while the dog sleeps at home) solves this most elegantly.

### Weight Limits for Cats

For cats under 8–10 lbs, be careful about tracker weight. A 35g tracker (Tractive Cat) is the practical minimum for daily cat wear without behavioral impact. Some cats tolerate 45–50g; most reject heavier trackers within days.

## FAQ

### Do GPS trackers work without cell service?

No. Real-time GPS trackers require cellular connection to transmit location data. Without cell coverage, they cannot update location. Some save location history and sync when back in range, but they won't alert you in real-time during a gap.

### Is a GPS tracker better than a microchip?

Both serve different purposes. A microchip is passive — it only works if someone finds your pet and scans it at a vet or shelter. A GPS tracker is active — it shows you where your pet is right now. Every pet should have both.

### Can cats use dog GPS trackers?

Most dog trackers are too heavy (60–100g) for cats. Use a cat-specific tracker (Tractive Cat LTE at 35g) or verify the tracker weight is under 40g before purchasing for a cat. The rule of thumb: a tracker should not exceed 5% of the animal's body weight.

### What's the accuracy of pet GPS trackers?

Modern LTE GPS trackers are accurate to 10–15 feet in open areas. In urban environments with building interference, accuracy can drop to 30–50 feet. GPS precision is sufficient to find a pet within a house, yard, or block — it's not precise enough to pinpoint a specific room in a building.
