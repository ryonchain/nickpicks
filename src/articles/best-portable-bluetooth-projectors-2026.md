## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Portable Projector

**Lumens matter more than spec sheets suggest:** Real-world brightness varies by room conditions. 300 ANSI lumens works well in a pitch-dark room but washes out in moderate light. 500+ ANSI lumens handles rooms with controlled ambient light. For outdoor use in darkness, 300+ is fine. For indoor use without blackout curtains, aim for 450+.

**Battery vs. AC power:** Most portable projectors run 2–4 hours on battery. If you're watching full-length films, 4+ hours is worth seeking out (Nebula Solar) or plan for a power bank with USB-C PD passthrough.

**Android TV vs. other platforms:** Android TV gives you the most app flexibility including the ability to sideload apps. Samsung Tizen and LG webOS are more polished but less flexible. For maximum compatibility, Android TV wins.

**Bluetooth audio:** All projectors on this list connect via Bluetooth to external speakers. If the built-in speaker is underpowered (LG PF510Q's 3W), budget for a Bluetooth speaker separately — the image quality improvement isn't wasted if the audio ruins the experience.

## FAQ

**Can you use a portable projector outdoors during the day?**
No practical portable projector performs well in direct sunlight. For outdoor daytime use, you'd need a fixed installation projector with 2,000+ ANSI lumens. Evening use after dusk is the target scenario for portable projectors.

**Do portable projectors need a screen?**
No — any smooth, flat, light-colored or white surface works. A blank wall is the most common setup. A proper projector screen improves image quality (especially contrast) but isn't required.

**How far do you need to be from a wall for a 100-inch image?**
Most portable projectors need 8–10 feet of throw distance for a 100-inch diagonal image. Short-throw and ultra-short-throw models can achieve large images from 1–3 feet, but those are typically not portable battery-powered models.

**Are portable projectors good for gaming?**
Portable projectors typically have input lag of 30–80ms — playable for casual games but not ideal for competitive gaming. If gaming is a priority, look for models with a dedicated "game mode" that reduces processing and lag.
