## Buyer's Guide

### Do I Need FAA Registration?
In the US, drones under 250g used recreationally don't require FAA registration (but rules vary). Drones over 250g or used commercially require FAA registration ($5, valid 3 years) and sometimes a Part 107 certificate.

**Under 249g drones:** DJI Mini 4 Pro, DJI Mini 2 SE, DJI Neo — all under 249g.

### Key Features to Look For
- **GPS**: Essential for beginners — enables auto-return-to-home
- **Obstacle avoidance**: Prevents crashes into trees/buildings
- **Camera quality**: 4K for photos worth printing; 2.7K for social media
- **Flight time**: 20+ minutes practical; under 15 min is frustrating
- **Stability**: 3-axis gimbal vs. EIS — gimbal wins for smooth footage

### Best Accessories
- Extra batteries (most important)
- ND filter kit (for cinematic video)
- Case/backpack
- Landing pad
- Propeller guards (for beginners)

### Where Can I Fly?
- **OK most places**: Over your own property, open fields, many parks (check B4UFLY app)
- **No-fly zones**: Within 5 miles of airports, national parks, stadiums, controlled airspace
- **Always**: Stay under 400ft AGL, keep drone in line of sight

## FAQ

**What's the best drone under $200?**
The DJI Neo ($199) or Holy Stone HS720E (~$159). For the extra $40, the Neo's DJI quality and safety features are worth it.

**Do I need a license to fly a drone?**
For recreational flying of drones under 249g, you don't need a license in most US jurisdictions (just register if over 249g and follow basic rules). Commercial flying requires a Part 107 certificate.

**How hard is it to learn to fly a drone?**
Modern GPS drones are very forgiving. Most beginners can fly safely within 30 minutes. Start in wide open spaces, use the return-to-home function frequently.

**What's the best drone for photography?**
DJI Mini 4 Pro. The 1/1.3" sensor, HDR video, and omnidirectional obstacle avoidance make it the best camera drone under $1,000.

**Can drones fly in rain?**
Most consumer drones are NOT weather-sealed. Light drizzle occasionally is OK, but avoid rain. The DJI Mavic 3 Enterprise has an IP43 rating for light rain.
