## Why Coffee Goes Cold (And How Smart Mugs Fix It)

Most coffee mugs are designed to hold liquid, not to maintain temperature. A ceramic mug absorbs heat from your coffee immediately after pouring, a standard room has a temperature differential of 70–80°F from your coffee target, and convection at the liquid surface continuously dissipates heat. A full mug of 150°F coffee in a 72°F room drops to 100°F in about 30 minutes without any intervention.

Smart mugs with heating elements maintain temperature by continuously measuring the internal liquid temperature and activating the heating element to compensate for heat loss. This is categorically different from an insulating mug (which just slows the loss) — a smart mug maintains your target temperature indefinitely, not just longer.

## Choosing the Right Temperature for Coffee and Tea

**Coffee:**
- Espresso drinks: 130–135°F
- Drip coffee: 135–140°F
- Americano/long black: 130°F

**Tea:**
- Green tea: 125–130°F (overheating extracts bitterness)
- White tea: 130–135°F
- Black tea: 135–140°F
- Herbal/tisane: 140–145°F
