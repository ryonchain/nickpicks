## How Body Composition Scales Work (And Their Limits)

All consumer smart scales use **BIA (Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis)**: a tiny, imperceptible electrical current passes through the body via the scale's electrode pads. Fat and muscle conduct electricity differently, allowing the scale to estimate body composition.

**Key limitations:**
- Hydration affects readings significantly. Weigh yourself at the same time daily (morning, before eating or drinking) for consistent comparisons
- Results are best viewed as trends over weeks, not single measurements
- Medical-grade accuracy requires DEXA scans — smart scales are directionally accurate, not clinically precise
- Foot placement matters — scales with position control (like Withings) deliver more consistent readings

**The right metric to track:** Body fat % and muscle mass trends over 4–8 weeks are more meaningful than daily weight fluctuations.
