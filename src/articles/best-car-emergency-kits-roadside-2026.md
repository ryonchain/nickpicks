## What to Look For

- **Jumper cable gauge:** 8-gauge or lower (thicker wire) is required for reliable cold-weather starts on larger engines. 10–12 gauge cables work in ideal conditions but struggle in the cold.
- **Jump starter vs. cables:** Portable lithium jump starters work without a second vehicle, which is more practical in isolated locations. Traditional cables are lighter and cheaper but require another car.
- **First-aid quality:** Check that the kit includes more than decorative bandages. Gauze pads, antiseptic wipes, a CPR mask, and an emergency blanket are the minimum for meaningful medical coverage.
- **Visibility gear:** Reflective triangles are the minimum; LED road flares or a reflective vest significantly improve safety during nighttime roadside stops.
- **Case quality:** Rigid cases protect contents from crushing; waterproof cases matter for kits stored in trunks exposed to temperature extremes. Avoid kits where contents rattle loose.
- **Expiration dates:** First-aid supplies expire. Check dates when you receive the kit and set a calendar reminder to inspect annually. Medications and bandage adhesives both degrade over time.
