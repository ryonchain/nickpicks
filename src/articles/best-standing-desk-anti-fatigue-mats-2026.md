## Flat vs. Contoured: Which Mat Type Is Right for You?

**Flat anti-fatigue mats** are the entry point: uniform foam or gel surface that distributes weight across a larger area than bare floor. They're unobtrusive, easy to clean, and work on any floor type. If you're new to standing work or share the mat with others, flat is the default choice.

**Contoured terrain mats** add raised ridges, heel cups, toe bars, and uneven surfaces that encourage your feet and legs to shift constantly while standing. The movement is subtle but continuous — your calf muscles contract and release, improving circulation and delaying fatigue onset. The tradeoff: contoured mats require a period of adaptation as your body learns the new surface.

**Thickness matters more than material.** A 3/4-inch mat provides meaningfully better cushioning than a 3/8-inch mat. Most commercial-grade anti-fatigue mats in factory and kitchen settings are 3/4 inch or thicker. If you're standing 4+ hours daily, don't go thinner than 1/2 inch.

## Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Anti-Fatigue Mat

### Thickness First, Features Second

If you must prioritize one factor, prioritize thickness: 3/4 inch is meaningfully better than 3/8 inch for sessions over 3 hours. Terrain features and material quality matter, but a thin mat limits how much any other feature can compensate.

### Match Mat Size to Your Movement Pattern

Measure how much your feet move while standing. A narrow 20-inch mat suits focused desk work; a 32-inch or longer mat accommodates pacing and wider stances. For active standing with balance boards or fidget bars, choose a mat at least 32 inches wide.

### Material Longevity

**Polyurethane** holds its cushioning the longest under continuous compression — preferred for 6+ hours of daily use and commercial environments. **PVC foam** is lighter and cheaper but compresses faster in high-use spots. **Memory foam** provides responsive initial cushioning but is more susceptible to heat softening and long-term compression.

### Don't Skip the Mat if You Use a Balance Board

Anti-fatigue mats and balance boards serve different purposes. Balance boards are for active exercise at the desk. Anti-fatigue mats are for passive fatigue reduction while working. Many heavy standing desk users use both: mat for default standing, balance board for focused breaks.
