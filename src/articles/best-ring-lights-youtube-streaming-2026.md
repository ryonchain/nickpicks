### Color Temperature: When to Use Warm vs. Cool

- **3000K–3500K (warm/tungsten):** Best for cozy, intimate aesthetics — matches candle or warm lamp light if you want a consistent interior look.
- **4000K–4500K (neutral):** Most flattering for skin tones — the sweet spot for most YouTube and streaming content.
- **5500K–6500K (daylight/cool):** Best for product photography and color-accurate work. Can look clinical on skin without additional warm fill.

### Positioning for Best Results

The standard streaming position puts the ring light directly behind the camera, centered at eye level, approximately 2–3 feet from your face. This produces the classic even lighting with the catchlight visible in the eye. Moving the light slightly to one side adds dimension. Never light from below — it creates an unflattering "horror movie" effect.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a ring light as my only light source?**
Yes, for most streaming and YouTube setups a single 18" ring light is sufficient as the sole key light. For a more professional look, add a secondary fill light or use a white backdrop to bounce light back from behind.

**Do ring lights work with glasses?**
Ring lights create a visible ring reflection in glasses that can be distracting. Angling the light slightly higher (above eye level) or tilting it backward reduces the reflection. Anti-reflective lenses also help significantly.

**How do I reduce ring light flicker on camera?**
Some ring lights flicker at certain shutter speeds. Set your camera shutter speed to a multiple of your local power frequency (1/50s in Europe, 1/60s in North America) to eliminate flicker. Most modern LED ring lights have low enough flicker to be imperceptible on camera.
