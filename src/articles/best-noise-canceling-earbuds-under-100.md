## Buying Guide: What to Look for in ANC Earbuds Under $100

**True ANC vs. passive isolation.** Passive isolation (good ear tip seal) blocks high-frequency noise like wind and typing. True ANC adds electronic cancellation of low-frequency noise like HVAC rumble, engine noise, and open-plan office ambient sound. For home office focus work, both matter — look for earbuds with both.

**Call quality matters more than music quality for remote workers.** Prioritize microphone quality over audio depth. An earbud with excellent music but mediocre microphones is a poor work tool. A good ergonomic [keyboard](/articles/best-ergonomic-keyboards-home-office-2026/) and earbuds together address two of the most common remote work comfort issues.

**Multipoint connectivity.** If you use earbuds with both a work laptop and personal phone, multipoint Bluetooth (connecting to two devices simultaneously) is a significant quality-of-life feature. Verify the earbuds you're considering support it.

**Fit and tip size.** ANC effectiveness depends heavily on ear tip seal. Most earbuds include small, medium, and large silicone tips. Spend two minutes trying each size — a proper seal is the foundation of good noise cancellation at any price point. If you work from a sofa or a non-desk setup, a [lap desk](/articles/best-lap-desks-sofa-working-2026/) and earbuds together make that an effective secondary workspace.
