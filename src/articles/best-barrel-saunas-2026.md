## Wood-Burning vs. Electric Heater: Which Is Right?

### Electric Heater
- **Pros:** Easy temperature control, digital timer, no ash or maintenance, works indoors or outdoors
- **Cons:** Requires 240V outlet installation ($200–500 electrician cost), ongoing electricity cost
- **Best for:** Most homeowners who want convenient, controllable sauna sessions

### Wood-Burning Heater
- **Pros:** Authentic Finnish sauna experience, no electricity required, faster heat with less energy cost
- **Cons:** Requires wood, ash cleanup, chimney installation, no thermostat control
- **Best for:** Off-grid locations, sauna enthusiasts who prefer traditional experience

## Site Preparation

### Foundation Options
1. **Concrete blocks:** Cheapest, adjust for leveling, no permanent attachment
2. **Gravel bed:** Good drainage, prevent moisture under the barrel
3. **Pressure-treated lumber frame:** Most stable, slight construction effort
4. **Concrete pad:** Most permanent, most professional appearance

### Electrical Requirement (for electric heaters)
Most barrel sauna heaters require 240V/30–60 amp service. Budget $200–500 for an electrician to run a dedicated circuit to your sauna location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does a barrel sauna take to heat up?**
A well-insulated barrel sauna reaches 170°F in 30–45 minutes with an electric heater. Wood-burning heaters often heat faster: 20–30 minutes.

**How long does a barrel sauna last?**
With proper annual sealing and maintenance, a quality cedar or Nordic spruce barrel sauna lasts 15–30 years. UV exposure is the primary enemy — shade and annual treatment dramatically extend lifespan.

**Do I need a permit to install a barrel sauna?**
Depends on your municipality. Some jurisdictions require permits for permanent outdoor structures and 240V electrical work. Always check local codes and HOA rules before purchasing.
