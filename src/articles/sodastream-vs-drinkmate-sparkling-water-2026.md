## The Core Difference: What Can It Carbonate?

**SodaStream** is designed exclusively for water. Attempting to carbonate juice or wine in a SodaStream will void the warranty and risks a mess — the overpressure from sugary liquids can spray when the nozzle is released. SodaStream's strength is the water-first approach with a massive syrup flavor library (Pepsi, 7UP, Dr Pepper, and 100+ proprietary flavors).

**Drinkmate OmniFizz** has a patented fizzing nozzle that controls carbonation release to handle any beverage safely. Flat wine becomes sparkling wine. Juice gets bubbles. You can re-carbonate flat sparkling water or flat soda. This is the differentiating feature — if you want to do anything beyond plain sparkling water, Drinkmate is the only choice.

**Winner:** Drinkmate for versatility; SodaStream for water-focused use with flavor ecosystem.

---

## CO2 Tank Compatibility

**SodaStream** uses proprietary CO2 cylinders. You exchange them at participating retailers (Target, Best Buy, Bed Bath & Beyond, online). Each cylinder makes approximately 60L of sparkling water. While widely available, you're locked into SodaStream's exchange ecosystem.

**Drinkmate** uses standard 60g CO2 cartridges and is also compatible with larger tanks via adapters. Standard CO2 tanks are widely available at homebrew shops, Walmart, and online at lower per-liter cost.

**Winner:** Drinkmate for CO2 flexibility and cost efficiency.

## Flavor Options

**SodaStream** has a massive proprietary syrup library — Classic Pepsi, 7UP, Dr Pepper, Mountain Dew, Sprite, plus 100+ signature flavors including energy drink, fruit, and sparkling tea syrups. The flavor ecosystem is SodaStream's biggest consumer advantage.

**Drinkmate** doesn't sell flavors — you add your own juice, syrup, or spirit directly to the carbonated liquid. More flexible creatively; requires sourcing your own mix-ins.
