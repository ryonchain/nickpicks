## How TENS Therapy Works

TENS units deliver low-voltage electrical pulses (1–150 Hz typically) through electrodes placed on skin near the pain source. Two competing theories explain the pain relief:

1. **Gate control theory:** Electrical signals "close the gate" on pain signals before they reach the brain
2. **Endorphin release:** Certain frequencies (4–8 Hz, low) trigger endorphin release for longer-lasting relief

**For acute pain relief:** Use higher frequency (80–150 Hz) for 20–30 minute sessions
**For chronic pain/endorphin effect:** Use lower frequency (2–10 Hz) for 45–60 minute sessions

Most home units run programs that alternate between these modes automatically.

## Electrode Pad Placement Guide

Correct placement dramatically affects effectiveness:

**Lower back pain:** Two pads flanking the spine at the pain level (L3–L5 for most); NOT directly on the spine
**Knee pain:** Pads on either side of the kneecap, not over it
**Shoulder:** One pad on the upper trapezius, one near the deltoid for referred pain
**Neck pain:** Pads on the paracervical muscles (sides of neck, not over spine or carotid)
**Carpal tunnel/wrist:** Pads on the forearm muscles proximal to the pain

Always read the included placement guide — incorrect placement reduces effectiveness and can cause skin irritation.

## FAQ

**Q: Is TENS therapy safe?**
A: FDA-cleared TENS units are safe for most adults. Do NOT use TENS: on or near the heart, on the head or neck above the shoulder, on broken or irritated skin, during pregnancy (without OB clearance), with a pacemaker, after recent surgery without clearance.

**Q: How long does a TENS session last?**
A: Most sessions run 20–60 minutes. Skin irritation is the primary risk of overuse — more isn't always better. Take 20-minute breaks between sessions if needed.

**Q: Will TENS electrodes work with any TENS unit?**
A: Yes, with the same connector type. Most consumer units use a 2mm snap connector. Replacement pads are universal and sold in multi-packs for $10–20.

**Q: How long do electrode pads last?**
A: Most manufacturers rate pads at 20–30 uses. Longevity extends with proper care: clean skin before placement, store pads in the plastic sheet, and keep out of heat and sunlight.
