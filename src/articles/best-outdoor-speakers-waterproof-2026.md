## What to Look For

- **IP waterproof rating:** IP67 means protected against dust and submersion up to 1m for 30 min. IP65–66 means water-jet resistant. IPX4 means splash-resistant only. For pool and beach use, aim for IP67 or IPX7 minimum.
- **Output wattage and volume:** Watts aren't a perfect indicator of loudness, but they correlate. 10–20W is fine for personal use; 30–50W works for small groups; 100W+ for large backyard parties.
- **Battery life:** 12 hours handles a typical beach or pool day. If you entertain late into the evening or rarely have charging access, 20+ hours is worth the extra spend.
- **Bluetooth range:** Most Bluetooth speakers advertise 30–100 ft range. Real-world range (with walls, bodies, and interference) is typically 50–70% of the advertised number.
- **Size and portability:** Compact speakers (JBL Flip, Soundcore Motion) fit in bags and pockets. Mid-size (JBL Charge) sit stable on any surface. Large party speakers (UE HYPERBOOM) are designed to stay put rather than travel light.
- **Multi-speaker pairing:** If you might want to expand your setup later, check whether the speaker supports multi-speaker pairing (JBL's PartyBoost, UE's PartyUp) for synchronized or stereo playback.
