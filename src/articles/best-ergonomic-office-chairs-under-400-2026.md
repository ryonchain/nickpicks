## Why Your Chair Matters More Than Your Desk

Sitting 8 hours in a non-ergonomic chair compresses lumbar discs, tightens hip flexors, and causes the forward head posture ("tech neck") seen in 70% of office workers. A proper chair:
- Supports your lumbar curve (natural inward curve of lower back)
- Positions your hips at 90–100 degrees
- Keeps your feet flat on the floor
- Allows armrests to support arms without raising shoulders

## What to Look For

**Lumbar adjustment:** Height AND depth adjustability is key. A fixed lumbar pad rarely hits the right spot.

**Seat depth:** Your back should touch the lumbar support while leaving 2–3 fingers of space between the seat edge and your knees.

**Armrest type:** 4D (height, width, depth, pivot) armrests prevent shoulder elevation that causes neck strain.

**Weight capacity:** Check manufacturer ratings. Most chairs under $300 are rated for 250–275 lbs; above that, look for commercial models.
