## What Actually Works for Hair Growth in 2026

Hair growth products have come a long way from biotin gummies and snake-oil claims. The serums that have earned real credibility fall into a few evidence-supported categories.

**Minoxidil** remains the FDA-approved gold standard for hair loss — available in 2% and 5% concentrations over the counter. It's not in any of the products on this list (those are sold separately and differently regulated), but it's worth knowing: if you have significant androgenetic alopecia, consult a dermatologist about minoxidil before spending money on serums.

**Peptides** are chains of amino acids that signal follicles to enter or extend their growth phase. Ingredients like Biotinoyl Tripeptide-1 and Acetyl Tetrapeptide-3 have meaningful research behind them. The Ordinary's formula is built around these.

**DHT blockers** — pumpkin seed oil, saw palmetto, caffeine — work by reducing the hormone responsible for follicle miniaturization. Pura D'or's approach leans heavily here.

**Scalp circulation stimulants** — caffeine, niacinamide, and physical exfoliants — improve blood flow to follicles and remove buildup that can impede growth. Nioxin's system is built on this principle.

**What to look for:**
- Clinically studied active ingredients at meaningful concentrations
- Regular application — consistency matters more than product choice
- Scalp compatibility (avoid heavy silicones near follicles)
- A realistic expectation: most users see results after 3–6 months of daily use

## FAQ

**How long do hair growth serums take to work?**
Realistic expectations are 90–120 days of consistent use for measurable density improvements. Shedding reduction typically shows earlier — around 4–8 weeks. Hair growth cycles are slow; there are no shortcuts.

**Can I use multiple products at once?**
Yes — a scalp serum like The Ordinary and a specialized shampoo like Nioxin or Pura D'or work well together. Avoid applying multiple serums to the same area simultaneously, as this can cause buildup.

**Are these products suitable for color-treated hair?**
All four products are safe for color-treated hair. The Ordinary Multi-Peptide and Kérastase Initialiste are specifically designed for compatibility. Nioxin systems 5 and 6 are formulated specifically for chemically treated hair.

**Do I need to see a doctor before using these?**
If you're experiencing sudden or significant hair loss, see a dermatologist first. Sudden shedding can indicate nutritional deficiencies, thyroid issues, or other medical causes that serums won't address.

**Is biotin worth taking alongside these products?**
Biotin supplements help only if you have a documented biotin deficiency — relatively rare. Most people in developed countries get sufficient biotin from diet. The hair-growth benefit of supplementation in non-deficient people is not well-supported by evidence.
