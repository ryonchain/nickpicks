# Best Grab Bars for Shower & Bathroom Safety in 2026

Falls in the bathroom are one of the leading causes of injury for adults over 65 — and for people of any age recovering from surgery or managing mobility challenges. A properly installed grab bar can prevent a serious fall. The key variables: mounting method (suction cup vs wall-mount), weight rating, bar diameter, and finish.

**Critical rule:** Wall-mounted bars anchored into studs or toggle bolts rated for the load are far more reliable than suction cup bars for full weight-bearing. Use suction cups only as supplemental support.

## Installation Notes

**Into studs:** Use 3-inch wood screws through the mounting flanges into studs. Most reliable.

**No stud access:** Use toggle bolts or Snap Toggle anchors rated for at least 250 lbs each. Never use standard drywall anchors alone.

**ADA height:** ADA recommends 33–36 inches from floor for horizontal bars in showers; 33–36 inches for toilet grab bars. Angle the bar 42–45 degrees if mounting in a corner.
