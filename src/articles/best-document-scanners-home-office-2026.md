## Do You Need a Flatbed Scanner?

Sheet-fed document scanners are ideal for loose paper. Flatbed scanners are better for:
- **Books and bound documents** (can't be fed through a feeder)
- **Photos** (flatbed preserves image quality better)
- **Fragile documents** (receipts, old papers)
- **Non-standard sizes** (large blueprints, odd-shaped items)

For a home office focused on document paperwork, a sheet-fed scanner is almost always the right choice. If you regularly scan books or photos, consider an Epson Perfection flatbed scanner alongside a sheet-fed model.

## Key Features to Evaluate

**ADF size**: A 50-page ADF handles most batch scanning tasks. A 20-page ADF means frequent reloading for larger jobs.

**Scan speed (ppm)**: Pages per minute. For light home office use (under 50 pages/day), 25+ ppm is plenty. For moderate workloads, aim for 35+ ppm.

**Duplex scanning**: Scans both sides of a page in one pass. Essential for double-sided documents and saves significant time.

**OCR software**: Optical Character Recognition converts scanned images to searchable, selectable text. Most bundled software includes basic OCR; advanced OCR may require third-party software like ABBYY FineReader.

**Connectivity**: Wi-Fi allows scanning directly to mobile devices or cloud storage without a PC. USB-only scanners must stay tethered to a computer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the best document scanner for a home office?**
For most home offices with moderate document volume, the **Canon imageFORMULA R40** offers excellent speed (40 ppm) at the best price. Heavier users should invest in the **Fujitsu ScanSnap iX1600** for its superior software and Wi-Fi flexibility.

**Can I scan receipts with a document scanner?**
Yes — sheet-fed scanners handle standard receipt sizes well. Use the straight-path or gentle scan setting for thin thermal paper receipts to avoid jamming. The Doxie Go SE is particularly good for receipts.

**Is a document scanner worth it for a home office?**
If you deal with regular paperwork — tax documents, contracts, medical records, bills — a scanner pays for itself quickly in reduced storage space, faster retrieval, and peace of mind from digital backup. Most scanners integrate with Google Drive or Dropbox so files are immediately backed up.

**What resolution do I need for document scanning?**
300 DPI is standard for readable text documents. 600 DPI is useful for detailed diagrams or fine-print documents. Higher resolutions (1200+ DPI) are for photo scanning, not documents.
