## Buying Guide: Getting the Most from Under-Desk Space

**Start with cables.** The biggest under-desk mess is almost always cables and power strips. Before adding drawers or shelves, mount a [cable management](/articles/best-cable-management-desk-2026/) tray to get the power strip off the floor. This single step visually transforms the space.

**Measure your desk underside depth.** Most under-desk drawers and keyboard trays mount 1–2 inches down from the desk surface. If your desk has a frame rail or cross-member, you may not have the clearance. Measure from the bottom of the desk surface to the floor and from the desk edge to any frame member.

**Adhesive vs. screw mounting.** Adhesive is fast and reversible but limited in weight capacity (typically 5–10 lbs). Screw mounting is permanent but handles 25+ lbs. For heavy items (power strip with many cables, full keyboard tray), always use screws.

**Leg room.** Under-desk storage reduces the space available for your legs. Leave at least 24 inches of knee clearance. Position any under-desk equipment toward the back of the desk, not between your legs.
