## Space Heater Types Explained

### Ceramic Heaters
The most common type. A ceramic element heats up quickly and a fan distributes the warm air. Best for rapid heat in medium rooms. Budget-friendly. Slight fan noise.

### Infrared Heaters
Infrared heats objects and people directly (like sunlight) rather than the air. More efficient for spot warming. Often quieter. Heat dissipates faster when unit is off.

### Oil-Filled Radiators
Silent operation. Retains heat after shutoff. Slower to warm up but distributes heat evenly. Best for continuous background warmth.

### Panel Heaters
Thin, wall-mountable units. Efficient for zone heating. Good for garages, workshops, or supplemental heat in specific spots.

## How to Choose the Right Space Heater

### Room Size
- Under 200 sq ft: Any 750W–1,000W heater works
- 200–400 sq ft: 1,500W ceramic or infrared
- 400–1,000 sq ft: Infrared dual-system (like the DR968) or oil-filled with fan

### Noise Sensitivity
- Bedroom/nursery: Oil-filled radiator (De'Longhi) or low-fan ceramic (Vornado VH200)
- Office/living room: Any ceramic heater is fine

### Safety Priorities
All heaters on this list include tip-over shutoff and overheat protection — non-negotiables. For bathrooms, only use a rated bathroom unit (Broan) or a hardwired wall unit.

### Energy Efficiency
Space heaters use 1,500W on high — the same as a hair dryer running continuously. Run only the room you're in. Use the built-in thermostat instead of running full blast. A programmable timer (most units have this) saves significant energy by shutting off when you leave.

## Space Heater Safety Rules

1. **Keep 3 feet clear** around the unit at all times — no furniture, curtains, or rugs nearby
2. **Never leave it on unattended** unless it has an auto-off thermostat
3. **Plug directly into a wall outlet** — never use an extension cord or power strip (fire risk)
4. **Never use in a bathroom** unless the unit is specifically bathroom-rated
5. **Check the cord** each season — discard if the cord is frayed, kinked, or warm to the touch

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to run a 1,500W space heater?**
At the U.S. average electricity rate (~$0.17/kWh), a 1,500W heater costs about $0.26/hour. Running 8 hours daily for a month = ~$62. Zone heating (just your room instead of the whole house) can offset this significantly.

**Can a space heater heat an entire house?**
No. Space heaters are designed for zone heating — one room at a time. For whole-house heating, you'd need multiple large units, which gets expensive quickly.

**What's the difference between 750W and 1,500W?**
750W is roughly half the heat output. Use 750W for small rooms or as a boost, 1,500W for primary heating in medium-to-large rooms.

**Are space heaters safe to leave on overnight?**
Only with a thermostat that cycles off when the target temperature is reached, tip-over protection, and overheat protection. The De'Longhi oil-filled and Vornado VH200 are the safest overnight options on this list. Never leave a space heater on near flammable materials.
