## Buyer's Guide: Laser vs. Inkjet vs. EcoTank

### Quick Decision Tree

**You mostly print documents (contracts, homework, recipes):** → Brother laser printer. Cheapest to run, fastest, toner never dries out.

**You print color and need decent quality (presentations, photos):** → HP OfficeJet Pro with Instant Ink or Epson EcoTank.

**You print infrequently but need color when you do:** → EcoTank. Inkjet cartridges dry out when unused; EcoTank bottles don't.

**You need to scan frequently:** → Any all-in-one with an ADF (auto document feeder). Flatbed-only models require manually placing each page.

### Ink Subscription Warning

HP and Canon both offer "ink as a service" subscriptions that are cost-effective for moderate users but become restrictive:
- HP+: Printer partially locked to HP Instant Ink ecosystem
- Canon SELPHY/PIXMA subscriptions: Similar model

Read terms before subscribing — some plans limit functionality if you cancel.

## FAQ

**What's the cheapest printer to run long-term?**
A monochrome laser printer like the Brother HL-L2370DW — toner at 0.3¢ per page with no ink drying out between uses.

**Are wireless printers reliable?**
Modern Wi-Fi printers are generally reliable on 2.4 GHz networks. Avoid placing the printer far from the router or behind thick walls — most drop-connection complaints trace to signal strength, not the printer.

**Should I avoid HP printers?**
HP's ink subscription model is aggressive, but HP printers are reliable hardware. If you're aware of the HP+ restrictions and either subscribe to Instant Ink or specifically buy a non-HP+ model, HP makes excellent hardware.

**Can I refill EcoTank ink with third-party bottles?**
Yes — compatible bottles are widely available at 30–50% lower cost than Epson brand. Quality varies by brand; Ecotankink and MIS are reliable third-party options.
