## FAQ

**Do laptop cooling pads actually work?**

Yes, but results depend on your laptop's thermal design. A quality cooling pad can reduce CPU and GPU surface temperatures by 5–15°C during sustained workloads, which translates to less thermal throttling and more consistent performance over long sessions. Thin and light laptops with bottom-facing intake vents benefit most from a pad's forced airflow. Laptops that exhaust heat primarily from the sides or rear see smaller gains, since the pad's fans aren't directing air toward the active intake zones.

**Should I get a USB-powered cooling pad or one with a separate power supply?**

USB-powered pads are the practical choice for most people — they draw power directly from one of your laptop's USB-A ports and require no extra cables or wall outlets. The tradeoff is that high-speed fans pull from your laptop's own power budget, which can modestly reduce battery life during unplugged use. Pads with a dedicated power supply avoid this drain entirely but add an extra cord to manage. Unless you're running a five-fan pad at maximum speed on battery for hours at a time, the difference is small enough that USB-powered pads are the more convenient option for the vast majority of users.

**How should I position a cooling pad for the best results?**

Position the pad so its fans align with your laptop's bottom intake vents — flip your laptop over to locate them before you set up. Most pads perform best in a flat or shallow-tilt position (15–20 degrees). Steeper angles can create a gap between the pad surface and the laptop's underside, letting air escape to the sides rather than being directed into the vents. Avoid placing the pad on soft surfaces like a bed, couch, or blanket; those surfaces block the underside of the pad's own fans and negate most of the benefit regardless of which pad you're using.
