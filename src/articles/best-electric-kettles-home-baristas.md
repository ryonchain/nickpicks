## Why Temperature Control Matters for Coffee

Water temperature is one of the most overlooked variables in home brewing:

- **Pour-over / drip coffee:** 195°F–205°F
- **Green tea:** 160°F–175°F
- **Black tea:** 205°F–212°F
- **French press:** 195°F–200°F
- **Cold brew:** Room temp or cold water

Boiling water (212°F) scalds delicate coffee grounds and green tea. A variable-temperature kettle lets you dial in the exact temp for your brew method.

---

## What to Look for in a Barista Kettle

- **Variable temperature control** — Look for 1-degree precision or at least multiple presets.
- **Hold temperature function** — Keeps water at target temp for 30–60 minutes while you prep.
- **Gooseneck spout** — Essential for controlled, even pours. Non-gooseneck designs dump water too fast.
- **Capacity** — 0.8–1.0L handles most single-serve and two-cup brews comfortably.
- **Boil speed** — 1200W+ heats water noticeably faster.
