## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Robot Vacuum Under $200

- **Suction power (Pa):** 1000+ Pa handles most floors; 2000+ Pa is needed for thick carpet. Random-navigation models often use less suction than they advertise.
- **Navigation type:** Random navigation covers rooms adequately with enough time; grid/LiDAR navigation is faster and more thorough. Grid nav is worth paying more for.
- **Battery life:** 60 min covers ~800 sq ft; 100+ min handles larger homes. Auto-recharge-and-resume eliminates the limitation for most users.
- **Pet hair:** Brush roll robots pick up pet hair well but tangle; tangle-free suction ports (ILIFE) or rubber brush rolls (Roomba) handle pet hair better.

## FAQ

**Q: Do robot vacuums replace regular vacuums?**
A: For daily maintenance, yes. For deep carpet cleaning or stairs, no. Robot vacuums are best used as a complement to a traditional upright vacuum for weekly deep cleans.

**Q: Can robot vacuums handle dog/cat hair?**
A: Yes, but look for models with rubber brush rolls or tangle-free ports. Traditional bristle brushes get clogged with pet hair and require frequent cleaning.

**Q: Is LiDAR navigation worth the extra cost?**
A: If your home is 1,000+ sq ft or has multiple rooms, LiDAR-mapped systematic navigation pays for itself in battery savings and more complete coverage.
