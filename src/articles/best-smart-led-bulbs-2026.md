## What to Look For in a Smart Bulb

Smart bulbs vary enormously in brightness output, color temperature range, app stability, and platform support. Price differences are real and tied to engineering quality, not just branding.

Key factors:

- **Lumens:** A standard 60W-equivalent bulb should output 800 lumens. Look for 800+ for general room lighting; 1100+ if you're replacing a 75W equivalent.
- **Color Temperature (CCT):** Measured in Kelvin. 2700K is warm white (cozy, evening). 4000K is neutral white (kitchen, office). 6500K is daylight (maximum alertness). Tunable white bulbs let you shift across this range.
- **Color Rendering Index (CRI):** Higher CRI (90+) means colors look more accurate under the light. Important for kitchens, art, and workspaces. Most budget smart bulbs are 80 CRI.
- **Protocol:** Wi-Fi bulbs connect directly to your router but can crowd your 2.4 GHz band. Zigbee/Thread bulbs use a hub and mesh networking — more reliable in large homes but require a bridge.
- **Platform compatibility:** Verify support for your ecosystem: Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit, Matter. HomeKit is the most restrictive; Matter-native bulbs (Thread-based) offer broadest future compatibility.

Philips Hue remains the benchmark smart bulb for good reason: rock-solid reliability, the best color accuracy in the category, and the deepest ecosystem of compatible accessories (light strips, gradient bulbs, motion sensors, switches). The Hue Bridge creates a local Zigbee mesh that doesn't depend on cloud connectivity for basic automation.

- Works with Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit, Siri

### 2. LIFX A19 Color — Best No-Hub Option

**Price:** ~$35 | [Check on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=LIFX+A19+Color+smart+bulb&tag=nickpicks-20)

LIFX connects directly to 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi with no hub required. The A19 Color puts out 1100 lumens — 37% brighter than Hue's A19 — and covers a wider color temperature range (2500–9000K). Color accuracy is genuinely competitive with Hue. The app is well-designed and automations run reliably.
