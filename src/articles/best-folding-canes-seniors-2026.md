# Best Canes for Seniors and Adults in 2026

A cane provides a third point of contact with the ground, extending your balance base and reducing the load on a weakened leg. The right cane depends on how much support is needed (standard tip vs. quad cane), where it will be used (folding for travel), and handle type (derby vs. offset vs. Fritz). Using the wrong height increases fall risk rather than reducing it.

## Cane vs. Walker: When to Upgrade

Consider a walker when:
- You frequently need both hands free for stability
- The cane doesn't feel like enough support
- You need to sit and rest during walks (rollator has a seat)
- Your doctor or physical therapist recommends it

A cane is not safer than a walker if the user really needs a walker — the fall risk from an inadequate assistive device exceeds any convenience benefit.

---

## Proper Cane Technique

**Which hand?** Hold the cane in the hand opposite the weaker or painful leg. When you step with the weak leg, the cane bears weight simultaneously — maintaining balance and reducing load on the weak side.

**Gait pattern:** Step with the weaker leg and the cane simultaneously, then step with the stronger leg.
