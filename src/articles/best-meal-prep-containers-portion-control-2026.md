## Buying Guide: How to Choose Meal Prep Containers for Portion Control

**Glass vs. plastic.** Glass doesn't absorb odors or stains, is safe for microwave and oven heating, and lasts indefinitely. Plastic is lighter, cheaper, and safer to drop. For daily commuters, lightweight plastic makes sense. For home reheating, glass is cleaner and more durable long-term.

**Compartments.** Single-compartment containers are versatile but mix foods during transport. Divided containers keep proteins, carbs, and vegetables separate — important if you're trying to maintain textures (crispy vs. saucy). For strict macro diets, divided containers are worth the trade-off in versatility.

**Leakproof seals.** Not all containers marketed as "leakproof" actually are. The Rubbermaid Brilliance 4-latch system genuinely prevents leaks. Others with single-latch or press-seal lids may leak under bag pressure. If you carry dressings or sauces, test the seal before trusting it in a work bag.

**Microwave and oven safety.** Remove lids before microwaving plastic containers — most lids are not microwave safe even when containers are. Oven safety requires borosilicate glass (Prep Naturals, Pyrex Simply Store) — standard glass cracks under oven heat. Always check manufacturer ratings.

**Set size.** A 5-day meal prep typically requires 5–10 containers (one per meal, two per day if prepping lunch and dinner). A 10-piece set handles full-week meal prep. Start with a 10-piece and expand if needed.

## FAQ

**Can I meal prep 5 days ahead?**
Most cooked proteins and grains stay fresh in the refrigerator 4–5 days. Vegetables (especially cooked) begin degrading after day 3. Salads with dressing should be stored separately until serving. For 5-day prep, freeze days 4–5 and refrigerate days 1–3.

**Are plastic containers safe for microwave use?**
BPA-free plastic is the standard for food-safe containers. Avoid microwaving plastic with cracks, cloudiness, or visible wear — degraded plastic is more likely to leach. When in doubt, transfer to a glass or ceramic container for microwaving.

**How do I prevent odors in plastic containers?**
Wash immediately after use — don't let tomato sauce or curry sit overnight. For existing odors, soak in a baking soda and warm water solution for 30 minutes. Sunning plastic containers (UV light) also helps remove odors. Glass is inherently odor-resistant.

**What size containers do I need for meal prep?**
For a standard lunch portion: 30–35 oz (approximately 850–1,000 mL) for a full meal. For snacks: 8–16 oz. The Bentgo Prep 2-compartment ~50oz total divided container is ideal for a protein-and-veg lunch. Start with a 30oz single compartment and adjust based on your portion needs.

**Can I put glass containers directly in the freezer?**
Yes — but let hot food cool to room temperature first. Placing a hot glass container directly into a cold freezer can cause thermal shock. Leave a small expansion gap at the top of liquid-heavy meals to prevent lid distortion as liquids expand when freezing.
