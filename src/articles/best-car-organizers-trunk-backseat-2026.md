## What to Look For

- **Structure:** Organizers with internal frames or reinforced panels hold their shape when loaded. Soft-sided bags collapse and become frustrating to use within days.
- **Anchoring system:** Trunk organizers must attach to cargo anchor points or have non-slip bases — unanchored organizers slide on every stop and corner.
- **Compartment design:** Match compartment sizes to what you actually carry. Deep single pockets fit fewer items than segmented layouts with a mix of small and large spaces.
- **Seat-back fit:** Backseat organizers need both a headrest top strap and a lower seat-back strap; one strap alone allows the unit to swing away from the seat.
- **Material:** Oxford cloth and heavy polyester outlast thin nylon significantly. For trunk use, water-resistant or waterproof materials prevent damage from wet items.
- **Collapsibility:** If the organizer will be removed and stored periodically, collapsible designs save meaningful space in a garage or closet.
