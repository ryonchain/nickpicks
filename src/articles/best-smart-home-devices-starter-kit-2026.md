## Buyer's Guide: Choosing Your Ecosystem

The most important smart home decision is your ecosystem — Alexa, Google, or Apple. All devices in your home should speak the same language.

| Ecosystem | Best If You Have | Key Hub | Strength |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|----------|
| Amazon Alexa | Prime, Fire TV, Kindle | Echo speaker | Widest device support |
| Google Home | Android, Google Workspace | Nest Hub | Best voice AI, display hubs |
| Apple HomeKit | iPhone, Mac, iCloud | HomePod mini | Most secure/private |

**Can I mix ecosystems?**
Yes — most devices (Philips Hue, Wyze, smart plugs) work across all three. But automations and routines work best when everything is in one ecosystem.

## FAQ

**What's the first smart home device I should buy?**
A smart plug ($6–8 each). It works with anything, requires no installation, and gives you immediate voice control over lamps and appliances.

**Do smart home devices work without the internet?**
Most require internet for initial setup. Some (Philips Hue on local network, Lutron Caseta) work locally without internet after setup. Cloud-dependent devices (Wyze, Ring) lose remote access if internet is down.

**Is a smart home worth it for renters?**
Yes — smart plugs, smart bulbs, and portable devices work without permanent installation. A smart thermostat may require landlord permission for installation.
