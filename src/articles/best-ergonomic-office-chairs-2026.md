## Ergonomic Chair Buying Guide

### Lumbar Support: What Actually Matters

Not all lumbar support is equal. There are three types:

- **Fixed lumbar pad:** A foam bump at a fixed height. Only works if the bump aligns with your lumbar curve — which it often doesn't.
- **Height-adjustable lumbar:** Better — you can move it up or down to find your curve. Still limited in depth.
- **Height + depth adjustable (or dynamic):** Best — you adjust both the position and how far the support pushes into your back. The Leap V2's LiveBack and Aeron's PostureFit SL are the best examples.

### Armrests: Why 4D Matters

- **1D:** Height only. Nearly useless for typing.
- **2D:** Height + width. Functional minimum.
- **3D:** Height + width + depth (forward/back). Good for most setups.
- **4D:** Height + width + depth + pivot (angle). The right answer for anyone who uses a keyboard for hours.

Pivoting armrests let your forearms angle inward to match shoulder width, which reduces shoulder strain significantly.

### Seat Depth

An often-overlooked adjustment: seat depth determines how much of your thigh is supported. Too deep and the edge cuts into the back of your knees. Too shallow and you lose leg support. Look for chairs with at least 2 inches of adjustment range.

## FAQ

**Is an expensive ergonomic chair worth it?**
For full-time home office workers (5+ hours/day), yes. Back pain treatment, chiropractor visits, and lost productivity cost far more over time than the difference between a $200 chair and a $1,400 Aeron. Buy the best you can afford.

**Herman Miller vs Steelcase — which is better?**
Different strengths. Herman Miller Aeron is better for people who stay relatively still and want the best mesh back. Steelcase Leap V2 is better for people who move constantly and prefer upholstered feel. Both carry 12-year warranties and roughly equivalent ergonomic credentials.

**Can I buy a used Herman Miller Aeron?**
Yes, and it's one of the best-value moves in office furniture. A used Aeron in good condition ($400–$700) delivers the same 12-year warranty on remaining years and the same ergonomic performance as a new one. Check eBay, Craigslist, and office liquidation sales.

**What chair is best for lower back pain specifically?**
The Herman Miller Aeron with PostureFit SL is the most frequently recommended chair for lumbar-specific issues. The PostureFit SL's sacral support is the differentiator — it supports the base of the spine where most lower back pain originates.
