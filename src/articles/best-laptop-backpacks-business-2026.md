## What to Look For in a Business Laptop Backpack

### Must-Have Features
- **Dedicated laptop sleeve:** Laptop should be suspended (not resting on the bottom panel). Look for foam padding and a sleeve that keeps the laptop vertical.
- **Comfortable straps:** Padded, wide, with chest strap for heavier loads.
- **Water-resistant material:** Not waterproof, but resistant enough that a caught shower doesn't destroy your laptop.
- **Professional appearance:** If you meet clients, it should look like you bought it intentionally, not grabbed whatever was at REI.

### Nice-to-Have Features
- TSA lay-flat design
- USB charging port
- Add-a-bag sleeve for rolling luggage
- RFID-blocking pocket
- Dedicated tablet compartment

### Common Mistakes
- **Buying on volume alone:** A 40L pack is not better than a 25L pack for commuting. You'll overfill it and strain your back.
- **Ignoring strap quality:** $40 bags often have narrow, poorly padded straps. After 2 miles of city walking with a 20lb bag, you'll notice.
- **No laptop suspension:** Laptops dropped on the bottom corner of a bag damage their SSD. Suspended compartments prevent this.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How big should my laptop backpack be?**
20–25L for daily commuting. 30–40L for business travel that includes a change of clothes. Bigger is not better — it invites over-packing and back strain.

**Is it worth spending $300+ on a laptop backpack?**
If you travel for work frequently, yes. Premium bags use better materials that last 5–10 years vs 2–3 years for budget options. Over 10 years, the Tumi at $400 is cheaper than replacing a $90 bag 4 times.

**What's the most important feature?**
Laptop compartment quality and strap comfort. Everything else is secondary.
