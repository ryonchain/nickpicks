## Sustainable Activewear Buying Guide

### The Problem with Conventional Athletic Wear

Standard athletic wear is made almost entirely from virgin nylon and polyester — petroleum-derived synthetics. The issues:
- **Microplastic shedding**: synthetic fabrics shed thousands of microplastic fibers per wash, which pass through water treatment and enter waterways
- **PFAS coatings**: many moisture-resistant athletic fabrics use per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (forever chemicals) that accumulate in the environment
- **Fast fashion cycle**: cheap athletic wear degrades quickly and encourages frequent replacement

Sustainable activewear addresses these issues through recycled materials, PFAS-free coatings, and designs built for longevity.

### Recycled vs. Organic: Which Is More Sustainable?

**Recycled polyester/nylon:**
- Diverts plastic waste from landfills and oceans
- Uses 30–50% less energy than virgin synthetic production
- Still sheds microplastics in washing (use a Guppyfriend bag to catch fibers)

**Organic cotton:**
- No synthetic pesticides or fertilizers
- Biodegradable at end of life
- Still requires significant water to grow (though some brands use rain-fed cotton)
- Less effective for high-intensity sweating (holds moisture vs. wicking)

For high-performance, sweat-heavy workouts: recycled synthetic fabrics. For yoga, casual training, and athleisure: organic cotton is an excellent option.

### The Microplastic Problem and What to Do About It

Even recycled synthetic activewear sheds microplastic fibers when washed. Mitigation strategies:
1. **Guppyfriend Washing Bag**: a specialized laundry bag that catches microplastic fibers shed during washing
2. **Wash less frequently**: air out athletic wear between washes when possible
3. **Cold water and gentle cycle**: higher temps and agitation increase fiber shedding
4. **Cora Ball**: a laundry ball that catches some microfibers — less effective than Guppyfriend but convenient

### Building a Sustainable Athletic Wardrobe

Rather than replacing your entire wardrobe at once (which defeats the sustainability purpose), build gradually:
1. Wear current athletic wear until it wears out
2. When replacing, upgrade to sustainable versions
3. Prioritize high-wear items: leggings, tees, shorts
4. Quality over quantity — two excellent pairs of leggings beat six cheap pairs

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does sustainable activewear perform as well as Lululemon or Nike?

At premium price points ($60–$100+), yes — Girlfriend Collective leggings and Patagonia technical pieces genuinely match or exceed conventional premium athletic wear on performance. At budget price points, sustainable options like tentree are competitive with mid-range conventional brands.

### How do I know if "recycled" claims are genuine?

Look for third-party certifications: REPREVE® (for recycled polyester from plastic bottles), bluesign® (responsible manufacturing), Global Recycled Standard (GRS), and OEKO-TEX certifications. Vague "made with recycled materials" without certification detail warrants skepticism.

### Is sustainable activewear worth the higher price?

The price premium is real — expect to pay 20–50% more than fast-fashion athletic brands. The trade-off: sustainable activewear is typically built to last 3–5x longer than fast-fashion alternatives. Amortized over the garment's lifetime, the cost per wear is often lower.

### Can I return sustainable activewear if it doesn't fit?

All brands listed above have standard return policies. Patagonia additionally offers repairs for damaged items and a worn-wear program for used gear. Girlfriend Collective's take-back program accepts worn-out items.

### What's the single most impactful switch in athletic wear?

If you do one thing: switch your leggings (or training pants) to recycled material. Leggings are the highest-volume synthetic athletic item most people own and wear repeatedly. Replacing them with Girlfriend Collective or similar is the largest single reduction in virgin plastic consumption for most athletic wardrobes.
