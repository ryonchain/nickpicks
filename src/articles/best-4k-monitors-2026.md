## Size Guide: 27" vs 32" at 4K

**27" at 4K (163 PPI):** Pixel density is high enough that everything appears sharp without scaling. Fits most desk depths comfortably. Default recommendation for most buyers.

**32" at 4K (138 PPI):** More screen area for multi-window workflows and larger creative canvases. Requires a deeper desk (60cm minimum) and a monitor arm for comfortable ergonomics. Text may need slight scaling on Windows.

## How to Choose a 4K Monitor

**Use case first:**
- **Gaming:** Need 120Hz+ and low response time. Prioritize refresh rate and G-Sync/FreeSync. HDMI 2.1 if you use consoles.
- **Productivity/development:** 60Hz is fine. Prioritize USB-C charging, color accuracy (99% sRGB), and ergonomic stand.
- **Creative work (photo/video):** Color gamut and accuracy matter most — 99% sRGB minimum, DCI-P3 for video production.

**Panel type summary:**
- **IPS** is the safest all-purpose choice — wide viewing angles, good color, available at every price point.
- **VA** offers deeper blacks and better contrast ratios (3000:1 vs 1000:1) but narrower viewing angles and slower response.
- **OLED** is best in class for everything but brightness — ideal for dark rooms, HDR content, and enthusiast gaming.

**GPU requirements:** 4K at 60Hz needs at least an RTX 3070 or RX 6700 XT for modern games at high settings. 4K at 120Hz+ requires an RTX 4080/4090 or RX 7900 XT for most titles.
