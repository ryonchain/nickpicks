## What to Know Before Buying a Budget Treadmill

**HP rating matters — and it's often misleading:** Look for "continuous-duty HP" ratings, not "peak HP." A 2.5 HP continuous motor is significantly more capable than a "4.0 HP peak" (which is the peak under no load). Budget treadmills frequently advertise peak HP.

**Weight capacity vs. actual capacity:** Manufacturers list maximum weight capacities that assume ideal conditions. For comfortable, durable use, look for a treadmill rated at least 50 lbs above your weight.

**Deck length:** 55" is the minimum for jogging. 60" is better for running. Under 50" is walking-only.

**Deck width:** 20" is comfortable for most users. 22" is better. 16–17" is tight and stressful at running speeds.

**Noise:** Budget treadmills are louder. If you live in an apartment, a treadmill mat ($40) reduces impact noise transmission to floors below.
