## How to Choose a Yoga Mat

Yoga mats look simple but the differences between them are substantial:

- **Thickness (mm):** Standard mats are 3–4mm — portable and stable for standing poses, but harder on joints during floor work. Thick mats (6–10mm) provide more cushioning for knees and hips in poses like child's pose and pigeon. Thicker mats compress more under standing poses, which can slightly reduce stability. 5–6mm is the sweet spot for most practitioners.
- **Grip (traction):** Open-cell rubber grips better when wet (sweaty practice). Closed-cell PVC creates firm grip when dry but becomes slippery when wet. Natural rubber provides the best grip-to-durability ratio. Microfiber tops (used in hot yoga mats) grip best when wet.
- **Material:** PVC (vinyl) is durable and inexpensive but not biodegradable. Natural rubber is biodegradable and grips well but has a rubber smell and isn't suitable for latex allergies. TPE (thermoplastic elastomer) is a middle ground — softer than rubber, more eco-friendly than PVC.
- **Eco-friendliness:** Natural rubber, organic cotton, and cork are the most environmentally sustainable. PVC is the least. Certifications to look for: OEKO-TEX (no harmful chemicals), FSC-certified rubber, B-Corp.
- **Length for tall users:** Standard mats are 68 inches. Tall practitioners (6'+) should look for 72–85-inch mats to avoid hands or feet overhanging during poses.

## FAQ

**How do I clean a yoga mat?**
Most mats clean with a mild dish soap + water spray wiped down after practice. Avoid soaking mats or machine washing unless the manufacturer explicitly allows it. Rubber mats degrade faster with harsh cleaners. Air dry flat — don't hang over a radiator.

**How long should a yoga mat last?**
Budget PVC mats: 1–2 years of daily use. Mid-range rubber mats: 3–5 years. Manduka PRO: lifetime if maintained properly. Signs you need a new mat: slipping when dry, visible pilling or flaking on the surface, or depression that doesn't spring back.

**What thickness is best for bad knees?**
6–10mm for maximum cushioning. For postures like pigeon, child's pose, or low lunge where the knee is on the floor, a thick mat makes a meaningful difference in comfort. Fold the mat in half under knees if you have an existing thin mat.
