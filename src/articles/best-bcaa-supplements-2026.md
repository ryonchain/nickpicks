# Best BCAA Supplements of 2026: Branch-Chain Amino Acids Worth Buying

BCAAs (leucine, isoleucine, and valine) are three of the nine essential amino acids your body can't produce on its own. They make up roughly one-third of your muscle protein, and during exercise, your body preferentially uses them for fuel. Supplementing reduces soreness, supports endurance, and helps preserve muscle during calorie restriction. Here are the best options in 2026.

## How to Use BCAAs Effectively

**For endurance athletes:** Take 5–10g before long training sessions to reduce muscle breakdown during sustained effort.

**For strength training:** 7–10g post-workout or intra-workout to accelerate recovery and reduce DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness).

**For calorie restriction/cutting:** BCAAs are most impactful here — they signal muscle preservation when overall protein intake is lower than ideal.

**For fasted training:** A serving of BCAAs before training gives amino acid availability without breaking your fast with full calories.

The baseline recommendation is 5g minimum per session, with 7–10g being the most commonly studied effective range. Higher doses exist but show diminishing returns above 10–12g.
