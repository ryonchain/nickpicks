## Why a Monitor Stand with Storage Matters

At the most basic level, a monitor stand raises your screen to eye level — the standard ergonomic recommendation is that the top of the monitor should be at or just below eye level when seated. Without a stand, most desk-height monitors force downward head tilt, which contributes to neck strain over time.

The storage component turns what would be dead space under your monitor into functional real estate for drives, notebooks, small plants, or desk accessories.

Key factors to evaluate:

- **Adjustable height:** Fixed-height stands lock you into one position. Adjustable stands (accordion, riser, or multi-step blocks) let you dial in the ergonomic sweet spot for your chair height and monitor size. Range of 3–7 inches covers most seated ergonomic setups.
- **Built-in drawer/organizer:** Single drawers work for remotes, pens, and small accessories. Wider open-front shelves fit a wireless keyboard underneath the monitor. Some models include both.
- **Cable routing:** Look for a rear cable pass-through channel or bottom slots that route monitor, USB hub, and power cables through the stand rather than around it. Small quality-of-life difference that becomes a big visual difference on a clean desk.
- **Weight capacity:** Most monitor stands support 20–30 lbs, which covers virtually any single flat-panel monitor. Ultrawide monitors (34"+) and CRTs push toward 30 lbs+ — verify the spec before buying if your monitor is large or unusually heavy.
- **Surface area:** A wider top platform is useful if you place items on either side of the base monitor, or if you run a dual-monitor setup where both screens share the stand.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Measure your monitor base first.** Wide-base monitors (ultrawide displays, monitors with large VESA stands) may overhang a narrow monitor stand platform. Measure the depth of your monitor base and compare to the stand's platform depth before ordering.

**Check your keyboard height before buying fixed-height stands.** A fixed 4-inch stand works great if your keyboard fits underneath — but only if the stand clearance accommodates your keyboard's thickness. Measure the height of your keyboard at its tallest point before buying.

**Weight capacity for ultrawide monitors.** A standard 27-inch monitor weighs 8–12 lbs. A 34-inch ultrawide weighs 15–22 lbs. A 49-inch super-ultrawide can hit 30+ lbs. Verify the stand's capacity against your monitor's weight.

**Cable management changes the desk experience.** A rear cable pass-through takes 30 seconds to set up and permanently changes how clean the desk looks. Prioritize stands with at least a basic rear cable slot if desk aesthetics matter to you.
