## The Art of Choosing a Luxury Perfume

Fragrance selection is more science than art once you understand a few key concepts:

**Concentration matters.** Parfum (or extrait) has the highest oil concentration (20–40%) and longest lasting power—12+ hours. Eau de Parfum (EDP) is 15–20% concentration, lasting 8–12 hours. Eau de Toilette (EDT) is 5–15%, lasting 4–8 hours. For investment pieces, EDP or parfum concentration gives you the most value per wear.

**The pyramid structure.** Every complex fragrance unfolds in three stages:
- **Top notes** — The first 15–30 minutes. Often citrus, herbs, or light florals. This is what you smell in the store.
- **Heart notes** — 30 minutes to 3 hours. The true character of the fragrance emerges here.
- **Base notes** — 3 hours to all day. Musks, woods, resins. What people remember you by.

**Your skin chemistry affects everything.** The same fragrance smells different on different people due to pH, diet, and hormones. Always test on your skin (not paper) and wear it for at least 2 hours before buying.

## How to Make Fragrance Last Longer

Even the most expensive perfume won't last without proper application:

1. **Apply to pulse points** — Wrists, neck, behind ears, inside elbows, behind knees. These warm spots amplify projection.
2. **Moisturize first** — Fragrance clings better to hydrated skin. Use unscented lotion or petroleum jelly as a base.
3. **Don't rub your wrists together** — This breaks down top notes faster. Dab or spray and let it settle.
4. **Spray on hair** — Hair holds scent beautifully. Spritz from 8–10 inches to avoid the alcohol drying strands.
5. **Layer with matching body products** — Many luxury fragrance lines offer matching lotion and shower gel. Using the full line doubles longevity.

## Storage Tips to Protect Your Investment

Fragrance degrades from light, heat, and air exposure:

- Store bottles in original boxes, in a cool dark drawer or closet
- Avoid bathroom storage (heat and humidity are enemies)
- Don't shake the bottle—it introduces air
- Use atomizers for travel to avoid exposing the main bottle to air repeatedly

Properly stored, a quality fragrance lasts 3–5 years unopened; 1–2 years after first use.
