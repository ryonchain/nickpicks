## How to Choose: Season and Occasion

**Spring/Summer:** Fresh, floral, citrus, or aquatic — lighter molecular structures project beautifully in warm air.

**Fall/Winter:** Oriental, gourmand, woody, amber — heavier base notes shine in cold air and on skin warmed by layers.

**Office/Day:** Moderate sillage (Jo Malone, Lancôme). Avoid heavy orientals in close quarters.

**Evening/Events:** Anything goes — Black Opium, heavier florals, orientals. More projection, longer-lasting.

## Application Tips

- Apply to pulse points: wrists, neck, behind ears, inner elbows
- Don't rub wrists together — this crushes fragrance molecules and accelerates top-note burnoff
- Spray clothes as well as skin for longer longevity (test for staining on light fabrics)
- Layer a matching body lotion to extend wear time by 2–4 hours
- Store away from heat and light — bathroom shelves degrade perfume over time

## Frequently Asked Questions

**EDP vs EDT: Which is better?**
Eau de Parfum (EDP) has higher fragrance concentration (15–20%) than Eau de Toilette (EDT, 5–15%). EDP lasts longer and projects more. EDT is typically lighter, better for daytime or warm weather. Both are valid — choose based on the scent's intensity and your preference.

**How many sprays should I use?**
2–4 sprays for most fragrances. Start lighter and add if you want more projection. You can always add; you can't subtract.

**Do perfumes expire?**
Yes — typically 3–5 years after opening, longer unopened. Oxidation changes the scent, especially citrus top notes. Proper storage (cool, dark, capped) extends shelf life significantly.

**Why does perfume smell different on me vs the store?**
Skin chemistry — pH, moisture level, body heat — alters how fragrance smells and performs. Always test on your skin, not a card, and wait 30 minutes for the dry-down.
