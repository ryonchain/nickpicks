## Why Precision Matters in Sous Vide

Sous vide eliminates guesswork by cooking food at the exact target temperature. A medium-rare steak cooks to 130°F every time — no thermometer checking, no cutting to peek. The key performance metrics:

- **Temperature accuracy (±°F/°C):** Most quality immersion circulators maintain ±0.1°C (±0.2°F). This precision is sufficient for all proteins and vegetables. Sub-±0.05°C units exist but the real-world difference is negligible for home cooking.
- **Wattage:** Higher wattage heats water faster and recovers quickly when cold food is added. 750W handles up to 5 gallons adequately. 1000–1200W recovers faster after adding frozen proteins and handles larger containers (8+ gallons) without straining.
- **Clamp mechanism:** Tool-free one-handed clamps are meaningfully better than screw-type for repeated daily use. Look for clamps that adjust to containers from 1 to 6 inches thick.
- **App connectivity:** Wi-Fi allows remote monitoring from anywhere. Bluetooth-only requires being within ~30 feet of the cooker. Manual dial models are simpler but can't notify you when cook time completes.
- **Max water volume:** Critical if you cook large roasts or whole chickens. Match container size to minimum and maximum water depth specifications.

## FAQ

**Do I need a vacuum sealer for sous vide?**
No. A ziplock bag with the air pressed out using the water displacement method works for most proteins. Vacuum sealers improve results for long cooks (24+ hours) and foods with marinades, but they're not required to start.

**How long can I safely cook sous vide?**
Most proteins are safe for 1–4 hours at proper temperatures. Extended cooks (8–72 hours) for tougher cuts like short ribs or brisket are safe and desirable for texture. Consult the Anova or ChefSteps guides for time/temperature combinations.

**What container should I use?**
A 12-quart polycarbonate container is the standard choice — inexpensive, see-through, and fits most immersion circulators. Camwear containers work identically. A stock pot works but retains less heat.
