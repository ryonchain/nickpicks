## What Does Collagen Actually Do?

### Skin
Hydrolyzed collagen peptides (Types I and III) are the most clinically studied. Eight-week studies consistently show improvements in:
- Skin hydration (+28% in one meta-analysis)
- Skin elasticity (+19%)
- Wrinkle depth reduction

### Joints
Type II collagen (from chicken cartilage) is the primary collagen in articular cartilage. Studies show 40mg/day of undenatured Type II collagen reduces joint pain in osteoarthritis patients.

### Hair and Nails
Type I collagen is the dominant structural protein in hair follicles and nails. Studies show collagen supplementation reduces nail brittleness and breakage.

### Gut Health
Collagen peptides support intestinal lining health. Glycine (abundant in collagen) has anti-inflammatory effects in the GI tract.

## FAQ

**What is the best collagen supplement for skin?**
Vital Proteins or Sports Research Type I + III bovine collagen peptides. 10–20g/day for 8–12 weeks.

**What is the best collagen for joints?**
Multi-collagen blends that include Type II collagen (from chicken cartilage) — Garden of Life Multi Collagen or Ancient Nutrition.

**Is marine collagen better than bovine?**
Marine collagen is Type I and absorbed faster due to smaller peptide size. Bovine is Type I + III, cheaper, and better studied. Either works for skin benefits.

**Do collagen supplements actually work?**
The evidence is strongest for hydrated skin and reduced joint pain in osteoarthritis. Results are modest but consistent across multiple randomized trials.
