## What "No Subscription" Actually Means

Most monitors use one of three models:

**DECT/FHSS (always free)**: These monitors transmit directly from camera to parent unit on a dedicated radio frequency. No internet required, no app, no subscription — ever. Examples: Eufy SpaceView Pro, Infant Optics DRX-8, HelloBaby HB6550.

**WiFi with optional subscription**: The camera streams to your phone via your home network. Live view is usually free. Cloud recording, sleep analytics, or advanced alerts may require a paid plan. Examples: Eufy Video WiFi, Vava.

**Subscription-required**: The core video or alert feature requires a paid plan. Examples: Nanit (sleep tracking), Owlet (vitals history). Not included in this list.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which baby monitor works without a subscription?**
The Eufy SpaceView Pro, Infant Optics DRX-8, and HelloBaby HB6550 all work without any monthly fees. DECT/FHSS monitors like these don't use the cloud at all — they connect directly to the parent unit.

**Can WiFi baby monitors work without a subscription?**
Yes. The Eufy Video Baby Monitor (WiFi version) and Vava Baby Monitor let you live-view from your phone without a paid plan. Subscriptions are optional for extra features like cloud recording or sleep analytics.

**What is the difference between a subscription and a no-subscription baby monitor?**
Subscription monitors (like Nanit) store video history, provide sleep analytics, and offer breathing motion alerts — all in the cloud. No-subscription monitors save video locally (or not at all) but give you live video for free forever.

**Are DECT baby monitors safer than WiFi monitors?**
DECT monitors operate on a dedicated 1.9 GHz frequency separate from your home network. They can't be hacked via the internet and don't collect your data. WiFi monitors are generally secure with strong passwords but involve cloud transmission.

**What is the best cheap baby monitor with no monthly fee?**
The HelloBaby HB6550 is the best budget pick under $60 with no subscription, no WiFi dependency, and clear 720p video. The VTech DM221 is even cheaper ($35) if audio-only monitoring is enough.
