## Swing vs. Bouncer: Which Do You Need?

**Swings** provide consistent automated motion over longer periods — great for extended soothing sessions. Better for fussy or colicky babies.

**Bouncers** are responsive to baby's own movement. More portable, usually cheaper. Better for alert babies who like self-soothing.

**Many families buy both** — a swing for newborn fussiness and a bouncer/rocker for daytime play as baby grows.

## Safety Reminder

The AAP recommends that babies sleep on their backs on a firm, flat surface. Swings and bouncers are for supervised soothing — not overnight sleep. The SNOO is an exception as it's specifically designed and tested for safe sleep.
