The best portable BBQ grills balance heat output, cooking space, fuel efficiency, and actual portability — some fold down to suitcase size, others sit on table legs, all are designed to cook real food in real conditions.

## How to Choose: Key Factors

**Fuel type:**
- **Charcoal** — best smoke flavor, cheapest to run, requires prep time (20-30 min to get to temperature)
- **Propane** — instant on/off, precise heat control, requires fuel canisters
- **Wood pellets** — best flavor and precise temperature control, requires power outlet
- **Wood/campfire** — no fuel cost, primal experience, requires fire

**Cooking area:**
- 150–180 sq in: 2–3 people
- 200–280 sq in: 4–5 people
- 280+ sq in: 6+ people or 2 rounds of burgers

**Transport:**
- Under 10 lbs: backpacking/hiking accessible
- 10–20 lbs: easy to carry, fits in a car
- 20–30 lbs: drive-to destinations only
