## How to Choose a Student Backpack

**Capacity guide:**
- **20–22L:** Minimalist carry — laptop, notebook, charger, water bottle. Light but limited.
- **27–30L:** The sweet spot for most students — room for textbooks, gym clothes, and daily gear.
- **34L+:** For students who carry everything — textbooks, gym gear, snacks, laptop, and a change of clothes.

**Laptop compartment what to look for:**
- Dedicated, padded sleeve (not just a slip pocket shared with other contents)
- Fleece or soft interior lining to prevent screen scratches
- Slightly recessed from the bottom of the bag (protects from drops)

**Back panel matters:** For daily campus walks with 15–20 lbs, a contoured or ventilated back panel makes a real difference. Budget bags use flat foam; technical bags use structured suspension systems.

## FAQ

**What size laptop compartment do I need for a 15.6" laptop?**
Look for a sleeve labeled "up to 15.6 inches" or larger. Most modern student backpacks accommodate 15.6" as the standard size. If you have a 16" or 17" laptop, verify the exact sleeve dimensions.

**Are waterproof backpacks worth it for students?**
Fully waterproof backpacks are overkill for most campus environments. DWR-coated bags (like the North Face Recon) handle typical rain and splashes. Only consider waterproof materials if you frequently bike in heavy rain or live in a rainy climate.

**Can I use a backpack as a personal item on a plane?**
Most student-sized backpacks (22–27L) fit under airplane seats and qualify as personal items. Confirm with your airline — typical personal item limits are 18"×14"×8" (JetBlue) to 18"×14"×8" (Southwest). The Timbuk2 Authority is specifically designed with TSA layouts for travel ease.
