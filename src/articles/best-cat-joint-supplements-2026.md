## Home Environment Modifications for Arthritic Cats

Supplements work better when paired with environmental accommodations:

**Litter box:** Use low-sided boxes or those with a cut-out entry. High litter box sides are a major barrier for arthritic cats, leading to inappropriate elimination.

**Resting spots:** Provide ramps or steps to favorite elevated areas (window perches, beds). Cats with arthritis still want high spots but can't jump safely.

**Warmth:** Arthritis pain worsens in cold environments. Heated cat beds provide meaningful pain relief and many arthritic cats will preferentially use them.

**Food and water bowls:** Raise bowls 3–4 inches to reduce neck flexion discomfort. Wide, shallow bowls prevent whisker fatigue.

**Grooming assistance:** Arthritic cats often can't groom their backs and hindquarters — gentle daily brushing helps coat health and provides opportunity to check for secondary skin issues.
