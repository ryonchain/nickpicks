## Reviews

### Traeger Pro 575 — Best Overall

Traeger invented the pellet grill and the Pro 575 distills 30+ years of refinement into an approachable package. WiFIRE connectivity lets you control temperature, check meat probes, and set timers from your phone (the app is genuinely good). D2 drivetrain delivers faster startup and more consistent temperature.

**Pros:** Rock-solid temperature control (±5°F); excellent app; big recipe community; reliable pellet feed.
**Cons:** Doesn't sear as hot as Weber or Camp Chef; premium price for the cooking area.

**Verdict:** The safest choice. You get industry-leading support, massive recipe resources, and proven hardware.

### Camp Chef Woodwind 24 — Best Value

Camp Chef undercuts Traeger on price while matching or exceeding it on features. The Woodwind 24 has 811 sq in of cooking space (more than the Pro 575), a side-mounted Sidekick burner port (add a propane burner or Sear Box separately), and a 10-position smoke dial for dialing in smoke intensity.

**Pros:** Largest cooking area in its price range; smoke control dial; compatible with Camp Chef Sidekick; excellent ash cleanout system.
**Cons:** App is functional but inferior to Traeger's; fewer recipe resources.

**Verdict:** The pick if you want maximum cooking space and value. Pair with the Sear Box to get searing capability.

### Weber SmokeFire EX4 — Best for High-Heat Searing

Weber fixed the early-generation SmokeFire reliability issues and delivered something genuinely different: a pellet grill that reaches 600°F for true high-heat searing. Smoke and sear on the same grill without a separate device.

**Pros:** 600°F max temp for real sear marks; excellent even heating across the grates; Weber quality build.
**Cons:** Most expensive mainstream option; pellet hopper holds less than competitors; earlier-generation models had reliability issues (now resolved).

**Verdict:** The pick for steak lovers who refuse to compromise on sear quality.

### Pit Boss 700FB — Best Budget Pellet Grill

The Pit Boss 700FB offers 700 sq in of cooking space and a direct-flame access slider for charcoal-like searing for around $400. The "Flame Broiler" access is unique in budget pellet grills — slide open a panel to expose direct flame for burgers and steaks.

**Pros:** Lowest price for the cooking area; direct-flame access; strong community.
**Cons:** Temperature swings wider than premium brands (±15–20°F); basic controls; lower build quality.

**Verdict:** The best pellet grill under $500 if you're willing to accept some temperature variability.

## Pellet Grill Buying Guide

**Cooking area:** Figure 100 sq in per person. A 575 sq in grill comfortably handles 4–6 people.

**Temperature range:** Smoking needs 225°F. Grilling needs 400–500°F. Searing needs 550°F+. Most grills cover smoking and grilling; only Weber and a few others reach true sear temps.

**WiFi connectivity:** Worth paying for if you do long cooks (8–16 hour briskets). Monitor from your phone without standing by the grill.

**Hopper size:** A 20-lb hopper lasts 10–20 hours at smoking temps. Enough for all-day cooks without refilling.

**Pellet brand:** Traeger pellets are premium but pricey. Lumber Jack and Bear Mountain offer excellent smoke flavor at lower cost.

## Bottom Line

The **Traeger Pro 575** wins on reliability and support. The **Camp Chef Woodwind 24** wins on value. The **Pit Boss 700FB** wins on budget. If you grill steaks more than smoke briskets, consider the **Weber SmokeFire** for its searing capability.
