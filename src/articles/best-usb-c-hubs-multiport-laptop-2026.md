## USB-C Hub Buying Guide

### Hub vs. Docking Station: What's the Difference?

**USB-C Hubs** are compact, portable, and bus-powered (no external power supply). They handle everyday expansion: HDMI, USB-A, SD cards. Good for travel and light desk use.

**Docking Stations** have their own power supply, support more ports, and handle dual monitors and high-wattage laptop charging. For a permanent desk setup with multiple monitors, a dock is worth the price.

### USB-C vs. Thunderbolt 4

**USB-C** is a connector type — bandwidth tops out at 10–20Gbps depending on version. **Thunderbolt 4** is a protocol that runs over USB-C connectors and delivers 40Gbps. Thunderbolt 4 docks (CalDigit TS4) work with both Thunderbolt and USB-C laptops, but only deliver full performance on Thunderbolt-equipped laptops.

For MacBook Pro and most Dell XPS users: your laptop has Thunderbolt 4. Use it.

### Power Delivery: How Much Do You Need?

- **MacBook Air M2:** 30W minimum, 67W preferred for fast charging
- **MacBook Pro 14":** 67W minimum, 96W preferred  
- **MacBook Pro 16":** 96W minimum, 140W for full-speed charging under load
- **Windows ultrabooks:** Typically 45–65W for charging while working

Check your laptop's rated wattage and buy a hub that matches or exceeds it.

### HDMI 4K@30Hz vs. 4K@60Hz

4K at 30Hz is workable for static content but noticeably choppy for scrolling, video playback, and any animated content. If you're using a 4K monitor as your main display, spend the extra money for 4K@60Hz (Satechi Slim V2, CalDigit TS4).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will a USB-C hub work with my MacBook?**
Yes — all USB-C hubs listed here are compatible with MacBook Air and MacBook Pro (M1/M2/M3). For Thunderbolt-only features (dual monitors, >40Gbps transfers), make sure your MacBook model has Thunderbolt ports.

**Do USB-C hubs reduce laptop performance?**
No. USB-C hubs don't affect CPU or GPU performance. However, USB-C to HDMI adapters using DisplayLink technology (required for some dual-monitor setups) add minor CPU overhead (2–5%).

**Can I charge my laptop through a USB-C hub?**
Yes — if the hub has Power Delivery pass-through. Check the wattage: 60W PD is fine for MacBook Air, 96W or more recommended for MacBook Pro 14" and 16".

**Why does my hub get hot?**
USB-C hubs convert power and handle multiple signals simultaneously — heat is normal under load, especially when driving 4K displays. Aluminum hubs dissipate heat better than plastic ones. Severe heat (too hot to touch) may indicate a compatibility issue.

**Do I need a Thunderbolt dock or will USB-C work?**
For single-monitor setups and standard peripherals, a USB-C hub is sufficient. For dual 4K monitors, multiple 10Gbps peripherals simultaneously, or NVMe external storage at full speed, Thunderbolt 4 is worth the premium.
