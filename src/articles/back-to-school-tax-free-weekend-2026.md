## Does Amazon Count for Tax-Free Weekend?

**Yes** — Amazon participates in state sales tax holidays. If your state has a tax-free weekend and you order qualifying items during the event window, Amazon will not collect sales tax on those items.

**How it works:**
- Amazon automatically applies your state's tax exemption at checkout during the qualifying window
- The item must ship and be invoiced during the tax-free period to qualify (not just ordered)
- Digital items (software, ebooks) may not qualify depending on state rules

**Important:** Check your cart at checkout during the weekend. If tax is still showing on qualifying items, verify your state and the item category.

## Tax-Free Weekend FAQ

**Do gift cards bought during tax-free weekend count?**
No — gift cards are not taxable purchases. You pay tax when you redeem them, and the redemption typically doesn't get the tax-free exemption.

**What if an item is on sale and also qualifies for tax-free weekend?**
You get both — the sale price and no sales tax. This is the best-case scenario for big purchases.

**Does Buy Now Pay Later (Afterpay, Klarna, etc.) qualify?**
Yes — what matters is when the purchase is invoiced, not when you make the final payment.

**Are computers per-item or per-transaction limits?**
Per-item. If a state exempts computers up to $1,500, you could buy two $1,200 laptops and both qualify.

**What if I'm shopping in a different state?**
You generally owe sales tax based on the state where the item ships to (for online purchases) or where you make the purchase (for in-store). If you live in Florida and buy during Florida's tax-free weekend, you qualify regardless of where the Amazon warehouse ships from.
