## How to Choose an Electric Toothbrush for Kids

**1. Age-Appropriate Brush Head Size**
A brush head that's too large will skip teeth in the back of small mouths. Look for brush heads labeled for your child's age range. Most kids' electric toothbrushes cover ages 3–12, but brush head size within that range varies. Sonicare and Oral-B both offer smaller heads for 3–6 age groups.

**2. Timer and Technique**
The most important feature isn't vibration speed — it's the timer. Any brush that runs for two minutes with 30-second interval cues is teaching your child the single most impactful habit in dental care. If a budget model has a reliable timer and soft bristles, it beats an expensive gimmick with no timer.

**3. App Engagement vs. Simplicity**
Connected apps genuinely help with 5-to-10-year-olds who resist brushing but love screen time. For younger children under 5, an app adds complexity without benefit. For teenagers, a no-nonsense brush with good performance matters more than characters.

**4. Replacement Head Availability and Cost**
Kids' brush heads should be replaced every 3 months. Oral-B and Sonicare heads are available everywhere — grocery stores, pharmacy chains, and Amazon. Proprietary heads from lesser-known brands can disappear from shelves, leaving you with a handle and no compatible head. Factor replacement cost into your decision.

## FAQ

**At what age should kids start using an electric toothbrush?**
Most dentists say ages 3–4 is appropriate, once a child can hold the brush without help and understands not to bite the head. Start with a soft-bristled kids' model and always supervise until age 7 or 8.

**Do electric toothbrushes clean kids' teeth better than manual?**
Studies consistently show electric toothbrushes remove more plaque than manual brushes, particularly because they brush more consistently and often include timers that ensure adequate duration. The key benefit for kids specifically is the timer — it removes the guesswork from a two-minute session.

**How often should kids' brush heads be replaced?**
Every three months, or sooner if bristles are visibly splayed. Splayed bristles are significantly less effective and can indicate the child is brushing too hard — a pressure sensor (Oral-B iO My Way) helps with that habit.

**Is it safe for kids with braces to use an electric toothbrush?**
Yes — many orthodontists specifically recommend electric toothbrushes for kids in braces because they do a better job cleaning around brackets. Use a brush head designed for orthodontic use or an ortho-specific head if your brand offers one.
