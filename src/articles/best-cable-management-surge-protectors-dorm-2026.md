## Why Surge Protection Matters in Dorms

Older dorm buildings have electrical systems that can experience voltage spikes during power fluctuations. A laptop (typically $800–$1,500) and monitor connected to an unprotected power strip can be damaged by a power surge. Surge protectors contain a sacrificial component (MOV) that absorbs the spike — the device is replaced instead of your laptop.

**Key spec to look for:** Joule rating. Higher is better. 600J is minimum; 1000J+ is recommended for expensive equipment. Don't buy a power strip — buy a surge protector with a stated joule rating.

## Cable Management: Taming the Setup

A good surge protector solves the outlet problem. Cable management solves the mess. Three products cover most dorm cable situations:

**Cable clips (Velcro or adhesive):** Stick to the back of a desk or along a wall and route cables through them. VELCRO Brand ONE-WRAP ties (~$8) are the most reusable — wrap and unwrap as needed, no residue. Adhesive clips ($10/20-pack) are more permanent.

**Cable sleeve:** For a bundle of cables running from a desk to the surge protector on the floor, a braided cable sleeve hides everything in a single wrapped tube. JOTO Cable Management Sleeve (~$8 for 2) covers 12–20 cables in one tidy bundle.

**Cable box:** Hide the surge protector and power brick in a cable management box (D-Line or similar, ~$25). Slots on the sides route cables in and out, the box lid covers the strip itself. The desk looks clean from above.
