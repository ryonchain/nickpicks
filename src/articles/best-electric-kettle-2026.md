## Buying Guide: What to Look for in an Electric Kettle

- **Capacity:** 0.8–1L for single-serve; 1.5–1.8L for households or multiple cups.
- **Temperature presets:** Essential for green and white teas; less critical for black tea and basic coffee. Variable control (1°F increment) is only needed for specialty brewing.
- **Gooseneck vs. standard spout:** Gooseneck spouts give pour-over coffee brewers precise flow control. Standard spouts are fine for everything else.
- **Keep-warm:** Useful if you brew slowly or make multiple cups; unnecessary for fast boiling routines.
- **Material:** Stainless steel interior is safest for taste neutrality; avoid plastic interior models for hot water contact.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the material of an electric kettle affect the taste of water?**
A: Yes. Stainless steel and glass interiors are taste-neutral. Plastic interiors (especially low-quality ones) can impart a faint plastic taste, especially when new. Always choose BPA-free plastic at minimum — stainless or glass is better.

**Q: What temperature should I use for green tea?**
A: Green tea brews best at 160–175°F. Boiling water (212°F) makes green tea bitter. A variable-temperature kettle is worth it if you drink green tea regularly.

**Q: How fast should an electric kettle boil?**
A: Most 1.5L kettles boil in 4–5 minutes. Smaller kettles (0.8L) can hit 3 minutes. Speed is determined by wattage — 1500W is standard; 1800W+ is faster.
