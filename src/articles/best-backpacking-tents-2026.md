## Backpacking Tent Buying Guide

### Seasons Rating
- **3-season:** Handles spring, summer, and fall. Not designed for snow loads or extreme cold.
- **3+ season:** Extends into light winter conditions — stormier shoulder-season use.
- **4-season:** Full winter and mountaineering conditions. Heavier and warmer.

### Weight Categories
- **Standard (3–5 lbs):** Best for casual weekend backpackers and beginners
- **Lightweight (2–3 lbs):** Right for most regular backpackers
- **Ultralight (under 2 lbs):** Thru-hikers and those who prioritize speed

### Freestanding vs. Non-Freestanding
Freestanding tents stand up without stakes — easier to set up on hard or rocky ground. Non-freestanding are lighter but require good staking conditions.

### Waterproof Rating (mm)
- **1,000mm:** Handles light to moderate rain
- **1,500mm:** Handles sustained moderate rain — minimum for most backpacking
- **2,000mm+:** Heavy rain, driven rain, extended storms

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I keep a backpacking tent dry?**
Always use a footprint under the tent floor to prevent abrasion and moisture seeping through. Keep the rainfly tensioned properly so water runs off instead of pooling.

**How long should a backpacking tent last?**
With proper care — cleaning, storing dry, using a footprint — a quality tent lasts 5–15 years. UV exposure and abrasion are the primary causes of early failure.

**Can I use a backpacking tent for car camping?**
Yes, but you're paying a weight premium for features you don't need car camping. For occasional car campers who also backpack, a lightweight tent does double duty perfectly.
