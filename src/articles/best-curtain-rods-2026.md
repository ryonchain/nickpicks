# Best Curtain Rods in 2026

A curtain rod's job is to stay in place while supporting the weight of the curtains. Most cheap rods sag in the middle under heavy drapes, bow at the ends, or slip out of tension brackets. The right choice depends on window width, curtain weight, and whether you want layered panels (double rod) or sheer + blackout combinations.

---

## Types of Curtain Rods

| Type | Best For | Weight Limit |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Tension rod | Closets, café curtains, inside mount | Light fabrics only |
| Standard wall-mount (single) | Most windows, medium-weight curtains | Up to ~15 lbs per bracket |
| Double rod | Sheers + drapes layered | Up to ~12 lbs per layer |
| Bypass/sliding | Closets, very wide windows | Moderate weight |
| Decorative bypass | Living rooms, floor-to-ceiling drapes | Medium weight |

## Choosing the Right Diameter

- **1/2"–5/8":** Lightweight sheers only
- **3/4"–1":** Standard curtains (most common)
- **1.25"–1.5":** Heavy drapes, velvet, lined curtains

---

## Hanging Height and Width

**Height:** Hang 4–6 inches above the window frame (or higher — up to ceiling height for drama). Higher installation makes windows appear taller.

**Width:** Extend the rod 8–12 inches past the window frame on each side. This allows curtains to stack to the sides and expose the full glass area when open.

**Note for blackout:** For true blackout, use wall-mounted brackets that pull the rod close to the wall and use curtain panels that overlap the window edge by at least 4 inches on each side.
