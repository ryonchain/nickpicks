## Neck Wallet Buying Guide

### RFID blocking: what it actually protects

RFID (radio-frequency identification) blocking prevents contactless card readers from reading your cards through fabric. Modern contactless credit cards (those with the wireless symbol), passports issued after 2007, and some hotel key cards all have RFID chips. Without blocking, a bad actor with a reader could potentially capture your card data in a crowded space.

Most quality neck wallets include RFID blocking. The certification to look for is "RFID shielded to 13.56 MHz" — which covers credit/debit cards and passports. Lower-frequency blocking (125 kHz) is for older key fobs, not payment cards.

### Strap safety: adjustability and breakaway

Neck strap length determines where the wallet sits on your chest. You want it below your collarbone but above your belt — if it drops too low, it's visible above waistbands. Adjustability range of 24–44 inches covers most adults wearing the strap under a shirt.

Look for a **breakaway clasp** — a tension-release mechanism that opens if someone grabs the strap and pulls hard. This prevents the strap from becoming a choking hazard. Some cheaper wallets use a fixed loop that won't break, which is a safety concern in specific scenarios.

### What to carry vs. what to leave at the hotel

| Carry in neck wallet | Leave in hotel safe |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Original passport | Extra credit cards |
| Primary credit card | Large amounts of cash |
| Travel insurance card | Jewelry |
| Emergency contact info | Second ID documents |

### Sizing: passport wallets vs. card-only wallets

A full US passport is 5 x 3.5 inches. European passports are slightly smaller. If you need to carry the physical document, look for internal dimensions of at least 5.25 x 3.75 inches. Many "passport neck wallets" technically fit a passport but are very tight — check the exact internal dimensions, not just "fits passport" marketing copy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a neck wallet better than a money belt?**
Neck wallets hang from your neck under clothing — easier access but can be bulkier around the chest. Money belts sit around your waist under your pants — flatter profile but harder to access in crowded spaces. For passport-sized documents, a neck wallet typically offers more capacity.

**Can pickpockets get into a neck wallet?**
Not easily. The wallet hangs under your shirt against your chest, accessible only by reaching inside your clothing. In extremely crowded places, be aware of your surroundings. Zipper pulls with small padlocks add another deterrent layer.

**Do neck wallets go through airport security?**
Yes, but you'll need to remove it at the security scanner if it contains metal (cards, coins) unless you have TSA PreCheck. Wallets with minimal metal hardware pass more easily.

**Will a neck wallet show through a thin shirt?**
Thicker/bulkier models can show under fitted or thin fabric. Look for slim profiles under 8mm and soft materials. Wearing the strap long enough that the wallet sits low on your chest also reduces visibility.
