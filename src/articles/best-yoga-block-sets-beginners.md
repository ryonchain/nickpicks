Yoga blocks are the most useful prop a beginner can own. ## Foam vs. Cork vs. Bamboo: Which Material Is Right for You?

The three most common yoga block materials each have a place:

- **Foam (EVA):** Lightweight, affordable, and forgiving on joints. Best for beginners who need cushioning and support.
- **Cork:** Firmer, heavier, and more eco-friendly. Better grip when hands get sweaty. Ideal as you advance.
- **Bamboo/Wood:** Most stable and premium-feeling. Rarely seen in beginner sets but excellent for advanced balance work.

**For beginners: start with foam.** It's gentler, lighter, and easier to position during practice.

---

## What to Look for in a Beginner Yoga Block Set

- **Size** — Standard blocks are 4" × 6" × 9". This size works for virtually all poses and body types.
- **Density** — Firm enough to support your weight in balance poses, soft enough to be comfortable under hands and knees.
- **Non-slip surface** — Especially important if you practice in socks or have sweaty hands.
- **Sold as a pair** — Most poses benefit from two blocks. Always buy a set.
- **Weight** — Lighter blocks are easier to move around during flow sequences.
