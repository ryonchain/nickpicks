## What Makes a Shower Speaker Worth Buying

A shower speaker lives in a uniquely hostile environment — steam, splashing water, and humid air year-round. Here's what actually matters:

- **IP Rating:** IP67 (fully waterproof to 1 meter for 30 min) is the minimum for shower use. IPX7 is similar but not necessarily dust-rated. Avoid anything below IPX6 for shower mounting.
- **Mounting System:** Suction cups, carabiner clips, or hook mounts keep the speaker in place on tile. Speakers that just sit on a soap dish vibrate off when bass hits.
- **Sound Quality in Tiled Spaces:** Hard surfaces in bathrooms create reverb. Speakers with slightly tighter EQ (less midrange boom) sound cleaner in tile-heavy bathrooms.
- **Battery Life:** Short shower sessions mean battery life matters less than portability — but 8+ hours means you can use the speaker outside the shower too.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### IP67 vs. IPX7: What's the Difference?
IPX7 means waterproof to 1 meter for 30 minutes, but not necessarily dust-rated. IP67 means both fully dustproof AND waterproof to the same depth. For bathroom use, either works fine. For outdoor use on dusty trails or beaches, IP67 is the better choice.

### Suction Cups vs. Clips and Straps
Suction cups on tile lose grip over time — steam and soap residue reduce suction. Carabiner clips (JBL Clip 4) and strap mounts (Bose, UE) are more reliable for permanent shower placement. Don't trust suction-only mounting for a speaker above a hard floor.

### Bluetooth Range Through Walls
Most shower speakers pair with phones inside the bathroom — but if you want to start music from outside, Bluetooth 5.0+ typically reaches 20–30 feet through typical walls. All speakers in this roundup use Bluetooth 5.0 or newer.

### Speaker Phone Use in the Shower
Several shower speakers include microphones for hands-free calls. Shower acoustics (reverb, water noise) make call quality variable. The Bose SoundLink Micro has the best microphone in this category — others work but sound noticeably worse in running water.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I leave a shower speaker in the shower permanently?**
Yes, IP67/IPX7 speakers handle permanent shower humidity without issue. Just ensure the charging port is completely dry before plugging in — most have rubber port covers to prevent moisture entry.

**How do I mount a shower speaker on tile?**
Carabiner clips on a towel bar or shower caddy rail work best. For wall mounting, Command waterproof adhesive hooks work well for speakers with strap loops. Avoid using regular Command strips — shower steam degrades the adhesive faster.

**Will steam damage a waterproof speaker?**
Steam and humidity are generally safe for IP67/IPX7 speakers. The real risk is submersion through an open charging port — always close the port cover before any water exposure.

**How loud should a shower speaker be?**
A typical shower runs 60–70 dB of water noise. A speaker needs to produce 75+ dB at shower distance to be clearly audible. All speakers in this roundup exceed that threshold at close range.

**Can shower speakers connect to multiple devices?**
Multipoint Bluetooth (two simultaneous devices) is available on select models. Most shower speakers connect to one device at a time. To switch devices, disconnect from the current one and pair the new one.
