## Power Bank Buying Guide

### Capacity: How Much Do You Actually Need?

Real-world capacity is typically 70–80% of the rated mAh due to voltage conversion losses. A 10,000 mAh bank delivers about 7,000–8,000 mAh of usable energy. 

Rough charging estimates:
- Smartphone (iPhone 15, Galaxy S25): ~3,500–5,000 mAh per full charge
- iPad / Android tablet: ~8,000–10,000 mAh per full charge
- MacBook Air (M3): ~52,600 mAh — requires 20,000+ mAh power bank

### USB-C PD (Power Delivery) Wattage

For phones: **18–45W** is sufficient — more is wasted. For tablets: **30–65W**. For most laptops: **65–100W**. For gaming laptops: **100–140W**. Buying a power bank with more wattage than you need doesn't hurt — the device draws only what it needs — but paying for 140W capability when you only charge phones is unnecessary.

### Airline Carry-On Rules

Most airlines follow TSA rules: power banks must be in carry-on luggage (not checked bags) and must be under 100Wh. 100Wh ≈ 27,000 mAh at 3.7V nominal. Banks from 100–160Wh require airline approval. Anything over 160Wh is prohibited. Most 26,000–27,800 mAh banks fall just under 100Wh — verify the Wh rating on the spec sheet, not just the mAh.

### Form Factor

**Pocketable (under 200g):** 10,000 mAh options like the Anker Nano. **Bag-friendly brick:** 20,000–27,000 mAh options. **Slim flat:** Baseus Blade (thin but still bag-only). For daily commuters, pocketable wins. For travelers with a bag, go for capacity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many times can a power bank charge my phone?**
Divide the power bank's mAh by your phone's battery size, then multiply by 0.75 (efficiency loss). Example: 10,000 mAh bank × 0.75 = 7,500 mAh usable ÷ 4,500 mAh (iPhone 15 Pro Max) = ~1.7 full charges. A 20,000 mAh bank gives roughly 3–4 full phone charges in practice.

**Can I take a power bank on a plane?**
Yes — in your carry-on only, not checked luggage. Most airlines allow up to 100Wh (~27,000 mAh). Some allow up to 160Wh with airline approval. Always check your specific airline's policy. Never put power banks in checked baggage (lithium battery fire risk).

**Is it bad to leave a power bank plugged in overnight?**
Modern power banks have overcharge protection circuits that stop charging at 100%. It's not ideal to leave them plugged in indefinitely (reduces long-term battery health over years), but occasional overnight charging won't damage them. Anker and Mophie both build in temperature protection that cuts charging if the unit overheats.

**What's the difference between USB-C and USB-C PD?**
Regular USB-C delivers up to 5W (standard) or 15W (some implementations). USB-C PD (Power Delivery) is a fast-charging standard that negotiates higher voltage/wattage — up to 240W in PD 3.1 spec. For laptop charging, you need USB-C PD. Always check the wattage spec on the port, not just whether the port is physically USB-C.
