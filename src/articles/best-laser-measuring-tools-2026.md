## What Separates Good Laser Measurers from Cheap Ones

### Measurement Accuracy
Specification accuracy (±1/16" or ±2mm) tells you the tolerance under ideal conditions. Real-world accuracy in bright ambient light, on uneven target surfaces, or at max range is usually worse. Professional-grade tools (Leica, Bosch, DeWalt) maintain accuracy at 85–90% of range; budget tools often degrade significantly past 50% of stated range.

### Target Surface Performance
Laser measurers reflect off the target surface. Dark, matte surfaces (black walls, raw concrete) absorb more laser than bright or retroreflective surfaces. Test any new tool on your most common surface before relying on it for measurements that matter.

### Outdoor Performance
Visible red laser is difficult to see outdoors in sunlight beyond 30–50 feet. Better outdoor tools use:
- **Green laser:** 5× more visible than red in bright conditions
- **Viewfinder/camera:** Built-in camera lets you see the laser dot on-screen outdoors
- **Higher power class:** More powerful lasers cut through ambient light better

### Calculation Features
All laser measurers in this comparison include:
- Distance, area, volume calculations
- Pythagorean theorem (indirect measurement for heights)
- Addition/subtraction memory
- USB charging (most modern units)
