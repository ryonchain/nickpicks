## Hair Dryer Terms Explained

**Ionic:** Emits negative ions that neutralize positive charges in wet hair — reducing static and sealing the cuticle for smoother results.

**Ceramic:** Even heat distribution. Gentler on hair than metal elements.

**Tourmaline:** A gemstone that when powdered emits more ions than standard ceramic. Useful for frizzy and coarse hair.

**Nano titanium:** Titanium creates far-infrared heat that penetrates the hair shaft and dries from inside out — faster and less damaging than surface heat.

**BLDC motor:** Brushless DC motor. Spins faster, lasts longer, and runs quieter than conventional AC motors. Found in Dyson and high-end tools only.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Dyson Supersonic worth $430?**
If you blow-dry 5+ times per week and have color-treated or damaged hair, yes. The heat protection and results are genuinely different. If you blow-dry once a week with healthy hair, the Shark or BaByliss will suffice.

**How long do professional hair dryers last?**
BaByliss Pro: 3–5 years with regular use. Dyson Supersonic: rated for 110-million-hour motor life — effectively the motor never dies. The heating element may need service eventually.

**Can professional dryers be used with a travel adapter?**
Only dual-voltage models can. Check the voltage rating on the label. The Dyson Supersonic R is explicitly dual-voltage; others are US-only (120V).
