## Gaming Mouse Buying Guide

### Which Grip Style Do You Use?

**Palm grip**: Whole hand rests on the mouse. Best for ergonomic right-handed designs like the DeathAdder V3. Avoid flat/symmetrical mice.

**Claw grip**: Fingertips and palm base touch the mouse; fingers arch. Works with most designs. Model O 2 and Superlight 2 are ideal.

**Fingertip grip**: Only fingertips contact the mouse. Requires the lightest mice possible. Superlight 2 and Model O 2 are the top picks.

### DPI: What Number Actually Matters?

Most competitive players use 400–800 DPI with high in-game sensitivity — this gives maximum precision for small flick shots. "Higher DPI = more accuracy" is a myth; above 3,200 DPI, optical sensors introduce jitter that's perceptible in FPS games.

For general use and productivity, 1,600–3,200 DPI is comfortable.

### Wireless vs. Wired in 2026

Modern wireless gaming mice (LIGHTSPEED, HyperSpeed) have sub-1ms polling rates — indistinguishable from wired in double-blind tests. The battery concern is real for budget options, but top-tier wireless mice now run 70–250 hours per charge. Wireless is no longer a competitive disadvantage.

### Polling Rate: 125Hz, 500Hz, or 1,000Hz?

1,000Hz polling (1ms report rate) is standard on all modern gaming mice and invisible above 30fps gameplay. Some premium mice now offer 4,000Hz+ "high polling" modes — the benefit is debated and requires USB 3.0 ports to function.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the best gaming mouse for FPS games?**
The Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 is used by most pro CS2/Valorant/Apex players for a reason — 60g weight, zero latency LIGHTSPEED wireless, and the most accurate sensor available. For budget FPS, the SteelSeries Aerox 3 at $59 performs at ~90% of the capability.

**Are heavy gaming mice bad?**
Heavier mice aren't inherently worse — it depends on grip and play style. Palm grip players often prefer slightly heavier mice (80–100g) for stability. Claw/fingertip grip players who make fast flicking movements benefit most from lighter mice (50–70g).

**How long do gaming mice last?**
Premium gaming mice (Logitech, Razer, SteelSeries) are rated for 20–80 million clicks. At 8 hours of gaming daily, a 20M-click mouse lasts ~7 years. Budget mice typically last 5+ years with normal use.

**Do I need a gaming mouse for productivity work?**
No — standard office mice from Logitech (MX Master 3S) or Microsoft are better for productivity than gaming mice due to ergonomic design and thumb wheel controls. Gaming mice are optimized for speed and precision, not scroll workflow.

**What is the best gaming mouse overall?**
The Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 is the best overall gaming mouse — at 60 grams, it's among the lightest wireless mice available, and its HERO 25K sensor is the most accurate optical sensor in the consumer market.

**How heavy should a gaming mouse be?**
Most competitive FPS players prefer mice under 70 grams for faster, more precise flicks. The Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 at 60g and the Glorious Model O 2 at 59g are the lightest high-performance options. Casual and MMO gamers generally prefer 80–100g mice for additional stability.

**What DPI setting should I use for gaming?**
Most competitive players use 400–1600 DPI combined with higher in-game sensitivity. Higher DPI is not inherently better — the optimal setting depends on your mouse pad size, resolution, and game type. The key is finding a consistent setting and building muscle memory.
