## Oral-B vs Sonicare: Which Technology Is Better?

**Oral-B (oscillating-pulsating):**
- Brush head rotates and pulses in a small circle
- Better for removing plaque at the gumline on individual teeth
- 48,000+ movements per minute at the premium tier
- Dentist-familiar (looks like a dental scaler tool)

**Philips Sonicare (sonic):**
- Brush head vibrates side-to-side at high frequency
- Creates fluid dynamics that clean between teeth without direct contact
- 62,000+ strokes per minute at the premium tier
- Gentler sensation; better for sensitive gum tissue

**Bottom line:** Both technologies remove plaque effectively. Choose Oral-B if you have crowded teeth or specific dentist-directed gumline care. Choose Sonicare if you have sensitivity or prefer a gentler vibration.

## How to Get the Most from Your Electric Toothbrush

1. **Replace brush heads every 3 months** — or when bristles look frayed, whichever comes first
2. **2 minutes twice daily** — every electric toothbrush includes a 2-minute timer; use it
3. **Let the brush do the work** — don't scrub; guide the brush head slowly tooth by tooth
4. **Brush before flossing** — removes food and plaque so floss can reach further between teeth

## FAQs

**Are electric toothbrushes really better than manual?**
Yes, clinical evidence is clear: oscillating-pulsating electric brushes remove 21% more plaque and cause 11% less gingival bleeding in 3-month studies (Journal of Clinical Dentistry, 2005, replicated multiple times since).

**How often should I replace my electric toothbrush head?**
Every 3 months, or when you see the indicator bristles fade from blue to white (Oral-B and Sonicare both include these).

**Is an expensive electric toothbrush worth it?**
The Oral-B Pro 1000 at $49 removes as much plaque as the io Series 9 at $249 in head-to-head clinical testing. You're paying for feedback features (app, display, AI coaching), not meaningfully better cleaning.

**Can kids use adult electric toothbrushes?**
Use age-appropriate brush heads only. Both Oral-B and Sonicare make children's replacement heads for their standard brush bodies. The brush body itself is fine for children 3 and older with supervision.
