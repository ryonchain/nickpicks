## Eco Water Bottle Buying Guide

### Why Reusable Beats Recycled Plastic

Even "recyclable" single-use plastic water bottles are rarely actually recycled — only 9% of plastic ever produced has been recycled globally. Choosing a durable reusable bottle is far more effective than hoping the recycling system handles disposal.

### Stainless Steel vs. Recycled Plastic

**Stainless Steel:** Higher upfront cost, heavier, but lasts decades with no degradation. Mining and processing stainless has an environmental cost, but the long lifespan more than offsets it. Best for daily use with hot or cold beverages.

**Recycled Plastic (Tritan):** Lighter, more affordable, transparent (helpful for tracking intake). Nalgene Sustain's 50% recycled content is the most eco-responsible plastic option. Not suited for hot beverages.

### Brand Ethics Matter

The best eco-friendly bottles come from brands that walk the talk:
- **1% for the Planet members:** Hydro Flask
- **B Corporation certified:** Klean Kanteen
- **90% recycled stainless steel:** Stanley
- **50% recycled plastic content:** Nalgene Sustain

### How Long Should a Reusable Bottle Last?

Quality stainless bottles should last 10+ years (or a lifetime with a warranty). Plastic bottles typically last 3–7 years before scratching, clouding, or retaining odors. Calculate the per-use environmental cost over the bottle's lifespan — stainless wins decisively.

### Insulation: When You Need It and When You Don't

Office use with cold water access → Nalgene Sustain or CamelBak Eddy+ (no insulation needed).

Gym, hiking, outdoor activities, or hot beverages → Hydro Flask, Stanley, or Klean Kanteen Insulated (temperature retention is worth the cost).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many plastic bottles does one reusable bottle replace?

At one use per day for a year, a reusable bottle replaces approximately 365 single-use bottles. Over a 10-year lifespan, that's 3,650 bottles diverted from landfill per household member.

### Are stainless steel water bottles truly eco-friendly?

Yes, when you account for the full lifecycle. While stainless steel production is energy-intensive, a bottle used for 10+ years dramatically outperforms disposable alternatives in total environmental impact. The longer it lasts, the better the math.

### What is BPA and why should I avoid it?

BPA (Bisphenol A) is an industrial chemical used in some plastics that can leach into food and beverages. It's been linked to hormonal disruption in studies. BPA-free Tritan plastic (used in Nalgene and CamelBak) and stainless steel both avoid this issue.

### Can you put boiling water in a reusable water bottle?

Insulated stainless bottles handle boiling water safely — no off-gassing or structural issues. Plastic bottles (even BPA-free) should not be used with boiling water, as high heat can accelerate plastic degradation.

### Which reusable water bottle is easiest to clean?

Wide-mouth bottles like the Nalgene are easiest — your hand fits inside for thorough cleaning. Straw-lid bottles like the CamelBak Eddy+ require a straw cleaning brush for the bite valve. Narrow-mouth bottles like the Hydro Flask Standard need a long bottle brush.
