The iPad Air 13" is the sweet spot between the entry iPad and the premium iPad Pro. Apple's M2 chip handles everything a student needs — PDF annotation, Procreate, video editing in iMovie, and multitasking across apps — at a price that stops short of the Pro's premium.

**What we like:**
- 13" display is the ideal size for split-screen multitasking and textbook reading
- M2 chip runs professional apps (Notability, GoodNotes 6, LumaFusion) without lag
- Apple Pencil Pro support — magnetic attachment, squeeze gesture, barrel roll
- Optional Magic Keyboard with trackpad transforms it into a laptop replacement

**What to watch:** Apple Pencil Pro and Magic Keyboard are sold separately — budget an extra $150–$350 for the full experience. 128GB base storage fills quickly with downloaded videos and apps.

## Buying Guide: Which Student Tablet Is Right for You?

### By Use Case

| Use Case | Best Tablet |
|---|---|
| Handwritten notes (math/science) | iPad Air + Apple Pencil Pro |
| PDF annotation and textbook reading | iPad Air or Samsung Galaxy Tab S10+ |
| Art, design, illustration | iPad Pro 11" |
| Campus-specific Windows software | Surface Go 4 |
| Android budget pick | Samsung Galaxy Tab S10 FE |

### Do You Need a Stylus?

For STEM, language learning, or visual note-taking — yes, a stylus changes everything. The best options:
- **Apple Pencil Pro** — best latency, best feel, but expensive and only works with iPads
- **Samsung S Pen** — included with all Galaxy Tab S10 models; excellent and pressure-sensitive
- **Surface Pen** — solid for annotation; good pressure sensitivity on Windows

### iPad vs. Android: The Honest Comparison

**iPadOS wins:** App quality (Notability, GoodNotes 6, Procreate), longer software support (6+ years), Apple ecosystem integration, and smoother multitasking.

**Android wins:** S Pen included in the box, MicroSD expansion, more customization, deeper Google integration, and price flexibility.

**Q: Can a tablet replace a laptop for college?**
Partially. An iPad with Magic Keyboard handles most writing and note-taking, but a laptop is still better for coding and complex software. Most students use a tablet as a companion device.

**Q: Is an iPad worth it for students?**
Yes, especially for handwritten notes and PDF annotation. GoodNotes 6 and Notability are significantly better on iPad than Android alternatives.

**Q: Which Samsung tablet is best for students?**
The Galaxy Tab S10 FE is the best value — includes the S Pen, large display, and 13-hour battery at $449.

**Q: What size tablet is best for students?**
11–13 inches. A 10-inch tablet is too small for split-screen note-taking; over 13 inches gets heavy to carry.
