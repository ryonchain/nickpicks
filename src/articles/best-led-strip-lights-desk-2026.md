## What to Know Before You Buy

**RGB vs. RGBIC:** RGB strips show a single color across the whole strip. RGBIC strips (Independent Control) display multiple colors simultaneously — gradients, wave effects, and scene patterns. If you want dynamic visual effects, get RGBIC.

**Smart vs. non-smart:** Smart strips connect via Wi-Fi or Bluetooth to an app and work with voice assistants. Non-smart strips use a simple remote and are significantly cheaper. Smart strips are worth it if you want scheduled on/off, scene syncing, or music reactivity.

**Wattage and brightness:** For bias lighting (behind a monitor), you want a dimmer strip (around 3W/m or less). For room accent lighting, brighter is better. Most desk strips are in the 7–14W per 5m range — bright enough for ambiance without washing out the room.

**Adhesive quality:** The included 3M adhesive tape is what determines whether strips stay stuck over time. Premium brands use industrial-grade adhesive; cheap strips peel within months. For permanent installs, add clips or aluminum channel mounts.

## How to Mount LED Strips on a Desk

**Behind monitor (bias lighting):** Clean the rear monitor surface with isopropyl alcohol, let dry, then peel and stick the strip along the top, sides, or all edges facing the wall. Keep 2–3 inches from the edge so the glow projects onto the wall cleanly.

**Under desk lip:** Run the strip along the underside front edge of the desktop, with the light facing down toward the floor or forward toward you. This creates accent lighting without visible LED glare.

**Cable management:** LED strip cables are the biggest visual issue in desk setups. Use adhesive cable clips to run the power cable along the desk leg or frame, keeping it out of sight. Pair with your [cable management solution](/articles/best-cable-management-desk-2026/) for a fully clean setup.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between RGB and RGBIC LED strip lights?**
RGB strips display a single color across the entire strip at once. RGBIC strips have independently addressable segments, so they can display multiple colors simultaneously — gradients, chasing effects, and scene patterns. RGBIC is significantly more visually dynamic for desk setups.

**How do I use LED strip lights for bias lighting?**
Mount a strip on the rear edges of your monitor facing the wall. The soft glow reduces the contrast between a bright screen and a dark room, reducing eye strain during long sessions. Use warm white or a color temperature that matches your screen.

**Are LED strip lights safe to leave on all day?**
Yes. LED strips run at low voltages (5V–24V DC), generate minimal heat, and are designed for continuous use. Most quality LED strips have a rated lifespan of 25,000–50,000 hours.

**What length do I need for a desk setup?**
For single monitor bias lighting: 6.5 feet (2m) is usually enough. For full desk perimeter strips: measure your desk perimeter and add 20% for corners. Most strips come in 6.5-foot, 10-foot, and 16.4-foot lengths.

**Which LED strips work with Alexa and Google Home?**
Govee, Kasa (TP-Link), and LIFX all work natively with Alexa and Google Home. Philips Hue works with all three voice assistants plus Apple HomeKit via the Hue Bridge.

**How much do good LED strip lights cost?**
Budget options for desk use start at $10–$20 for basic USB strips. Mid-range smart RGBIC strips run $25–$45. Premium options like Philips Hue and LIFX cost $75–$200 but offer better integration, reliability, and color quality.
