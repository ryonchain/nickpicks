## How to Choose the Right Wireless Charger

### Qi2 vs. MagSafe vs. Qi: What's the Difference?

| Standard | Max Speed | Works With | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| MagSafe | 15W | iPhone 12+ | Apple proprietary; requires MFi certification |
| Qi2 | 15W | iPhone 13+, Qi2 Android | Open standard based on MagSafe magnet profile |
| Qi | 5–10W | Most smartphones | Universal but slower; no magnets |

**For iPhone users:** Qi2 and MagSafe are functionally equivalent for charging speed. Qi2-certified third-party chargers offer the same 15W as official Apple MagSafe at lower prices.

**For Android users:** Choose Qi2 if your phone supports it (Samsung Galaxy S24+ and newer, Pixel 9+ series); otherwise standard Qi at 5–10W.

### Single Pad vs. Multi-Device Station

- **Single pad:** Best for travel, desk use with one device, or if you only need to charge a phone
- **2-in-1:** Ideal for phone + AirPods or phone + watch overnight
- **3-in-1:** Best for Apple ecosystem users (iPhone + Apple Watch + AirPods) on nightstand

### Heat Management

Wireless charging generates more heat than wired charging. Models with CryoBoost (ESR) or active cooling reduce heat buildup, which extends long-term battery health. Heavier cases (especially non-MagSafe) can also increase heat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does wireless charging damage phone batteries?**
Modern wireless charging is safe for batteries. Heat is the main concern — Qi2 and well-designed chargers manage heat well. Avoid cheap, no-name chargers that run hot.

**Do I need a special case for Qi2 charging?**
No — Qi2 works through most cases up to ~4mm thick. MagSafe cases with built-in magnets improve alignment and are recommended for Qi2 pads, but they're not required.

**How much slower is wireless vs. wired charging?**
Qi2 at 15W is roughly the same as wired charging for moderate sessions. Very fast wired charging (30W, 65W, 100W) will still beat wireless for a quick top-up before leaving the house.

**Can I charge through a wallet case or battery case?**
Wallet cases with cards block wireless charging. Battery cases vary — most bypass wireless to charge the battery case, which then charges the phone.

**Is it safe to leave my phone on a wireless charger overnight?**
Yes — modern phones stop drawing power at 100% and manage trickle charging automatically. Qi2 and MagSafe chargers are designed for overnight use.
