## White Noise vs Pink Noise vs Brown Noise

**White noise:** Equal energy across all frequencies. Sounds harsh to some — like TV static. Best at masking speech frequencies.

**Pink noise:** Emphasizes lower frequencies. More natural-sounding — like steady rain or rustling leaves. Research suggests it may improve deep sleep quality.

**Brown noise:** Even heavier bass than pink. Sounds like a strong waterfall or distant thunder. Popular with people who have tinnitus.

Most people prefer pink or brown noise for sleep. White noise is best for pure sound masking (blocking out noise).
