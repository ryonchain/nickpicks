## Pet Camera Buying Guide

### Key Features to Evaluate

**Camera Resolution and Night Vision**
1080p is the minimum for clear enough video to identify what your pet is doing. Night vision is essential — most pets are most active at night, and rooms with blinds/curtains can be very dark during the day. Look for "color night vision" or at least "infrared night vision with clear detail."

**Treat Dispenser Range and Compatibility**
Treat toss range matters — 2–6 feet launches the treat far enough to be interactive (your pet chases it). Dispensers that drop into a bowl nearby are less engaging. Also check treat compatibility: most work with standard small dry treats (like Zuke's Mini Naturals or similar), but some have size restrictions.

**Two-Way Audio Quality**
Your voice should be recognizable and clear — a tinny speaker that barely sounds like you won't reassure an anxious pet. Test reviews that specifically mention audio quality before buying.

**Field of View**
- 130°: Small rooms only, won't see the whole space
- 160°: Most living rooms and bedrooms
- 360° pan/tilt: Follows your pet anywhere

**Cloud Storage and Subscription Costs**
Most cameras offer free cloud clips (10–30 seconds) and sell subscriptions for longer recording, AI alerts, and 24/7 recording. Factor subscription costs into your total:
- Furbo Dog Nanny: $6.99/month
- Petcube Care: $4/month
- Wyze Cam Plus: $1.99/month per camera

### Dog vs. Cat: Does It Matter?

Most pet cameras work for both, but note:
- **Dogs:** Treat toss range and volume matter more. Look for higher-capacity treat hopper.
- **Cats:** Laser pointer is a unique differentiator. Cats are less motivated by treats than play.
- **Multiple pets:** 360° cameras or wide-angle fixed cameras with motion alerts work better.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How far can a pet camera treat dispenser toss treats?**
Most toss treats 2–6 feet in an arc. You control the angle from the app. Treats must be small (roughly the size of a pea) to load and launch properly — most recommend treats under 0.4 inches in diameter.

**Do pet cameras work without Wi-Fi?**
No — all require a Wi-Fi connection to stream video and receive commands. If your home Wi-Fi goes down, you lose access until it's restored. Pets are safe — the camera just becomes non-functional.

**Can I leave the treat dispenser full all day?**
Yes, but be mindful of overfeeding. Most pet cameras let you set treat limits in the app. A standard 100-treat hopper can last several days of normal use. Also ensure treats won't expire or go stale in the hopper.

**Is my pet camera footage private?**
This depends on the brand. Reputable brands like Furbo and Petcube store footage encrypted in the cloud and don't share footage. Check each brand's privacy policy. You can also opt for cameras with local storage (microSD cards) for no cloud exposure.

**What's the best treat to use in a Furbo?**
Furbo specifically recommends small training treats under 0.5 inches. Popular choices: Zuke's Mini Naturals, Old Mother Hubbard Mini Biscuits, and Hill's Science Diet Small Treats. Avoid large biscuits, wet treats, or anything sticky that could jam the mechanism.
