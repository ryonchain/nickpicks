## Ultrasonic vs. Nebulizing vs. Heat Diffusers

### Ultrasonic (Recommended for Home Office)
Uses ultrasonic vibrations to turn water + essential oil into a cool mist. No heat means the oil's therapeutic compounds aren't degraded. Doubles as a humidifier. Most home office diffusers use this technology.

### Nebulizing
Uses pressurized air to atomize pure oil — no water. Delivers the strongest scent intensity and preserves all oil compounds. More expensive to run (uses oil faster). Better for larger spaces or serious aromatherapy practitioners.

### Heat/Candle Warmers
Heats oil to release scent. Simple and cheap, but heat degrades some therapeutic compounds. Not recommended for serious aromatherapy use.

**For a home office:** Ultrasonic diffusers are the right choice — quiet, affordable, long run times, and effective.

## Diffuser Maintenance (Keep It Working)

Clean your diffuser weekly to prevent oil buildup and bacterial growth:
1. Empty remaining water after each use
2. Weekly: fill halfway with water, add 10 drops of white vinegar, run for 5 minutes, rinse
3. Monthly: wipe interior chamber with a cotton ball soaked in isopropyl alcohol

Neglected diffusers develop biofilm and release contaminated mist. 5 minutes weekly keeps it clean.
