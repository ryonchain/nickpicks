## Chaga: What the Evidence Shows

**Antioxidant activity:**
- ORAC value of chaga is estimated at 146,700 per 100g — among the highest of any natural substance
- Melanin pigments (the black exterior of chaga) are potent free radical scavengers
- Polyphenol content is exceptionally high compared to other medicinal mushrooms

**Immune support:**
- Beta-glucans in chaga (both beta-1,3 and beta-1,6 glucans) stimulate macrophage and NK cell activity
- Animal studies show chaga extract reduces inflammatory markers including TNF-α and IL-6

**Blood sugar:**
- Mouse studies show chaga polysaccharides improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood glucose — human trials are limited but mechanisms are plausible

**Betulinic acid:**
- Derived from birch bark where chaga grows; shows cytotoxic activity against cancer cell lines in vitro, but human clinical evidence is limited

**Practical note:** Chaga pairs well with vitamin C (found in rose hips and camu camu) — some evidence suggests vitamin C enhances chaga's antioxidant activity in the gut.
