## Why Labor Day Is the Best Day to Buy a Grill

Grilling season in the US runs Memorial Day through Labor Day. After September 1, outdoor cooking slows and retailers have no ability to move unsold grill inventory until the following spring — which is 7+ months away. The alternative to discounting deeply is storing, which costs money.

## Grill Buying Guide: What to Consider Before Labor Day

**Gas vs. Pellet vs. Charcoal?**

| Type | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|------|------|------|---------|
| Gas | Instant heat, easy control | Less flavor than charcoal | Weeknight grilling, convenience |
| Pellet | Set-and-forget smoking | Slower heat-up, cost of pellets | Low-and-slow BBQ, ribs, brisket |
| Charcoal | Best flavor, cheaper to buy | Longer setup, ash cleanup | Steak, burgers, anyone who loves the ritual |
| Flat Top | Versatile, commercial-style | Not traditional grilling | Breakfast, hibachi, smash burgers |

**Cooking area needed?**
- 1–2 people: 200–300 sq in
- 3–4 people: 300–450 sq in
- 5–8 people: 450–600 sq in
- Regular entertaining: 600+ sq in

**BTU doesn't matter much past 30,000.** Marketing materials emphasize BTU, but heat retention (from grate material and lid design) matters more than raw BTU count.
