## Generator Buying Guide

**How many watts do you actually need?**

| Appliance | Running Watts | Surge Watts |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Refrigerator | 150–400W | 800–1,200W |
| Window AC (5,000 BTU) | 500–900W | 1,200–2,000W |
| Sump pump | 800–1,050W | 1,300–2,150W |
| Furnace fan | 300–600W | 800–2,000W |
| Lights (LED, 10 bulbs) | 50–100W | — |

Add your running watts for simultaneous loads, then add the largest single surge draw. Buy at least 20% headroom above your total.

**Inverter vs. conventional:** Inverter generators produce clean sine wave power (safe for electronics), run quieter, and use fuel more efficiently. Conventional generators produce dirtier power and are louder but cost less per watt of output. For home use with medical equipment or electronics, inverter is worth the premium.

**Safety non-negotiables:** Never run a generator indoors, in a garage, or within 20 feet of any door, window, or vent. Carbon monoxide kills silently. CO detectors inside the home are mandatory when running a generator outdoors.
