## Knife Sets vs. Individual Knives

**The case for buying individual knives:**

Most knife sets fill out the count with knives you'll rarely use (boning knife, fillet knife, 4 steak knives, a cleaver) while compromising on the quality of the chef's knife — the one you use 80% of the time.

**Build your own mini set instead:**

For most cooks, the ideal collection is:
1. **8" Chef's knife** — 80% of your cutting
2. **Serrated bread knife** — for bread, tomatoes, delicate items

You can buy all three quality knives for $150–200 and out-cut a $400 knife block set.

## German vs. Japanese Steel: The Trade-offs

| Attribute | German (Wüsthof, Henckels) | Japanese (Shun, Global, MAC) |
|-----------|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| Edge angle | 14–16° | 15–16° per side |
| Hardness | 56–58 HRC | 60–66 HRC |
| Edge retention | Good | Excellent |
| Durability | More forgiving of misuse | Chips if used on hard materials |
| Sharpening | Honing rod + pull-through | Whetstone preferred |
| Weight | Heavier | Lighter |
| Best cut style | Rocking | Push cut / slice |

## How to Maintain Your Knife (The Only Advice That Matters)

1. **Hand wash and dry immediately** — dishwasher heat and detergent destroy edges.
2. **Use a honing rod before each session** — realigns the edge without removing steel.
3. **Cut on wood or plastic only** — glass, stone, and ceramic destroy edges instantly.
4. **Sharpen 1–2x per year** — whetstone (400/1000/6000 progression) or professional service.
5. **Never store loose in a drawer** — edge-to-edge contact dulls knives fast. Use a magnetic strip or block.
