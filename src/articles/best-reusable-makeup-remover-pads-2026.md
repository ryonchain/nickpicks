## Why Switch to Reusable Makeup Remover Pads

The average person goes through 200–400 disposable cotton rounds per year. Multiply that across a lifetime of skincare routines and the waste — and the cost — adds up fast. A quality set of reusable pads costs $15–$30 upfront and replaces that disposable habit entirely. Most sets include a laundry bag and are machine-washable, so maintenance is minimal.

Beyond sustainability, reusable pads generally perform better than single-use cotton rounds. Disposable rounds tend to shred under pressure and leave fibers on the skin. Reusable options — made from bamboo, microfiber, or specialty fabric blends — hold their structure, grip makeup more effectively, and feel softer against the skin.

For cleansing cloths, the magic is in the microfiber construction: the tiny fibers mechanically lift and trap makeup without needing a separate makeup remover product. Some people still prefer to use micellar water or a cleansing oil with their pads; others find water alone is sufficient with the right cloth.

**What to evaluate:**
- **Material:** Bamboo, microfiber, and organic cotton each have different textures and cleansing properties
- **Dual-sided design:** One smooth side for gentle application; one textured side for cleansing
- **Washability:** Check how many wash cycles the manufacturer claims; most reputable brands say 1,000+
- **Laundry bag included:** Essential for keeping small pads from going missing in the wash
- **Size:** Larger pads cover more area; smaller ones offer more precision

## FAQ

**How do I wash reusable pads?**
Place in the included mesh laundry bag and wash on a warm or hot cycle with your regular detergent. Avoid fabric softener — it coats microfiber and bamboo fibers and reduces their cleansing ability. Air dry or tumble dry on low.

**Can reusable pads replace cotton balls entirely?**
For makeup removal and product application, yes. For nail polish removal (which requires saturation with acetone), standard cotton balls are still more practical.

**How many pads do I need?**
Seven pads is a comfortable weekly supply for most people — one per day if you're replacing disposable rounds. If you use two per day (morning and evening routines), step up to a 14–20 pad set.

**Do I need makeup remover product with these pads?**
It depends on the product. The Makeup Eraser works with water alone. LastRound and Bambaw work best paired with micellar water or cleansing oil for heavier makeup. Burt's Bees definitely needs a product.

**How do I remove stains from reusable pads?**
Pre-treat stubborn mascara or lipstick stains with a small amount of micellar water or dish soap before washing. Most stains lift after a hot wash cycle.
