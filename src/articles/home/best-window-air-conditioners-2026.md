## BTU Guide: How Much Cooling Do You Need?

BTU (British Thermal Units) is the measure of cooling capacity. Under-sizing means the unit runs constantly and can't cool the room in peak heat. Over-sizing cools too fast without removing humidity, leaving the room cold and clammy.

| Room Size | Recommended BTU |
|-----------|----------------|
| Up to 150 sq ft | 5,000 BTU |
| 150–250 sq ft | 6,000 BTU |
| 250–350 sq ft | 8,000 BTU |
| 350–450 sq ft | 10,000 BTU |
| 450–550 sq ft | 12,000 BTU |
| 550–700 sq ft | 14,000 BTU |
| 700–1,000 sq ft | 18,000 BTU |
| 1,000–1,500 sq ft | 24,000 BTU |

**Adjustments:** Add 10% BTU if the room gets direct afternoon sun. Add 600 BTU per additional regular occupant beyond 2 people. Subtract 10% if the space is heavily shaded.

## Buyer's Guide

### Energy Costs: EER vs. CEER

**EER** (Energy Efficiency Ratio) = BTU/hour ÷ watts at full load. Higher is better.

**CEER** (Combined Energy Efficiency Ratio) includes standby power use. Energy Star requires a minimum CEER of 12.1 for 8,000 BTU units.

Running an 8,000 BTU window AC 8 hours/day at $0.15/kWh costs approximately $30–45/month depending on EER. The Midea U-Shaped at EER 15.0 vs. a conventional 10.0 EER unit saves ~30% on monthly bills.

### Smart Features Worth Paying For

- **Scheduling:** Set cooling to start before you arrive home — avoids cooling an empty space all day
- **Sleep mode:** Gradually raises temperature 2°F over 7 hours as your body temperature drops
- **Voice control:** Convenience when hands are full
- **Energy monitoring:** See actual usage and spot inefficiencies

### Installation Tips

- Measure window width before buying — AC units require a window opening within their size range
- All window ACs need 6" clearance on sides for airflow
- 120V units (most up to 14,000 BTU) plug into standard household outlets
- 240V units (typically 18,000+ BTU) require a dedicated circuit — consult an electrician if not already in place
