## PSI and GPM: What You Actually Need

**PSI (pounds per square inch)** = cleaning pressure. Higher PSI strips dirt faster but can damage soft surfaces.

**GPM (gallons per minute)** = water volume. Higher GPM rinses more efficiently.

**Cleaning units = PSI × GPM.** Both matter.

**For home use:**
- Cars/delicate surfaces: 1,200–1,500 PSI
- Wood decks/fences: 1,500–2,000 PSI
- Concrete driveways: 2,000–3,000 PSI
- Stripping paint or heavy commercial work: 3,000+ PSI (gas territory)

Most homeowners need 1,500–2,300 PSI. Gas machines at 3,400 PSI will strip deck wood grain and damage vehicle paint if used carelessly.

## Buyer's Guide: Surface-by-Surface Recommendations

| Surface | Recommended PSI | Nozzle | Notes |
|---------|----------------|--------|-------|
| Cars/boats | 1,200–1,500 PSI | 40° or soap | Keep nozzle 12+ inches away |
| Wood decks | 1,500–2,000 PSI | 25–40° | Always go with wood grain |
| Vinyl siding | 1,500–2,000 PSI | 25–40° | Top-to-bottom angle |
| Concrete driveways | 2,000–3,000 PSI | 15–25° | Sweep at consistent distance |
| Stripping paint | 2,500–3,500 PSI | 15° or 0° | Extreme caution on surfaces |

### Do You Need a Surface Cleaner Attachment?

For driveways and large flat concrete, a rotating surface cleaner attachment ($30–80) connects to any pressure washer and covers a 10–15 inch wide swath per pass. Eliminates striping, reduces time by 50%, and delivers more uniform results. It's one of the best accessory investments for anyone with a concrete driveway.
