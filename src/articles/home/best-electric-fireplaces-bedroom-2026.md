## Bedroom Placement Tips

**Against a wall, opposite the bed:** The most impactful placement — you see the flames from the bed. Wall-mounted units work best here.

**Corner placement:** Freestanding fireplaces in bedroom corners take minimal floor space and create warm ambient light in a frequently dark area.

**Above a dresser:** A smaller insert or floating wall-mounted unit positioned above a dresser creates a bedroom "focal wall" similar to a living room mantle.

**Clearances:** Keep 3 feet clear of bedding, curtains, and furniture on the front and sides. Never block the air intake (usually on the back or sides).

## What Makes a Bedroom Fireplace Different from a Living Room Model

**Smaller scale:** Bedroom fireplaces work best at 20–36 inches wide. 50-inch living room models dominate a bedroom visually.

**Quieter operation:** Bedroom use means overnight heat occasionally. Choose models with fan-only and flame-only modes so you control noise level.

**Auto shut-off timer:** Essential for bedroom safety. All recommended models include automatic timers.

**Remote control:** Being able to adjust settings from bed without getting up is a genuine quality-of-life feature for bedroom fireplaces.
