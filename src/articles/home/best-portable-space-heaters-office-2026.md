## Energy-Saving Home Office Heating Strategy

**Zone heat, don't whole-house heat.** Drop your main thermostat to 65–66°F, use a space heater at your desk to maintain 70–72°F while working. Net result: you're comfortable; the rest of the house (which you're not in for 8 hours) is cooler; total energy cost is lower.

**Use the lowest setting that works.** Most heaters have 750W and 1500W modes. 750W is appropriate for an already-warm room that just needs maintenance heating. 1500W is for heating a cold room quickly. Drop to 750W once you reach comfort temperature.

**Pre-heat before you start.** If you start your workday in a cold office, run the heater on high for 15 minutes before sitting down, then drop to low once the room reaches temperature. Efficient and comfortable.

**Programmable timers pay off.** Set the heater to start 20 minutes before your workday begins. The office is warm when you arrive; you don't waste the first 20 minutes waiting for it to heat up.
