## How to Choose a Solar Generator

### Capacity (Wh)
- **Weekend camper:** 500–1,000Wh
- **Week-long trip:** 1,000–2,000Wh
- **Van life/off-grid home:** 2,000Wh+

### Solar Input Speed
Higher wattage input = faster charging from solar panels. Look for at least 200W input for practical daylight recharge.

### Output Power (Watts)
Match to your highest-wattage device. A coffee maker needs 1,000W minimum; power tools often need 1,800W+.

### Weight vs. Capacity Trade-off
Larger batteries weigh more. If you're car camping, weight doesn't matter. If you're backpacking, prioritize Wh-per-pound.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a solar generator run a mini fridge?**
Yes — most 500Wh+ generators can run a 12V or compact fridge. A full-size fridge requires 1,800Wh+ capacity.

**Do I need to buy solar panels separately?**
Most generators are sold without panels. Look for kits that bundle the generator and compatible panels for savings.

**How long does it take to charge via solar?**
Depends on panel wattage and sunlight hours. A 500Wh generator with a 200W panel fully charges in about 3–4 sunny hours.
