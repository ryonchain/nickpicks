# Best Window Air Conditioners in 2026

Window AC units are the most cost-effective way to cool individual rooms without central air. The critical spec is BTU (British Thermal Units) — matching BTU to room size prevents both undercooling (underpowered unit runs constantly) and overcooling (oversized unit short-cycles, leaving rooms humid and uncomfortable). EER (Energy Efficiency Ratio) is the key efficiency metric — higher EER = lower electricity bills.

---

## BTU Size Guide

| Room Size | Recommended BTU |
|-----------|----------------|
| Up to 150 sq ft | 5,000–6,000 BTU |
| 150–250 sq ft | 6,000–8,000 BTU |
| 250–350 sq ft | 8,000–10,000 BTU |
| 350–550 sq ft | 10,000–12,000 BTU |
| 550–1,000 sq ft | 14,000–18,000 BTU |

**Adjustments:** Add 10% for sunny rooms, add 600 BTU per person if more than 2 people regularly occupy the room, add 4,000 BTU if unit is in a kitchen.

## Inverter vs. Fixed Speed

**Fixed speed (standard):** Compressor cycles on/off at full power. Cheaper, louder, less efficient.

**Inverter compressor:** Modulates speed continuously to match load. 20–40% more efficient, significantly quieter, and better humidity control. Worth the $80–$150 premium if you use the AC daily.
