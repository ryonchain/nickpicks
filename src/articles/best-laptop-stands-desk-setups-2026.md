## Why You Need a Laptop Stand

Working on a laptop screen at desk height forces your neck to flex 15–30 degrees downward. Over 8 hours, this causes:
- Cervical spine compression (adds 10+ lbs of force per degree of flex)
- Upper trapezius muscle strain ("tech neck")
- Shoulder impingement from typing with raised arms

A laptop stand elevates your screen to eye level. Combined with an external keyboard and mouse, you get a near-perfect ergonomic setup for ~$70–$100 total.

## What to Look For

**Height range:** Your eyes should look straight ahead at the top third of your laptop screen. Measure from your desk to your eye level when seated — choose a stand that hits that height.

**Stability:** A wobbly stand vibrates during typing. Test by pressing the laptop at the corners — good stands don't rock.

**Heat dissipation:** Aluminum stands conduct heat away from the laptop bottom. Plastic stands insulate heat. If your laptop runs hot, choose aluminum.

**Portability:** Fixed stands are most stable; folding stands sacrifice some rigidity for portability.
