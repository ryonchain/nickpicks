## What to Look for in an Under-Desk Treadmill

Under-desk treadmills are purpose-built for walking, not running. They operate at 0.5–4 mph, require no handrail for balance at walking pace, and need to be quiet enough not to disturb calls. Key specs:

**Motor:** 2.0–3.0 HP continuous-duty motors handle 8+ hours of daily use. Lower-powered motors (under 1.5 HP) overheat during extended use. Look for continuous HP ratings, not peak HP.

**Noise level:** Most quality under-desk treadmills operate at 50–65 dB — comparable to normal conversation. Check decibel ratings and user reviews for real-world noise levels. This matters enormously for video calls.

**Speed range:** Most top out at 3.5–4 mph for walking sessions. Ensure the minimum speed (0.5 mph) is slow enough for focused work.

**Deck size:** Minimum 16 × 45 inches for comfortable walking. Wider and longer decks are more natural and reduce fatigue during long sessions.

**Portability:** Folding models and built-in wheels make storage easier. Measure your desk clearance before buying — most under-desk treadmills need 6–10 inches of vertical clearance.

**App and data tracking:** Bluetooth connectivity to fitness apps (Zwift, Apple Health, Garmin Connect) is useful for long-term tracking. Some models include a control console; others rely entirely on the companion app or remote control.

## Setting Up for Under-Desk Walking Success

**Desk height matters.** Walking slightly raises your body — your desk may need to be 1–2 inches higher than your seated typing height. A standing desk that adjusts to walking height is ideal. Standard desks are typically too low for comfortable walking posture.

**Start slow.** Your first week, aim for 20–30 minute walking sessions at 1–1.5 mph during low-focus tasks (email, Slack, meetings). Gradually increase to 2 mph during deeper work as your body adapts.

**Typing on a walking pad.** Walking speeds above 2 mph make precise typing difficult for most people. Use walking sessions for reading, video meetings, and light correspondence. Reserve seated time for writing-intensive work.

**Mat underneath:** A rubber anti-fatigue mat or treadmill mat under the unit reduces vibration transfer to floors and protects hardwood or LVP flooring from wear.

**Noise management on calls.** At 1–1.5 mph, most under-desk treadmills are inaudible on a call when your microphone is set to cardioid mode. Test before a client call. Mute when you increase speed.
