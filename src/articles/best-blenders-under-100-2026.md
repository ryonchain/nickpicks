## What Blender Features Actually Matter

**Motor wattage.** 700W handles soft fruit and vegetables well. 900W+ handles ice and frozen fruit without stalling. 1,200W+ crushes ice cubes in seconds and handles nut butters.

**Blade assembly.** Stacked blades (multiple layers — Ninja) pull ingredients down consistently. Single blade assemblies (NutriBullet, Hamilton Beach) are sufficient for personal-size blending.

**Container material.** Glass doesn't scratch or absorb odors but is heavy. Plastic (BPA-free) is lighter and more durable for daily use.

**Auto-iQ / Smart programs.** Automatic blend-and-pulse programs simplify getting the right texture. Worth having if you blend frequently and want consistent results.

## FAQ: Blender Questions

**Does more watts mean better blending?**
Up to a point. For frozen smoothies, 900W+ makes a meaningful difference. Above 1,400W, differences are minimal for home use — Vitamix motors over 2,000W are built for commercial continuous-use durability, not necessarily better blending.

**Can a $100 blender replace a Vitamix?**
For daily smoothies: yes. For commercial volume, ultra-fine nut butters, or heavy daily restaurant use: no. The Vitamix's main advantages are longevity (10-year warranty), noise enclosure, and commercial-grade construction.

**How do I clean a blender?**
Add a drop of dish soap and warm water, blend for 30 seconds on high, rinse. Most blenders clean in under 60 seconds this way.
