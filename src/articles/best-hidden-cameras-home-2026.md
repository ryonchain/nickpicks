## How to Choose a Hidden Camera for Home

### Prioritize Your Use Case: Disguise vs. Discreet

There are two distinct categories in this guide. Object-disguised cameras (KAMRE clock, Fuvision smoke detector) are truly covert — they're indistinguishable from everyday items. Small mainstream cameras (Wyze, Blink Mini, LaView) are discreet but identifiable as cameras on close inspection. If your goal is monitoring a space without any visible camera presence, choose an object-disguised model. If you simply want a compact, low-profile camera that doesn't dominate a room, a small mainstream camera is a better choice — better video quality, smarter features, and app connectivity.

### Local vs. Cloud Storage

Object-disguised cameras almost universally use local MicroSD storage — there's no cloud service, no monthly fee, and no remote viewing unless you retrieve the card. Mainstream cameras (Wyze, Blink) offer both local and cloud options. For hidden camera use cases, local storage is often preferable: no footage leaves your home, no account can be compromised, and there's no subscription cost.

### Night Vision: Color vs. Infrared

Color night vision (available on Wyze Cam v3 and Blink Mini 2) produces footage that's dramatically more useful for identifying people, clothing colors, and details. Infrared night vision (standard on disguised cameras) produces usable but black-and-white footage. If night monitoring is a priority, favor mainstream cameras with color night vision over disguised cameras with infrared-only sensors.

### Legal Boundaries

Regardless of which camera you choose, placement is governed by law. In the US, recording is permitted in areas without a reasonable expectation of privacy — living rooms, entryways, kitchens, hallways. Recording in bathrooms, bedrooms, or changing areas without consent is a criminal offense in every state. Many states (including California, Illinois, and Pennsylvania) require all-party consent for audio recording. If you're using a hidden camera in a rental property or for monitoring employees, consult a local attorney before deployment.
