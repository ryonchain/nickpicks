## How to Choose a Robot Mop

**1. Vacuum-Mop Combo vs. Dedicated Mop**
If you don't already own a robot vacuum, a combo unit is almost always the better value. The Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra and Dreame L20 Ultra handle both tasks well. If you already have a quality vacuum, a dedicated mopper like the Braava Jet m6 makes more sense.

**2. Self-Cleaning Dock**
This is the single feature that separates truly hands-off operation from a machine you still have to babysit. Models with auto-wash and hot-air-dry docks prevent mildew buildup and eliminate the most unpleasant maintenance task. Budget accordingly — these docks add $200–$400 to the price of an otherwise similar machine.

**3. Floor Type and Layout**
Sealed hardwood and tile clean easily with most robot mops. Textured tile grout, rough stone, and luxury vinyl plank benefit from rotating or oscillating pad designs that apply more mechanical pressure. Homes with lots of carpet need a robot that auto-lifts the mop pad reliably — otherwise you get wet carpet, which is worse than dirty carpet.

**4. Navigation Quality**
LiDAR-based navigation (Roborock, Ecovacs, Dreame, Narwal) produces consistent, repeatable cleaning paths and adapts to moved furniture. Bump-and-map navigation (Bissell budget models) is adequate for simple floor plans but misses corners and can get stuck in complex rooms. If your home has lots of obstacles, invest in LiDAR.

## FAQ

**Do robot mops actually clean as well as mopping by hand?**
For regular maintenance cleaning — keeping sealed floors free of dust, light grime, and pet hair — yes. For deep cleaning after heavy spills or ground-in stains, a manual scrub first followed by a robot mop for ongoing maintenance is the realistic workflow.

**Can robot mops handle pet hair?**
The vacuum-mop combos (Roborock, Ecovacs, Dreame, Narwal) handle pet hair well because suction lifts it before the mop pad touches the floor. Pure mopping robots like the Braava should be used after vacuuming, or hair will just get pushed around.

**How often do mop pads need replacing?**
Reusable pads on self-cleaning models last 3–6 months with proper care. Disposable pads on the Braava Jet cost roughly $0.25–$0.50 each and should be swapped every 1–2 runs. Factor pad costs into total ownership cost when comparing models.

**Are robot mops safe on hardwood floors?**
Yes, as long as the hardwood is properly sealed. Robot mops use controlled water dispensing and won't over-soak floors the way a traditional wet mop can. Avoid using them on unsealed or waxed hardwood, which can be damaged by any moisture.
