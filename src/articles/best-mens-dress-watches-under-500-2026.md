## What Defines a Great Dress Watch

### Dress Watch Principles
A dress watch is defined by what it lacks as much as what it has:
- **No more than two complications** (date is acceptable; day/date often too busy)
- **Case size: 36–40mm** (37–38mm is the classic sweet spot for a true dress watch)
- **Slim profile:** Under 10mm case depth for clean suit cuff clearance
- **Integrated or simple strap:** Leather strap that flows from the case without visible lugs

### Movement for Dress Watches
- **Quartz:** Ultra-accurate (±15 sec/month), thin movement, no maintenance. Correct for formal wear where timekeeping precision matters.
- **Swiss automatic:** Heritage credibility, conversation piece, romantic. Runs for decades with regular service. Gains/loses 10–30 seconds/day depending on caliber quality.
- **Japanese automatic (Miyota/Seiko calibers):** Reliable, accurate, affordable. Not Swiss, but excellent real-world performance.

### Case Finishing for Dress Watches
Dress watches use **polished finishing only** or **mixed polish/satin**. The alternating polished/brushed finishing that looks great on sport watches reads as wrong on a dress piece. Look for:
- Polished case sides
- Sharp anglage (edges) between surfaces
- Dial printing or applied indices (raised markers look more expensive)

## Dress Watch Care and Pairing

**Suit pairing:** Dress watches wear inside the cuff, not on the outside. 36–38mm reads traditional; 40mm reads contemporary.

**Metal matching:** Silver watch case → silver belt buckle and cufflinks. Gold tone → gold accessories. Don't mix.

**Leather strap care:** Rotate straps, avoid water exposure, condition quarterly with leather conditioner.

**Winding an automatic:** If not worn for 48+ hours, manually wind 20–30 turns before wearing. Most automatics should not be shaken to wind.
