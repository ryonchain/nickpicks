## What to Avoid

- **Frames that only say "UV protection" without specifying UV400** — UV protection at lower levels (UV380, UV350) still allows damaging rays through. Look specifically for UV400 or "100% UVA/UVB protection."
- **Extremely dark lenses without UV labeling** — lens darkness is not correlated with UV protection. A dark lens without UV400 coating is actually worse than a light lens without it, because pupils dilate behind dark lenses, allowing more UV in.
- **Frames with plastic hinges** — plastic hinges loosen and break faster than spring hinges. Look for spring hinge construction in the product specs.
- **Lenses described as "glass-like" without optical clarity specifics** — some budget lenses have visible distortion at the edges that causes eye strain. If possible, check reviewer feedback for lens quality mentions.
