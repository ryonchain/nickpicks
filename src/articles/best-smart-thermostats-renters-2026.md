## What Renters Need to Know Before Buying

**C-wire check:** Your thermostat's existing wiring determines compatibility. Pull off the cover and count the wires. If you have fewer than 5 wires and no C-wire, buy a model that includes a power adapter kit.

**Get landlord approval:** Even easy installs modify existing wiring. Text or email your landlord for written approval before opening the thermostat cover. Keep the original thermostat to reinstall when you move out.

**Reversibility:** All models on this list can be uninstalled in 10-15 minutes, leaving no damage. Take a photo of the original wiring before disconnecting anything.

**System compatibility:** Most smart thermostats work with 24V central HVAC (gas furnace, central AC). Electric baseboard heaters, radiant heat, and steam radiators need specific models.

## FAQ

**Can I install a smart thermostat in a rental apartment?**
Yes, with landlord permission. Document the original wiring, save the original thermostat, and reinstall it when you leave. No permanent modification required.

**What if my HVAC has only 2 wires?**
Two-wire systems (common in older buildings) are tricky. Mysa handles 240V two-wire baseboard systems. For 24V two-wire, the Sensi and Nest Trim Kit handle many configurations — verify on their compatibility check tools.

**Does a smart thermostat actually save money?**
Studies show 10-15% energy savings on average. Actual savings depend on how disciplined you were with manual scheduling before.
