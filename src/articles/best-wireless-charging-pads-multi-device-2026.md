## Wireless Charging Buying Guide

### MagSafe vs. Qi2 vs. Standard Qi

**MagSafe (Apple):** Apple's proprietary magnetic wireless charging system. 15W charging on iPhone 12+. Magnets align the charging coil precisely for maximum efficiency. Works on iPhone 12 and later. First-party is Apple's charger; third-party requires MFi certification.

**Qi2:** The open standard version of MagSafe, introduced in 2023. Also 15W, also uses magnets. Compatible with iPhone 12+. Supported by Android flagships from 2024+ (Samsung Galaxy S24 series, Google Pixel 9+). A Qi2 charger works identically to MagSafe on iPhones.

**Standard Qi:** The universal wireless charging standard, without magnets. Up to 15W on some Android phones; 7.5W on iPhone; 5W on AirPods. Works on essentially all modern smartphones. No magnetic alignment — placement needs to be manually centered.

**Bottom line:** If you have an iPhone 12 or later, pay for MagSafe or Qi2 speed (15W). For iPhone 11 or older, or for Android phones without Qi2, standard Qi is your maximum.

### Android Wireless Charging Compatibility

- **Samsung Galaxy S-series:** Up to 15W Qi (standard), 15W Qi2 on S24+
- **Google Pixel 9+:** Qi2 compatible at 15W
- **OnePlus, Motorola:** Standard Qi at various speeds; check device specs

For Android users, the LISEN 3-in-1 or Anker Qi2 pad both support up to 10–15W depending on your device.

### Does Wireless Charging Damage Battery?

Wireless charging generates slightly more heat than wired charging, and sustained heat is the enemy of lithium battery lifespan. To minimize heat impact:
- Remove your phone from thick cases while wireless charging
- Don't leave the phone on the charger indefinitely after 100% (modern phones limit this automatically)
- Use a quality charger from a reputable brand — cheap chargers generate more heat

The impact is minor with quality chargers. Your battery will degrade slowly over years regardless — wireless charging doesn't meaningfully accelerate this with modern phones.

### How Far Can the Charging Coil Be from the Device?

Qi wireless charging works through about 4–8mm (0.16–0.3 inches). Thin cases are fine; thick phone wallets are not. Typical thin phone cases (1–3mm) don't affect charging. MagSafe and Qi2 magnetic alignment ensures the coil stays centered even through cases.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I charge my iPhone and Android phone on the same wireless charger?**
Yes, any Qi or Qi2 charger works with both. The 3-in-1 stations here all support Android phones on the main phone charging spot. The Apple Watch spot is Apple-specific (uses Apple's magnetic connector).

**Does wireless charging work through phone cases?**
Yes, through most thin cases. Very thick cases (over 5mm), battery cases, or cases with metal plates block charging. Credit card cases with metal plates in them definitely block charging.

**What wattage do I need for overnight charging?**
Any wattage. Even 5W Qi charges a modern iPhone from 0–100% in about 3.5–4 hours. 7.5W takes about 2.5 hours. 15W MagSafe takes about 90 minutes. For overnight charging, any of these speeds is sufficient.

**Is it safe to leave my phone on a wireless charger all night?**
Yes. Modern iPhones and Android flagships have Optimized Battery Charging (or equivalent) that learns your schedule and stops at 80% until shortly before you typically wake up. This reduces heat and cycle stress.

**Why is my wireless charger not working?**
Common causes: misaligned coil (move phone slightly until indicator light changes), incompatible case, software issue (toggle airplane mode), adapter wattage too low (wireless chargers require 18W+ wall adapters — cheap 5W cube adapters often don't deliver enough power).
