## What to Look For

- **Wireless thermometer range:** 100–150 ft is enough for most backyards; if you want to monitor from inside during long smokes, look for 300 ft rated range.
- **Bristle safety:** Avoid thin, widely spaced wire brushes that shed bristles easily. Look for tightly packed stainless bristles or go bristle-free with a coil or grate scraper design.
- **Tool length:** 16–18 inches keeps hands safely away from high heat. Shorter tools are fine for low-and-slow but risky over direct flames.
- **Grate material:** Cast iron holds heat longer for better searing but requires seasoning and rust prevention. Stainless is low-maintenance. GrillGrates are the best upgrade for any grate type.
- **Chimney capacity:** Standard chimneys hold 80–100 briquettes. Size up if you regularly cook on a large charcoal grill or offset smoker.
- **Tool sets vs. singles:** Starter sets offer value; once you have preferences, invest in individual best-in-class tools for the jobs you do most.
