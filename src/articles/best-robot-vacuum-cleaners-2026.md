## How to Choose the Right Robot Vacuum

### Suction Power

Measured in Pascals (Pa). For hardwood and tile, 1,000–2,000 Pa is sufficient. For thick carpet or pet hair embedded in rugs, look for 3,000 Pa minimum. Flagship models at 6,000–11,000 Pa make carpet cleaning noticeably more effective.

### Mapping Technology

| Type | How It Works | Accuracy |
|------|-------------|---------|
| LiDAR | Laser rangefinder creates precise map | Excellent |
| SLAM (camera-only) | Visual landmarks for navigation | Good |
| Gyroscope only | Pattern-based movement | Basic |

LiDAR is the gold standard — maps are consistent, room recognition is reliable, and the robot doesn't get lost in the dark.

### Auto-Empty: Is It Worth It?

Auto-empty bases are worth it for most users. They allow the robot to clean multiple times between manual interventions (typically 7–60 days depending on bin size). For allergy sufferers, allergens stay sealed in the dock bag instead of being released when you open the onboard bin.

### Mopping: What to Expect

Robot mops handle light soiling and maintenance cleaning well. They're not a substitute for occasional hand-mopping of heavily soiled floors. Models with sonic scrubbing or spinning pads (Roborock, Ecovacs) do noticeably better on stuck-on grime.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can robot vacuums handle pet hair without tangling?**
Yes — modern models with anti-tangle brushes (Ecovacs ZeroTangle, iRobot dual rubber extractors) are specifically designed for pet hair. Traditional bristle brushes tangle more; look for rubber roller designs if you have heavy shedders.

**Do robot vacuums work on thick carpet?**
Most flagships with 3,000+ Pa suction handle medium-pile carpet well. For very thick or shag carpet, verify the robot's specs — low-profile robots may struggle to transition. Roborock and Ecovacs flagships with 5,500+ Pa are the safest bets.

**How long does battery last?**
Entry-level models: 90–100 minutes. Mid-range to flagship: 150–200+ minutes. Most robots return to dock and recharge automatically mid-clean if needed.

**Are robot vacuums good for stairs?**
No — robot vacuums cannot navigate stairs and use cliff sensors to stop at edges. You'll still need a handheld or upright vacuum for stairs.

**How often should I run a robot vacuum?**
Daily for pet owners; 3–4 times per week for regular households. Smart scheduling via app makes it easy to set-and-forget.
