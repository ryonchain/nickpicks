## Buying Guide: Outdoor Tech Worth the Investment

### Solar Lighting: What to Know

**Charge time:** Most solar garden lights need 6–8 hours of direct sun to charge to full. Shaded gardens may see reduced performance — consider plug-in low-voltage lighting for heavily shaded paths.

**Lumen output:** Solar pathway lights run 5–25 lumens — enough for path marking and accent, not security flood lighting. For security applications, look for solar motion-sensor floodlights (100–1000 lumens).

**Winter performance:** Solar lights work year-round but run shorter hours in winter due to reduced daylight. Many auto-adjust run time based on charge level.

### Smart Sprinklers: Is the Cost Worth It?

Yes, if you have an existing irrigation system. Studies show smart sprinkler controllers reduce outdoor water usage by 30–50% on average. At typical water rates, the Rachio 3 pays for itself in 1–3 seasons. WaterSense certification means it qualifies for rebates in many water utility districts — check your local utility before buying.
