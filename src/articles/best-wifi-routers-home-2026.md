## How to Choose the Right Router

### 1. Match Your Internet Speed
| Internet Speed | Minimum Router | Recommended |
|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| Under 100 Mbps | Wi-Fi 5 AC1200 | Wi-Fi 6 AX1800 |
| 100–500 Mbps | Wi-Fi 6 AX3000 | Wi-Fi 6 AX3000+ |
| 500 Mbps–1 Gbps | Wi-Fi 6 AX5400 | Wi-Fi 6E or Wi-Fi 7 |
| 1 Gbps+ (Multi-gig) | Wi-Fi 6E AX7800 | Wi-Fi 7 BE19000+ |

### 2. Calculate Coverage Needed
- **Apartment/condo (under 1,500 sq ft):** Single router fine
- **House 1,500–3,000 sq ft:** Strong single router or 2-node mesh
- **House 3,000–5,000 sq ft:** 2–3 node mesh
- **House 5,000+ sq ft:** 3+ node mesh with wired backhaul

### 3. Count Your Devices
- **Under 20 devices:** Any Wi-Fi 6 router handles it
- **20–50 devices:** Look for MU-MIMO 4x4 and OFDMA
- **50+ devices:** Mesh system with dedicated backhaul

### 4. Wired Backhaul is a Game-Changer
If you can run an Ethernet cable between mesh nodes (even if just to your most important room), you'll double effective throughput. Eero Max 7's 10G ports make this especially powerful.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need Wi-Fi 7 in 2026?**
Not yet for most people. Wi-Fi 6 and 6E handle gigabit internet and 30+ devices easily. Wi-Fi 7 future-proofs you for devices entering the market in 2027–2028.

**What's the difference between Wi-Fi 6 and 6E?**
Wi-Fi 6E adds the uncongested 6 GHz band — less interference in dense neighborhoods (apartments). Wi-Fi 6E devices are still limited but growing.

**Should I buy or rent a router from my ISP?**
Buy. ISP routers are typically 3–5 years behind in technology. Most ISPs charge $10–15/month — a $100 router pays for itself in under a year.

**How often should I replace my router?**
Every 4–5 years as a rule. If you're on Wi-Fi 5 (AC) and upgraded to gigabit internet, now is a good time.

**Can a better router increase my internet speed?**
Yes — if your current router is the bottleneck. A Wi-Fi 5 router caps throughput around 300–500 Mbps even on a 1 Gbps plan.
