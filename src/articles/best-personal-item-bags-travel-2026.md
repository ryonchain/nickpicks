## Personal Item Bag Buying Guide

### Airline size restrictions: the numbers that matter

Personal item size limits vary by carrier, but these are the most important benchmarks:

| Airline | Personal Item Limit |
|---------|-------------------|
| Spirit | 18 x 14 x 8 in |
| Frontier | 18 x 14 x 8 in |
| American | 18 x 14 x 8 in |
| Delta | 22 x 14 x 9 in |
| United | 17 x 10 x 9 in |
| Southwest | 18.5 x 13.5 x 8.5 in |

The safest bet: keep your personal item at or under 18 x 14 x 8 inches to pass even the most restrictive carriers. Many bags marketed as "personal items" are too large for Spirit and Frontier — check dimensions carefully.

### How much can you fit in a personal item?

A 20L personal item bag can typically hold:
- 2 changes of clothes (rolled compression-style)
- 1 pair of shoes (packed around clothing)
- Toiletries bag (TSA-compliant)
- Electronics: laptop, phone, cables
- In-flight items: headphones, snacks, book
- Passport, wallet, travel docs

For trips under 3 days, this is a full bag. For longer trips, a personal item + overhead carry-on covers 7–10 days without checking.

### Backpack vs. tote: which works better under a seat?

**Backpacks** with a structured back panel tend to hold their shape better under seats. Unstructured soft-sided backpacks can collapse and become harder to access mid-flight. A wide clamshell-opening backpack is the most functional: easy to pack, easy to access.

**Totes** with structured bases sit flat under the seat without slouching, but they're harder to manage when loaded with heavy items since all the weight falls from one shoulder. A tote works best for lighter loads (laptop, tablet, documents, snacks).

### The budget airline hack: personal item only

On Spirit and Frontier, the overhead carry-on fee can reach $100+ at the gate. A personal item is always free. Packing everything into a 18 x 14 x 8-inch bag is genuinely achievable for 2–4 day trips — the Tortuga Setout Divide or Amazon Basics bag can fit more than you'd expect when packed efficiently.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size is a personal item for most airlines?**
Most US airlines allow a personal item of approximately 18 x 14 x 8 inches or smaller. Spirit and Frontier are most restrictive at exactly this size. Always check your specific airline's current policy before packing.

**Can I bring a personal item AND a carry-on bag?**
Yes — on most airlines you are entitled to one full-size carry-on (overhead bin) and one personal item (underseat). Budget carriers charge for the overhead carry-on but allow a free personal item.

**What should I keep in my personal item during a flight?**
Keep items you'll need during the flight: headphones, neck pillow, chargers, a book or tablet, snacks, your passport or ID, and medications. The personal item stays accessible under the seat while your overhead carry-on may need to be stowed elsewhere.

**What is the difference between a personal item and a carry-on?**
A carry-on goes in the overhead bin (up to 22 x 14 x 9 inches typically). A personal item goes under the seat in front of you and must be smaller — approximately 18 x 14 x 8 inches on most carriers.
