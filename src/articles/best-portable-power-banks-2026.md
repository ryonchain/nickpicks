## Power Bank Buyer's Guide

### Capacity: mAh vs. Wh

**mAh (milliamp hours)** is the raw capacity rating. **Wh (watt hours)** is what airlines actually measure.

The conversion: Wh = mAh ÷ 1,000 × voltage (usually 3.7V or 3.6V).

- 10,000 mAh ≈ 36–37 Wh
- 20,000 mAh ≈ 72–74 Wh
- 26,800 mAh ≈ 96 Wh

**Airline rule:** Most airlines (FAA/IATA) allow power banks up to 100 Wh in carry-on (not checked luggage). Banks 100–160 Wh require airline approval; over 160 Wh are prohibited.

### Real-World Charges Per Capacity

Actual charges are ~85% of rated capacity due to conversion losses:

| Capacity | iPhone 15 (3,349 mAh) | Galaxy S24 (4,000 mAh) | MacBook Air M3 (52.6 Wh) |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 10,000 mAh | ~2.5 charges | ~2 charges | ~0.5 charges |
| 20,000 mAh | ~5 charges | ~4 charges | ~1 full charge |
| 26,800 mAh | ~6.5 charges | ~5.5 charges | ~1.3 charges |

### Charging Speed: Watts Matter

- **Under 20W:** Too slow for laptops; fine for phone top-ups
- **20–45W:** Charges phones quickly (0→50% in 30 min); partial laptop charging
- **65W+:** Full laptop charging at moderate speed
- **100W+:** Near-wall speed for most laptops

Older USB-A ports typically deliver 5–18W. USB-C PD delivers 20–140W depending on the power bank.

### Weight and Daily Carry Trade-Offs

- **Under 200g:** Pocketable; 10,000 mAh only
- **200–400g:** Jacket pocket; 10,000–20,000 mAh range
- **400–750g:** Bag carry only; 20,000–27,000 mAh range

For everyday carry without a bag, stay under 200g. For travel bags, capacity matters more than weight.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I bring a power bank on a plane?

Power banks must go in carry-on luggage, not checked bags. The limit is 100 Wh (no airline approval needed) or 160 Wh (with airline approval). Check the power bank's Wh rating on the label — not just mAh. Most 20,000 mAh banks are 72–74 Wh (safe); most 26,800 mAh banks are 96 Wh (safe).

### How do I know if my power bank is depleting my device's battery?

If the power bank output (watts) is lower than your device's draw when actively using it, the bank will charge it slowly or keep it at the same percentage rather than gaining charge. For laptops, match power bank wattage to your laptop's charging wattage (found on your wall charger).

### How long does it take to recharge a power bank?

Divide capacity (Wh) by charging wattage to get hours. A 20,000 mAh bank (74 Wh) charged at 20W = ~3.7 hours. Charged at 65W = ~1.5 hours. Higher-wattage input cables and adapters matter here.

### Why does my power bank say 10,000 mAh but only charge my phone once?

Voltage conversion (from 3.7V battery to 5V USB output) plus heat losses reduce real-world capacity to ~70–85% of rated mAh. A 10,000 mAh bank realistically delivers 7,000–8,500 mAh at the USB port — enough for 2 iPhone charges, not 3.
