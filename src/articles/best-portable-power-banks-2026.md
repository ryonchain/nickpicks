## Capacity vs. Portability: Finding Your Balance

**Capacity is measured in mAh (milliamp hours)** — but the real-world charge count is 60–70% of rated capacity due to conversion loss during charging. A 20,000 mAh power bank delivers roughly 12,000–14,000 mAh of usable charge. A modern iPhone 15 has a 3,274 mAh battery, so a 10,000 mAh bank provides roughly 2–2.5 full charges.

**Output wattage determines charging speed.** USB-A legacy ports cap at 12W (standard) or 18W (Quick Charge). USB-C PD ports start at 18W and scale to 140W+ for high-end options. Laptop charging requires at least 45W; premium laptops need 65–100W. If you want a single power bank that charges both phone and laptop, you need a USB-C PD port rated at least 65W.

**Size and weight scale with capacity.** A 10,000 mAh bank weighs 180–220g — fits in a jacket pocket. A 26,800 mAh bank weighs 450–650g — backpack territory. The sweet spot for most users is 10,000–20,000 mAh with at least one high-wattage USB-C port.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Power Bank

### Wattage Over Capacity for Laptop Users

If you're charging a laptop, check the manufacturer's rated charger wattage (printed on the included adapter) and match it. Charging at 45W on a 100W laptop works but is slow; charging at 100W on a 65W laptop is fine — the laptop self-limits. Prioritize getting the right wattage before worrying about extra mAh.

### Airline Carry-On Rules

The FAA and most international aviation authorities allow power banks up to 100Wh (watt-hours) in carry-on bags — not checked luggage. Convert mAh to Wh: mAh × nominal voltage (3.7V) ÷ 1000. A 26,800 mAh bank = 99.16Wh — just under the limit. Banks over 100Wh require airline approval and are often rejected.

### Recharge Time Matters

A 26,800 mAh power bank on a standard 18W charger takes 10–14 hours to fully recharge. If you travel daily, prioritize banks with high-wattage input ports so the bank refills overnight, not over two nights.

### Brand Reliability

For lithium battery products, brand reputation matters more than most categories — counterfeit capacity and unprotected battery cells are real risks. Anker, Baseus, and RAVPower have proven track records. For unknown brands, look for UL certification on the listing.
