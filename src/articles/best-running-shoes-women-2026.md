**Stability features that work:**

- Gel cushioning in both heel and forefoot (forefoot Gel is new to version 31)
- FF BLAST+ PLUS midsole foam for rebound energy
- Engineered mesh upper with reinforced overlays
- 10mm drop (moderate — good for overpronators transitioning from higher-drop shoes)

**Best for:** Runners with mild-to-moderate overpronation, flat feet, or anyone who's been told by a run specialty store they need stability shoes.

**Limitation:** Heavier than neutral trainers. Not ideal for runners who prefer fast-feeling shoes.

## Finding the Right Shoe: Key Variables

**Neutral vs. Stability:**
- Supinators (foot rolls outward): neutral shoe, often with firm midsole (Hoka, Brooks Ghost)
- Neutral gait: neutral shoe (most of the options above)
- Overpronators: stability shoe (ASICS Kayano, Brooks Adrenaline, New Balance 860)

**Heel drop:**
- High drop (10–12mm): best for new runners, forefoot strikers in transition
- Low drop (0–6mm): best for midfoot/forefoot strikers, HOKA wearers, barefoot fans
- Medium drop (6–8mm): the safe middle for most runners

**When to replace:** Track mileage. Replace at 300–500 miles or when the midsole shows visible compression. Don't wait for the upper to fail — the cushion goes first.

**Width:** Women's standard is B; D is wide; 2A is narrow. Wrong width is the most common cause of blisters and black toenails.
