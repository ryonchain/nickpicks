## Dogs vs. Cats: Key Differences

GPS trackers for cats have different requirements than dog trackers — not all dog trackers translate well to cats.

- **Weight:** Cats should wear nothing heavier than 1 oz on their collar. Most dog trackers (1.3–2.0 oz) are too heavy for cats under 10 lbs. Check the weight spec carefully before purchasing for a cat.
- **Collar breakaway safety:** Cat collars should have a breakaway/safety-release buckle so cats don't get caught on branches or fences. Many trackers designed for dogs attach via a fixed loop that prevents use with breakaway collars — look specifically for breakaway-compatible attachment hardware for cats.
- **Update frequency needs:** Cats roam in defined territories and rarely bolt at sprint speed the way dogs do. A 2–3 minute update frequency is often sufficient for cats; dogs on the move benefit from 2–3 second live tracking.
- **Subscription considerations:** Most GPS trackers require the same subscription whether for a cat or dog. Some carriers offer multi-pet plans that reduce per-device cost.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Calculate total cost of ownership over 3 years.** A $30 tracker at $12/month costs $462 over 3 years; a $100 tracker at $5/month costs $280. Hardware price is a poor proxy for total value.

**Match tracker weight to your pet's size.** General rule: tracker weight should not exceed 5% of the pet's body weight. A 10 lb cat or dog should wear nothing heavier than 0.5 oz — which limits you to the Jiobit or AirTag. A 50 lb dog handles 2 oz without discomfort.

**Cats require breakaway collar compatibility.** A cat trapped by a non-breakaway collar on a fence or branch can choke. Verify that the tracker's attachment hardware works with a safety-release buckle before purchasing. The Tractive Cat Edition and Jiobit are explicitly designed for this; most dog-focused trackers are not.

**GPS accuracy is consistent across brands.** All GPS trackers use the same satellite system; accuracy varies by how aggressively the device updates, not by brand. Open terrain: accurate to 10–30 feet. Dense urban or forested areas: expect 30–100 feet variance. No tracker overcomes satellite physics.

**Subscription coverage matters in rural areas.** Most trackers run on a single cellular carrier. If you live rurally or hike with your dog in areas with limited LTE, verify the tracker's carrier coverage before purchasing.
