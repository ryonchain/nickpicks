## A Mechanical Keyboard Is the Best Ergonomic Upgrade Most People Ignore

You spend 6–8 hours per day typing. The feedback, actuation force, and travel distance of each keystroke adds up to millions of key presses per year. The right mechanical keyboard reduces finger fatigue, improves typing accuracy, and lasts 20+ years (vs. 2–5 years for membrane keyboards). For home office workers, it's one of the highest-impact desk upgrades available.

---

## Mechanical vs. Membrane: The Core Difference

**Membrane keyboards:** A rubber dome collapses to register a keypress. Mushy feedback, shorter lifespan (2–5 years of daily use), no tactile confirmation until bottom-out.

**Mechanical keyboards:** Individual switches with a spring mechanism. Consistent feedback, longer lifespan (50–100 million keystrokes per switch), and switch types tuned to different feedback preferences.

---

## Switch Types Explained

**Linear (Red, Yellow):** Smooth travel, no tactile bump, quieter. Best for gamers and typists who prefer no resistance variation.

**Tactile (Brown, Clear):** A noticeable bump at the actuation point without an audible click. The most popular for office use — you feel confirmation without noise.

**Clicky (Blue, Green):** Tactile bump + audible click. Satisfying to type on; annoying for open-plan offices or video calls.

**Silent (Silent Red, Silent Brown):** Quieter versions of linear/tactile switches. Best for open offices, shared workspaces, or frequent video calls.
