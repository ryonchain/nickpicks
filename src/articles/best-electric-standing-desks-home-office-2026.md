## What Makes a Good Electric Standing Desk

**Dual motors vs. single motor:** Dual-motor desks (one per leg) produce 2–3x more lifting force and wobble significantly less. Single-motor desks are acceptable for lighter loads (monitor arm, keyboard, laptop), but shaky under dual monitors + desktop PC setups.

**Height range:** A good desk spans at least 24"–50" to accommodate both seated (for short users) and full standing (for tall users). Verify both the minimum and maximum.

**Weight capacity:** 200 lbs is the practical minimum for a full workstation setup. 275–350 lbs handles dual monitors, a PC tower, and accessories.

**Frame warranty:** Frame quality determines long-term reliability. Look for 5+ years on the frame; motors often come separately warranted.

## Final Advice

Buy a desk rated for more than your current setup. A 200 lbs capacity desk that you load to 195 lbs will wobble more and wear faster than a 350 lbs desk at 195 lbs load. Overspec on weight capacity — it has no downside.

And if you buy the frame only, invest in the tabletop. A premium surface (butcher block, solid bamboo) on a good frame lasts a decade. A poor tabletop on a good frame is a waste of the frame.
