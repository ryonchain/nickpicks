## Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Smart Lock

### Security Ratings Explained

**ANSI/BHMA Grade 1** is tested to 250,000 cycles, withstands 360 lb kick resistance, and meets the highest pick/drill resistance standards. Grade 2 meets residential security standards for normal use. For primary entry doors in higher-crime areas, Grade 1 is recommended.

### Retrofit vs Full Replacement

**Retrofit locks** (August, Level) install on the interior only — your existing exterior hardware stays. Good for renters and anyone who wants their lock invisible from outside. **Full replacement locks** (Schlage, Yale, Kwikset) replace your entire deadbolt for a new keyway and higher security options.

### Hub vs Wi-Fi Built-In

Many older smart locks require a proprietary hub or Z-Wave/Zigbee bridge to enable remote access. The best 2026 models include built-in Wi-Fi — look for this to eliminate extra hardware.

### Battery Life Benchmarks

- 3–4 months: Budget Wi-Fi locks with high polling frequency
- 6 months: Standard for most AA-battery smart locks
- 12+ months: Level Lock+ and Bluetooth-only locks with low duty cycles

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can smart locks be hacked?**
Reputable brands use AES-128 or AES-256 encryption for app communication. Physical security (Grade 1 vs Grade 2 deadbolt steel and bolt length) matters more for break-in resistance than wireless security — criminals rarely pick locks or crack encryption.

**Do smart locks work during a power outage?**
Yes. All smart locks in this review run on AA or CR2 batteries — they work entirely independently of home power. Wi-Fi features require router power, but local entry methods (keypad, fingerprint, key) work without internet.

**Can I install a smart lock without drilling new holes?**
Yes, if your door already has a standard single-cylinder deadbolt with a 2-1/8" bore hole. Retrofit options (August) require no modification. Full-replacement locks use the same bore hole as standard deadbolts.

**What if my phone dies — can I still get in?**
Every lock in this review includes a physical key backup. Most also support a keypad code even if the app isn't available. The Ultraloq adds micro-USB emergency charging.
