## Range Reality Check

Manufacturer range claims assume ideal conditions: flat terrain, low speed (typically 9–12 mph), light rider (around 130 lbs), moderate temperature. Real-world range for a 165 lb rider at 15 mph with normal hills is roughly **60–70% of the stated max**. The estimates in this guide reflect that.

## Electric Scooter Buying Guide

**Check local laws first.** Many US cities limit electric scooters to 15–20 mph and require them on bike paths or roads only. Some ban them from sidewalks entirely. Know the rules before buying.

**Range vs carry weight tradeoff.** More range = bigger battery = heavier scooter. If you carry it up stairs daily, 26–32 lbs matters. If it stays on street level, 40+ lbs is fine.

**Pneumatic vs solid tires:** Pneumatic (air-filled) tires absorb road vibration for a much smoother ride. Solid tires are puncture-proof but harsher. Urban streets with cracks and potholes benefit most from pneumatic tires.

**Water resistance ratings:** IPX4 = splash resistant. IPX5 = light rain. Neither rating means immersion-safe — avoid puddles regardless of rating.
