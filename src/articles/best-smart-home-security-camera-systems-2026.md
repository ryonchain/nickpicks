## Smart Home Security Camera Buying Guide

### How Many Cameras Do You Need?

For a typical home, plan for:
- **Minimum coverage (4 cameras):** Front door, back door, main driveway, and one indoor common area
- **Standard coverage (6–8 cameras):** All entry points, garage, backyard, and 1–2 indoor cameras
- **Full coverage (8+ cameras):** All of the above plus side gates, detached garage, shed, and upstairs hallway

### Wired vs. Wire-Free

**Wired cameras** offer continuous recording, better video quality, and never need battery changes. They're harder to install but more reliable. **Wire-free cameras** install in minutes but require regular charging (or solar panels). For a whole-home system, a mix of both often works best.

### Should You Pay for a Cloud Subscription?

All the systems here offer meaningful free tiers for basic use. Pay for a subscription when you need:
- 30+ days of video history for insurance or police reports
- Advanced smart detection (package detection, familiar faces)
- Continuous 24/7 recording (not just motion clips)
- Cross-camera intelligence (Ring Protect, Arlo Secure)

### Local Storage vs. Cloud

Local storage (microSD or NAS) is free, private, and doesn't stop working if the company shuts down. Cloud storage is accessible from anywhere and harder to physically steal. Best practice: use both when possible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which home security camera system has the best night vision?**
Arlo Pro 5S and Wyze Cam v4 both offer excellent color night vision with built-in spotlights. Arlo's color night vision performs better in zero-light conditions without the spotlight.

**Do security cameras work without a subscription?**
Yes. All cameras listed here work without a subscription for live viewing and local recording. Subscriptions add cloud storage history and advanced detection features. Reolink is the best fully-free option.

**Can I mix cameras from different brands?**
Technically yes — they'll appear in their respective apps. But for the best experience, pick one ecosystem (Ring, Arlo, Wyze, or Nest/Google Home) and stick with it for unified alerts and automation.

**How long do battery cameras last?**
Depends on motion frequency. A front-door camera in a busy area might need charging every 4–6 weeks. A low-traffic backyard camera might last 4–6 months. Solar panels eliminate this concern entirely.

**Are security cameras worth it for renters?**
Yes — wire-free battery cameras like the Arlo Pro 5S and Wyze Cam v4 don't require drilling and are perfect for renters. Just check your lease for indoor camera restrictions.
