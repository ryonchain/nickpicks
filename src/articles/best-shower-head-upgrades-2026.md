## Shower Head Buying Guide

### Key Specs to Understand

**GPM (Gallons Per Minute)**
- Standard: 2.5 GPM (most homes, good pressure)
- Eco: 1.5–2.0 GPM (water-saving, lower pressure feel)
- High-pressure specialty: Uses lower GPM but concentrates spray for better perceived pressure

**Spray Patterns**
- **Full body:** Wide, even coverage — best for everyday use
- **Massage/pulse:** High-pressure streams — good for muscle tension
- **Mist/gentle:** Light spray — good for sensitive skin or rinsing
- **Combo:** Alternates between patterns for varied sensation

### Installation Notes
Most shower heads use standard 1/2-inch NPT thread — virtually universal for US plumbing. No plumber needed. Tools required: adjustable wrench (wrapped in cloth to avoid scratching). Use plumber's tape (Teflon tape) on the threads to prevent leaks.

### Rain Shower Heads in Low-Pressure Systems
Rain shower heads typically require 40–80 PSI for proper function. In low-pressure homes (under 40 PSI), a rain shower head will feel weak. Choose a high-pressure model like the Speakman instead, or install a shower pressure booster ($30–80) upstream.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I install a shower head without a plumber?**
Yes. Almost all shower heads use the same 1/2-inch threaded connection. Unscrew the old head by hand or with a wrench, apply plumber's tape to the pipe threads, and hand-tighten the new head. The whole process takes 10–15 minutes.

**Will a new shower head increase water pressure?**
A high-pressure shower head (like Speakman) concentrates water flow to create better perceived pressure even if your supply pressure is low. It doesn't actually increase water supply pressure, but it optimizes what's available.

**How often should I replace my shower head?**
Standard shower heads work well for 5–10 years. Replace sooner if you notice: mineral buildup that reduces spray quality, cracking or discoloration, or leaking around the connection. Filtered models need filter changes every 6 months.
