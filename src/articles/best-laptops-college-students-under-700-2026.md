## How to Choose a College Laptop

**Pick based on your major:**
- STEM (engineering, CS, data science): Get 16GB RAM minimum. Python, MATLAB, and data science tools are memory-hungry.
- Pre-med / nursing: Battery life matters most — long clinical days mean no power access. Aim for 10+ hours.
- Arts / media: Display quality and storage matter. Consider external storage.
- Business / liberal arts: Any option above works. Prioritize portability and battery.

**Avoid these mistakes:**
- Buying 4GB RAM — it's not enough for modern Windows and will feel slow by sophomore year.
- Skimping on storage below 256GB — between apps, documents, and downloads, you'll fill it fast.
- Choosing a gaming laptop for general college use — they're heavier, run hot, and battery lasts 3–4 hours.

## FAQ

**Do I need a laptop with a dedicated GPU for college?**
No, unless you're in game design, video production, or 3D modeling. Integrated graphics handle 99% of college workloads and dramatically improve battery life and reduce weight.

**Is 8GB RAM enough for college in 2026?**
It depends. For basic tasks (documents, web, Zoom), 8GB is fine. For STEM workloads, coding, or running multiple apps simultaneously, 16GB is a meaningful improvement. Upgrade if your budget allows.

**Should I buy a Mac or a Windows laptop for college?**
Windows offers more flexibility at the sub-$700 price point. MacBooks don't start until $999. If your program uses specific Windows-only software (like AutoCAD or certain engineering tools), stay with Windows. If your program has no requirements and budget allows, a MacBook Air M2 is worth the jump to $999.
