## Prime Day Tablet Overview: What to Expect

Tablets split into three tiers with distinct Prime Day behaviors:

**Amazon Fire tablets** — deepest discounts (30–50%), lowest prices of the year. Amazon uses Prime Day to move inventory and pull customers into its ecosystem.

**Apple iPads** — moderate discounts (10–20% off), but Apple rarely discounts retail. Amazon and third-party sellers sometimes offer better pricing than Apple's own site.

**Samsung Galaxy Tabs** — competitive with iPads on specs, often more aggressive discounting (20–35% off).

## What to Skip on Prime Day

**iPad Pro** — Apple rarely discounts the Pro line, and when it does, it's minimal. If you need an iPad Pro, buy it when you need it.

**Amazon Fire 7** — The 7" screen is too small for comfortable use. Spend $20 more and get the Fire HD 8. The resolution and screen size gap is significant.

**Off-brand Android tablets** — Prime Day surfaces a lot of no-name Android tablets at $60–$100. Most have outdated chips, poor displays, and no software support. Spend the extra money on a Fire HD or entry iPad.

**Older Samsung Tabs (S7, S8 era)** — Tempting at Prime Day clearance prices, but Qualcomm support windows mean these may stop receiving major Android updates soon.
