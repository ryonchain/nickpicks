## Men's Fall Boot Buying Guide

**Chelsea vs. Chukka vs. Work Boot:**
- **Chelsea:** Slip-on with elastic panels. Clean, versatile. Dress up or down.
- **Chukka:** Ankle-height with 2–3 lace eyelets. Casual, slightly dressy.
- **Work boot:** Higher shaft, lug sole, maximum durability. More casual.
- **Dress boot:** Similar silhouette to dress shoes but higher ankle.

**Leather types:**
- **Full-grain:** Most durable, develops patina. Top-tier option.
- **Nubuck:** Like suede but from the outer hide. Softer, requires waterproofing.
- **Suede:** Soft and stylish, not waterproof. Best for dry fall days.
- **Faux leather:** Budget, more weather-resistant, shorter lifespan.

**Sole types:**
- **Vibram:** Industry-standard aftermarket sole; hard-wearing and replaceable.
- **Crepe:** Flexible and comfortable; not as durable on city pavement.
- **Lug sole:** High traction for outdoor use; adds casual bulk.

**Goodyear welt construction:** Look for this in premium boots — it means the sole is stitched (not glued) and can be replaced, extending boot life significantly.

**Boot care:**
- Waterproof spray: Apply before first wear on suede and nubuck
- Cedar shoe trees: Maintain shape and absorb moisture between wears
- Leather conditioner: Apply every 2–3 months to full-grain leather
- Brush after muddy use before the mud dries
