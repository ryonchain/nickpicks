A lithium jump starter in your glove box changes that calculus entirely. These compact units can start a dead car battery in under 30 seconds, fit in a bag, and double as a power bank for your phone.

Modern lithium jump starters have replaced the old bulky lead-acid units. They're lighter, charge faster, and maintain their charge for months. Here are the five best for 2026.

---

## What to Look for in a Jump Starter

**Peak amps vs. cranking amps:** Peak amps is the marketing number — it's a momentary burst. What matters is cranking amps (CA) or cold cranking amps (CCA), which reflect sustained output. For a 4-cylinder gas engine, 400+ CCA is adequate; 6- and 8-cylinder engines need 800+ CCA.

**Engine compatibility:** The label will list max engine size in liters. A 1000A unit typically handles up to 7L gas and 4.5L diesel. Match the unit to your largest vehicle.

**Safety features:** Look for reverse polarity protection (alert when you've connected the cables backwards), spark-proof clamps, and over-current protection. These aren't optional for a device attached to your battery.

**Charge retention:** Lithium units self-discharge slowly — a quality unit holds 70%+ charge after 12 months in a glove box. Cheap units lose charge within weeks.

**USB power bank function:** Most lithium jump starters double as 10,000–20,000mAh power banks. A secondary feature that adds genuine value for emergency phone charging.
