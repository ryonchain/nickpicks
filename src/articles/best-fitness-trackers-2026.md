# Best Fitness Trackers (2026)

---

## How to Choose a Fitness Tracker

The right tracker depends on whether you need a basic activity counter, a health monitoring device, or a training tool. These categories have very different price-to-value trade-offs.

Key factors to evaluate:

- **Heart rate accuracy:** Optical HR sensors on the wrist vary considerably. Garmin and Apple have the most rigorously validated sensors; budget trackers trade accuracy for cost.
- **GPS:** Built-in GPS is essential for runners and cyclists who want accurate pace and distance without carrying a phone. Many budget trackers have "connected GPS" only (piggybacks on your phone's GPS).
- **Battery life:** Smartwatch-style trackers (Apple Watch, Galaxy Watch) typically last 18–36 hours. Fitness bands (Fitbit Charge, Xiaomi Band) can run 5–14 days. Garmin GPS watches can run 13 days in smartwatch mode or 20+ hours in GPS mode.
- **Sleep tracking:** All modern trackers detect sleep stages, but depth of insight varies. Fitbit and Garmin provide detailed sleep scoring and coaching.
- **Platform ecosystem:** Apple Watch requires iPhone. Fitbit and Garmin work cross-platform but have tighter feature sets on non-native platforms.

### 2. Fitbit Charge 6 — Best Overall Tracker

**Price:** ~$160 | [Check on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Fitbit+Charge+6&tag=nickpicks-20)

Fitbit's Charge 6 adds built-in GPS (the Charge 5 had it, the Charge 4 had it — it disappeared briefly and is back) along with Google Maps turn-by-turn navigation, Google Wallet NFC payments, and YouTube Music controls. The Fitbit platform remains the best for everyday users who care about sleep, stress (EDA sensor), and activity trends without training analytics.

**Pros:** Built-in GPS, EDA stress sensor, ECG, Google ecosystem integration, 7-day battery.  
**Cons:** Requires Fitbit Premium for deeper insights (~$10/month), limited smart notification functionality compared to smartwatches.
