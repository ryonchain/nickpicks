## Pellet Grill Buying Guide

### Cooking Area: How Much Do You Need?

| Household Size | Minimum Area | Recommended |
|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| 2–3 people | 300 sq in | 450–500 sq in |
| 4–6 people | 500 sq in | 600–700 sq in |
| 6+ people | 700 sq in | 900+ sq in |

Rule of thumb: 1 lb of meat per person per cooking session as a baseline.

### Wood Pellet Flavors: What They Add

- **Hickory:** Bold, traditional American BBQ smoke. Best for pork and beef.
- **Apple:** Mild and slightly sweet. Best for chicken, pork, and fish.
- **Mesquite:** Intense, earthy. Use sparingly on beef and game meats.
- **Cherry:** Mild sweet flavor, beautiful mahogany color on the meat surface. All-purpose.
- **Competition Blend:** Mixed woods. A good all-purpose starting point.

Avoid off-brand pellets with fillers — they can cause temperature inconsistency and leave ash buildup.

### Wi-Fi vs. No Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi connectivity lets you monitor and adjust temperature from your phone. Useful for long overnight smokes. Most sub-$500 pellet grills lack it — if remote monitoring matters, budget for a Wi-Fi-enabled model or add a separate meat thermometer with Bluetooth.

## FAQ

**How long do wood pellets last?**
Most grills burn 1–3 lbs of pellets per hour depending on temperature setting. A 20 lb bag ($12–$20) provides 10–20 hours of cooking at medium smoke temperatures.

**Can pellet grills sear like gas grills?**
Many pellet grills reach 450–500°F for reasonable searing. Models with a direct flame access hole (like the Pit Boss 700FB) can reach 900°F+ for restaurant-quality sear marks. Pure searing isn't where pellet grills shine — for 800°F+ searing, a gas grill or dedicated infrared sear station is better.

**What maintenance does a pellet grill need?**
Empty ash after every 2–3 cooks. Clean grease trap monthly. Vacuum the firebox area every 5–10 cooks. Season new grates with cooking oil before first use.
