## What Makes a Watch "Luxury" at Under $1,000?

**Movement:** At this tier, you get Swiss-made or Japanese-made automatic movements — far more prestigious than quartz (battery-powered) movements. Swiss ETA movements power Tissot and Hamilton; Seiko and Orient have fully in-house movements.

**Crystal:** Sapphire crystal (hardness 9 on Mohs scale) resists scratching far better than mineral glass. Nearly all watches on this list include sapphire.

**Case finishing:** Luxury watches combine brushed and polished surfaces. Poorly finished cases look uniform and cheap; quality cases show deliberate differentiation.

**Power reserve:** A key measure of movement quality — a 60–80 hour reserve means the watch keeps running through the weekend if you take it off Friday night.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I buy Swiss or Japanese?**
Both produce excellent movements. Swiss (Tissot, Hamilton, Longines) have the prestige heritage. Japanese (Seiko, Orient) often deliver more movement complexity per dollar. No wrong answer.

**Do these watches hold value?**
Watches in the $500–$1,000 tier typically depreciate 20–40% when purchased new. If value retention matters, consider pre-owned watches — you can often buy a watch from this list at 30% below retail on the used market.
