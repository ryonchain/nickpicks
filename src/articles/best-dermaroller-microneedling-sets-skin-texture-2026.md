## How Microneedling Works at Home

Microneedling creates thousands of controlled micro-injuries in the skin using a roller covered with surgical-grade needles. These micro-channels trigger the skin's wound-healing response: collagen and elastin production accelerates, and growth factors flood the treated area. Over weeks, skin texture smooths, pores appear smaller, and superficial scarring fades.

**Needle depth guide:**
- **0.25mm:** Beginner-safe. Enhances product absorption; mild collagen stimulation. No downtime.
- **0.5mm:** Moderate collagen induction. Visible pinkness for a few hours. Best for skin texture and pore size.
- **1.0mm:** Advanced. Targets acne scars and deeper texture issues. Requires recovery; not recommended for home use without experience.

At-home devices should stay at 0.25–0.5mm. Deeper treatment should be done by a licensed aesthetician or dermatologist.

## Buying Guide

### Needle Material: Titanium vs. Stainless Steel
Titanium needles hold their edge longer and are gentler on skin than stainless steel — worth the slight cost premium. Look for "surgical titanium" in product descriptions.

### Before and After Care
- **Before:** Cleanse thoroughly, apply numbing cream if desired (0.5mm+), sanitize roller in 70% isopropyl alcohol
- **After:** Apply hyaluronic acid serum immediately post-rolling (absorption is dramatically increased), avoid retinol or vitamin C acids for 24 hours

### Sanitation is Critical
A contaminated roller can introduce bacteria into micro-channels. Sanitize with 70% isopropyl alcohol before and after every session. Replace rollers every 3–4 months regardless of visible wear.

## FAQ

### How long before I see results from dermarolling?
Initial brightness improvements appear after 1–2 sessions. Visible texture smoothing builds over 4–8 weeks of consistent use. Acne scar improvement takes 3–6 months. Results continue improving for up to 6 months after a consistent protocol.

### Can I use a dermaroller with active acne?
Do not roll over active pimples or cystic acne — this spreads bacteria and can worsen breakouts. Wait until blemishes are fully healed. You can roll areas of the face without active breakouts.

### What serums work best after microneedling?
Hyaluronic acid is the safest post-rolling serum — deeply hydrating with no irritation risk. Peptide serums and growth factor serums amplify collagen stimulation. Avoid vitamin C, retinol, or AHA/BHA acids for 24–48 hours post-rolling, as these can cause significant irritation when absorbed through micro-channels.
