## Why Silicone Beats Plastic and Stand-Up Pouches

Traditional zip-lock plastic bags are one-and-done. Silicone food bags last 3,000+ uses, withstand temperatures from -40°F to 428°F, and don't leach chemicals into food the way some plastics do at heat. The main trade-off is upfront cost and a seal that requires more deliberate closing than a plastic zip.

Key buying criteria:

- **Seal type:** Pinch-press seals (like Stasher's "pinch-loc") create an airtight closure by pressing two silicone ridges together. Zip-style silicone seals use a sliding zipper mechanism. Pinch seals are more durable long-term; zip seals are more intuitive for daily use.
- **Dishwasher safety:** All silicone bags claim dishwasher compatibility, but the question is how the seal holds up over 300+ cycles. Look for brands that specifically test seal integrity after repeated washing.
- **Freezer safety:** Quality silicone remains flexible at freezer temperatures (-40°F) without cracking. Cheaper silicone formulations can become brittle and split at seams.
- **Capacity sizes:** You want a set that covers snack (~8 oz), sandwich (~24 oz), and half-gallon/gallon for bulk storage. A complete set covers meal prep, lunch packing, and bulk freezing.
- **BPA-free certification:** All reputable silicone bags are BPA-free. Look for platinum food-grade silicone and LFGB or FDA certification — stricter than just a BPA-free label.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Pinch seal vs. zip seal:** Pinch-loc seals (Stasher-style) create a better airtight closure for liquids but have a steeper learning curve — household members need to know how to properly press the seal closed or it leaks. Zip seals are more intuitive but the zipper component can be harder to dry fully after washing.

**Snack vs. sandwich vs. gallon:** Think through your actual use case. Snack size (8 oz) works for nuts, cut fruit, and crackers. Sandwich size (15–24 oz) covers sandwiches, salads, and lunch portions. Half-gallon and gallon are for bulk freezer storage, soups, and batch cooking. Most households need at least 2 snack, 2–3 sandwich, and 1–2 larger bags.

**Cleaning logistics:** The biggest adoption barrier is cleaning. Bags need to be inverted and washed thoroughly — residual grease or food particles cause odors over time. Dishwasher-safe bags simplify this dramatically, but tall bags may need specific rack placement to stay open and drain properly.

**True temperature range:** "Freezer safe" doesn't mean all silicone performs equally at -20°F. Platinum-grade silicone maintains flexibility at extreme cold; cheaper formulations crack at seams. For long-term freezer storage (3+ months), invest in platinum silicone certification.
