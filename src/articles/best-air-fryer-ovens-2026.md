## Buying Guide: How to Choose an Air Fryer Oven

**Capacity:** For 1–2 people, a 10-qt or 0.6 cu ft model handles most meals. For families of 3–4, look for 1 cu ft minimum to handle full sheet pan meals and roasts.

**Heating technology:** Rear element + rear fan (most models) is adequate. Dual element (Ninja) or multi-element (Breville) delivers more even results — worth the extra cost if evenness matters to you.

**Air fry performance vs. basket fryer:** Air fryer ovens are slightly less crispy than basket-style fryers for the same batch size because there's more internal volume to circulate heat through. The gap is small for small batches; it grows for larger ones.

**Presets vs. manual control:** More presets mean less guesswork but a steeper learning curve. If you cook a limited range of foods, fewer presets with better individual performance is often preferable.

**Cleaning:** All models require periodic cleaning of the interior, crumb tray, and accessories. Look for models with dishwasher-safe accessories — it's the single factor that most affects how often people actually clean their oven.

## FAQ

**Are air fryer ovens worth it over a regular air fryer?**
If you use a toaster oven or regularly need to cook items larger than a basket air fryer can handle, yes. If you only make small batches of wings or fries, a basket fryer is more efficient. The oven format adds versatility but sacrifices some crisp intensity for larger batches.

**Can an air fryer oven replace a microwave?**
Not really. Air fryer ovens reheat food better (crispier, more even browning) but much slower than a microwave. Most households use both — microwave for speed, air fryer oven for quality results.

**How much counter space does an air fryer oven need?**
Plan for roughly 20–22 inches wide and 15–17 inches deep for a 0.6 cu ft model. The Breville Smart Oven is larger at about 21.5 x 17.5 inches. All models also need several inches of clearance above for heat dissipation.

**Do air fryer ovens use a lot of electricity?**
A typical air fryer oven runs at 1,500–1,800 watts. That's less than a full-size oven (2,000–5,000 watts) and more efficient for small meals because it preheats faster and heats a smaller space.
