## What's DOCSIS 3.1 and Do You Need It?

**DOCSIS 3.1** is the current cable modem standard, capable of supporting internet speeds up to 10 Gbps downstream. If your ISP plan is 500 Mbps or faster — especially Comcast's Gigabit or multi-gig plans — you need DOCSIS 3.1.

**DOCSIS 3.0** is the older standard, typically maxing out at 400–600 Mbps practical speeds on most ISP infrastructure. Fine for plans under 500 Mbps; limiting if you upgrade to Gigabit.

## Modem Router Combo vs. Separate Modem + Router

**Combo advantages:**
- One device, one power cord, one app
- Lower total cost than two separate quality units
- Less to set up and troubleshoot

**Separate modem + router advantages:**
- Replace one without replacing the other (if ISP changes standards or router breaks)
- Choose the best modem for your ISP and the best router independently
- Gaming and mesh routers are only available as standalone units
- Better performance ceiling — the best standalone routers outperform combo units

**Our recommendation:** If you want simplicity and cost savings, a combo unit from ARRIS or NETGEAR is the right call. If you want the best possible Wi-Fi performance (gaming, multi-node mesh), buy a separate DOCSIS 3.1 modem and pair it with your router of choice.

## FAQ

**Will buying my own modem affect my internet speed?**
No — as long as you buy a DOCSIS 3.1 modem approved by your ISP. In some cases, a higher-quality modem improves speeds over the ISP's budget rental equipment.

**Do all cable ISPs accept third-party modems?**
Most do — Xfinity, Cox, Spectrum, and others maintain approved device lists. Fiber-based ISPs (Google Fiber, AT&T Fiber) provide their own equipment and typically don't support third-party modems.

**Can I return my ISP's rental equipment immediately?**
Yes. Activate your new modem/combo first via your ISP's website, confirm internet works, then return the rental equipment. Don't return it before activation — you'll have a brief outage.
