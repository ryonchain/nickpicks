## How to Use Packing Cubes Effectively

**System 1 — By category:** Tops in one cube, bottoms in another, underwear/socks in small cubes. Fastest for daily use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do packing cubes actually save space?**
Standard packing cubes organize space but don't compress it — they make packing faster and keep clothes separated, but you won't fit more into the same bag. Compression packing cubes (with a second zip that squeezes out air) can reduce clothing volume by 30–50%, letting you pack more in the same space.

**What size packing cubes do I need for a carry-on?**
For a standard 21–22-inch carry-on, a set of one large cube (17 x 12 in), one medium cube (13 x 9 in), and two small cubes (11 x 7 in) typically fits perfectly. Many brands sell carry-on-specific sets designed to fill a 22 x 14 x 9-inch bag with minimal dead space.

**Are packing cubes worth it for short trips?**
Yes — even for a 2-night trip, packing cubes let you unpack directly into a hotel drawer or shelf without sorting, then repack in under 5 minutes. The biggest benefit isn't space savings; it's the time you save at both ends of the trip.

**Can I wash packing cubes in a washing machine?**
Most nylon and polyester packing cubes are machine-washable on a gentle cycle with cold water. Zipper longevity improves when washed inside a mesh laundry bag. Most brands do not recommend putting packing cubes in a dryer — hang or lay flat to dry.
