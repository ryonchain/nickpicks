## How to Choose

**Weight:** Consider how often you'll lift it into a car trunk. Lightweight strollers (under 15 lbs) are great for travel; heavier ones are often more feature-rich for daily use.

**Fold type:** One-hand folds matter most when you're holding a baby in the other arm. Practice the fold in the store if possible.

**From birth or later:** Only strollers with a full flat recline are safe from birth. Some strollers require an infant car seat adapter until the child can support their head.

**Travel system vs. standalone:** Travel systems (stroller + car seat) save money and ensure compatibility. But the stroller portion is often heavier than a standalone equivalent.

**Double vs. single:** If you're expecting a second child within 2–3 years, consider a stroller that converts to a double (like the UPPAbaby VISTA V2).
