## What to Look for in a Beginner Metal Detector

**Discrimination:** The ability to reject trash metals (iron nails, bottle caps) while keeping targets you want (coins, jewelry). All machines on this list have it; the F22 has the most detailed discrimination display.

**Operating Frequency:** 
- 6–10 kHz: best for coins and jewelry in all soil types
- 18–40 kHz: best for small gold nuggets (not needed for most beginners)

**Waterproofing:** 
- "Weatherproof" = can hunt in light rain
- "Waterproof coil" = coil can be submerged; control box needs protection
- "Fully submersible" = wade, snorkel, or dive

**Pinpointing:** Once you get a signal, pinpointing narrows the exact location to save digging time. Important feature to look for.

**Battery Life:** Standard AA batteries last 20–30 hours. Rechargeable detectors (Minelab) save money long-term.

## Where Can You Metal Detect?

- Your own yard (no permission needed)
- Public parks (check local ordinances first)
- Beaches (most allow it; check rules)
- Old homesteads and farms (get written permission)
- **Never detect on National Parks, monuments, or protected historical sites** — federal law prohibits it
