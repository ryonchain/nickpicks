## What to Look for in a Women's Fitness Tracker

### Cycle Tracking Quality
Look for temperature-based tracking (wrist or skin temperature sensors), not just period logging. Temperature data combined with HRV gives you cycle phase detection that improves over time. The Fitbit Sense 3, Apple Watch, and Oura Ring all offer temperature-based cycle insights.

### Sleep Analysis Depth
Sleep quality is more impactful than most fitness metrics. Look for trackers that report sleep stages (light, deep, REM), not just total hours. HRV during sleep is an especially valuable metric.

### Comfort for All-Day and Sleep Wear
If you won't wear it while sleeping, you miss the most valuable data. A silicone band that's too stiff, a watch case that's too heavy, or a clasp that digs in while lying down will reduce compliance. Lightweight bands and slim profiles matter more than spec sheets suggest.

### Battery Life Expectations
Smartwatches (Apple Watch): charge nightly. Dedicated fitness trackers (Fitbit, Garmin): 5–10 days. Rings (Oura): 7 days. Choose based on your actual charging habits.

## FAQ

**Q: Do fitness trackers accurately track women's menstrual cycles?**
A: Temperature-based cycle tracking (Fitbit Sense 3, Apple Watch, Oura) is more accurate than simple logging. Over time, these systems learn your patterns and improve predictions. They're not medically reliable for contraception, but for health awareness, they're excellent.

**Q: Are fitness trackers safe to wear while pregnant?**
A: Wearable fitness trackers are generally considered safe during pregnancy. Avoid devices that use electrical impedance (like smart scales in BIA mode). Garmin and Apple both offer dedicated pregnancy tracking modes.

**Q: Can I wear a fitness tracker in the shower?**
A: All trackers in this roundup are water-resistant to at least 5 ATM (swim-proof). The Oura Ring is rated to 100m.

**Q: What's better for women — a fitness tracker or a smartwatch?**
A: Depends on priorities. Dedicated trackers (Garmin Lily, Fitbit Sense) have longer battery life and focus on health metrics. Smartwatches (Apple Watch) are more useful as daily wrist computers but need daily charging.
