## Creatine Buying Guide

**Monohydrate is enough.** Creatine HCl, Kre-Alkalyn, and "buffered" versions are marketed as superior but show no meaningful advantage in research. Stick to monohydrate and redirect the savings to food.

**Micronized vs. standard:** Micronized mixes better and is gentler on the stomach for most people. If you mix creatine in a blender bottle or shake, standard works fine. If you drink it straight in water, go micronized.

**Price per serving is what matters:** A $60 tub that lasts three months is identical in chemistry to a $15 tub that lasts the same time. Compare price per 5g serving.

**Flavored creatine:** Flavored products add sugars, sweeteners, or dyes. They're fine, but you're paying for flavoring — the creatine itself is the same.

## FAQ

**Is creatine monohydrate the best form of creatine?**
Yes — creatine monohydrate is the most researched form with the strongest evidence for strength and muscle gains. Newer forms like HCl and buffered creatine offer no proven advantage and cost significantly more per serving.

**Do I need to load creatine?**
Loading (20g/day for 5–7 days) saturates muscle stores faster but is optional. A standard 3–5g daily dose reaches full saturation within 3–4 weeks. Most people skip loading and take a consistent daily dose.

**What is micronized creatine?**
Micronized creatine monohydrate has been milled into smaller particles for better mixability and faster dissolution. It has the same effectiveness as standard creatine monohydrate but mixes more easily in water.

**Should I take creatine on rest days?**
Yes — creatine works by saturating muscle stores over time, so daily dosing on rest days maintains those stores. Timing matters less than consistency.
