## What to Know Before Buying an Espresso Machine

**Pressurized vs. non-pressurized portafilter.** Pressurized portafilters (De'Longhi Stilosa, Mr. Coffee) are forgiving but limit shot quality. Non-pressurized (Breville Bambino) extract better shots but require a consistent grind.

**You need a burr grinder.** Pre-ground "espresso" from a bag is inconsistent and goes stale quickly. A $50–$70 burr grinder dramatically improves any semi-automatic machine.

**Pod machines vs. semi-automatic.** Pods win on convenience and consistency. Semi-automatic wins on cost-per-shot and quality ceiling. Choose based on whether you want a hobby or a tool.

**Pressure numbers are marketing.** 15 bar vs. 19 bar means little in practice — optimal extraction happens around 9 bar and machine construction matters more than the rated max pressure.

## FAQ: Espresso Machine Questions

**Do I need to buy a separate grinder?**
For the best results with a semi-automatic machine, yes. A baratza Encore or similar entry-level burr grinder runs $80–$120 and makes a meaningful quality difference.

**How much does it cost per shot vs. café?**
Ground espresso costs $0.15–$0.40 per double shot. A Nespresso capsule is ~$1.00. A café espresso is $3.50–$5.00. Break-even on a $150 machine vs. daily café habit: roughly 6 weeks.

**What espresso machine should I upgrade to later?**
The Breville Barista Express (built-in grinder, $500) or Gaggia Classic Pro ($400) are natural next steps for enthusiasts.
