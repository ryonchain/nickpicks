## What Sensitive Skin Actually Needs After Shaving

Shaving removes the top layer of skin cells alongside the hair. For sensitive skin, this leaves the barrier compromised and the nerve endings exposed. The right aftershave does three things:

1. **Reduces inflammation:** Actives like aloe, chamomile, allantoin, and bisabolol calm the redness and swelling response
2. **Repairs the barrier:** Glycerin, ceramides, and emollient oils rehydrate and seal the skin surface
3. **Skips irritants:** Alcohol, synthetic fragrance, menthol at high concentrations, and dyes are common aftershave ingredients that worsen sensitive skin reactions

The classic cologne-splash aftershave is the worst choice for sensitive skin. Alcohol content of 60–70% stings, dries, and often causes the post-shave redness that men attribute to the shave itself.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Alcohol-free is the first filter:** For sensitive skin, eliminate any aftershave with alcohol (ethanol, isopropanol, or SD alcohol) in the first five ingredients. Witch hazel extracts at low percentages are acceptable.

**Fragrance-free vs. natural fragrance:** "Fragrance" on an ingredient list means synthetic fragrance blend — a common allergen. Natural essential oils (listed by botanical name) are generally better-tolerated. Fragrance-free is the safest option for reactive skin.

**Balm for dry skin, gel for oily:** Balms and creams provide more intensive moisture repair. Gels absorb faster and work better for oily or combination skin that doesn't need heavy moisture after shaving.

**Apply immediately after rinsing:** The 30–60 seconds after rinsing off shaving cream is when the skin is most receptive to soothing actives. Don't delay aftershave application.
