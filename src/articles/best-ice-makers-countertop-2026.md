## Buyer's Guide: Choosing Your Ice Maker

**Nugget vs. Bullet Ice:**
- Nugget ice (soft, chewable) is ideal for sodas, cocktails, and smoothies. More expensive.
- Bullet ice (hollow cylinder) is good for cooling drinks quickly. Budget-friendly.
- Full cube or crescent ice makers exist but are less common in portable form.

**Questions to Ask:**
- How many pounds of ice do you need per day? For a couple: 10–15 lbs is fine. For parties: 30+ lbs.
- Do you want Wi-Fi scheduling? The GE Opal 2.0 is the only widely available model with app control.
- Are you putting it in an RV or moving it often? Choose the lightest model with a compact footprint.

**Maintenance Tips:**
- Run the self-cleaning cycle once a week with citric acid solution.
- Empty and dry the bin if the unit won't be used for 2+ days.
- Scale buildup is the #1 cause of failure — descale every 2–3 months with a 1:1 water/white vinegar mix.

## FAQ

**Do countertop ice makers need to be drained?**
Yes — water left sitting in the reservoir can develop mold. Most units have a drain plug. If leaving unused for 2+ days, drain and dry.

**Are countertop ice makers worth it?**
Yes, if you regularly run out of ice or entertain. Producing a batch in under 10 minutes is a genuine quality-of-life improvement over ice cube trays.

**Does the ice go back to water if the bin is full?**
Yes. Most countertop ice makers recycle excess ice — melted water drains back into the reservoir and refreezes.
