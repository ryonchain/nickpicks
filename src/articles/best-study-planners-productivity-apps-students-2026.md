## Why Physical + Digital Beats Either Alone

Neuroscience research consistently shows that writing by hand improves memory encoding compared to typing. Using a physical planner for weekly review and planning — then a digital app for daily task capture and reminders — gives you the best of both approaches.

The physical planner sets the week's priorities. The app manages the details (deadlines, reminders, subtasks). The combination reduces cognitive load and makes it harder to forget assignments.

## Building Your Student Productivity System

### The Weekly Setup (15 Minutes Every Sunday)
1. Open your physical planner — fill in the week's deadlines, classes, and commitments
2. Review Todoist — confirm all upcoming deadlines are captured
3. Set your 3 Most Important Tasks (MITs) for the week

### Daily Flow (5 Minutes Each Morning)
1. Open your physical planner — review today's schedule
2. Check Todoist — what's due today?
3. Set a Forest session for your first study block

### The Pomodoro Structure
- 25 minutes focused study (Forest running)
- 5-minute break
- After 4 cycles: 20-minute longer break

Research shows this rhythm outperforms marathon sessions and reduces burnout over the semester.
