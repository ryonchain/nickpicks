## Two Dehydrator Designs

### Box/Shelf Dehydrators (Horizontal Flow)
- Fan at the rear pushes air horizontally across all trays simultaneously
- Consistent drying on every tray—no rotation needed
- Better for thick items (jerky slabs, whole mushrooms, fruit halves)
- Takes more counter/cabinet space (wider, less tall)
- Examples: Excalibur, Cosori, Magic Mill

### Stackable Round Dehydrators (Vertical Flow)
- Fan at top or bottom; air rises or falls through stacked rings
- Outer trays and inner areas may dry at different rates (rotation helpful)
- More compact storage (stack height adjustable)
- Good for thin slices, herbs, small fruit pieces
- Examples: Nesco, BioChef, Presto

**Winner:** Box dehydrators for performance; stackable for space-constrained kitchens.

## Key Specs to Compare

### Temperature Range
- Herbs need 95–115°F (high temp kills volatile oils)
- Fruit needs 130–135°F
- Jerky needs 160°F to meet USDA food safety standards
- Look for: **95°F–165°F adjustable range**

### Fan and Airflow
Rear-mounted fans (box dehydrators) outperform bottom/top-mounted fans in uniformity tests. The difference is measurable: outer tray vs. center tray temperature variance of ±3°F (rear fan) vs. ±15°F (stackable).

### Timer
A built-in timer lets you set the dehydrator overnight without monitoring. Essential for 8–12 hour jerky batches. Look for at least a 12-hour timer.

## Dehydrating Guide: Beginner Tips

### Essential Items for First Batches
1. **Mandoline slicer** — consistent 1/4" slices dry uniformly; inconsistent thickness creates some overdried pieces before others are done
2. **Parchment sheets** — cut to fit trays for fruit leather or sticky items
3. **Meat thermometer** — verify jerky reaches 160°F internal before removing

### Jerky in 8 Steps (Basic Recipe)
1. Buy lean beef (eye of round, top round) — remove all visible fat
2. Freeze 1 hour to firm up for easier slicing
3. Slice 1/8"–1/4" thick, with or against the grain (against = tender; with = chewy)
4. Marinate 4–24 hours (soy sauce, Worcestershire, garlic powder, black pepper)
5. Pat dry before dehydrating
6. Dehydrate at 165°F for 4–8 hours
7. Test: meat should bend without breaking; no visible moisture inside
8. Condition: rest in open container 24 hours before sealing (lets moisture redistribute)

### Storage Life
- Jerky: 1–2 months room temp; 6 months refrigerated; 1 year frozen
- Dried fruit: 6–12 months in airtight container
- Dried herbs: 1 year (use oxygen absorbers for maximum potency)
- Fruit leather: 4–6 weeks at room temperature
