## Food Dehydrator Buying Guide

### Tray Configuration
- **Round stackable:** More affordable, more compact. Minor airflow variation between top/bottom trays.
- **Box/square with rear fan:** More even drying. Professional models all use this design.

### Airflow Direction
- **Top/bottom fan:** Common in budget models. May require tray rotation.
- **Rear horizontal fan:** Even airflow through all trays simultaneously. No rotation needed.

### Temperature Range
- **95–115°F:** Herbs, flowers, and raw food diets
- **125–135°F:** Fruits and vegetables
- **145–165°F:** Meat and jerky (USDA recommends 160°F minimum for beef jerky)

### Capacity
Measured in square inches of drying surface. Most 4–6 tray models give you 28–47 sq in per tray.

### Noise
Rear-fan models tend to be quieter than top-fan budget models. If you're running an overnight batch, check reviews for noise levels.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a food dehydrator worth it?**
Yes — if you make jerky even a few times per year, the savings vs. store-bought jerky alone often pay back the purchase price. Dried fruits and homemade trail mix save significant money over pre-packaged alternatives.

**How long does dehydrated food last?**
Properly dehydrated and stored (vacuum sealed, cool, dark storage): 6 months to 2 years. Dehydrated jerky is typically good for 1–2 months at room temperature, longer if vacuum sealed.

**Can I use my oven as a dehydrator?**
You can — set it to the lowest temperature (usually 170°F+) and prop the door open. Results are less consistent than a dedicated dehydrator, and it uses significantly more energy.
