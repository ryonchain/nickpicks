## FAQ

**What is the difference between magnesium glycinate, citrate, and malate?**
Magnesium glycinate is chelated with glycine — highly bioavailable with a calming effect, widely used for sleep and anxiety. Magnesium citrate is bound to citric acid — good bioavailability with a gentle laxative effect, commonly used for digestive regularity. Magnesium malate is bound to malic acid — supports ATP energy production in the Krebs cycle, popular among those targeting energy and muscle function.

**What is the best form of magnesium for sleep?**
Magnesium glycinate is the most widely recommended form for sleep support. The glycine component is itself a calming amino acid that supports GABA receptor activity. Customers consistently report improved sleep quality and reduced nighttime restlessness with magnesium glycinate taken 1–2 hours before bed.

**How much magnesium should I take per day?**
The RDA for magnesium is 310–420mg/day for adults depending on age and sex. Supplemental doses commonly range from 100–400mg elemental magnesium per day. Note: check supplement labels for elemental magnesium content, not total compound weight — a 500mg magnesium glycinate capsule typically contains only 50–75mg elemental magnesium.

**Does magnesium cause digestive upset?**
Magnesium oxide and magnesium sulfate have the strongest laxative effect and are most likely to cause GI upset at high doses. Magnesium glycinate and magnesium malate are the gentlest on the digestive system. Magnesium citrate has a mild laxative effect that many find beneficial for regularity, but can cause loose stools at high doses.
