## Buying Guide: What to Look For

**Split vs. curved.** Fully split keyboards (Freestyle2) let you position each hand independently for maximum ergonomic benefit. Fixed curved keyboards (K860, Sculpt) angle the halves in a fixed position — less customizable but easier to adapt to.

**Wireless vs. wired.** Wireless is preferred for ergonomic keyboards because cable management shouldn't constrain your hand position. USB dongle keyboards (Sculpt) have more reliable connections than Bluetooth in busy wireless environments; Bluetooth keyboards (K860, Freestyle2 Bluetooth) are cleaner for multi-device setups.

**Wrist rest.** An integrated foam wrist rest (K860) is more convenient than a separate accessory. If a keyboard doesn't include one, add a gel wrist rest — $15–$25 makes a meaningful difference. A [standing desk mat](/articles/best-standing-desk-mats-2026/) under your feet completes the ergonomic setup if you alternate between sitting and standing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What makes a keyboard ergonomic?**
Ergonomic keyboards use split layouts, curved key arrangements, or negative tilt to keep your wrists in a neutral position during typing. This reduces ulnar deviation and wrist pronation — the two main causes of keyboard-related repetitive strain.

**Is the Logitech ERGO K860 worth the price?**
Yes — the K860 is the best sub-$100 ergonomic keyboard for most users. Its curved split design and integrated foam palm rest address the main causes of wrist fatigue with minimal learning curve.

**How long does it take to adjust to an ergonomic keyboard?**
Fixed curved keyboards like the K860 or Microsoft Sculpt take 2–3 days to feel natural. Fully split keyboards like the Kinesis Freestyle2 require about a week to reach comfortable typing speed.

**Can an ergonomic keyboard fix wrist pain?**
An ergonomic keyboard can reduce or eliminate keyboard-induced wrist pain within 2–4 weeks. It works best combined with proper desk height and monitor positioning.

**What is the difference between the Logitech K860 and MX Keys?**
The K860 has a curved split layout specifically designed for ergonomics. The MX Keys is a flat premium keyboard with excellent key feel and a low profile — more comfortable than a standard keyboard but without the split ergonomic design.
