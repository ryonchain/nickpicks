Cyber Monday 2026 falls on **December 1** — just 3 days after Black Friday. For TVs, it's a second bite at the same deals (or better), with most Black Friday prices extended and some online-exclusive discounts added. If you missed Black Friday, Cyber Monday is your best second chance at the year's lowest TV prices.

## When Do Cyber Monday TV Deals Start?

| Date | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| Black Friday (Nov 28) | Most deals already live |
| Weekend (Nov 29–30) | Prices hold; some restocking of sold-out models |
| Cyber Monday (Dec 1) | Online exclusives; some prices drop further |
| Cyber Week (Dec 2–7) | Many deals extend through the week |
| Last chance (Dec 15–20) | Final clearance before Christmas shipping deadlines |

**Key insight:** Cyber Monday for TVs is largely the same as Black Friday pricing — but it's your chance to grab models that sold out Black Friday weekend, and retailers sometimes sweeten deals with free soundbars or gift cards.

## Cyber Monday vs. Black Friday: Which Is Better for TVs?

| Factor | Black Friday | Cyber Monday |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Price | Usually the lowest | Usually matches BF |
| Inventory | Can sell out fast | Better stock after restocking |
| Online exclusives | Fewer | More |
| Free bundles (soundbar, gift cards) | Some | More common |
| Doorbusters | Yes (in-store) | No (online only) |

**Bottom line:** Prices are usually identical. Cyber Monday is better if you missed a sold-out model or want to shop entirely online. Black Friday is better for in-store doorbusters.
