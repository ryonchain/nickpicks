## Toaster Ovens in 2026: Countertop Cooking Has Grown Up

The modern toaster oven is no longer just a quicker way to reheat leftovers. The category now spans from $35 basic 4-slice models to $400 countertop convection ovens with air frying, dehydrating, and pizza modes that rival full-size ovens for most cooking tasks. The driving factor: energy efficiency. A countertop convection oven uses 50–80% less energy than a full-size range oven and preheats in 4–6 minutes vs. 12–15 minutes.

The dividing line in the market is **convection technology**. True convection (a dedicated fan circulating hot air) distributes heat evenly and reduces cooking time by 20–30%. Basic toaster ovens without convection rely on radiant heat from elements — adequate for toast, unreliable for baking. For anything beyond bread and reheating, convection is the essential upgrade.

## Toaster Oven Buying Guide

**Convection vs. no convection:** Convection circulates hot air for even heating and 20–30% faster cooking. For anything beyond toast and reheating, convection is worth the $30–80 premium.

**Capacity sizing:** 0.5 cu ft handles up to 4 people for basic tasks. 0.9–1.0 cu ft handles a family of 4 for baking and roasting. If you plan to replace oven use for meals, target 0.9 cu ft minimum.

**Air fry function:** True air fry requires a high-speed fan mode distinct from standard convection. Not all "convection" toaster ovens air fry well — look for models with a dedicated air fry preset and fan speed.

**Preheat time:** Full-size ovens take 12–15 minutes to preheat. Good toaster ovens preheat in 4–7 minutes. Infrared models (Panasonic) are instant. For weeknight cooking, preheat time matters.

**Counter space:** Large convection ovens (Breville, Cuisinart) require 18–22 inches of counter width. Measure before buying — many kitchens can't accommodate the largest models.
