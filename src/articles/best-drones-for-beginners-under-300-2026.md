## What to Look for in a Beginner Drone

**GPS vs. No GPS:** GPS drones hover in place when you let go of the sticks. Critical for beginners. Non-GPS drones drift in any breeze — much harder to control.

**Under 249 Grams:** In the US, drones under 249g don't require FAA registration for recreational use. DJI Mini 4K and Potensic Atom both hit this threshold.

**Flight Time:** Most beginner drones offer 20–34 minutes per battery. Buy an extra battery — you'll want it.

**Obstacle Avoidance:** Entry-level drones have basic downward sensors or none at all. Don't rely on it; fly conservatively.

**Camera Stabilization:** EIS (Electronic Image Stabilization) is standard. Mechanical gimbals (3-axis) produce dramatically smoother video — expect to pay $250+ for this feature at beginner level.

---

## FAA Rules for Beginners (Quick Summary)

- Drones under 249g: recreational use, no registration required
- Drones 250g and above: register with FAA ($5 fee), valid 3 years
- Never fly above 400 feet AGL
- Never fly within 5 miles of an airport without authorization
- Keep the drone in visual line-of-sight
