## Low-Cut vs. Mid-Cut vs. High Boot: Which Height?

### Low-Cut (Trail Runners)
- Lighter weight
- Better for flat, well-maintained trails
- Less ankle support
- Good: speed hiking, established trails, warm conditions

### Mid-Cut
- Balance of ankle support and weight
- Good for most terrain and conditions
- **Best for: 80% of hikers**

### High-Cut
- Maximum ankle support
- Heavier
- Good for heavy packs, off-trail, or weak ankles

## Waterproofing: GORE-TEX vs. Brand Proprietary

**GORE-TEX (GTX):** The gold standard. Independently tested, breathable waterproof membrane. Every GTX-labeled boot meets the same performance standard.

**Brand proprietary** (Merrell M-Select DRY, KEEN.DRY): Perform similarly in light to moderate wet conditions; often slightly less breathable than GORE-TEX. Usually found in lower-priced options.

**No waterproofing:** Appropriate for dry climates, fast-drying trail runners, or hikers who prefer to treat with DWR spray.

## How to Ensure the Right Fit

1. **Shop late in the day** — feet swell by afternoon, better representing your hiking condition
2. **Wear hiking socks** when trying on boots (not regular socks)
3. **Test downhill** — your toes should not hit the front on steep descents
4. **Lace tightly** — the heel should not move; ankle should feel supported but not constricted
5. **Walk on inclines** if possible in-store

## FAQ

**Q: Should I size up in hiking boots?**
A: Often yes — a half-size up is common to accommodate thick socks and swelling. Follow brand-specific guidance; KEEN often runs slightly small.

**Q: How do I waterproof hiking boots?**
A: Apply a DWR (Durable Water Repellent) spray like Nikwax to the exterior. Reapply every few months of use or when water stops beading on the surface.

**Q: How long do hiking boots last?**
A: 500–1,000 miles for most mid-range boots. Leather boots with resoling can last significantly longer.

**Q: Do I need hiking boots or can I hike in trail runners?**
A: Trail runners work for most day hikes on established trails. Boots provide better ankle support and protection for off-trail terrain, heavy packs, or rocky surfaces.
