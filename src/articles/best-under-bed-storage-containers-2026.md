## Under-Bed Storage in 2026: Unlocking 30+ Cubic Feet of Hidden Space

The space under a standard bed is 4–6 inches of clearance (platform beds) to 12–18 inches (traditional beds with legs). That's 30–80 cubic feet of prime storage space most households use for nothing except dust bunnies.

Under-bed storage categories:

1. **Rolling containers with wheels** — Easy extraction without bending, good for heavy items
2. **Flat rigid boxes** — Maximum capacity, stackable, require bending to access
3. **Zippered fabric bags** — Best for seasonal items, flexible sizing, collapse when empty
4. **Sliding drawers** — Most convenient access, integrated handle, limited height

**Quick verdict:** The **IRIS USA rolling box** is the best all-purpose under-bed storage — it slides out easily on wheels, holds a significant volume, and the clear lid lets you identify contents without opening. For seasonal items like holiday decorations or extra bedding, **Ziploc WeatherShield bags** are better because they compress, protect from moisture and pests, and don't require rigid clearance measurements.

## Buying Guide: Key Measurements

**Clearance height is everything.** Measure the exact clearance between your floor and the bottom of your bed frame before ordering. Common clearances:
- Platform beds: 4–6 inches — requires low-profile containers
- Beds with legs: 8–14 inches — accommodates most containers
- Tall beds / bed risers: 12–18 inches — works with virtually any container

**Measure twice, order once.** Most returned under-bed containers come back because they're too tall by 1–2 inches. Add your container height, leave ½ inch of clearance, and confirm the math before buying.

**What to store:**
- Seasonal clothing (best in airtight bags for pest protection)
- Extra bedding (comforters, duvet covers, pillow cases)
- Out-of-season shoes

## FAQs

**Do I need a dust cover for under-bed storage?**
If you use open boxes or bags, dust will collect on them over time. Rigid containers with lids protect contents adequately. For fabric bags, a simple wipe-down once per season is usually sufficient.

**Can under-bed storage damage my hardwood floors?**
Wheeled containers can scratch hardwood if wheels are hard plastic. Look for soft rubber wheels or felt pads on the base. Alternatively, place a thin rug or shelf liner under storage areas.

**How do I access items stored deep under the bed?**
Wheeled containers solve this entirely — they roll out in seconds. For non-wheeled options, use bed risers to increase clearance height, making containers easier to grab.
