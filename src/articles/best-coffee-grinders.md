## Coffee Grinder Buyer's Guide

### Burr vs. Blade: Why It Matters
Blade grinders chop coffee randomly, producing a mix of fine dust and large chunks. That uneven particle size causes simultaneous over- and under-extraction — the result tastes simultaneously bitter and weak. Burr grinders crush beans between two abrasive surfaces with a fixed gap, producing consistent particle sizes that extract evenly. Even a budget burr grinder ($80–$100) will produce meaningfully better coffee than a blade grinder.

### Conical vs. Flat Burrs
- **Conical burrs:** More forgiving, better at retaining flavor oils, good for espresso and medium grinds. Common in most home grinders.
- **Flat burrs:** More consistent particle size distribution, especially at coarse settings. Preferred by pour-over enthusiasts. Generally found in higher-end home grinders (Fellow Ode).

### Grind Size Guide by Brewing Method

| Brewing Method | Grind Size | Baratza Encore Setting |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------|
| Espresso | Extra fine | 5–8 (ESP variant) |
| Aeropress | Fine–Medium | 10–20 |
| Pour-over (V60) | Medium-fine | 15–20 |
| Drip coffee maker | Medium | 20–28 |
| French press | Coarse | 28–35 |
| Cold brew | Extra coarse | 35–40 |

### Retention: Why Low Retention Matters
Retention is the amount of ground coffee that stays inside the grinder instead of reaching your cup. High-retention grinders (0.5g+) waste coffee and cause flavor carryover when you switch beans. Single-dose grinders like the Fellow Ode (0.1g retention) minimize waste and cross-contamination.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I really need a burr grinder, or will a blade grinder do?
If you're making drip coffee with grocery store beans, a blade grinder is fine. But if you're buying specialty coffee (from a local roaster, Blue Bottle, Onyx, etc.) or brewing pour-over or espresso, a burr grinder will transform your results. The improvement is not subtle — it's the single highest-ROI upgrade in home coffee.

### What's the best coffee grinder for espresso under $300?
The Breville Smart Grinder Pro is the best espresso option under $300. It won't match a dedicated espresso grinder ($400+) but produces acceptable results for home espresso machines. For true espresso performance on a budget, the Baratza Encore ESP (~$200) is the community-standard recommendation.

### How often should I clean my coffee grinder?
Rinse the grounds container weekly. Fully disassemble and brush the burrs monthly. For electric grinders, use grinder-cleaning tablets (like Urnex Grindz) monthly — they absorb coffee oils and purge old grounds. Never use water on the burrs.

### Can I grind flavored coffee in a burr grinder?
Technically yes, but flavored coffee leaves oily residue on burrs that affects future grinds. If you grind flavored coffee, run two full cycles of plain beans afterward to purge the flavor, or dedicate a separate grinder to flavored beans.

### Is a $100 burr grinder noticeably better than a $300 one?
Yes — the jump from $0 (blade) to $100 is dramatic. From $100 to $200 is still significant. From $200 to $300 is noticeable to enthusiasts but subtle to casual drinkers. Beyond $300, improvements require careful side-by-side comparison to detect. For most home users, $150–$200 is the sweet spot.
