## Do Slow Feeders Prevent Bloat?

No feeder eliminates bloat risk entirely, but studies show eating speed is a significant contributing factor. Additional steps:
- **Feed twice daily** (smaller portions)
- **No vigorous exercise** 1 hour before and 2 hours after eating
- **Elevated feeders:** Controversial — some studies show elevated bowls increase bloat risk in large breeds. Ask your vet.
- **Know the signs:** Unproductive retching, distended abdomen, restlessness, rapid breathing = emergency vet immediately.
