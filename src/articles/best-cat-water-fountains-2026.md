## What to Look for in a Cat Water Fountain

**Noise level:** Cats are sensitive to sound. A fountain that whirs or gurgles in the background can deter shy cats from using it. Aim for < 35 dB for a bedroom-safe unit.

**Filter type:** Basic foam filters remove visible debris. Carbon filters remove odor and chlorine taste. Triple-action filters (carbon + ion exchange resin) produce noticeably better-tasting water, which increases how much your cat drinks.

**Capacity:** For one cat: 60–84 oz is fine. For two cats: 84–120 oz. For three or more cats: 120+ oz. Larger reservoirs mean less frequent refills and less risk of running dry.

**Material:** Plastic is cheapest but harbors bacteria in microscopic scratches over time. Stainless steel and ceramic don't scratch and stay cleaner longer. Cats prone to chin acne should avoid plastic.

**Pump accessibility:** You'll need to clean the pump weekly. Fountains where the pump is hard to reach accumulate slime faster. Look for models where the pump lifts out in one step.
