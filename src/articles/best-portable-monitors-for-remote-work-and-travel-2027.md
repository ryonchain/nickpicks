## How to Choose the Right Portable Monitor

### Size vs. Portability Trade-off

The sweet spot for most people is 14-16 inches. Below 14 inches, you're not gaining much over your laptop screen. Above 16 inches, the portability benefit diminishes fast. If you genuinely need the screen real estate and don't mind the extra bulk, the ViewSonic VX1755 at 17.3" is the outlier worth considering.

### Resolution Matters More Than You Think

A 1080p display at 15.6" looks noticeably softer than a QHD or higher resolution panel. If you're doing any detail work, budget for at least 2560×1440 or higher. Your eyes will thank you after a long work session.

### USB-C Is the Standard — But Verify Your Laptop's Output

Most modern laptops support video output over USB-C, but not all do. Verify that your laptop's USB-C port supports DisplayPort Alt Mode before purchasing a USB-C-only display. If it doesn't, look for models that include an HDMI input as a fallback.

### Brightness for Your Environment

Working in a dark hotel room? 250 nits is fine. Working near windows or outdoors? Look for 400 nits or higher. Anything below 300 nits will wash out in any direct light.
