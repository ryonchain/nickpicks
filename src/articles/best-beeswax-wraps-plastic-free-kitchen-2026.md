## Plastic-Free Kitchen Wrap Buying Guide

### Beeswax vs. Silicone: Which Should You Choose?

These are complementary, not competing products. Most zero-waste kitchens use both:

- **Beeswax wraps** are ideal for dry foods, produce, cheese, bread, and covering bowls. They're compostable at end of life — the most circular option available. Not suitable for raw meat or liquid-heavy foods.
- **Silicone bags** handle everything beeswax can't: raw meat, liquids, wet marinades, frozen items. They're dishwasher safe and extremely durable, but they don't biodegrade.

A complete plastic-free transition typically requires both types, plus a few good glass storage containers for the remainder.

### How to Care for Beeswax Wraps

Proper care dramatically extends the life of beeswax wraps:

1. **Wash in cool water only** — hot water will melt the wax coating off permanently
2. **Use dish soap sparingly** — a small amount is fine, avoid heavy degreasing soaps
3. **Air dry flat** — don't wring or bundle while wet
4. **Warm with your hands** before wrapping — the wax softens from body heat and forms to the shape
5. **Refresh in the oven** — if the wrap loses its tackiness over time, place on a lined baking sheet in a 150°F oven for 30 seconds to redistibute the wax

### What You Can (and Can't) Wrap

**Safe to wrap:**
- Cut vegetables and fruit
- Cheese and charcuterie
- Bread and baked goods
- Avocado (cut side down, seals the air out)
- Sandwiches and dry snacks
- Bowls and containers of non-liquid foods

**Do NOT wrap:**
- Raw meat (food safety — cannot sanitize beeswax wraps with hot water)
- Raw fish
- Very juicy fruits over delicate surfaces (may drip through)
- Hot food (the wax will melt off)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do beeswax wraps actually keep food fresh as long as plastic wrap?

For most applications, yes — and sometimes longer. The breathable nature of beeswax wraps can actually reduce condensation and mold compared to airtight plastic wrap, particularly for produce and bread. For applications requiring a genuine airtight seal (cut citrus, marinating meat), silicone bags perform better.

### Can beeswax wraps go in the dishwasher?

No. The dishwasher's hot water and heat will melt the wax coating off the fabric. Rinse in cool or room-temperature water only.

### How do I know when my beeswax wrap needs to be replaced?

When the wrap no longer sticks to itself or to containers, the wax has worn off. You can try a brief oven refresh (150°F for 30 seconds) to redistribute remaining wax. When the fabric starts to thin or develop holes, it's time to compost it and replace it.

### Are there beeswax wraps that are certified organic?

Yes — Bee's Wrap uses GOTS-certified organic cotton. Abeego uses certified organic materials as well. Check product listings for certification details, as not all brands verify their cotton sourcing.

### Can I put beeswax wraps in the compost?

Yes — beeswax wraps made with natural wax, tree resin, and organic cotton are fully compostable. They'll break down in a home compost pile or municipal composting. Silicone alternatives are not compostable but are extremely long-lasting and recyclable through specialty programs.
