## Feel and Firmness

**Casper Original:** Casper's zoned support foam places more pressure relief at the shoulders and firmer support under the hips. The result is a balanced medium-firm feel that works well for back sleepers, combination sleepers, and couples with different firmness preferences.

**Purple:** Purple's flagship product is the 2-inch hyper-elastic polymer grid — a grid of air channels that flex under pressure points (shoulders, hips) while providing more resistance where support is needed (lumbar). It feels unlike any other mattress: pressure-relieving but not "sinking" the way memory foam does. Combination sleepers who frequently shift positions respond well to it.

**Nectar Premier:** Nectar uses 4 inches of gel memory foam that conforms closely to your body. This creates a "hugging" sensation that side sleepers and pressure point sufferers love. The downside: movement can feel sluggish, and stomach sleepers may find it too soft.

---

## Cooling and Temperature Regulation

**Purple** is the clear winner. The grid structure allows air to circulate freely through the sleeping surface, making it the best option for hot sleepers. It stays neutral all night without relying on cooling gel alone.

**Casper** uses a layer of open-cell foam and a phase-change material cover. Performance is good — typical foam sleepers won't overheat. However, it doesn't match Purple's airflow.

**Nectar Premier** uses gel-infused memory foam. The gel helps dissipate heat better than traditional memory foam, but memory foam inherently traps some body heat. Hot sleepers may still experience warmth.

**Winner:** Purple for hot sleepers, by a significant margin.

---

## Motion Isolation

**Nectar** leads here — memory foam absorbs movement exceptionally well. Partners won't disturb each other during the night.

**Casper** offers good motion isolation, especially compared to spring mattresses. Slightly less isolating than Nectar due to its responsive foam layer.

**Purple's** grid can transfer some motion compared to memory foam, though performance is still solid.

**Winner:** Nectar for couples and light sleepers.

---

## Edge Support

All three have average edge support compared to traditional innerspring mattresses, which is typical for foam beds. Casper and Nectar perform slightly better at the edge than Purple.
