A dedicated DAC/amp combo costs $100–300 and unlocks the full performance of headphones you already own.

The PCM5102 DAC chip from Texas Instruments is a proven performer at this price tier.

Output power of 200mW at 32Ω handles the majority of popular headphones: Audio-Technica ATH-M50x (38Ω), Sony MDR-7506 (63Ω), and Beyerdynamic DT 770 Pro 80Ω version all perform significantly better from the E10K-TC than from a laptop jack. - Compact form factor fits on any desk

**Cons:**
- 3.5mm SE only — no 6.35mm (1/4") adapter included
- 200mW insufficient to properly drive 250–300Ω headphones at loud volumes

**Best for:** First-time DAC/amp buyers upgrading from laptop or phone output to 32–80Ω headphones.

## How to Match a DAC/Amp to Your Headphones

| Headphone | Impedance | Recommended Amp |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| ATH-M50x, MDR-7506 | 38–63Ω | FiiO E10K-TC |
| Beyerdynamic DT 990 Pro 80Ω | 80Ω | FiiO E10K-TC or K5 Pro |
| Beyerdynamic DT 990 Pro 250Ω | 250Ω | iFi Zen DAC V2 or K5 Pro |
| Sennheiser HD 650, HD 600 | 300Ω | iFi Zen DAC V2 or K5 Pro |
| Planar magnetics (Audeze, HiFiMAN) | 30–60Ω high current | Schiit Hel 2E or K5 Pro |

*General rule: Headphones over 150Ω require a proper amp. Below 80Ω, a good DAC matters more than amplifier power.*
