The choices break down into three categories: **wrist rest mouse pads** (gel or memory foam support pad attached), **large extended desk pads** (cover the full desk, absorb vibration), and **ergonomic contoured pads** (angle the wrist correctly). ## What to Look for in an Ergonomic Mouse Pad

**Gel vs. memory foam vs. hard surface:** Gel provides uniform cushioning; memory foam adapts to your shape but compresses slowly; hard surfaces reduce friction. Try gel first — it suits the widest range of users.

**Size matters:** A pad smaller than your full mouse movement range forces you to grip and reposition, increasing wrist strain. For standard DPI (400–800): get at least a 12" surface. For gaming or large monitors: 18"+ or a full desk pad.

**Wrist neutral posture:** Your wrist should be flat or very slightly elevated — never bent upward. Wrist rests are to park your wrist during pauses, not to rest on while actively mousing. The best ergonomic habit is to move your whole forearm, keeping your wrist relatively still.

**Anti-slip base:** A pad that slides creates micro-corrections that fatigue your wrist over time. Rubber-backed pads stay put.

**Surface material:** Cloth surfaces provide control but absorb oils and require washing. Hard surfaces are easy to clean but feel different. Extended desk pads protect the desk surface and reduce arm contact with sharp edges.
