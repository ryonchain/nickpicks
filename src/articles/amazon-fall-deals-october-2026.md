# Amazon Fall Deals October 2026 — Big Deal Days, Prime Exclusive Discounts

*As an Amazon Associate, I earn from qualifying purchases.*

Amazon's October sale event — now formally called **Big Deal Days** — is the second-largest shopping event after Prime Day. Historically scheduled for early-to-mid October, Big Deal Days gives Prime members access to deep discounts on electronics, home goods, and Amazon devices before the holiday shopping rush begins.

---

## What Is Amazon Big Deal Days 2026?

Big Deal Days is Amazon's fall Prime member sale event, launched in 2022. It runs for 48 hours and mirrors the Prime Day format: Lightning Deals, Prime Exclusive Discounts, and category-wide markdowns.

**2025 dates:** October 8–9, 2025
**2026 estimated dates:** October 7–8, 2026 (historically first or second week of October)

Key differences from Prime Day:
- Slightly smaller deal pool than Prime Day (which is in July)
- More focused on back-to-holiday gift categories
- Amazon devices (Echo, Kindle, Ring) always see the deepest discounts of any sale event

## How to Prepare for Amazon Big Deal Days 2026

### Step 1: Get Amazon Prime (Required)

Big Deal Days deals are Prime members only. A Prime membership costs $14.99/month or $139/year. You can also start a 30-day free trial.

[Start Amazon Prime Free Trial →](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=amazon+prime+membership&tag=nickpicks-20){rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank"}

### Step 2: Build Your Wish List Before October 7

Add everything you're considering to your wish list starting September 15. When the sale starts, you can quickly scan what's discounted.

### Step 3: Set Deal Alerts

Use Amazon's "Watch this deal" feature on Lightning Deals you want. You'll get a notification when they go live — these deals sell out in minutes.

### Step 4: Price Check with CamelCamelCamel

Before buying, verify the sale price is actually a discount. Amazon inflates some "original" prices. CamelCamelCamel shows the full 90-day price history.

### Step 5: Compare to Walmart and Target

During Big Deal Days, Walmart and Target run competing sales. On electronics especially, check all three before checking out.
