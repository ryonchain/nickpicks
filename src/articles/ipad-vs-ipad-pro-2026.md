## Display: The Biggest Real-World Difference

The iPad Pro's OLED Ultra Retina XDR display is genuinely better than the LCDs in the standard iPad and iPad Air — in two specific ways that matter:

**1. Black levels and contrast:** OLED produces true blacks by turning off pixels. The iPad's LCD always has some backlight bleed. For video watching in dark rooms, the Pro's display is noticeably better.

**2. ProMotion 120Hz:** The iPad Pro's adaptive refresh (1–120Hz) makes scrolling look smoother and drawing with Apple Pencil feel more immediate. If you draw or write with Pencil, this matters significantly.

For everything else — photos, web browsing, reading, social media — the standard iPad display is excellent and most people can't tell the difference in normal use.

**When display upgrade matters:**
- Professional photo or video editing
- Precision drawing/illustration
- Medical or scientific visualization
- Film/TV consumption in dark environments

**When display upgrade doesn't matter:**
- Reading, web browsing, light video
- Kids' use
- Presentations
- Note-taking

## Performance: M4 vs A14 — How Big Is the Gap?

**iPad (10th gen):** A14 Bionic — the chip from iPhone 12 (2020). Still fast for everything most users do: web, video, apps, Office, casual games.

**iPad Air (M2):** M2 chip from 2022 MacBook Air. Significantly faster than A14. Handles intensive tasks like LumaFusion video editing, Procreate for large files, and AI tasks without breaking a sweat.

**iPad Pro (M4):** Apple's most powerful chip — faster than most laptop processors. Neural Engine at 38 TOPS. The M4 won't be a performance bottleneck for any app available in 2026.

**Real-world impact:** Unless you're editing 4K ProRes video, running AI image generation apps, or using a professional app with desktop-class rendering (like Vectornator or DaVinci Resolve for iPad), you won't notice the difference between M2 and M4 in daily use.

The A14 in the standard iPad is the one that can feel slow — specifically in multi-app scenarios, large Procreate canvases, and complex spreadsheet editing.

## Magic Keyboard Compatibility

All iPads support Magic Keyboard, but there are version differences:

- Standard iPad and iPad Air use the standard Magic Keyboard for iPad
- iPad Pro (11" and 13") uses the new Magic Keyboard for iPad Pro — a more substantial keyboard with larger trackpad and function row

If you're considering iPad + Magic Keyboard as a laptop replacement, the iPad Pro combo is noticeably better (the keyboard is actually good). The iPad + keyboard combo is functional but feels like a compromise.
