# Best Cordless Power Tool Sets Under $200 (2026 Combos Reviewed)

Whether you're setting up your first workshop or replacing a worn-out set, a cordless power tool combo kit gives you the essentials without the cord clutter. The best combo kits under $200 bundle a drill, impact driver, and sometimes a circular saw — all powered by the same battery platform so you never end up with a drawer full of incompatible chargers.

This guide breaks down the top picks by brand, use case, and what you actually get for the money.

---

## What to Look for in a Cordless Tool Set

**Battery voltage:** 18V/20V MAX tools handle most home and light construction tasks. Higher voltage (60V) is for heavy trade use.

**Battery capacity (Ah):** 2.0Ah batteries are fine for occasional use; 4.0Ah or 5.0Ah give longer runtime between charges.

**Included tools:** Most budget combos include a drill/driver and impact driver. A circular saw or reciprocating saw adds major value.

**Bare tool vs. kit:** Some listings are "bare tools" (no battery). Always confirm batteries and charger are included.

**Brand ecosystem:** Buying within one brand (DeWalt, Milwaukee, Ryobi) means batteries are interchangeable across their entire lineup.

## FAQ

**Q: Is DeWalt 20V the same as DeWalt MAX 20V?**
Yes. DeWalt branded both as 20V MAX — the batteries are fully interchangeable across the line.

**Q: Can I use Milwaukee batteries on Ryobi tools?**
No. Each brand uses proprietary battery connections. Pick one ecosystem and stick with it.

**Q: What's the difference between a drill/driver and an impact driver?**
A drill/driver is for drilling holes and driving screws slowly with precision. An impact driver adds rotational hammering action — it's faster for driving long screws and lag bolts.

**Q: Do combo kits include a warranty?**
DeWalt and Milwaukee offer 3-year limited warranties on tools, 1-year on batteries. Ryobi offers 3 years on tools and batteries.
