## Frequently Asked Questions

**What travel accessories do remote workers actually need?**
The highest-impact travel accessories are a universal charging adapter, a noise-canceling headset for calls in loud environments, and a compact USB hub. A portable monitor and foldable laptop stand upgrade productivity significantly if you work from a laptop full-time.

**Are portable monitors worth it for remote workers?**
Yes if you regularly work from hotel rooms, co-working spaces, or Airbnbs for more than 2–3 days. A 15.6-inch portable monitor doubles your screen real estate for $150–200 and fits in most laptop bags.

**What is the best noise-canceling headphone for remote work travel?**
The Sony WH-1000XM5 leads the category with industry-best ANC, 30-hour battery, and call quality that handles airports and coffee shop background noise. It's the standard recommendation at $350.

**What should I look for in a travel USB hub?**
Look for a compact hub with at least 2 USB-A ports, HDMI or DisplayPort output, and USB-C Power Delivery passthrough. For travel, compact size and a short built-in cable matter more than port count.

**How do I charge all my devices internationally without a power strip?**
An international travel adapter with built-in USB-A and USB-C ports (like the Anker 727) handles most device charging directly without a separate power strip. Add a compact GaN charger for laptops that draw more than 45W.
