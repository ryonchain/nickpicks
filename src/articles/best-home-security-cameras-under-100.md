## What Makes a Great Security Camera Under $100?

Before diving into picks, here's what separates the keepers from the duds:

- **Resolution**: 1080p is the floor in 2026. Some budget cameras now hit 2K.
- **Night vision**: Color night vision is worth paying for — black-and-white is harder to use as evidence.
- **Storage**: Local microSD or subscription-free cloud beats a required monthly plan.
- **Motion alerts**: AI-powered human/vehicle detection cuts false alerts dramatically.
- **Power source**: Wired cameras are more reliable; battery cameras offer placement flexibility.

## Installation Tips

**Wired vs battery:** Battery cameras are simpler to install and relocate, but you'll charge them every 2–6 months. Wired cameras need an outlet nearby but never need charging.

**Placement matters:** Corner mounting gives the widest view angle. Keep cameras under eaves for weather protection, even on IP-rated cameras.

**WiFi range:** Most cameras require a strong 2.4GHz signal. A WiFi extender or mesh node near the camera helps with outdoor placements far from your router.

**Local storage vs cloud:** Local microSD storage is free forever but can be stolen with the camera. Cloud storage survives theft but requires a monthly fee.
