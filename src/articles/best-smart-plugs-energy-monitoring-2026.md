## Smart Plug Buyer's Guide

### Energy Monitoring: Worth It?

Energy monitoring smart plugs cost $5–10 more than basic ones, but they reveal surprising power draws. Common discoveries:
- **Space heaters:** 1,000–1,500W — expensive to run continuously
- **Old refrigerators:** 150–400W — monitoring can justify an upgrade
- **Gaming PCs:** 300–600W at load vs. 20W idle
- **Phone chargers at standby:** <1W — virtually nothing

If you have appliances you suspect are running up your bill, energy monitoring pays for itself quickly.

### Voice Assistant Compatibility

- **Alexa + Google Home:** All models in this guide
- **Apple HomeKit:** Wemo Insight (native), Kasa EP25/KP125M (via Matter)
- **Matter:** Kasa EP25, KP125M — future-proof, works across all ecosystems

Matter is the new universal smart home standard. If you're building a smart home from scratch or expect to switch ecosystems, Matter-certified plugs are worth the slight premium.

### Surge Protection in Smart Plugs

Only a few smart plugs include true surge protection. Most standard smart plugs (including all in this guide) use MOV-based protection that handles minor spikes but not major surge events. For sensitive electronics (TVs, computers, audio equipment), use a dedicated smart power strip with surge protection instead of a standard smart plug.

### Setup Ease

All modern smart plugs use 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi (not 5 GHz — confirm your router broadcasts 2.4 GHz if you have a dual-band setup). Most apps set up in under 5 minutes. Matter-based plugs require an initial app setup, then work natively in Apple Home, Google Home, or Amazon Alexa without the original app.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do smart plugs work without Wi-Fi?

Smart plugs require Wi-Fi to respond to app commands and voice assistants. However, schedules programmed in the app often run locally — meaning your lights will still turn on/off at scheduled times even if internet goes down.

### Can smart plugs reduce electricity bills?

Yes, indirectly. Energy monitoring reveals high-draw devices, and scheduling turns off devices that don't need to run overnight (TVs left on standby, coffee makers, phone chargers).

### Are smart plugs safe for high-wattage devices?

Most smart plugs are rated 15A (1875W). Space heaters, air conditioners, and other high-draw appliances near this limit are technically supported but should be verified against the plug's spec. Never use a smart plug with a device that exceeds the rated wattage.

### What's the difference between Matter and Zigbee/Z-Wave?

Zigbee and Z-Wave are mesh protocols that require a hub (SmartThings, Hubitat). Matter is a Wi-Fi/Thread-based standard that works directly with Alexa, Google, and Apple Home without a hub. For most homeowners, Matter is simpler.
