## What to Look for in an Apartment Smart Lock

**Installation:** Most apartment deadbolts are standard single-cylinder. Confirm your deadbolt is standard before buying. Some buildings have mortise locks (different format) — check first.

**Landlord approval:** Get written permission. Smart locks replace the deadbolt hardware — more involved than a thermostat swap.

**Keypad vs app-only:** A keypad gives you a backup when your phone dies. Always choose keypad + app combos for an apartment.

**Wi-Fi vs Bluetooth:** Wi-Fi locks connect directly to your router — remote lock/unlock from anywhere. Bluetooth-only locks require being nearby. For remote access, choose Wi-Fi.

**Guest codes:** The biggest apartment benefit. Create time-limited codes for dog walkers, cleaners, or guests. Revoke instantly from the app.

**Auto-lock:** Essential. Set the lock to auto-engage 30 seconds after door closes. Never leave for work with an unlocked door again.

## FAQ

**Can I install a smart lock in a rental apartment?**
With landlord permission, yes. Retrofit locks (August, Level Bolt) are least invasive. Full deadbolt replacements require more explicit approval.

**What happens when the battery dies?**
Most smart locks include a 9V battery terminal on the exterior keypad for emergency power. Keep a 9V battery in your go-bag.

**Are smart locks less secure than traditional locks?**
Quality smart locks (Schlage Grade 1) are as secure mechanically as traditional deadbolts. The added risk is app/cloud security — choose brands with strong encryption track records.
