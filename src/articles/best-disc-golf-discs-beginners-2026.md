# Best Disc Golf Discs for Beginners in 2026

Disc golf is the fastest-growing outdoor sport in North America — the PDGA (Professional Disc Golf Association) added over 700,000 members in the last three years alone. The appeal: free to play at most courses, easy to learn, and playable at any age. Beginners make one critical mistake: buying overstable drivers they can't throw. Slower arm speeds produce less spin, which means understable discs (designed to fly straight or turn right for right-handed backhand throwers) perform dramatically better for new players.

**Beginner starter kit:** 1 putter + 1 mid-range + 1 fairway driver.

---

## Flight Ratings Explained

All discs are rated on four numbers:
- **Speed:** How fast the disc must be thrown (1–14). Beginners: under 7.
- **Glide:** How long the disc stays aloft (1–7). Higher = better for beginners.
- **Turn:** Tendency to curve right during high-speed flight (positive = straight/left, negative = right turn). Beginners want -1 to -3 for understable flight.
- **Fade:** End of flight curve to the left (0 = straight, 5 = sharp left). Beginners want 0–1.

## What to Avoid as a Beginner

- **Any disc with speed 10+:** You won't have enough arm speed. These discs will "understable crash" immediately — flying hard right and landing 50 feet to your right.
- **Distance drivers marketed as "for beginners":** No distance driver is actually for beginners. These marketing claims are misleading.
- **Overstable discs (positive Turn, Fade of 3+):** These are for experienced players with high arm speeds.
