## Smart Chargers vs. Traditional Chargers

**Smart chargers (trickle/float)** detect battery voltage and apply charge only when needed. They can't overcharge a battery — when full, they switch to maintenance mode. Safe to leave connected for weeks or months. Ideal for storage and keeping a battery at peak health.

**Traditional chargers** apply constant amperage and must be disconnected when charging is complete to prevent overcharging. Better for quick recovery of completely dead batteries but require attention.

**The most useful home tool:** A 1–5A smart charger handles 95% of residential scenarios: seasonal maintenance for a motorcycle, keeping a second car topped off, or recovering a battery left discharged over winter.

## Buyer's Guide

### What Amperage Do You Need?

| Use Case | Recommended Amps |
|----------|-----------------|
| Seasonal storage (motorcycle, RV) | 1–1.25A |
| Daily driver maintenance | 1–5A |
| Recovering dead battery overnight | 5–10A |
| Large truck/marine battery | 10A+ |
| Emergency jump + charge | 30A with boost mode |

### Battery Types Supported

| Battery Type | What It Is | Charger Requirement |
|--------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Wet/flooded | Standard lead-acid | Any smart charger |
| AGM | Absorbed glass mat (sealed) | AGM-mode required |
| Gel | Sealed, gel electrolyte | Gel-mode required |
| EFB | Enhanced flooded (start-stop vehicles) | EFB-mode required |
| LiFePO4 | Lithium iron phosphate | Lithium-mode required |

Most modern smart chargers (NOCO Genius series) support all types. Budget chargers (Battery Tender Plus) typically cover standard and AGM only — verify before buying a lithium motorcycle battery or EFB start-stop battery.

### When to Replace vs. Recondition a Battery

NOCO's REPAIR mode (desulfation) can recover batteries that are reading 2–11.9V. A battery below 2V or one that won't hold a charge after a full reconditioning cycle should be replaced. Signs of a dead battery that won't recover: voltage stays below 10V despite extended charging, capacity drops sharply after a full charge, or the battery is more than 5–7 years old.
