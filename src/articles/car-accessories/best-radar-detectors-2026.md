## Radar Detector Legality Note

Radar detectors are legal in all 50 US states for private passenger vehicles on public roads (federal law). They are illegal in Virginia and Washington D.C. for all vehicles. Military bases and Canada prohibit them. Commercial vehicles (trucks over 10,000 lbs GVWR) are prohibited from using radar detectors by federal law. Verify local regulations before purchase.

## Radar Detector Buying Guide

### Understanding Radar Bands

Police radar operates on specific frequency bands:
- **K-band (24.125 GHz)**: Used by most US police agencies
- **Ka-band (33.4–36.0 GHz)**: Wide-band used by state troopers, most common for modern speed guns
- **X-band (10.525 GHz)**: Older systems, also used by automatic door openers (causes false alerts)
- **Laser**: Instant-on, brief detection window — best countered with laser jammer (separate product)

All premium detectors cover all bands. K-band and Ka-band coverage are most critical in the US.

### GPS False Alert Filtering: Essential for Urban Use

Modern automatic door openers, adaptive cruise control systems in other cars, and radar-based speed signs emit K-band signals that trigger false alerts on detectors without filtering. GPS filtering marks these locations and mutes repeat false alerts after you've passed them 2–3 times. In suburban and urban environments, GPS filtering is the difference between a usable detector and an unusably noisy one.

### Laser Detection vs. Laser Jamming

Radar detectors detect laser in real time — but laser guns are instant-on and aimed at a specific vehicle, so detection often comes as you're already tagged. Laser detection is an alert that you've already been hit, not advance warning. Laser jammers (Blinder, AntiLaser Priority) are separate devices that actively jam the laser signal — they're illegal in some states. Verify legality before considering a jammer.

## FAQ

### Will a radar detector prevent all speeding tickets?

No. Radar detectors provide advance warning when a radar source is detected ahead. Instant-on radar guns and laser are much harder to defend against — the window between detection and the gun timing you is very small. The detector is a tool for awareness, not a guarantee.

### What about apps like Waze or Radarbot?

Waze crowdsources speed trap alerts from users — it's free and useful. It works differently from a hardware detector: Waze alerts you to known locations other drivers have reported, while a radar detector actually detects the radar signal in real time. The best setup combines both: a detector for real-time signals and a crowdsourcing app for known locations.

### Is a $40 detector from Amazon worth buying?

Generally no. Budget detectors under $100 have short detection range, poor false alert filtering, and will false alert continuously in urban environments (making them more annoying than useful). The minimum functional price point is ~$150 for the Uniden R3.
