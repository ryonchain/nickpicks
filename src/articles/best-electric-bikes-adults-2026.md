## How to Choose an Electric Bike

**Motor type:**
- **Hub drive (rear):** Most common, lower cost, adequate for flat-to-moderate terrain
- **Mid-drive (crank):** Uses bike gears for efficiency — better for hills and range, higher cost

**Motor power:**
- **250–350W:** Light commuting, flat terrain, Class 1
- **500–750W:** Hills, cargo, Class 2–3 speeds
- **1,000W+:** Off-road, high speed (often not street-legal)

**Range factors:** Range varies widely by rider weight, terrain, assist level, and temperature. Subtract 20–30% from advertised range for real-world riding with hills.

**Class guide:**
| Class | Top Speed (assist) | Throttle | Best Use |
|-------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Class 1 | 20 mph | No | Bike trails, paths |
| Class 2 | 20 mph | Yes | General commuting |
| Class 3 | 28 mph | Sometimes | Road commuting |

**What's included:** A good e-bike should include fenders, integrated lights, and a rear rack. Buying these separately adds $100–$300 — factor into price comparisons.

## E-Bike Maintenance Tips

1. **Charge regularly, not fully:** Keep battery at 20–80% for longest life — avoid storing fully depleted or fully charged
2. **Keep the drivetrain clean:** E-bike motors add stress to chains — clean and lube every 100–200 miles
3. **Check tire pressure weekly:** Low pressure increases rolling resistance and battery drain
4. **Store indoors in winter:** Cold degrades Li-ion battery capacity — bring the battery inside if storing the bike in a cold garage
5. **Annual professional service:** Brake bleed, wheel truing, and drivetrain inspection once per year
