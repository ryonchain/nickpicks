## What to Look for in a Home Safe

**Fire Rating:** Look for UL Classified ratings at 1700°F for 30–60 minutes. If you store digital media, you need a safe rated to keep internal temps below 125°F — paper survives higher temps than hard drives.

**Water Resistance:** Often overlooked, waterproofing matters in flood-prone areas or homes with sprinkler systems. ETL Verified water ratings are the benchmark to trust.

**Access Method:** Biometric is fastest under stress but requires battery power. Keypad is reliable. Always choose a safe with a physical key override for emergencies.

**Anchoring:** Even a heavy safe should be bolted down. Most residential safes can be carried or carted away if not anchored. Pre-drilled mounting holes make this straightforward.

**Capacity:** Measure what you need to store before buying. Most people underestimate their storage needs and wish they'd bought one size larger.
