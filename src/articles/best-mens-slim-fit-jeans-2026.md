## Men's Slim Fit Jean Buying Guide

**Fit terminology:**
- **Slim:** Fitted through seat and thigh, tapered leg opening (13–15"). The standard.
- **Skinny:** Very close-fitting throughout. Narrow leg opening (11–13"). More fashion-forward.
- **Athletic slim:** Roomier through seat/thigh, still tapers to slim leg. Best for muscular builds.
- **Slim straight:** Slim fit that doesn't taper — consistent width from thigh to ankle.

**Stretch vs. rigid:**
- **Rigid (no stretch):** Better fade development, more structured look. Less comfortable for long sitting.
- **Stretch (1–3% elastane):** More comfortable for all-day wear, holds shape well, slightly less fade character.
- **Super stretch:** Most comfortable, tends to bag at knees over time.

**Wash selection for versatility:**
- **Dark indigo/rinse:** Dressiest — can substitute for chinos.
- **Medium wash:** Most versatile — casual but not beat up looking.
- **Light/distressed wash:** Casual only. Not office-appropriate.
- **Black:** Surprisingly versatile — can dress up or down.

**Inseam length:**
- Most men need 30–32" inseam for slim jeans (they hit the shoe without breaking much)
- Tapered slim jeans should just skim the top of the shoe
- No-break or slight break (rather than full stack) looks best with slim silhouettes
