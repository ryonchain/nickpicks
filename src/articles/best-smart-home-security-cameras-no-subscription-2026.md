## What "No Subscription" Actually Means

Security camera companies use "subscription" to mean different things. Here's what to look for:

**Local SD card storage:** The camera writes video to an SD card you insert. No cloud required. Access clips via the manufacturer's app over Wi-Fi. Best for standalone camera setups.

**NVR (Network Video Recorder):** A dedicated box on your home network stores footage from multiple cameras simultaneously. Best for multi-camera setups where you want continuous recording.

**NAS (Network Attached Storage):** For tech-savvy users — cameras write to a home server. Maximum control and storage capacity.

**Free cloud clips:** Some cameras offer a short clip history (24–72 hours) for free, with paid tiers for longer history. Free tiers often work fine for most home users.

## How to Store Footage Without a Subscription

### SD Card (Simplest)
Insert an SD card (64GB–256GB recommended) into the camera's slot. Most cameras loop-record — they overwrite the oldest footage when full. Works for 1–3 cameras. Typical cost: $15–$30 for a quality SD card.

### NVR System (Best for Multi-Camera)
A Network Video Recorder connects to multiple cameras and stores footage on a dedicated hard drive. Reolink, Amcrest, and Hikvision all offer bundled NVR kits (4-camera or 8-camera) starting around $200–$400 for the entire system including cameras.

### Home NAS (For Tech Users)
If you already have a Synology or QNAP NAS, ONVIF-compatible cameras (like Amcrest) can write directly to your NAS over your home network. Scales to as many terabytes as you want.

## Motion Detection vs. 24/7 Recording

**Motion detection only** (most cameras default): Records a clip when motion is detected. Misses events between triggers. Lower storage usage — SD cards last weeks.

**24/7 continuous recording**: Records every second. Catches everything. Requires more storage — SD cards fill in days, NVRs or large HDDs needed for longer retention.

For most home security use cases, motion-triggered recording with pre-buffer (the 5 seconds before the trigger) is sufficient and the most practical choice for SD card setups.
