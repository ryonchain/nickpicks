## What to Look for in a Prenatal Vitamin

Before choosing, understand these non-negotiables:

### Methylfolate vs. Folic Acid

This is the most important distinction in prenatal vitamins. Around 40% of women have a genetic variant (MTHFR) that reduces the body's ability to convert synthetic folic acid into active folate.

- **Methylfolate (L-methylfolate)** — the active, directly-usable form. Better for everyone.
- **Folic acid** — synthetic form that requires conversion. Adequate for most women but not optimal.

If you're unsure of your MTHFR status, choose a vitamin with methylfolate. It's effective for everyone.

### Key Nutrients to Check

| Nutrient | Recommended Daily Amount |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Folate (methylfolate) | 400–800 mcg |
| Iron | 27 mg |
| Calcium | 200–300 mg |
| Vitamin D | 600 IU (many experts recommend 1,000–2,000 IU) |
| DHA (Omega-3) | 200–300 mg (often separate supplement) |
| Iodine | 150 mcg |
| Choline | 450 mg (most prenatals are low on this) |

## Gummies vs. Capsules vs. Softgels

| Form | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Capsules | Best nutrient density, includes iron | Can cause nausea on empty stomach |
| Gummies | Easy to take, good for nausea | No iron, more sugar |
| Softgels | Good DHA delivery, easy to swallow | Harder to control other nutrient forms |
| Tablets | Cheapest, widely available | Harder to swallow, lower bioavailability |

**Recommendation:** Capsules or softgels if you can tolerate them. Gummies if you struggle with nausea or pill swallowing — just add a separate iron supplement.

## When to Start Prenatal Vitamins

**Ideally: 3 months before trying to conceive.** Neural tube development (folate-critical) begins in weeks 3–4 of pregnancy — before most women know they're pregnant. If you're actively trying, start now.

If you just found out you're pregnant: start immediately, even if you're in the first trimester.
