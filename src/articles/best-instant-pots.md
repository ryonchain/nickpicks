## Multi-Cooker vs Dedicated Appliance

**Multi-cooker (Instant Pot, Ninja Foodi):** One appliance handles pressure cooking, slow cooking, steaming, sautéing, and often air frying. Best if counter space is limited or you want versatility.

**Dedicated rice cooker (Zojirushi, Cuckoo):** One appliance, one function — but done perfectly. Fuzzy logic rice cookers adjust temperature and time based on rice type, water ratio, and detected moisture. The result is consistently better rice than any multi-cooker.

**Rule of thumb:** If you cook rice 4+ times per week and care about quality, get a dedicated rice cooker. For everything else, a multi-cooker handles the versatility.

## Key Specs Explained

**Pressure levels:** Most Instant Pots operate at 10–12 PSI (pounds per square inch). This reduces cooking time by 60–70%. "High" and "Low" pressure settings matter for delicate ingredients.

**Capacity:** 3 Qt for 1-2 people; 6 Qt for 3-6 people (most popular); 8 Qt for large families or batch cooking.

**Programs:** "7-in-1" means 7 preset cooking programs (pressure cook, slow cook, steam, sauté, yogurt, keep warm, rice). More presets are convenient but manual cooking achieves any of them.

**Air fryer lid (Ninja Foodi):** A separate lid attachable to the cooker base that circulates hot air. Same crispy results as a standalone air fryer in the same appliance. The lid must be swapped out manually between pressure and air frying functions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is an Instant Pot actually faster than a slow cooker?**
Dramatically. Instant Pot reduces most slow cooker recipes by 75-80%. Beef stew: 8 hours slow cook → 45 minutes Instant Pot. The results are similar for soups and stews.

**Can I use an Instant Pot as a regular slow cooker?**
Yes. The slow cook function works, but it runs slightly hotter than a traditional Crock-Pot. Reduce liquid by 25% and reduce time by 30% for best results.

**How much electricity does an Instant Pot use?**
About 700–1000W during pressure cooking, but cycles on and off during the process. A typical 45-minute recipe uses approximately 0.2–0.4 kWh — much less than an oven running for 3 hours.

**Is the Ninja Foodi worth the premium over Instant Pot?**
If you regularly make dishes that benefit from browning after pressure cooking (ribs, chicken, roasts), yes. If you mostly make soups, stews, and beans, the $80 Instant Pot is adequate.
