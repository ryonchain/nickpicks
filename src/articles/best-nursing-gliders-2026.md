## What to Look for in a Nursing Glider

Before choosing, understand what actually matters after 3 AM:

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Arm height | Arms too low = neck strain; arms at elbow level = no pain |
| Seat depth | Shallow seat = sliding forward; deep seat = can't reach the ground |
| Back height | Low back = no head support for dozing off; high back = safe for naps |
| Lumbar support | Breastfeeding puts strain on the lower back for 20–40 min per session |
| Ottoman included | Essential — elevating feet reduces back and hip pressure |
| Washable covers | Guaranteed spills; removable covers save the chair |
| Easy to exit | C-section recovery, night exhaustion, and holding a sleeping baby all require chairs you can get out of one-handed |

## Glider vs. Rocking Chair vs. Recliner

| Type | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Glider | Smooth motion, quieter than rocker, less floor space | No locking position |
| Rocking Chair | Classic, good for soothing | Can catch toes/feet underneath, louder creak |
| Recliner | Full body support, can sleep in it | Larger footprint, usually heavier |
| Swivel Glider | Pivots to reach items on both sides | Some swivel mechanisms wear quickly |

**Best for most people:** A **swivel glider with an ottoman**. The swivel lets you pivot toward a nursing pillow, side table, or nightstand without getting up. The ottoman elevates your feet to take strain off your lower back.
