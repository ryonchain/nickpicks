## Platform First, Then Tools

The most important power tool decision is **which battery platform to commit to** — your batteries and chargers only work with tools from the same brand (with a few exceptions). Once you buy a DeWalt battery, it works in every DeWalt 20V MAX tool. The same goes for Milwaukee M18, Makita 18V LXT, and Ryobi ONE+.

**Choose your platform based on:**

- **Homeowner / DIYer:** Ryobi ONE+ — largest tool selection (300+ tools), cheapest platform to expand, batteries are reasonably priced. Good enough for 95% of home projects.
- **Serious DIYer / part-time tradesperson:** DeWalt 20V MAX — strong value per tool, excellent availability at Home Depot, proven durability. The most popular platform in the US.
- **Professional tradesperson:** Milwaukee M18 FUEL or Makita 18V LXT — M18 FUEL has the strongest motor performance; Makita 18V LXT has the broadest professional tool catalog. Pick based on your trade.

## What to Watch For

**Combo kits vs. bare tools:** If you're starting a new platform, combo kits with batteries are the best value. If you already have batteries, bare tool bundles save you $40–$100 per tool versus kits with batteries you don't need.

**Brushed vs. brushless:** Brushless motors run cooler, last longer, and extract more power from the same battery. Add ~$30–$60 per tool for brushless; worth it for tools you use heavily.

**Battery Ah (capacity):** More Ah = longer runtime per charge. 1.5Ah is enough for occasional home use; 4–5Ah is what professionals want for all-day work.

**Lightning Deals:** Power tool Lightning Deals sell out fast. Have your items wishlisted the day before and check early on both July 13 and July 14.
