## Switch Guide: Which Feel Is Right for You?

### Linear (Smooth, No Click)
- **Examples:** Cherry MX Red, Gateron Red, Akko V3 Cream
- **Best for:** Gaming, quiet offices, fast typists
- **Sound:** Low thud

### Tactile (Bump, No Click)
- **Examples:** Cherry MX Brown, Gateron Brown, Boba U4
- **Best for:** All-around typing and gaming
- **Sound:** Moderate thud

### Clicky (Tactile + Audible Click)
- **Examples:** Cherry MX Blue, Kailh Box White
- **Best for:** Writers who love feedback
- **Sound:** Loud click — not for shared offices

## Key Terms Explained

**Hot-swap:** Switch sockets that let you pull switches out and replace them without soldering. Highly recommended — it's the difference between a keyboard you can customize and one you're stuck with.

**PBT keycaps:** A harder plastic that resists shine and wear over time. Better than ABS keycaps.

**N-key rollover:** Every keystroke is registered simultaneously, even when many keys are pressed at once. Matters for gaming.

**Gasket mount:** The PCB rests on silicone gaskets rather than being screwed directly to the case — results in a softer, bouncier typing feel.
