## What Makes a Good Swim Goggle?

### Anti-Fog Coating

Every good goggle has an anti-fog coating — the question is how long it lasts. The coating degrades each time you wipe the lens. Rinse your goggles in cold water after every swim; never wipe the inside of the lens.

### Seal Type

| Seal Type | Best For | Trade-off |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Silicone gasket | Recreational, most swimmers | Slight suction; comfortable for 60+ minutes |
| Hard lens/no gasket | Competition | No drag; less comfortable for long sessions |
| Foam padded | Beginners, triathletes | Extra comfort; degrades faster |

### Lens Color

| Lens Color | Best Conditions |
|-----------|----------------|
| Clear | Indoor pool, low light |
| Smoke/Gray | Bright outdoor conditions |
| Mirrored | Open water, high sun |
| Blue | All-purpose; reduces glare without going dark |
| Yellow/Orange | Low light, dawn/dusk open water |

## Swim Goggle Maintenance

| Action | Frequency | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Rinse in cold water | After every swim | Removes chlorine that degrades anti-fog |
| Air dry (don't wipe) | After every swim | Wiping destroys the anti-fog coating |
| Rinse before swimming | Before every swim | Activates the anti-fog coating |
| Replace anti-fog | Every 2–3 months | Anti-fog sprays extend coating life |
