A smart plug is the fastest, cheapest way to add home automation to devices that aren't smart natively. Plug it between the outlet and any appliance — a lamp, fan, coffee maker, space heater, or window AC — and you gain remote control, scheduling, and (on some models) energy monitoring. No rewiring. No electrician. No hub required.

The catch: smart plugs vary enormously in reliability, ecosystem compatibility, and energy monitoring capability. Here are the best options for every type of smart home in 2026.

## Smart Plug Use Cases: What to Automate First

### High-impact automations

**Lamps and lighting:** Schedule lamps to turn on at sunset and off at midnight. Works with any floor lamp or table lamp — no smart bulbs required.

**Coffee makers:** Schedule your coffee maker to start brewing 10 minutes before your alarm. Any drip machine with an on/off button becomes a smart coffee maker.

**Space heaters and window AC units:** Schedule heating/cooling to start 30 minutes before you wake up or return home. A 1500W space heater costs ~$0.18/hr — smart scheduling prevents wasteful all-day heating.

**TV and entertainment systems:** Create a "Movie Night" routine that turns on the TV, soundbar, and ambient lighting simultaneously.

### Energy monitoring use cases

Plug energy monitors into high-draw appliances to baseline their cost:
- Space heater: 1500W = ~$1.80/day at $0.12/kWh
- Window AC: 900–1400W = ~$1.10–$1.70/day
- Gaming PC: 200–500W = ~$0.25–$0.60/day
- Mini fridge: 100W avg = ~$0.29/day

Use this data to set auto-shutoff timers on devices you forget to turn off.
