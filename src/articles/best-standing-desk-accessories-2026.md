## Ergonomic Setup Guide

**Monitor position:** Top of the screen at eye level (or slightly below). Distance: arm's length from your face.

**Standing posture:**
- Feet shoulder-width apart, slight bend in knees
- Hips over ankles, not tilted forward
- Wrists straight, elbows at 90° or slightly below keyboard

**Sit-stand ratio:** Research suggests 1:1 or 2:1 (sit:stand) — alternating every 30–60 minutes is better than standing all day. Most powered standing desks have timer reminders built in.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a standing desk mat necessary?**
Yes, on hard floors. Standing on tile or hardwood without a mat causes significant lower back and leg fatigue within an hour. Even a $30 flat mat makes a measurable difference.

**Can I use my existing desk with a monitor arm?**
Most desks have clamp-mounting on any desk edge 1"–4" thick. Check that your desk is thick enough — most desks qualify, but very thin IKEA desktops sometimes don't.

**Do I need a dedicated cable management system or will velcro ties work?**
Velcro ties work for the desk surface. For runs to the floor and to wall outlets, a raceway or spine looks significantly better and is worth the small investment.
