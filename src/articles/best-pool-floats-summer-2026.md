## Types of Pool Floats

**Lounge floats:** Flat or slightly reclined surface for lying and relaxing. The classic pool float. Best for sunbathing.

**Giant inflatables:** Novelty shapes (flamingos, swans, pizza slices, etc.). Large enough for 1-2 people, designed as much for photos as function.

**Float loungers with cup holders:** More practical lounge floats with built-in holders for drinks and phones. Good for real all-day pool use.

**Swim rings / tubes:** Classic inner tube style. Smaller, easier to store, good for active play.

**Island platforms:** Large flat platforms that multiple people can sit on simultaneously. Best for groups.

## Pool Float Durability: What to Look For

**Gauge (vinyl thickness):** Measured in millimeters. Standard pool floats are 0.18-0.28mm. Better quality floats are 0.28mm+. More expensive floats often use 0.35-0.4mm vinyl that resists punctures significantly better.

**Valve type:** Twist valves hold air better than push-in plugs. Check reviews specifically for reports of slow leaks.

**Seam construction:** Heat-welded seams (not glued) hold longer. Most quality floats use heat welding.

**Size vs. portability:** Larger floats are more fun but harder to store deflated. The Funboy floats come in fitted bags; most standard floats pack loosely.

---

## How to Make Pool Floats Last

**Rinse after each use:** Chlorine degrades vinyl over time. A quick rinse with fresh water extends life significantly.

**Dry before storing:** Store deflated but dry — moisture inside encourages mold and weakens vinyl.

**Avoid sharp edges:** Keep away from pool coping edges, rough concrete, and pool toys with sharp edges.

**Partial inflation when not in use:** Store slightly inflated (30-40%) to reduce fold-line stress on vinyl — better than fully deflated.

**Repair kit ready:** A vinyl repair kit ($5) patches small holes before they become large ones.
