## FAQ

**What is power delivery pass-through on a USB-C hub?**
Power delivery (PD) pass-through means the hub can charge your laptop while you use it. A 100W PD hub takes one USB-C cable from a charger, passes the power through to your laptop, and simultaneously runs all the other ports. Without PD pass-through, using a hub disconnects your laptop from its charger — which matters on a thin laptop that drains fast under load.

**Do USB-C hubs work with all laptops?**
USB-C hubs work with any laptop that has a USB-C port, but not all USB-C ports support every feature. HDMI output requires your laptop's USB-C port to support DisplayPort Alt Mode — most modern laptops do, but some budget Windows laptops don't. Power delivery pass-through requires USB Power Delivery support. Check your laptop's USB-C port spec before assuming the HDMI or charging features will work.

**What is the difference between a USB-C hub and a docking station?**
A USB-C hub is bus-powered — it draws power from your laptop's USB-C port and requires no external adapter. It's compact, portable, and typically under $50. A [docking station](/articles/best-usb-c-docking-stations-2026/) has its own power brick, charges your laptop at higher wattages (85–100W), supports more demanding displays, and is designed to stay on a desk permanently. The hubs in this article bridge the gap: they include docking station features like HDMI, Ethernet, and SD card slots, but in a portable form factor.
