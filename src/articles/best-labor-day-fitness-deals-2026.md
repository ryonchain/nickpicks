## Why Labor Day Is the Best Time to Buy Fitness Equipment

**Summer clearance:** Retailers bring in fitness gear for spring/summer and need to move inventory before Q4. Labor Day weekend is the natural clearance window.

**Pre-fall spending:** Back-to-school spending winds down, and the next major spending wave (holidays) is months away. Retailers need to drive traffic with deals.

**Pattern over 3 years:**
- Treadmills: 20–35% off at major retailers
- Exercise bikes: 15–30% off (Peloton typically runs $150–$300 off)
- Dumbbells and kettlebells: $20–$50 off on Amazon
- Resistance bands, mats, foam rollers: 20–40% off
- Gym apparel: 30–50% off

## What NOT to Buy on Labor Day

**Kettlebells (cast iron):** These don't go on sale significantly — the metal is the cost. Standard price at Amazon is about right year-round.

**The latest Peloton model:** Labor Day deals are typically on previous generation. The newest model stays at launch price.

**Supplements and protein powder:** These don't go on sale for Labor Day specifically. Amazon runs supplement deals throughout the year — subscribe and save + coupon stacking is the better strategy.
