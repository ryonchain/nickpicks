## Buying Guide

**Portable vs. Stationary:** Portable tables fold and carry for mobile practices. Stationary tables are heavier, more stable, and often have electric height adjustment — ideal for a dedicated room.

**Foam Depth:** 2.5 inches is the professional minimum for client comfort in longer sessions. 3+ inches is preferred for deep tissue work where clients need to relax into the surface.

**Table Width:** Standard is 28 inches. Wider tables (30–32 inches) are more comfortable for larger clients and side-lying positions but are heavier to carry.

**Upholstery:** PVC is the most affordable and easy to clean. Eco-leather and Natursoft alternatives are softer, more breathable, and hold up better to daily sanitizing.

**Frame Material:** Aluminum is lightest (ideal for mobile therapists). Hardwood is more stable and feels more solid. For stationary tables, hardwood or steel base is preferable.

**Weight Capacity:** Professional minimum is 500 lbs. Therapists who work with bariatric clients should prioritize 750-lb+ capacity tables.
