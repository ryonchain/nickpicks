## Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Slow Cooker

### Capacity Guidelines

- **1–2 people:** 3–4 quarts
- **3–4 people:** 5–6 quarts
- **5+ people / meal prep:** 6–8 quarts

A slow cooker performs best when filled ½ to ¾ full. Too full risks overflow; too empty results in fast evaporation and potential burning.

### Manual vs Digital

**Manual (dial):** Simple, reliable, and no programming required. You turn it on and off yourself. Best for casual cooks who will be home while cooking.

**Digital:** Set a cook time and forget it — it shifts to Keep Warm automatically. Essential for workday cooking where you can't monitor the pot.

### Sear Capability

Browning meat before slow cooking significantly improves flavor through Maillard reaction. Most slow cookers require a separate pan for this step. If you sear regularly, choose the Ninja Foodi or a slow cooker with a stovetop-compatible insert to minimize cleanup.

### Temperature Probe

If you cook whole poultry, pork shoulders, and large roasts, a built-in probe that triggers Keep Warm at target internal temperature prevents the overcooking that ruins a 6-hour effort. The Hamilton Beach Set & Forget is the best budget option with this feature.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between "Low" and "High" settings?**
Both reach approximately 200°F — the difference is time to reach that temperature. High reaches cooking temperature faster (~2–3 hours) vs Low (~4 hours). "High" does not cook hotter, just faster, so 8 hours on Low generally equals 4 hours on High for most recipes.

**Can I leave a slow cooker on while I'm at work?**
Yes — that's the primary use case. Digital slow cookers with auto-warm are safest: they shift to a low-heat hold when cooking time ends, preventing overcooking and excessive evaporation. Older manual models require you to turn them off in person.

**Can I put frozen meat in a slow cooker?**
No — frozen meat keeps the cooker in the USDA "danger zone" (40°F–140°F) too long, allowing bacterial growth. Thaw meat in the refrigerator before slow cooking.

**Do I need to add liquid?**
Most meats and vegetables release moisture during cooking. Generally 1/2 to 1 cup of liquid is sufficient for a 6-quart pot. Too much liquid produces watery results — slow cookers don't evaporate moisture like stovetop cooking.
