## Buying Guide: What to Look for in Diffusers and Soy Candles

### Ultrasonic Diffusers: Key Specifications

**Tank Capacity = Runtime**  
This is the most practical spec to compare. Smaller tanks (100–200ml) run 3–6 hours and suit bedside or desk use. Medium tanks (300–500ml) run 6–10 hours for medium rooms. Large tanks (600ml+) run 12–20 hours and suit open-plan living spaces. Most people find a 300–500ml tank hits the sweet spot for daily home use.

**Mist Output: Cool Mist, Not Steam**  
All ultrasonic diffusers produce cool mist — not heat — which preserves the aromatic compounds in essential oils and is safe around kids and pets (with appropriate oils). Heat diffusers (candle-based diffusers, plug-ins) alter the chemical composition of oils and don't provide the same aromatherapy effect.

**Noise Level**  
Most ultrasonic diffusers operate at 25–35 dB — barely audible. If you're sensitive to white noise while sleeping, choose models marketed specifically as "whisper quiet" or check that the noise level is under 30 dB.

**Safety Features**  
Always verify auto shut-off when the tank empties — running a diffuser dry can damage the ultrasonic plate and create a fire risk. All reputable diffusers include this; avoid any that don't.

### Essential Oils: Safety and Quality
Not all essential oils are created equal — cheap oils cut with carrier oils or synthetic fragrance compounds won't diffuse effectively and may irritate sinuses. Look for "100% pure essential oil" with the botanical name listed (e.g., Lavandula angustifolia for lavender). Brands like Plant Therapy, NOW Foods, and Rocky Mountain Oils are well-regarded on Amazon.

Note: some essential oils are toxic to pets (tea tree, eucalyptus, and many citrus oils are dangerous for cats). Research before diffusing around animals.

### Soy Candles: What "Soy" Actually Means
Not all soy candles are pure soy. "Soy blend" candles may be 80% paraffin with 20% soy — barely different from a conventional candle. Look for **100% soy wax** (or coconut-soy blend, which is also clean-burning). Paraffin candles release toluene and benzene when burned — low levels, but avoidable with soy.

**Wick Material**: Cotton or wood wicks burn cleanest. Avoid metal-core wicks (though rare now after regulations tightened).

**Burn Time**: A quality soy candle should burn 1 hour per ounce of wax. A 9 oz candle claiming 40–50 hours is in the right ballpark; a 9 oz candle claiming 100 hours is either intermittently burned or marketing fiction.

**Fragrance Load**: Higher fragrance load (8–10%) = stronger scent throw. Look for candles that mention their fragrance load, or rely on verified buyer reviews mentioning scent throw in medium/large rooms.
