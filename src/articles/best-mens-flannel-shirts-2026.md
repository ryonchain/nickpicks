## Men's Flannel Shirt Buying Guide

**Cotton vs. wool flannel:**
- **Cotton flannel:** Most common, softer feel, machine washable, less warm than wool.
- **Wool flannel:** Warmer, naturally odor-resistant, moisture-wicking, lasts decades. Requires more careful washing.

**Weight categories:**
- **Lightweight (3–4 oz):** Better as a true shirt layer. Comfortable in mild fall temperatures.
- **Midweight (5–6 oz):** The all-purpose standard. Works as a shirt or light outer layer.
- **Heavyweight (7+ oz):** Best as a true outer layer in cold weather.

**Flannel styling for fall 2026:**
- **Flannel + dark jeans + work boots** — the classic
- **Flannel open over a tee + slim jeans + Chelsea boots** — smart casual
- **Flannel tucked into chinos + layer under a wool coat** — elevated professional
- **Flannel as an outer layer + base thermal layer + cargo pants** — outdoor utility

**Care tips:**
- Cotton flannels: machine wash cold, tumble dry low
- Wool flannels: hand wash or machine wool setting, lay flat to dry
- Pre-wash new flannels — they soften significantly with the first few washes
