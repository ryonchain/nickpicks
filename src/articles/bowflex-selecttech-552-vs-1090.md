## Head-to-Head: Where Each Model Wins

### Weight Range

**Winner: 1090** — 90 lbs vs 52.5 lbs ceiling is significant if you're an experienced lifter.

But: Most home gym users realistically max out around 50–60 lbs on dumbbells. If you're pressing 70 lbs per hand on dumbbell chest presses, you probably already know which model you need.

### Price

**Winner: 552** — The 552 is ~$80 cheaper per pair. Over a decade, both last equally long so the savings compound.

### Granularity / Fine Adjustment

**Winner: 552** — The 2.5-lb increments up to 25 lbs are better for progressive overload on lighter lifts. The 1090 only goes in 5-lb steps throughout its full range.

### Physical Size & Storage

**Winner: 552** — At 15.75" the 552 is 1.75" shorter than the 1090 per dumbbell. That's meaningful in a home gym where rack depth matters, and it also affects the feel on certain pressing movements.

### Best Value Overall

**Winner: 552 (for most buyers)** — Unless you're regularly pushing above 50 lbs per hand, the extra $80 and extra length of the 1090 don't pay off for the average home gym lifter.
