## Bowflex SelectTech 552: Specs

- **Weight range:** 5–52.5 lbs per dumbbell (15 weight settings)
- **Increment steps:** 2.5 lbs (up to 25 lbs), then 5 lbs increments
- **Dumbbell length:** 15.75"
- **Amazon price:** ~$349/pair — https://www.amazon.com
- **ASIN:** B001ARYU58

The 552 dials to weights in 2.5-lb increments up to 25 lbs, then jumps by 5 lbs to the max of 52.5 lbs. That granularity is ideal for progressive overload at the lighter end — important for shoulder exercises, isolation work, and beginners building a baseline.

## Bowflex SelectTech 1090: Specs

- **Weight range:** 10–90 lbs per dumbbell (17 weight settings)
- **Increment steps:** 5 lbs
- **Dumbbell length:** 17.5"
- **Amazon price:** ~$429/pair — https://www.amazon.com
- **ASIN:** B001ARYU2O

The 1090 gives you an extra 37.5 lbs of max weight per hand over the 552. The tradeoff: it's heavier when set to lower weights and the handle is 2 inches longer — that changes the feel on some lifts.

## Head-to-Head: Where Each Model Wins

### Weight Range

**Winner: 1090** — 90 lbs vs 52.5 lbs ceiling is significant if you're an experienced lifter.

But: Most home gym users realistically max out around 50–60 lbs on dumbbells. If you're pressing 70 lbs per hand on dumbbell chest presses, you probably already know which model you need.

### Price

**Winner: 552** — The 552 is ~$80 cheaper per pair. Over a decade, both last equally long so the savings compound.

### Granularity / Fine Adjustment

**Winner: 552** — The 2.5-lb increments up to 25 lbs are better for progressive overload on lighter lifts. The 1090 only goes in 5-lb steps throughout its full range.

### Physical Size & Storage

**Winner: 552** — At 15.75" the 552 is 1.75" shorter than the 1090 per dumbbell. That's meaningful in a home gym where rack depth matters, and it also affects the feel on certain pressing movements.

### Best Value Overall

**Winner: 552 (for most buyers)** — Unless you're regularly pushing above 50 lbs per hand, the extra $80 and extra length of the 1090 don't pay off for the average home gym lifter.

## Who Should Buy the 552

- Beginners building strength progressively
- Intermediate lifters (most exercises top out at 40–50 lbs)
- Anyone training upper body in isolation movements (lateral raises, curls, flyes)
- Home gym lifters who prioritize compact storage and want the option to [work from the sofa](/articles/best-lap-desks-sofa-working-2026/) between sets

---

## Who Should Buy the 1090

- Experienced strength trainers who regularly row or press 60+ lbs
- People who primarily do heavy compound accessory work (heavy dumbbell rows, hip hinges)
- Those who want to future-proof their dumbbell investment as they progress
