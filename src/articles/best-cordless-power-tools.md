## Reviews

### DeWalt DCK277C2 — Best Overall Starter Kit

The DCK277C2 includes a brushless drill/driver and impact driver with two 1.5Ah batteries and charger. DeWalt's 20V MAX platform is the most widely supported ecosystem in the US — your batteries work across 200+ tools. The brushless motors extend both tool life and battery runtime versus brushed alternatives.

**Pros:** Complete package at fair price; brushless motors; massive ecosystem; reliable brand; widely available at Home Depot.
**Cons:** 1.5Ah batteries are smaller — fine for light use, upgrade to 5Ah for heavy work.

**Verdict:** The right starting kit for homeowners. The 20V MAX ecosystem grows with your project list.

### Milwaukee M18 2997-23 Three-Tool Kit — Best Pro Kit

Contractors and serious DIYers choose Milwaukee M18. The 2997-23 includes a hammer drill, impact driver, and circular saw — a complete kit for framing, electrical, and general construction. POWERSTATE brushless motors deliver measurably more torque than DeWalt in side-by-side tests.

**Pros:** More torque than the competition; REDLINK electronics protect tools from overload; excellent ergonomics; pro-grade build quality.
**Cons:** Expensive; M18 batteries are heavier than 20V MAX; heavier tools overall.

**Verdict:** The pro ecosystem. Once you're on M18, you'll add tools for years.

### DeWalt DCD777C2 — Best Budget Drill/Driver

The DCD777C2 delivers brushless motor performance at a brushed price. It's the lightest brushless drill in the 20V MAX lineup at 2.0 lbs — significant for overhead work. Two-speed transmission (0–450 / 0–1,500 RPM) handles both fastening and drilling.

**Pros:** Lightweight; brushless at a budget price; two batteries included; 20V MAX compatibility.
**Cons:** Less torque than mid-range options; smaller 1.5Ah batteries limit runtime.

**Verdict:** The smart entry point into the DeWalt ecosystem if you want brushless for less.

### Ryobi PCL206K1 — Best Budget Platform

Ryobi's 18V ONE+ platform is the world's largest power tool battery platform — over 300 tools share these batteries. The PCL206K1 drill/driver with PCL245B impact driver is sold at Home Depot for around $100 combo price. Performance is adequate for weekend projects.

**Pros:** Cheapest full platform; ONE+ battery compatibility is unmatched; available everywhere.
**Cons:** Less torque and runtime than DeWalt/Milwaukee; plastic build feels less robust; not suitable for heavy-duty work.

**Verdict:** The right choice for renters, beginners, and light home maintenance. Don't use it for professional work.

### Makita XPH12T — Best Hammer Drill

When you're drilling into concrete, brick, or masonry, you need hammer drill mode — rotary percussion that breaks material with impact while drilling. The Makita XPH12T is the best cordless hammer drill for homeowners: 530 in-lbs of torque, 3-mode operation (drill/driver, drill, hammer drill), and compatibility with the 18V LXT ecosystem.

**Pros:** Best hammer drill performance in class; three-mode operation; excellent LXT ecosystem; durable cast-aluminum gear housing.
**Cons:** LXT batteries are more expensive than entry platforms; heavier than standard drills.

**Verdict:** The right tool for concrete anchors, tile backing boards, and masonry projects.

## Platform Guide: Which Ecosystem to Choose?

**DeWalt 20V MAX:** Best overall ecosystem for homeowners. Widely available, fair prices, huge selection.

**Milwaukee M18:** Best for professionals and serious DIYers who prioritize torque and build quality.

**Makita 18V LXT:** Strong in Japan-influenced precision tools; excellent for woodworkers.

**Ryobi 18V ONE+:** Best for beginners and budget builders. Don't use on demanding projects.

**Key insight:** Buy into ONE platform and stick with it. Battery cross-compatibility is the most valuable feature in power tools — having 4 batteries shared across 6 tools beats having 12 single-tool batteries.

## What to Buy First

1. **Drill/driver** — the most-used tool in every kit
2. **Impact driver** — for long screws and lag bolts (different torque delivery from a drill)
3. **Circular saw** — when you start cutting lumber
4. **Reciprocating saw** — demolition and rough cuts
5. **Oscillating tool** — precision cuts, scraping, sanding

## Bottom Line

Start with the **DeWalt DCK277C2 combo kit** — the 20V MAX ecosystem will serve you for a decade. Upgrade to **Milwaukee M18** when you're doing professional-grade work. Choose **Ryobi ONE+** only for light weekend tasks.
