Apple Watch vs Fitbit — these are the two most recognized names in wearable fitness technology. They serve very different users with very different priorities. Here's a direct comparison to help you decide.

---

## Quick Verdict

- **Buy Apple Watch** if: You have an iPhone and want the most comprehensive smartwatch with deep iOS integration, apps, calls, and premium health sensors.
- **Buy Fitbit** if: You want a focused fitness and health tracker with outstanding battery life, simpler UI, and lower cost — and you're less interested in full smartwatch features.

---

## Platform Compatibility

**Apple Watch:** iPhone-only. It does not work with Android smartphones. If you have an Android phone, Apple Watch is not an option.

**Fitbit:** Works with both iPhone and Android. Platform-agnostic — the Fitbit app is excellent on both operating systems.

**Winner:** Fitbit for cross-platform support. Apple Watch wins for iPhone users who want deep iOS integration.

---

## Health and Fitness Tracking

**Apple Watch Series 10** sensors: heart rate, ECG (electrocardiogram), blood oxygen (SpO2), temperature sensor, crash detection, fall detection, irregular heart rhythm notifications, sleep stages, menstrual cycle tracking.

**Fitbit Sense 2** sensors: heart rate, ECG (select markets), EDA (electrodermal activity/stress) sensor, blood oxygen (SpO2), skin temperature, sleep stages, daily readiness score, stress management score.

Both are excellent health trackers. Apple Watch's ECG and medical-grade sensors are best-in-class. Fitbit's daily readiness score and stress tracking (EDA sensor) are differentiators that Apple Watch lacks.

**Winner:** Tie — Apple Watch leads on raw sensor capability; Fitbit's daily readiness and stress features are genuinely useful health insights.

---

## Smartwatch Features

**Apple Watch:** Full app ecosystem (App Store), answer calls, respond to texts, Apple Pay, Siri, music controls, Wallet, streaming apps, third-party app notifications. It's a full mini-iPhone on your wrist.

**Fitbit:** Basic notifications (see texts and calls), contactless payments (Fitbit Pay), music controls, Alexa built-in on select models, limited app support. Not a full-featured smartwatch.

**Winner:** Apple Watch by a wide margin.

---

## Battery Life

**Apple Watch Series 10:** 18 hours typical, 36 hours in Low Power Mode. You'll charge nightly.

**Apple Watch Ultra 2:** Up to 60 hours.

**Fitbit Charge 6:** Up to 7 days.

**Fitbit Sense 2:** Up to 6 days.

**Winner:** Fitbit — dramatically better battery life. Not charging your tracker daily is a real quality-of-life difference.

---

## Sleep Tracking

Both brands track sleep stages (light, deep, REM, awake). Fitbit's sleep tracking has historically been best-in-class among consumer wearables, with detailed nightly analysis and a long track record. Apple Watch sleep tracking (watchOS 10+) has improved significantly and now tracks sleep stages.

**Winner:** Fitbit has a slight edge from years of refinement; Apple Watch has largely closed the gap.

---

## Price

**Apple Watch SE (2nd Gen):** ~$249 (entry-level Apple Watch)
**Apple Watch Series 10:** ~$399
**Fitbit Charge 6:** ~$120
**Fitbit Sense 2:** ~$200
**Fitbit Versa 4:** ~$170

**Winner:** Fitbit — costs significantly less at every comparable tier.

---

## Who Should Buy What

**Buy Apple Watch Series 10** if: You have an iPhone, want the most capable smartwatch available, regularly use apps on your wrist, and don't mind charging daily. Best health sensors + best smartwatch = best overall wearable for iPhone users.

**Buy Apple Watch SE** if: You want Apple Watch integration with your iPhone at a lower price. Core health features intact.

**Buy Fitbit Charge 6** if: You want an excellent fitness tracker with 7-day battery, great health features, and compatibility with any phone — at half the Apple Watch price.

**Buy Fitbit Sense 2** if: You want Fitbit's best health device including the EDA stress sensor, ECG, and premium sleep tracking.

---

## Bottom Line

If you have an iPhone and want a full smartwatch, Apple Watch is the clear choice — the depth of features and iOS integration is unmatched. If you have an Android phone, want long battery life, or mainly care about fitness tracking without smartwatch overhead, Fitbit wins. The price difference is real — Fitbit gives you strong health tracking for less money.
