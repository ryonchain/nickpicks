## What Matters in a Portable Bluetooth Speaker

**Sound quality** depends on driver size, acoustic chamber design, and DSP processing. Bigger speakers move more air — physics applies here. A 30W speaker with a 4-inch driver will always out-bass a 10W speaker with a 2-inch driver.

**Waterproofing** matters for outdoor use. IP67 means dust-tight and 1 meter submersion for 30 minutes — fully pool/beach proof. IP67 is the minimum for beach or boat use. IPX5/IPX6 handles rain and splashes but not submersion.

**Battery life** — real-world battery varies from rated numbers. Manufacturers test at 50% volume. At party volume (80-90%), expect 50-60% of rated life.

**Bluetooth codec**: SBC is universal; AAC sounds better on Apple devices; aptX is marginally better on Android. The speaker's driver and amplifier matter far more than codec for sound quality.

**Portability**: Weight and size matter more than specs on paper. A 2kg speaker requires thought about whether you'll actually carry it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How loud do outdoor Bluetooth speakers get?**
The JBL Charge 6 and Anker Motion X600 both reach 85-90dB+ — loud enough to fill a beach party area for 20-30 people. Smaller speakers like the Flip 7 handle 10-15 people comfortably.

**Can I use a Bluetooth speaker in the shower?**
IP67-rated speakers (Charge 6, SoundLink Max, Motion X600) can handle shower steam and direct splashing. Don't submerge them intentionally; IP67 allows brief submersion but is not designed for extended underwater use.

**How far does Bluetooth reach on portable speakers?**
Bluetooth 5.0+ has a spec range of 30+ meters, but walls and obstacles reduce this to 10-15 meters in real-world use. Most portable speakers won't drop connection within a house.

**Do portable speakers support stereo pairing?**
JBL Charge 6 and Flip 7 support stereo pairing via JBL PartyBoost (two identical speakers). Bose SoundLink Max supports stereo pairing via SimpleSync. Anker supports SoundSync with compatible Soundcore speakers.
