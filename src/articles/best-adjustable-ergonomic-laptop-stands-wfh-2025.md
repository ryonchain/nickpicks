## Buying Guide: What to Look for in an Ergonomic Laptop Stand

### The Core Ergonomic Principle: Eye Level = Screen Level
The top of your laptop screen should be at or just below eye level when you're sitting with proper posture (back straight, feet flat on floor). Most laptop stands achieve this by raising the screen 8–12 inches above desk level. If you're taller or shorter than average, look for stands with the widest adjustment range to dial in your exact ideal height.

### Height Adjustment: Preset Levels vs. Continuous
Most budget stands use preset notch heights (5–13 levels). These are perfectly adequate for home use where you sit in one position. For standing desk users who switch between seated and standing work throughout the day, look for stands with continuous or near-continuous height adjustment to quickly match your screen height as your desk moves.

### Stability and Weight Capacity
Check the maximum weight rating — especially important for 15"–17" gaming laptops that can weigh 5–8 lbs. Most quality stands support 22 lbs or more, far exceeding any laptop. The base design matters too: wider triangular or cross-base stands resist tipping better than narrow single-foot designs, especially when typing vigorously.

### Portability: Desk Only vs. On-the-Go
If you primarily work at one desk, a heavier, more stable stand like the Rain Design mStand or HUANUO is ideal. If you move between home, coffee shops, and coworking spaces, prioritize folding or telescoping stands like the Nexstand K2 or Nulaxy C3 that pack flat and weigh under 2.5 lbs.

### Ventilation and Heat Management
Laptop vents are usually on the bottom or sides. Stands that cradle the laptop from the sides (rather than covering the bottom) or use an open-frame design allow better airflow — important for laptops that throttle performance under heat. If you run CPU-intensive work (video editing, gaming), prioritize open-frame stands.

### Pairing With External Keyboard and Mouse
Once your laptop screen is at eye level, your laptop keyboard will be too high to type on comfortably. Budget $30–$60 for an external Bluetooth keyboard and mouse — this completes the ergonomic setup and turns your laptop into a full workstation.
