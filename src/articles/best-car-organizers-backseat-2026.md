## Buying Guide

**Attachment method:** Look for wide, padded headrest straps that grip securely without scratching the headrest hardware. Some cheap organizers use thin, slippery straps that gradually slide.

**Pocket quantity vs. quality:** More pockets isn't always better — oversized main compartments with a few well-sized specialty pockets (tablet, insulated cups) are more usable than 15 tiny pockets.

**Material durability:** Heavy-duty 600D polyester or nylon handles weight and daily use better than thin Oxford cloth. Check that pocket seams are reinforced.

**Anti-kick panel:** If children are in the back seat, an extended lower panel that protects the back of the front seat from kicks is essential.
