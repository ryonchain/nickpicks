## Sleep Mask Buying Guide

### Why Blackout Matters for Sleep
Melatonin production responds to light at the retinal level — even light through closed eyelids suppresses melatonin via the suprachiasmatic nucleus. Complete darkness during sleep:
- Increases melatonin duration
- Supports deeper sleep stages (N3 slow-wave, REM)
- Reduces cortisol interruptions during light phases
- Particularly important for shift workers sleeping during daylight

### Flat vs Contoured Masks
- **Flat masks:** Cheaper, more compact, press against eyelids, sufficient for darkness but may irritate
- **Contoured/3D cups:** Create an eye cavity, no lid pressure, true blackout, slightly bulkier
- **Weighted masks:** No containment of light, more about sensory pressure than blackout

### Strap Matters for Side Sleepers
- Standard elastic straps can press uncomfortably between the ear and pillow for side sleepers
- Velcro straps allow custom tension adjustment
- Some masks have top-head straps (like Manta's optional upgrade) that eliminate ear pressure entirely

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a sleep mask replace blackout curtains?**
A contoured mask provides complete blackout for the sleeper regardless of room lighting — it's more reliable than curtains because you take it with you traveling. For partners with different sleep schedules, a mask is often more practical than curtains.

**Will a sleep mask cause wrinkles or eyelash damage?**
Flat masks that press against the skin can increase friction on the delicate eye area. Contoured (3D cup) and silk masks minimize this risk. If you're concerned about skin friction, the Manta or Alaska Bear silk are the safest choices.

**How do I clean a sleep mask?**
- Silk masks: hand wash cold with gentle detergent, air dry
- Foam/polyester masks: spot clean or gentle machine wash cold, air dry only
- Weighted bead masks: check manufacturer instructions — most are machine washable on delicate
