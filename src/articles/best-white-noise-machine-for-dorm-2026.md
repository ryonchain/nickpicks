## White Noise vs Pink Noise vs Brown Noise: Which Works Best?

**White noise:** Equal energy across all frequencies — the classic "static" sound. Most effective for masking sudden sounds like slamming doors.

**Pink noise:** Weighted toward lower frequencies — a deeper, smoother sound than white noise. Many people find it more comfortable for extended listening.

**Brown noise:** Even heavier on low frequencies — deep rumble, like a strong wind or waterfall. Popular for deep focus and sleep for people who find white noise too sharp.

**Fan sounds:** Mechanically generated or electronically simulated fan noise — familiar, non-distracting, widely used for sleep.

**For dorm use:** Start with white noise (most effective at masking sudden sounds). If you find it harsh for extended sessions, try brown or pink. The LectroFan gives you all options to experiment.
