## What to Avoid

- **Metal-clasped hair ties** — the metal bar on traditional hair ties grabs and breaks hair at the point of contact. Switch to no-clasp elastics for any tie you'll use repeatedly.
- **Hard plastic headbands without grip lining** — hard plastic without velvet or rubber lining on the inner curve will slide backward within the hour. Grip lining is non-negotiable for headbands that need to stay in place.
- **Claw clips made from thin, brittle plastic** — the cheap plastic claw clips from fast-fashion brands snap under normal hair tension. The hinge mechanism fails first, then the teeth. Quality resin or acetate costs more per clip but lasts years vs. weeks.
- **Metallic or jeweled accessories with exposed metal edges** — sharp or rough metal edges on barrettes and hair clips catch and break individual hairs, particularly fine or color-treated hair.
