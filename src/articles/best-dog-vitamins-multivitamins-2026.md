## Vitamins Dogs Actually Need (vs. Hype)

**Evidence-supported:**
- **Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA):** Strong evidence for skin, joints, cognition, kidneys
- **Vitamin E:** Antioxidant; beneficial for dogs on high omega-3 doses or with oxidative stress
- **Vitamin D:** Deficiency possible in dogs with limited sun and dark coats; bloodwork can identify
- **B12:** Important for dogs with chronic GI conditions affecting absorption
- **Zinc:** Deficiency causes skin/coat issues; some breeds (Siberians, Malamutes) are predisposed

**Usually marketing:**
- **"Superfoods" complexes:** Kale, spirulina, turmeric in tiny amounts that don't reach effective doses
- **Vitamin C:** Dogs synthesize their own; supplementation rarely necessary
- **Proprietary blends:** Hide individual ingredient amounts, preventing meaningful comparison

## When Dogs Actually Benefit from Vitamins

✓ Homemade or raw diet (most common need)
✓ Dogs with chronic illness (IBD, kidney disease, EPI)
✓ Senior dogs (7+ years) with declining nutrient absorption
✓ Recovering from illness or surgery
✓ Dogs with specific diagnosed deficiencies via bloodwork
✓ Working dogs with extreme caloric demands

✗ Healthy adult dogs on quality commercial dog food
✗ "Just in case" supplementation without deficiency diagnosis
✗ Dogs whose food already includes targeted supplements
