## AI Detection Features: What to Look For

The biggest differentiator in 2026 cameras isn't resolution — it's AI classification. Every camera detects motion, but the best ones classify what triggered the alert:

- **Person detection**: distinguishes humans from animals, cars, or wind-blown branches
- **Package detection**: flags a box being placed or removed from your porch
- **Vehicle detection**: logs cars entering a driveway or parking area
- **Face recognition**: identifies household members vs. unknown visitors (premium tier)
- **Animal detection**: optional filter to reduce false alerts from pets

Subscription tiers matter: most cameras offer free person/motion detection but lock face recognition, extended clip history (30-day vs. 7-day), and cloud storage behind a monthly plan. Cameras with **local storage** (SD card or NAS) are the best value for people who don't want recurring costs.

## Buying Guide: Picking the Right Security Camera

### Wired vs. Battery-Powered
Wired cameras never run out of power and support 24/7 continuous recording. Battery cameras offer flexible placement and simple installation but require periodic recharging (every 1–6 months depending on activity). Solar panels solve the recharging problem for outdoor cameras with adequate sun exposure.

### Cloud vs. Local Storage
Cloud storage costs $5–$15/month per camera but is accessible remotely and survives if the camera is stolen. Local storage (SD card, NAS, or hub) has no recurring cost but may be lost if the device is stolen. The best setup uses both: local as primary, cloud as backup.

### Resolution and Detail
- **1080p**: Adequate for general area monitoring; faces at distance are marginal
- **2K**: Comfortable for doorbell and close-range identification
- **4K**: Required for license plate reading at 15+ ft or facial detail at distance

### AI Detection Accuracy
Test alert accuracy within your first 30 days. Better cameras reduce false alerts from motion (tree branches, headlights) by classifying the type of motion. Person + vehicle detection is the minimum threshold for a useful camera; package detection is increasingly standard at the mid-range.

### Privacy and Data
Read the privacy policy of any camera you install, particularly around law enforcement data sharing (relevant for Ring) and whether video is processed on-device or in the cloud. Cameras with local processing (Eufy) handle AI detection on-chip with no video sent to servers.
