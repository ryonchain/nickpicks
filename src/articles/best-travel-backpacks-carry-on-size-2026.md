## Travel Backpack Buying Guide

### Volume: how much do you actually need?

| Trip Length | Recommended Volume |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Weekend (2–3 nights) | 25–30L |
| 1 week | 35–40L |
| 2 weeks | 40–45L |
| 3+ weeks (washing clothes) | 40–45L |

Most one-bag travelers settle on 40L as the sweet spot — enough for 1–2 weeks of actual travel while still fitting standard carry-on size requirements.

### Opening style: front-load vs. top-load vs. clamshell

**Front-panel zip** (like Osprey Farpoint): Opens one full face of the bag. Better than top-load for access but you still can't lay it flat.

**Clamshell** (like Tortuga, Peak Design): Opens the entire bag into two flat halves like a suitcase. Best packing experience and easiest to find items.

**Top-load** (like Cabin Zero): Traditional hiking backpack style — you access items by reaching down from the top. Fine with packing cubes to organize layers, poor for finding specific items quickly.

For airport and hotel use, front-load or clamshell is strongly preferred over top-load.

### Harness comfort: do you need a hip belt?

For bags under 25 lbs, a padded shoulder harness is sufficient. For heavier loads (near carry-on weight limits of 15–22 lbs depending on carrier), a hip belt transfers weight to your hips and significantly reduces shoulder fatigue on long walks through airports.

The best travel backpacks have hip belts that stow away in a zippered panel — so they're there when you need them but hidden when you want to look more like a city bag than a hiking pack.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size backpack fits as a carry-on?**
Most airlines accept backpacks up to 22 x 14 x 9 inches as a carry-on — which corresponds to approximately 40–45L of volume. A 40L pack generally fits most overhead bins on US domestic flights.

**Is a 40L backpack big enough for 2 weeks of travel?**
Yes, for most climates and trips. One week of clothes takes 15–20L. Electronics, toiletries, and accessories take another 5–10L. A 40L bag has room to spare for moderate packers.

**Should I get a travel backpack with a laptop sleeve?**
Yes if you're working remotely or need a laptop for the trip. A dedicated padded sleeve keeps your laptop accessible for airport security and protected from the rest of your packed gear.

**What's the difference between a hiking backpack and a travel backpack?**
Travel backpacks have front-loading or clamshell openings, luggage pass-through straps, lockable zippers, and organizational pockets designed for airports and hotels. Hiking backpacks are top-loaders optimized for trail carry. You can travel with a hiking pack but the organization and accessibility are significantly worse.
