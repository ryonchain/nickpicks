## What to Look for in a Home Massage Chair

### Track Type: S-Track vs. L-Track
- **S-Track:** Follows the natural S-curve of your spine. Covers neck through lower back. Standard in most chairs.
- **L-Track:** Extends from neck all the way down to the glutes and upper hamstrings. Dramatically better for lower back and hip tension. Worth paying extra for.

### Zero Gravity Positioning
Inspired by NASA research, zero-gravity recline distributes your weight evenly so the massage rollers can work deeper with less pressure. A necessity for anyone with back issues.

### Body Scan Technology
Premium chairs scan your spine length and shoulder width before each session, customizing roller position to your exact body. The difference in effectiveness versus fixed-position chairs is noticeable.

### Heat Therapy
Lumbar heat is the minimum. The best chairs add heat to the seat and calves. Heat loosens muscles, making the massage 30–40% more effective by most user reports.

### Air Compression Coverage
Look for air bags in the shoulders, arms, hips, calves, and feet—not just one or two zones. Full-body compression mimics lymphatic drainage and significantly reduces whole-body tension.
