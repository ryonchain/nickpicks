## What to Expect From a Robot Vacuum Mop Combo

### Realistic Performance Expectations

Robot vacuum mop combos are maintenance tools, not replacement tools. They keep floors clean between deep cleans — they don't replace manual cleaning entirely. What they do very well:

- **Daily maintenance vacuuming** of dust, pet hair, and crumbs on hard floors
- **Light mopping** of sealed hardwood, tile, and LVP — wet debris and light spills
- **Scheduled cleaning** while you're away — set it at 9 AM, floor is done by 10 AM

What they don't replace:
- Deep scrubbing of grout lines
- Steam cleaning or sanitizing sealed stone
- Edge-to-edge cleaning around furniture with complex legs

### Floor Compatibility

| Floor Type | Robot Vacuum Mop Compatible? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sealed hardwood | Yes | Works well — moisture exposure is minimal with current systems |
| Tile | Yes | Excellent — mop handles grout between tiles well |
| LVP/Vinyl plank | Yes | Best combination — durable to moisture and debris |
| Unsealed hardwood | Caution | Moisture can damage — disable mop on unsealed wood |
| Low-pile carpet | Yes (vacuum only) | Mop lifts automatically on carpet |
| High-pile carpet | Partial | High suction handles it; mop pad doesn't reach pile base |
| Stone (marble, granite) | Caution | Verify sealing before use; acidic cleaners damage stone |

### Pet Hair Performance

All models in this roundup handle pet hair — the suction power and brush roll design have improved substantially for pet households. Key difference: robots with rubber roller brush rolls (Roborock) tangle less than models with bristle brush rolls when dealing with long dog or cat hair. If long-haired pets are the primary concern, verify the brush type before buying.
