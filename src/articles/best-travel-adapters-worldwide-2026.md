## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between a travel adapter and a voltage converter?**
A travel adapter changes the plug shape to fit foreign outlets but does NOT change voltage. A voltage converter changes voltage from 220V to 110V (or vice versa). Most modern electronics (laptops, phones, camera chargers) are dual-voltage (100–240V) and only need an adapter. Check your device's charger label — if it says '100-240V input,' you only need an adapter, not a converter.

**Do I need a different adapter for each country?**
Not with a universal all-in-one adapter. A quality universal adapter covers Type A (USA/Canada), Type B (USA/Canada grounded), Type C (Europe/South America), Type G (UK/Hong Kong/Singapore), Type I (Australia/New Zealand), and Type F outlets — covering 150+ countries with one device.

**Can I charge multiple devices at once with a travel adapter?**
Yes — most modern universal travel adapters include both USB-A and USB-C ports alongside the AC outlet, allowing 3–5 devices to charge simultaneously. Look for adapters with USB-C Power Delivery (PD) output (65W or 100W) to fast-charge laptops and newer phones alongside standard USB-A ports for older devices.

**Are cheap universal adapters safe to use?**
Not always. Look for adapters with surge protection, a safety shutter mechanism on the AC outlet (prevents children from inserting objects), and certifications like CE, RoHS, or FCC. Uncertified adapters from unknown brands can be a fire risk, particularly when used with high-wattage devices. Stick to brands with listed safety certifications.
