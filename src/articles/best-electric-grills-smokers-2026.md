## Electric Grill vs. Electric Smoker: Which Do You Need?

**Electric grill:** High heat (400°F+), fast cooking (minutes), direct heat searing. Best for burgers, steaks, chicken breasts, vegetables. Ideal for apartment balconies and quick weeknight meals.

**Electric smoker:** Low heat (200–275°F), long cooking (4–16 hours), indirect heat with wood smoke. Best for brisket, ribs, pork shoulder, whole chickens. Requires outdoor use and time investment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use an electric grill on an apartment balcony?**
Most apartments that prohibit propane and charcoal grills permit electric grills — but check your lease and local fire codes. The Weber Lumin, Ninja Woodfire, and Cuisinart Griddler are safe for restricted spaces.

**Do electric smokers produce real smoke flavor?**
Yes, but less intensely than offset smokers or pellet grills. Electric smokers with wood chips produce genuine smoke. The Traeger pellet system (technically a pellet grill, not a pure electric smoker) produces the most authentic smoke flavor in this category.

**How do I add more smoke flavor to an electric smoker?**
Use smaller wood chunks or chips with the smoker at lower temperatures. Extended smoking time (low and slow) also increases smoke penetration. The Masterbuilt's front-load chip tray makes adding chips without temperature loss easy.

**What wood chips work best for different meats?**
Mild (poultry, fish): applewood, cherrywood. Medium (pork, ribs): hickory, pecan. Strong (beef brisket): mesquite, oak. Avoid treating wood — only use food-grade smoking wood.
