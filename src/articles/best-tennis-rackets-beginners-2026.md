## What Makes a Good Beginner Tennis Racket

### Head Size
Tennis racket head sizes range from 95–115+ square inches. Larger heads mean:
- **Larger sweet spot**: off-center hits still go where intended
- **More power**: the "trampoline" effect of larger string beds adds power to slower swings
- **More forgiveness**: mishits produce playable shots rather than shanks

**Beginner recommendation:** 100–110 sq. in. Avoid anything under 98 sq. in. — those are for advanced players with consistent mechanics.

### Weight
Adult rackets range from 8–12+ oz (unstrung). **Lighter is better for beginners:**
- **8–9 oz**: easiest to swing, less arm fatigue, more maneuverability
- **9–10 oz**: balanced — accessible for most beginners
- **10+ oz**: reserve for experienced players who generate their own power

### String Pattern
- **Open string pattern (16×19)**: more spin potential, more power, more forgiving on mishits — recommended for beginners
- **Dense string pattern (18×20)**: more control, less power — for advanced players

### Grip Size
Tennis grip sizes run from 4" (L0) to 4⅝" (L5). Incorrect grip size causes arm problems. To find yours: measure from the middle crease of your palm to the tip of your ring finger. Most adult men grip L2–L3 (4¼"–4⅜"); most women L0–L2 (4"–4¼").

### Length
Standard tennis rackets are 27". Extended length rackets (27.5"–29") add reach and leverage for beginners who struggle to generate power from the baseline.

## Buying Guide

**Strung vs. unstrung:** Always buy strung for beginners. Stringing a new racket adds $15–30 and requires finding a racket stringer — strung rackets are ready to play immediately.

**When to restring:** Replace strings when they break or after ~40 hours of play. "Play more, string more" — recreational players should restring 2x per year minimum. Unplayed strings still lose tension.

**Overgrip vs. replacement grip:** All rackets ship with a replacement grip. Overgrips ($8–12 for 3-pack) go over the existing grip when it wears out — much easier to replace and customizes grip thickness.

**String tension:** Factory strung rackets are typically 55–60 lbs. Lower tension (50 lbs) = more power. Higher tension (65+ lbs) = more control. For beginners, factory tension is fine — adjust as you develop preferences.
