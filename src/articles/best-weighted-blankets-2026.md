## How to Choose the Right Weight

The standard recommendation is 10% of body weight. This guideline has clinical backing from occupational therapy research:

| Body Weight | Recommended Blanket Weight |
|------------|---------------------------|
| 80–110 lbs | 8–10 lbs |
| 110–150 lbs | 10–12 lbs |
| 150–190 lbs | 15 lbs |
| 190–250 lbs | 20 lbs |
| 250+ lbs | 20–25 lbs |

Children under 2 should not use weighted blankets. Children 2–12 should use only under adult supervision with blankets 5–7 lbs maximum.

## Fill Types and Their Effects

### Glass Beads
- Smallest fill available; distributes most evenly
- Quieter than plastic pellets
- Heavier per volume (smaller blanket for same weight)
- Premium standard: found in Gravity Blanket, Baloo, YnM premium versions

### Plastic Poly Pellets
- Older technology; bulkier
- Noisier when moved (subtle rustling)
- Less even weight distribution
- Found in budget models

### Chunky Knit (No Fill)
- Weight comes from thick, heavy yarn construction
- Breathable—no fill beads to trap heat
- Visually striking; designed for display when not in use
- Can't be machine-washed (hand wash or dry cleaning only)
- Best example: Bearaby Cotton Napper

## Buying Guide

### Who Benefits Most from Weighted Blankets
Clinical research shows the strongest benefits for:
- Anxiety disorders (GAD, social anxiety)
- Insomnia and sleep-onset difficulty
- ADHD (sensory regulation)
- Autism spectrum disorder (sensory processing)
- Restless leg syndrome
- General stress and overstimulation

For otherwise healthy adults seeking better sleep quality, expect modest but measurable improvement in time-to-sleep and perceived sleep quality.

### Temperature Considerations
- **Hot sleepers:** Bearaby Napper (open knit) or Baloo Living (matelassé cotton)
- **Cold sleepers:** YnM or Gravity Blanket (bead-filled, heat-retaining)
- **Temperature neutral:** Luna (standard cotton construction)

### Size Selection
Weighted blankets are sized to cover a person, not a bed. A 48"×72" throw works for one adult on a queen bed; for couples wanting simultaneous weighted coverage, buy two individual blankets (a shared blanket loses distribution effectiveness when two people pull it in different directions).
