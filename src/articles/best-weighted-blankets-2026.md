## How to Choose the Right Weight

The standard guidance is **10% of your body weight**, but personal preference matters. Here's a practical guide:

| Body Weight | Recommended Blanket Weight |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Under 100 lbs | 5–7 lbs |
| 100–150 lbs | 10–12 lbs |
| 150–200 lbs | 15 lbs |
| 200–250 lbs | 20 lbs |
| 250+ lbs | 25 lbs |

When in doubt, go lighter. You can always add another blanket; you can't reduce a blanket that's too heavy and feels restrictive.

**Caution:** Weighted blankets are not recommended for:
- Children under 2 years old
- People with respiratory or circulatory conditions
- Anyone who cannot remove the blanket independently

## Weighted Blanket for Anxiety: What the Research Says

Studies published in the *Journal of Sleep Medicine & Disorders* and the *Scandinavian Journal of Occupational Therapy* show that weighted blankets can:
- Reduce physiological anxiety markers (heart rate, cortisol)
- Improve sleep duration and quality in adults with insomnia
- Provide calming effects for adults with ADHD

The mechanism is deep pressure stimulation — similar to massage — which activates the parasympathetic nervous system.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are weighted blankets too hot to sleep under?**
Standard weighted blankets (micro-fleece, polyester cover) do sleep warm. If you're a hot sleeper, choose the Bearaby Cotton Napper or a cooling bamboo-covered option like Degrees of Comfort.

**Can weighted blankets be machine washed?**
Most blankets up to 15 lbs can be washed in a standard home washing machine. Heavier blankets (20–25 lbs) require a commercial laundromat washer. Always use cold water and gentle cycles; avoid hot dryers.

**How long do weighted blankets last?**
Quality blankets with glass bead fill last 5–10 years. The outer cover typically shows wear before the blanket itself. Consider brands with replaceable/washable covers for longevity.

**Are weighted blankets safe for kids?**
For children ages 4 and older without sensory or respiratory issues, a 3–5 lb weighted blanket can be beneficial. Always supervise young children and consult a pediatric occupational therapist for sensory processing concerns.
