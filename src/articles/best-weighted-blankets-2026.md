# Best Weighted Blankets for Sleep and Anxiety 2026: Top Picks Reviewed

Weighted blankets use deep pressure stimulation — the same calming mechanism as a firm hug — to reduce anxiety, improve sleep onset, and increase serotonin and melatonin production. The research on weighted blankets is increasingly solid: studies consistently show improvements in sleep quality, anxiety symptom reduction, and focus for individuals with sensory processing differences.

The challenge is that not all weighted blankets are created equal. Cheap options use poor-quality beads that shift and clump. Poorly-designed covers trap heat. Wrong weights produce discomfort rather than calm. This guide cuts through the marketing to focus on blankets that actually perform.

## Weighted Blanket Buying Guide

**Choosing the right weight.**
The standard recommendation is 10% of body weight ±2 lbs. However, research suggests a range of 7–12% body weight works well — choose lighter if you're trying the concept for the first time, heavier if you want more intense deep pressure.

| Body Weight | Recommended Blanket Weight |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| 80–120 lbs | 7–10 lbs |
| 120–160 lbs | 10–15 lbs |
| 160–200 lbs | 15–20 lbs |
| 200+ lbs | 20–25 lbs |

**Fill types explained.**
- **Glass microbeads:** Most popular. Dense, quiet(er), even distribution. Heavy for size.
- **Plastic pellets:** Budget option. Noisy with movement, less even distribution.
- **Natural fiber (Bearaby):** Bead-free. Completely silent. Best breathability. Single weight per blanket.

**Hot sleeper considerations.**
Standard weighted blankets trap heat — the density creates warmth. Solutions: choose bead-free (Bearaby Tree Napper), choose a dual-sided cooling cover (Layla), or go 1–2 lbs lighter than the standard recommendation to reduce the insulation effect.

**Are weighted blankets safe?**
Weighted blankets are not safe for:
- Children under 2 years old
- People with respiratory conditions, claustrophobia, or low blood pressure
- Anyone who cannot easily remove a blanket independently

For everyone else — adults, older children with sensory needs, people with anxiety — they're safe and often highly beneficial.
