## What to Look for in a Tower Fan

**CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute):** Higher CFM = more air moved. Look for 400+ CFM for bedrooms, 600+ CFM for living rooms. Many brands don't publish CFM directly — use noise level and speed ratings as proxies.

**Noise level:** Sleep-mode noise at 40 dB or under is ideal. At 50 dB you'll hear the fan — fine for daytime use, disruptive for some light sleepers.

**Oscillation angle:** 90–120° oscillation covers most rooms. Look for models that advertise horizontal AND vertical oscillation (like Dyson) for corner placement.

**Smart features:** Alexa/Google integration matters if you want to control the fan with your voice or schedule it from your phone. If you don't care about that, save $30–50 buying the non-smart version.

**Filter vs no filter:** Only Dyson (and a few others) combine HEPA filtration with tower fan airflow. Non-filter tower fans move air and may have an ionizer (which generates trace ozone — some people prefer to turn this off).
