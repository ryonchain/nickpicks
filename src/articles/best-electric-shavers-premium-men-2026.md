## What Separates Premium Electric Shavers from Mid-Range

At the $200–$350 price point, electric shavers earn their cost with three things mid-range models can't match:

1. **Motor speed and power:** Premium foil shavers (Braun, Panasonic) run at 40,000–52,000 strokes per minute vs. 10,000–20,000 on budget models. Higher motor speed means fewer passes needed to cut hair cleanly.
2. **Adaptive head systems:** Philips Series 9000 uses SkinIQ and BeardAdapt technology that senses skin resistance and adjusts motor torque in real time. Braun Series 9 Pro+ uses AutoSense technology for the same effect.
3. **Integrated cleaning systems:** Premium models include automatic cleaning and lubricating stations. Hair debris and oxidized cutting edges are the primary cause of shaver performance degradation — automatic stations extend the cutting life meaningfully.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Rotary or foil first:** This is the most important decision. Rotary (Philips) = curved face shapes, multiple-direction hair growth. Foil (Braun, Panasonic) = maximum closeness, straight stubble, flat surface areas.

**Cleaning station vs. manual:** Premium electric shavers benefit significantly from automatic cleaning. The micro-debris that accumulates in cutting elements degrades performance quickly. If you won't clean manually after every use, invest in a cleaning station.

**Wet or dry priority:** All models reviewed are waterproof, but their wet performance varies. The Panasonic Arc5 with shaving gel delivers the most noticeable wet shaving improvement. The Philips rotary heads work well wet but are slightly less efficient.

**Stubble density:** Light stubble → Philips Series 9000 is most comfortable. Dense coarse hair → Panasonic Arc5's high-speed motor is purpose-built for it. Medium daily stubble → Braun Series 9 is the most balanced.
