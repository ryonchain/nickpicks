## WiFi vs. Non-WiFi Baby Monitors: Which is Safer?

**WiFi monitors** (Nanit, Owlet, some Eufy models):
- Allow remote viewing from anywhere via smartphone
- Risk: subject to internet security vulnerabilities if not properly secured
- Always choose monitors with end-to-end encryption and two-factor authentication

**Non-WiFi monitors** (Infant Optics, VTech):
- Use dedicated radio frequency — cannot be accessed via internet
- Limited to home range (typically 600–1,000ft)
- No remote viewing when away from home

**Our recommendation:** Non-WiFi for most parents. WiFi only if remote viewing is a priority and you choose a brand with strong encryption practices.

## What to Look for in a Baby Monitor Camera

**Night vision:** True infrared night vision (not just digital brightness boost) makes a big difference in image clarity.

**Range:** 700–1,000ft open field is sufficient for most homes. WiFi models aren't range-limited at home.

**Battery life on parent unit:** 8–10 hours lets you leave the unit charging and carry it overnight.

**Two-way audio:** Lets you soothe baby with your voice without entering the room.

**Temperature display:** Some camera units include a room thermometer shown on the parent display — useful for monitoring nursery temp.
