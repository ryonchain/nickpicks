## What Makes a Good Dorm Tower Fan

### Noise Level

This is the most important spec for a dorm fan. Your roommate is sleeping three feet away. The spec to look for is **dB rating on lowest setting** — anything under 40dB works for sleeping. The Dreo Tower Fan runs at 25dB on low (quieter than a whisper).

Fans without a published dB spec tend to be louder — avoid them.

### Footprint Size

Tower fans take up less floor space than box fans or pedestal fans. But there's a range:
- 25-30 inches tall: small footprint, good for desks and cramped floor space
- 36-42 inches: standard tower fan height, takes up more floor space but circulates air better in larger rooms

For a typical 12x10 dorm, a 28-32 inch tower fan is the sweet spot.

### Timer and Remote

A sleep timer is essential — you don't want to get up at 3am to turn it off. Look for:
- At least 4-hour timer (8-hour timer preferred)
- Remote control so you can adjust from bed
- Multiple speed settings (3 is the minimum, more is better)

### Oscillation

Oscillating fans distribute air throughout the room instead of directing it in one spot. For a shared dorm room, oscillation means both you and your roommate get airflow without pointing the fan directly at either of you.

## Tower Fan vs. Other Dorm Fan Types

**Tower fans vs. box fans:** Tower fans have a smaller footprint, look better, and are quieter. Box fans move more raw air but are louder and take more floor space. Tower fan wins for dorms.

**Tower fans vs. desk fans:** Desk fans sit on your desk and only cool you — not the room. If you want to cool down while studying and don't share a room, desk fans work. For a shared dorm, tower fans are more considerate.

**Tower fans vs. window AC units:** Check your dorm policy. Many dorms don't allow window AC units. Tower fans are universally allowed and far cheaper to run.

## Placement Tips for Dorm Tower Fans

- Place in a corner to maximize oscillation coverage
- Set at a slight diagonal to the center of the room for best airflow
- Keep at least 12 inches from the wall for efficient air intake
- Don't put on carpet if possible — elevated placement helps airflow
