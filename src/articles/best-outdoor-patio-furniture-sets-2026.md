## Patio Furniture Buying Guide

**Material comparison: what lasts.**

| Material | Durability | Maintenance | Cost |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------|
| Teak (Grade A) | 25–30 years | Annual oiling optional | $$$$$ |
| HDPE (Polywood) | 20+ years | Soap and water only | $$$ |
| Powder-coated aluminum | 15–20 years | Soap and water | $$–$$$ |
| Powder-coated steel | 10–15 years | Touch-up paint needed | $$–$$$ |
| Synthetic wicker/aluminum | 8–12 years | Annual inspection | $$ |
| Synthetic wicker/steel | 5–8 years | Watch for rust | $ |

**Cushion fabric matters as much as the frame.**
- **Sunbrella acrylic:** 5–10 year outdoor life. Resists fading and mildew.
- **Olefin/polyolefin:** 3–5 year outdoor life. Better than basic polyester.
- **Standard polyester:** 1–3 year outdoor life. Budget option.

**Size your space before buying.**
Leave 18–24 inches of walking clearance around dining sets. For lounge configurations, leave 30–36 inches for traffic flow. Sectionals on small patios leave no room for people to move — measure twice.

**Storage: extend furniture life significantly.**
Even weather-resistant furniture lasts longer with winter storage or covers. Polywood and HDPE can genuinely stay outside year-round. Teak prefers it. Wicker and fabric cushions benefit from indoor storage or high-quality covers during extended off-season periods.
