## Why Wine Storage Temperature Matters

Wine stored too warm ages prematurely — tannins break down, fruit flavors flatten, and subtle aromatics fade. Wine stored too cold (like a regular fridge) can damage cork and strip volatile aromatics. The sweet spot for long-term storage is 55°F with minimal vibration and light exposure.

A dedicated wine cooler gives you:

- **Temperature precision:** Stable temps within ±1°F vs. a regular fridge that cycles widely
- **Vibration control:** Thermoelectric models have zero vibration; compressor models use vibration-dampening mounts
- **Humidity management:** Good coolers maintain 50–70% humidity to prevent cork drying
- **UV protection:** Tinted glass doors block light that degrades wine
