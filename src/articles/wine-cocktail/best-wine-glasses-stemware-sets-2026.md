## Why Glass Shape Matters

A tulip-shaped bowl concentrates aromas toward the nose. A narrow rim focuses wine onto the tip of the tongue where sweetness registers. A wide rim spreads it to the sides where tannin and acidity dominate. These aren't marketing claims — they're anatomy.

The practical takeaway: a well-shaped wine glass makes even a $12 bottle taste noticeably better.

What to evaluate:

- **Rim thinness:** Thinner rims deliver wine more cleanly to the palate — crystal outperforms thick glass here
- **Bowl shape:** Wide bowls suit bold reds (Cabernet, Syrah); narrow bowls suit whites and aromatic wines
- **Stem vs. stemless:** Stems keep wine cool; stemless is more casual and storage-friendly
- **Durability:** Dishwasher-safe crystal (Schott Zwiesel Tritan) exists and is worth the premium over standard glass
