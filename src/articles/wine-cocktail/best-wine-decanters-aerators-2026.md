## Why Aeration Matters

Wine — especially young, tannic red wine — contains volatile compounds that need oxygen exposure to fully express themselves. Tight, closed wines smell flat and taste harsh straight from the bottle. A decanter gives the wine surface area to breathe over 30–120 minutes. An aerator speeds up that process by injecting air bubbles into each pour.

What to look for:

- **Volume:** Most decanters hold 750ml (one standard bottle). Magnum decanters hold 1.5L for larger-format bottles
- **Base width:** Wider bases increase surface area exposure — better for young, tannic wines
- **Ease of cleaning:** Simple shapes are easier to clean; intricate designs require decanter beads
- **Aerator compatibility:** Pour-through aerators sit at the bottle neck; handheld models aerate while pouring
