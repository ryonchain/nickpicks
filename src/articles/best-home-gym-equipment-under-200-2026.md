## Buying Guide

### The Most Efficient Use of $200

Rather than buying one $200 machine, buy multiple tools. A jump rope + resistance bands + kettlebell covers more movement patterns than any single machine.

### What to Prioritize

**For fat loss:** Jump rope + mini stepper (cardio volume)
**For muscle:** Kettlebell + resistance bands (progressive overload)
**For mobility:** Bands + foam roller (flexibility and recovery)

### What to Skip at This Budget

Avoid weight benches, squat racks, and cable machines under $200 — quality is poor at this price point. These require $300+ for a reliable build.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I build real muscle with under $200 of equipment?**
Yes — especially as a beginner. Resistance bands and kettlebells provide enough resistance for significant muscle growth in the first 6–12 months. After that, you'll want to add weight (heavier kettlebell or adjustable dumbbells).

**Is home gym equipment under $200 durable?**
Cast iron (kettlebells) and latex (resistance bands) are durable. Avoid cheap plastic cardio machines at this budget. Stick to simple, non-mechanical tools.

**What's the single best piece of equipment if I can only buy one thing?**
A 35 lb kettlebell (~$45). Swings alone are a complete cardio + strength workout. Add goblet squats, rows, and presses and you have full-body training covered.
