## What Makes an Air Fryer Worth Buying

Four specs determine whether an air fryer will actually improve your cooking:

- **Capacity:** 2–3 qt for 1–2 people; 4–6 qt for 2–4 people; 7–10 qt for 5+ people. Overpacking the basket blocks airflow and produces uneven, soggy results — size up if in doubt.
- **Wattage:** 1,500–1,800W is the sweet spot. Higher wattage means faster preheat and more consistent heat distribution. Units under 1,200W struggle to get crispy without extended cook times.
- **Airflow Design:** Rapid Air Technology (Philips) and 360° circulation models cook more evenly than single-fan designs. Look for perforated baskets with elevated grates that let fat drip away.
- **Ease of Cleaning:** Non-stick, dishwasher-safe baskets are non-negotiable for regular use. Avoid models with intricate parts or recessed heating elements that trap grease.

## Buying Guide: What to Actually Look For

### Basket Shape: Round vs. Square
Round baskets are traditional but waste corner space. Square baskets (like Cosori's) fit noticeably more food at the same rated quart capacity. If maximizing usable space matters, choose square.

### Preheat Requirements
Most air fryers benefit from 2–3 minutes of preheating, especially for items that need a sear (chicken thighs, frozen fries). Some models (Instant Vortex, Cosori) have dedicated preheat buttons; others require you to run them empty for a few minutes manually.

### Air Fryer vs. Air Fryer Oven
Basket-style air fryers circulate heat more effectively and tend to get crispier results than countertop air fryer ovens. Ovens offer more cooking surface and can handle sheet-pan cooking — if you frequently bake, the oven style is more versatile.

### What "Healthy" Actually Means
Air frying uses 70–80% less oil than deep frying for comparable crispiness. It won't make fried food a salad, but it meaningfully reduces fat intake compared to submersion frying while delivering similar texture.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you cook raw meat in an air fryer?**
Yes — chicken, beef, pork, and fish all cook well. Use a meat thermometer: chicken needs 165°F internal temperature; beef varies by doneness preference. The air fryer's dry heat creates a crust similar to oven-roasting.

**Do air fryers cause cancer from non-stick coating?**
Modern non-stick coatings (PTFE/Teflon) are safe below 500°F under normal conditions. PFOA, the chemical previously linked to health concerns, was phased out of non-stick manufacturing by 2013. Don't use metal utensils that scratch the coating.

**How do I keep food from drying out?**
For lean proteins, lightly spray with avocado or olive oil before cooking. Don't overcook — start checking temperature 5 minutes before the recipe's suggested time. Thicker cuts retain moisture better than thin ones.

**Can I use aluminum foil or parchment paper?**
Yes — perforated parchment paper designed for air fryers prevents sticking and simplifies cleanup. Regular foil works but blocks airflow beneath food; keep it to small pieces and never cover the basket entirely.
