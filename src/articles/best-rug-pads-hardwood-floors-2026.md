# Best Rug Pads for Hardwood Floors in 2026

A rug pad serves three purposes: it keeps rugs from sliding (safety), adds cushion underfoot (comfort), and protects your hardwood floor (longevity). What most people don't know: the wrong rug pad can damage hardwood floors. PVC/vinyl pads leave residue or stick to finished hardwood. Natural rubber and felt are the safe choices.

---

## What NOT to Use on Hardwood

**Avoid PVC/vinyl "non-slip" pads:** These can bond to polyurethane finishes over time, leaving marks when removed. Common in cheap rug pads sold on Amazon.

**Avoid dyed latex:** Some latex pads leach dye onto light hardwood under heat and pressure.

**Safe options:** Natural rubber, recycled felt, or natural rubber + felt combination.

## Sizing: Always Size Down

The rug pad should be **1 inch smaller than the rug on all sides** — so 2 inches shorter and 2 inches narrower total. This prevents the pad from peeking out from under the rug edges. For an 8x10 rug, buy a 7'10" x 9'10" pad (or trim down a standard 8x10 pad).

Most manufacturers label pads as the rug size they're intended for — meaning the actual pad is already sized correctly. Check the listed dimensions if unsure.

## Rug Pad for Other Floor Types

- **Tile:** Any non-slip option works — PVC is fine on tile (only problematic on hardwood)
- **LVP/Laminate:** Same rule as hardwood — natural rubber or felt+rubber
- **Carpet:** You don't need a rug pad on carpet; the carpet itself provides grip
