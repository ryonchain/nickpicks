# Best Reading Glasses in 2026

Presbyopia (age-related loss of close-up focus) typically begins around age 40 and progresses through the 50s. Reading glasses are the simple, no-prescription solution — available in diopter strengths from +1.00 to +3.50 at pharmacies and Amazon for $10–$40 a pair. The right strength is determined by testing (available at most optical counters) or by the simple self-test below.

---

## Finding Your Diopter Strength

**Self-test:** Hold this text at your normal reading distance. If you don't have reading glasses yet:
- If text is clear: You don't need readers yet
- If slightly blurry: Start with +1.00 or +1.25
- If moderately blurry: Try +1.50 or +1.75  
- If significantly blurry: Try +2.00 or +2.25
- If very blurry at all distances: See an optometrist — you may need prescription glasses

**Age guide (approximate):**
- Age 40–45: +1.00 to +1.25
- Age 45–50: +1.25 to +1.75
- Age 50–55: +1.75 to +2.25
- Age 55–60: +2.25 to +2.75
- Age 60+: +2.50 to +3.25

## When to See an Eye Doctor

Reading glasses are only appropriate if:
1. Your near-vision problem is symmetric (both eyes need the same correction)
2. You don't have other vision problems (astigmatism, very different prescriptions in each eye)

See an optometrist if:
- You get headaches with readers that seem the right strength
- One eye is noticeably worse than the other
- You're younger than 40 and having reading difficulty
- Your distance vision is also blurry

Reading glasses won't correct astigmatism or significant prescription differences between eyes — only a proper eye exam and prescription glasses will.
