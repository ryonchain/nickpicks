## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Portable Projector

**Brightness (ANSI lumens) is the most important spec.** 300 ANSI lumens requires a completely dark room for a good image. 500–800 ANSI lumens works in a dimmed room with ambient light. 1,000+ ANSI lumens handles modest ambient light and outdoor twilight use. Brightness ratings are often inflated by manufacturers — look for ANSI-certified measurements.

**Resolution.** 1080p is the standard for most portable projectors and produces a sharp 80–120" image. Native 4K is available in premium models but requires a dark room and large screen to fully appreciate. Many budget "4K" projectors interpolate 4K content from a 1080p chip — check for "native 4K."

**Built-in battery vs. AC.** Battery-powered projectors offer true portability for outdoor use. Most batteries run 2–3 hours — enough for a feature film. AC-only projectors offer unlimited runtime but require an outlet within cable reach.

**Smart TV OS vs. external device.** Projectors with Google TV or Android TV give you native access to all streaming services. AC-only projectors without a built-in OS require a Fire TV Stick, Roku, or Apple TV — add $30–$100 to the budget.

**Auto vs. manual keystone correction.** Manual keystone requires precise placement to get a rectangular image. Auto keystone adjusts on the fly — essential for camping, travel, and any situation where you can't optimize projector position.

## FAQ

**How bright does a room need to be for a portable projector?**
A completely dark room is ideal for budget projectors (300 ANSI lumens). With blackout curtains, most 300-lumen projectors produce a usable 80" image. For living rooms with ambient light, target 500+ ANSI lumens. Outdoor after dark use typically needs 800–1,000+ ANSI lumens.

**What size screen can I get from a portable projector?**
Most portable projectors hit their sweet spot at 80–120". At 80" you'll see crisp detail from a 1080p source at normal viewing distances. Going beyond 150" significantly reduces brightness and perceived sharpness. Check the projector's throw ratio for your room size.

**Do I need a projection screen or can I use a wall?**
A flat, matte white wall works fine for casual use. A dedicated screen adds contrast, color accuracy, and ambient light rejection. For indoor use, a $50–$100 pull-down screen noticeably improves the image. Outdoor use benefits from a screen to capture the full image without absorption.

**What's the best portable projector for outdoor movies?**
The BenQ GP20 for brightness and IP54 weather resistance. For budget outdoor use, pair the Vankyo Leisure 3W with a Fire TV Stick and outdoor extension cord — it handles after-dark viewing well.

**Can portable projectors replace a TV?**
For dedicated home theaters or rooms without much ambient light, yes. Premium models like the XGIMI Horizon Ultra genuinely rival flat panel TVs at equivalent screen sizes. For a bright living room as a primary TV replacement, you'll need 1,500+ ANSI lumens and a true 4K model.
