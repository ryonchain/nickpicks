  - question: "What is VESA compatibility and how do I know if my monitor supports it?"
    answer: "VESA is a standardized hole pattern on the back of monitors used to attach mounts and arms. The most common patterns are 75×75mm and 100×100mm. To check compatibility: look at the back of your monitor for a 4-hole square pattern, or check the monitor's spec sheet under 'VESA mount.' ## Monitor Arm Buying Guide

**Measure before buying:**
1. Weigh your monitor (check spec sheet or weigh with kitchen scale)
2. Measure desk thickness (most clamps work up to 3.5")
3. Confirm VESA pattern on back of monitor (75×75 or 100×100mm most common)
4. Measure how far back you want your screen from the desk edge

**Clamp vs grommet mounting:**
- **Clamp (C-clamp):** Attaches to desk edge. Works on most solid desks. Slightly unstable on hollow-panel desks.
- **Grommet:** Passes through a hole in the desk surface. More stable, looks cleaner, requires a pre-drilled hole or access to drill one.
- **Freestanding:** Sits on desk surface. No attachment required. Takes up some surface area.

**Single vs dual arm:**
- Single arm: One monitor, cleaner setup, lower cost
- Dual arm (separate): More flexibility in positioning, but two mounting points
- Dual arm (shared pole): One mounting point for two monitors, cleanest setup, most common choice

**Ergonomic positioning checklist:**
- Top of monitor at or just below eye level
- Screen at arm's length (60–90 cm for 27" monitors)
- Screen tilted slightly back (0–5°) to reduce neck strain
- Both monitors at same height if using dual setup
