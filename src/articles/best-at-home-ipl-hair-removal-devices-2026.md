## How At-Home IPL Works

IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) emits broad-spectrum light absorbed by melanin in hair follicles. The absorbed energy converts to heat, which damages the follicle's ability to regrow hair. Unlike single-wavelength laser hair removal, IPL uses a range of wavelengths filtered for hair removal specificity.

**Key requirements for IPL effectiveness:**
- Works best on skin tones Fitzpatrick I–IV (pale to medium-dark)
- Most effective on dark hair (high melanin contrast with skin)
- Not suitable for Fitzpatrick V–VI skin or light blonde, gray, or white hair
- Requires 6–8 weekly sessions for 70–90% reduction

**Expected timeline:** Hairs begin shedding at week 2–3. Visible thinning by week 4–6. 70–90% reduction by week 16–24.

## Buying Guide

### Skin Tone and Hair Color Compatibility
IPL works by targeting melanin contrast. Dark hair on light to medium skin (Fitzpatrick I–IV) achieves the best results. Results are reduced on light brown or dark blonde hair, and ineffective on gray, white, red, or very light blonde hair. Darker skin tones (V–VI) are not suitable — consult a dermatologist for darker skin treatment options.

### Flash Count vs. Session Speed
Unlimited/high flash count devices eliminate the recurring cost of cartridge replacement. Faster gliding modes (Braun) reduce total treatment time significantly — relevant if treating multiple body zones per session.

### With or Without Cooling
Cooling makes sessions dramatically more comfortable, especially for underarms, bikini line, and upper lip. If you're IPL-sensitive or targeting sensitive areas frequently, prioritize devices with cooling technology (Ulike, Jovs).

## FAQ

### How many sessions are needed for permanent hair reduction?
Most people achieve 70–90% permanent hair reduction after 8–12 weekly sessions. After the initial protocol, monthly maintenance sessions sustain results. Complete permanence in all follicles is not guaranteed — some maintenance is always required.

### Can I use IPL on my face?
Yes — with a dedicated precision head and lower intensity settings. Avoid the eye area completely; use protective goggles if treating nearby areas. Most devices include a face-specific mode or attachment. Do not use IPL on eyebrows.

### How long after waxing or shaving can I start IPL?
Shave 24 hours before each session — the hair follicle must be present for IPL to target it, but surface hair should be removed to prevent the light from being absorbed by hair above skin. Do not wax or epilate before IPL — the follicle must be in the skin for the treatment to work.
