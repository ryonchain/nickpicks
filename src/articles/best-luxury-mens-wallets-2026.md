## What Makes a Luxury Wallet Worth the Price?

### Full-Grain vs. Corrected-Grain Leather
Full-grain leather uses the outermost hide layer — strongest, develops the best patina, and ages gracefully. Corrected-grain leather has the surface sanded and re-coated — it looks good new but doesn't age the same way. All quality luxury wallets use full-grain.

### Construction
Look for: flat stitching (not fraying threads), tight seam edges, smooth interior leather, consistent dye (no blotchiness). Test by flexing the wallet open and closed — it should open smoothly without resistance.

### Maintenance
Luxury leather wallets need almost no maintenance:
- Condition leather 1–2x per year with a leather conditioner
- Avoid overstuffing (damages stitching and causes stretching)
- If wet, let dry at room temperature, not with heat

### Slim vs. Full Billfold
- **Slim/cardholder (< 12mm):** Best for front pocket carry; 3–6 cards, minimal cash
- **Billfold (12–20mm):** Traditional back pocket wallet; 6–12 cards, full cash compartment
