## Countertop Dishwasher Buying Guide

### Faucet Adapter Compatibility

All countertop dishwashers connect to a standard threaded kitchen faucet via an adapter. The included adapter fits most US standard faucets. Pull-out faucets (the type where the faucet head pulls out) and pull-down faucets require a specific adapter — verify compatibility before purchasing.

**If your faucet doesn't accept an adapter:** You can connect directly to the hot water supply valve under the sink using a Y-splitter and quick-connect fittings ($15–$30 at hardware stores). Search for "[your dishwasher model] under-sink connection" for specific instructions.

### Place Settings: How Many Do You Need?

| Household | Place Settings |
|-----------|---------------|
| 1 person, minimal cooking | 4–5 place settings |
| 1–2 people, regular cooking | 6 place settings |
| 2–3 people | Consider portable or standard dishwasher |

One place setting = 1 plate, 1 bowl, 1 glass, 1 cup, 1 fork, 1 knife, 1 spoon. This is the industry standard for measuring capacity.

### What Doesn't Fit in Countertop Dishwashers

- Large pots and pans (anything over ~9" diameter)
- Sheet pans and baking sheets
- Woks and cast iron (neither should go in any dishwasher)
- Full-size mixing bowls

For most daily dish loads (plates, bowls, cups, glasses, cutlery, smaller pots), countertop dishwashers handle everything. Large baking sessions may require hand washing the big items.

## FAQ

**Does a countertop dishwasher need to be plumbed in permanently?**
No — that's the key advantage. You connect the water intake to your kitchen faucet via an adapter (takes 2 minutes) and route the drain hose into the sink. It's removable at any time, making it ideal for renters.

**Are countertop dishwashers as effective as full-size models?**
For properly loaded dishes without heavy stuck-on food, yes — modern countertop dishwashers achieve the same cleaning performance as full-size models. Pre-rinsing is rarely necessary for items soiled within 24 hours.

**Can I use regular dishwasher pods/detergent?**
Yes. Standard dishwasher pods (Cascade, Finish), powder, or gel all work in countertop dishwashers. Use a standard detergent amount — most units hold one pod or a tablespoon of powder.
