## What to Look For

- **Laptop size compatibility:** Measure your laptop's diagonal screen size and physical dimensions before buying. A bag labeled "fits 15.6 inch" may be too snug for some 15" models depending on thickness.
- **Padded sleeve vs. padded compartment:** A sleeve (separate padded section) offers better protection than just padding sewn into the main compartment walls. Sleeves also prevent your laptop from being scratched by keys, pens, or other contents.
- **Carrying style:** Totes look more professional but distribute weight onto one shoulder. Backpacks distribute weight evenly and are better for heavy loads or long commutes. Convertible bags offer both.
- **Organization depth:** Count the number of useful pockets (not decorative ones). A great laptop bag should have dedicated spots for phone, charger, keys, pens, cards, and a water bottle — without making you dig through one cavernous compartment.
- **Material and weather resistance:** Full leather looks best but requires care and is heavy. Nylon is lighter, often water-resistant, and easier to maintain. Waxed canvas splits the difference.
- **RFID protection:** If you carry contactless payment cards or a passport, an RFID-blocking pocket adds a meaningful security layer against electronic pickpocketing.
