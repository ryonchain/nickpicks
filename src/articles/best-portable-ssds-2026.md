## Portable SSD Buying Guide

### USB Speed Tiers: What Actually Matters

| Standard | Max Speed | What It Means |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| USB 3.0 (3.1 Gen 1) | 5 Gbps (~450 MB/s) | Legacy — avoid for new SSDs |
| USB 3.1/3.2 Gen 2 | 10 Gbps (~1,000 MB/s) | Sweet spot for most users |
| USB 3.2 Gen 2x2 | 20 Gbps (~2,000 MB/s) | Needed only for video editing |
| Thunderbolt 3/4 | 40 Gbps (~3,000+ MB/s) | Desktop-class, requires TB port |

### MacBook Compatibility Notes

All USB-C portable SSDs work with modern MacBook Pro and MacBook Air. For full performance:
- **MacBook Air M2/M3/M4**: USB 3.2 Gen 2 (max 1,050 MB/s from standard port)
- **MacBook Pro M3 Pro/Max**: USB 3.2 Gen 2 from USB-C, full Thunderbolt 4 from Thunderbolt ports (up to 3,000+ MB/s with TB-native drives)
- **Older MacBook with USB-A**: Use an included USB-A cable or adapter

### Capacity Guide

| Use Case | Recommended Capacity |
|----------|---------------------|
| MacBook Time Machine backup | Match or exceed your internal SSD (512GB minimum) |
| Portable media library | 2TB |
| Video editing workspace | 2–4TB |
| Gaming external storage (PS5) | 1–2TB |

## FAQs

**Do portable SSDs work with PS5 or Xbox?**
Yes. The PS5 supports USB SuperSpeed SSDs in its expansion port for storing PS4 games and moving PS5 games. For internal PS5 expansion, you need an M.2 NVMe drive (not portable). Xbox Series X/S supports USB storage for Xbox One games.

**How long do portable SSDs last?**
Quality SSDs from Samsung, WD, and SanDisk are rated for 150–600TB of total writes (TBW rating, found in specs). For typical backup and file transfer use, that's 5–10+ years. Flash storage reliability exceeds traditional HDDs significantly.

**Is it safe to use a portable SSD as a daily MacBook backup?**
Absolutely. Time Machine on macOS works natively with any USB drive. Format as APFS (or use a WD My Passport SSD pre-formatted for Mac), then designate it as your Time Machine disk in System Preferences.
