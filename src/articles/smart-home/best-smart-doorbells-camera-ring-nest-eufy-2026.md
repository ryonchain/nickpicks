# Best Smart Doorbells with Camera (2026) — Ring vs. Nest vs. Eufy

A smart video doorbell lets you see, hear, and talk to whoever is at your door from anywhere in the world — whether you're upstairs, at work, or on vacation. The challenge is choosing between three very different subscription models and ecosystems.

Ring requires a subscription for video history. Google Nest requires Nest Aware. Eufy offers local storage with no subscription. The right choice depends heavily on which ongoing cost model fits your situation.

---

## The Subscription Debate

**Ring Protect:** $4.99/month per device or $10/month for the entire home. Stores 60–180 days of video history and enables advanced features like People Only Mode and rich notifications. Without it, you get live view and motion alerts but no video history.

**Google Nest Aware:** $8/month for 30 days of event history or $15/month for 60 days. Integrates with Google Home and enables Familiar Face alerts.

**Eufy Security:** Free local storage (via HomeBase or SD card). Optional cloud storage add-on available. Best option for avoiding subscriptions entirely.

## Installation Considerations

**Existing doorbell wiring:** Most hardwired doorbells require 8–24V AC power from existing doorbell transformer wiring. Check your current doorbell chime location — if it has a mechanical chime, you likely have the wiring for a wired smart doorbell.

**No wiring (renters or new installs):** Battery-powered options from Ring, Eufy, or Arlo require only a mount and Wi-Fi — no electrical work.
