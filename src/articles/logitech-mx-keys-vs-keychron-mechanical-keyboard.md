## Two Completely Different Keyboards, Same Price Bracket

The **Logitech MX Keys S** (~$110) and the **Keychron K4** (~$100) cost almost the same and are both excellent keyboards for home office work. But they represent opposite philosophies: the MX Keys prioritizes quiet, low-profile typing for office environments; the K4 delivers the tactile feedback of full mechanical switches on a 96% layout that fits a small desk.

Which one you should buy depends entirely on one thing: how you feel about typing noise and tactile feedback.

- Backlight: RGB per-key
- Frame: Aluminum (hot-swap version) or plastic (base version)
- OS: Windows, macOS (keycap legends for both)

## Head-to-Head Analysis

### Typing Feel

**Winner: Keychron K4 (for tactile feedback); MX Keys (for quiet satisfaction)**

This is subjective but also the whole decision. Mechanical switches — especially Gateron Browns — deliver a satisfying tactile bump that makes typing feel deliberate and responsive. Writers and coders who spend hours typing often prefer this. Logitech's scissor switches are smooth and quiet but feel more like a premium laptop keyboard than a mechanical board. Neither is objectively better; it depends on what you value.

### Noise Level

**Winner: Logitech MX Keys S**

The MX Keys S is genuinely quiet — appropriate for open offices, shared spaces, and video calls without muting. The Keychron K4 with Brown switches is moderately loud (noticeably clickier than a laptop), and with Blue switches it becomes a coworker complaint waiting to happen. If you're on video calls frequently or work in a shared space, MX Keys is the choice.

### Multi-Device Switching

**Winner: Tie**

Both keyboards support up to 3 connected devices and switch between them. The MX Keys S uses Logi Bolt for one of its connections (giving 2.4GHz stability for that device), which is an edge for mixed setups. The Keychron K4 adds a 2.4GHz receiver slot as well in addition to Bluetooth. For most users, switching on either keyboard works identically well.

### Build Quality

**Winner: Keychron K4 (aluminum version)**

The hot-swap Keychron K4 with aluminum top frame is noticeably more solid than the MX Keys' plastic frame. It's heavier but doesn't flex. If build feel matters, the Keychron wins. The plastic version of the K4 is roughly equivalent to MX Keys on this dimension.

### Battery Life

**Winner: Logitech MX Keys S**

5 months between charges (without backlighting) vs. 2 weeks on the Keychron K4. If you want to charge infrequently, MX Keys is far ahead.

### Desk Footprint

**Winner: Keychron K4**

Counterintuitive, but the 96% K4 takes up less space than the full-size MX Keys despite both having numpads. The 96% layout eliminates the gap between the numpad and navigation cluster, shrinking overall width. If your [home office setup](/articles/best-home-office-setup-under-1000/) is space-constrained, the K4 fits more cleanly.

**Should I buy the Logitech MX Keys or a Keychron mechanical keyboard?**

**How noisy is the Logitech MX Keys compared to mechanical keyboards?**
The MX Keys S is office-quiet — its low-profile scissor switches are virtually inaudible to people sitting nearby. Keychron K4 with Red switches is moderate; Brown switches add audible tactile click; Blue switches are clearly loud. Only Reds or silent switches are office-appropriate.

**Which keyboard is better for multi-device switching?**
Both keyboards support up to 3 devices, but the Logitech MX Keys S uses Easy Switch buttons for one-button device switching without re-pairing. The Keychron K4 switches between Bluetooth and 2.4GHz wireless using a physical mode switch, requiring a second button press to select the target device.

Yes. The Keychron K4 with Brown switches provides satisfying tactile feedback for all-day typing without the fatigue that linear switches can cause for writers. The 96% layout keeps the numpad without adding desktop width, making it efficient for data-entry work.

**Which keyboard has better battery life — Logitech MX Keys or Keychron?**
The Logitech MX Keys S lasts 10 days with backlight on and up to 5 months with backlighting off. The Keychron K4 runs approximately 2 weeks wirelessly at moderate backlight use. For battery anxiety-free use, the MX Keys S in backlight-off mode wins decisively.
