## Foil vs. Rotary: The Real Difference

This is the first decision to make when buying an electric shaver.

**Foil shavers** (Braun, Panasonic) trap hairs in a perforated metal foil and cut them with oscillating blades beneath. They excel on flat facial areas, are generally gentler on sensitive skin, and produce a closer shave on most men. They're less flexible around curved areas like the neck.

**Rotary shavers** (Philips Norelco) use three or four spinning circular heads that flex independently to follow facial contours. They're better for men with circular or irregular growth patterns and work excellently around the chin, jawline, and neck. They're slightly less close on large flat areas like cheeks.

## What to Look For When Buying

**Match the shaver type to your growth pattern:** Straight or minimal growth → foil. Curly, irregular, or heavy neck growth → rotary.

**Wet/dry rating is worth paying for:** Shaving in the shower softens hair more effectively than any pre-shave product, and it's significantly faster. Almost every quality shaver in 2026 supports wet/dry use.

**Cleaning stations save time but add cost:** They're convenient for daily users — but they use cleaning cartridges (~$15–$25 per cartridge, lasting 2–3 months). Budget accordingly.

**Head replacement is an ongoing cost:** Factor ~$50/year for premium shaver heads when evaluating total cost of ownership.
