## What to Look For in a Power Recliner

### Motor Quality
Cheap motors are loud and fail within a few years. Look for brands with at least a 1-year motor warranty; better brands offer 3–5 years.

### Upholstery
- **Genuine leather:** Most durable, premium feel, expensive
- **Top-grain leather:** Slightly less durable, still high quality
- **Bonded/faux leather:** Budget-friendly, wipes clean, peels over time
- **Fabric:** Comfortable, breathable, harder to clean

### Seat Depth and Width
Measure your current chair. Most standard recliners have a 21-inch seat width. Petite users do better with 19–20 inches; larger users need 24+ inches.

### Additional Features
- **USB charging:** Nearly standard on mid-range and above
- **Massage:** Vibration (not true massage) — worth it for relaxation, not medical therapy
- **Heat:** Helpful for lumbar tension and cold climates
- **Zero-gravity:** A preset position that elevates legs above heart — reduces pressure on the spine

### Wall Clearance
Standard recliners need 10–12 inches from the wall. Wall-huggers need 4–6 inches. Measure before you buy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do power recliners last?**
A quality power recliner with proper care should last 10–20 years. The motor is typically the first component to wear; look for brands with motor warranties of 3+ years.

**Can power recliners be repaired?**
Yes — most motors and mechanisms are standard parts that can be replaced. La-Z-Boy, in particular, has an extensive repair network.

**Are power recliners worth the premium over manual?**
For elderly users, those with mobility limitations, or anyone who uses their recliner daily, yes — the ability to adjust position precisely without effort makes a significant difference.
