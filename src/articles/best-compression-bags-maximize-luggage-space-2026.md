## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between roll-up compression bags and vacuum compression bags?**
Roll-up compression bags remove air by rolling from the bottom up, sealing it out as you go — no pump needed. They work well for travel where you don't want to carry a vacuum. Vacuum compression bags require a vacuum or pump to fully remove air and achieve maximum compression (up to 80% volume reduction). Vacuum bags achieve more compression but require a vacuum at home for initial packing.

**Do compression bags wrinkle clothes?**
Yes — compressing clothes will cause wrinkles, particularly in structured items like dress shirts and trousers. Compression bags work best for casual clothes, sweaters, jeans, and soft items. For wrinkle-sensitive clothing, roll clothes before placing in compression bags (reduces crease lines), or use packing cubes instead for those specific items.

**Can you use compression bags for carry-on travel?**
Yes — roll-up compression bags (no vacuum required) are ideal for carry-on travel. They reduce volume as you pack and don't require a pump to repack when returning. Vacuum bags require vacuum access at your destination to repack, making them better suited to checked bag travel.

**How much space do compression bags actually save?**
Roll-up bags typically reduce clothing volume by 40–60%. Vacuum bags achieve 60–80% reduction for soft items like sweaters and jackets. Results vary significantly by item type — jeans compress less than fleece, and structured items like jackets compress less than t-shirts. Realistic expectation: fit 2–3 extra days of casual clothing in the same bag space.
