## What to Look For

- **Drawer slide quality:** Ball-bearing slides are essential in a tool chest. Friction slides bind when loaded and wear out quickly. Always confirm ball-bearing construction before buying.
- **Steel gauge:** Lower gauge numbers mean thicker, stronger steel. 18-gauge or better for drawer bodies; 20-gauge is acceptable for cabinet walls. Avoid unnamed brands that don't specify gauge.
- **Load capacity per drawer:** Professional chests rate drawers at 75–100 lbs each. Budget units with unrated drawers will sag and bind when fully loaded with sockets and wrenches.
- **Bag material:** For portable bags, 1680D or higher ballistic nylon or polyester resists abrasion and tearing. Lower-denier materials look fine initially but degrade quickly on a jobsite.
- **Mobility needs:** Decide whether you need the storage to move before you buy. A stationary chest has more drawer space; a rolling unit trades some capacity for jobsite flexibility.
- **Lock quality:** If tools will be in a shared space or truck bed, confirm the lock mechanism is keyed and secures all drawers simultaneously rather than one at a time.
