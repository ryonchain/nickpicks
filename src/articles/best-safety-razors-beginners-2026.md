## Why Switch to Safety Razors

The economics alone justify the switch: cartridge refills run $3–$5 per cartridge; double-edge blades run $0.15–$0.50 each. A safety razor pays for itself in 6–12 months versus even mid-priced cartridge systems.

Beyond cost:
- **Fewer ingrown hairs:** One blade cuts once; cartridge razors cut 3–6 times per stroke, increasing lifting and ingrown hair risk
- **Better for sensitive skin:** Less blade passes = less cumulative skin irritation
- **Closer shave (with practice):** A sharp single blade at the correct angle outperforms a dulling cartridge on most skin types
- **Environmental:** A handful of blades per month versus disposable plastic cartridges

## Essential Beginner Tips

**Always shave with the grain on your first pass.** Against-the-grain is a second-pass technique for experienced shavers. Going ATG too early leads to irritation and cuts.

**No pressure — let the weight work.** If you feel the blade pulling, you're pressing. Relax the grip and let the razor glide.

**Short strokes beat long strokes.** 1–2 inch strokes with blade rinse between give better angle control than long swipes.

**Buy a blade sampler before committing:** Astra, Derby, Feather, Gillette Silver Blue, and Personna all shave differently on different skin and hair types. Start with Astra Superior Platinum; branch from there.
