## Understanding LED Grow Light Specs

### PAR vs Lumens vs Wattage

**Lumens** measure light as perceived by human eyes — useless for plants. **PAR** (Photosynthetically Active Radiation) measures light in the 400–700nm wavelength range plants use. Look for **PPFD** (Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density) in μmol/m²/s at the canopy distance — this is the single most useful spec.

Target PPFD by stage:
- Seedlings/clones: 100–300 μmol/m²/s
- Vegetative growth: 400–600 μmol/m²/s
- Flowering/fruiting: 600–900 μmol/m²/s

**Wattage** tells you power consumption, not light output. LED efficiency varies enormously. A 200W LED from a quality brand outperforms a 400W LED from a cheap brand.

### Full Spectrum: What It Actually Means

True "full spectrum" LEDs include:
- Blue (400–500nm): Vegetative growth, photosynthesis efficiency
- Red (600–700nm): Flowering and fruiting
- Green (500–600nm): Canopy penetration
- IR (700–800nm): Stem elongation, photomorphogenesis
- UV (280–400nm): Trichome development, flavonoid production (not all models)

Cheap "full spectrum" lights often omit IR and UV and produce lower efficiency per watt.

## Full Spectrum Grow Light Buying Guide

### Matching Light to Plant Type

**Low-light plants (pothos, snake plant, peace lily):** Any supplemental light works — even Barrina T5 strips or a clip-on light adds meaningful growth improvement over window light alone.

**Medium-light plants (herbs, most leafy greens, seedlings):** Barrina T5 strips or Mars Hydro TS 1000 on medium setting. PPFD target: 200–400 μmol/m²/s.

**High-light plants (tomatoes, peppers, cannabis, orchids):** Spider Farmer SF-2000 or AC Infinity IONBOARD at proper distance. PPFD target: 600–900 μmol/m²/s.

### Light Duration by Plant Type

- Seedlings/clones: 18–20 hours light / 4–6 hours dark
- Vegetative plants: 16–18 hours light
- Flowering/fruiting: 12 hours light / 12 hours dark (triggers flowering in many species)
- Herbs and greens: 14–16 hours light
- Succulents/cacti: 12–14 hours light

### Distance from Canopy

The PPFD values above assume a specific distance (typically 12–24 inches). Moving the light 2× farther drops intensity to 25% (inverse square law). Most manufacturers provide a PPFD chart showing intensity at different heights — use it to dial in your setup.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What does "full spectrum" mean for grow lights?**
It means the light covers the PAR range (400–700nm) plants use for photosynthesis, typically including blue (vegetative), red (flowering), and sometimes IR and UV. All grow lights on this list are full spectrum. The quality of that spectrum (diode efficiency, wavelength coverage) varies significantly.

**How many hours should grow lights run?**
Most indoor plants thrive on 14–18 hours of light per day with 6–10 hours of darkness. Always use a timer — plants need a consistent dark period for proper hormonal regulation. Continuous light harms most species.

**Are LED grow lights dangerous to look at?**
Yes. Never look directly into an operating LED grow light without UV-blocking safety glasses. The high-intensity blue wavelengths cause retinal damage over time even if the light doesn't appear extremely bright.

**Do grow lights increase electric bills significantly?**
A 200W grow light running 16 hours/day uses 3.2 kWh/day or ~96 kWh/month. At $0.15/kWh average, that's ~$14.40/month. Factor this into your growing cost analysis, especially for cannabis or year-round herb gardens.
