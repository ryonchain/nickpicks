## Hair Serum Buying Guide

### Oil vs. Serum: What's the Difference?

Hair oils (like Moroccanoil and Kerastase Elixir) deliver fatty acids and shine primarily by coating the cuticle. Hair serums (like Olaplex No. 7) may use a water or silicone base to carry active ingredients deeper into the shaft for structural repair. The best luxury options often do both.

### How to Pick by Hair Type

**Fine hair:** Avoid heavy silicone formulas that flatten. Look for featherlight oils (Bumble and bumble, Moroccanoil Light) or concentrated serums (Olaplex No. 7 — one pump is enough).

**Thick, coarse hair:** Heavier oil blends (Kerastase Elixir Ultime) provide the weight needed to smooth and control.

**Color-treated hair:** Prioritize bond-building (Olaplex No. 7) and heat protection.

**Aging or thinning hair:** Scalp-focused serums (Kerastase Chronologiste) address the root cause rather than the symptom.

### When to Apply

Apply most hair serums on **damp hair** after towel-drying, before heat styling. For finishing, use 1 drop on **dry hair** to tame flyaways. Scalp serums go on a dry or slightly damp scalp, not on lengths.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best luxury hair serum for damaged hair?**
Olaplex No. 7 Bonding Oil is the gold standard for damaged, bleached, or chemically processed hair. Its patented bond-building technology repairs internal hair structure — not just the surface — which is unique in the serum category. **Can I use a luxury hair serum every day?**
Most finishing serums (Moroccanoil, Kerastase Elixir Ultime) can be used daily in small amounts. Scalp treatment serums like Kerastase Chronologiste are designed for 2–3 times per week. Overusing any serum can lead to buildup — if hair starts to feel heavy or look dull, use a clarifying shampoo once a week.

**Is Kerastase or Moroccanoil better?**
Both are exceptional — the choice depends on your goal. Kerastase Elixir Ultime excels for shine and overall hair health, while Moroccanoil Treatment is the better frizz-fighter for fine and blonde hair. Many stylists use both: Moroccanoil before blow-drying and Kerastase for finishing.
