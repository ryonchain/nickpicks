A [standing desk](/articles/best-standing-desk-under-500/) without an anti-fatigue mat is a genuine mistake. ## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Standing Desk Mat

**Flat vs. terrain.** Flat mats provide basic cushioning and are fine for standing sessions under 30 minutes. If you're still dialing in your [standing desk ergonomics](/articles/how-to-set-up-standing-desk/), getting the right mat height is part of that process. Terrain-style mats (Topo) provide better fatigue reduction for longer sessions because they encourage movement. If you plan to stand for 1+ hour at a stretch, terrain is worth the extra cost.

**Thickness matters.** The effective range is 0.5–0.75 inches. Thinner mats compress too quickly. Mats over 1 inch become unstable underfoot. Most quality options fall in the 0.65–0.75 inch range.

**Size relative to your desk.** A mat should be at least as wide as your shoulder width (roughly 18–24 inches minimum). Standard sizes are 20" × 39" or 24" × 36" — either works for a single-person standing desk.

**Beveled edge is non-negotiable.** A flat-cut edge on a floor mat is a tripping hazard. Confirm the mat you're buying has a tapered or beveled perimeter.

**Cleaning.** If you eat at your desk, a mat with a smooth top surface (WellnessMats, Gorilla Grip) is easier to wipe clean than a textured foam surface. Textured mats look fine but trap crumbs.

**How thick does an anti-fatigue mat need to be?**
At least 3/4 inch of thickness is needed to meaningfully reduce joint compression from hard floors. Mats under 1/2 inch provide minimal cushioning benefit. The Topo at 3/4 inch base and WellnessMats at 3/4 inch flat are both at the right thickness threshold.

**Do anti-fatigue mats work on carpet?**
Yes, but carpeted floors already provide some cushioning, so the benefit is reduced compared to hard floors. Choose a mat with a non-slip bottom designed for carpet surfaces, as smooth-bottom mats slide on carpet pile.
