## Pizza Oven Buying Guide

**Outdoor vs. indoor:** Outdoor gas and wood-fired ovens reach 950°F — the authentic Neapolitan sweet spot. Indoor electrics peak at 750–850°F and take 2–3 minutes instead of 60–90 seconds. Both produce vastly better pizza than a conventional oven.

**Gas vs. wood vs. charcoal:** Gas offers repeatable convenience — fire it up, set the heat, cook. Wood and charcoal add authentic smoky flavor and the satisfaction of fire management. Multi-fuel ovens like the Karu 12G let you choose per session.

**Stone size matters:** A 12-inch stone accommodates most family pizzas. A 16-inch stone handles larger pies and makes it easier to rotate without touching the sides. If you regularly cook for groups, the larger stone is worth the price premium.
