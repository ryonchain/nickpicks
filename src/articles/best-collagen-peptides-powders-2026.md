## How to Take Collagen Effectively

**Dose:** Most clinical trials showing skin benefits used 2.5–10g/day. Benefits at higher doses (15–20g) are similar to lower doses in some studies. 10–20g is the typical supplementation range.

**Timing:** Timing appears to matter less than consistency. Morning in coffee is the most popular routine — the heat dissolves it easily and it becomes a habit anchor.

**Vitamin C pairing:** Vitamin C is required for collagen synthesis. Take collagen alongside a vitamin C-rich food or supplement to support your body's ability to incorporate the amino acid building blocks.

**What to expect:**
- Skin elasticity and hydration: 6–8 weeks
- Wrinkle reduction: 8–12 weeks
- Joint discomfort reduction: 8–12 weeks
- Hair and nail improvements: 4–8 weeks

**Who benefits most:**
- Adults over 35 (natural collagen production is declining)
- People with joint pain from exercise or osteoarthritis
- Anyone concerned about skin aging
- Vegetarians who consume fish (for marine collagen)

Note: Collagen is not a complete protein — it lacks tryptophan and is low in some essential amino acids. It supplements, rather than replaces, protein from complete sources.
