## What Size Rug for a Dorm Room?

Most dorm rooms are 12x10 to 15x12 feet — with furniture taking up most of it. The sweet spot for a rug:

**5x7 or 5x8** — covers the center of the room without going under furniture. Best choice for most dorms.

**4x6** — works for single-person rooms where you want coverage under the desk chair area or in front of the bed.

**3x5** — too small unless you're just covering one zone (desk area or bedside).

Avoid anything bigger than 6x9 unless you're sure about clearance from your floor plan.

## Rug Pad vs. No Rug Pad in Dorms

**On hard floors (tile, hardwood, laminate):** Get a rug pad. Without one, the rug will slide every time you step on it and it's a trip hazard. A thin non-slip rug pad costs $15–20 and makes any rug safer.

**On carpet:** Many dorms have institutional carpet. A rug pad still helps prevent bunching but is less critical. Low-profile rugs work better on carpet than thick shag styles.

## Dorm Rug Care

**Vacuuming:** Once a week for high-traffic areas. Low-pile rugs clean faster.

**Spot cleaning:** Blot spills immediately — never rub. A small bottle of carpet spot cleaner ($8) lives under most dorm desks.

**Machine washing (if applicable):** Use cold water, gentle cycle, air dry flat. Tumble drying can cause shrinkage and pill the surface.

**End-of-year storage:** Roll, don't fold. Folding creates permanent creases. A storage bag ($10) protects it over summer break.
