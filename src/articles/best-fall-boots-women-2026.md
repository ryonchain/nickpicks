## How to Choose Women's Fall Boots

**By heel height:**
- **Flat/Chelsea:** Most wearable, good for walking. Classic and versatile.
- **Block heel (1–2 in):** More polished than flat; stable enough for all-day wear.
- **Stiletto/kitten (2–3 in):** Dressier; less practical for long days.

**By shaft height:**
- **Ankle:** Most versatile. Works with jeans, dresses, skirts, trousers.
- **Mid-calf:** Good with skirts and dresses; can make legs appear shorter.
- **Knee-high:** Polished. Works with miniskirts, pencil skirts, slim pants.
- **Over-the-knee:** Statement-making. Best with short hemlines.

**By material:**
- **Genuine leather:** Lasts longest, develops a patina, takes water if untreated.
- **Faux leather/vegan:** More affordable, more weather-resistant, shorter lifespan.
- **Suede:** Beautiful but requires waterproofing spray; avoid in rain.
- **Rubber:** For rain only. Hunter, Joules, Muck Boot.

**Calf fit:** If you have wider calves, look for boots with stretch panels or adjustable calf circumference. Wide-calf options are increasingly available but still limited.
