## The Core Question

Apple's lineup now has two AirPods models with ANC: the AirPods 4 (ANC) at $179 and AirPods Pro 2 at $249. The $70 gap is the most interesting decision point in Apple's earbud lineup. This breakdown tells you exactly what you get for those extra dollars.

**TL;DR verdict:** Get AirPods Pro 2 if you commute, travel, or prioritize strong noise cancellation. Get AirPods 4 (ANC) if you prioritize all-day comfort without silicone tips, or if you mostly listen in moderate-noise environments.

## ANC: The Biggest Difference

This is where AirPods Pro 2 justifies its price premium.

**AirPods Pro 2 ANC** is among the best in any earbud category. In-ear silicone tips create a physical seal that blocks passive noise before ANC even activates. Apple's H2 chip then adds computational noise cancellation on top. Combined result: outstanding on airplanes, subways, and open-plan offices.

**AirPods 4 ANC** is physically limited by the open-ear design. Without a seal, passive isolation is minimal — ANC must do all the work against ambient noise that's already penetrating the ear. It handles low-frequency drone (HVAC, distant traffic) reasonably well, but high-frequency sounds (nearby conversations, keyboard clatter) pass through more than on the Pro 2.

**Real-world impact:**
- Quiet home/office → Both work fine
- Coffee shop → Both work fine
- Commute (subway/bus) → Pro 2 clearly better
- Airplane → Pro 2 clearly better
- Open-plan office → Pro 2 clearly better

## Fit: The AirPods 4 Advantage

AirPods Pro 2 use silicone ear tips (XS/S/M/L included). For most listeners, this in-ear fit is comfortable and provides superior acoustic performance. But for some users — particularly those who find in-ear tips uncomfortable after 2-3 hours — it's a dealbreaker.

AirPods 4 rest in the outer ear without any tip. They're essentially weightless for extended wear. If you wear earbuds for 6+ hours daily (work calls, music, podcasts), the open-ear comfort of AirPods 4 may be more important than Pro 2's ANC advantage.

---

## Health Features: Pro 2 Exclusive

AirPods Pro 2 received a major iOS 18 software update adding:
- **Clinical Hearing Test:** An audiologist-grade hearing assessment you take in 5 minutes at home via the iPhone Health app
- **Hearing Aid Mode:** FDA-cleared hearing aid functionality for mild to moderate hearing loss — equivalent to $1,000+ OTC hearing aids
- **Hearing Protection:** Real-time loud noise alerts and exposure tracking

These features are exclusive to AirPods Pro 2. If hearing health is a concern — or if you have a family member with hearing difficulty — the Pro 2 justifies its price on these features alone.

## Battery: Nearly Equal

- **AirPods 4 (ANC):** 5 hrs/charge (ANC on), 30 hrs total with case
- **AirPods Pro 2:** 6 hrs/charge (ANC on), 30 hrs total with case

The 1-hour per-charge difference is minor for most users. Both give you full-day listening when you use the case for top-ups.

**Case charging:**
- AirPods 4 (ANC) includes wireless charging (Qi/Qi2)
- AirPods Pro 2 includes MagSafe (stronger magnetic alignment) + Qi/Qi2

## Which Should You Buy?

### Buy AirPods Pro 2 ($249) if:
- You commute by subway, train, or plane regularly
- You work in a loud open-plan office
- You want the strongest possible ANC
- Hearing health features interest you (hearing test + hearing aid mode)
- You're fine with in-ear silicone tip fit

### Buy AirPods 4 ANC ($179) if:
- You primarily use earbuds at home, in quiet offices, or outdoors
- You find in-ear silicone tips uncomfortable for long wear
- You want ANC but don't need it to be class-leading
- $70 matters (it's real money)
- You mostly use earbuds for calls and casual music

### Don't buy AirPods 4 ANC if:
- You travel or commute frequently — the ANC gap is noticeable in high-noise environments
