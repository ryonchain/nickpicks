## Jade Roller vs. Gua Sha: Which Do You Need?

**Jade Rollers** are gentler and better for beginners. The rolling motion promotes lymphatic drainage and reduces puffiness, especially when the stone is chilled in the refrigerator. They're also good for pressing serums into skin immediately after application.

**Gua Sha** requires slightly more technique — you scrape the stone along contour lines at a 45-degree angle — but produces stronger results for facial sculpting, releasing tension in the jaw and neck muscles, and improving product absorption. Most experienced users eventually use both.

If you can only pick one, start with a roller for ease of use. If you're ready to commit to a technique, the gua sha delivers more visible results.

## How to Use Jade Rollers and Gua Sha

**For jade rolling:**
1. Cleanse and apply serum or facial oil (tools glide better and help products absorb)
2. Start at the neck and roll upward and outward — always upward, never down
3. Roll each area 3–5 times with light pressure
4. Use the small end around eyes and nose bridge

**For gua sha:**
1. Apply a facial oil generously — dry scraping can irritate skin
2. Hold the stone at 15–45 degrees against the skin
3. Use light pressure with long, upward strokes along contour lines
4. Spend extra time on jaw, neck, and any tension points

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is jade or rose quartz better?**
Nephrite jade stays cooler naturally and is slightly denser. Rose quartz has a warmer, more porous texture. Both work well — the quality of the frame and stone cut matters more than the mineral type.

**How often should I use a jade roller?**
Daily is fine. Many people use their roller morning and evening — morning to depuff, evening to press in nighttime serums.

**Do I need to refrigerate my roller?**
Not required, but refrigerating your roller for 10–15 minutes before use amplifies the depuffing effect significantly. Many people keep a dedicated roller in their bathroom fridge.

**Can gua sha cause bruising?**
Only if you use too much pressure. The goal is gentle, consistent strokes — not aggressive scraping. Start with very light pressure and build.

**How do I clean my tools?**
Wipe down with a damp cloth after each use. For deeper cleaning, a gentle soap and lukewarm water works. Avoid submerging for long periods, which can loosen the adhesive in the roller frame.
