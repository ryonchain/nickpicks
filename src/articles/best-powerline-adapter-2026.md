# Best Powerline Adapters 2026: Wired-Speed Internet Through Any Room

Sometimes Wi-Fi isn't the answer. If you have a gaming console in a basement, a streaming box in a bedroom, or a desktop PC on a different floor from your router — and running Ethernet cable isn't practical — a powerline adapter kit delivers a wired-quality connection through electrical outlets you already have.

Powerline adapters transmit data over your home's existing electrical wiring. Plug one adapter into an outlet near your router and connect it via Ethernet. Plug the second anywhere in your home, connect your device, and you have a near-wired connection without drilling holes or hiding cable runs under rugs.

## Powerline Adapter vs Wi-Fi Extender vs Mesh Wi-Fi

| Solution | Best For | Speed | Setup | Cost |
|----------|----------|-------|-------|------|
| Powerline adapter | Wired devices far from router | High, consistent | Very easy | $30–$130 |
| Wi-Fi extender | Wireless devices in dead zones | Medium (50% loss) | Easy | $40–$180 |
| Mesh Wi-Fi node | Whole-home wireless upgrade | High, seamless | Moderate | $100–$400+ |

**Choose powerline when:** You want to connect a gaming console, streaming stick, smart TV, or desktop PC in a room where running Ethernet cable isn't practical. Nothing beats wired for consistency.

**Choose a Wi-Fi extender when:** You have many wireless devices spread across a dead zone and don't want to run any cables at all.

**Choose mesh Wi-Fi when:** Your whole home has poor coverage and you want the cleanest, fastest wireless solution with seamless roaming.

## What Affects Powerline Speed?

**Electrical wiring age:** Older wiring (pre-1990s) or aluminum wiring can reduce speeds significantly. Newer copper wiring in modern homes performs best.

**Circuit proximity:** Both adapters should ideally be on the same electrical circuit. Adapters on different circuits passing through a breaker panel will see reduced speeds.

**Surge protectors:** Do NOT plug powerline adapters into surge protectors or power strips. The filtering components in surge protectors block the powerline signal. Always plug directly into a wall outlet.

**Other electrical interference:** DECT phones, dimmer switches, and some appliances can cause interference. If speeds are low, try different outlets.

## FAQ

**Are powerline adapters as fast as Ethernet cable?**
No — even the best powerline adapters deliver 60–75% of their rated speed in real-world conditions, and that speed varies with wiring quality. But they're far more consistent than Wi-Fi extenders and sufficient for 4K streaming and online gaming.

**Do powerline adapters work between floors?**
Yes, in most homes. Performance between floors is typically slightly lower than on the same floor, but still sufficient for streaming and gaming in most modern homes.

**How many powerline adapters can I add to one network?**
Most kits support up to 16 adapters on a single network. Additional adapters (sold separately) can extend to more rooms.
