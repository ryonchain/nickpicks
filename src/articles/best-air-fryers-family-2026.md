## What Makes a Great Family Air Fryer

Cooking for a family isn't the same as cooking for yourself. You need capacity for full portions, speed to get dinner on the table fast, and a basket that doesn't take 20 minutes to clean afterward. Here's what to prioritize:

- **Capacity:** 5 qt is the minimum for a family of 4. 6–8 qt is the sweet spot. Dual-basket designs effectively double your cooking space.
- **Speed:** A 1700–1800W heating element cooks faster than 1500W budget models — important when you're racing against hungry kids.
- **Easy cleaning:** Dishwasher-safe baskets are non-negotiable for daily family cooking. Hand-washing a greasy basket every night gets old fast.
- **Temperature range:** 105–450°F covers everything from dehydrating fruit snacks to high-heat chicken thighs.
- **Safety features:** Auto-shutoff, cool-touch handles, and stable non-tip bases matter when small hands are nearby.

## Buying Tips for Family Air Fryers

**Measure your counter space first.** An 8 qt air fryer takes up significantly more counter space than a 5 qt. Measure the width, depth, and overhead clearance (allow 4–6 inches above the unit) before buying.

**Dual basket vs. large single basket:** Dual-basket models let you cook two different foods simultaneously at different settings — worth the premium for most families. Large single baskets are better for batch cooking where everything cooks at the same setting.

**Check accessory availability.** Popular models like Cosori and Ninja have robust accessory ecosystems — silicone liners, skewer sets, pizza pans, and multi-layer racks that multiply what you can cook. Lesser-known brands may not have compatible accessories.
