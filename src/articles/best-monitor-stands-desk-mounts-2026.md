## Monitor Stand & Desk Mount Buying Guide

### Arm vs. Riser: Which Do You Need?

| Setup Goal | Best Solution |
|-----------|--------------|
| Raise monitor height only | Riser stand (budget, no modification) |
| Full adjustability + desk space | Articulating monitor arm |
| Two monitors | Dual monitor arm |
| Heavy ultrawide (25+ lbs) | Heavy-duty arm (North Bayou F80 or Ergotron Heavy Duty) |

### VESA Compatibility: What to Check

Every monitor with VESA support has a bolt-hole pattern on the back. The most common are:

| VESA Pattern | Monitor Size Range |
|-------------|------------------|
| 75x75mm | Smaller monitors, 22" and under |
| 100x100mm | Most 24"–34" monitors |
| 200x100mm | Some ultrawide monitors |
| 200x200mm | Large 40"+ displays |

Check your monitor's spec sheet for VESA pattern before purchasing an arm. Some monitors (particularly slim designs) are not VESA compatible.

### Key Features to Prioritize

**1. Weight rating vs. your monitor's actual weight** — Find your monitor's weight in its specs page. Leave at least 10–20% headroom under the arm's max rating for longevity.

**2. Desk clamp thickness** — Most desk clamps max at 2.5"–3.5" thickness. Measure your desk edge before ordering, especially for thick hardwood desks.

**3. Cable management** — Internal cable routing (like Ergotron and Humanscale) results in a dramatically cleaner setup than clip-based routing.

**4. Gas spring vs. spring tension** — Gas springs (Ergotron LX) hold position more precisely and last longer. Spring tension arms (North Bayou) are heavier-duty but require occasional retensioning.

**5. Reach** — Most single arms extend 20–25" from the mount point. Confirm the arm can reach the center of your desk from the edge where the clamp attaches.

## FAQs

**Does using a monitor arm void my monitor warranty?**
No. Mounting a monitor via its VESA mount points is an intended use case. Monitor warranties cover manufacturing defects, not mounting choices. Just ensure you use the correct VESA bolts for your monitor's depth (too-long bolts can damage the panel from behind).

**Can I use a monitor arm on a standing desk?**
Yes — and they work especially well on sit-stand desks. As you adjust desk height, the monitor arm lets you independently fine-tune the screen position for both sitting and standing ergonomics. Confirm the arm's C-clamp fits your desk's edge thickness.

**What's the correct monitor height when using an arm?**
The top of your monitor should be at or slightly below eye level when seated with your back straight and head neutral. Your eyes should naturally rest at roughly the top 1/3 of the visible screen area. This typically means raising most monitors 2–5" above where the stock stand puts them.
