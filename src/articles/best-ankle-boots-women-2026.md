## Ankle Boot Fit Tips

**Width:** Most ankle boots are cut for medium (B) width. If you have wide feet, look for brands that offer wide-width options (New Balance, Clarks, Naturalizer).

**Calf opening:** Doesn't matter for ankle boots — they sit below the calf. You only need to worry about calf fit for knee-high or OTK boots.

**Block heel vs. stiletto:** Block heel = more stable for walking. Stiletto = dressier, harder on feet. For work, block heel wins. For evening, your call.

**Break-in period:** All leather boots require 1–2 weeks of wearing short periods before they conform to your foot. Don't judge a leather boot by the first few wears.

## Styling Ankle Boots

**Ankle boots + skinny jeans:** Tuck jeans into the boots or cuff above the ankle to show the boot shaft.

**Ankle boots + wide-leg jeans:** Let the hem skim the top of the boot. The contrast between the chunky denim and the boot looks intentional.

**Ankle boots + midi dress/skirt:** Leave a sliver of bare leg (or wear tights) between the hem and the boot top. Monochromatic colors create the most elegant effect.

**Ankle boots + trousers:** Slightly cropped trousers that show the ankle and boot shaft look the most polished.
