## How to Choose a USB Hub

### Identify your most important ports first

Make a list of what you'll actually plug in: monitor (HDMI?), USB drives (USB-A?), wired internet (Ethernet?), phone charging (USB-C?), camera cards (SD?). A 10-port hub is only useful if it has the ports you need. Most users are fully covered by HDMI + 2× USB-A + Ethernet + SD + PD charging.

### Power Delivery: 85W vs. 100W

For most 13–14" laptops, 85W PD is sufficient. For 15–16" MacBook Pro or high-performance Windows laptops (120W+ native chargers), look for 100W PD pass-through to maintain full charging speed.

### 4K@30Hz vs. 4K@60Hz HDMI

For an office monitor used primarily for documents, spreadsheets, and web browsing, 4K@30Hz is adequate. For design work, video editing, gaming, or any fast-moving content, 4K@60Hz is worth the small premium. The Ugreen 9-in-1 and Belkin Connect both offer 4K@60Hz.

### USB hub vs. Thunderbolt dock

If your laptop has Thunderbolt 4, consider a Thunderbolt dock for your permanent desk setup. Thunderbolt docks provide dedicated bandwidth to each port (no bandwidth sharing), support multiple 4K monitors, and deliver stable high-speed data. For travel or casual use, a standard USB-C hub is sufficient and much cheaper.
