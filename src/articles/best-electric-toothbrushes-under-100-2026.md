## Buying Guide

### Oscillating vs. Sonic: Which Technology Is Better?

**Oscillating-Rotating (Oral-B):**
- Small round head moves 8,800 times/minute
- Most clinical research supporting effectiveness
- Better for thorough individual-tooth cleaning
- Slightly more aggressive feel

**Sonic (Sonicare):**
- Full brush head moves 31,000 times/minute
- Fluid dynamics action cleans between teeth
- Gentler feel — better for sensitive users
- Excellent stain removal

Both technologies work. The difference is small. Choose based on sensitivity preference.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to floss if I use an electric toothbrush?**
Yes. Electric brushes clean surfaces better, but flossing (or a water flosser) is still necessary for between-tooth cleaning.

**Can children use adult electric toothbrushes?**
Most brands have children's versions with smaller heads and lower pressure. Adults can share handles but should use separate brush heads.

**How long should each brush head last?**
Replace every 3 months, or when bristles visibly fray — whichever comes first. Many brushes have color-fade indicator bristles.
