A luxury fragrance in beautiful packaging creates a multi-sensory gift experience that most recipients don't buy for themselves. The unboxing, the bottle, the scent experience — it hits differently than practical gifts. And unlike clothing or jewelry, sizing is never an issue.

Chanel No. 5 is the best-selling fragrance in history. Created by Coco Chanel in 1921, it's an abstract floral aldehyde — meaning it doesn't smell like any specific flower, but rather like the idea of luxury femininity. The formula has evolved subtly over the decades but remains immediately recognizable.

Gifting Chanel No. 5 is never wrong. The recipient understands instantly that they received something meaningful.

**Scent profile:** Floral aldehyde. Top notes: aldehydes, bergamot, lemon. Heart: rose, jasmine, iris, ylang ylang. Base: sandalwood, vetiver, vanilla, amber, civet.

**Gift sets available:** EDP + body lotion, EDP + shower gel, mini discovery set (4 x 0.05 oz), and full 1.7 or 3.4 oz EDP.

**Best for:** Mothers, grandmothers, formal gift occasions, classic-style women, recipients who appreciate heritage and tradition.

**Why the price is justified:** Chanel sources jasmine exclusively from Grasse, France — the most expensive jasmine cultivation region in the world. The formula uses real floral absolutes at concentrations that would be cost-prohibitive in any other fragrance.

## What to Know About Fragrance Longevity

Luxury fragrances last longer than mass-market perfumes because of higher fragrance oil concentrations. General expectations:

- **Chanel No. 5 EDP:** 6-8 hours on most skin types
- **Jo Malone Colognes:** 4-6 hours (they're EDCs by definition — lighter concentration)
- **Dior Miss Dior Blooming Bouquet:** 4-5 hours (EDT); 6-7 hours (EDP version)
- **YSL Black Opium EDP:** 7-9 hours (the base notes are particularly lasting)
- **REPLICA EDP:** 5-7 hours depending on the scent

For longevity: apply to pulse points (wrists, neck, inner elbow), don't rub after applying (it crushes the top notes), and moisturize skin first (fragrance lasts longer on hydrated skin).
