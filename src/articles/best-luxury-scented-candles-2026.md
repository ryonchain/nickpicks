## Luxury Candle Buying Guide

### What Makes a Candle "Luxury"?
True luxury candle markers:
- **Fragrance concentration**: 10–15% fragrance oil load vs 6–8% in mass-market
- **Wax quality**: Coconut wax or soy blend burns cleaner than pure paraffin
- **Wick type**: Cotton core wicks (not metal) reduce soot
- **Cold throw**: Quality fragrance is detectable without lighting
- **Vessel quality**: Glass or ceramic vessels (not tin) that are worth keeping

### Understanding Burn Time Claims
Manufacturer burn time claims assume ideal conditions: one room, no drafts, burning 3–4 hours per session. Real-world performance is typically 10–20% shorter. A 60-hour candle in real use: plan for 48–50 hours.

### How to Maximize Candle Life
- **First burn**: burn until the wax pool reaches all edges (2–3 hours). This prevents tunneling.
- **Trim the wick**: trim to 5mm before each burn to prevent excessive soot and uneven burning
- **Burn duration**: maximum 3–4 hours per session; let the candle cool completely before relighting
- **Placement**: avoid drafts and vents, which cause uneven burn

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are Diptyque candles worth the price?**
Yes, for most buyers. The fragrance concentration, cold throw quality, and brand pedigree are unmatched. The 190g candle at $68–$75 delivers 60 hours of burn — roughly $1.15–$1.25 per hour of excellent scent. Compared to a $30 mass-market candle with 40 hours, the cost difference is minor in practice.

**What's the best-smelling luxury candle?**
Scent is subjective, but Diptyque Baies and Nest Bamboo consistently top "best-smelling" rankings across publications and buyer reviews. Both are safe choices for most preferences.

**Can I use the Diptyque jar after the candle is finished?**
Yes. The oval glass jar is one of the most popular containers to repurpose. Clean the remaining wax with hot water and a small amount of dish soap. The jar works well as a small plant pot, jewelry holder, or pen cup.

**How long do luxury candles stay fresh if unlit?**
Most luxury candles retain full fragrance quality for 12–24 months if stored in a cool, dark place with the lid on. After opening, fragrance degradation accelerates slightly — use within 12 months of opening for best cold throw.
