## Air Purifier Buying Guide

### Understanding CADR Ratings

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) measures how many cubic feet per minute of clean air the purifier delivers for three particle types: smoke, dust, and pollen.

| Room Size | Minimum CADR Needed |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Up to 150 sq ft | 100 CFM |
| 150–300 sq ft | 150–200 CFM |
| 300–500 sq ft | 200–300 CFM |
| 500+ sq ft | 300+ CFM |

For bedrooms, aim for at least 4–5 Air Changes per Hour (ACH) — meaning the full room volume of air passes through the filter 4–5 times per hour.

### What to Look for in a Bedroom Air Purifier

**1. Noise level at lowest setting** — Bedroom purifiers spend most of their time on the lowest setting while you sleep. Look for units rated 25–35 dB on sleep/quiet mode.

**2. Filter cost per year** — Budget $30–$80/year for replacement filters. Factor this into the total cost of ownership, not just the purchase price.

**3. True HEPA vs. "HEPA-type"** — True HEPA captures 99.97% of particles ≥0.3 microns. "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-style" are marketing terms for inferior filters. Check for this distinction.

**4. Night-friendly display** — Bright LEDs or displays can disrupt sleep. Look for purifiers with display-off modes or very dim indicators.

**5. Auto mode with sensors** — Purifiers with built-in air quality sensors can automatically increase fan speed when pollution spikes (cooking smells, outdoor air coming in) and reduce it during clean periods.

## FAQs

**Where should I place an air purifier in my bedroom?**
Place it 3–5 feet off the floor (on a table or shelf), away from walls and corners where airflow is restricted. Keep it 6–12 inches from walls. The optimal position is near the source of pollutants or near your bed's breathing zone — not tucked in a corner.

**How often should I change the HEPA filter?**
Most manufacturers recommend every 6–12 months depending on air quality and usage hours. A good indicator: if the filter looks visibly gray/brown or air quality readings worsen despite the unit running, it's time to change it. Never wash a true HEPA filter — only pre-filters can be rinsed.

**Will an air purifier help with COVID or flu viruses?**
True HEPA filters capture particles ≥0.3 microns, which includes respiratory aerosols. Some viruses are smaller, but they typically travel on larger carrier droplets that HEPA does capture. Units with UV-C light or PlasmaWave technology add an additional inactivation layer. No air purifier eliminates all infection risk — ventilation and other precautions remain important.
