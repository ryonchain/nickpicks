## Buyer's Guide: Lip Plumpers vs. Glosses

### True Plumpers vs. Shine-Illusion Glosses
- **Active plumpers** (Too Faced Lip Injection): contain ingredients like capsaicin, ginger, or hyaluronic acid that genuinely increase blood flow, hydration, and temporary volume
- **Shine-illusion glosses** (NYX, Fenty): the reflective high-shine creates a visual fullness illusion — no active plumping but no tingling either
- **Overnight treatments** (Laneige): rebuild lip volume over time through deep conditioning

### Ingredients That Actually Plump
- **Hyaluronic acid:** Draws moisture in for hydration-based plumping
- **Ginger/capsaicin:** Creates a tingly sensation that increases blood flow to lips
- **Peptides:** Signal collagen production over time
- **Menthol:** Creates immediate swelling sensation

### Application Tips
- Apply plumper 10–15 minutes before an event for maximum effect
- Layer gloss over matte lipstick for added dimension and volume illusion
- For longest wear, use a lip liner as a base before gloss application

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do lip plumpers actually work?**
Yes — but the mechanism matters. Tingly plumpers (Too Faced) increase blood flow for temporary swelling. Hyaluronic acid plumpers hydrate for modest but real volume. High-shine glosses create a visual illusion. None permanently change lip shape.

**Are lip plumpers safe?**
Generally yes when used as directed. The tingling sensation from capsaicin or ginger is normal. If you experience pain, burning beyond a tingle, or swelling beyond the lip line, discontinue use.

**How long do results last?**
- Active plumpers: 30 minutes to 4 hours
- High-shine glosses: as long as the gloss stays on (1–3 hours)
- Overnight treatments: cumulative results build over 2–4 weeks

**Can I use a plumper under lipstick?**
Yes — apply plumper, wait 5 minutes, then apply lipstick for both plumping and color coverage.
