## Electric Pressure Cooker vs. Stovetop: Which Is Better?

**Electric (Instant Pot style):**
- Fully automated — set it and walk away
- Safer (no stovetop supervision, automatic pressure release)
- Also works as slow cooker, yogurt maker, rice cooker
- Slower to come to pressure (10–15 minutes) vs. stovetop

**Stovetop (Fissler, Kuhn Rikon):**
- Faster to pressure (5–7 minutes)
- Higher pressure ceiling = slightly faster cooking
- More manual monitoring required
- No slow cook/extra function

For most home cooks, electric is the right choice. Stovetop is for enthusiasts who want speed and don't mind watching the stove.

## Sizing Guide

- **3 quart:** Singles or couples cooking small batches.
- **6 quart:** The sweet spot — serves 2–5 people, fits most recipes.
- **8 quart:** Families of 5+, meal preppers, batch cooking soups and stocks.

**Pressure cooker minimum fill rule:** Most require at least 1 cup of liquid to reach pressure. You can't dry-sauté in pressure mode.
