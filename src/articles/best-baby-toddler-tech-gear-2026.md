## Buying Guide: Baby Tech That Earns Its Keep

### What Parents Actually Use vs. What Gets Returned

**High use:** Baby monitors (every night from day 1), white noise machines (every sleep), feeding trackers if breastfeeding (first 3 months)

**Moderate use:** Owlet and health trackers (first 6 months, then retired)

**Often returned:** Smart bassinets at $1,000+ (budget-busting for limited use window), Bluetooth pacifiers, elaborate smart bottle warmers

The highest ROI baby tech items are the ones that solve a daily, recurring problem. Sleep quality and monitoring anxiety are the two biggest ones for most parents.

Yes — strong evidence. The Marpac Dohm's mechanical noise is the pediatrician-recommended option because there are no digital loop artifacts. Place it across the room at medium volume.

**Q: Is the OK-to-wake clock worth it?**
Very much yes for toddlers. The Hatch Rest+ is the most recommended option — the color ring communicates "stay in bed" vs. "it's morning" in a way a 2-year-old can understand.

**Q: Is the Owlet safe?**
It's safe as a home reassurance tool. It's not FDA-cleared as a clinical medical device — discuss appropriate use with your pediatrician, especially for preemies.

**Q: What volume should I set white noise?**
Below 50 dB at the crib ear level per AAP guidelines. Position the machine 6+ feet away and use medium settings.
