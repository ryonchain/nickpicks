## Two Approaches to Home Brewing

**Extract brewing (beginner-friendly):** You use pre-made malt extract instead of raw grains. Skips the mashing step, cuts brew day to 2–3 hours. The result is excellent beer with significantly less complexity. Most starter kits use this method.

**All-grain brewing (intermediate-advanced):** Full grain-to-glass process including mashing (converting starches to sugars). 5–6 hour brew days. Maximum control over flavor. Requires more equipment but produces the most nuanced results.

For first-time brewers, extract brewing is strongly recommended. You'll be drinking good beer within 2–4 weeks.

## What's Included in a Good Starter Kit

A complete beginner kit should include:
- **Fermenter:** Bucket or glass carboy, minimum 5-gallon for standard batches
- **Airlock and stopper:** Lets CO₂ escape during fermentation without letting air in
- **Auto-siphon and tubing:** For transferring beer without oxidation
- **Bottle capper and caps:** For bottling (or a keg system)
- **Hydrometer:** Measures specific gravity to track fermentation
- **Sanitizer:** Star San or similar no-rinse sanitizer (critical — contamination ruins batches)
- **Ingredient kit (recipe):** Malt extract, hops, yeast, specialty grains

## The Brewing Process (Extract Method)

A typical 5-gallon extract brew day:

1. **Sanitize everything** (60 min before) — Star San, no rinsing required
2. **Steep specialty grains** in 3 gal water at 155°F for 30 min — adds color, body, flavor
3. **Add malt extract** — dissolve fully before boiling
4. **60-minute boil** — add hops per schedule
5. **Cool wort** to 70°F using ice bath or wort chiller (critical — don't add yeast above 80°F)
6. **Transfer to fermenter** — add remaining cold water to reach 5 gallons
7. **Pitch yeast** — add dry or liquid yeast packet
8. **Ferment** 1–4 weeks at 65–70°F
9. **Bottle** with priming sugar — adds carbonation over 2 weeks
10. **Drink** — enjoy the fruits of your labor

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take to brew beer at home?**
A: Brew day is 3–4 hours for extract, 5–6 hours for all-grain. Primary fermentation takes 1–3 weeks. Bottle conditioning (carbonation) takes another 1–2 weeks. Expect 4–5 weeks total from brew day to drinking.

**Q: How much does home brewing cost per batch?**
A: After the initial equipment investment ($50–130), ingredient kits for 5-gallon batches cost $25–45. That's approximately $0.50–$1.00 per bottle — significantly cheaper than craft beer.

**Q: Can I brew lagers at home?**
A: Yes, but they require fermentation at 45–55°F for 3–6 weeks — you need a temperature-controlled environment (a spare fridge with a temperature controller works). Ales ferment at room temperature (65–70°F) and are much easier to start with.

**Q: What's the biggest mistake beginners make?**
A: Skimping on sanitation. Contamination from wild bacteria or wild yeast is the #1 cause of off-flavors. Use Star San or similar and sanitize everything that touches the beer post-boil.
