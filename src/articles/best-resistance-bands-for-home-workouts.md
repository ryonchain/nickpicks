## How to Choose the Right Resistance Bands

### Identify Your Primary Use Case

This determines the band type more than anything:
- **Physical therapy/rehab:** TheraBand flat bands
- **Lower body/glute work:** Mini bands or fabric loop bands
- **Full-body home workout:** Tube bands with handles
- **Travel/light workout:** Loop bands

### Resistance Levels

Start lighter than you think you need. Bands are deceptive — a "medium" resistance band burns out muscles you didn't know you had when used with slow tempo and proper form. Most people can start with a light-to-medium set and progress from there.

### Latex vs. Fabric

Latex offers more stretch range and is generally more effective for exercises requiring significant elongation. Fabric is more comfortable against skin and stays in place better for leg exercises. Many people end up owning both types for different exercises.

### Safety Check

Inspect bands before every use. Look for small tears, discoloration, or surface cracks that indicate the material is degrading. A band that snaps under tension is painful and dangerous. Replace bands that show wear, and store them away from UV light and heat.
