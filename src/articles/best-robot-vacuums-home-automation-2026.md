## Why LiDAR Mapping Changed Everything

Early robot vacuums ran random bump patterns — they eventually cleaned a room but couldn't be trusted to cover it systematically. LiDAR (laser distance sensing) maps your space on the first run and creates a floor plan the robot follows on every subsequent run. You can name rooms, set no-go zones, and schedule room-by-room cleaning from a smartphone app.

**Camera-based obstacle avoidance** (Roborock, iRobot) adds another layer: the robot identifies and avoids cables, shoes, socks, and pet waste without getting stuck. On older models, these objects cause mid-run failures that require human intervention.

## Buying Guide: Choosing a Robot Vacuum

### LiDAR vs. Camera Navigation

LiDAR maps your space with laser precision — great for consistent, repeatable cleaning paths. Camera-based navigation (used by some iRobot models) can adapt to dynamic obstacles in real time but may be less consistent in the dark. Premium models combine both.

### Self-Emptying: Worth It?

Self-emptying bases transform robot vacuums from daily-interaction tools to weekly-interaction tools. The dock empties the robot's small onboard bin after each run. Capacity ranges from 30 to 60 days. For pet owners or dusty homes, self-emptying is highly recommended.

### Mop Combo: Yes or No?

Vacuum-mop combos are excellent on hard floors but require more dock management. The best models (Roborock, Ecovacs) auto-wash and dry mop pads. Budget combos skip the wash step — wet mop pads stored in the dock grow mold and smell. If you buy a combo, pay for auto-mop washing.

### Suction Numbers

Pa (Pascal) ratings give relative suction power. Numbers above 5,000 Pa handle embedded carpet dirt well. Numbers above 10,000 Pa are largely marketing — real-world pickup differences above 5,000 Pa are minimal on typical household dirt. Pet hair matters more for brush type than Pa number.

## FAQ

**Do robot vacuums replace upright vacuums?**
For maintenance cleaning, yes — running daily keeps floors cleaner than weekly upright sessions. For deep cleaning (post-party, renovation debris), a powerful upright is still better. Most households use both.

**How long do robot vacuums last?**
Premium models (Roborock, iRobot) last 4–6 years with brush and filter replacements. Budget models 2–3 years. Batteries typically need replacement at 2–3 years.

**Can robot vacuums handle rugs and carpet transitions?**
Yes, modern models transition from hard floor to carpet and back automatically. High-pile rugs (above 1 inch) can be problematic; set no-go zones for rugs the robot gets stuck on.

**Are robot vacuums safe around pets?**
Most pets habituate to robot vacuums within 1–2 weeks. The concern is the dock and charger cables — cover cables, and confirm the dock base is stable. Models with obstacle avoidance are safer in pet-dense homes.
