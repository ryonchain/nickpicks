## Pen Tablet vs Display Tablet

**Pen tablet (no screen):** You draw on a flat pad and look at your monitor. Most professional workflows use this — once learned, it's faster and causes less eye strain. All Wacom Intuos and many Huion/XP-Pen products are this type.

**Display tablet (built-in screen):** You draw directly on the screen. More intuitive for beginners. Wacom Cintiq, Huion Kamvas, and XP-Pen Artist products have built-in displays.

**Recommendation:** Beginners often assume they need a display tablet, but most professional illustrators use pen tablets. The learning curve for pen tablets is 1–2 weeks; the payoff is better ergonomics and focus.

---

## Key Specs Explained

**Pressure levels:** 8192 is now standard on all quality tablets. The difference between 4096 and 8192 is negligible in practice — software and pen responsiveness matter more.

**LPI (Lines Per Inch):** Resolution of the active area. 5080 LPI is standard; adequate for all but the most precise professional work.

**Report rate:** How often the tablet sends position data per second. 200 reports/second is standard; 400+ is premium (reduces pen-to-cursor lag).

**Active area:** Larger is generally better but requires more arm movement. Most artists prefer Medium (about 8"x5") or Large (10"x6.25") for comfortable strokes. Small tablets work for photo editing and quick sketching; large tablets for full illustration.

## Which Drawing Tablet Should You Choose?

**Just starting digital art:** XP-Pen Deco 01 V2 ($50) or Wacom Intuos Small ($80). Start with a pen tablet — screen tablets look intuitive but pen tablets train better habits.

**Hobbyist who wants a screen:** Huion Kamvas 13 Gen 2 ($200) — QHD display, full lamination, excellent value.

**Freelance illustrator or animator:** Wacom Cintiq 16 ($650) — invest in driver reliability when clients depend on you.

**Anime/manga illustration:** Huion Kamvas 13 Gen 2 with Clip Studio Paint is a popular community choice at a reasonable price.

**Photo editing (no illustration):** Wacom Intuos Small or XP-Pen Deco 01 V2 — display tablets are unnecessary for photo retouching.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does drawing tablet work with Photoshop, Illustrator, and Procreate?**
Wacom tablets have the deepest integration with all three. Huion and XP-Pen work well with Photoshop and Illustrator. Procreate is iPad/Apple Pencil only — no desktop drawing tablets support it directly.

**What software do I use with a drawing tablet?**
Popular options: Clip Studio Paint (illustration and manga), Krita (free, excellent), Adobe Photoshop/Illustrator (subscription), Procreate (iPad only), Medibang Paint (free).

**Is a larger tablet better?**
Not always. Larger tablets require more arm movement for cursor travel. Match tablet size to your monitor size — a rule of thumb is 1:4 ratio (10" tablet for 40" monitor).

**Do you need a drawing tablet for photo editing?**
Not required, but useful for precise selections and masking. A small pen tablet ($50–80) is adequate for photo editing without display.
