## Why Hydration Matters for Cats

Cats evolved in arid environments and have a naturally low thirst drive — they're wired to get most moisture from prey. Domestic cats on dry kibble diets are chronically under-hydrated, which increases risk of:

- Chronic kidney disease (leading cause of death in cats 10+)
- Urinary tract infections and crystals (especially in males)
- Constipation and digestive issues

A circulating fountain increases average water intake by 50–70% compared to a static bowl in most cats — making it one of the highest-ROI health investments for cat owners.

## Fountain Maintenance Tips

**Filter replacement:** Replace carbon filters every 2–4 weeks (or when they start to discolor). Waiting too long defeats the filtration purpose.

**Cleaning frequency:** Disassemble and fully clean (including the pump impeller) every 2–4 weeks. Biofilm builds in the pump housing even with filtered water.

**Water level:** Never run the pump dry — it burns out the motor. Most fountains have a minimum fill line marked on the reservoir.
