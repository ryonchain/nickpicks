## GPS vs Bluetooth: What's the Difference?

**GPS trackers** (Tractive, Whistle, Fi) use cellular networks to report location in real-time. They work anywhere with LTE coverage but require a monthly subscription and have shorter battery life.

**Bluetooth trackers** (AirTag) ping nearby devices to relay location — no subscription, long battery, but location updates are passive and depend on the density of compatible devices nearby.

**For outdoor-roaming cats:** GPS with real-time tracking is worth the subscription cost.
**For primarily indoor cats:** AirTag is sufficient and significantly cheaper long-term.

## Collar Fit and Safety

- Cat collars should fit **2 fingers snugly** between the collar and neck
- Always use a **breakaway (safety release) buckle** — cats can get collars caught on branches
- GPS units should weigh **no more than 5% of the cat's body weight** (most adult cats: 1.5 oz max)
- Check fit monthly as cats gain/lose weight seasonally
