## Buying Guide

### What You Give Up Under $50

**No built-in GPS.** Budget trackers record distance using step count and stride length estimates — not GPS satellite data. If you want accurate outdoor running distance, you'll need a connected GPS (GPS from your phone via Bluetooth) or spend more on a dedicated GPS running watch. For walking and gym workouts, this rarely matters.

**No ECG.** Electrocardiogram sensors that can detect atrial fibrillation require certification and cost more to manufacture. If AFib detection is your goal, look at Apple Watch Series 4+ or Withings ScanWatch.

**App ecosystems are smaller.** Amazfit's Zepp app and Xiaomi's Mi Fitness are solid, but they have fewer third-party integrations than Fitbit or Garmin Connect. Most users won't notice this.

### What You Don't Give Up

**Step accuracy.** Budget trackers use the same accelerometer technology as premium devices. Step counting accuracy is comparable.

**Heart rate monitoring.** Optical heart rate sensors have become commoditized. A $35 Amazfit tracks resting heart rate and workout zones as accurately as a $350 Garmin for most activities (swimming excepted).

**Sleep tracking.** Many budget trackers actually outperform premium ones in sleep stage detail because they prioritize battery life and sensor quality over GPS and cellular.

### Key Features to Look For

- **Battery life:** Under $50 trackers should last at least 7 days. Anything less is a dealbreaker.
- **Water resistance:** Look for 5 ATM (50 meters) for swimming; IP67 for splash-proof only.
- **Display size:** AMOLED displays are now common even in budget trackers — avoid trackers with tiny or dim screens.
- **App compatibility:** Confirm iOS/Android support before buying.
- **SpO2:** Blood oxygen monitoring is now standard in this price range. Skip trackers that don't include it.

## FAQ

**Can a $50 fitness tracker accurately track steps?**
Yes. Budget trackers from Fitbit, Xiaomi, and Amazfit use the same three-axis accelerometer technology as premium devices. Step counting accuracy is generally within 5-10% of actual steps.

**Do cheap fitness trackers work with iPhones and Android?**
Most trackers under $50 work with both iOS and Android. The Xiaomi Mi Band and Amazfit Band both support iOS 10+ and Android 5+. Always check compatibility on the product page.

**How long do fitness tracker batteries last under $50?**
Budget trackers often outlast premium ones — the Amazfit Band 7 lasts 18 days, Xiaomi Smart Band 8 lasts 16 days. No GPS or large power-hungry screens means far longer runtime.

**Is Fitbit Inspire 3 worth it at under $100?**
The Fitbit Inspire 3 typically sells for $70-100 and adds Daily Readiness Score, Active Zone Minutes, and color display. If you want the most polished app experience, it's worth the extra spend.
