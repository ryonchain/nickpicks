## Buying Guide

### What You Get Under $100

**Notification mirroring:** All smartwatches in this range mirror phone notifications reliably — calls, texts, apps.

**Basic health tracking:** Heart rate, SpO2, steps, sleep — these sensors have become cheap enough to include in budget watches without quality compromise.

**Long battery life:** Budget watches without LTE or complex processors often have better battery life than premium smartwatches. 10-15 days is typical.

**Decent displays:** AMOLED is now standard even in budget smartwatches. Color, touch-responsive, always-on display option.

### What You Don't Get Under $100

**App ecosystem:** Only Wear OS watches (which typically start at $150+) have access to Google Play watch apps. Budget watches run proprietary OS with limited third-party apps.

**Built-in GPS:** Most budget smartwatches use phone-connected GPS (Bluetooth GPS from your phone). This means workout maps are available but require your phone nearby. Built-in GPS starts at ~$150.

**ECG/Blood pressure:** Medical-grade sensors are expensive and FDA-regulated. Budget watches track SpO2 and heart rate but not ECG or blood pressure.

**NFC payments:** Contactless payment via the watch requires NFC hardware and banking partnerships. Rare under $100 (Galaxy Watch FE on sale is an exception).

### Brands to Avoid

No-name watches on Amazon under $40 often lack reliable companion apps, receive no software updates, and have inaccurate sensors. Stick to Amazfit, Xiaomi, Samsung, or Garmin in this category.

## FAQ

**Are cheap smartwatches worth buying?**
For basic notifications, step counting, and sleep tracking — yes. If you want GPS, ECG, or app ecosystem, budget smartwatches fall short.

**What smartwatch brand is best under $100?**
Amazfit offers the best build quality and battery life. Samsung Galaxy Watch FE on sale is the best full smartwatch experience at ~$100.

**Do budget smartwatches work with iPhone?**
For notifications — yes. For Apple Pay, Siri, or iMessage replies from the watch — no. Amazfit works with iPhone via Zepp app.

**How long do budget smartwatches last?**
2-4 years with major brands (Amazfit, Samsung). Unknown brands may stop receiving app updates within a year.
