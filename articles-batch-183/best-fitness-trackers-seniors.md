## Buying Guide

### Features That Matter Most for Seniors

**Fall Detection:** Automatic fall detection (Apple Watch, Samsung Galaxy Watch) is the most important safety feature. It calls for help even if the wearer is unconscious or disoriented after a fall.

**Emergency SOS:** All major smartwatches have SOS buttons. Apple Watch adds satellite SOS for use without cellular coverage — critical in rural areas or during outdoor activities.

**Heart Rate Alerts:** Notifications for unusually high or low heart rate are standard on Fitbit, Apple Watch, and Garmin. Some watches add AFib detection (irregular heart rhythm).

**Large Display:** The 45mm Apple Watch, Garmin Venu 3, and Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 44mm have the largest displays. All support accessibility text size settings.

**Battery Life:** Daily charging is a significant inconvenience. Fitbit (7 days), Garmin Venu 3 (14 days), and Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 (40 hours) minimize charging frequency.

### Medical Alert Watches vs. Fitness Trackers

Standard smartwatches (Apple Watch, Samsung) have SOS features but are not medical alert devices — they call contacts or 911 directly. **Dedicated medical alert services** (Lively, Bay Alarm Medical, Medical Guardian) include 24/7 monitoring centers that can assess the situation and dispatch help even if the wearer can't communicate. For seniors at significant fall risk who live alone, a dedicated medical alert service is worth considering alongside or instead of a fitness tracker.

### Ease of Use for Tech-Hesitant Seniors

**Simplest:** Fitbit Charge 6, Fitbit Inspire 3 — band form factor, single scroll interface, easy setup via the Fitbit app

**Moderate:** Apple Watch, Samsung Galaxy Watch — more features, more settings, but guided setup and family-assisted configuration available

**For iPhone users:** Apple Watch is natural; iPhone settings transfer and family sharing makes setup straightforward
