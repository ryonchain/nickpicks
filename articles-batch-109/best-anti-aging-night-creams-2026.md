## Night Cream Routine: How to Layer

1. **Cleanse** — double cleanse if you wear SPF or makeup
2. **Toner** (optional) — balances pH, preps skin
3. **Serum** — apply targeted actives (vitamin C, peptides, retinol serum)
4. **Night cream** — locks in serums and delivers its own actives
5. **Occlusive** (optional, dry skin) — petroleum jelly, squalane, or face oil as the final seal

For retinol use: apply retinol serum OR use a retinol-containing night cream — not both at the same time. Too much retinol accelerates irritation without adding benefit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**When should I start using anti-aging night cream?**
Late 20s for prevention. If you're starting retinol, your late 20s or early 30s is ideal. There's no such thing as starting too early with broad-spectrum SPF and antioxidants.

**How long before I see results?**
Retinol: texture improvement in 4–6 weeks, line reduction in 8–12 weeks. Peptides: firmness improvement in 4–8 weeks. Hydration benefits: 24–48 hours.

**Should I use a different moisturizer day and night?**
Yes. Night creams are typically richer and contain actives incompatible with daytime use (retinol, for example, degrades in UV and increases photosensitivity). Day creams should always include or be layered under SPF 30+.

**Can I use retinol every night?**
After a 4–8 week adaptation period, yes. Start with 2–3 nights per week and build up as your skin tolerates it.
