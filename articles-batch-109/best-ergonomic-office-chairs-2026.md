## Ergonomic Setup: The Chair Is Not Enough

The best ergonomic chair fails if the rest of your setup is wrong.

### Desk and Monitor Height
- Eyes should be level with the top 1/3 of your monitor
- Elbows at 90° to keyboard — neither reaching up nor hunching down
- Screen 20–28" from face (arm's length)

### Sitting Position Checklist
- [ ] Feet flat on the floor (or footrest)
- [ ] Knees at 90° or slightly below hip height
- [ ] Lumbar support pressing gently into the natural curve of your lower back
- [ ] Shoulders relaxed, not elevated or rounded forward
- [ ] Elbows at keyboard height, forearms parallel to desk

### Take Breaks
Even the best ergonomic chair doesn't compensate for sitting still for 4 hours. Stand for 5 minutes every hour. Walk during calls.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a $1,400 ergonomic chair worth it?**
If you sit 8 hours/day, 5 days/week, the cost per day works out to under $0.50/day over 10 years — less than a coffee. Chronic back pain treatment (physical therapy, chiropractic, imaging) easily exceeds $2,000/year. Yes, it's worth it.

**What's the difference between a gaming chair and an ergonomic chair?**
Gaming chairs prioritize aesthetics (racing seat bucket style) over ergonomics. Most lack proper lumbar support geometry and have fixed bucket seats that don't distribute pressure well. They're not designed for 8-hour professional use.

**Should I get a chair with a headrest?**
Only if you recline regularly. Headrests are most useful at 110–120° recline. In upright working posture, headrests are often positioned incorrectly and push the head forward into poor posture.

**What weight capacity do ergonomic chairs support?**
Standard: 250–275 lbs. Aeron Size C: 300 lbs. Steelcase Amia and Gesture: 400 lbs. Look for the rated capacity in the specs.
