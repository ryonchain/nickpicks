## What Makes a Good Standing Desk

Before the reviews, the specs that matter:

**Stability:** Wobble at standing height is the #1 complaint with cheap standing desks. At 48"+ height, any lateral play is amplified. Look for desks with dual-motor systems and H-frame or cross-bar stabilizers.

**Weight capacity:** A monitor, laptop stand, keyboard, and peripherals add up fast. Aim for 200 lbs+ rated capacity — not because you'll load that much, but because higher-rated desks have stronger frames.

**Lift speed:** Budget desks move at 1 in/sec. Mid-range desks run at 1.5–2 in/sec. It doesn't sound like much, but adjusting your desk 50 times a day adds up.

**Height range:** You need a desk that reaches your elbow height when standing. For most people (5'4"–6'4"), a range of 24"–50" covers both sitting and standing without a stool.

## Ergonomic Setup Guide

**Finding your standing height:** Elbow at 90° when arms hang naturally. For most people standing, this is roughly your hip height plus 8–10 inches.

**Finding your sitting height:** Thighs parallel to floor, feet flat, elbows at 90° when typing.

**The right routine:** Don't stand all day — it's as bad as sitting all day. Start with 20–30 minute standing intervals, alternating with sitting. Most powered desks have timer reminders built in — use them.

**What to buy with your standing desk:**
- Anti-fatigue mat — essential if you're on hard floors
- Monitor arm — frees desk surface and gets the screen to eye level
- Cable management — a sit-stand desk without cable management becomes a mess quickly

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much should I spend on a standing desk?**
$400–$600 gets you a dual-motor desk with real stability. Under $300, you're accepting wobble. Over $700, you're paying for premium frame specs and warranty coverage.

**Do I need a dual-motor standing desk?**
For a single-monitor home office setup, a quality single-motor desk is fine. For dual monitors or 60+ lbs of load, dual-motor provides better stability and longevity.

**How long do standing desks last?**
Quality desks with brushless motors last 10+ years with normal use. Budget single-motor desks typically last 3–5 years before the motor shows wear.

**Is a memory keypad worth it?**
Yes. The friction of manually dialing in your height every time you want to switch is enough to make people stop using the feature. Memory presets remove that friction entirely.
