## Setup and Maintenance

**Initial setup:** Unbox, lower into pool with the power supply on the deck, connect cable, press start. That's it.

**Filter cleaning:** Remove filter after each use, rinse with garden hose. Don't use high-pressure spray — it damages the filter mesh.

**Cable management:** Let cable float loosely in the pool. Never let the cable wrap under the cleaner's wheels.

**Storage:** Store indoors or in shade. UV degrades rubber and plastic over years of exposure. Most models include a cord keeper and storage bag.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does a robotic pool cleaner run?**
Most cycles run 1.5–3 hours per clean. For a standard 30-foot inground pool, a 2-hour cycle 3x/week maintains clean water with minimal additional vacuuming.

**Do robotic cleaners replace my pool filter?**
No — the pool's filtration and circulation system still runs daily. Robotic cleaners reduce the debris load on the filter and eliminate manual vacuuming, but don't replace chemical treatment or circulation.

**Can I leave a robotic cleaner in the pool?**
No. Remove it after each cleaning cycle. Prolonged submersion can shorten motor life, and the rubber tires leave marks on pool surfaces. Store clean and dry.

**What's the best pool size for each model?**
- Under 33 feet: Wybot Osprey 500 or Dolphin Escape
- 33–50 feet: Dolphin Nautilus CC Plus
- 50+ feet or complex shapes: Dolphin Premier or Polaris P965iQ
