## TSA Power Bank Rules (Critical for Travelers)

Before buying, understand the rules:

- **Under 100Wh (≈27,000mAh at 3.7V):** Carry-on allowed, no airline approval needed
- **100–160Wh:** Carry-on allowed, requires airline approval (most airlines grant it)
- **Over 160Wh:** Not allowed on passenger aircraft

Most power banks sold for consumers are under 100Wh. A 26,800mAh bank at 3.7V = ~99.2Wh — just under the limit. Always check the Wh rating on the label, not just mAh.

## How Many Charges Can I Get?

A rough calculation: iPhone 15 battery = ~3,349mAh. Real-world power bank efficiency is ~85% (some energy lost to heat during transfer).

- 5,000mAh bank → ~1.3 iPhone charges
- 10,000mAh bank → ~2.5 iPhone charges
- 20,000mAh bank → ~5 iPhone charges
- 24,000mAh bank → ~6 iPhone charges

For laptops, check the laptop's battery size in Wh (usually on the bottom or in specs). A 100Wh MacBook Pro battery from a 24,000mAh/99.7Wh bank will give you roughly one full charge — accounting for efficiency loss.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does mAh or Wh matter more?**
Wh (watt-hours) is the more accurate measure because it accounts for voltage. When comparing banks, use Wh if the label shows it. If only mAh is listed, multiply by 3.7V to get approximate Wh.

**Can I bring two power banks on a plane?**
Yes — there's no stated limit on the number of banks under 100Wh, though airlines can ask you to keep it reasonable. Two 26,800mAh banks are common and generally fine. Power banks must go in carry-on, never checked baggage.

**How do I maximize my power bank's lifespan?**
Don't let it sit fully charged or fully discharged for extended periods. Charge to 80% for storage. Avoid extreme heat. Most lithium cells last 300–500 full charge cycles before capacity degrades noticeably.
