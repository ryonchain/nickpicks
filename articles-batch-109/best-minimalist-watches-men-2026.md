## How to Choose: Key Considerations

### Automatic vs Quartz

**Automatic:** No battery, powered by wrist movement. More craft, more maintenance (service every 5–10 years), less accurate (±5–20 sec/day). Appreciated for mechanical craft.

**Quartz:** Battery-powered. ±1–15 sec/month accuracy. No servicing. Better for everyday durability. Underrated by enthusiasts, preferred by pragmatists.

### Case Size

| Wrist Circumference | Recommended Case Size |
|---|---|
| Under 6.5" | 34–38mm |
| 6.5–7" | 38–40mm |
| Over 7" | 40–44mm |

Minimalist watches tend to run 36–40mm — this is intentional. Large cases look sporty; moderate cases look clean.

### Crystal Types

| Crystal | Scratch Resistance | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Acrylic | Low — scratches easily, polishes out | Cheapest |
| Hardlex (Seiko) | Medium — better than acrylic | Mid |
| Sapphire | Very high — only diamond scratches it | Premium |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's a good first automatic watch?**
Seiko 5 Sports for under $150, Seiko Presage for under $400, or Tissot Everytime Swissmatic for verified Swiss quality. All improve on DW and similar fashion watches in movement quality.

**Are cheap automatics worth it over quartz?**
Not if accuracy and reliability are top priorities. A good quartz is more accurate than most automatics. But automatics offer a connection to mechanical craft that quartz can't replicate — if that matters to you, yes.

**Do minimalist watches stay in style?**
The styles most consistent here (simple dial, leather strap, 38–40mm) have been fashionable since the 1960s and show no sign of dating. Chasing trends in watches almost always produces disappointment.
