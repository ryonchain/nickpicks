## Gaming Chair vs Ergonomic Office Chair: What's the Difference?

**Traditional gaming chairs (racing style):**
- High-back bucket seat design from racing car seats
- Usually PU leather or leatherette — premium-looking, but traps heat
- Built-in neck and lumbar pillows (adjustment is limited)
- Often lock into one recline position or the other
- Wide recline range for casual play
- Focus on aesthetics over long-session ergonomics

**Premium gaming/ergonomic hybrid chairs:**
- Proper lumbar support systems with depth and height adjustment
- Breathable mesh or fabric options
- Multi-dimensional armrests (4D or better)
- Continuous recline with tension control — not just preset positions
- Designed for 6–8 hours, not just aesthetics

**Verdict:** For 1–2 hour gaming sessions, budget racing chairs are adequate. For 4+ hour daily sessions, invest in ergonomic design over aesthetics.

## Setup Tips for Long Gaming Sessions

**Every 45–60 minutes:** Stand, stretch your hip flexors, roll your shoulders backward. Even the best chair can't substitute for movement breaks.

**Monitor height:** Top of screen at eye level. Most desk setups are too low — use a monitor arm or stand.

**Armrest height:** Elbows at ~90–100° with forearms parallel to desk surface. Wrong armrest height causes wrist and shoulder strain over hours.

**Seat height:** Feet flat on the floor (or footrest), thighs parallel to ground. If your feet dangle, you need a footrest.
