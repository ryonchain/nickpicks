## Smart Plug Protocols Explained

**Wi-Fi (2.4GHz):** Connects directly to your home router. No hub required. Works with Alexa, Google Home, and Siri Shortcuts. Slight latency (~1 second). The standard for most smart plugs.

**Zigbee:** Ultra-low power mesh network. Requires a hub (SmartThings, Philips Hue Bridge, Amazon Echo). Very reliable, no cloud dependency, fast response. Better for large smart home setups.

**Matter:** New open standard (2022+) that works across Apple Home, Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and SmartThings without brand lock-in. Runs over Wi-Fi or Thread (ultra-low latency). The future of smart home, but adoption is still growing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do smart plugs work without Wi-Fi?**
They work manually (just push the button on the plug). Remote control, scheduling, and voice control require Wi-Fi. Thread-based plugs like the Eve Energy can work without cloud service over local network if your hub supports it.

**Will a smart plug interfere with my Wi-Fi?**
Smart plugs use 2.4GHz Wi-Fi and add a small amount of traffic. In homes with many smart plugs (20+), you may notice mild congestion. Thread-based devices (Matter over Thread) use a mesh protocol that actually helps extend coverage.

**Can smart plugs handle high-draw appliances?**
Most smart plugs are rated 15A (about 1,800W at 120V). That covers lamps, fans, TVs, gaming consoles, and most coffee makers. Do NOT use smart plugs with space heaters, window AC units, or other high-draw appliances unless the plug is specifically rated for the load.
