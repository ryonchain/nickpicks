## What the Research Says About Sound Masking

Sleep science is consistent on a few points:

**Consistency matters more than content.** Any steady, moderate-volume ambient sound reduces sleep interruptions. The specific type (white, pink, brown, fan) matters less than having something consistent.

**Volume sweet spot: 50–65 dB.** Quiet enough not to cause hearing concerns over time; loud enough to mask most ambient noise. This is approximately "conversational background" level — think coffee shop background noise.

**Pink and brown noise for deep sleep.** A 2021 *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience* study found pink noise increased slow-wave sleep (deep sleep) duration more than white noise in healthy adults. If you're choosing from a machine with multiple options, start with pink or brown noise.

**Non-looping audio.** The brain habituates to repeating patterns. Machines with looped audio (many budget options) can paradoxically become disruptive once your sleeping brain learns the loop.
