## Beard Trimming Tips

**Always trim dry:** Wet hair is heavier and hangs lower, making it look longer than it is when dry. Trim dry to cut to the actual length you want. Exception: after a shower is fine if you dry the beard with a towel first.

**Start longer, go shorter:** Set your guard 1-2mm longer than your target length. You can always go shorter; you can't undo a too-short cut.

**Neckline placement:** The natural neckline is one finger-width above the Adam's apple. Shave everything below that line clean. A neckline that's too high (above the jaw) looks like a floating beard; too low looks ungroomed.

**Cheek line:** Let your cheek line grow naturally or connect cleanly from sideburn to mustache. Shaping the cheek line higher creates a cleaner look; most men don't need to shave the cheek area unless hair grows unusually high.

**Oil your blades:** One drop of clipper oil on the blade every 3-4 trims extends blade life significantly and prevents rust. Most trimmers include a small oil bottle.
