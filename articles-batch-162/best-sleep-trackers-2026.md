## What Sleep Data Actually Tells You

**Focus on trends, not single nights:** A bad night's HRV doesn't mean your health is deteriorating — stress, illness, alcohol, and sleep position all affect nightly readings. Look for week-over-week trends.

**The variables that move your scores:**
- **Alcohol:** Suppresses REM sleep and elevates resting heart rate for 24-36 hours after drinking
- **Late screens:** Blue light exposure delays melatonin onset by 1-3 hours
- **Exercise timing:** Morning/afternoon exercise improves sleep quality; intense evening exercise within 2 hours of bedtime disrupts sleep onset
- **Consistent sleep schedule:** The single biggest HRV and deep sleep predictor

**HRV baseline:** Your HRV is personal — don't compare your absolute number to population averages. Track your own trend. HRV consistently lower than your 30-day baseline = stress, illness, or overtraining.
