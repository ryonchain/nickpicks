## Understanding Grow Light Specs

**PPFD (µmol/m²/s):** The only metric that matters for plant growth. Measures actual photon delivery to the plant canopy. Discount any claim that doesn't include PPFD data at a stated distance.

**Actual wattage vs. claimed wattage:** Many grow lights claim "1000W equivalent" while drawing 100W from the wall. Actual draw from the outlet is what matters — it's what you're paying for and what drives real-world performance.

**Full spectrum vs. blurple:** Full-spectrum white LEDs (3000-5000K + targeted red 660nm) outperform red/blue "blurple" LEDs for most plant growing. The full spectrum supports all photosynthesis pathways and plant processes, not just the peaks blurple LEDs target.

**Coverage area:** Manufacturer coverage specs assume optimal conditions. Real-world coverage is typically 75-80% of stated area. The intensity also drops significantly at the edges — PPFD maps (grids showing intensity across the coverage area) reveal this. Look for brands that publish PPFD maps, not just center-point numbers.

## What to Grow (and What Light Each Needs)

**Low light (100-200 PPFD):** Pothos, peace lily, snake plant, Chinese evergreen, ZZ plant. GE Grow BR30 in a standard lamp is sufficient.

**Medium light (200-400 PPFD):** Most herbs (basil, parsley, mint), leafy greens (lettuce, spinach), and medium-light tropicals. Soltech Aspect or a dimmed SF-1000/TS-1000.

**High light (400-600 PPFD):** Fruiting vegetables (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers), cannabis, and high-light succulents. TS-1000 or SF-1000 at closer hanging distance.

**Seedlings:** 200-400 PPFD, 16-18 hours light. Any quality LED at maximum height works.
