## What to Know Before Buying

### Size compliance is everything
Airlines enforce size gauges inconsistently — but when they do enforce, a gate-check fee ($35–$75) negates the point of carry-on travel. Measure twice. Pay attention to the 9-inch depth: most carry-ons hit this limit when packed and the depth is where airlines focus.

### Spinner quality varies more than price suggests
A $400 Rimowa's spinners and a $150 Samsonite's spinners are more different than the price implies. Test spinners before buying when possible — look for double-spinner (two wheels per corner) and feel for smooth rolling without lateral wobble.

### Interior organization matters more than people think
A single main compartment sounds simple until you're rummaging at 11pm for your charger. Compression straps, a zippered divider, and a laundry section (even just a sleeve) make a real difference in daily usability.

### TSA locks vs. combinations
TSA-approved locks let agents open your bag without cutting the lock. Any carry-on you bring as checked or that goes in overhead is fine without a TSA lock — but having one avoids lock destruction if your bag gets secondary screening.
