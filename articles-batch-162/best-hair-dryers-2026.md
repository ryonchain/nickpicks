## Hair Type Guide

**Fine hair:** Use lower heat settings; your hair dries quickly and is more susceptible to heat damage. Revlon One-Step is excellent. Dyson's temperature control is beneficial if you're prone to heat damage.

**Thick hair:** Maximum wattage matters. BaBylissPRO or Dyson; budget pick is Conair 1875W. Use a concentrator nozzle to direct airflow and section the hair.

**Curly/wavy hair:** Diffuser attachment is non-negotiable. Dyson's diffuser is the best — it cups curls without disrupting them. Use low heat and low speed to minimize frizz.

**Frizz-prone hair:** Ionic technology is important. All picks here are ionic; Dyson's intelligent heat control prevents the localized heat that causes frizz.
