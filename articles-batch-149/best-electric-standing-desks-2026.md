## What to Know Before You Buy

**Dual motor vs. single motor:** Dual-motor desks lift more weight (300–500 lbs vs. 150–220 lbs) and are more stable at full extension. If you have a multi-monitor setup, go dual motor.

**Desktop size:** 48" x 24" is the minimum for a dual-monitor setup. 60" x 24" is recommended. 72" x 30" is ideal for ultra-wide monitors.

**Height range:** Standard ranges (22"–48") work for most people. If you're under 5'2" or over 6'3", check the specific model's range.

**Weight capacity:** Add up your monitors, laptop, desktop, accessories, and monitors arms. Exceed 60% of the rated capacity and you'll see sway at standing height.

**Anti-collision:** Essential if pets or children are near your workspace. The desk stops if it hits an obstacle on the way down.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are standing desks actually better for you?**
Research shows alternating sitting and standing reduces lower back pain and improves afternoon energy levels. The key is alternating — pure standing for 8 hours is also harmful.

**How long do electric standing desks last?**
Quality dual-motor desks last 7–15 years with normal use. Motor failures are rare; frame wear from daily cycling is the primary concern. Uplift's 15-year warranty is the industry benchmark.

**Can I use my existing desktop?**
Most standing desk frames are sold separately from the desktop, so you can reuse a surface you already have — provided it's the right size and can be drilled for the frame attachment.
