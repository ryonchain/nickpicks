## Choosing the Right Crate Size

|------------|-------------------|----------------|
| 50–65 lbs | 42" crate | 41"L x 28"W x 30"H |
| 65–90 lbs | 48" crate | 47"L x 30"W x 32"H |
| 90–110 lbs | 48" XL or 54" | 53"L x 36"W x 37"H |
| 110–130 lbs | 54" or Impact XL | 52"L x 33"W x 37"H |

**The standing test:** Your dog should be able to stand fully upright with 2–3 inches of clearance, turn a full circle, and lie stretched out. Measure your dog nose-to-tail and add 2–4 inches for the right length.

## Crate Training Tips for Large Dogs

**Make it positive:** Feed meals in the crate, toss treats inside, and reward calm behavior near the crate before expecting your dog to stay in it.

**Start short:** Begin with 15-minute sessions with the door closed while you're home. Extend gradually over 1–2 weeks.

**Never use the crate for punishment:** The crate should be a den, not a timeout. Dogs that associate crates with punishment are more likely to develop escape behavior.

**Exercise before crating:** A tired large dog crates far more calmly than a bored one. A 30-minute walk before an 8-hour crating period makes a significant difference.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long can a large dog be left in a crate?**
The general guideline: adult dogs (2+ years) can be crated 6–8 hours during the day. Puppies under 6 months should not be crated more than their age in months + 1 hour (a 3-month-old puppy: 4 hours maximum).

**Should my dog sleep in a crate?**
Many dogs choose to sleep in their crate voluntarily once properly crate-trained — it becomes their den. Overnight crating is appropriate for dogs of all ages once they're fully crate-trained.

**Can a dog be in a crate 8 hours while I'm at work?**
Yes for adult dogs, with a midday walk from a dog walker or neighbor if possible. Ensure the crate is appropriately sized, the dog has been exercised before crating, and fresh water is accessible.
