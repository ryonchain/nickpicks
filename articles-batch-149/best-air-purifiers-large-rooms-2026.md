## Understanding CADR Ratings

CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) tells you how fast a purifier cleans a given room size. It's measured in cubic feet per minute (CFM) for three pollutants:

- **Smoke** (finest particles — hardest to filter)
- **Pollen** (coarse particles — easiest to filter)
- **Dust** (mid-range particles)

**The rule of thumb:** To achieve 5 air changes per hour (the standard for effective air purification), choose a CADR rating at least ⅔ of your room's square footage. For a 500 sq ft room, look for a CADR of 333+ for smoke.

Most manufacturers advertise "room size" at 5 air changes per hour — this is the standard you should hold them to.

## What to Know Before You Buy

**True HEPA vs. "HEPA-type":** Only True HEPA filters capture 99.97% of particles ≥0.3 microns. "HEPA-type" filters have no standardized specification. Always verify the spec sheet says "True HEPA" or "Medical Grade HEPA."

**HEPA + carbon is essential for odors:** HEPA alone captures particles but doesn't address VOCs (formaldehyde from furniture, cooking odors, pet smells). Activated carbon layers handle chemical filtration. If odors are a priority, verify the model has a substantial carbon layer — not just a thin sprinkle coating.

**Filter replacement costs:** Add annual filter costs to the upfront price. Most True HEPA filters cost $30–$80/year for replacement. Washable pre-filters reduce ongoing costs.

**Ozone generators — avoid:** Some air purifiers (particularly older ionic models) produce ozone as a byproduct. The EPA classifies ozone as a lung irritant. Look for CARB-certified or ozone-free certifications.
