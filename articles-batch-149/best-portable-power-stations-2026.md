## LFP vs. NMC: Battery Chemistry Explained

**LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate):** Safer (no thermal runaway), longer cycle life (3,000–3,500 cycles), and more stable at partial states of charge. Heavier and more expensive to manufacture. Available in: EcoFlow DELTA 2 Max, Bluetti AC200L, Anker SOLIX C1000.

**NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt):** Higher energy density (lighter for same capacity), slightly faster discharge rates. Shorter cycle life (800–1,500 cycles). More common in older or budget models: Jackery Explorer series.

**For emergency prep:** LFP is strongly preferred. A 3,000-cycle battery used monthly for outages lasts 250 years; an 800-cycle NMC battery lasts 67 years. But for typical use, both will outlast their practical relevance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a portable power station run my refrigerator during an outage?**
Most refrigerators draw 100–400W running and 700–1,500W starting. A 2kWh station at 2,400W output handles most full-sized refrigerators — but you'll need to recharge the station every 8–20 hours via solar or when power returns.

**How long do portable power stations last?**
LFP batteries: 3,000–3,500 full cycles. At 365 cycles per year (daily use), that's 8–10 years. For emergency-only use (20 cycles per year), LFP batteries last 150+ years — effectively indefinite.

**Can I use solar to keep my station charged indefinitely?**
Yes, with enough solar panels. A 2kWh station with 500W of solar input charges fully in ~4–5 hours of peak sun. Off-grid cabins and RVs use exactly this configuration.
