## Wire vs. Wire-Free: Which Type Do You Need?

**Boundary wire models (Husqvarna, Worx, Gardena):** Require a perimeter wire buried or stapled around the lawn edge. One-time installation effort (~2–6 hours for DIY) but more reliable navigation in complex yards with obstacles. Most models under $2,000 use this approach.

**GPS wire-free models (Mammotion, EcoFlow BLADE):** No wire installation — define zones in an app using RTK/GPS positioning. Setup is faster and works for renters. Navigation is improving rapidly but can be affected by tree canopy or signal interference. Premium pricing ($1,500–$3,000).

## What to Know Before You Buy

**Yard size:** Check square footage before buying. Robot mowers are sized for specific areas. Buying undersized means the mower runs 24/7 to keep up; buying oversized wastes money.

**Slope:** Measure your steepest grade. Most mowers handle 25–35° slopes. If your yard is steeper, you need the Husqvarna Automower or Mammotion Luba AWD models specifically.

**Obstacles:** Trees, flower beds, and garden furniture require either obstacle avoidance sensors (available as upgrades on Worx Landroid) or careful boundary wire routing.

**Blade replacement:** All robot mowers use disposable razor blades that need replacement every 1–2 months depending on usage. Blade packs are ~$8–$15 and snap in without tools.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do robot lawn mowers work in rain?**
Most models rated IPX5 or higher continue mowing in light rain. They'll return to the dock in heavy rain thanks to built-in rain sensors.

**Do I need a professional to install the boundary wire?**
DIY is possible for most homeowners in 2–6 hours using the included pegs and wire. Professional installation runs $200–$400 and is worth it for complex yard layouts.

**Will a robot mower leave tracks on my lawn?**
No — robot mowers use random or systematic patterns (depending on model) that avoid repetitive paths. Your lawn actually benefits from the frequent light trimming that encourages denser growth.
