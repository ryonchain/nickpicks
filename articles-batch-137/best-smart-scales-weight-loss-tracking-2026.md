## What to Look for in a Smart Scale

### BIA Accuracy: Manage Expectations
All consumer smart scales use Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis (BIA) to estimate body composition. These readings fluctuate with hydration, time of day, and food intake — differences of 2–3% are normal. The real value is **tracking trends over weeks and months**, not reading individual measurements as precise.

**Pro tip:** Weigh yourself at the same time each day (morning, after using the bathroom, before eating) for the most consistent readings.

### App Quality Matters as Much as Hardware
A smart scale is only as good as its companion app. Look for:
- Trend charts over time
- Goal setting and progress tracking
- Integration with your existing health apps (Apple Health, MyFitnessPal, etc.)
- Multi-user support

### Wi-Fi vs Bluetooth Sync
Bluetooth scales require your phone to be nearby to sync. Wi-Fi scales (Withings, Garmin, Eufy P2 Pro) sync automatically in the background. If you want your data waiting for you in the app without any manual steps, choose Wi-Fi.

## FAQ

**Q: How accurate are smart scale body fat readings?**
A: Consumer BIA scales are accurate to within 3–5% of clinical methods like DEXA scans. They're reliable for tracking relative changes over time, but don't treat any single reading as medical-grade data.

**Q: Can multiple people use the same smart scale?**
A: Yes — all scales in this roundup support multiple user profiles, and some (like Eufy P2 Pro and Garmin Index S2) automatically recognize which user is stepping on based on prior weight readings.

**Q: How often should I weigh myself when trying to lose weight?**
A: Daily weigh-ins at the same time give you the most data, but focus on the weekly average, not day-to-day swings. Weight fluctuates 1–3 lbs daily based on hydration and meals.

**Q: Do smart scales work during pregnancy?**
A: Bioelectrical impedance is not recommended during pregnancy. Use a standard non-BIA scale during pregnancy.
