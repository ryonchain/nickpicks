## Resistance Band Buying Guide

### Loop Bands vs Tube Bands With Handles

**Loop bands** (flat loop): Better for lower body work, mobility, yoga, Pilates, and glute activation. No handles, so they're not suitable for heavy rowing or pressing.

**Tube bands with handles**: Better for mimicking dumbbell and cable machine movements — rows, curls, presses, tricep pushdowns. The door anchor unlocks most cable machine movements.

**Fabric bands**: Specialist lower-body bands. Best grip, most comfortable, zero rolling. Worth buying as a complement to a tube band set.

### Resistance Levels to Start With
For beginners: Start with the Light and Medium bands in whatever set you choose. Heavy and X-Heavy bands are harder to use correctly and easy to injure yourself with if your form isn't dialed in first.

### What Exercises Can You Replace With Bands?
- Bicep curls, tricep pushdowns, shoulder press, lateral raises (tube bands)
- Lat pulldowns, rows, chest press (tube bands with door anchor)
- Squats, hip thrusts, glute bridges, clamshells, side steps (loop or fabric bands)
- Pull-up assistance, stretching, mobility (heavy loop bands)

What you **can't** replace well: heavy deadlifts, bench press with heavy loads, Olympic lifts.

## FAQ

**Q: Can resistance bands build real muscle?**
A: Yes — progressive overload works with bands as well as weights. Bands provide increasing resistance through the range of motion (hardest at the peak of the movement), which can be advantageous for muscle building.

**Q: How long do resistance bands last?**
A: Quality latex bands last 1–3 years with regular use. Store them away from sunlight and heat to extend lifespan. Replace bands that show cracking, thinning, or white stress marks.

**Q: Are resistance bands good for seniors?**
A: Excellent for seniors. Low joint impact, adjustable resistance, and usable while seated or lying down. Physical therapists commonly use loop bands for rehab and strength maintenance with older adults.

**Q: Can I do a full workout with only resistance bands?**
A: Yes — full-body workouts covering push, pull, hinge, squat, and core patterns are achievable with a good tube band set and door anchor. Many people combine bands with bodyweight training for complete sessions.
