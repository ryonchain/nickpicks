## The Honest Verdict First

Infrared sauna blankets are worth it **if** you fit the right use case. They deliver real benefits: sweating, cardiovascular activation, relaxation, and some evidence-backed improvements in muscle soreness, circulation, and sleep quality. They are **not** equivalent to a traditional Finnish sauna or a full-spectrum infrared sauna cabin — the heat distribution is less even, you can't move freely, and they require careful cleaning between uses.

The best users: people who would use a sauna regularly but don't have access to one, and who are disciplined about the setup and cleanup routine a blanket requires.

## Are Infrared Sauna Blankets Actually Worth It?

### What the Research Says
The evidence for infrared sauna benefits — not specific to blankets but to infrared therapy in general — is strongest for:
- **Cardiovascular health:** Regular sauna use is associated with reduced cardiovascular risk (Finnish population studies, 20+ years of data)
- **Muscle recovery:** Post-exercise heat therapy reduces DOMS and perceived soreness
- **Relaxation:** Heat triggers parasympathetic nervous system activation and improves subjective sleep quality
- **Skin health:** Sweating supports skin microbiome balance

The evidence **doesn't** strongly support claims about heavy metal "detox" (your liver and kidneys handle detox, not sweat glands) or dramatic weight loss (you lose water, not fat, during a sauna session).

### Blankets vs Traditional Saunas
A traditional Finnish sauna heats the air to 170–200°F, creating dry convective heat that's immediately pervasive. An infrared blanket heats from close contact with the heated surface — similar concept but different thermal experience. Both trigger a heat response; the traditional sauna is generally more intense and better for socialization, relaxation, and breath/steam dynamics. Blankets win on cost, space, and portability.

## FAQ

**Q: How long should you use an infrared sauna blanket?**
A: Start with 15–30 minutes at a lower temperature (120–130°F). Work up to 45–60 minute sessions at higher temps over several weeks. Hydrate before and after.

**Q: Do you need to wear anything in an infrared sauna blanket?**
A: Wear thin cotton clothing or a sauna suit liner inside the blanket. This protects the interior surface, makes cleanup easier, and is more comfortable for most users.

**Q: How do you clean an infrared sauna blanket?**
A: Wipe down the interior surface with a damp cloth or gentle disinfecting spray after each use. Let it air out fully before folding. Some blankets (Reviiv) have machine-washable interiors.

**Q: Are infrared sauna blankets safe?**
A: Yes, when used correctly. Stay hydrated, don't use if pregnant or if you have heart conditions without medical clearance, start with shorter sessions at lower temperatures, and don't fall asleep inside one.

**Q: Can you use an infrared sauna blanket every day?**
A: Daily use is safe for most healthy adults. Start with 3–4 times per week and scale from there. Listen to your body — if you feel fatigued or dehydrated, reduce frequency.
